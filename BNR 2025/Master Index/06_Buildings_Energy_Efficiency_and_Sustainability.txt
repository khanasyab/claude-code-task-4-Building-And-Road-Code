2.1 CLIMATE ZONES IN SRI LANKA

ZONE 1: LOW COUNTRY WET ZONE
Locations: Colombo, Galle, Kalutara, Ratnapura
Characteristics:
- High rainfall (>2500mm annually)
- High humidity (75-85%)
- Moderate temperature (25-30°C)
- Two monsoon seasons

ZONE 2: LOW COUNTRY DRY ZONE
Locations: Hambantota, Puttalam, Trincomalee
Characteristics:
- Low rainfall (<1750mm annually)
- Lower humidity (60-75%)
- Higher temperatures (27-35°C)
- Hot and arid conditions
Design Implications: Solar shading, cooling loads

ZONE 3: HILL COUNTRY (UP-COUNTRY)
Locations: Kandy, Nuwara Eliya, Badulla
Characteristics:
- Moderate to high rainfall
- Moderate humidity
- Cool temperatures (15-25°C depending on elevation)
- Pleasant climate
Design Implications: Natural ventilation, minimal cooling

ZONE 4: INTERMEDIATE ZONE
Locations: Kurunegala, Matale
Characteristics:
- Moderate rainfall (1750-2500mm)
- Transitional characteristics
- Moderate temperatures (24-28°C)
Design Implications: Balanced approach

2.2 OVERALL THERMAL TRANSFER VALUE (OTTV) ⭐

DEFINITION:
OTTV measures average heat gain through building envelope in W/m²

FORMULA:
OTTV = α × (1-WWR) × Uw × TDeq + (WWR × Uf × ΔT) + (WWR × SC × SF)

Where:
- α = Solar absorptance of opaque wall
- WWR = Window-to-Wall Ratio
- Uw = Thermal transmittance of opaque wall (W/m²·K)
- TDeq = Equivalent temperature difference (°C)
- Uf = Thermal transmittance of fenestration (W/m²·K)
- ΔT = Temperature difference indoor/outdoor
- SC = Shading Coefficient of glass
- SF = Solar Factor (W/m²)

MAXIMUM OTTV VALUES:
- Commercial buildings: 50 W/m² (maximum)
- Industrial buildings: 60 W/m² (maximum)
- High performance: <40 W/m² (recommended)
- Best practice: <35 W/m² (recommended)

2.3 BUILDING ENVELOPE REQUIREMENTS

OPAQUE WALLS:
Maximum U-values:
- Above grade walls: 1.5 W/m²·K
- Recommended: <1.2 W/m²·K
- High performance: <0.8 W/m²·K

Insulation Materials:
- Expanded polystyrene (EPS)
- Extruded polystyrene (XPS)
- Mineral wool
- Polyurethane foam
- Reflective insulation
- Natural materials (coconut fiber) ⭐

Solar Absorptance:
- Light colors (white, cream): α = 0.2-0.4 (recommended)
- Medium colors: α = 0.4-0.6
- Dark colors: α = 0.6-0.9 (avoid)

ROOF REQUIREMENTS:
Maximum U-values:
- Standard: 1.0 W/m²·K
- Recommended: <0.8 W/m²·K
- High performance: <0.5 W/m²·K

Roof Color:
- Light colors required (white, light gray)
- Solar Reflectance Index (SRI): >78 recommended
- Cool roof coatings beneficial ⭐

FENESTRATION (WINDOWS AND GLAZING):

Window-to-Wall Ratio (WWR):
- North and South: Maximum 50%
- East and West: Maximum 40%
- Overall building average: Maximum 40%

Glass Specifications:
Shading Coefficient (SC):
- Maximum: 0.60
- Recommended: <0.50
- High performance: <0.40

Solar Heat Gain Coefficient (SHGC):
- Maximum: 0.40
- Recommended: <0.35
- High performance: <0.25

Visible Light Transmittance (VLT):
- Minimum: 0.40 (40%)
- Recommended: >0.50

U-Value (Thermal Transmittance):
- Maximum: 3.3 W/m²·K (single glazing)
- Recommended: <2.8 W/m²·K
- High performance: <2.0 W/m²·K (double glazing)

Glass Options:
- Performance glazing (low-E double): SC 0.30-0.40, U 1.6-2.0 W/m²·K ⭐
- Low-E glass: SC 0.50-0.70, U 3.8-4.5 W/m²·K
- Reflective glass: SC 0.40-0.60, U 5.5 W/m²·K
- Tinted glass: SC 0.70-0.80, U 5.7 W/m²·K

EXTERNAL SHADING:
Devices highly recommended:
- Horizontal overhangs (effective for south)
- Vertical fins (effective for east/west)
- Operable louvers and blinds ⭐
- Vegetation (deciduous trees, vines)

2.4 HVAC SYSTEMS AND REQUIREMENTS

HVAC systems account for 50-60% of building energy consumption

MINIMUM EQUIPMENT EFFICIENCY:

Package Air Conditioners:
- <12 kW: EER ≥2.9 W/W
- 12-40 kW: EER ≥2.7 W/W
- >40 kW: EER ≥2.6 W/W

Water-Cooled Chillers:
- Centrifugal: COP ≥5.0 (full load), IPLV ≥6.0 ⭐
- Screw chillers: COP ≥4.2 (full load), IPLV ≥5.0

Air Handling Units:
- Fan power: <2.0 W/(L/s)
- Filter pressure drop: <250 Pa

Cooling Towers:
- Efficiency: ≥65 L/s per kW heat rejection

VENTILATION RATES:
Based on ASHRAE Standard 62.1-2007
- Offices: 8-10 L/s per person
- Retail: 7.5 L/s per person
- Classrooms: 8 L/s per person
- Hotel guest rooms: 12 L/s per person
- Restaurants: 10-12 L/s per person

CONTROLS:
- Programmable thermostats required
- Temperature range: 23-26°C
- Time-based control for unoccupied periods
- Building Management System (BMS) for >2,000 m² ⭐
- Demand-controlled ventilation (DCV) ⭐

COMMISSIONING:
For systems >350 kW cooling capacity:
- Design review
- Installation verification
- Functional performance testing ⭐
- Systems manual
- Training

2.5 LIGHTING SYSTEMS

Lighting accounts for 20-30% of building energy

LIGHTING POWER DENSITY (LPD):
Maximum values (W/m²):
- Office spaces: 12 W/m²
- Corridors: 5 W/m²
- Conference rooms: 13 W/m²
- Classrooms: 13 W/m²
- Retail sales: 15 W/m²
- Hotel guest rooms: 10 W/m²
- Restaurant dining: 11 W/m²
- Parking garage: 2 W/m²

LAMP EFFICACY:
- LED: ≥75 lumens/Watt (recommended) ⭐
- T5 fluorescent: ≥90 lumens/Watt
- T8 fluorescent: ≥75 lumens/Watt
- CFL: ≥55 lumens/Watt

LIGHTING CONTROLS (MANDATORY):
- Occupancy sensors (private offices, conference rooms)
- Time scheduling for buildings >500 m²
- Daylighting controls (continuous dimming) ⭐
- Photocell sensors
- Automatic shut-off during unoccupied hours

2.6 RENEWABLE ENERGY INTEGRATION ⭐

SOLAR PHOTOVOLTAIC (PV):
Benefits:
- Abundant solar resource (4.5-5.5 kWh/m²/day)
- Grid-connected or standalone
- Net metering available
- Rapid payback

Design Considerations:
- Roof orientation and tilt
- Shading analysis
- Structural capacity
- System sizing

SOLAR WATER HEATING:
Applications:
- Hotels (guest room hot water)
- Hospitals
- Residential
- Industrial processes

System Types:
- Flat plate collectors
- Evacuated tube collectors
- Thermosyphon or pumped systems

Benefits:
- High efficiency (50-70%)
- Short payback period

2.7 WATER EFFICIENCY IN EEBC

LOW-FLOW FIXTURES:
- Faucets: Maximum 6-8 L/min
- Dual-flush toilets: 3/6 liters
- Low-flow toilets: 6 liters per flush
- Showerheads: Maximum 9 L/min, Low-flow: 6-7 L/min

RAINWATER HARVESTING:
Encouraged for:

- Toilet flushing

System Components:

2.8 COMPLIANCE PATHS

PRESCRIPTIVE PATH:

PERFORMANCE PATH: ⭐

CHAPTER 4: USA IgCC 2024 - INTERNATIONAL GREEN CONSTRUCTION CODE
======================================================================

4.1 OVERVIEW OF IgCC

The IgCC provides comprehensive requirements for green building design,
construction, and operation across multiple categories:
- Site Sustainability
- Water Use Efficiency
- Energy Efficiency
- Indoor Environmental Quality (IEQ)
- Materials and Resources
- Construction and Plans for Operation

4.2 SITE SUSTAINABILITY (IgCC CHAPTER 5)

SITE SELECTION:
Prohibited Development:
- Flood hazard areas (Category IV buildings)
- Fish and wildlife habitat conservation areas (<150 ft)
- Wetlands (<100 ft)
- Previously undeveloped sensitive areas

Preferred Sites:
- Previously developed sites
- Brownfield remediation ⭐
- Greyfield redevelopment ⭐
- Infill development

STORMWATER MANAGEMENT: ⭐
Greenfields:
- Retain 95th percentile precipitation event on-site
- Maintain site water balance
- Infiltration, evapotranspiration, harvesting

Greyfields:
- Retain 60th percentile event
- Limit TSS to 25 mg/L in discharge

HEAT ISLAND EFFECT MITIGATION:
Site Hardscape (50% minimum):
- Shading with vegetation
- High SRI paving (SRI ≥29)
- Permeable pavement ⭐
- Open-grid pavers

Roofs (75% minimum):
- High SRI (≥64 for low slope, ≥25 for steep)
- Green roofs ⭐
- Solar panels

LIGHT POLLUTION REDUCTION: ⭐
- BUG ratings (Backlight, Uplight, Glare)
- Lighting zones (LZ0-LZ4)
- Shielded luminaires
- Dark-sky compliant design

TRANSPORTATION MITIGATION:
Pedestrian and Bicycle:
- Pedestrian walkways (≥5 ft wide)
- Bicycle paths and connectivity
- Bicycle parking (5% of occupant load minimum)
- Secure storage facilities

Electric Vehicles: ⭐
Group A, B, E, F, I, M, S:
- 4% EV ready or EVSE spaces
- 30% EV capable spaces

Group R-1, R-2, R-4:
- 20% EV ready or EVSE spaces
- 75% EV capable spaces

4.3 WATER USE EFFICIENCY (IgCC CHAPTER 6) ⭐

INDOOR WATER USE REDUCTION:

Fixtures and Fittings:
- Water closets: Maximum 1.28 gal/flush (4.8 L)
- Urinals: Maximum 0.5 gal/flush (1.9 L), or waterless ⭐
- Public lavatory faucets: 0.5 gpm (1.9 L/min)
- Residential bathroom faucets: 1.5 gpm (5.7 L/min)
- Residential kitchen faucets: 1.8 gpm (6.8 L/min)
- Residential showerheads: 2.0 gpm (7.6 L/min)
- Water-bottle filling stations required ⭐

Appliances:
- ENERGY STAR® clothes washers: WF ≤5.4 gal/ft³
- ENERGY STAR® dishwashers: WF ≤3.8 gal/cycle

OUTDOOR WATER USE:

Irrigation:
- 60% of landscape not irrigated (except establishment)
- Smart irrigation controllers ⭐
- ET-based scheduling
- Moisture sensors
- Drip irrigation for non-turf areas

Irrigation System Design:
- Hydrozones
- Backflow prevention
- Master valves
- Flow sensors ⭐
- Pressure-compensating drip emitters

HVAC WATER USE:
- Once-through cooling prohibited
- Cooling towers: ≥90% of maximum cycles of concentration
- Drift eliminators (≤0.002% counterflow, ≤0.005% cross-flow)
- Condensate recovery (>65,000 Btu/h) ⭐

HOT WATER DISTRIBUTION:
- Maximum pipe volume: 64 oz (1.9 L) from water heater
- Maximum pipe volume: 24 oz (0.71 L) from circulation loop
- Maximum length: 50 ft (15 m)

WATER METERING AND MONITORING: ⭐
Main Meters:
- Potable water >1000 gal/day
- Reclaimed water >1000 gal/day
- Alternate sources >500 gal/day
- Remote communication capability

Submeters Required:
- Cooling towers (makeup and blowdown)
- Evaporative coolers
- Steam/hot water boilers
- Irrigated landscape >25,000 ft²
- Large water-using processes >1000 gal/day
- Tenant spaces >1000 gal/day

Leak Detection: ⭐
- Electronic leak detection devices
- Flow, acoustic, or pressure monitoring
- Alert communication system

ON-SITE WATER TREATMENT: ⭐
- Reverse osmosis systems (automatic shutoff)
- On-site reclaimed water treatment (NSF/ANSI 350)
- Greywater reuse systems
- Blackwater treatment

4.4 ENERGY EFFICIENCY (IgCC CHAPTER 7)

The IgCC references and enhances IECC and ASHRAE 90.1 requirements
with additional provisions for:
- Enhanced envelope performance
- High-efficiency HVAC systems
- Advanced lighting controls ⭐
- Renewable energy integration ⭐
- Building commissioning ⭐
- Energy monitoring and verification ⭐

4.5 INDOOR ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY (IgCC CHAPTER 8) ⭐

INDOOR AIR QUALITY:

Ventilation:
- Comply with ASHRAE 62.1 and 62.2
- Enhanced minimum rates
- Outdoor air delivery monitoring ⭐
- CO₂ sensors for DCV

Filtration:
- Minimum MERV 13 (or ePM1-50% ISO 16890) ⭐
- MERV 8 minimum upstream of cooling coils
- Ozone removal in nonattainment areas
- Filter sealing requirements

Smoking and Vaping:
- Prohibited inside building
- Designated areas ≥25 ft from entrances

Building Entrances:
- 3-surface entryway system ⭐
  * Scraper surface (3 ft minimum)
  * Absorption surface (3 ft minimum)
  * Finishing surface (4 ft minimum)

MATERIAL EMISSIONS: ⭐

Low-Emitting Materials Required:
- Adhesives and sealants (SCAQMD Rule 1168 or emissions testing)
- Paints and coatings (CARB SCM or emissions testing)
- Floor coverings (CDPH/EHLB/Standard Method)
- Composite wood (ultra-low formaldehyde or NAF)
- Office furniture (ANSI/BIFMA X7.1 and e3)
- Ceiling and wall systems
- Insulation

Testing Standards:
- CDPH/EHLB/Standard Method ⭐
- ISO/IEC 17025 accredited laboratories
- ISO/IEC 17065 certified products

THERMAL COMFORT:
- ASHRAE 55 compliance
- Design and documentation requirements
- Occupant thermal satisfaction

MOISTURE CONTROL:
- Dynamic heat and moisture analysis (ASHRAE 160)
- Steady-state vapor transmission analysis
- No condensation in envelope components

ACOUSTICAL CONTROL: ⭐

Interior Background Noise:
Maximum levels by space type:
- Enclosed offices: 35 dBA (Leq), 45 dBA (Lmax)
- Conference rooms: 35 dBA (Leq), 45 dBA (Lmax)
- Open offices: 45 dBA (Leq), 55 dBA (Lmax)
- Residential sleeping: 35 dBA (Leq), 45 dBA (Lmax)

Sound Transmission:
- Minimum cSTC values by space type
- Dwelling/sleeping units: cSTC 55, IIC 55
- Conference rooms: cSTC 50, IIC 50
- Enclosed offices: cSTC 45, IIC 45

Reverberation Control:
- Maximum T60 by space type
- Enclosed offices: 0.6 seconds
- Conference rooms: 0.6 seconds

Property Line Sound Levels:
- Maximum hourly average Leq by property type
- Non-industrial to non-industrial: 50 dBA

INDOOR LIGHTING QUALITY: ⭐

Controllability:
- Continuous dimming to 10% or less
- Local manual dimming control
- Phase-cut dimmers (NEMA SSL 7A)

Color Rendition:
- Rf ≥85, Rf,h1 ≥85
- Rg ≥92
- Rcs,h1: -7% to +19%
- IES TM-30 compliance

Flicker Control: ⭐
- Percent amplitude modulation limits
- SVM ≤0.4, Pst ≤1.0
- Testing per NEMA 77 or California Title 24

DAYLIGHTING: ⭐

Large Spaces with High Ceilings:
- ≥50% of floor area in daylight area
- Skylights ≥3% of daylight area
- Skylight effective aperture ≥1%
- VT ≥0.40
- Haze value >90% (diffuse)

Sidelighting:
Required spaces:
- Classrooms, conference rooms
- Enclosed and open offices
- Lounges, breakrooms
- Library reading areas
- Patient rooms (healthcare)

Minimum sidelighting effective aperture:
- Zones 0-3B: 0.10
- Zones 3C-8: 0.15

Daylight Simulation Alternative: ⭐
- Spatial daylight autonomy (sDA) ≥40%
- Annual sunlight exposure (ASE) ≤20%
- IES LM 83 methodology

SOLAR GLARE CONTROL:
- Operable glare control devices
- Reduce VT to ≤3%
- Occupant override capability
- Or ASE ≤3%

EXTERIOR VIEWS:
- ≥50% of floor area with line-of-sight to windows
- Maximum distance: 40 ft
- Glazing area ≥8% of floor area
- VT ≥20%, haze <3%

4.6 MATERIALS AND RESOURCES (IgCC CHAPTER 9) ⭐

CONSTRUCTION WASTE MANAGEMENT:
- Waste management plan required
- ≥90% land-clearing debris diverted
- Recycling and reuse emphasis

BUILDING REUSE:
Points for:
- 50% reuse: 1 point
- 75% reuse: 2 points
- 95% reuse: 3 points

SUSTAINABLE MATERIALS:

Recycled Content:
- Post-consumer and post-industrial
- Target: 15-30% of material cost

Regional Materials:
- Extracted and manufactured within 500 km
- Support local economy
- Reduce transportation impacts

Rapidly Renewable:
- ≤10-year harvest cycle
- Bamboo, coconut products ⭐
- Cork, natural fibers

Certified Wood:
- FSC certified preferred
- Sustainable forestry
- Chain of custody
