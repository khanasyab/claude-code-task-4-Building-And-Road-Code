2.1 CHAPTER 1: GENERAL PROVISIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------
SOURCE: BNBC_2020_Part_04_Fire_Protection.txt (Lines 18-150)

2.1.1 SCOPE AND PURPOSE
• Fire prevention and protection requirements for all buildings
• Safeguard life from fire, smoke, and toxic fumes
• Protect property and facilitate fire service operations
• Minimize fire spread between buildings

2.1.2 APPLICABILITY
• All new buildings and structures
• Existing buildings undergoing alteration
• Change of occupancy situations
• Buildings being repaired after fire damage
• Temporary structures for public assembly

2.1.3 KEY DEFINITIONS
• Fire Resistance Rating: Time (minutes/hours) material withstands fire
• Fire Door: Door assembly with specified fire resistance rating
• Fire Compartment: Space enclosed by fire-resisting construction
• Means of Egress: Continuous path from any point to place of safety
• Fire Load: Total combustible materials (kg/m² or MJ/m²)
• Occupant Load: Maximum persons in building or portion
• Automatic Sprinkler System: Integrated fire suppression system
• Smoke Control System: Engineered smoke movement control
• Fire Alarm System: Detection and alert devices

2.1.4 FIRE TESTS AND RATINGS
Standard Fire Tests:
• ASTM E119: Building construction and materials
• BS 476: Building materials and structures
• ISO 834: Fire-resistance tests
• BSTI approved equivalent tests

Fire Resistance Requirements:
• Type 1 Construction (Fire-resistive): 3-4 hours
• Type 2 Construction (Non-combustible): 2-3 hours
• Type 3 Construction (Ordinary): 2 hours
• Type 4 Construction (Heavy timber): 1-2 hours
• Type 5 Construction (Wood frame): 1 hour

High-Rise Buildings (over 23m): 2 hours minimum
Fire Doors: 0.5-2 hours based on application

2.1.5 FIRE DRILL REQUIREMENTS
Mandatory Drill Frequency:
• Commercial buildings: Quarterly (every 3 months)
• Residential high-rises: Semi-annually (every 6 months)
• Schools and institutions: Monthly during session
• Hospitals and care facilities: Quarterly on each shift
• Factories and industrial: Quarterly

Fire Drill Procedures:
• Activation of fire alarm
• Orderly evacuation of all occupants
• Use of designated escape routes
• Assembly at designated safe areas
• Accounting for all occupants
• Timing and evaluation of drill
• Documentation and record keeping

2.2 CHAPTER 2: PRECAUTIONARY REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------
SOURCE: BNBC_2020_Part_04_Fire_Protection.txt (Lines 151-484)

2.2.1 OCCUPANCY CLASSIFICATION FOR FIRE SAFETY

GROUP A - ASSEMBLY (High Risk)
• A-1: Theaters, cinemas, concert halls (fixed seating)
• A-2: Restaurants, nightclubs, bars
• A-3: Churches, mosques, temples, community halls
• A-4: Indoor sports arenas, swimming pools
• A-5: Outdoor assembly venues
Fire Risk: High due to large occupant numbers

GROUP B - BUSINESS (Moderate Risk)
• Offices, banks, post offices, professional services
Fire Risk: Moderate due to normal occupant awareness

GROUP C - EDUCATIONAL (Moderate to High Risk)
• Schools, colleges, universities, training centers
Fire Risk: Higher for younger students, moderate for adults

GROUP D - INSTITUTIONAL (High Risk)
• D-1: Hospitals, nursing homes
• D-2: Prisons, detention facilities
• D-3: Day care centers (over 5 children)
Fire Risk: High due to occupant limitations

GROUP E - RESIDENTIAL (Moderate Risk)
• E-1: Hotels, motels, boarding houses
• E-2: Apartment buildings, dormitories
• E-3: Single and two-family dwellings
Fire Risk: Moderate, higher in high-density occupancies

GROUP F - MERCANTILE (Moderate to High Risk)
• Retail stores, shopping centers, markets
Fire Risk: High in large malls, moderate in small shops

GROUP G - INDUSTRIAL (High Risk)
• G-1: Moderate hazard factories
• G-2: Low hazard factories
Fire Risk: Variable based on processes and materials

GROUP H - HIGH HAZARD (Very High Risk)
• H-1: Explosives manufacturing
• H-2: Flammable gas/liquid storage
• H-3: Combustible materials processing
• H-4: Toxic materials handling
Fire Risk: Very high, special provisions required

GROUP I - STORAGE (Variable Risk)
• I-1: Moderate-hazard storage
• I-2: Low-hazard storage
Fire Risk: Based on stored materials

2.2.2 CONSTRUCTION TYPES AND FIRE RESISTANCE

TYPE 1: FIRE-RESISTIVE CONSTRUCTION
• All structural elements non-combustible
• Highest fire resistance ratings (3-4 hours)
• Required for high-rise buildings (over 23m)
• Suitable for all occupancies
• Materials: Reinforced concrete, protected steel

TYPE 2: NON-COMBUSTIBLE CONSTRUCTION
• Structural elements non-combustible
• Moderate fire resistance (1-3 hours)
• Suitable for multi-storey buildings
• Materials: Concrete, protected steel, masonry

TYPE 3: ORDINARY CONSTRUCTION
• Exterior walls non-combustible
• Interior elements may be combustible
• Fire resistance: 1-2 hours
• Height limitations apply
• Materials: Masonry walls, wood or steel interior

TYPE 4: HEAVY TIMBER CONSTRUCTION
• Large timber members with fire resistance
• Non-combustible exterior walls
• Fire resistance through mass
• Materials: Large dimension timber, masonry

TYPE 5: WOOD FRAME CONSTRUCTION
• Combustible construction permitted
• Lowest fire resistance (0.5-1 hour)
• Height and area limitations
• Primarily for low-rise residential
• Materials: Wood framing throughout

2.2.3 FIRE ZONES AND FIRE DISTRICTS

FIRE ZONE 1 (Very High Density Urban):
• Central business districts
• High-value commercial areas
• Dense residential zones
• Type 1 or 2 construction required
• Combustible exterior finishes prohibited

FIRE ZONE 2 (High Density):
• Urban commercial and residential
• Type 2 or 3 construction minimum
• Fire separation requirements

FIRE ZONE 3 (Moderate Density):
• Suburban areas, mixed use zones
• Type 3 or better construction

FIRE ZONE 4 (Low Density):
• Rural areas, agricultural zones
• Type 5 construction permitted

SPECIAL FIRE DISTRICTS (Bangladesh Context):
• Old Dhaka congested areas
• Chittagong port area
• Industrial zones (Tongi, Gazipur)
• Market areas (New Market, Chawkbazar)
Requirements: Enhanced fire protection, wider streets, mandatory
sprinklers, fire department access

2.2.4 FIRE SEPARATION AND COMPARTMENTATION

Maximum Compartment Areas:
• High-rise buildings: 2000 m² per floor
• Hospitals: 1000 m² per compartment
• Hotels: 2000 m² per floor
• Offices: 3000 m² per floor
• Shopping centers: 2000 m² per tenant
• Warehouses: 5000 m² maximum

Fire Compartment Separation:
• Fire walls with 2-4 hour rating
• Fire doors with appropriate ratings
• Fire-rated penetration seals
• Automatic fire dampers in ducts

Fire Wall Requirements:
• Extend from foundation to roof
• Minimum 3-hour fire resistance
• Non-combustible materials
• Structurally independent
• No openings (or protected openings only)
• Extend 600mm above roof surface

Fire Doors and Shutters:
• Rating equal to wall (up to 3 hours)
• Self-closing or automatic closing
• Approved hardware (panic bars for egress)
• Labeled by approved testing agency
• Maintained in working condition
• Not propped open (unless magnetic hold-open with alarm release)

2.2.5 INTERIOR FINISH MATERIALS

Flame Spread Classification:

CLASS A (Flame Spread 0-25):
• Non-combustible materials
• Cement plaster, concrete, ceramic tiles, stone, gypsum board
• Required: Enclosed exit stairs, corridors in high-occupancy buildings

CLASS B (Flame Spread 26-75):
• Low flame spread materials
• Treated wood panels, some plastics and composites
• Permitted: Most occupancies, general use areas

CLASS C (Flame Spread 76-200):
• Moderate flame spread
• Untreated wood, some carpets and textiles
• Permitted: Low-risk occupancies only

CLASS D (Flame Spread over 200):
• High flame spread, combustible
• Prohibited in most buildings
• Special approval required if used

Application Requirements:
EXIT STAIRS AND CORRIDORS:
• Walls and ceilings: Class A only
• Floors: Class B maximum

ASSEMBLY OCCUPANCIES (Group A):
• Walls and ceilings: Class A or B
• Floors: Class B
• Decorative materials: Flame-retardant treated

RESIDENTIAL (Group E):
• Walls: Class B maximum
• Ceilings: Class B maximum
• Floors: Class C permitted

2.2.6 ELECTRICAL, GAS AND MECHANICAL SERVICES

Electrical Fire Safety:
• Comply with BNBC Part 8
• Approved wiring methods
• Circuit protection (MCBs, fuses)
• Grounding and earthing systems
• Emergency power systems for life safety
• Separation from combustible materials

Fire Risk Areas:
• Garment factories: Enhanced wiring protection
• Electrical rooms: 2-hour fire-rated enclosure
• High-rise buildings: Protected vertical cable runs

Gas Systems (LPG and Natural Gas):
• Comply with BNBC Part 8, Chapter 8
• Automatic shut-off valves
• Gas leak detection systems
• Proper ventilation
• Separation from ignition sources
• Regular leak testing

HVAC Systems:
• Fire dampers in duct penetrations
• Smoke detectors in return air ducts
• Emergency shut-down capability
• Fire-rated equipment rooms
• Kitchen exhaust hood fire suppression

Smoke Control Systems:
• Required in atriums and large spaces
• Stairwell pressurization in high-rises
• Coordinated with fire alarm system
• Regular testing and maintenance

2.2.7 SPECIAL HAZARDS

COMMERCIAL KITCHENS:
• Type I hood with fire suppression system
• Automatic extinguishing for cooking equipment
• Grease duct fire protection
• Class K fire extinguishers
• Gas automatic shut-off
• Regular cleaning and maintenance

GARMENT FACTORIES (Bangladesh Specific):
• Enhanced fire detection throughout
• Multiple means of egress (minimum 2 per floor)
• Automatic sprinkler systems
• Emergency lighting and exit signs
• Fire-resistant storage for materials
• Lint control and housekeeping
• Regular fire drills (monthly)
• Fire safety officer on site

HAZARDOUS MATERIALS STORAGE:
• Separate fire-rated room or building
• Ventilation to exterior
• Explosion-proof electrical
• Spill containment
• Fire suppression systems
• Approved containers
• Maximum quantity limits

2.3 CHAPTER 3: MEANS OF EGRESS
---------------------------------------------------------------------
SOURCE: BNBC_2020_Part_04_Fire_Protection.txt (Lines 486-776)

2.3.1 FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLES

Means of Egress Components:
1. EXIT ACCESS: Path from any occupied point to exit
2. EXIT: Protected path to exterior (stairs, doors)
3. EXIT DISCHARGE: Path from exit to public way

Design Criteria:
• Continuous and unobstructed
• Adequate capacity for occupant load
• Protected from fire and smoke
• Properly illuminated
• Clearly marked with exit signs
• Maintained free of obstructions

2.3.2 OCCUPANT LOAD CALCULATION

Formula: Occupant Load = Floor Area / Occupant Load Factor

Occupant Load Factors (m² per person):

ASSEMBLY USES:
• Fixed seating: Actual number of seats
• Assembly without fixed seating: 0.65 m²/person
• Standing space (waiting areas): 0.28 m²/person
• Stages and platforms: 1.4 m²/person
• Restaurants (dining area): 1.4 m²/person
• Kitchen (restaurant): 9.3 m²/person

BUSINESS USES:
• Office space: 9.3 m²/person
• Bank: 4.6 m²/person

EDUCATIONAL USES:
• Classrooms: 1.85 m²/person
• Shops and laboratories: 4.6 m²/person
• Assembly areas: 0.65 m²/person

RESIDENTIAL USES:
• Hotels/dormitories: 18.5 m²/person
• Apartments: Actual occupants or 18.5 m²/person

MERCANTILE USES:
• Retail sales (ground floor): 2.8 m²/person
• Upper floors: 5.6 m²/person
• Storage areas: 27.9 m²/person

INDUSTRIAL:
• General: 9.3 m²/person
• Concentrated equipment: 4.6 m²/person

2.3.3 EXIT CAPACITY

WIDTH REQUIREMENTS:

Doors:
• Minimum clear width: 750mm (single leaf)
• Preferred width: 900mm
• Maximum leaf width: 1200mm
• Capacity: 1 person per 5mm width

Corridors and Exit Access:
• Minimum width: 1100mm
• Serving occupant load over 50: 1200mm minimum
• Capacity: 1 person per 5mm width

Stairs:
• Minimum width: 1100mm
• Serving over 50 persons: 1200mm minimum
• High-rise buildings: 1500mm minimum
• Capacity: 1 person per 7.6mm width

MINIMUM NUMBER OF EXITS:
• 1-500 persons: 2 exits minimum
• 501-1000 persons: 3 exits minimum
• Over 1000 persons: 4 exits minimum

Special Requirements:
• High-rise buildings: Minimum 2 exit stairs per floor
• Assembly occupancies: Exits based on occupant load
• Each floor must have minimum 2 exits (except single dwelling units)

Exit Arrangement:
• Remote from each other (minimum 1/3 diagonal distance apart)
• Accessible from any point in building
• Not through high-hazard areas
• Not through kitchens, storage, or restrooms
• Arranged to minimize confusion

2.3.4 EXIT ACCESS

TRAVEL DISTANCE LIMITATIONS:

Without Sprinklers:
• Assembly, Educational, Institutional: 30m
• Residential: 35m
• Business: 45m
• Factory (low hazard): 60m
• Storage (low hazard): 75m

With Automatic Sprinklers (add 25m):
• Assembly, Educational, Institutional: 55m
• Residential: 60m
• Business: 70m
• Factory (low hazard): 85m
• Storage (low hazard): 100m

Dead-End Corridors:
• Maximum length: 6m (unsprinklered)
• Maximum length: 15m (sprinklered)

CORRIDOR REQUIREMENTS:
• Minimum width: 1100mm (1200mm if over 50 occupants)
• Clear height: 2100mm minimum
• Fire-rated construction:
  - 1-hour rating for buildings over 4 storeys
  - 2-hour rating for high-rise buildings
• Protected by smoke detectors
• Emergency lighting provided
• Exit signs visible from all points

DOORS IN MEANS OF EGRESS:
• Swing in direction of egress (occupant load over 50)
• Panic hardware for assembly occupancies over 50
• Minimum clear width: 750mm
• Maximum leaf width: 1200mm
• No mirrors on egress side
• Door swing not obstruct corridor
• Self-closing or automatic closing
• Readily openable from inside without key

Prohibited Features:
• Revolving doors as required exits
• Turnstiles counting as exits
• Sliding doors (except special cases)
• Locks preventing egress
• Delayed egress (except with approved special locking)

2.3.5 EXIT STAIRS

Interior Exit Stairs:
• Enclosed in fire-rated shaft:
  - 2 hours for buildings up to 4 storeys
  - 2 hours for buildings over 4 storeys
  - 2 hours minimum for high-rise
• Pressurized in high-rise buildings
• Fire-rated doors (1-1.5 hours)
• Self-closing doors with panic hardware
• Emergency lighting
• Exit signs at each floor
• Communication system in high-rise

STAIR DIMENSIONS:

Tread and Riser:
• Minimum tread depth: 250mm (280mm preferred)
• Maximum riser height: 190mm (175mm preferred)
• Uniform dimensions throughout flight
• Maximum variation: 5mm

Landings:
• Width not less than stair width
• Length not less than 1100mm
• At door openings: swing not reduce effective width

Handrails:
• Required both sides
• Height: 865-965mm above nosing
• Continuous along flight and landings
• Smooth gripping surface, 35-50mm diameter
• Extension 300mm beyond top and bottom
• Load capacity: 0.9 kN/m

EXTERIOR EXIT STAIRS:
When permitted:
• Open to exterior air
• Protected from weather (canopy/overhang)
• Non-combustible construction
• Proper drainage
• Non-slip surface
• Adequate lighting
• Not more than 50% of required exits

2.3.6 RAMPS

Design Requirements:
• Maximum slope: 1:12 (8.33%)
• Preferred slope: 1:20 for accessibility
• Minimum clear width: 1100mm
• Surface: Non-slip, uniform
• Landings: Every 9m of rise and at direction changes
• Landing length: 1500mm minimum
• Handrails: Both sides if rise over 150mm
• Fire rating: Same as stair if enclosed

2.3.7 EXIT SIGNS AND EMERGENCY LIGHTING

EXIT SIGNS REQUIRED AT:
• All exit doors
• Exit access doors to stairs
• Changes of direction to exits
• Locations where exit path is not obvious

Sign Requirements:
• Word "EXIT" or "বহির্গমন" (Bengali)
• Letter height: 150mm minimum
• Visibility: 30m minimum in clear air
• Illuminated: Internally or externally
• Green color on white background (international standard)
• Battery backup: 90 minutes minimum
• Pictograms: ISO standard running man acceptable

Directional Exit Signs:
• Where exit not immediately visible
• Multiple directions possible
• Long corridors
• Sign spacing: Every 30m maximum

EMERGENCY LIGHTING REQUIRED IN:
• Exit stairs and corridors
• Exit doors
• Assembly areas
• All means of egress

Performance Requirements:
• Automatic activation on power failure
• Battery backup: 90 minutes minimum
• Illumination level: 10 lux minimum at floor
• Transition: Within 10 seconds of power loss
• Monthly testing required

2.3.8 SPECIAL EGRESS PROVISIONS

HIGH-RISE BUILDINGS (over 23m):
• Minimum 2 exit stairs
• Pressurized stairs
• Fire service elevator
• Emergency voice communication
• Refuge areas (every 15 floors in tall buildings)
• Enhanced emergency lighting
• Stair identification signs at each floor

ASSEMBLY OCCUPANCIES:
• Multiple exits based on occupant load
• Exit capacity: All occupants in 4 minutes
• Panic hardware on all exit doors
• No turnstiles in exit path
• Aisles to exits with proper width
• Fixed seating: Aisle requirements

HOSPITALS AND CARE FACILITIES:
• Horizontal exits (fire-rated corridors to separate compartment)
• Refuge areas with smoke protection
• Minimum 2 exits per floor
• Wider corridors: 2400mm minimum
• Automatic door openers
• Areas of rescue assistance

SCHOOLS:
• Direct access to exterior from ground floor
• Multiple exits per classroom wing
• Assembly areas: Stadium egress
• Wider corridors and stairs
• Panic hardware
• Drill requirements: Monthly

2.4 CHAPTER 4: FIRE DETECTION AND ALARM SYSTEMS
---------------------------------------------------------------------
SOURCE: BNBC_2020_Part_04_Fire_Protection.txt (Lines 778-902)

2.4.1 GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

WHERE REQUIRED:
• High-rise buildings (all types)
• Hotels and dormitories over 3 storeys
• Hospitals and care facilities (all)
• Schools over 2 storeys
• Assembly occupancies over 300 persons
• Shopping centers over 500 m²
• Factories and warehouses over 2000 m²
• Underground areas over 200 m²

SYSTEM TYPES:
• Conventional Systems: Zone-based detection
• Addressable Systems: Individual device identification
• Analog Addressable: Level of detection reported
Preference: Addressable systems for buildings over 5 storeys

2.4.2 SYSTEM COMPONENTS

CONTROL PANEL:
• Listed and approved system
• Located in fire command center or entry lobby
• Accessible to fire service
• Display zones/devices in alarm
• Manual evacuation control
• Backup power supply

DETECTION DEVICES:

Smoke Detectors:
• Ionization or photoelectric type
• Ceiling-mounted in general areas
• Coverage: 80 m² per detector (open areas)
• Required in: corridors, stairs, mechanical rooms, elevator lobbies

Heat Detectors:
• Fixed temperature or rate-of-rise
• Kitchens, boiler rooms, attics
• Areas unsuitable for smoke detectors
• Coverage: 50 m² per detector

Manual Pull Stations:
• Located at exits
• Each floor near stairways
• Maximum travel distance: 60m
• Height: 1200-1500mm above floor
• Break-glass type

Beam Detectors:
• Large open spaces
• High ceilings (over 10m)
• Warehouses, atriums

NOTIFICATION DEVICES:

Audible Alarms:
• Bells, horns, sirens
• Sound level: 75 dB minimum at 3m
• 15 dB above ambient noise
• Distinct from other signals
• Temporal-3 pattern (ISO 8201)

Visual Alarms:
• Strobe lights
• Required in public areas and corridors
• Accessibility for hearing impaired
• Flash rate: 1-3 Hz

Voice Evacuation:
• Required in high-rise buildings
• Pre-recorded messages
• Live microphone capability
• Minimum 75 dB sound level
• Two-way communication in exit stairs

2.4.3 SYSTEM OPERATION

Alarm Sequence:
1. Control panel receives signal
2. Alarm verification (if programmed)
3. Audible and visual alarms activate
4. Elevator recall to ground floor
5. Door hold-open releases
6. Smoke control activates
7. Emergency lighting activates
8. Notification to fire service (automatic or manual)

Zoning:
• Each floor separate zone
• Large floors: Multiple zones
• Zone size: Maximum 2000 m²
• Stairwells: Separate zones
• Mechanical spaces: Separate zones

2.4.4 POWER SUPPLY

Primary Power:
• Connected to normal building power
• Dedicated circuit
• Located in fire-rated electrical room
• Protection from fire and water damage

Backup Power:
• Battery backup: 24 hours standby + 15 minutes alarm
• Generator backup (high-rise)
• Automatic transfer
• Low battery alarm
• Regular testing

2.4.5 MONITORING AND MAINTENANCE

Central Monitoring:
• 24-hour monitoring for high-rise buildings
• Connection to fire service
• Automatic signal transmission
• Response protocol established

Testing and Maintenance:
• Weekly: Visual inspection, test functions
• Monthly: Battery testing, alarm testing
• Quarterly: Full system testing
• Annual: Comprehensive inspection by licensed technician
• Records maintained minimum 3 years
• Testing by BSTI approved agencies

2.5 CHAPTER 5: FIRE SUPPRESSION SYSTEMS
---------------------------------------------------------------------
SOURCE: BNBC_2020_Part_04_Fire_Protection.txt (Lines 904-1122)

2.5.1 AUTOMATIC SPRINKLER SYSTEMS

WHERE REQUIRED:
• High-rise buildings (all floors)
• Hotels over 3 storeys
• Hospitals and care facilities (all areas)
• Assembly occupancies over 1000 persons
• Shopping centers over 1000 m²
• Garment factories (all areas)
• Warehouses over 5000 m²
• Underground areas over 500 m²
• Atriums and large open spaces

SYSTEM TYPES:

Wet Pipe Systems:
• Pipes filled with water under pressure
• Immediate discharge when head activates
• Most common type for heated buildings

Dry Pipe Systems:
• Pipes filled with compressed air
• Water admitted after valve opens
• Unheated spaces, freezing risk areas

Pre-Action Systems:
• Requires detector activation before water flows
• Computer rooms, museums, libraries
• Prevents water damage from accidental activation

Deluge Systems:
• Open sprinkler heads
• Valve opens to flood area
• High-hazard areas, chemical storage

DESIGN STANDARDS:
• NFPA 13: Automatic Sprinkler Systems
• Hydraulic calculations required
• Water supply adequate for demand
• Minimum 30-minute duration at design density

Sprinkler Spacing:
• Maximum coverage: 12 m² per head (ordinary hazard)
• Maximum spacing: 4.6m between heads
• Distance to wall: 2.3m maximum
• Minimum clearance below head: 450mm

WATER SUPPLY:

Sources:
• Municipal water main (preferred)
• On-site water tanks with pumps
• Combined system

Tank Capacity (if used):
• High-rise buildings: 200,000 liters minimum
• Mid-rise buildings: 100,000 liters minimum
• Plus domestic water demand

Pressure:
• Minimum: 100 kPa at most remote head
• Fire pumps if municipal pressure insufficient
• Jockey pump to maintain pressure

2.5.2 STANDPIPE SYSTEMS

REQUIRED IN:
• Buildings over 4 storeys
• High-rise buildings (dry and wet standpipes)
• Enclosed stairs
• Stages over 100 m²
• Underground areas

TYPES:

Class I (Fire Department Use):
• 65mm hose connections
• 380 liters/minute per connection
• Fire department connects own hoses

Class II (Occupant Use):
• 40mm hose stations
• 40 liters/minute
• Pre-connected hose (30m length)

Class III (Combined):
• 65mm connections
• 40mm adapters
• Both fire department and occupant use

Locations:
• Enclosed exit stairs
• Exit landings
• Maximum 60m hose travel from any point
• Clearly marked "FIRE HOSE"

2.5.3 PORTABLE FIRE EXTINGUISHERS

TYPES AND CLASSES:

Class A: Ordinary combustibles (wood, paper, cloth)
• Water, foam, dry chemical extinguishers
• Red color band

Class B: Flammable liquids (oil, gasoline, solvents)
• CO2, dry chemical, foam extinguishers
• Yellow color band

Class C: Electrical fires
• CO2, dry chemical (non-conductive)
• Blue color band

Class D: Combustible metals
• Specialized dry powder
• Special training required

Class K: Kitchen fires (cooking oils)
• Wet chemical extinguishers
• Required in commercial kitchens

DISTRIBUTION:

Minimum Rating and Travel Distance:

Class A (Ordinary):
• Light hazard: 2A rating, 23m travel
• Moderate hazard: 2A rating, 15m travel
• High hazard: 4A rating, 11m travel

Class B (Flammable):
• Low hazard: 5B rating, 15m travel
• Moderate hazard: 10B rating, 9m travel
• High hazard: 20B rating, 9m travel

Installation:
• Mounted on wall brackets or cabinets
• Height: 1.5m maximum to top
• Clearly visible
• Access not obstructed
• Signage: "FIRE EXTINGUISHER" in English and Bengali
• Annual inspection and recharging

2.5.4 SPECIAL SUPPRESSION SYSTEMS

Commercial Kitchen Hood Systems:
• Automatic detection (fusible links)
• Manual pull station
• Wet chemical suppression
• Discharge nozzles over cooking surfaces
• Automatic gas/power shut-off
• Mechanical ventilation shutdown

Clean Agent Systems:
• For computer rooms, data centers, museums
• FM-200, Novec 1230, Inergen gases
• No water damage to equipment
• Safe for occupied spaces
• Pre-discharge alarm and delay
• Proper ventilation after discharge

Foam Systems:
• For flammable liquid storage
• Gasoline storage facilities
• Chemical warehouses
• Airport hangars
• Foam concentrate and delivery system

2.5.5 FIRE DEPARTMENT ACCESS

Fire Service Vehicles:
• Minimum access road width: 6m
• Turning radius: 12m minimum
• Vertical clearance: 4.5m minimum
• Load capacity: 40 tons
• Grade: Maximum 10%
• All-weather surface
• Clear access to building entrances

Fire Hydrants:
• Spacing: Maximum 120m along streets
• 100m maximum to building entrance
• 65mm outlets (minimum 2)
• Minimum pressure: 250 kPa
• Color coded: Red for public hydrants
• Clear access maintained
• Regular testing and maintenance

Fire Department Connection (FDC):
• Required for sprinkler and standpipe systems
• Exterior wall near main entrance
• Within 30m of fire hydrant
• Visible from street
• Labeled "FIRE DEPARTMENT CONNECTION - SPRINKLER/STANDPIPE"

11.1 DESIGN PHASE CHECKLIST
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Preliminary Design:
☐ Determine occupancy classification
☐ Establish construction type required
☐ Calculate occupant loads
☐ Determine required means of egress
☐ Identify fire protection system requirements
☐ Plan fire department access
☐ Consider performance-based alternatives if needed

Design Development:
☐ Detail fire-rated construction assemblies
☐ Design automatic sprinkler system
☐ Design fire alarm and detection system
☐ Plan means of egress with proper capacity
☐ Design smoke control systems (if required)
☐ Detail fire compartmentation
☐ Specify interior finishes
☐ Design emergency power systems
☐ Plan fire command center (if required)

Construction Documents:
☐ Complete fire protection drawings
☐ Coordinate with all disciplines
☐ Prepare specifications
☐ Calculate hydraulics for sprinkler systems
☐ Prepare fire safety plans
☐ Coordinate with authorities having jurisdiction

11.2 APPROVAL AND PERMITTING
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Bangladesh Context:
☐ Submit fire safety plan to FSCD
☐ Obtain construction NOC
☐ Schedule inspections during construction
☐ Obtain occupancy NOC before building use
☐ Plan for annual NOC renewal

International Context:
☐ Submit to fire marshal/fire code official
☐ Obtain building permits
☐ Schedule required inspections
☐ Obtain certificate of occupancy
☐ Maintain compliance documentation

11.3 CONSTRUCTION PHASE
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Fire Protection System Installation:
☐ Qualified contractors and installers
☐ Approved materials and equipment
☐ Installation per approved plans
☐ Coordination between trades
☐ Protection of installed systems

Inspections:
☐ Underground piping (before backfill)
☐ Rough-in inspections
☐ Final system inspections
☐ Acceptance testing
☐ System commissioning

☐ Evacuation drills conducted

Ongoing Compliance:

☐ Keep systems operational
☐ Update fire safety plans as needed
☐ Conduct required fire drills
☐ Annual NOC renewal (where required)
