BASE SOURCES:

PAKISTAN CONTEXT:
- Energy crisis: 4,500-8,000 MW shortfall, load shedding 8-12 hours/day
- Solar potential: 5.5-6.5 kWh/m²/day (among world's highest)
- Climate zones: Hot-dry (Karachi), Warm-humid (Lahore), Composite (Islamabad),
  Temperate (Murree), Cold (Northern Areas)
- Net metering: NEPRA regulations 2015 (revised 2019)
- Carbon emissions: 218 million tons/year, buildings sector 35%

================================================================================
SECTION 1: GENERAL REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

1.1 SCOPE AND APPLICATION
-------------------------
Buildings covered:
- All new construction >500 m² floor area
- Additions >100 m² or >50% existing building area
- Alterations where equipment/systems are replaced
- Change of occupancy requiring permits

Exemptions:
- Religious buildings (mosques, churches, temples)
- Temporary buildings <180 days occupancy
- Agricultural buildings (livestock, crop storage)
- Industrial process buildings where >80% energy for manufacturing

Base: IECC 2024 Section C101

1.2 COMPLIANCE PATHS
--------------------
Three options (building owner selects):

PATH 1: Prescriptive Method
- Meet all individual requirements (envelope, mechanical, electrical, service water heating)
- Simplest approach, most common for small buildings

PATH 2: Performance Method
- Computer energy simulation (DOE-2, EnergyPlus, eQUEST, TRNSYS)
- Proposed design ≤ baseline design annual energy cost
- Allows trade-offs between systems

PATH 3: Energy Rating Index (ERI)
- Residential buildings only
- ERI score ≤ target (typically 50-60)
- Uses RESNET HERS methodology

Base: IECC 2024 Section C401

1.3 CLIMATE ZONES (PAKISTAN)
----------------------------
Zone 1 (Hot-Dry): Karachi, Hyderabad, Quetta, Multan
- Summer design: 38-45°C dry bulb, 24-28°C wet bulb
- Winter design: 5-10°C
- Cooling dominant, minimal heating

Zone 2 (Warm-Humid): Lahore, Faisalabad, Sialkot, Gujranwala
- Summer: 40-48°C dry bulb, 28-32°C wet bulb
- Winter: 2-8°C
- High humidity, cooling + moderate heating

Zone 3 (Composite): Islamabad, Rawalpindi, Peshawar
- Summer: 35-42°C
- Winter: -2 to 5°C
- Balanced cooling and heating loads

Zone 4 (Temperate): Murree, Abbottabad, Azad Kashmir (lower elevations)
- Summer: 25-30°C
- Winter: -5 to 2°C
- Heating dominant

Zone 5 (Cold): Gilgit, Skardu, Chitral, Muzaffarabad (higher elevations)
- Summer: 20-28°C
- Winter: -15 to -5°C
- High heating loads, snow loads

Base: ASHRAE 90.1-2022 Climate Zone definitions adapted for Pakistan
Pakistan Green Building Code 2023 Section 2.3

================================================================================
SECTION 2: BUILDING ENVELOPE
================================================================================

2.1 THERMAL INSULATION REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------
Minimum R-values (m²·K/W):

ROOFS (Insulation entirely above deck):
- Zone 1 (Hot-Dry): R-2.6 (U-0.38)
- Zone 2 (Warm-Humid): R-3.3 (U-0.30)
- Zone 3 (Composite): R-4.4 (U-0.23)
- Zone 4 (Temperate): R-5.3 (U-0.19)
- Zone 5 (Cold): R-6.2 (U-0.16)

WALLS (Mass walls - concrete/masonry):
- Zone 1: R-1.3 (U-0.77) - e.g., 230mm brick + 50mm EPS
- Zone 2: R-1.8 (U-0.56) - e.g., 230mm brick + 75mm EPS
- Zone 3: R-2.3 (U-0.43) - e.g., Cavity wall + 75mm EPS
- Zone 4: R-2.8 (U-0.36) - e.g., Cavity wall + 100mm EPS
- Zone 5: R-3.5 (U-0.29) - e.g., Cavity wall + 125mm EPS

FLOORS (Slab-on-grade):
- Zones 1-2: No insulation required (cooling dominant)
- Zone 3: R-1.0 perimeter insulation, 600mm depth
- Zone 4: R-1.5 perimeter insulation, 900mm depth
- Zone 5: R-2.0 full slab insulation

WINDOWS (Fenestration):
- Zone 1-2: U-factor ≤ 3.4 W/m²·K, SHGC ≤ 0.25 (solar heat gain control critical)
- Zone 3: U-factor ≤ 2.8 W/m²·K, SHGC ≤ 0.40
- Zone 4-5: U-factor ≤ 2.3 W/m²·K, SHGC ≥ 0.40 (passive solar heating beneficial)

Maximum window-to-wall ratio: 40% (all zones)

Base: IECC 2024 Tables C402.1.3, C402.1.4
Pakistan Green Building Code 2023 Table 4.1

2.2 AIR LEAKAGE CONTROL
-----------------------
Building envelope air tightness:
- Residential: ≤ 5.0 air changes per hour at 50 Pa (ACH50)
- Commercial: ≤ 2.0 L/s·m² at 75 Pa

Testing: Blower door test per ASTM E779 (residential) or E1827 (commercial)
Required for:
- Buildings >2,000 m² conditioned area
- All high-performance/green certified buildings

Air barrier materials:
- Gypsum board, exterior sheathing, house wrap, fluid-applied membranes
- All joints/penetrations sealed (caulk, foam, gaskets)
- Continuous from foundation to roof

Base: IECC 2024 Section C402.5
ASHRAE 90.1-2022 Section 5.4.3

2.3 THERMAL BRIDGING
--------------------
Metal-framed walls (steel studs):
- Cavity insulation + continuous insulation (ci)
- Zone 1-2: R-1.3 ci
- Zone 3: R-1.8 ci
- Zone 4-5: R-2.3 ci

Balconies/parapets:
- Thermal break at junction with conditioned space
- Structural thermal break materials: Isokorb, Schöck

Base: IECC 2024 Section C402.2.6

================================================================================
SECTION 3: MECHANICAL SYSTEMS (HVAC)
================================================================================

3.1 EQUIPMENT EFFICIENCY STANDARDS
-----------------------------------
Air Conditioning (Split/Window units):
- Capacity ≤ 19 kW (5 tons): EER ≥ 3.0 (10.2 BTU/Wh)
- Capacity > 19 kW: EER ≥ 3.2 (10.9 BTU/Wh)
- Inverter units preferred: SEER ≥ 4.2 (14 BTU/Wh)

Pakistan Energy Efficiency Labels (2023):
- 5-star rating: EER ≥ 3.5
- 4-star: EER 3.2-3.49
- 3-star: EER 3.0-3.19
- Minimum 3-star for new installations

Chillers (Central plants):
- Air-cooled: COP ≥ 2.8 (EER ≥ 9.5)
- Water-cooled: COP ≥ 5.5 (EER ≥ 18.8)
- Variable-speed drives mandatory for >150 kW capacity

Heat Pumps (Northern Areas):
- Heating COP ≥ 3.3 at 8.3°C outdoor
- Cooling EER ≥ 3.5 at 35°C outdoor

Furnaces/Boilers (Heating):
- Gas furnaces: AFUE ≥ 90%
- Oil furnaces: AFUE ≥ 85%
- Gas boilers: Combustion efficiency ≥ 88%

Base: ASHRAE 90.1-2022 Tables 6.8.1-A through 6.8.1-L
Pakistan ENERCON regulations 2023

3.2 HVAC SYSTEM DESIGN
----------------------
Economizers (Free cooling):
- Required for cooling capacity >18 kW
- Air-side economizer: Outside air dampers, relief dampers, controls
- Economizer controls: Differential dry-bulb or differential enthalpy
- Exception: Warm-humid climates (Lahore, Faisalabad) - use only dry-bulb control

Ventilation:
- Demand-controlled ventilation (DCV) for occupancies >100 persons
- CO₂ sensors, setpoint 1,000 ppm
- Variable air volume (VAV) systems

Zone controls:
- Individual thermostatic controls for each thermal zone (≤500 m²)
- Setback controls: Unoccupied periods reduced to 29°C cooling, 15°C heating
- Programmable thermostats or building automation system (BAS)

Duct insulation:
- Supply ducts (conditioned space): R-0.6 minimum
- Return ducts (unconditioned space): R-0.6 minimum
- Outdoor air ducts: R-1.0 minimum
- Duct leakage: ≤ 4% (sealed class per SMACNA)

Pipe insulation:
- Chilled water: R-0.5 (indoor), R-0.9 (outdoor)
- Hot water (heating): R-0.9 (indoor), R-1.3 (outdoor)
- Steam condensate: R-0.9

Base: IECC 2024 Section C403.3, C403.6
ASHRAE 90.1-2022 Section 6.5

3.3 VENTILATION AND IAQ
-----------------------
Minimum outdoor air rates (ASHRAE 62.1-2022):
- Offices: 10 L/s per person + 0.6 L/s·m²
- Classrooms: 10 L/s per person + 0.6 L/s·m²
- Retail: 7.5 L/s per person + 0.6 L/s·m²
- Restaurants: 7.5 L/s per person + 0.9 L/s·m²

Heat recovery:
- Energy recovery ventilation (ERV) for systems >2,500 L/s outdoor air
- Sensible effectiveness ≥ 60%
- Latent effectiveness ≥ 50% (warm-humid climates)

Filtration:
- Minimum MERV 8 filters (outdoor air and return air)
- MERV 13 recommended for hospitals, laboratories
- Pre-filters + final filters for critical environments

Base: ASHRAE 62.1-2022, ASHRAE 90.1-2022 Section 6.5.6

================================================================================
SECTION 4: SERVICE WATER HEATING
================================================================================

4.1 EQUIPMENT EFFICIENCY
------------------------
Storage water heaters:
- Electric (≤55 gallons/208L): Energy Factor EF ≥ 0.93
- Gas (≤55 gallons): EF ≥ 0.67, Thermal Efficiency ≥ 80%

Instantaneous (tankless) water heaters:
- Electric: EF ≥ 0.99
- Gas: EF ≥ 0.82

Heat pump water heaters (HPWH):
- Integrated: Uniform Energy Factor UEF ≥ 3.1
- 200% - 300% more efficient than electric resistance

Solar water heating:
- Mandatory for hospitals, hotels >20 rooms, hostels
- Solar fraction ≥ 60% (annual hot water from solar)
- Backup conventional heater required

Base: IECC 2024 Section C404.2
ASHRAE 90.1-2022 Table 7.8

4.2 DISTRIBUTION SYSTEMS
------------------------
Pipe insulation:
- Pipe diameter ≤40mm: R-0.5
- Pipe diameter >40mm: R-0.7
- Recirculation systems: R-0.9

Hot water recirculation:
- Automatic controls (time clock, occupancy sensor, temperature sensor)
- Maximum 66°C supply temperature (scalding prevention)

Low-flow fixtures (water conservation):
- Showerheads: ≤9.5 L/min (2.5 gpm)
- Lavatory faucets: ≤8.3 L/min (2.2 gpm) or 1.9 L per cycle
- Kitchen faucets: ≤8.3 L/min

Base: IECC 2024 Section C404.5

================================================================================
SECTION 5: ELECTRICAL SYSTEMS AND LIGHTING
================================================================================

5.1 LIGHTING POWER DENSITY (LPD)
--------------------------------
Maximum interior LPD (W/m²):

Building Area Method:
- Offices: 9.7 W/m²
- Schools (classrooms): 11.8 W/m²
- Retail: 14.0 W/m²
- Warehouse: 7.5 W/m²
- Healthcare (patient rooms): 10.8 W/m²
- Hotels (guest rooms): 11.8 W/m²
- Mosques: 12.9 W/m²
- Residential: 6.5 W/m²

Space-by-Space Method (more detailed):
- Office enclosed: 11.8 W/m²
- Office open: 9.7 W/m²
- Conference rooms: 13.4 W/m²
- Corridors: 5.4 W/m²
- Restrooms: 9.7 W/m²
- Storage: 8.6 W/m²

Exterior lighting:
- Building facades: 2.2 W/m² (horizontal), 5.4 W/m² (vertical)
- Parking areas: 1.1 W/m²
- Walkways <3m wide: 10.8 W/linear meter

Base: ASHRAE 90.1-2022 Tables 9.5.1, 9.6.1

5.2 LIGHTING CONTROLS
---------------------
Automatic shut-off (mandatory):
- Occupancy sensors: Enclosed offices, restrooms, storage, classrooms
- Time-switch control: Open offices, corridors (override ≤2 hours)
- Daylight-responsive controls: Within 4.5m of windows (primary sidelit zone)

Daylight harvesting:
- Continuous dimming or stepped control (min 2 steps)
- Photo sensors calibrated at commissioning
- Potential savings: 20-60% in perimeter zones

Manual controls:
- Individual control for ≤90 m² area, ≤10 luminaires
- Multi-level switching (minimum 50% off, 100% on)

Base: IECC 2024 Section C405.2
ASHRAE 90.1-2022 Section 9.4

5.3 LAMP AND LUMINAIRE EFFICACY
--------------------------------
Minimum efficacy requirements:
- Linear fluorescent (T8, T5): ≥90 lumens/watt
- LED downlights: ≥75 lumens/watt
- LED troffers: ≥90 lumens/watt
- High-bay LED: ≥120 lumens/watt

Prohibited lamps:
- Incandescent (except specialty applications)
- T12 fluorescent
- Mercury vapor

Base: IECC 2024 Table C405.3.2

5.4 TRANSFORMERS AND MOTORS
---------------------------
Transformers (≥10 kVA):
- Dry-type: NEMA TP-1 efficiency levels
- 15 kVA: ≥98.0%
- 150 kVA: ≥98.9%

Motors (≥0.75 kW):
- Premium efficiency: IE3 (IEC 60034-30-1)
- Super premium efficiency: IE4 (for critical loads)

Variable speed drives (VSD):
- Mandatory for motors >7.5 kW variable loads (pumps, fans)

Base: ASHRAE 90.1-2022 Tables 8.2, 10.8-1

================================================================================
SECTION 6: RENEWABLE ENERGY SYSTEMS
================================================================================

6.1 SOLAR PHOTOVOLTAIC (PV)
---------------------------
Pakistan solar resource:
- Karachi: 5.8 kWh/m²/day
- Lahore: 5.5 kWh/m²/day
- Quetta: 6.3 kWh/m²/day (highest in Pakistan)
- Islamabad: 5.2 kWh/m²/day

Mandatory solar PV:
- Government buildings >1,000 m²: ≥20% of electrical load
- Commercial buildings >5,000 m²: ≥15% of electrical load
- Hospitals, educational institutions: ≥10% of electrical load

System sizing:
- Peak sun hours (PSH): 5.5 hours/day (annual average Pakistan)
- Array DC capacity = Daily load (kWh) / (PSH × derate factor)
- Derate factor: 0.75-0.80 (soiling, temperature, mismatch losses)

Net metering:
- NEPRA regulations 2019 (revised 2023)
- Residential: Up to 1 MW capacity
- Commercial/industrial: Up to 1 MW per phase
- Bi-directional meter, credit rollover to next month
- DISCO connection agreements: LESCO, K-Electric, IESCO, FESCO, etc.

Installation standards:
- Roof-mounted: Tilt = Latitude ± 10° (Karachi 25°, Lahore 31°, Islamabad 33°)
- Azimuth: Due south (180°) for maximum yield
- Clearances: 100mm under modules (air cooling), 1m from roof edge
- Wind load per BNR seismic/wind provisions

Inverter specifications:
- Grid-tied: UL 1741, IEEE 1547 compliance
- Anti-islanding protection
- Efficiency ≥97% (transformerless), ≥95% (transformer-based)

Base: IgCC 2024 Section 406.3
Pakistan Alternative Energy Development Board (AEDB) guidelines 2023
NEPRA Net Metering Regulations 2019 (amended 2023)

6.2 SOLAR WATER HEATING
------------------------
Mandatory applications:
- Hospitals: 80% of hot water load
- Hotels >20 rooms: 60% of hot water load
- Hostels, guest houses: 50% of hot water load

Collector types:
- Flat-plate collectors: Efficiency 60-70% (most common Pakistan)
- Evacuated tube collectors: Efficiency 70-80% (better for cold climates)

Sizing:
- Per capita consumption: 40-50 L/day at 60°C
- Collector area: 1.5-2.0 m² per person (flat-plate)
- Storage tank: 50-75 L per m² collector area

Installation:
- Tilt angle: Latitude + 10° (winter optimization)
- Due south orientation
- Freeze protection (Northern Areas): Glycol mix or drainback systems

Base: Pakistan Green Building Code 2023 Section 6.2

6.3 WIND ENERGY
---------------
Coastal areas (Karachi, Gwadar):
- Average wind speed: 6-8 m/s at 50m height
- Small wind turbines: 5-50 kW (distributed generation)

Grid-scale wind farms:
- Jhimpir, Gharo: 1,320 MW operational (2023)
- AEDB wind corridor (Sindh): 9,700 km² area, 43,000 MW potential

Building-integrated wind:
- Vertical axis wind turbines (VAWT): Quieter, lower vibration
- Minimum 12 m/s sustained wind for economic viability
- Structural analysis required for roof-mounted installations

Base: AEDB Wind Power Policy 2006 (updated 2023)

SECTION 7: GREEN BUILDING CERTIFICATION
================================================================================

7.1 PAKISTAN GREEN BUILDING STANDARDS
--------------------------------------
Pakistan Green Building Council (PGBC) - established 2009

Certification systems:
1. LEED (U.S. Green Building Council)
   - LEED v4.1: Most common in Pakistan
   - Levels: Certified (40 pts), Silver (50), Gold (60), Platinum (80)
   - Categories: Location, Water, Energy, Materials, Indoor Environmental Quality

2. Pakistan Environmental Rating System (PERS)
   - Developed by PGBC 2018
   - Pakistan-specific climate, materials, regulations
   - Lower cost than LEED

3. EDGE (Excellence in Design for Greater Efficiencies)
   - IFC World Bank system
   - Focus: 20% savings in energy, water, embodied energy
   - Popular for affordable housing

Mandatory for:
- Federal government buildings >5,000 m²: Minimum LEED Silver
- Punjab government buildings: Minimum LEED Certified
- Sindh government buildings: Green building assessment required

Base: Pakistan Green Building Code 2023 Chapter 8
Punjab Green Building Code 2020

7.2 ENERGY PERFORMANCE TARGETS
-------------------------------
High-performance buildings:
- 30% better than ASHRAE 90.1-2022 baseline (LEED minimum)
- 50% better than baseline (LEED Platinum, net-zero ready)

Net-zero energy buildings (NZEB):
- Annual energy consumption ≤ on-site renewable energy production
- Site net-zero (most common) vs. source net-zero
- Examples: Pakistan Council of Scientific & Industrial Research (PCSIR) HQ Islamabad

Nearly-zero energy buildings (nZEB):
- EU standard: Primary energy ≤75 kWh/m²·year (residential)
- Very high efficiency envelope + renewable energy

Base: IgCC 2024 Section 602.1
ASHRAE 189.1-2020

SECTION 8: WATER EFFICIENCY AND CONSERVATION
================================================================================

8.1 INDOOR WATER USE
--------------------
Pakistan water crisis:
- Per capita availability: 1,017 m³/year (2023) - water-stressed (<1,700 m³)
- Projection 2025: <1,000 m³/year (water-scarce)
- Urban supply: 4-8 hours/day intermittent

Maximum fixture flow rates:
- Water closets (WC): 6.0 L per flush (dual-flush 3.0/6.0 L preferred)
- Urinals: 4.0 L per flush (0.5 L preferred) or waterless
- Showerheads: 9.5 L/min
- Lavatory faucets: 8.3 L/min or 1.9 L per cycle (metering faucets)
- Kitchen faucets: 8.3 L/min

Savings potential:
- Conventional WC: 13 L/flush → Dual-flush 4.5 L/flush = 65% reduction
- Conventional urinal: 7 L/flush → Waterless = 100% reduction

Base: IgCC 2024 Section 702.1
Pakistan Green Building Code 2023 Section 7.1

8.2 OUTDOOR WATER USE (LANDSCAPING)
------------------------------------
Climate-appropriate landscaping:
- Native species: Drought-tolerant, low maintenance
- Turf grass limits: ≤25% of landscaped area
- Examples: Bougainvillea, Neem, Guava, Date Palm, Jasmine

Irrigation:
- Drip irrigation: 90-95% efficiency (vs. 60-70% sprinkler)
- Smart controllers: Weather-based (ET), soil moisture sensors
- Mandatory for >1,000 m² landscaped area

Rainwater harvesting:
- Mandatory for plots >500 m² (Lahore, Islamabad regulations)
- Collection from roof, paved areas
- Storage: Minimum 10% of annual rainfall × catchment area
- Uses: Irrigation, toilet flushing, vehicle washing

Greywater reuse:
- Sources: Showers, laundry, hand-washing (NOT kitchen, WC)
- Treatment: Filtration, disinfection
- Uses: Toilet flushing, irrigation

Base: IgCC 2024 Section 703
Lahore Water & Sanitation Agency (WASA) Rainwater Harvesting Policy 2019

================================================================================
SECTION 9: WASTE MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

9.1 CONSTRUCTION WASTE
----------------------
Diversion targets:
- 50% of construction waste diverted from landfill (by weight)
- 75% target for high-performance buildings

Materials to salvage/recycle:
- Concrete, masonry: Crush for aggregate, base course
- Metals: Steel, aluminum, copper - high scrap value
- Wood: Reuse, mulch, biomass fuel
- Cardboard, paper: Recycling

Waste management plan:
- Designate areas for sorting on site
- Contracts with recyclers (steel mills, scrap dealers)
- Documentation: Weight tickets, invoices

Base: IgCC 2024 Section 505
LEED v4.1 MR Credit: Construction Waste Management

9.2 OPERATIONAL WASTE
---------------------
Recycling collection:
- Minimum 3 streams: Paper, plastic/metal/glass, organic
- Indoor bins for occupants (multi-stream)
- Centralized collection areas

Organic waste:
- Composting for food waste (hotels, restaurants, residential)
- Biogas digesters (large facilities): Methane for cooking/heating

E-waste:
- Separate collection for electronics, batteries
- Pakistan E-Waste Management Rules 2023 (draft)

Base: IgCC 2024 Section 806

SECTION 10: COMPLIANCE AND VERIFICATION
================================================================================

10.1 DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
-------------------------------
Construction documents:
- Energy compliance forms (IECC/ASHRAE)
- Equipment specifications, cut sheets
- Lighting plans with LPD calculations
- Mechanical load calculations

10.2 COMMISSIONING
------------------
Mandatory for buildings >5,000 m²:
- Commissioning authority (CxA): Third-party independent
- Owner's project requirements (OPR)
- Basis of design (BOD)
- Systems commissioned: HVAC, lighting controls, domestic hot water, renewable energy

Functional performance testing:
- Equipment verified at full-load, part-load conditions
- Control sequences verified
- TAB (testing, adjusting, balancing) reports

Base: ASHRAE Guideline 0-2019 (Commissioning Process)
IECC 2024 Section C408

10.3 BUILDING ENERGY AUDITS
---------------------------
Mandatory audits:
- Commercial buildings >5,000 m²: Every 5 years
- Industrial facilities >10,000 m²: Every 3 years

Energy audit levels (ASHRAE Standard 211):
- Level 1 (Walk-through): Benchmarking, preliminary assessment
- Level 2 (Energy survey): Detailed analysis, savings calculations
- Level 3 (Investment-grade): Engineering analysis for major retrofits

Pakistan ENERCON (Ministry of Energy):
- Energy audit regulations 2016
- Mandatory for industries >200 TOE (tons oil equivalent) annual consumption

Base: ASHRAE Standard 211-2018
Pakistan ENERCON Regulations
