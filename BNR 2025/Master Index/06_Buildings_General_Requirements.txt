SECTION 1: SCOPE AND APPLICABILITY
================================================================================

1.1 CODE SCOPE
[Enhancement: IBC 2024 Chapter 1]

A. BUILDINGS COVERED
   - All new buildings
   - Additions and alterations to existing buildings
   - Change of occupancy
   - Repairs and renovations
   - Temporary structures (>180 days)

B. EXEMPTIONS
   - Agricultural buildings in rural areas
   - Temporary structures <180 days
   - Single-story tool sheds <10 sq m
   - Minor repairs not affecting structure/safety
   - Historical buildings (with special provisions)

1.2 LEGAL BASIS
   - Federal/Provincial building control acts
   - Local government ordinances
   - Environmental protection requirements
   - Fire safety regulations

================================================================================
SECTION 2: DEFINITIONS AND TERMINOLOGY
================================================================================

2.1 KEY BUILDING TERMS
[Base: IBC 2024 Chapter 2]

BUILDING: Any structure used for shelter, occupancy, or enclosure

STORY: Space between floor and ceiling/roof above, >1.4m height

BASEMENT: Story with floor level below grade on ALL sides

MEZZANINE: Intermediate floor <1/3 of floor area it occupies

HEIGHT (Building): Vertical distance from grade to highest roof surface

AREA (Building): Maximum horizontal projected area of building

OCCUPANT LOAD: Number of persons for which building/space is designed

MEANS OF EGRESS: Continuous path of travel to public way

SECTION 3: OCCUPANCY CLASSIFICATION
================================================================================

3.1 OCCUPANCY GROUPS
[Source: IBC 2024 Chapter 3, Pakistan context adapted]

GROUP A - ASSEMBLY (Public gatherings)
   A-1: Theaters, cinemas (fixed seats), >300 persons
   A-2: Restaurants, cafes, banquet halls
   A-3: Mosques, churches, community halls, libraries
   A-4: Indoor sports, swimming pools (spectators)
   A-5: Outdoor sports stadiums, cricket grounds

   Pakistan Context:
   - Mosques (GROUP A-3): Special provisions for prayer halls
   - Separate facilities for men/women mandatory
   - Ablution areas (wudu khana) required
   - Shoe storage provisions

GROUP B - BUSINESS
   - Offices, banks, professional services
   - Educational above 12th grade
   - Outpatient clinics
   - Testing laboratories

GROUP C - INSTITUTIONAL (Supervised/unsupervised residents)
   C-1: Hospitals, nursing homes (24-hour medical care)
   C-2: Jails, prisons, detention facilities
   C-3: Orphanages, rehabilitation centers
   C-4: Day care centers (>5 children, <24 hours)

   Pakistan Context:
   - Dar-ul-Aman (women shelters): GROUP C-3
   - Madrassah boarding: GROUP C-3

GROUP D - RESIDENTIAL (Multi-family)
   D-1: Hotels, motels
   D-2: Apartment buildings, flats
   D-3: Dormitories, hostels

GROUP E - EDUCATIONAL
   - Schools (up to 12th grade)
   - Day care (>6 children)

GROUP F - FACTORY/INDUSTRIAL
   F-1: Moderate hazard (manufacturing, processing)
   F-2: Low hazard (packaging, assembling)

GROUP G - STORAGE (Non-hazardous)
   G-1: High-piled combustible storage
   G-2: Low hazard storage, warehouses

GROUP H - HAZARDOUS
   H-1: Explosives (detonation hazard)
   H-2: Deflagration hazard
   H-3: Physical hazard (gases, liquids)
   H-4: Health hazard (corrosives, toxics)
   H-5: Semiconductor fabrication

   Pakistan Context:
   - LPG storage facilities: GROUP H-2
   - Petrol pumps: GROUP H-3
   - Pharmaceutical manufacturing: GROUP H-4

GROUP I - UTILITY/MISCELLANEOUS
   - Carports, sheds
   - Agricultural buildings
   - Tanks, towers

GROUP R - RESIDENTIAL (Single/Two-Family)
   R-1: Detached single-family houses
   R-2: Two-family houses (duplexes)
   R-3: Row houses, townhouses

3.2 MIXED OCCUPANCIES
   - Accessory occupancy (≤10% of area, same floor)
   - Non-separated (most restrictive requirements apply)
   - Separated (fire-rated partitions between occupancies)

SECTION 4: CONSTRUCTION TYPES
================================================================================

4.1 CLASSIFICATION BY FIRE RESISTANCE
[Source: IBC 2024 Chapter 6]

TYPE I - FIRE-RESISTIVE (Most restrictive)
   I-A: 3-hour structure, non-combustible throughout
   I-B: 2-hour structure, non-combustible throughout

   Materials: Reinforced concrete, structural steel (protected)
   Use: High-rise buildings, large assembly, hospitals

TYPE II - NON-COMBUSTIBLE
   II-A: 1-hour fire-rated structure
   II-B: No fire rating required, but non-combustible

   Materials: Unprotected steel, concrete block, non-combustible
   Use: Warehouses, factories, low-rise commercial

TYPE III - ORDINARY CONSTRUCTION
   III-A: 1-hour exterior walls, non-combustible
   III-B: 1-hour exterior walls, combustible interior allowed

   Materials: Masonry/concrete exterior, wood interior
   Use: Traditional commercial buildings, mixed-use

TYPE IV - HEAVY TIMBER
   Large cross-section wood (>150mm)
   Slow-burning, non-combustible exterior walls

   Materials: Heavy timber columns/beams, masonry walls
   Use: Historic mills, barns (rarely used in Pakistan)

TYPE V - WOOD FRAME (Least restrictive)
   V-A: 1-hour fire-rated wood frame
   V-B: No fire rating, combustible throughout

   Materials: Wood framing, wood sheathing
   Use: Single-family houses, small buildings
   Pakistan Context: Limited use, brick masonry preferred

4.2 PAKISTAN COMMON CONSTRUCTION TYPES

BRICK MASONRY LOAD-BEARING (Predominant)
   - Clay brick walls (230mm typical)
   - RCC roof slabs
   - Classified as Type III or V depending on fire rating

CONFINED MASONRY (Seismic zones)
   - Masonry infill with RC columns/beams
   - Mandatory in Seismic Zones IV-V
   - Type III-A classification

REINFORCED CONCRETE FRAME
   - RC columns and beams
   - Brick infill or RC shear walls
   - Type I-B or II-A depending on protection

STEEL FRAME
   - Structural steel with masonry/concrete infill
   - Protected steel: Type I-B
   - Unprotected steel: Type II-B

SECTION 5: BUILDING HEIGHT AND AREA LIMITATIONS
================================================================================

5.1 ALLOWABLE HEIGHTS AND AREAS
[Source: IBC 2024 Chapter 5, Tables 503, 504, 506]

GENERAL PRINCIPLE:
   - More fire-resistant construction → Greater height/area allowed
   - Higher hazard occupancy → Lower height/area limits
   - Sprinklers provide height/area increases

5.2 HEIGHT LIMITATIONS BY TYPE

TYPE I-A: No height limit (unlimited stories)
TYPE I-B: 160 feet or 11 stories
TYPE II-A: 65 feet or 5 stories
TYPE II-B: 55 feet or 4 stories
TYPE III-A: 65 feet or 5 stories
TYPE III-B: 55 feet or 4 stories
TYPE IV: 65 feet or 5 stories
TYPE V-A: 50 feet or 3 stories
TYPE V-B: 40 feet or 2 stories

Increases Allowed:
   - Sprinklered: +1 story (except Type V-B)
   - Frontage increase: +area based on open space

5.3 AREA LIMITATIONS
   - Base tabular areas from IBC Table 506
   - Sprinkler increase: 3x tabular area (multi-story), 4x (single-story)
   - Frontage increase: Up to 3x based on open space percentage

5.4 HIGH-RISE BUILDINGS (>24 meters)
[Special Requirements - Pakistan context]

DEFINITION: Building height >24m above lowest fire vehicle access

MANDATORY FEATURES:
   - Type I construction required
   - Automatic sprinkler system throughout
   - Fire alarm with voice evacuation
   - Emergency power supply
   - Fire command center at ground floor
   - Pressurized stairwells (smoke control)
   - Fire fighter elevator
   - Standpipe system (wet)

Pakistan High-Rise Context:
   - Major cities: Karachi, Lahore, Islamabad, Rawalpindi
   - 2005 Kashmir Earthquake: Enhanced seismic requirements
   - Fire safety critical (Ali Enterprises fire 2012 lessons)

SECTION 6: SETBACKS AND OPEN SPACES
================================================================================

6.1 BUILDING SETBACKS
[Pakistan Planning Standards]

FRONT SETBACK:
   - Residential: 15-25 feet (based on plot size)
   - Commercial: 10-20 feet
   - Industrial: 25-40 feet

SIDE SETBACKS:
   - Residential: 5-10 feet (one side may be zero for row houses)
   - Commercial: 0-10 feet
   - Industrial: 10-20 feet

REAR SETBACK:
   - Residential: 10-15 feet
   - Commercial: 10-15 feet
   - Industrial: 15-25 feet

6.2 PLOT COVERAGE AND FAR

FLOOR AREA RATIO (FAR):
   - Low-density residential: 1.0-1.5
   - Medium-density residential: 2.0-2.5
   - High-density residential: 3.0-4.0
   - Commercial: 3.0-5.0
   - Mixed-use: 4.0-6.0

MAXIMUM PLOT COVERAGE:
   - Residential: 50-70%
   - Commercial: 75-85%
   - Industrial: 60-75%
   - Remainder: Open space, landscaping, parking

6.3 OPEN SPACE REQUIREMENTS
   - 25-50% of plot (depending on density)
   - Ground-level or podium gardens
   - Children's play areas (residential developments >100 units)

SECTION 7: ACCESSIBILITY REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

7.1 GENERAL ACCESSIBILITY
[Source: IBC Chapter 11, Pakistan context]

SCOPE: All new buildings, additions, alterations affecting public use areas

ACCESSIBLE ROUTE:
   - Width: 900mm minimum (1200mm preferred)
   - Slope: Maximum 1:12 (ramps)
   - Handrails: Both sides of ramps
   - Signage: Tactile and Braille

ENTRANCES:
   - At least 1 accessible entrance
   - Power-operated doors (public buildings)
   - Level threshold or ramp

ELEVATORS:
   - Required: Buildings >3 stories OR >1000 sq m per floor
   - Accessible car dimensions
   - Tactile and audio floor indicators

PARKING:
   - Accessible spaces: 2% of total (minimum 1)
   - Width: 3.6m (with 1.2m access aisle)
   - Van accessible: 4.8m width
   - Signage and pavement markings

TOILET FACILITIES:
   - At least 1 accessible toilet per floor
   - Ambulant accessible stalls
   - Grab bars and proper fixture heights
   - Clear floor space for wheelchair

7.2 PAKISTAN-SPECIFIC PROVISIONS
   - Accessible ablution facilities (mosques)
   - Tactile paving for visually impaired
   - Step-free access in educational buildings
   - Accessible prayer spaces (men and women)

SECTION 8: CLIMATE AND REGIONAL PROVISIONS
================================================================================

8.1 PAKISTAN CLIMATE ZONES
[Pakistan Building Code 2021]

ZONE 1 - HOT-DRY (Sindh, South Punjab, Baluchistan plains)
   - Summer: 40-50°C
   - Low humidity
   - High solar radiation
   - Design: Thick walls, small windows, shading, light colors

ZONE 2 - WARM-HUMID (Coastal Karachi)
   - High humidity year-round
   - Moderate temperatures
   - Salt air corrosion
   - Design: Cross-ventilation, large openings, corrosion protection

ZONE 3 - COMPOSITE (Punjab, Islamabad)
   - Hot summers, cold winters
   - Variable conditions
   - Design: Balanced approach, insulation, shading

ZONE 4 - TEMPERATE (Murree, hill stations)
   - Moderate temperatures
   - Rainfall significant
   - Design: Pitched roofs, drainage, moderate insulation

ZONE 5 - COLD (Gilgit-Baltistan, Northern areas)
   - Severe winters (-20°C)
   - Heavy snowfall
   - Design: High insulation, snow load, heating systems

8.2 CLIMATE-RESPONSIVE DESIGN
   - Orientation: North-south preferred (hot climates)
   - Shading devices: Overhangs, louvers, screens
   - Natural ventilation: Cross-ventilation, stack effect
   - Thermal mass: High (hot-dry), Low (warm-humid)
   - Roof insulation: Mandatory all zones

SECTION 9: PARKING REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

9.1 MINIMUM PARKING SPACES
[Pakistan Standards]

RESIDENTIAL:
   - Single-family: 2 spaces per unit
   - Apartments: 1 space per unit (<100 sq m), 2 spaces (>100 sq m)

COMMERCIAL:
   - Offices: 1 space per 30 sq m
   - Retail: 1 space per 20 sq m
   - Restaurants: 1 space per 5 seats

INSTITUTIONAL:
   - Schools: 1 space per 10 students (high school+)
   - Hospitals: 1 space per 3 beds + 1 per 2 staff
   - Mosques: 1 space per 10 worshippers

INDUSTRIAL:
   - Factories: 1 space per 3 workers (maximum shift)

9.2 PARKING DESIGN STANDARDS
   - Car space: 2.4m x 5.0m minimum
   - Aisle width: 6m (two-way), 3.5m (one-way)
   - Motorcycle parking: Separate area, 1 space per 5 car spaces
   - Bicycle parking: 1 space per 10 car spaces
   - Accessible parking: 2% of total (minimum 1)

================================================================================
SECTION 10: SPECIAL OCCUPANCY REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

10.1 MOSQUES AND PRAYER HALLS
[Pakistan-specific]

LAYOUT:
   - Main prayer hall (men): Minimum ceiling height 4.5m
   - Women's prayer area: Separate entrance and facilities
   - Ablution area (wudu khana): 1 tap per 50 worshippers
   - Toilets: Separate for men/women
   - Shoe storage: Adequate provisions

CAPACITY:
   - Design: 1.2 sq m per worshipper (including circulation)
   - Exits: Minimum 2, width based on occupant load
   - Accessible facilities mandatory

MINARETS:
   - Structural design for wind and seismic loads
   - Lightning protection
   - Public address system for Azaan

10.2 EDUCATIONAL BUILDINGS
   - Classroom: 1.2 sq m per student minimum
   - Ceiling height: 3m minimum
   - Natural light: 20% of floor area
   - Separate play areas (elementary schools)
   - Library and laboratory requirements
   - Accessible throughout

10.3 HEALTHCARE FACILITIES
   - Infection control design
   - Emergency generator (100% capacity)
   - Medical gas systems
   - Isolation rooms (negative pressure)
   - Separate entry for emergency
