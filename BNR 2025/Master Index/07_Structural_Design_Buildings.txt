SECTION 1: GENERAL STRUCTURAL REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

1.1 DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

LIMIT STATE DESIGN (LSD) / LOAD AND RESISTANCE FACTOR DESIGN (LRFD)
   - Ultimate Limit State: Strength and stability
   - Serviceability Limit State: Deflection, cracking, vibration

DESIGN PRINCIPLES:
   - Factored loads > Factored resistance
   - Load factors: 1.2-1.6 depending on load type
   - Resistance factors: 0.65-0.90 depending on material

1.2 CODES AND STANDARDS INTEGRATION

CONCRETE DESIGN:
   - IS 456:2000 (Plain and Reinforced Concrete)
   - IRC 112:2020 (Modern concrete provisions)
   - ACI 318 (Reference for advanced topics)

STEEL DESIGN:
   - IS 800:2007 (Steel Structures - Limit State Method)
   - AISC 360 (USA - Reference)

MASONRY DESIGN:
   - IS 1905:1987 (Structural use of unreinforced masonry)
   - IS 13920 (Ductile detailing in seismic zones)

SEISMIC DESIGN:
   - IS 1893:2016 (Earthquake Resistant Design - Parts 1-5)
   - Building Code of Pakistan Seismic Provisions 2007
   - ASCE 7 Chapter 12 (Reference)

FOUNDATIONS:
   - IS 1904:1986, IS 2911 (Piles), IS 6403 (Footings)

SECTION 2: LOADS ON BUILDINGS
================================================================================

2.1 DEAD LOADS (DL)
[Source: IS 875 Part 1, ASCE 7 Chapter 3]

MATERIAL UNIT WEIGHTS:
   - Reinforced concrete: 25 kN/m³
   - Plain concrete: 24 kN/m³
   - Brick masonry: 19-20 kN/m³
   - Stone masonry: 20-26 kN/m³
   - Structural steel: 77 kN/m³

TYPICAL FLOOR FINISHES:
   - Tiles (20mm): 0.45 kN/m²
   - Marble/granite: 0.65 kN/m²
   - False ceiling: 0.25 kN/m²
   - Plaster (12mm): 0.30 kN/m²
   - Waterproofing: 0.15 kN/m²

PARTITIONS:
   - Light partitions: 1.0 kN/m²
   - 115mm brick wall: 2.75 kN/m²
   - 230mm brick wall: 5.50 kN/m²

2.2 LIVE LOADS (LL)
[Source: IS 875 Part 2, IBC Table 1607.1]

RESIDENTIAL:
   - Dwelling rooms: 2.0 kN/m²
   - Corridors, stairs: 3.0 kN/m²
   - Balconies: 3.0 kN/m²

ASSEMBLY:
   - Fixed seating (mosques, theaters): 4.0 kN/m²
   - Movable seating: 5.0 kN/m²
   - Corridors/aisles: 5.0 kN/m²

OFFICES:
   - Office rooms: 2.5 kN/m²
   - Lobbies, corridors: 4.0 kN/m²

EDUCATIONAL:
   - Classrooms: 3.0 kN/m²
   - Corridors: 4.0 kN/m²

COMMERCIAL:
   - Retail: 4.0 kN/m²
   - Storage warehouses: 6.0-10.0 kN/m² (based on height)

PARKING:
   - Cars: 2.5 kN/m² (or concentrated load 20 kN/wheel)
   - Trucks: Design for vehicle loads

ROOFS:
   - Accessible (gardens): 1.5 kN/m² minimum
   - Inaccessible: 0.75 kN/m² (maintenance)

LIVE LOAD REDUCTION:
   - Allowed for tributary areas >20 m²
   - Reduction factor: Based on tributary area and occupancy
   - Maximum reduction: 50% (residential), 40% (assembly)

2.3 WIND LOADS (WL)
[Source: IS 875 Part 3, ASCE 7 Chapter 27]

BASIC WIND SPEED (Pakistan):
   - Karachi (coastal): 180 km/h (50 m/s)
   - Lahore, Islamabad: 160 km/h (44 m/s)
   - Interior cities: 140 km/h (39 m/s)
   - Coastal areas: Cyclone-prone, higher speeds

WIND PRESSURE CALCULATION:
   pz = 0.6 Vz² (N/m²) where Vz = design wind speed at height z

FACTORS:
   - Terrain category (1-4): Open/rough terrain
   - Height factor k2
   - Topography factor k3
   - Importance factor: 1.0-1.15
   - Directionality: 0.85-1.0

DESIGN PROVISIONS:
   - Main Wind Force Resisting System (MWFRS)
   - Components and Cladding (C&C)
   - Enclosed buildings vs. Open structures
   - Pressure and suction on surfaces

2.4 SEISMIC LOADS (EQ)
[Source: IS 1893:2016 Part 1, Pakistan Seismic Code 2007]

PAKISTAN SEISMIC ZONES:
   - Zone II (Low): Karachi, South Sindh (Z = 0.10)
   - Zone III (Moderate): Central Punjab, North Sindh (Z = 0.16)
   - Zone IV (High): Northern Punjab, KPK (Z = 0.24)
   - Zone V (Very High): Kashmir, Northern Areas (Z = 0.36)

SEISMIC ZONE FACTOR (Z):
   Represents Peak Ground Acceleration (PGA) as fraction of g

IMPORTANCE FACTOR (I):
   - Ordinary buildings: 1.0
   - Important buildings (hospitals, fire stations): 1.5
   - Critical facilities: 1.75

RESPONSE REDUCTION FACTOR (R):
   - Ordinary RC moment frame: 3.0
   - Special RC moment frame (ductile): 5.0
   - RC shear walls: 4.0
   - Dual system (frame + shear wall): 5.0
   - Steel moment frame: 5.0
   - Confined masonry: 2.5
   - Unreinforced masonry: PROHIBITED in Zones IV-V

BASE SHEAR CALCULATION:
   Vb = (Z I Sa/g) / (R) × W

Where:
   Sa/g = Spectral acceleration (from response spectrum)
   W = Seismic weight of building

SEISMIC DESIGN REQUIREMENTS:
   - Ductile detailing (IS 13920) mandatory in Zones III-V
   - Soft story prohibited
   - Strong column - weak beam design
   - Confining reinforcement in columns/beams
   - Special boundary elements in shear walls

2005 KASHMIR EARTHQUAKE LESSONS:
   - Magnitude 7.6, 73,000 deaths
   - Unreinforced masonry (URM) collapsed extensively
   - Confined masonry performed well
   - Non-engineered construction major vulnerability

2.5 SNOW LOADS (SL)
[Source: ASCE 7 Chapter 7 - Pakistan mountain areas]

APPLICABILITY: Gilgit-Baltistan, Kashmir, high-altitude areas

GROUND SNOW LOAD:
   - Murree: 1.5 kN/m²
   - Gilgit: 2.0 kN/m²
   - Skardu: 2.5 kN/m²
   - Higher elevations: Site-specific study

ROOF SNOW LOAD:
   pf = 0.7 Ce Ct I pg

Where:
   Ce = Exposure factor (0.8-1.2)
   Ct = Thermal factor (1.0-1.2)
   I = Importance factor (0.8-1.2)
   pg = Ground snow load

DRIFT LOADS: Consider for multi-level roofs

2.6 RAIN/PONDING LOADS
   - 75mm depth minimum (flat roofs)
   - Check for ponding instability
   - Adequate drainage (roof drains + scuppers)

2.7 LOAD COMBINATIONS
[Source: IS 875 Part 5, ASCE 7 Chapter 2]

ULTIMATE LIMIT STATE (STRENGTH):
   1. 1.5 DL
   2. 1.2 DL + 1.6 LL
   3. 1.2 DL + 1.6 WL + 0.5 LL
   4. 1.2 DL + 1.0 EQ ± 0.3 LL
   5. 0.9 DL ± 1.6 WL (uplift/overturning)

SERVICEABILITY LIMIT STATE:
   1. DL + LL
   2. DL + 0.8 LL
   3. DL + 0.8 WL

SECTION 3: REINFORCED CONCRETE DESIGN
================================================================================

3.1 MATERIALS
[Source: IS 456:2000, IRC 112:2020]

CONCRETE GRADES:
   - Ordinary structures: M20, M25
   - Multi-story buildings: M25, M30
   - High-rise buildings: M30, M35, M40
   - Prestressed concrete: M35 minimum

   Notation: M20 = 20 MPa characteristic cube strength at 28 days

REINFORCEMENT:
   - Fe 415: 415 MPa yield strength (most common)
   - Fe 500: 500 MPa (high-strength)
   - Fe 550: 550 MPa (special applications)

COVER TO REINFORCEMENT (Pakistan climate):
   - Footings: 50mm (in contact with soil), 40mm (above ground)
   - Columns: 40mm (interior), 50mm (exterior)
   - Beams: 25mm (interior), 30mm (exterior) + bar diameter
   - Slabs: 20mm (interior), 30mm (exterior)
   - Coastal areas (Karachi): +10mm (corrosion protection)

3.2 FLEXURAL DESIGN (BEAMS AND SLABS)

ASSUMPTIONS:
   - Plane sections remain plane (linear strain)
   - Perfect bond between concrete and steel
   - Concrete tension neglected
   - Stress-strain curves per IS 456 Cl. 38

DESIGN STEPS:
   1. Determine factored moment: Mu
   2. Calculate effective depth: d
   3. Check for singly or doubly reinforced
   4. Calculate steel area: Ast
   5. Check for minimum and maximum steel
   6. Check deflection limits

REINFORCEMENT LIMITS:
   - Minimum steel: 0.85/fy × bd (beams), 0.12% (slabs)
   - Maximum steel: 0.04 bD (4%)
   - Redistribution: ±15% (ductile sections), ±10% (limited ductility)

DEFLECTION LIMITS:
   - Span/depth ratios (IS 456 Fig. 4)
   - Simply supported: span/250
   - Cantilever: span/125
   - Final deflection: span/250 (appearance)

3.3 SHEAR DESIGN

NOMINAL SHEAR STRESS:
   τv = Vu / (bd)

SHEAR STRENGTH OF CONCRETE (τc):
   - Depends on grade of concrete and % steel
   - IS 456 Table 19

SHEAR REINFORCEMENT:
   - When τv < τc/2: No shear reinforcement
   - When τc/2 < τv < τc: Minimum stirrups
   - When τv > τc: Design stirrups

MINIMUM STIRRUPS:
   - Asv/sv ≥ 0.4/0.87fy

3.4 COLUMN DESIGN

TYPES:
   - Short columns: Axial load dominant
   - Slender columns: Bending + axial (consider slenderness)

EFFECTIVE LENGTH:
   - Based on end conditions (IS 456 Cl. 25.2)
   - Braced frame: 0.65L to 1.0L
   - Unbraced frame: 1.2L to 2.0L

REINFORCEMENT:
   - Minimum: 0.8% (gross area)
   - Maximum: 4% (6% at laps)
   - Minimum 4 bars (rectangular), 6 bars (circular)
   - Bar diameter: 12mm minimum

TIES/STIRRUPS:
   - Diameter: 6mm minimum (≤12mm main bars), 8mm (>12mm)
   - Spacing: Least of 16φ, 300mm, least lateral dimension
   - Lap splices: Not at same location

SEISMIC DETAILING (IS 13920):
   - Closely spaced ties at top and bottom
   - Spacing: 100mm at ends (2d length)
   - 135° hooks

3.5 SHEAR WALLS

TYPES:
   - Cantilever walls
   - Coupled shear walls
   - Core walls

REINFORCEMENT:
   - Vertical: 0.0025 of gross area
   - Horizontal: 0.0025 of gross area
   - Maximum spacing: 300mm or 3× wall thickness

BOUNDARY ELEMENTS:
   - Required at edges of walls
   - When extreme fiber compression >0.2fc'
   - Confining reinforcement per IS 13920

SECTION 4: STRUCTURAL STEEL DESIGN
================================================================================

4.1 MATERIALS AND SECTIONS
[Source: IS 800:2007]

STEEL GRADES:
   - Fe 250: 250 MPa yield (mild steel, less common)
   - Fe 410: 410 MPa yield (common structural steel)
   - Fe 540: 540 MPa yield (high-strength)

SECTIONS:
   - I-beams: ISMB, ISJB, ISLB (Indian Standards)
   - Channels: ISMC, ISJC
   - Angles: ISA (equal/unequal leg)
   - Hollow sections: CHS (circular), RHS (rectangular), SHS (square)
   - Built-up sections: Plate girders, box columns

4.2 TENSION MEMBERS

DESIGN STRENGTH:
   Td = Ag × fy / γm0  (gross section yielding)
   Td = 0.9 An × fu / γm1  (net section rupture)

Where:
   γm0 = 1.10, γm1 = 1.25 (partial safety factors)
   An = Net area (accounting for bolt holes)

SLENDERNESS LIMIT: l/r < 400 (preferably < 300)

4.3 COMPRESSION MEMBERS (COLUMNS)

DESIGN COMPRESSIVE STRENGTH:
   Pd = Ae × fcd

Where:
   fcd = (χ × fy) / γm0
   χ = Stress reduction factor (buckling curves)
   Ae = Effective sectional area

SLENDERNESS RATIO:
   λ = KL/r (where K = effective length factor)

EFFECTIVE LENGTH:
   - Pinned-pinned: 1.0 L
   - Fixed-fixed: 0.5 L
   - Fixed-pinned: 0.7 L
   - Fixed-free: 2.0 L

4.4 FLEXURAL MEMBERS (BEAMS)

PLASTIC MOMENT CAPACITY:
   Md = βb Zp × fy / γm0

Where:
   βb = 1.0 (plastic/compact sections)
   Zp = Plastic section modulus

SHEAR CAPACITY:
   Vd = Vn / γm0
   Vn = Av × fy / √3

LATERAL-TORSIONAL BUCKLING:
   - Unbraced length limits
   - Bracing requirements
   - Reduced moment capacity for long spans

4.5 CONNECTIONS

BOLTED CONNECTIONS:
   - Bolt grades: 4.6, 8.8, 10.9
   - Bearing type vs. Friction type (HSFG)
   - Minimum edge distance: 1.5d
   - Minimum pitch: 2.5d

WELDED CONNECTIONS:
   - Fillet welds (most common)
   - Butt welds (full penetration)
   - Weld throat thickness design
   - Welding electrodes: E41, E51

4.6 SEISMIC DETAILING
   - Special moment frames (ductile connections)
   - Reduced beam sections (dog-bone)
   - Column panel zone checks
   - Strong column - weak beam

SECTION 5: MASONRY STRUCTURES
================================================================================

5.1 TYPES OF MASONRY
[Pakistan Context]

UNREINFORCED MASONRY (URM):
   - Clay brick walls (most common)
   - Cement mortar
   - Load-bearing walls
   - PROHIBITED in Seismic Zones IV-V for >2 stories

CONFINED MASONRY:
   - Masonry constructed first
   - RC confining elements (columns/beams) cast after
   - Mandatory in Seismic Zones IV-V
   - Excellent seismic performance (2005 earthquake)

REINFORCED MASONRY:
   - Vertical/horizontal reinforcement in grouted cells
   - Hollow concrete blocks
   - Less common in Pakistan

5.2 MATERIALS

BRICKS:
   - Class A (>10 MPa): Structural
   - Class B (7-10 MPa): General construction
   - Dimensions: 230×115×75mm (typical)

MORTAR:
   - Cement:Sand ratios: 1:3, 1:4, 1:5, 1:6
   - 1:4 most common for load-bearing walls

COMPRESSIVE STRENGTH:
   - Brick masonry (1:4 mortar): 3.5-7.5 MPa (IS 1905 Table 8)

5.3 DESIGN OF LOAD-BEARING WALLS

EFFECTIVE HEIGHT:
   - hef = β × h (clear height)
   - β depends on support conditions (0.75-2.0)

EFFECTIVE THICKNESS:
   - tef = thickness (solid wall)
   - tef = 2/3 × thickness (cavity wall)

SLENDERNESS RATIO:
   - SR = hef / tef
   - Maximum: 27 (unreinforced), 30 (reinforced)

STRESS REDUCTION FACTOR:
   - Based on slenderness ratio
   - IS 1905 Table 5

COMPRESSIVE STRENGTH:
   P = σ × A × SR factor

5.4 SEISMIC PROVISIONS (CONFINED MASONRY)

RC CONFINING ELEMENTS:
   - Columns: 230×230mm minimum
   - Beams (tie-beams): At all floor levels
   - Reinforcement: 4-12mm bars + 6mm stirrups @ 150mm
   - Seismic band at lintel level: 150mm depth

WALL DIMENSIONS:
   - Minimum thickness: 230mm
   - Maximum unsupported length: 5m (between columns)
   - Maximum unsupported height: 3.5m (between beams)

OPENINGS:
   - Lintel beams over all openings
   - Vertical bars at jambs

SECTION 6: FOUNDATIONS
================================================================================

6.1 GEOTECHNICAL INVESTIGATION
[Source: IS 1892, IS 6403]

SOIL EXPLORATION:
   - Minimum 1 borehole per 300 sq m (or per foundation)
   - Depth: 1.5× foundation width or to rock/hard stratum
   - SPT testing every 1.5m
   - Laboratory tests: Atterberg limits, grain size, shear parameters

ALLOWABLE BEARING CAPACITY:
   - From SPT: qa (kN/m²) ≈ 10× N (for sandy soils)
   - From plate load test
   - From soil parameters (Terzaghi equations)
   - Typical Pakistan soils: 100-250 kN/m² (clayey), 150-300 kN/m² (sandy)

6.2 SHALLOW FOUNDATIONS

TYPES:
   - Isolated footings (columns)
   - Combined footings (2 columns)
   - Strap footings
   - Mat/raft foundations

DEPTH OF FOUNDATION:
   - Minimum: 1.0m below ground level
   - Below seasonal moisture variation
   - Below scour depth (near water bodies)

DESIGN:
   - Ultimate bearing capacity check
   - Settlement check (≤75mm total, ≤50mm differential)
   - Structural design (bending, shear, punching shear)

6.3 DEEP FOUNDATIONS (PILES)

TYPES:
   - Bored cast-in-place piles (most common Pakistan)
   - Driven precast concrete piles
   - Steel H-piles

CAPACITY:
   - End-bearing piles (rock/dense strata)
   - Friction piles (soft/medium soils)
   - Static analysis (Meyerhof, Vesic methods)
   - Dynamic analysis (driven piles)

LOAD TESTING:
   - Initial test: 1% of piles, minimum 2 (to 2× design load)
   - Routine test: 2% of piles (to 1.5× design load)

PILE CAP DESIGN:
   - Transfer column loads to pile group
   - Structural design for bending and shear
