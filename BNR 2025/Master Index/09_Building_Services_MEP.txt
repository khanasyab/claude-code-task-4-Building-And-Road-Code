SECTION 1: MECHANICAL SYSTEMS (HVAC)
================================================================================

1.1 SCOPE AND CODES

SCOPE:
- Heating, ventilation, and air conditioning
- Ventilation for indoor air quality
- Thermal comfort
- Energy efficiency

CODES:
- IMC 2024 (International Mechanical Code)
- ASHRAE 62.1 (Ventilation for Acceptable Indoor Air Quality)
- ASHRAE 55 (Thermal Environmental Conditions for Human Occupancy)

1.2 VENTILATION REQUIREMENTS (IMC 2024 Chapter 4, ASHRAE 62.1)

NATURAL VENTILATION:
- Openable area: Minimum 5% of floor area
- Operable windows/doors to outdoors
- Adequate for low-occupancy residential, small offices

MECHANICAL VENTILATION REQUIRED:
- When natural ventilation inadequate
- Densely occupied spaces
- Spaces without operable windows
- Air-conditioned buildings

OUTDOOR AIR RATES (ASHRAE 62.1):
- Offices: 10 liters/second per person
- Classrooms: 8 liters/second per person
- Retail: 7.5 liters/second per person
- Auditoriums/assembly: 5 liters/second per person
- Gymnasiums: 10 liters/second per person
- Parking garages: 7.5 liters/second per sq m

AIR CHANGE RATES (Alternative Method):
- Offices: 6-8 air changes per hour (ACH)
- Classrooms: 6-8 ACH
- Auditoriums: 10-15 ACH
- Kitchens (commercial): 20-30 ACH
- Toilets: 10-15 ACH
- Parking garages: 6 ACH minimum (or CO monitoring)

1.3 HVAC SYSTEM TYPES

SPLIT AIR CONDITIONING:
- Most common in Pakistan (residential, small commercial)
- Indoor unit (evaporator) + outdoor unit (condenser/compressor)
- Cooling only or heat pump (heating + cooling)
- Capacity: 1-5 tons (3.5-17.5 kW)
- Refrigerant: R-410A, R-32 (replacing R-22 ozone-depleting)

VARIABLE REFRIGERANT FLOW (VRF):
- One outdoor unit, multiple indoor units (up to 64)
- Individual zone control
- Simultaneous heating and cooling (heat recovery)
- High efficiency (part-load operation)
- Medium to large commercial buildings
- Higher initial cost, lower operating cost

CENTRAL CHILLED WATER SYSTEM:
- Chiller plant (produces chilled water 5-7°C)
- Distribution (pumps, pipes)
- Air handling units (AHUs) or fan coil units (FCUs)
- Suitable: Large buildings, high-rise, campuses
- Chiller types: Centrifugal, screw, absorption

PACKAGED AIR CONDITIONING:
- Self-contained unit (all components in one or two packages)
- Rooftop units common (USA), less in Pakistan
- Simple installation
- Commercial buildings

DIRECT EXPANSION (DX) UNITS:
- Package AC, split AC (refrigerant directly cools air)
- Simpler than chilled water
- Shorter piping runs

1.4 HVAC DESIGN PARAMETERS (ASHRAE 55, Pakistan Context)

INDOOR DESIGN CONDITIONS:
- Summer cooling: 24°C ± 2°C, 50% ± 10% relative humidity
- Winter heating: 20-22°C (where heating required - Northern Pakistan)
- Air velocity: <0.25 m/s (comfort)

OUTDOOR DESIGN CONDITIONS (Pakistan Major Cities):
- Karachi: 38°C dry bulb (summer), 26°C wet bulb
- Lahore: 45°C dry bulb, 28°C wet bulb
- Islamabad: 42°C dry bulb, 26°C wet bulb
- Multan: 48°C dry bulb (extreme heat), 30°C wet bulb
- Gilgit: -10°C (winter heating design)

HEAT GAIN CALCULATION:
- Solar radiation through windows (major load)
- Conduction through walls/roof
- Infiltration/ventilation
- Occupants (75-100W per person sensible, 50W latent)
- Lighting (10-20 W/sq m offices)
- Equipment (computers, machinery)

COOLING LOAD ESTIMATE (Simplified):
- Offices: 150-200 W/sq m (Pakistan hot climate)
- Retail: 200-250 W/sq m
- Restaurants: 250-350 W/sq m
- Residential: 100-150 W/sq m

1.5 DUCTWORK (IMC 2024 Chapter 6)

DUCT MATERIALS:
- Galvanized steel (most common)
- Aluminum (corrosive environments, coastal)
- Fiberglass duct board (insulated, sound attenuation)
- Flexible duct (short connections)

DUCT SIZING:
- Velocity method: 5-8 m/s (supply), 4-6 m/s (return)
- Friction rate: 1 Pa/m (low velocity systems)
- Equal friction method: Maintain constant friction rate

INSULATION:
- External insulation: 25-50mm fiberglass (prevents condensation, heat gain)
- Internal lining: Acoustic treatment (if required)

FIRE DAMPERS:
- In ducts penetrating fire-rated walls/floors
- Fusible link (73°C) closes damper
- Prevents fire spread through ductwork

1.6 EQUIPMENT EFFICIENCY STANDARDS

AIR CONDITIONERS (Pakistan Energy Efficiency Standards):
- Split AC: Minimum EER 10.0 (3.5 Star rating)
  - EER = Cooling capacity (BTU/h) / Power input (Watts)
  - Higher EER = more efficient
- Inverter AC: 15-20% more efficient (variable speed compressor)
- 5 Star: EER >11.5 (highly efficient)

CHILLERS:
- Centrifugal: COP 5.5-6.5 (water-cooled)
- Screw: COP 4.5-5.5
- Absorption (gas-fired): COP 1.2-1.5 (useful where waste heat available)
  - COP = Cooling output / Energy input

FANS:
- High-efficiency motors (IE3 class minimum)
- Variable frequency drives (VFDs) for part-load efficiency

1.7 REFRIGERATION SAFETY (IMC 2024 Chapter 11)

REFRIGERANT TYPES:
- Group A1 (Low toxicity, non-flammable): R-410A, R-134a, R-32
- Group A2L (Low toxicity, mildly flammable): R-32, R-454B (new low-GWP)
- Group A3 (Low toxicity, flammable): R-290 (propane), R-600a (isobutane)

REFRIGERANT LIMITS (Occupied Spaces):
- Based on toxicity and room volume
- Mechanical ventilation required (if limits exceeded)
- Leak detection (flammable refrigerants)

PHASEOUT:
- R-22 (HCFC) phased out (ozone depletion)
- R-410A being phased down (high GWP - global warming potential)
- Transition to low-GWP refrigerants (R-32, R-454B, R-290)

SECTION 2: PLUMBING SYSTEMS
================================================================================

2.1 SCOPE AND CODES

SCOPE:
- Water supply (hot and cold)
- Sanitary drainage and venting
- Storm drainage
- Plumbing fixtures

CODES:
- IPC 2024 (International Plumbing Code)
- ASME A112 (Plumbing Fixture Standards)
- Pakistan Plumbing Regulations (provincial)

2.2 WATER SUPPLY SYSTEM

SOURCES:
- Municipal water supply (WASA, PHED, etc.)
- Tube wells/bore wells (private)
- Tanker supply (where mains unavailable)

WATER PRESSURE:
- Minimum at fixtures: 140 kPa (20 psi)
- Maximum: 550 kPa (80 psi) - pressure reducing valve if exceeded
- Static pressure (Pakistan municipal): 200-400 kPa (varies by location and time)

WATER STORAGE:
- Underground tank (sump): 1-2 days storage
  - Capacity: 150-200 liters/person/day (residential)
  - Offices: 50 liters/person/day
  - Hotels: 200 liters/person/day
- Overhead tank: 1 day storage (gravity supply)
- Pumping: Sump to overhead (automatic control)

TANK REQUIREMENTS:
- Watertight construction (RCC or plastic)
- Covered (prevent contamination)
- Access manhole with locked cover
- Overflow pipe (screened end)
- Drain valve at bottom
- Cleaning: Semi-annual

2.3 PIPE SIZING (FIXTURE UNIT METHOD - IPC 2024)

FIXTURE UNITS (FU):
- Water closet (flush tank): 3 FU
- Urinal (flush valve): 5 FU
- Lavatory (wash basin): 1 FU
- Shower: 2 FU
- Kitchen sink: 1.5 FU
- Washing machine: 3 FU

PIPE SIZING:
- Total fixture units → Table lookup → Pipe diameter
- Example: 20 FU → 32mm (1.25") pipe
- Velocity: <2.5 m/s (minimize noise, erosion)

PIPE MATERIALS (SUPPLY):
- Galvanized Iron (GI): Traditional, rusting issues
- UPVC: Non-corrosive, smooth (lower friction), NSF-certified for potable
- CPVC: Hot water (rated to 80°C)
- PPR (Polypropylene Random): Modern, hot/cold, fusion welding
- Copper: Excellent but expensive (rare in Pakistan)

2.4 HOT WATER SYSTEM

HEATING METHODS:
- Solar water heaters (most common Pakistan)
  - Flat plate collectors or evacuated tube
  - 100-300 liters capacity (residential)
  - Backup electric heating element
  - Payback: 2-4 years

- Electric geysers (instant or storage)
  - Capacity: 15-100 liters (storage)
  - Power: 1.5-3 kW
  - Insulated tank (reduce standby loss)

- Gas water heaters (instant)
  - Natural gas or LPG
  - Tankless (on-demand)
  - Venting required

TEMPERATURE:
- Storage: 60°C (prevent Legionella bacteria)
- Delivery at fixture: 48-52°C (tempered to prevent scalding)
- Tempering valve (mix hot+cold to safe temperature)

CIRCULATION:
- Dead legs: Keep <6m (minimize wait time for hot water)
- Recirculation pump (large buildings, hotels) - maintains hot water in pipes

2.5 SANITARY DRAINAGE SYSTEM (IPC 2024 Chapter 7)

FIXTURE UNITS (DRAINAGE):
- Water closet: 4 FU
- Urinal: 4 FU
- Lavatory: 1 FU
- Shower: 2 FU
- Kitchen sink: 2 FU

PIPE SIZING:
- Horizontal drains: Minimum slope 1:50 (2%) for 100mm pipe, 1:100 (1%) for >100mm
- Vertical stacks: No slope (gravity flow)
- Example: 20 FU → 75mm (3") drain

PIPE MATERIALS (DRAINAGE):
- Cast iron (CI): Durable, quiet, expensive (rare now)
- UPVC: Most common (lightweight, economical, non-corrosive)
- HDPE: Modern alternative

TRAPS:
- P-trap: Standard (horizontal outlet)
- S-trap: Vertical outlet (less common, can siphon)
- Water seal: 50-100mm depth
- Purpose: Prevent sewer gas entry

CLEANOUTS:
- Access points for drain cleaning
- Location: Base of stacks, change of direction, every 15m horizontal
- Size: Same as drain pipe

2.6 VENTING SYSTEM (IPC 2024 Chapter 9)

PURPOSE:
- Allow air into drainage system
- Prevent trap siphonage (maintain water seal)
- Prevent pressure buildup (allow gases to escape)

VENT TYPES:
- Individual vent: Each fixture separately vented to stack or roof
- Common vent: 2 fixtures share one vent
- Wet vent: Drain pipe also serves as vent
- Stack vent: Extension of soil/waste stack above highest fixture

VENT TERMINAL:
- Extend through roof
- Minimum 150mm above roof
- Minimum 3m from operable windows (prevent gas entry)
- Vent cap (prevent rain entry, allow gas exit)

2.7 PLUMBING FIXTURES (ASME A112 Standards)

WATER CLOSETS (WC):
- Floor-mounted (squat type - common in Pakistan)
  - Water seal depth: 50mm minimum
  - Flush volume: 10-13 liters (older), 6 liters (low-flow, modern)

- Wall-hung (Western style)
  - Seat height: 400-430mm
  - Flush: 6 liters (dual-flush: 3/6 liters)

URINALS:
- Wall-hung or floor-mounted
- Flush valve: Manual or automatic (sensor)
- Flush volume: 3-4 liters
- Waterless urinals (cartridge-based, saves water)

LAVATORIES (WASH BASINS):
- Countertop, wall-hung, pedestal
- Faucet flow rate: 6 liters/minute (older), 2 liters/minute (low-flow, aerators)

SHOWERS:
- Flow rate: 10 liters/minute (standard), 6-8 liters/minute (low-flow)
- Mixing valve (hot+cold, adjustable temperature)
- Anti-scald valve (limit maximum temperature)

KITCHEN SINKS:
- Single or double bowl
- Grease trap (commercial kitchens)

ACCESSIBILITY:
- Accessible fixtures: See Chapter 11

2.8 SEWAGE DISPOSAL

MUNICIPAL SEWERAGE:
- Connection to public sewer (where available)
- House connection trap (gully trap)
- Inspection chamber/manhole
- Municipal sewer line

SEPTIC TANK SYSTEM (ON-SITE):
- Where municipal sewer unavailable (common in Pakistan)

Components:
1. Septic Tank (Primary treatment)
   - Capacity: 1-3 days sewage storage
   - Design: 2-chamber or 3-chamber
   - Function: Solids settle, anaerobic digestion, scum floats
   - Outlet: Liquid effluent

2. Soakage Pit/Leach Field (Secondary treatment/disposal)
   - Percolate effluent into ground
   - Brick or concrete rings (perforated)
   - Gravel backfill
   - Sizing: Based on soil percolation test
   - Multiple pits (alternating use extends life)

MAINTENANCE:
- Septic tank desludging: Every 1-3 years
- Soakage pit: May clog over time (rebuild)

ALTERNATIVE:
- Small sewage treatment plant (STP): Residential societies, hotels
- Activated sludge or aerobic treatment
- Treated effluent: Irrigation or discharge

2.9 STORM DRAINAGE (IPC 2024 Chapter 11)

ROOF DRAINAGE:
- Rainfall intensity: Pakistan (varies by region)
  - Karachi: 150-200mm/hour (design storm)
  - Lahore: 100-150mm/hour
  - Monsoon regions: Higher

- Roof drains: 1 per 200 sq m roof area (minimum)
- Sizing: Based on rainfall intensity and roof area

GUTTER AND DOWNSPOUT:
- Gutter slope: 1:200 minimum
- Downspout: 75-100mm diameter (residential)

DISPOSAL:
- Storm sewer (separate system - where available)
- Soakage pit (on-site percolation)
- Combined sewer (older systems, not recommended)

SECONDARY (OVERFLOW) DRAINS:
- Required where roof drainage blockage hazards
- Scuppers (openings in parapet wall)
- Secondary drains (separate system)

2.10 WATER CONSERVATION (Pakistan Context)

WATER SCARCITY:
- Major issue in Pakistan
- Declining water table
- Seasonal shortages

CONSERVATION STRATEGIES:
- Low-flow fixtures mandatory (new construction)
- Dual-flush toilets (3/6 liter)
- Faucet aerators (reduce flow without compromising function)
- Fix leaks promptly

RAINWATER HARVESTING:
- Roof collection
- Storage tank (underground)
- Filtration
- Use: Landscaping, toilet flushing, groundwater recharge

GRAY WATER REUSE:
- Gray water: Wash basin, shower, laundry (not toilet)
- Treatment: Filtration, disinfection
- Reuse: Toilet flushing, landscape irrigation
- Savings: 30-50% potable water reduction

SECTION 3: ELECTRICAL SYSTEMS
================================================================================

3.1 SCOPE AND CODES

SCOPE:
- Power distribution
- Lighting
- Earthing/grounding
- Emergency power

CODES:
- NEC 2020 (National Electrical Code - USA reference)
- IEC Standards (International)
- Pakistan Electricity Rules
- WAPDA/K-Electric Connection Requirements

3.2 POWER SUPPLY

UTILITY CONNECTION:
- WAPDA (Water and Power Development Authority) - most of Pakistan
- K-Electric (Karachi only)
- Voltage: 230V single-phase, 400V three-phase
- Frequency: 50 Hz

SERVICE ENTRANCE:
- Utility meter
- Main circuit breaker/switch
- Main distribution board (MDB)

LOAD SHEDDING (Pakistan Context):
- Scheduled power outages (common, especially summer)
- Duration: 2-12 hours/day (varies by area and season)
- Necessity: Backup power (generator, UPS, solar)

3.3 LOAD CALCULATION (NEC Article 220)

GENERAL LIGHTING:
- Residential: 20-25 W/sq m
- Offices: 15-20 W/sq m (LED)
- Retail: 25-30 W/sq m
- Schools: 10-15 W/sq m

RECEPTACLES (POWER OUTLETS):
- General use: 180 VA per outlet (assumed)
- Specific appliances: Actual nameplate rating

HVAC:
- Major load (30-50% of total in commercial)
- Nameplate rating (compressor + fans)

DEMAND FACTOR:
- Not all loads operate simultaneously
- Residential: 40-60% demand factor (for total connected load)
- Commercial: 70-90%

EXAMPLE (Residential):
- Floor area: 200 sq m
- Lighting: 200 × 25 = 5000 W
- Receptacles: 20 outlets × 180 VA = 3600 W
- HVAC: 5000 W (2 × 1.5 ton AC)
- Water pump: 1000 W
- Kitchen appliances: 3000 W
- Total connected: 17,600 W
- Demand (60%): 10,560 W
- Current (230V): 46 A
- Service: 63 A main breaker

3.4 DISTRIBUTION SYSTEM

MAIN DISTRIBUTION BOARD (MDB):
- Service entrance location
- Main circuit breaker (isolates entire building)
- Branch circuit breakers or fuses
- Residual current device (RCD/RCCB) for protection

SUB-DISTRIBUTION BOARDS (SDB):
- Fed from MDB
- Located on each floor or area
- Final circuit breakers

CIRCUIT TYPES:
- Lighting circuits: 10-16 A breaker, 1.5-2.5 sq mm cable
- Power circuits (receptacles): 16-20 A, 2.5-4 sq mm cable
- HVAC circuits: Dedicated, 20-63 A (based on unit size)
- Water heater: Dedicated, 16-32 A

WIRING METHODS (Pakistan Common):
- PVC conduits (concealed in walls/slabs)
- Surface conduits (industrial, where concealment not required)
- Cable trays (large buildings, accessible ceilings)

CABLE TYPES:
- PVC insulated copper cables (most common)
- Sizes: 1.5, 2.5, 4, 6, 10, 16, 25 sq mm (residential/commercial)
- Armored cables (where mechanical protection needed)

3.5 CIRCUIT PROTECTION

MINIATURE CIRCUIT BREAKERS (MCB):
- Overcurrent protection (short circuit, overload)
- Ratings: Type B (3-5× trip), Type C (5-10× trip), Type D (10-20× trip)
- Magnetic trip (instant, short circuit) + Thermal trip (delayed, overload)

RESIDUAL CURRENT DEVICE (RCD/RCCB):
- Earth leakage protection (electrocution prevention)
- Sensitivity: 30 mA (personnel protection), 100-300 mA (fire protection)
- Trip time: <30 ms (30 mA)
- Required: Bathroom circuits, outdoor outlets, swimming pools

RCBO (Residual Current Breaker with Overcurrent):
- Combined MCB + RCD (one device)
- More expensive, saves space

SURGE PROTECTION:
- Protect against voltage spikes (lightning, switching)
- Surge protective device (SPD) at main panel
- Type 2 SPD (most common)

3.6 EARTHING/GROUNDING (IEC 60364, Pakistan Electricity Rules)

PURPOSE:
- Personnel safety (fault current path to earth)
- Equipment protection
- Lightning protection

EARTHING SYSTEMS:

TN-S (Separate neutral and earth):
- Utility provides separate earth conductor
- Most reliable
- Rare in Pakistan residential

TN-C-S (Combined then Separate):
- Utility neutral is earthed
- Building separates earth from neutral
- Common in Pakistan

TT (Separate earth electrode):
- Building provides its own earth electrode (no utility earth)
- Most common in Pakistan
- Requires RCD (higher earth fault loop impedance)

EARTH ELECTRODE:
- Types: Pipe electrode, plate electrode, strip electrode
- Pipe: 38-50mm diameter GI pipe, 2.5-4m length, driven vertically
- Plate: 600×600×6mm copper/GI plate, buried 1.5m depth
- Resistance: <5 ohms (preferred <1 ohm for sensitive equipment)
- Testing: Annual with earth resistance tester

BONDING:
- Main earthing terminal (MET)
- Earth all metal parts (plumbing, conduits, enclosures, structural steel)
- Equipotential bonding

3.7 EMERGENCY POWER SYSTEMS

STANDBY GENERATOR:
- Fuel: Diesel, natural gas, LPG
- Sizing: 100% of essential loads (emergency lighting, fire alarm, elevators) + 30-50% of non-essential
- Start time: 10-30 seconds (automatic transfer switch - ATS)
- Location: Ground floor, outdoor or ventilated room
- Fuel storage: 8-24 hours full-load runtime
- Testing: Weekly (30 minutes under load)

UNINTERRUPTIBLE POWER SUPPLY (UPS):
- Instant switchover (<10 ms, no interruption)
- Battery backup: 15-30 minutes (bridge to generator start)
- Critical loads: Computers, servers, medical equipment, fire alarm
- Types: Offline, Line-interactive, Online (double-conversion)

SOLAR PV WITH BATTERY STORAGE:
- Growing in Pakistan (net metering available)
- Hybrid system: Solar + Grid + Battery
- Daytime: Solar powers load + charges battery
- Night/cloudy: Battery + Grid
- Load shedding: Battery backup
- Cost: Payback 4-7 years

3.8 LIGHTING DESIGN

ILLUMINATION LEVELS (Lux):
- Offices (general): 300-500 lux
- Offices (detailed work): 500-750 lux
- Classrooms: 300-500 lux
- Corridors: 100-200 lux
- Parking: 50-100 lux
- Retail: 500-1000 lux
- Residential living: 150-300 lux

LIGHTING TYPES:
- LED (Light Emitting Diode): Standard for new construction
  - Efficacy: 80-120 lumens/watt
  - Life: 25,000-50,000 hours
  - Dimmable, instant on, no UV/IR
  - Higher initial cost, lowest operating cost

- Fluorescent (T5, T8, CFL): Being phased out
  - Efficacy: 50-90 lumens/watt
  - Life: 10,000-20,000 hours
  - Contains mercury (disposal concern)

- Incandescent: Banned in many countries (inefficient)
  - Efficacy: 10-20 lumens/watt

CONTROLS:
- Occupancy sensors (toilet, corridors, storage)
- Daylight harvesting (dim artificial when daylight adequate)
- Timers (outdoor, signage lighting)
- Dimmers (residential, hospitality)

EMERGENCY LIGHTING:
- Battery backup (90 minutes)
- Illumination: 10 lux (egress paths), 1 lux minimum
- Exit signs (illuminated, green)

3.9 ELECTRICAL SAFETY

REGULATIONS:
- Pakistan Electricity Rules
- WAPDA/K-Electric Wiring Regulations
- Pakistan Engineering Council (PEC) Standards

REQUIREMENTS:
- Licensed electrical contractor (PEC registered)
- Inspection before connection
- Periodic inspection (commercial: 3-5 years)

COMMON HAZARDS:
- Overloading circuits
- Damaged insulation
- Improper earthing
- Wet locations without RCD
- DIY wiring (unlicensed work)

SECTION 5: ELEVATORS AND VERTICAL TRANSPORTATION
================================================================================

5.1 REQUIREMENT (IBC Chapter 30)

ELEVATORS REQUIRED:
- Buildings >3 stories
- Buildings >1000 sq m per floor
- Any building with essential services on upper floors

5.2 ELEVATOR TYPES

TRACTION ELEVATORS (GEARED/GEARLESS):
- Machine room at top (traditional) or machine-room-less (MRL)
- Steel ropes/belts over traction sheave
- Counterweight
- Suitable: Mid-rise to high-rise (up to 500m)
- Speed: 1-10 m/s (gearless faster)

HYDRAULIC ELEVATORS:
- Hydraulic ram pushes car up
- Cylinder in pit or telescopic
- Suitable: Low-rise (2-7 stories)
- Speed: 0.5-1 m/s
- Simpler, lower cost

5.3 CAPACITY PLANNING

POPULATION:
- Offices: 1 person per 10 sq m
- Residential: 2 persons per unit
- Hotels: 1.5 persons per room

ELEVATOR QUANTITY:
- Rule of thumb: 1 elevator per 200-300 occupants
- Calculation: Traffic analysis (peak 5-minute demand)

ELEVATOR CAPACITY:
- 8-13 persons (630-1000 kg) typical
- Larger buildings: 16-21 persons (1275-1600 kg)

SPEED:
- Low-rise (≤5 floors): 1 m/s
- Mid-rise (6-15 floors): 1.5-2.5 m/s
- High-rise (>15 floors): 3-6 m/s

5.4 ELEVATOR SAFETY

SAFETY FEATURES:
- Overspeed governor (arrests car if speeding)
- Safety brakes (engage if rope breaks)
- Buffer (absorbs energy at bottom)
- Door interlocks (prevent door opening unless car at floor)
- Overload sensor

FIRE SERVICE MODE:
- Elevator recalls to ground floor on fire alarm
- Manual control by firefighters (override normal operation)
- Firefighter elevator required (high-rise >24m)

EMERGENCY COMMUNICATION:
- Intercom/phone in car to monitoring station/security

BACKUP POWER:
- Emergency generator powers 1 elevator (evacuate disabled persons)
- Returns all elevators to ground floor first, then 1 continues on generator

5.5 ACCESSIBILITY (See Chapter 11)

ACCESSIBLE ELEVATOR:
- Car dimensions: 1100×1400mm minimum
- Door width: 800mm minimum
- Control buttons: 900-1200mm height, tactile and Braille
- Audio announcements
- Handrails in car

5.6 ESCALATORS

TYPES:
- Parallel (side-by-side, same direction)
- Criss-cross (up and down)

DIMENSIONS:
- Width: 600mm (rare), 800mm (standard), 1000mm (heavy traffic)
- Speed: 0.5 m/s (standard), 0.65 m/s (heavy traffic)
- Angle: 30° (standard), 35° (max)

CAPACITY:
- 800mm: 4500-5000 persons/hour
- 1000mm: 6750-7500 persons/hour

APPLICATIONS:
- Shopping malls, metro stations, airports
- Where high-volume vertical movement needed
