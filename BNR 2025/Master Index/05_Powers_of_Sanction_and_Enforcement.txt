SECTION 1: FUNDAMENTAL CONDITIONS
================================================================================

1.1 CONSTITUTIONAL BASIS (BNR 1994 Paras 5.1-5.7)

CARDINAL PRINCIPLE (Para 5.2):
Before any work can commence, TWO essential requirements must be met:

A. ACT OF SANCTION by Competent Authority
   - Administrative approval OR
   - Technical sanction
   - Specific to the work proposed

B. ACT OF APPROPRIATION of Funds by Competent Authority
   - Budget allocation made
   - Funds available in proper head
   - Approval to incur expenditure

NO WORK SHALL COMMENCE WITHOUT BOTH CONDITIONS FULFILLED

1.2 ANTICIPATION OF SANCTION (Para 5.3)

A. PERMITTED CIRCUMSTANCES
   - When sanction expected within 3 months
   - Work urgency justified
   - Officer willing to accept responsibility
   - Authority has been approached for sanction

B. OFFICER'S PERSONAL RESPONSIBILITY
   - If sanction refused: Officer personally liable
   - If sanction reduced: Excess expenditure officer's responsibility
   - Written justification mandatory in file

C. LIMITS
   - Maximum 25% of estimated cost may be incurred
   - Time limit: 3 months
   - High-risk decision

1.3 COMPONENT PARTS SANCTION (Para 5.4)

A. SEGMENTED APPROVAL
   - Large projects may be sanctioned in parts
   - Each part must be complete/functional in itself
   - Overall project approval required first
   - Useful when phased funding available

B. CONDITIONS
   - Total project cost disclosed
   - Commitment to complete stated
   - Technical feasibility of phases confirmed

1.4 BUDGET ANTICIPATION (Para 5.7)

A. ANTICIPATION OF BUDGET GRANT
   - Next year's budget may be anticipated (last quarter only)
   - Limit: 10% of current year budget
   - Finance Department approval mandatory
   - High confidence of budget approval required

B. RESTRICTIONS
   - No new works from anticipated budget
   - Ongoing works continuation only
   - Payment in next year

SECTION 2: DELEGATION OF POWERS - ADMINISTRATIVE APPROVAL
================================================================================

2.1 FINANCIAL POWERS FOR ADMINISTRATIVE APPROVAL (BNR 1994 Chapter V + Updates)

A. GOVERNMENT (PROVINCIAL/FEDERAL)
   Financial Limit: Above Rs. 500 million

   Scope:
   - Major infrastructure projects
   - Strategic importance works
   - Multi-year mega-projects
   - Cabinet approval required

B. ADMINISTRATIVE SECRETARY/DEPARTMENTAL HEAD
   Financial Limit: Rs. 100-500 million

   Scope:
   - Departmental priority projects
   - Major facilities
   - Provincial-level importance

C. CHIEF ENGINEER
   Financial Limit: Rs. 20-100 million (indexed from Rs. 20,000)

   Scope:
   - Divisional offices and complexes
   - Major bridges and roads
   - Multi-subdivision projects
   - Technical recommendation to government for higher values

D. SUPERINTENDING ENGINEER
   Financial Limit: Rs. 5-20 million

   Scope:
   - Circle-level works
   - Subdivision buildings
   - Medium bridges
   - Repair and rehabilitation projects

E. DIVISIONAL OFFICER/EXECUTIVE ENGINEER
   Financial Limit: Up to Rs. 5 million

   Scope:
   - Division administrative works
   - Minor buildings
   - Small bridges and culverts
   - Road improvements

F. SUB-DIVISIONAL OFFICER
   Financial Limit: Up to Rs. 1 million

   Scope:
   - Petty construction
   - Minor repairs and maintenance
   - Local improvements
   - Urgent works

2.2 SPECIAL POWERS (Para 5.8-5.10)

A. RESIDENTIAL BUILDINGS FOR OFFICIALS (Para 5.8)

   Government Powers (Special Conditions):
   - Residential buildings for specific posts
   - Standard rent ≤ 10% of average emoluments
   - Adequate return on capital cost demonstrated
   - Assignment to specific office/post holder

   Rationale: Prevent uneconomic residential construction

B. REPAIR CLASSIFICATIONS
   - Original Works: Increases capital value (capital budget)
   - Repairs: Maintains existing condition (revenue budget)
   - Special provisions for roads: Up to Rs. 200,000 may be charged to repairs

SECTION 3: DELEGATION OF POWERS - TECHNICAL SANCTION
================================================================================

3.1 FINANCIAL POWERS FOR TECHNICAL SANCTION (BNR 1994 + 2025 Update)

A. CHIEF ENGINEER
   Financial Limit: Above Rs. 50 million

   Requirements:
   - Complete designs and calculations
   - Detailed estimates with rate analysis
   - Specifications
   - All technical reports (soil, materials, structural)
   - Environmental clearance (if applicable)

B. SUPERINTENDING ENGINEER
   Financial Limit: Rs. 10-50 million

   Requirements:
   - Same as Chief Engineer
   - Structural calculations certified by competent engineer
   - Material test reports
   - Standard specifications adherence

C. DIVISIONAL OFFICER/EXECUTIVE ENGINEER
   Financial Limit: Rs. 4-10 million

   Requirements:
   - Detailed designs
   - Detailed estimates
   - Rate analysis
   - Specification conformity

   Special Powers:
   - Selected experienced Executive Engineers: Up to Rs. 10 million
   - Subject to Chief Engineer discretion
   - Good track record required

D. SUB-DIVISIONAL OFFICER
   Financial Limit: Up to Rs. 4 million

   Requirements:
   - Detailed measurements
   - Rate schedule adherence
   - Simple designs (standard types)

3.2 TECHNICAL SANCTION COMPONENTS (Para 5.11-5.22)

A. DESIGN APPROVAL
   - Structural calculations verified
   - Safety factors adequate
   - Code compliance demonstrated
   - Constructability reviewed

B. ESTIMATE VERIFICATION
   - Quantities checked
   - Rates current and reasonable
   - Rate analysis provided
   - Contingencies appropriate (3-5%)
   - Establishment charges (2%)

C. SPECIFICATIONS APPROVAL
   - Materials specified meet standards
   - Workmanship standards clear
   - Testing requirements defined
   - Quality assurance provisions

SECTION 4: DELEGATION OF POWERS - STORES AND PROCUREMENT
================================================================================

4.1 STORES PURCHASE POWERS (BNR 1994 Para 5.23-5.25 + PPRA 2004)

A. CHIEF ENGINEER
   Financial Limit: Above Rs. 10 million (single procurement)

   Method: Open competitive bidding mandatory

B. SUPERINTENDING ENGINEER
   Financial Limit: Rs. 2-10 million

   Method: Open competitive bidding or limited bidding

C. DIVISIONAL OFFICER
   Financial Limit: Rs. 500,000 - 2 million

   Method: Limited bidding (minimum 3 quotations)

D. SUB-DIVISIONAL OFFICER
   Financial Limit: Up to Rs. 500,000

   Method: Local purchase (minimum 3 quotations for >Rs. 100,000)

4.2 SPECIAL STORE PROVISIONS

A. EMERGENCIES
   - Direct purchase allowed
   - Limit: Up to Rs. 2 million (any officer)
   - Justification report within 7 days
   - Regularization by next higher authority

B. PROPRIETARY ITEMS
   - Certificate of sole supplier required
   - Technical justification
   - Price reasonableness verification
   - Approval by two levels higher authority

C. RATE CONTRACTS
   - Annual contracts for repetitive items
   - Open tender once annually
   - All divisions may draw against rate contract
   - Chief Engineer approval for rate contracts

SECTION 5: MISCELLANEOUS SANCTIONS
================================================================================

5.1 REMISSION OF DEPARTMENTAL CHARGES (Para 5.26)

A. CIRCUMSTANCES
   - Natural calamity damage
   - Fire damage to government property
   - Theft/burglary of government stores
   - Acts of God

B. POWERS
   - Chief Engineer: Up to Rs. 1 million
   - Government: Above Rs. 1 million

C. PROCEDURE
   - Investigation report
   - Police report (if theft/arson)
   - Insurance claim filed first
   - Unrecovered amount only remitted

5.2 WRITE-OFF OF STORES (Para 5.27)

A. OBSOLETE/UNSERVICEABLE STORES
   - Condemnation committee (3 officers)
   - Survey report prepared
   - Disposal through auction

B. WRITE-OFF POWERS
   - Sub-Divisional Officer: Up to Rs. 100,000
   - Divisional Officer: Rs. 100,000 - 500,000
   - Superintending Engineer: Rs. 500,000 - 2 million
   - Chief Engineer: Above Rs. 2 million

C. SHORTAGES/LOSSES
   - Investigation mandatory
   - Recovery from responsible person
   - Write-off only if irrecoverable
   - Higher authority approval required

5.3 LOSS OF GOVERNMENT PROPERTY (Para 5.28)

A. REPORTING
   - Immediate to Divisional Officer
   - Police FIR if theft
   - Insurance intimation within 24 hours

B. INVESTIGATION
   - Fact-finding inquiry
   - Responsibility determination
   - Recovery action
   - Insurance claim processing

C. WRITE-OFF
   - After all recovery efforts exhausted
   - Insurance proceeds adjusted
   - Balance written off by competent authority
   - Disciplinary action if negligence found

SECTION 6: CONTRACTS AND AGREEMENTS
================================================================================

6.1 CONTRACT SIGNING POWERS (BNR 1994 Appendix I)

A. AUTHORITY TO EXECUTE CONTRACTS

   Government:
   - All contracts >Rs. 100 million
   - Special strategic projects
   - PPP agreements
   - International contracts

   Chief Engineer:
   - Contracts Rs. 20-100 million
   - Standard construction contracts

   Superintending Engineer:
   - Contracts Rs. 5-20 million
   - Circle-level projects

   Divisional Officer:
   - Contracts up to Rs. 5 million
   - Division-level projects

   Sub-Divisional Officer:
   - Work orders up to Rs. 1 million
   - Petty contracts

B. DEEDS AND AGREEMENTS (Appendix I)

   Types of Documents:
   1. Construction Contracts (various values)
   2. Supply Contracts (stores and materials)
   3. Lease Deeds (rental agreements)
   4. Maintenance Contracts (annual maintenance)
   5. Consultant Agreements (design/supervision)

   Execution Requirements:
   - Proper stamp duty paid
   - Witness signatures
   - Notarization (contracts >Rs. 10 million)
   - Registration (land-related deeds)

SECTION 8: ACCOUNTABILITY AND AUDIT
================================================================================

8.1 FINANCIAL RESPONSIBILITY

A. PERSONAL LIABILITY
   - Officer exceeding powers: Personally liable for excess
   - Unauthorized expenditure: Recovery from officer
   - Negligence causing loss: Surcharge by audit

B. AUDIT OBJECTIONS
   - Prompt response mandatory
   - Justification with evidence
   - Recovery if objection sustained
   - Disciplinary action for serious irregularities

8.2 PERFORMANCE MONITORING

A. UTILIZATION REVIEWS
   - Quarterly: Budget utilization vs. physical progress
   - Annual: Delegation effectiveness review
   - Powers may be withdrawn for poor performance

B. TRANSPARENCY
   - Sanction registers maintained
   - Public disclosure of contracts >Rs. 10 million (2025)
   - PPRA portal publication
   - RTI compliance
