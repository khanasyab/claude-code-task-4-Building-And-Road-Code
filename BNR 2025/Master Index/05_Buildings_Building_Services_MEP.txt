1.1 PURPOSE AND OBJECTIVES
---------------------------
This master index provides comprehensive guidance on Mechanical,
Electrical, and Plumbing (MEP) systems for buildings, encompassing:

- Building services installations and requirements
- Mechanical systems (HVAC, ventilation, refrigeration)
- Electrical systems (power distribution, lighting, emergency power)
- Plumbing systems (water supply, drainage, fixtures)
- Fire protection systems
- Vertical transportation systems
- Energy efficiency and sustainability ⭐
- Smart building integration ⭐
- Renewable energy systems ⭐

2.1.1 SCOPE AND APPLICATION
- Residential buildings (single-family, multistoried apartments)
- Commercial buildings and offices
- Industrial facilities (factories, warehouses, yards)
- Transportation facilities (rail stations, airports)
- Institutional buildings
- Special occupancies

2.1.2 ELECTRICAL SUPPLY SYSTEMS
- Utility supply characteristics: 220/230V single phase, 380/400V three phase, 50 Hz
- Service connection types and procedures
- Metering requirements (single phase, three phase, smart meters ⭐)
- Substation requirements (11KV/0.4KV) for loads exceeding 50KW
- Distribution companies: DESCO, DPDC, DESA, WZPDCL, NESCO, PBS

2.1.3 ELECTRICAL DESIGN AND INSTALLATION
- Load calculation methods (connected load, demand factors)
  * Residential: 20-30 W/m² lighting, 5-10 W/m² power outlets
  * Commercial offices: 15-25 W/m² lighting, 20-30 W/m² power outlets
  * HVAC loads: 40-80 W/m² (office), 60-100 W/m² (shopping malls)
- Distribution system design (MDB, SDB, FDB)
- Wiring systems and methods (conduit, trunking, cable tray)
- Conductor types and sizing (copper, aluminum, PVC, XLPE)
- Voltage drop limits: 3% lighting, 5% power, 5% total maximum

2.1.4 CIRCUIT PROTECTION
- Overcurrent protection (MCB, MCCB, ACB)
- MCB types: Type B (residential), Type C (commercial), Type D (industrial)
- Earth fault protection (RCD, RCCB, RCBO)
- Surge protection devices (SPD) Types 1, 2, and 3 ⭐
- Discrimination and coordination

2.1.5 EARTHING AND BONDING
- Earthing system design (TN-S, TN-C-S)
- Earth electrode types (rod, plate, strip, foundation)
- Earth resistance requirements: Target <5 ohms, Maximum 10 ohms
- Main earthing terminal and equipotential bonding
- Supplementary bonding for wet areas

2.1.6 LIGHTING DESIGN
- Illumination levels by occupancy type
  * Residential: 100-300 lux
  * Commercial offices: 300-500 lux
  * Educational: 300-500 lux
  * Industrial: 200-1000 lux
- LED technology implementation ⭐
- Lighting controls (occupancy sensors, daylight sensors, dimmers) ⭐
- Emergency lighting and exit signage
- Energy-efficient lighting design ⭐

2.1.7 POWER DISTRIBUTION
- Socket outlet types and installation (13A, 15A, 16-20A for AC)
- Heights and spacing requirements
- RCD protection requirements
- Dedicated circuits for major appliances
- Data and communication cabling (Cat 5e/6/6a, fiber optic) ⭐

2.1.8 SPECIAL SYSTEMS
- Elevator electrical systems
- Fire alarm systems (power supply, wiring, backup)
- Pump and motor installations
- HVAC electrical systems
- Lightning protection systems (IEC 62305)

2.1.9 EMERGENCY AND STANDBY POWER
- Emergency power requirements by system type
- Diesel generator installations
  * Sizing and capacity determination
  * Generator room requirements
  * Fuel storage (3-6 hours minimum)
  * Automatic transfer switches (ATS)
- UPS systems (off-line, line-interactive, on-line) ⭐
- Solar power systems (grid-tied, off-grid, hybrid) ⭐
- Battery energy storage systems ⭐

2.1.10 ENERGY EFFICIENCY AND CONSERVATION
- Building envelope requirements
- HVAC efficiency standards
- Maximum lighting power density
- ISO 50001 Energy Management System ⭐
- Building Management System (BMS) integration ⭐
- Energy audit and monitoring ⭐

2.1.11 SAFETY REQUIREMENTS
- Working space clearances (1.0m front, 0.75m sides, 2.0m height)
- Electric shock prevention (earthing, RCD, double insulation)
- Arc flash protection
- Fire hazard prevention
- Safe work practices and lockout/tagout procedures

2.1.12 ELECTRICITY GRID CODE 2019
- BERC regulations and compliance
- Voltage levels: 400 kV, 230 kV, 132 kV, 33 kV, 11 kV, 400 V
- Power quality requirements
  * Voltage variations: ±5% (132kV+), ±6% (33/11kV), ±10% (400V)
  * Frequency: 49.5-50.5 Hz normal, 49.0-51.0 Hz acceptable
- Harmonics and THD limits
- Power factor requirements (>0.85 or 0.9)
- Demand side management and load control

2.1.13 TESTING, COMMISSIONING, AND MAINTENANCE
- Pre-commissioning tests (insulation resistance, continuity, polarity, earth resistance)
- Functional testing (protection devices, controls, metering)
- Commissioning documentation requirements
- Routine maintenance schedules (monthly, quarterly, annual)
- Record keeping and incident reporting

2.1.14 PROFESSIONAL REQUIREMENTS
- Licensed electrical engineers (IEB members)
- Licensed electricians and contractors
- Inspection authorities (utility, building, regulatory)
- Design certifications and approvals

5.1 WATER SUPPLY SYSTEMS
-------------------------

5.1.1 WATER SUPPLY AUTHORITIES
- DWASA (Dhaka Water Supply and Sewerage Authority)
- CWASA (Chittagong WASA)
- KWASA (Khulna WASA)
- District Metered Area (DMA) system
- Connection procedures and requirements

5.1.2 WATER DEMAND ESTIMATION
- Per capita consumption rates
  * Apartments: 150-200 liters/person/day
  * Single-family houses: 200-250 liters/person/day
  * Offices: 50-75 liters/person/day
  * Hotels: 200-300 liters/guest/day
  * Hospitals: 300-450 liters/bed/day
- Peak demand factors (1.5-2.5 times average)
- Storage tank sizing (24-hour minimum, 1-2 day recommended)

5.1.3 WATER SUPPLY SYSTEM DESIGN
- Direct supply systems (limited application)
- Indirect supply systems (most common in Bangladesh)
  * Underground water tank (UWT) design
  * Pumping systems (submersible, surface, duty/standby)
  * Overhead water tank (OWT) design
  * Distribution piping network
- Pressure requirements: Minimum 100 kPa at highest fixture
- Booster pump systems for high-rise buildings

5.1.4 PIPE MATERIALS AND SIZING
- GI (Galvanized Iron) pipes - traditional
- UPVC (Unplasticized PVC) - increasingly popular
- HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene) - underground
- PPR (Polypropylene Random) - hot and cold water ⭐
- CPVC (Chlorinated PVC) - hot water applications
- Copper pipes - premium installations
- Fixture unit method for pipe sizing
- Velocity limits: 0.6-2.5 m/s

5.1.5 FIXTURES AND FITTINGS
- Water closets (flush tank, dual flush ⭐)
- Lavatories (pedestal, wall-hung, counter-mounted)
- Kitchen sinks and dishwashers
- Bathtubs and showers
- Floor drains and trap requirements
- Sensor faucets and touchless fixtures ⭐
- Low-flow fixtures for water conservation ⭐

5.1.6 HOT WATER SYSTEMS
- Individual electric heaters (storage, instant)
- Central hot water systems for large buildings
- Solar water heating systems ⭐
- Heat pump water heaters ⭐
- Temperature control and safety (anti-scalding devices) ⭐
- Circulation pumps and insulation
- IPC Chapter 5: Water Heaters

5.1.7 WATER CONSERVATION MEASURES
- Low-flow fixtures (shower heads 6-9 L/min)
- Dual flush toilets (3/6 liters)
- Sensor faucets and automatic shut-off valves
- Rainwater harvesting systems ⭐
- Greywater reuse systems ⭐
- Leak detection and monitoring ⭐
- Water-efficient landscaping

5.1.8 BACKFLOW PREVENTION
- Air gap separation
- Check valves
- Backflow preventers (double check, RPZ devices)
- Applications: Building supply, irrigation, industrial connections
- Testing and maintenance requirements

5.2 SANITARY DRAINAGE SYSTEMS
------------------------------

5.2.1 DRAINAGE SYSTEM COMPONENTS
- Building drain (horizontal, lowest pipe)
- Building sewer (to public sewer or treatment)
- Soil stack (vertical, includes WC discharge)
- Waste stack (vertical, excludes WC discharge)
- Branch drains (horizontal collectors)
- Stack vents and vent pipes

5.2.2 DRAINAGE PIPE MATERIALS
- Cast iron (CI) - durable and quiet
- UPVC - most common, lightweight, corrosion resistant
- Vitrified clay - underground drains
- Concrete pipes - large diameter sewers
- PVC Schedule 40/80 specifications

5.2.3 PIPE SIZING AND SLOPES
- Drainage fixture unit (DFU) method
  * Water closet: 4-6 DFU
  * Lavatory: 1 DFU
  * Bathtub/shower: 2 DFU
  * Kitchen sink: 2 DFU
- Minimum slopes by pipe size:
  * 50mm: 2%
  * 75mm: 1.5%
  * 100mm: 1%
  * 150mm: 0.7%
  * 200mm+: 0.5%
- Self-cleansing velocity: Minimum 0.6 m/s

5.2.4 TRAPS AND TRAP SEALS
- Trap seal requirements (50-100mm depth)
- P-trap (horizontal outlet)
- S-trap (vertical outlet - discouraged)
- Bottle trap (compact, aesthetic)
- Floor drain traps
- Trap seal maintenance and venting

5.2.5 VENTING SYSTEMS
- Stack vent (extension of soil/waste stack)
- Vent stack (separate vertical vent)
- Branch vents and individual vents
- Common vents and loop vents
- Vent termination (150mm above roof minimum)
- Air admittance valves (AAV) where permitted ⭐
- IPC Chapter 9: Vents

5.2.6 CLEANOUTS AND ACCESS
- Cleanout locations (base of stacks, direction changes, 30m intervals)
- Cleanout sizing (same as pipe up to 100mm)
- Access provisions for maintenance
- Rodding eyes and inspection chambers

5.2.7 GREASE INTERCEPTORS AND SEPARATORS
- Commercial kitchen grease traps
- Oil separators for parking areas
- Sizing based on fixture units and flow rates
- Maintenance and cleaning schedules
- IPC Chapter 10: Traps, Interceptors and Separators

5.3 STORM WATER DRAINAGE
-------------------------

5.3.1 SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS
- Separate storm and sanitary systems (mandatory)
- Design rainfall intensity (150-250 mm/hour for Bangladesh)
- Return period: 10-25 years
- No cross-connections between systems

5.3.2 ROOF DRAINAGE DESIGN
- Roof outlets and sizing (minimum 75mm)
- Conductor pipes (downpipes) - 75-100mm typical
- Roof gutters and slope requirements (1% minimum)
- Overflow provisions and emergency drains
- IPC Chapter 11: Storm Drainage

5.3.3 SURFACE AND AREA DRAINAGE
- Balcony and terrace drains (trapped)
- Basement drainage and sump pumps
- Parking area drainage
- Catch basins and area drains
- Grading and surface water management

5.3.4 STORM SEWER CONNECTION
- WASA approval requirements
- Inspection chambers at property line
- Discharge to public storm sewers
- Alternative: Discharge to natural drainage (with environmental approval)
- Detention and retention systems for large sites ⭐

5.4 SEWAGE TREATMENT
--------------------

5.4.1 PUBLIC SEWERAGE SYSTEM
- WASA sewerage network coverage
- Connection requirements and procedures
- Inspection chambers and design
- Payment of connection fees
- Compliance with discharge standards

5.4.2 ON-SITE SEWAGE TREATMENT
- Septic tank systems
  * Capacity: 3-5 days retention
  * Minimum 2 chambers with baffles
  * Sizing: 1 m³ per 5-6 persons, 2.5 m³ minimum
  * Construction: Reinforced concrete or brick masonry
- Soak pits and drain fields
  * Percolation rate testing
  * Sizing based on soil conditions
  * Minimum 1.5m from water table
- Desludging requirements (every 2-3 years)

5.4.3 PACKAGE TREATMENT PLANTS
- Extended aeration systems
- Moving Bed Biofilm Reactor (MBBR) ⭐

  * pH: 6.5-9.0
  * BOD: Maximum 50 mg/L
  * COD: Maximum 200 mg/L
  * Suspended Solids: Maximum 150 mg/L

  1. Power failure detection

  4. Transfer to generator power

  6. Transfer back to normal power
  7. Generator cool-down and shutdown

- Bypass isolation provisions for maintenance
- Manual override capability

8.3.1 UPS TYPES

8.3.2 UPS APPLICATIONS

- kVA rating and power factor considerations
- Battery backup duration (5-15 minutes typical, expandable)

- Scalability and modular designs ⭐

8.3.4 UPS BATTERY SYSTEMS
- Valve-Regulated Lead-Acid (VRLA) batteries
- Lithium-Ion batteries (increasing adoption) ⭐
- Battery sizing calculations
- Battery room requirements (ventilation, temperature control)
- Battery monitoring systems ⭐
- Replacement schedules (5-10 years typical)
