SECTION 1: CLASSIFICATION OF OPERATIONS
================================================================================

1.1 FUNDAMENTAL CLASSIFICATION (BNR 1994 Paras 2.1-2.3)

A. ORIGINAL WORKS
   - New construction projects
   - Additions to existing structures
   - Major alterations changing character
   - Capital account expenditure

   Definition: Any work that increases the capital value of an asset or
   creates a new asset

B. REPAIRS
   - Maintenance and ordinary repairs
   - Replacement of worn-out parts
   - Minor alterations not changing character
   - Revenue account expenditure

   Definition: Work required to maintain existing assets in serviceable
   condition without increasing capital value

C. SPECIAL PROVISIONS
   - Petty works threshold: Rs. 2,500 (BNR 1994) → Rs. 50,000 (2025 indexed)
   - Road repairs up to Rs. 10,000 → Rs. 200,000 (2025 indexed)
   - Emergent repairs: Simplified approval process

1.2 EXPENDITURE CLASSIFICATION
   - Capital Works: Funded from development/capital budget
   - Revenue Works: Funded from current/revenue budget
   - Local Fund Works: Funded by local governments
   - Deposit Works: Funded by requesting department

SECTION 2: ADMINISTRATIVE APPROVAL AND TECHNICAL SANCTION
================================================================================

2.1 DUAL APPROVAL SYSTEM (BNR 1994 Paras 2.4-2.17)

FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLE:
No work shall commence without BOTH:
1. Administrative Approval (financial/policy clearance)
2. Technical Sanction (engineering/design approval)
3. Budget Appropriation (funds allocated)

A. ADMINISTRATIVE APPROVAL (Para 2.4-2.13)

Definition: Concurrence of administrative department that:
- The work is necessary and justified
- Estimated expenditure is acceptable
- Project aligns with government priorities
- Funds will be made available

Application Requirements:
- Preliminary estimate
- Justification report
- Site feasibility
- Expected benefits
- Maintenance cost implications
- Environmental clearance (if required)

Approval Authority by Financial Limit:
- Chief Engineer: Up to Rs. 20 million (indexed from Rs. 20,000)
- Provincial Secretary: Rs. 20-100 million
- Provincial Cabinet: Rs. 100-500 million
- Federal Government: Above Rs. 500 million

Validity: 3 years from date of issue (if work not commenced)

B. TECHNICAL SANCTION (Para 2.4-2.13)

Definition: Professional/technical approval certifying:
- Designs are technically sound
- Specifications are appropriate
- Estimates are accurate and reasonable
- Rates are current and justified
- Work is executable as designed

Application Requirements:
- Detailed designs and drawings
- Detailed estimates with rate analysis
- Specifications
- Soil investigation reports
- Material testing reports
- Structural calculations

Sanctioning Authority:
- Sub-Divisional Officer: Up to Rs. 4 million
- Divisional Officer: Rs. 4-10 million
- Superintending Engineer: Rs. 10-50 million
- Chief Engineer: Above Rs. 50 million

Validity: 2 years from date of issue

C. VARIATIONS AND EXCESSES (Para 2.21, 2.109-2.114)

Tolerance Limits without Re-approval:
- Detailed vs. Preliminary Estimate: 5% variation acceptable
- Execution vs. Sanctioned Estimate: 10% acceptable

Excess Requiring Revised/Supplementary Estimates:
- 10-25%: Supplementary estimate required
- >25%: Revised estimate and re-sanction mandatory

Re-approval Authority:
- Same as original sanctioning authority
- Justification for excess mandatory
- Investigation if excess due to negligence

2.2 ESTIMATE PREPARATION STANDARDS

A. PRELIMINARY ESTIMATES (Para 2.22-2.32)
   - Based on preliminary designs
   - Used for administrative approval
   - Accuracy target: ±10%
   - Components:
     - Site report
     - Outline specifications
     - Approximate quantities
     - Unit rates (current)
     - Contingencies: 5% (minor works), 3% (major works)
     - Establishment charges: 2%

B. DETAILED ESTIMATES (Para 2.22-2.32)
   - Complete designs and drawings
   - Used for technical sanction
   - Accuracy target: ±2%
   - Components:
     - Detailed report
     - Complete specifications
     - Detailed measurements
     - Current rate analysis
     - Abstract of cost
     - Contingencies: 3%
     - Establishment: 2%
     - Tools and plant: 1%

C. RATE ANALYSIS
   - Labor rates: Current PWD schedule
   - Material rates: Market survey (3 quotations minimum)
   - Transport: Actual distance and road conditions
   - Contractor's profit: 10-15% (competitive items)
   - Overhead: 5-7%

SECTION 3: REQUISITIONS BY CIVIL DEPARTMENTS
================================================================================

3.1 REQUISITION PROCEDURES (BNR 1994 Paras 2.14-2.21)

A. PETTY WORKS (≤ Rs. 50,000 indexed)
   - Simplified procedure
   - Verbal requisition acceptable
   - Sub-Divisional Officer execution
   - Charges recovered later

B. MAJOR WORKS (> Rs. 50,000)
   - Written requisition mandatory
   - Budget provision confirmation
   - Detailed requirements specified
   - Administrative approval required
   - Recovery of full costs

C. REPAIRS
   - Annual maintenance agreement
   - Inspection-based requisitions
   - Emergency repairs provision
   - Cost recovery monthly

3.2 NON-ACCOMMODATION CERTIFICATE (Para 3.11)
   - Required before hiring private accommodation
   - Certifies no government building available
   - Rental reasonableness verification
   - Annual renewal required

SECTION 4: PROCUREMENT PROCEDURES
================================================================================
[Enhancement: PPRA Rules 2004 Integration]

4.1 PROCUREMENT METHODS

A. OPEN COMPETITIVE BIDDING (PPRA Rule 12)
   - Mandatory for works >Rs. 2 million
   - Public advertisement (newspapers + PPRA website)
   - Minimum 15 days bidding period
   - Two-envelope system (technical + financial)
   - Public opening

B. LIMITED BIDDING (PPRA Rule 13)
   - Works Rs. 0.5-2 million
   - Pre-qualified contractors (3 minimum)
   - 7 days minimum bidding period

C. DIRECT CONTRACTING (PPRA Rule 42)
   - Emergency works (life/property threat)
   - Sole source justification
   - Proprietary items
   - Prior approval mandatory

D. FORCE ACCOUNT/DAILY LABOR
   - Small/petty works
   - Maintenance activities
   - When contractor unavailable
   - Proper muster rolls mandatory

4.2 CONTRACT TYPES (BNR 1994 Paras 2.56-2.72)

A. LUMP SUM CONTRACT
   - Fixed total price
   - Complete work scope defined
   - Payment on completion/milestones
   - Suitable for repetitive/standard works

B. ITEM RATE CONTRACT
   - Rates for bill of quantities items
   - Payment based on actual quantities
   - Most common for civil works
   - Variations allowed within limits

C. PERCENTAGE RATE CONTRACT
   - Percentage above/below schedule of rates
   - Suitable when detailed quantities unknown
   - Flexibility in execution

D. COST-PLUS CONTRACT
   - Actual costs + fixed percentage/fee
   - Used when scope uncertain
   - Requires detailed accounts
   - Management-intensive

4.3 SECURITY REQUIREMENTS (BNR 1994 Para 2.56-2.72)

A. EARNEST MONEY
   - 2-5% of estimated cost
   - Submitted with bid
   - Refunded to unsuccessful bidders
   - Forfeited if bid withdrawn

B. PERFORMANCE SECURITY
   - 5-10% of contract value
   - Submitted by successful bidder
   - Held until completion + defects liability
   - Bank guarantee or cash

C. RETENTION MONEY
   - 5-10% of running payment
   - Released after completion
   - Part release: 50% on completion, 50% after defects liability

4.4 TENDER EVALUATION (PPRA Rule 36)
   - Lowest evaluated responsive bidder wins
   - Technical compliance first
   - Financial evaluation second
   - Negotiations prohibited (except Rule 33)
   - Award within bid validity

SECTION 5: EXECUTION OF WORKS
================================================================================

5.1 COMMENCEMENT PROCEDURES (BNR 1994 Paras 2.82-2.101)

A. PRE-CONSTRUCTION REQUIREMENTS
   - Site possession certificate
   - Contractor mobilization
   - Material testing and approval
   - Work program submission
   - Safety plan approval
   - Quality assurance plan

B. NOTICE TO PROCEED
   - Issued after contract signing
   - Specified commencement date
   - Time allowed for mobilization (28 days typical)
   - Construction period starts

C. SITE ESTABLISHMENT
   - Site office and stores
   - Labor camps (if required)
   - Water and power arrangements
   - Safety signage
   - Access roads

5.2 PROGRESS MONITORING

A. WORK PROGRAM
   - Bar chart/CPM required
   - Monthly update mandatory
   - Critical path identification
   - Resource loading

B. PROGRESS REPORTS (Para 4.9)
   - Weekly: Site level
   - Monthly: Division level with photographs
   - Quarterly: Circle/Chief Engineer level
   - Physical vs. financial progress

C. PROGRESS PAYMENTS
   - Monthly running bills
   - Based on measurement book
   - Retention deduction
   - Advance payment (mobilization): 10% maximum

5.3 VARIATIONS AND DEVIATIONS (Para 2.109-2.114)

A. MINOR VARIATIONS (≤10%)
   - Site Engineer approval
   - Recorded in measurement book
   - No contract modification required

B. MAJOR VARIATIONS (>10%)
   - Supplementary estimate required
   - Higher authority approval
   - Contract addendum necessary
   - Rate adjustment negotiations

C. EXTRA ITEMS
   - Items not in original contract
   - Rate analysis required
   - Negotiated rates or schedule rates
   - Prior approval mandatory

SECTION 6: MEASUREMENTS AND PAYMENTS
================================================================================

6.1 MEASUREMENT PROCEDURES (BNR 1994 Chapter IV)

A. MEASUREMENT BOOK (Para 4.5-4.8)
   - Foundation of all payments
   - Prepared by Sub-Divisional Officer
   - Detailed measurements with sketches
   - Signed by measuring officer
   - Counter-signed by contractor
   - Preserved for 15 years

B. CHECK MEASUREMENT REQUIREMENTS (Para 4.5-4.8)

Sub-Divisional Officer Check Measurement:
- Works >Rs. 25 million: 100% personal measurement
- Works Rs. 5-25 million: 35% check measurement
- Works Rs. 0.2-5 million: 50% check measurement

Divisional Officer Check Measurement:
- Minimum 24 bills per year OR 10% of total payments (whichever more)
- Within 4 weeks of original measurement
- Separate Check Measurement Book

Tolerance Levels:
- Original works: 2% difference acceptable
- Repairs: 5% acceptable
- Earth work: 10% acceptable
- Beyond tolerance: Full re-measurement mandatory

C. PAYMENT PROCEDURES
   - Measurement book → Bill preparation → Verification → Payment
   - Time limit: 30 days from bill submission
   - Interest on delayed payment: 10% per annum (PPRA)
   - Final bill: Within 90 days of completion

SECTION 7: COMPLETION PROCEDURES
================================================================================

7.1 COMPLETION CERTIFICATE (BNR 1994 Paras 2.115-2.125)

A. PHYSICAL COMPLETION
   - All works per contract executed
   - Approved materials used
   - Quality standards met
   - Site cleaned and restored
   - As-built drawings submitted

B. PROVISIONAL COMPLETION CERTIFICATE
   - Issued on physical completion
   - Starts defects liability period (12 months typical)
   - 50% retention released
   - 50% performance security released

C. FINAL COMPLETION CERTIFICATE
   - After defects liability period
   - All defects rectified
   - Final measurements completed
   - Final bill settled
   - Full retention and security released

7.2 COMPLETION REPORT (Para 2.115-2.125)
   - Technical report on work executed
   - Comparison with estimates
   - Variations explained
   - Photographs (before/during/after)
   - Lessons learned
   - Asset handover certificate

7.3 AS-BUILT DRAWINGS
   - Updated to reflect actual construction
   - Include all variations
   - Digital format (AutoCAD + PDF)
   - Part of completion documentation

SECTION 8: SAFETY RISK ASSESSMENT
================================================================================
[Enhancement: UK DMRB GG 104]

8.1 MANDATORY RISK ASSESSMENT
   - Required for ALL works
   - Prepared before tender
   - Updated during execution
   - Reviewed quarterly

8.2 RISK ASSESSMENT COMPONENTS
   - Hazard identification
   - Risk evaluation matrix
   - Control measures
   - Residual risk assessment
   - Emergency response plans

8.3 SAFETY RESPONSIBILITIES
   - Client: Overall safety policy
   - Designer: Design safety
   - Contractor: Construction safety
   - Workers: Follow safety procedures

================================================================================
SECTION 9: DEPOSIT AND SUSPENSE WORKS
================================================================================

9.1 DEPOSIT WORKS (BNR 1994 Section H)
   - Works funded by other departments
   - Full cost recovery
   - 10% departmental charges
   - Separate accounts maintained

9.2 LAPSED SANCTIONS (Para 2.87)
   - Sanction lapses if work not started in 3 years
   - New approval required
   - Rates and designs updated
