SECTION 1: INITIAL RECORDS AND ACCOUNTS
================================================================================

1.1 MUSTER ROLLS (Paragraphs 4.2-4.4)
-------------------------------------------
Definition:
  - Official record of attendance of departmental labor
  - Mandatory for all work sites and departmental establishments
  - Serves as primary evidence for labor engagement and wage payment

Key Requirements:
  - Daily recording of worker presence, absence, and hours worked
  - Countersignature by supervising officer mandatory
  - Must be preserved for audit verification
  - Separate rolls for different categories of labor
  - Progressive maintenance (daily updates, no back entries)

Information to be Recorded:
  - Date, month, and year
  - Name and designation of worker
  - Daily attendance (Present/Absent)
  - Work category/location
  - Signature of recording officer
  - Signature of verifying officer
  - Total wages for pay period

Maintenance Standards:
  - Single ink pen (no pencil)
  - No erasures or overwriting
  - Clear and legible entries
  - Chronological sequence maintained
  - Physical preservation (safe storage, protected from elements)

Archival Requirements:
  - Retention for minimum 3 years after project completion
  - Submission to headquarters for record
  - Reference during audit and inquiry proceedings


1.2 MEASUREMENT BOOKS (Paragraphs 4.5-4.8)
-------------------------------------------
Definition:
  - Official record of all work measurements and quantities
  - Primary basis for determining cost of work and contract payments
  - Legal document for dispute resolution

Structure and Maintenance:
  - Sequential numbering of entries (no gaps)
  - Record of date, location, and type of work
  - Details of measurements taken
  - Signature of measuring officer
  - Countersignature of verifying officer
  - Final acceptance certification

Contents of Entries:
  - Detailed dimensions (length, width, height, depth)
  - Quantities of materials used
  - Sketch or diagram (where applicable)
  - Reference to work estimate or contract
  - Progress percentage calculations
  - Quality notes and observations

Quality Standards:
  - Accuracy within acceptable tolerance
  - Professional and legible handwriting
  - Mathematical accuracy verified
  - Cross-reference with payment bills
  - Protection from damage and deterioration

Progress Reports:
  - Extracted from measurement books
  - Submitted periodically to supervising authority
  - Summary of work progress and achievements
  - Comparison with estimated schedule
  - Identification of delays and remedial actions


1.3 MEASUREMENT BOOK STANDARDS FOR REPAIRS (Special Rule)
-------------------------------------------
Standard Measurement Books:
  - Maintained in office of each Divisional Officer
  - Shows detailed measurements of each work type
  - Updated annually with current renewal/repair items
  - Serves as baseline for repair estimate preparation
  - Reduces time and errors in estimate preparation

Quadrennial Repairs:
  - Detailed measurements recorded for all renewal items
  - Standard book consulted for consistency
  - Updated after each repair cycle
  - Reference standard for future repairs

SECTION 2: CASH MANAGEMENT AND CUSTODY
================================================================================

2.1 CASHIERS AND CASH CUSTODY (Paragraphs 4.12-4.14)
-------------------------------------------
Authorized Cashiers:
  - Chief Engineer (for division headquarters)
  - Senior Finance Officer (delegated authority)
  - Designated accountants (with security deposit)
  - Authorized sub-divisional officers (limited amounts)

Cash Security Requirements:
  - Minimum security deposit required (fixed by Chief Engineer)
  - Amount based on cash handled and local conditions
  - Deposit maintained with Department Treasurer
  - Insurance coverage recommended
  - Personal liability for cash shortages

Cash Custody Procedures:
  - Daily opening/closing procedures documented
  - Cash balance verification at end of each working day
  - Overnight storage in secure vault/safe
  - Keys held by authorized custodian
  - Separate keys for different compartments (if applicable)
  - Access log maintained

Cash Handling Standards:
  - No mixing of different fund types
  - Separate accounts for each category of receipt
  - Daily deposit of excess cash beyond working limit
  - Immediate withdrawal of departmental cheques
  - No personal use of departmental cash


2.2 LOSS OF CASH (Paragraph 4.41)
-------------------------------------------
Reporting Requirements:
  - Immediate notice to supervising authority
  - Detailed report of circumstances
  - Investigation by authorized officer
  - Audit examination mandatory
  - Notification to police (if theft suspected)

Investigation Process:
  - Examination of cash handling procedures
  - Review of recent cash transactions
  - Interview of staff involved
  - Physical inspection of custody area
  - Verification against records

Determination of Responsibility:
  - Negligence or gross negligence assessment
  - Deliberate misconduct investigation
  - System failure analysis
  - Accountability assignment
  - Recovery proceedings initiated

Recovery and Discipline:
  - Amount recovered from security deposit
  - Disciplinary action against responsible officer
  - Possible criminal proceedings
  - Compensation claim from officer
  - Preventive measures implementation

SECTION 3: STORES MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

3.1 STORES - GENERAL PRINCIPLES (Paragraphs 4.15-4.17)
-------------------------------------------
Definition and Scope:
  - All materials, tools, equipment purchased by Department
  - Tools and machinery (major and minor)
  - Consumable stores (paper, paint, cement, etc.)
  - Spare parts and equipment
  - Stationery and office supplies
  - Materials for works under execution

Classification of Stores:
  - Class A: High-value items (machinery, equipment, vehicles)
  - Class B: Medium-value items (tools, spare parts, materials)
  - Class C: Low-value consumable items (stationery, small tools)
  - Perishable stores (materials with shelf life)
  - Non-perishable stores (permanent equipment)

Storage Location:
  - Central divisional stores (main warehouse)
  - Sub-divisional stores (branch warehouse)
  - Work site temporary stores (protected sheds)
  - Workshop stores (for equipment maintenance)
  - Office stores (stationery, consumables)

Custody and Responsibility:
  - Storekeeper maintains all records
  - Division Officer supervises stores management
  - Issue against authorized requisitions only
  - Separation of receipt and issue (dual control)
  - Monthly balance verification


3.2 ACQUISITION AND MANUFACTURE OF STORES (Paragraphs 4.18-4.25)
-------------------------------------------
Purchasing Authority:
  - Chief Engineer (large quantities, special items)
  - Superintending Engineer (divisional items)
  - Division Officer (routine purchases, small orders)
  - Authorized store purchase committee (competitive quotations)

Procurement Methods:
  - Direct purchase (for small quantities, urgent needs)
  - Tender system (for standard items, large quantities)
  - Rate contract (for regular requirement items)
  - Local purchase (for non-contract items)
  - Import procedure (for specialized equipment)

Quality and Specification:
  - Detailed specifications prepared before purchase
  - Samples tested before bulk purchase
  - Inspection on receipt at stores
  - Acceptance certificate issued by authorized officer
  - Rejection procedure for substandard items

Manufacturer Coordination:
  - Direct supply from Government workshops
  - Private contractor manufacturing approval
  - Quality assurance during manufacturing
  - Intermediate inspection at factory
  - Delivery schedule compliance


3.3 RESERVE OF STOCK (Paragraphs 4.26-4.27)
-------------------------------------------
Stock Level Management:
  - Minimum stock fixed for each item type
  - Determines purchase timing and quantity
  - Based on usage rate and lead time
  - Maximum stock limit to prevent overstocking
  - Economic order quantity principles

Stock Maintenance:
  - Regular consumption monitored
  - Replenishment orders placed in advance
  - Seasonal variations considered
  - Storage space constraints addressed
  - Prevention of stock-outs

Stock Holding Standards:
  - Adequate buffer stock for emergencies
  - Inventory turns monitored monthly
  - Dead stock identified and addressed
  - Obsolete items written off
  - Cost of holding calculated


3.4 STOCK TAKING (Paragraphs 4.28-4.30)
-------------------------------------------
Annual Physical Verification:
  - Mandatory at end of financial year
  - Physical count of all items
  - Comparison with book balance
  - Discrepancy investigation
  - Adjustment entries recorded

Stock Verification Procedure:
  - Advance notification to all stakeholders
  - Segregation of items during counting
  - Count team appointed (minimum 3 members)
  - Separate counters for verification
  - Random recounts for accuracy

Discrepancy Analysis:
  - Difference investigation mandatory
  - Natural loss due to:
    - Evaporation
    - Breakage during storage
    - Rust and corrosion
    - Rodent and pest damage
  - Unaccounted loss investigation
  - Theft or negligence assessment

Adjustment and Write-off:
  - Natural loss admitted up to tolerance limit
  - Shortage beyond limit investigated
  - Write-off approval by Division Officer
  - Recovery from responsible person
  - Book balance corrected


3.5 DEPRECIATION OF PLANT AND MACHINERY (Paragraphs 4.31-4.37)
-------------------------------------------
Definition:
  - Systematic reduction in asset value over time
  - Reflects wear, tear, and obsolescence
  - Determined by asset condition and age

Depreciation Rates:
  - Heavy machinery: 10-15% per annum
  - Road equipment: 12-18% per annum
  - Minor tools: 20-25% per annum
  - Vehicles: 15-20% per annum
  - Special equipment: 10-25% (based on type)

Valuation Method:
  - Original cost less accumulated depreciation
  - Annual revaluation at year-end
  - Physical condition assessment
  - Market value consideration
  - Technical obsolescence factor

Maintenance Impact:
  - Well-maintained items lower depreciation
  - Poor maintenance accelerates depreciation
  - Regular servicing extends asset life
  - Major repairs affect depreciation schedule
  - Replacement timing optimization

Asset Replacement:
  - Depreciation schedule guides replacement timing
  - Residual value determined
  - Surplus asset disposal procedures
  - Sale or auction process
  - Transfer of ownership documentation


3.6 MATHEMATICAL INSTRUMENTS (Paragraphs 4.38-4.39)
-------------------------------------------
Categories:
  - Surveying instruments (theodolites, levels, chains)
  - Measuring instruments (scales, gauges, calipers)
  - Drawing instruments (set squares, compass, rulers)
  - Optical instruments (binoculars, magnifiers)

Custody and Maintenance:
  - Appointed custodian responsible
  - Individual record card for each instrument
  - Regular calibration and testing
  - Protective storage in padded cases
  - Environmental control (temperature, humidity)

Calibration Standards:
  - Annual calibration mandatory
  - Certified by authorized testing laboratory
  - Accuracy verification records
  - Out-of-tolerance instruments removed
  - Calibration cost budgeted annually

Issue and Return Procedures:
  - Requisition form required for issue
  - Officer signature for receipt
  - Return inspection for damage
  - Maintenance log updated
  - Periodic custody verification


3.7 STOREKEEPERS (Paragraph 4.40)
-------------------------------------------
Appointment and Qualification:
  - Literate and trustworthy person
  - Preferably with stores experience
  - Basic knowledge of accounts
  - Physical fitness for work
  - Character certificate required

Responsibilities:
  - Receive and verify incoming stores
  - Maintain stores ledger and registers
  - Issue stores against authorized requisitions
  - Preserve stores in good condition
  - Prevent pilferage and loss
  - Submit inventory reports
  - Maintain cleanliness of stores area

Competencies Required:
  - Record-keeping skill
  - Numerical accuracy
  - Organization and tidiness
  - Communication skills
  - Honesty and integrity

Authority and Limits:
  - Issue only against duly signed requisitions
  - Refuse improper or unauthorized requests
  - Report irregularities to supervisor
  - Maintain security of stores keys
  - Daily closing procedures

SECTION 5: TRANSFERS OF CHARGES
================================================================================

5.1 TRANSFERS - GENERAL PRINCIPLES (Paragraph 5.11)
-------------------------------------------
Definition:
  - Handing over of responsibility from one officer to another
  - Change of posting/transfer of officer
  - Change of project assignment
  - Retirement or resignation of officer

Necessity:
  - Mandatory before officer assumes new position
  - Ensures continuity of work and accountability
  - Protects both relieving and relieved officer
  - Establishes clear cut-off points for transactions
  - Prevents disputes over missing or damaged items

Responsibility:
  - Relieving officer prepares detailed inventory
  - Relieved officer verifies accuracy
  - Both sign transfer certificate
  - Supervising authority witnesses
  - Headquarter notification made

Scope of Transfer:
  - All cash and financial records
  - Store items and equipment
  - Work-in-progress documentation
  - Original records and plans
  - Office equipment and furniture
  - Keys and access credentials


5.2 TRANSFERS - ORIGINAL WORKS (Paragraphs 5.12-5.15)

Inventory Check:

Cost and Financial Status:

SECTION 6: RECORD KEEPING AND DOCUMENTATION
================================================================================

6.1 OFFICIAL RECORDS MANAGEMENT
-------------------------------------------
Definition:
  - All documents generated during work execution
  - Correspondence and orders
  - Administrative decisions
  - Financial and audit documents
  - Technical plans and specifications

Record Categories:
  - Permanent Records:
    - Completion certificates
    - Final plans and drawings
    - Audit reports
    - Major decisions
  - Semi-Permanent Records:
    - Working drawings
    - Progress reports
    - Correspondence (5-7 years)
  - Temporary Records:
    - Memos and notes
    - Preliminary drafts
    - Internal communications (1-2 years)

Storage and Preservation:
  - Organized filing system
  - Protection from elements
  - Regular inspection for deterioration
  - Pest and moisture control
  - Fire safety measures


6.2 DESTRUCTION OF OFFICIAL RECORDS (Paragraph 1.133)
-------------------------------------------
Authorized Destruction:
  - Temporary records after retention period
  - Obsolete documents after archival review
  - Approved by competent authority
  - Documented destruction process
  - Certification issued

Retention Periods:
  - Muster rolls: 3 years minimum
  - Measurement books: 5 years minimum
  - Vouchers and receipts: 3-5 years
  - Correspondence: 3 years
  - Plans and drawings: Permanent (working copies 5 years)
  - Financial records: 7 years minimum

Destruction Process:
  - List of records to be destroyed prepared
  - Authority approval obtained
  - Supervised destruction (burning/shredding)
  - Certificate issued by authorized officer
  - Record of destruction maintained

Exception Records:
  - Never destroy without authorization
  - Audit records (as per audit law)
  - Dispute-related records (pending settlement)
  - Historical/heritage documentation
