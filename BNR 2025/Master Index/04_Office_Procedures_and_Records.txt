SECTION 1: INITIAL RECORDS OF ACCOUNT
================================================================================

1.1 MUSTER ROLLS (BNR 1994 Paras 4.2-4.4)

A. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
   - Daily attendance record for labor
   - Basis for payment of wages
   - Essential for daily labor works
   - Form 21 (BNR standard form)

B. PREPARATION REQUIREMENTS
   - Prepared by Sub-Divisional Officer or authorized subordinate
   - Worker name, father's name, CNIC number (2025)
   - Trade/category of work
   - Daily attendance marked
   - Work description
   - Rate of wages
   - Amount payable

C. VERIFICATION AND PAYMENT
   - Countersigned by Sub-Divisional Officer
   - Payment within 7 days of muster roll closure
   - Labor Report (Form 124) prepared monthly
   - Cross-verification with measurement book

D. SCRUTINY REQUIREMENTS (Para 4.3)
   - Divisional Officer: Quarterly review
   - Check against measurement book
   - Verify rates applied
   - Confirm workers actually worked
   - Investigate discrepancies

E. PRESERVATION
   - Retained for 5 years
   - Digital scanning recommended (2025)

1.2 MEASUREMENT BOOKS (BNR 1994 Paras 4.5-4.8)

A. FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLE (Para 4.5)
   "The Measurement Book forms the foundation of all quantity accounts"

   No payment shall be made without:
   - Proper measurement recorded
   - Authorized officer signature
   - Contractor counter-signature

B. MEASUREMENT BOOK CONTENTS
   - Work description with location
   - Detailed dimensions with sketches
   - Calculation of quantities
   - Date of measurement
   - Stage of work
   - Signature of measuring officer
   - Contractor's acknowledgement

C. RECORDING PROCEDURES
   - Measurements in standard units (meters, cubic meters, etc.)
   - Neat sketch showing measured work
   - Clear reference to drawing number
   - Running total calculations
   - No erasures (strike-through corrections only)
   - Each entry dated and initialed

D. EXCEPTIONS TO MEASUREMENT BOOK (Para 4.6)

   Three situations where work may proceed without prior measurement:

   1. Petty Periodic Repairs:
      - Standard measurement books prepared
      - Based on previous experience
      - Annual adjustment allowed

   2. Emergency Urgent Works:
      - Maximum 75% payment in advance
      - Actual measurement within 30 days
      - Final settlement after measurement

   3. Lump Sum Contracts:
      - Milestone-based payments
      - Proper certificate required
      - Final measurement for variations only

E. CHECK MEASUREMENT (Para 4.7-4.8)

   Sub-Divisional Officer Requirements:
   - Works >Rs. 25 million: 100% personal measurement mandatory
   - Works Rs. 5-25 million: 35% check measurement required
   - Works Rs. 200,000-5 million: 50% check measurement required

   Divisional Officer Requirements:
   - Minimum 24 bills per year, OR
   - 10% of total annual payments (whichever is MORE)
   - Check measurement within 4 weeks of original measurement
   - Separate Check Measurement Book maintained

   Tolerance Limits (Para 4.8):
   - Original works: 2% difference acceptable
   - Repairs: 5% difference acceptable
   - Earthwork: 10% difference acceptable
   - Exceeding tolerance: Full re-measurement required
   - Investigation if excess due to negligence

F. PRESERVATION
   - Retained for 15 years
   - Most important accounting document
   - Basis for audit verification

1.3 PROGRESS REPORTS (BNR 1994 Para 4.9)

A. FREQUENCY
   - Weekly: Site-level internal
   - Monthly: Divisional Officer to Superintending Engineer
   - Quarterly: Superintending Engineer to Chief Engineer

B. CONTENTS
   - Physical progress (percentage completion)
   - Financial progress (expenditure vs. budget)
   - Comparison with program
   - Issues and constraints
   - Photographs (monthly reports)
   - Critical path status
   - Labor and equipment deployed

C. FORMAT
   - Standardized proforma
   - Digital submission (2025)
   - Dashboard integration for monitoring

SECTION 2: CASH CUSTODY AND MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

2.1 CUSTODY OF CASH (BNR 1994 Paras 4.10-4.11)

A. GENERAL PRINCIPLES
   - Minimize cash holdings
   - Bank deposits daily (if >Rs. 50,000)
   - Secure cash chest mandatory
   - Insurance for cash-in-transit

B. SECURITY MEASURES
   - Double lock system (two key-holders)
   - Police guard if holdings >Rs. 500,000
   - CCTV surveillance (2025 standard)
   - Fireproof safe

2.2 CASHIERS (BNR 1994 Paras 4.12-4.14)

A. APPOINTMENT
   - By Divisional Officer
   - Security deposit mandatory: Rs. 50,000 (indexed from Rs. 500)
   - Fidelity insurance required (2025)
   - Background verification

B. RESPONSIBILITIES
   - Receipt and payment of cash
   - Maintain cash book
   - Daily cash reconciliation
   - Banking of surplus cash
   - Safe custody of cash and securities

C. SUPERVISION
   - Monthly cash verification by Sub-Divisional Officer
   - Quarterly surprise check by Divisional Officer
   - Annual audit

2.3 LOSS OF CASH (BNR 1994 Para 4.41)

A. REPORTING
   - Immediate report to Divisional Officer
   - Police report (FIR) filed
   - Insurance claim initiated
   - Investigation ordered

B. RECOVERY
   - From guilty officer
   - From insurance (if insured)
   - Court decree if theft/fraud

SECTION 3: STORES MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

3.1 GENERAL STORES PROVISIONS (BNR 1994 Paras 4.15-4.39)

A. STORES DEFINITION (Para 4.15-4.17)
   - All materials procured for construction
   - Tools and equipment
   - Spare parts and consumables
   - Fuel and lubricants

B. STORES CATEGORIES
   - Stock: Regularly maintained items
   - Stores: Project-specific materials
   - Machinery and equipment
   - Condemned stores (for disposal)

3.2 ACQUISITION AND MANUFACTURE (Paras 4.18-4.25)

A. PROCUREMENT (Reference: BNR Chapter II, PPRA Rules)
   - Competitive bidding preferred
   - Local purchase for petty items (<Rs. 50,000)
   - Quality inspection before acceptance
   - Stores receipt voucher issued

B. MANUFACTURE IN WORKSHOPS (Para 4.19-4.22)
   - Estimate required if value >Rs. 200,000 (indexed)
   - Material issue voucher for raw materials
   - Labor and overhead charged
   - Quality inspection before issue

3.3 RESERVE STOCK (Paras 4.26-4.27)

A. PURPOSE
   - Emergency requirements
   - Avoid procurement delays
   - Seasonal procurement advantages
   - Buffer stock

B. LIMITS
   - Fixed by competent authority
   - Based on annual consumption
   - Typically 3-6 months requirement
   - Review annually

3.4 STOCKTAKING AND VERIFICATION (Paras 4.28-4.31)

A. FREQUENCY
   - Physical verification: Semi-annually (June 30 & December 31)
   - Surprise checks: Quarterly by Divisional Officer
   - Annual audit verification

B. PROCEDURES
   - Count all items physically
   - Compare with ledger balances
   - Investigate discrepancies (>2%)
   - Write-off excess/obsolete stores
   - Revaluation if market prices changed significantly

C. VERIFICATION CERTIFICATE
   - Signed by verifying officer
   - Storekeeper counter-signs
   - Discrepancies reported with explanation
   - Action taken on shortages

3.5 ISSUE OF STORES (Paras 4.32-4.35)

A. ISSUE PROCEDURES
   - Material issue voucher prepared
   - First-in-first-out (FIFO) principle
   - Quality check before issue
   - Receiver's acknowledgement
   - Entered in stock ledger

B. CHARGING RATES
   - Issue at cost price
   - Include transport and handling
   - Add overhead (5-10%)
   - Book adjustment for obsolete items

3.6 DISPOSAL OF STORES (Paras 4.36-4.37)

A. SURPLUS STORES
   - Transfer to other subdivisions (preferred)
   - Sell through auction
   - Government approval if value >Rs. 1 million
   - Proper auction procedure

B. CONDEMNED STORES
   - Condemnation certificate
   - Survey committee (3 officers)
   - Auction for scrap value
   - Environment-friendly disposal

3.7 MATHEMATICAL INSTRUMENTS (Paras 4.38-4.39)
   - Personal custody of engineers
   - Annual verification
   - Insurance recommended
   - Replacement on fair wear basis

3.8 STOREKEEPERS (BNR 1994 Para 4.40)

A. APPOINTMENT AND SECURITY
   - Security deposit: Rs. 25,000 (indexed from Rs. 250)
   - Proper godown facilities
   - Fire safety measures
   - Rodent and moisture protection

B. RECORDS MAINTAINED
   - Stores ledger
   - Bin cards
   - Receipts and issue vouchers
   - Stock verification registers

SECTION 5: TRANSFER OF CHARGES
================================================================================

5.1 TRANSFER PROCEDURES (BNR 1994 Paras 4.44-4.58)

A. SITUATIONS REQUIRING TRANSFER OF CHARGE
   - Officer transfer/retirement
   - Death of officer
   - Leave exceeding 90 days
   - Disciplinary suspension
   - Promotion requiring transfer

B. HANDING-OVER PROCESS (Detailed in Paras 4.45-4.57)

   Step 1: Advance Notice
   - Transfer order issued 30 days prior
   - Hand-over/take-over date fixed
   - Relieving officer arrives 2-3 days early

   Step 2: Records Verification
   - Cash verification (count in presence of both officers)
   - Stores physical verification
   - Measurement books check
   - Bill registers review
   - Correspondence files review
   - Seal and keys handover

   Step 3: Works Inspection
   - Visit all ongoing works
   - Note stage and condition
   - Pending issues documented
   - Contractor claims reviewed

   Step 4: Formalities
   - Hand-over report prepared (quadruplicate)
   - Both officers sign
   - Copies to: Divisional Officer, Accounts Officer, File
   - Relieving officer issues charge assumption certificate

C. SPECIAL CASES (Para 4.58)

   Death of Officer:
   - Senior subordinate takes temporary charge
   - Immediate cash and stores verification
   - Sealed pending permanent arrangement
   - Divisional Officer urgent visit

   Abrupt Transfer:
   - 7 days minimum notice
   - Abbreviated procedure
   - Full verification within 30 days by relieving officer

SECTION 6: RECORDS MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

6.1 RECORD CATEGORIES

A. PERMANENT RECORDS (Preserved indefinitely)
   - Service books
   - Land acquisition records
   - Important contracts (>Rs. 10 million)
   - Major project completion reports
   - As-built drawings
   - Asset registers

B. LONG-TERM RECORDS (10-20 years)
   - Measurement books: 15 years
   - Estimates and sanctions: 10 years
   - Bills and vouchers: 10 years
   - Contracts: 10 years (after completion)
   - Correspondence: 10 years

C. MEDIUM-TERM RECORDS (5-10 years)
   - Muster rolls: 5 years
   - Check measurement books: 5 years
   - Stores records: 5 years
   - Routine correspondence: 5 years

D. SHORT-TERM RECORDS (1-3 years)
   - Daily progress reports: 2 years
   - Gate passes and issue vouchers: 2 years
   - Temporary establishment records: 3 years after cessation

6.2 RECORD PRESERVATION (BNR 1994 Appendix II)

A. PHYSICAL PRESERVATION
   - Proper filing and indexing
   - Fireproof cabinets for important records
   - Pest control measures
   - Climate control (temperature and humidity)
   - Regular inspection

B. DIGITAL ARCHIVING (2025 Enhancement)
   - Scanning of important records
   - Cloud backup
   - Metadata tagging
   - Search functionality
   - Access control

6.3 DESTRUCTION OF RECORDS (BNR 1994 Para 1.133)

A. AUTHORIZATION
   - After retention period expiry
   - List prepared by Sub-Divisional Officer
   - Approved by Divisional Officer
   - Certificate of destruction maintained

B. PROCEDURE
   - Committee supervision (2 officers)
   - Shredding or secure burning
   - Certificate of destruction (3 copies)
   - Cross-verified with register

SECTION 7: EXPENDITURE CONTROL
================================================================================

7.1 HEAVY EXPENDITURE IN MARCH (BNR 1994 Para 4.59)

A. PROHIBITION
   - No rush of expenditure in final month
   - Avoid booking unexecuted work
   - Smooth distribution throughout year
   - Exception: Genuine works executed

B. SANCTIONS
   - Investigate irregular March expenditure
   - Disciplinary action for false booking
   - Audit scrutiny enhanced

7.2 BUDGET MONITORING
   - Monthly expenditure vs. budget review
   - Quarterly re-appropriations
   - Surrender of savings by February
   - Supplementary grant requests by November
