ADMINISTRATIVE AND ESTABLISHMENT
Master Index: BNR 1994 + BNBC 2020 Integration
Compiled: November 2025

OVERVIEW
This master index integrates legacy Building and Roads Department administrative
structures (BNR 1994) with modern BNBC 2020 requirements for digital permit
systems and professional standards. Legacy provisions are preserved while
modern practices are marked with ⭐.

================================================================================
SECTION 1: ORGANIZATIONAL AND HIERARCHICAL STRUCTURE
(Legacy BNR Chapter I: Establishment + Modern BNBC Digital Framework)
================================================================================

1.1 ORGANIZATIONAL HIERARCHY - CLASSIC BNR STRUCTURE

Department of Buildings and Roads Operating Structure:

A. CHIEF ENGINEER (Paragraphs 1.38-1.41)
   - Apex authority for all B&R operations
   - Responsible for departmental policy and strategic direction
   - Powers of administrative sanction and technical recommendation
   - Chair: Administrative and Technical Boards

B. REGIONAL/PROVINCIAL ENGINEER OFFICER (Paragraphs 1.42-1.43)
   - Provincial/regional authority under Chief Engineer
   - Oversees multiple divisional offices
   - Intermediate authority for large project approvals

C. SUPERINTENDING ENGINEER (Paragraphs 1.44-1.51)
   - Senior divisional authority
   - Technical review and approval authority
   - Manages division-level budgets and resources
   - Typically oversees 2-4 subdivisions

D. SUPERINTENDENT OF WORKS (Paragraph 1.52)
   - Operational field authority
   - Direct site supervision
   - Quality control and inspection authority

E. DIVISIONAL OFFICER (Paragraphs 1.53-1.69)
   - Administrative head of subdivision
   - Requisitions and works coordination
   - Revenue collection and accounts
   - Multiple divisional officers under one Superintending Engineer

F. SUB-DIVISIONAL OFFICER (Paragraph 1.70)
   - Smallest administrative unit
   - Local implementation authority
   - Direct interaction with public

1.2 MODERN ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE - BNBC 2020 DIGITAL FRAMEWORK ⭐

New institutional arrangements for code administration:

A. CHIEF BUILDING OFFICIAL (BNBC Part 2, Chapter 2)
   - Modern equivalent to Chief Engineer
   - Digital permit system authority
   - Code interpretation and enforcement
   - Department establishment and policy

   Reporting Structure:
   - Deputy Building Official(s)
   - Plan Review Division
   - Inspection Division
   - Enforcement Division
   - Records and Documentation Division (Digital Database)

B. DEPARTMENT OF BUILDING SAFETY ⭐ (BNBC Part 2, Section 2.1)
   New institutional division responsible for:

   ⭐ PLAN REVIEW DIVISION
      - Architectural review
      - Structural review
      - MEP (Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing) review
      - Fire safety review
      - Digital plan processing

   ⭐ INSPECTION DIVISION
      - Foundation and excavation
      - Structural frame
      - MEP systems
      - Final occupancy inspections
      - Digital reporting system

   ⭐ ENFORCEMENT DIVISION
      - Violation notices
      - Stop-work orders
      - Unsafe building declarations
      - Demolition orders
      - Legal proceedings

   ⭐ RECORDS AND DOCUMENTATION DIVISION
      - Digital database maintenance
      - Permit issuance and tracking
      - Plan storage and retrieval
      - Inspection records management

C. COORDINATING AUTHORITIES (BNBC Part 2, Section 1.2.2)
   ⭐ Multi-jurisdictional enforcement:
   - RAJUK (Rajdhani Unnayan Kartripakkha) - Dhaka Metropolitan
   - City Corporations (major cities)
   - Municipalities (330+)
   - Local Government Engineering Department (LGED)

3.1 LEGACY APPROVAL FRAMEWORK (BNR Chapter II, Sections 2.4-2.13)

ADMINISTRATIVE APPROVAL AND TECHNICAL SANCTION AUTHORITY

Classification of Works (2.1-2.3):
- Original new construction works
- Repair and maintenance works
- Capital account expenditure works
- Local fund works

Powers of Administrative Approval by Authority Levels:
- Chief Engineer: Maximum financial limit for all works
- Provincial Engineer Officer: Moderate limit
- Superintending Engineer: Divisional limit
- Divisional Officer: Subdivision limit

SANCTION PROCEDURES:
a) Preliminary Sanction (Administrative Approval)
   - Authority reviews requirement
   - Financial feasibility assessment
   - Departmental priority determination

b) Technical Sanction (Engineering Approval)
   - Design and specification review
   - Estimate preparation
   - Rate and costing verification
   - Technical compliance assessment

c) Limitations of Sanction (2.21):
   - Works within sanctioned scope only
   - Financial limits cannot be exceeded without re-sanction
   - Variations require supplementary estimate approval

3.2 MODERN DIGITAL PERMIT SYSTEM - BNBC 2020 INTEGRATION ⭐

⭐ DIGITAL BUILDING PERMIT FRAMEWORK (BNBC Part 2, Chapter 3)

3.2.1 REQUIREMENT FOR BUILDING PERMIT ⭐ (Section 3.1)
   No construction, alteration, repair, demolition, or occupancy change
   without building permit from Building Official

   Separate Permits Required for:
   - Building construction/alteration
   - Electrical installations
   - Plumbing installations
   - Mechanical systems
   - Fire protection systems
   - Elevator installations
   - Demolition work
   - Temporary structures

3.2.2 DIGITAL PERMIT APPLICATION PROCEDURES ⭐ (Section 3.2)

   Application Components:

   A. Completed Digital Application Form including:
      - Owner information (name, address, contact)
      - Property details (location, plot number, khatian number)
      - Project description
      - Estimated construction cost
      - Proposed use/occupancy classification
      - Construction timeline

   B. Site Documentation:
      - Land ownership documents (certified copy)
      - Updated survey plan
      - Soil investigation report
      - Site photographs

   C. Construction Drawings (all digitally signed/sealed):
      - Architectural plans, elevations, sections
      - Structural drawings and calculations
      - Electrical drawings and load calculations
      - Plumbing and sanitary drawings
      - Mechanical/HVAC drawings
      - Fire protection drawings
      - Landscaping plan

   D. Technical Reports:
      - Structural design calculations
      - Geotechnical investigation
      - Environmental impact assessment (if required)
      - Traffic impact study (large projects)
      - Fire safety certificate from Fire Service
      - Utility connection approvals

   E. Compliance Documents:
      - BNBC compliance statement
      - Setback and FAR calculations
      - Parking provision details
      - Energy efficiency calculations
      - Accessibility compliance statement

3.2.3 DIGITAL SUBMISSION OPTIONS ⭐
   - Online portal submission
   - Approved digital formats (PDF, DWG)
   - 6 sets (RAJUK/City Corps), 4 sets (Municipalities)
   - 1 additional set for Fire Service (multi-storey)

3.2.4 DIGITAL PLAN REVIEW PROCESS ⭐ (Section 3.3)

   Initial Digital Review:
   - Unique application number assignment
   - Completeness verification
   - Incomplete application return (7 days)
   - Distribution to review divisions (automated)
   - Fire Service notification (automatic for qualifying projects)

   Standard Review Timelines:
   - Small projects (≤3 storeys): 30 days
   - Medium projects (4-10 storeys): 45 days
   - Large projects (>10 storeys): 60 days
   - Special structures: 90 days

   Fire Service Coordination:
   - Buildings >3 storeys: simultaneous Fire Service submission
   - Fire Service 30-day review period
   - Fire Safety Certificate prerequisite
   - Building Official cannot issue without Fire Service approval

3.2.5 COMMENT CYCLES AND RESUBMISSION ⭐
   - Building Official issues written comments within review period
   - Applicant responds with revised documents
   - Re-review timeline: 50% of original period
   - Multiple comment cycles as needed

3.2.6 DIGITAL PERMIT ISSUANCE ⭐ (Section 3.4)

   Permit Components:
   - Permit number and date
   - Project details and location
   - Approved construction scope
   - Validity period
   - Inspection requirements
   - Conditions and restrictions
   - Permit fees paid record

   Permit Fees ⭐:
   - Plan review fee: 0.5-1% of construction cost
   - Building permit fee: 0.5-1% of construction cost
   - Inspection fees: Included or separate
   - Re-inspection fees: As established

   Permit Validity:
   - Standard buildings: 2 years
   - Large projects: 3 years
   - Extension possible before expiration
   - Lapsed permits require new application

3.2.7 WORK EXEMPT FROM DIGITAL PERMITS ⭐
   - Minor repairs (non-structural)
   - Painting, wallpapering, tiling
   - Replacement of damaged materials
   - Single-storey tool sheds <10 sq.m
   - Temporary site offices <6 months
   - Agricultural buildings in rural areas
   - Boundary walls <2 meters height

   Note: Exemption from permit does not exempt from code compliance

3.2.8 SPECIAL PERMITS AND APPROVALS ⭐
   - Demolition permits
   - Temporary structures
   - Building relocation
   - Change of occupancy

3.2.9 BUILDING BOARD OF APPEALS ⭐ (BNBC Part 2, Section 2.4)

   Establishment:
   - Appeals authority for Building Official decisions

   Composition:
   - Chairman: Retired judge or senior architect/engineer
   - Structural Engineer (IEB registered)
   - Architect (IAB registered)
   - Fire Safety Expert
   - Urban Planning Expert
   - Three-year terms, renewable

   Powers:
   - Hear appeals from Building Official
   - Interpret code provisions
   - Recommend code modifications
   - Grant variances in specific cases
   - Uphold, modify, or reverse decisions

   Appeal Procedures:
   - File within 30 days of Building Official decision
   - Written notice with grounds stated
   - Filing fee required
   - Hearing within 60 days
   - Decision within 30 days of hearing
   - Appeal to civil court possible

SECTION 4: POWERS OF SANCTION
(Legacy BNR Chapter V + Modern BNBC Enforcement Authority)
================================================================================

4.1 LEGACY SANCTION AUTHORITY FRAMEWORK (BNR Chapter V)

SANCTION POWERS BY ADMINISTRATIVE LEVEL

Authority Structure for Work Approval:
- Only officers in prescribed authority levels may sanction works
- Financial limits define maximum sanction authority
- Expenditure beyond limits requires higher authority approval

Categories of Sanction:
a) Full Sanction: Complete administrative and technical approval
b) Supplementary Sanction: Additional funds beyond original estimate
c) Revised Estimate Sanction: Complete redesign/re-estimate approval
d) Emergency Sanction: Expedited approval for urgent works

Sanctions Limitations:
- Works must comply with sanctioned scope
- Expenditure cannot exceed sanctioned amount
- Variations require separate sanction
- No work may commence without proper sanction
- Sanction lapses if work not commenced within specified period (2.87)

4.2 MODERN ENFORCEMENT AUTHORITY - BNBC 2020 ⭐

⭐ POWERS OF BUILDING OFFICIAL ENFORCEMENT (BNBC Part 2, Chapter 2)

A. PERMIT MANAGEMENT AUTHORITY ⭐
   - Issue or deny building permits
   - Impose conditions on permits
   - Suspend or revoke permits
   - Extend or modify permit scope
   - Issue partial or staged permits

B. INSPECTION AND APPROVAL AUTHORITY ⭐ (BNBC Part 2, Chapter 4)

   Mandatory Inspection Points (Section 4.2.1):
   - Pre-construction meeting
   - Foundation inspection
   - Plinth level inspection
   - Structural frame (each floor)
   - Roof level inspection
   - Electrical rough-in
   - Plumbing rough-in
   - Mechanical rough-in
   - Fire protection systems
   - Insulation and weatherproofing
   - Final inspection

   Special Inspections (Section 4.2.2):
   - Structural steel welding
   - High-strength concrete placement
   - Post-tensioned concrete
   - Deep foundation installation
   - Curtain wall systems
   - Fire-resistive assemblies
   - Special mechanical systems

   Inspection Procedures (Section 4.3):
   - Request 48 hours advance
   - Schedule within 2 working days
   - Provide written reports
   - Pass/Fail determination
   - Fail status stops work
   - Re-inspection on corrections

C. ENFORCEMENT POWERS ⭐ (BNBC Part 2, Section 2.2.2)

   Violation Enforcement:
   - Issue violation notices
   - Order stop-work
   - Issue unsafe building declarations
   - Order demolition (with proper authority)
   - Initiate legal proceedings

   Emergency Powers:
   - Order immediate evacuation
   - Authorize emergency repairs
   - Take safety protective measures
   - Demolish dangerous structures

D. CERTIFICATE ISSUANCE AUTHORITY ⭐
   - Occupancy certificate upon project completion
   - Fire safety certificate (with Fire Service coordination)
   - Code compliance certificates
   - Demolition certificates

4.3 APPROVAL TIMELINE REQUIREMENTS ⭐

Digital Processing Timeline:
- Complete application receipt: Day 0
- Initial completeness review: 7 days
- Plan review period: 30-90 days (by project size)
- Comment response period: 50% of original review
- Permit issuance: Upon final approval
- Inspection scheduling: Within 2 working days of request
- Inspection completion: On scheduled date
- Report issuance: Within 1 working day

4.4 SANCTION LAPSES AND REVALIDATION (BNR Chapter II, Section 2.87)

Legacy Framework:
- Sanction lapses if work not commenced within specified period
- Lapsed sanction requires new approval/authorization
- Partially completed work follows lapsed sanction rules

Modern Digital Equivalent ⭐:
- Building permit validity: 2-3 years from issuance
- Extension possible with written request before expiration
- Lapsed permit requires new application
- Re-application may reference previous approved plans
