BASE SOURCES:

- USA: ANSI A117.1-2017
- India: Harmonised Guidelines and Standards for Universal Accessibility 2021
- UK: BS 8300-2:2018 Design of an accessible and inclusive built environment

PAKISTAN CONTEXT:
- Disabled Persons (Employment and Rehabilitation) Ordinance 1981
- Pakistan Bait-ul-Mal (PBM) National Centre for Rehabilitation 2010
- UN Convention on Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD) - ratified 2011
- Census 2017: 3.3 million persons with disabilities (1.57% population)
- Actual estimate: 10-15 million (underreporting due to social stigma)
- Types: Mobility (42%), visual (28%), hearing (18%), cognitive (12%)

================================================================================
SECTION 1: GENERAL REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

1.1 SCOPE AND APPLICATION
-------------------------
Buildings and facilities covered:
- All public buildings (government, educational, healthcare, commercial)
- Multi-family residential buildings (>3 units)
- Places of assembly (mosques, theaters, stadiums)
- Transportation facilities (airports, bus terminals, railway stations)

Exemptions:
- Single-family detached houses
- Duplexes (2 units)
- Industrial buildings (employee work areas only - exception)

Base: IBC 2024 Section 1103

1.2 REFERENCED ACCESSIBILITY STANDARDS
---------------------------------------
Primary standard: ICC A117.1-2017
Sections:
- Chapter 3: Building blocks (reach ranges, clear floor space, operable parts)
- Chapter 4: Accessible routes
- Chapter 5: General site and building elements
- Chapter 6: Plumbing elements and facilities
- Chapter 7: Communication elements and features
- Chapter 8: Special rooms and spaces
- Chapter 9: Built-in elements
- Chapter 10: Dwelling and sleeping units

Compliance required for:
- New construction
- Additions (accessible path to addition)
- Alterations (altered elements must comply)

Base: IBC 2024 Section 1101.2

1.3 PAKISTAN LEGAL FRAMEWORK
-----------------------------
Disabled Persons Ordinance 1981:
- 2% quota government employment
- Free education, vocational training
- Accessibility to public buildings (not enforced historically)

Proposed legislation (2023 draft):
- Pakistan Persons with Disabilities Act 2023
- Mandatory accessibility for all new public buildings
- Penalties for non-compliance: Rs 100,000-500,000
- Accessibility audits every 3 years

Provincial initiatives:
- Punjab Barrier-Free Access Rules 2021
- Sindh accessibility guidelines 2019
- Khyber Pakhtunkhwa disability policy 2018

Base: Disabled Persons Ordinance 1981
Punjab Barrier-Free Access Rules 2021

================================================================================
SECTION 2: ACCESSIBLE ROUTES
================================================================================

2.1 GENERAL REQUIREMENTS
------------------------
Accessible route:
- Continuous, unobstructed path
- Connects all accessible spaces and elements
- May include: Walkways, ramps, curb ramps, elevators, platform lifts

Minimum number of routes:
- General: ≥1 accessible route
- Multilevel buildings: Accessible route to all levels
- Dwelling units: ≥1 accessible route to primary entry

Width:
- Minimum clear width: 915 mm (36 inches)
- Preferred width: 1,200 mm (for passing)
- Two-way traffic: 1,525 mm (60 inches)

Headroom:
- Minimum vertical clearance: 2,030 mm (80 inches)
- Signage/objects: ≤2,030 mm height (cane-detectable if protruding >100 mm)

Base: ICC A117.1 Section 402
IBC 2024 Section 1104

2.2 WALKING SURFACES
--------------------
Surface requirements:
- Firm, stable, slip-resistant
- Changes in level: ≤6 mm (1/4 inch) vertical without treatment
- 6-13 mm (1/4 - 1/2 inch): Beveled edge (max 1:2 slope)
- >13 mm: Ramp required

Running slope: ≤1:20 (5%) - if steeper, considered ramp
Cross slope: ≤1:48 (2%)

Grating openings:
- Maximum 13 mm (1/2 inch) in one direction
- Long dimension perpendicular to travel

Carpet:
- Maximum pile thickness: 13 mm (1/2 inch)
- Level-loop, textured loop, level cut-pile, or level cut/uncut pile
- Firm cushion or backing
- Securely attached edges

Pakistan context:
- Avoid marble/polished stone (slippery when wet during monsoon)
- Textured tiles, anti-slip coatings preferred
- Drainage critical (waterlogging common)

Base: ICC A117.1 Section 302

2.3 RAMPS
---------
Maximum slope:
- 1:12 (8.33%) - steepest allowed
- 1:16 (6.25%) - preferred
- 1:20 (5%) - ideal for long ramps

Maximum rise:
- 760 mm (30 inches) per run
- Landings required at top, bottom, and every 760 mm rise

Minimum width:
- Clear width: 915 mm (36 inches) between handrails
- Wheelchair turning: 1,525 mm (60 inches)

Landings:
- Length ≥1,525 mm (direction of travel)
- Width ≥ ramp width
- Slope ≤1:48 (2%) in all directions
- Door swing: Landing must accommodate door + wheelchair (1,525 mm)

Edge protection:
- Curb (100 mm min height) or
- Barrier/guardrail
- Exception: Ramps not required where adjacent surface within 75 mm vertical

Handrails (both sides):
- Height: 865-965 mm (34-38 inches) above ramp surface
- Gripping surface: Circular diameter 32-51 mm (1.25-2 inches)
- Extension: 305 mm (12 inches) beyond top and bottom
- Clearance to wall: 38 mm (1.5 inches)

Pakistan challenges:
- Existing buildings: Steep sites (Islamabad, Murree, Kashmir)
- Switchback ramps required for elevation changes >3m
- Protection from sun/rain: Covered ramps preferred

Base: ICC A117.1 Section 405
IBC 2024 Section 1012

2.4 CURB RAMPS
--------------
Required at:
- Pedestrian crossings
- Parking lot access
- Any route crossing curb

Types:
- Perpendicular curb ramp (most common)
- Parallel curb ramp (constrained sites)
- Blended transition (no change in grade)

Slope: Maximum 1:12 (8.33%)
Width: Minimum 1,220 mm (48 inches) excluding flared sides
Sides: If not protected by handrails/guardrails, flared sides max 1:10 slope

Detectable warning surface:
- Truncated domes (diameter 23 mm, spacing 60 mm)
- Contrasting color (visual impairment)
- Entire curb ramp width, 610 mm (24 inches) depth
- Material: Durable (concrete, metal, composite)

Pakistan implementation:
- Karachi: Curb ramps pilot project 2018 (limited deployment)
- Lahore: Ramps at major intersections (Mall Road, Jail Road)
- Challenge: Encroachments, street vendors blocking ramps

Base: ICC A117.1 Section 406

================================================================================
SECTION 3: BUILDING ENTRANCES AND DOORS
================================================================================

3.1 ACCESSIBLE ENTRANCES
-------------------------
Minimum number:
- 1-story building: ≥60% of public entrances
- Multi-story: ≥1 accessible entrance per accessible level

Requirements:
- Connected to accessible route
- Same level of security (if controlled entry)
- Proximity to accessible parking

Mosques (Pakistan-specific):
- Separate accessible entrances for men/women sections (if segregated)
- Wudu (ablution) areas: Accessible route + accessible fixtures

Base: IBC 2024 Section 1105.1

3.2 DOORS AND DOORWAYS
----------------------
Clear opening width:
- Minimum: 815 mm (32 inches) with door open 90°
- Preferred: 865 mm (34 inches)
- Double doors: ≥1 leaf meets minimum

Threshold:
- Maximum height: 13 mm (1/2 inch) vertical
- Beveled if >6 mm
- Exterior doors: 19 mm (3/4 inch) allowed (weather seal)

Maneuvering clearances:
Front approach (pull side):
- Latch side clearance: 610 mm (24 inches)
- Depth: 1,525 mm (60 inches)

Front approach (push side):
- Depth: 1,220 mm (48 inches)
- No latch-side clearance required if width ≥915 mm

Hinge approach:
- Pull side: 1,370 mm depth, 915 mm latch-side clearance
- Push side: 1,370 mm depth, 560 mm latch-side clearance

Door hardware:
- Operable with one hand, no tight grasping/pinching/twisting
- Lever handles, U-shaped pulls, push-type mechanisms
- Height: 865-1,220 mm (34-48 inches)

Opening force:
- Interior hinged doors: ≤22 N (5 lbf)
- Sliding/folding doors: ≤22 N
- Fire doors: Minimum needed for rating (typically 30-50 N)

Automatic doors:
- Preferred for high-traffic entrances
- Power-assisted doors: Push-button or motion sensor
- Opening speed: ≥3 seconds from open to 70° position
- Closing speed: ≥5 seconds

Vision panels:
- Required in doors on accessible routes
- Lowest point: ≤1,065 mm (43 inches) above floor

Pakistan context:
- Security gates: Common in residential/commercial - must have accessible leaf
- Charpai/rope beds blocking doorways: Enforcement challenge

Base: ICC A117.1 Sections 404, 504.4
IBC 2024 Section 1010

================================================================================
SECTION 4: VERTICAL ACCESSIBILITY
================================================================================

4.1 ELEVATORS (PASSENGER)
--------------------------
When required:
- Buildings ≥4 stories or >3,660 mm (12 feet) between accessible levels
- Exception: Small buildings <465 m² per floor, <3 stories

Car dimensions:
- Minimum interior: 1,370 mm wide × 1,370 mm deep (center-opening doors)
- Preferred: 2,030 mm wide × 1,370 mm deep (deeper car, better maneuvering)
- Rear-opening doors: 2,030 mm wide × 1,650 mm deep

Door width:
- Clear opening: 815 mm (32 inches) minimum
- Preferred: 915 mm (36 inches)
- Center-opening preferred (power doors)

Call buttons:
- Height: 380-1,220 mm (15-48 inches) centerline
- Minimum size: 19 mm (3/4 inch) diameter
- Raised characters, Braille
- Visual and audible signals

Car controls:
- Height: 380-1,220 mm (15-48 inches)
- Parallel approach: Max 1,370 mm (54 inches) high
- Buttons: Minimum 19 mm diameter
- Raised characters and Braille
- Emergency controls: Tactile, grouped at bottom

Pakistan challenges:
- Frequent power outages: Battery backup for safe landing
- Overcrowding: Enforcement of capacity limits
- Maintenance: Extended downtimes common

Base: ICC A117.1 Section 407
ASME A17.1/CSA B44 Safety Code for Elevators and Escalators

4.2 PLATFORM LIFTS (WHEELCHAIR LIFTS)
--------------------------------------
Permitted uses:
- Where ramps/elevators not feasible
- Vertical travel ≤3,660 mm (12 feet)
- Small occupancy loads

Types:
- Vertical platform lift (VPL): Straight up/down
- Inclined platform lift (IPL): Along stairway
- Limited use/limited application (LULA) elevator

Platform size:
- Minimum: 915 mm × 1,370 mm (36 × 54 inches)
- Preferred: 1,065 mm × 1,370 mm

Load capacity: Minimum 300 kg (660 lbs)

Safety features:
- Platform barriers/gates (min 1,065 mm high)
- Interlocks (platform won't move if gate open)
- Emergency stop button
- Emergency lowering (manual or battery)

Pakistan application:
- Mosques: Platform lifts for minbar (pulpit) access
- Existing buildings: Retrofits where space limited
- Schools: Access to upper floors (cost-effective vs. elevator)

Base: ICC A117.1 Section 410
ASME A18.1 Safety Standard for Platform Lifts

================================================================================
SECTION 5: TOILET AND BATHING FACILITIES
================================================================================

5.1 GENERAL REQUIREMENTS
------------------------
Where required:
- Each public/common-use toilet room
- Minimum 1 accessible toilet room per sex per floor

Exception: Single-user toilet rooms
- If clustered (≤2 rooms per cluster), ≥50% must be accessible
- Unisex accessible toilet acceptable if equivalent access

Turning space:
- Circular: 1,525 mm (60 inches) diameter
- T-shaped: 915 mm wide × 1,525 mm deep

Pakistan context:
- Mosques: Separate accessible wudu areas + toilets for men/women
- Western vs. Asian-style toilets: Both options where feasible
- Water supply: Hand-held bidet spray (culturally preferred)

Base: IBC 2024 Section 1109.2
ICC A117.1 Section 603

5.2 WATER CLOSETS (TOILETS)
---------------------------
Clear floor space:
- Width: 1,525 mm (60 inches) minimum
- Depth: 1,680 mm (66 inches) wheelchair accessible
- Depth: 1,425 mm (56 inches) ambulatory accessible stall

Seat height:
- Top of seat: 430-485 mm (17-19 inches) above floor
- Standard toilet: 380-405 mm (low for wheelchairs)
- "Comfort height" toilets: 430-485 mm (ideal for accessibility)

Location:
- Centerline: 405-455 mm (16-18 inches) from side wall
- Clearance: ≥455 mm (18 inches) to any obstruction

Grab bars:
Side wall:
- Length: 1,065 mm (42 inches) minimum
- Height: 865-965 mm (34-38 inches)
- Distance from rear wall: Max 305 mm (12 inches)

Rear wall:
- Length: 915 mm (36 inches) minimum
- Height: 865-965 mm
- Extension: 305 mm (12 inches) beyond centerline (both sides if space)

Grab bar specifications:
- Diameter: 32-38 mm (1.25-1.5 inches)
- Clearance from wall: 38 mm (1.5 inches)
- Load capacity: 1,112 N (250 lbf) any direction

Flush controls:
- Location: Open (transfer) side
- Height: ≤1,220 mm (48 inches)
- Operable with one hand, no tight grasping

Toilet paper dispenser:
- Height: 380-1,220 mm (15-48 inches) above floor
- Location: 180 mm (7 inches) minimum in front of water closet
- Not above grab bar

Asian-style toilets (Pakistan):
- Squat-type: Grab bars at 685 mm (27 inches) height
- Raised platform option: 150-200 mm height with steps + handrails

Base: ICC A117.1 Section 604

5.3 LAVATORIES (WASHBASINS)
---------------------------
Height and clearance:
- Rim height: ≤865 mm (34 inches)
- Knee clearance: ≥685 mm (27 inches) height, 205 mm (8 inches) depth
- Toe clearance: ≥230 mm (9 inches) height, 430 mm (17 inches) depth

Clear floor space:
- Width: 760 mm (30 inches) minimum
- Depth: 1,220 mm (48 inches) forward approach

Faucets:
- Lever handles, push-button, electronic (touchless)
- No tight grasping or twisting
- Temperature: Max 49°C (120°F) - scalding prevention

Mirrors:
- Bottom edge reflecting surface: ≤1,015 mm (40 inches) above floor
- Or tilted mirror (usable from wheelchair)

Pakistan context:
- Water conservation: Metering faucets (auto-shutoff) preferred
- Hand-washing: Cultural/religious importance (wudu before prayers)

Base: ICC A117.1 Section 606

5.4 BATHING FACILITIES
----------------------
Transfer-type shower:
- Size: 915 mm × 915 mm (36 × 36 inches)
- Clearance: 915 mm × 1,370 mm adjacent to opening
- Seat: 405-485 mm (16-19 inches) height, folding or fixed
- Grab bars: Three walls, 865-965 mm height

Roll-in shower:
- Size: 915 mm × 1,525 mm (36 × 60 inches) minimum
- No curb/threshold (≤13 mm beveled)
- Seat: Folding, 405-485 mm height (optional for 1,525 mm depth)

Bathtubs (with seat):
- Clear floor space: 760 mm × 1,525 mm (30 × 60 inches)
- Seat: 430-485 mm (17-19 inches) depth, full tub width
- Grab bars: Back wall, foot end

Controls:
- Height: 380-1,220 mm (15-48 inches)
- Location: Between seat and front edge
- Fixed shower spray + hand-held shower (1,525 mm hose)

Pakistan context:
- Showers more common than bathtubs (water conservation)
- Hand-held sprays culturally preferred

Base: ICC A117.1 Sections 607, 608

================================================================================
SECTION 6: KITCHENS AND KITCHENETTES
================================================================================

6.1 ACCESSIBLE KITCHENS (RESIDENTIAL)
--------------------------------------
Work surface:
- Height: 865 mm (34 inches) - portion ≥760 mm (30 inches) length
- Knee clearance below: 685 mm height, 205 mm depth

Sink:
- Depth: ≤165 mm (6.5 inches) bowl depth
- Knee clearance: Same as lavatory
- Faucet: Single-lever or touchless

Cooktop/range:
- Clear floor space: 760 mm × 1,220 mm (forward approach)
- Knee clearance below cooktop preferred
- Controls: Front or side-mounted (not reaching over burners)

Oven:
- Side-opening door preferred (easier wheelchair access)
- Self-cleaning (reduce maintenance reaching)

Refrigerator:
- Side-by-side preferred (50% below 1,370 mm)
- Bottom freezer option

Storage:
- 50% of storage at 380-1,220 mm height
- Pull-out shelves, lazy susans, drawer organizers

Base: ICC A117.1 Section 804

SECTION 7: PARKING AND PASSENGER LOADING
================================================================================

7.1 ACCESSIBLE PARKING SPACES
------------------------------
Minimum number required:

Total parking spaces | Accessible spaces required
1-25                 | 1
26-50                | 2
51-75                | 3
76-100               | 4
101-150              | 5
151-200              | 6
201-300              | 7
301-400              | 8
401-500              | 9
501-1,000            | 2% of total
1,001+               | 20 + 1 per 100 over 1,000

Van-accessible spaces: Minimum 1 per 6 accessible spaces

Dimensions:
- Car space width: 2,440 mm (96 inches)
- Van space width: 2,740 mm (108 inches)
- Access aisle width: 1,525 mm (60 inches) minimum
- Van access aisle: 2,440 mm (96 inches) preferred

Vertical clearance:
- Van spaces: 2,350 mm (98 inches) minimum

Location:
- Closest to accessible entrance
- Maximum travel distance: 60 m (200 feet)
- On shortest accessible route

Surface:
- Slope: ≤1:48 (2%) in all directions
- Firm, stable, slip-resistant

Signage:
- International Symbol of Accessibility (ISA)
- Minimum height: 1,525 mm (60 inches) to bottom of sign
- "Van Accessible" sign where applicable

Pakistan challenges:
- Enforcement: Accessible spaces often occupied by non-disabled
- Street parking: Lack of designated accessible spaces
- Markets/bazaars: No accessible parking infrastructure

Base: IBC 2024 Section 1106
ICC A117.1 Section 502

7.2 PASSENGER LOADING ZONES
----------------------------
Dimensions:
- Access aisle width: 1,525 mm (60 inches) minimum
- Length: 6,100 mm (20 feet) minimum
- Full-length parallel to vehicle space

Vertical clearance:
- 2,900 mm (114 inches) minimum

Required at:
- Hotels, motels (valet parking)
- Airports, bus terminals, train stations
- Hospitals, medical facilities

Pakistan context:
- Rickshaws/Qinqi accessibility adaptations pilot (Lahore 2019)
- Paratransit services limited (NGOs: Aao Chalein Foundation, Al-Mustafa Welfare)

Base: ICC A117.1 Section 503

================================================================================
SECTION 8: SIGNAGE AND WAYFINDING
================================================================================

8.1 VISUAL CHARACTERS
---------------------
Character height:
- Overhead signs (>2,130 mm): 75 mm (3 inches) minimum
- Door/room signs: 40-50 mm (1.6-2 inches)
- Proportional to viewing distance

Contrast:
- Light characters on dark background or vice versa
- Minimum 70% contrast ratio

Finish:
- Matte or eggshell (non-glare)

Font:
- Sans-serif (Arial, Helvetica, Calibri)
- No italic, script, narrow, decorative fonts
- Stroke width/height ratio: 1:5 to 1:10

Base: ICC A117.1 Section 703.2

8.2 TACTILE CHARACTERS (BRAILLE)
---------------------------------
Raised characters:
- Height: 0.8-2.0 mm (1/32 - 1/16 inch)
- Upper case only
- Accompanied by Grade 2 Braille

Braille:
- Dome height: 0.6-0.9 mm
- Located below corresponding text
- Separation from text: 9.5 mm (3/8 inch)

Mounting height:
- Centerline: 1,220-1,525 mm (48-60 inches) above floor
- Preferred: 1,370 mm (54 inches)

Location:
- Latch side of door (not on door itself)
- 75-150 mm (3-6 inches) from door frame

Pakistan context:
- Urdu Braille: Different from English (transcription required)
- Low literacy rates (visual impairment): Audio/tactile wayfinding needed

Base: ICC A117.1 Section 703.3

8.3 INTERNATIONAL SYMBOL OF ACCESSIBILITY (ISA)
------------------------------------------------
Required at:
- Accessible parking spaces
- Accessible entrances (if not all entrances accessible)
- Accessible toilet/bathing rooms (where not all are accessible)
- Accessible elevators (if not all elevators accessible)

Display:
- White figure on blue background (Pantone 294C)
- Minimum size: Proportional to viewing distance
- Parking signs: 305 mm × 305 mm (12 × 12 inches) minimum

Base: ICC A117.1 Section 703.6

================================================================================
SECTION 9: COMMUNICATION FEATURES
================================================================================

9.1 ASSISTIVE LISTENING SYSTEMS
--------------------------------
Required at:
- Assembly areas (fixed seating >50, amplified sound)
- Courtrooms
- Meeting rooms (>50 occupancy, audio-amplification)

Minimum number of receivers:
- Seating 50-200: 2 receivers
- 201-500: 2 + 1 per 25 seats over 200
- 501-1,000: 2 + 1 per 33 seats over 500
- 1,001-2,000: 35 + 1 per 50 seats over 1,000

Types:
- Induction loop: Magnetic field (hearing aid compatible)
- FM system: Radio frequency transmission
- Infrared (IR): Line-of-sight, confidential

Signage:
- International symbol of access for hearing loss
- "Assistive Listening Available" sign

Pakistan context:
- Mosques: Friday sermons (Jummah) - large assemblies
- Wedding halls (Shaadi halls): High ambient noise challenges

Base: ICC A117.1 Section 706

9.2 TTY/TDD (TEXT TELEPHONES)
------------------------------
Public telephones:
- Where ≥4 pay phones: ≥1 with TTY
- Hospitals: ≥1 TTY per floor with public phones

Superseded by modern technology:
- Video relay service (VRS)
- IP relay, captioned telephones
- Smartphones with text/video capability

Pakistan context:
- Mobile penetration: 87% (2023) - SMS/WhatsApp common
- Pakistan Sign Language (PSL): 250,000 users
- Video interpretation services emerging (NGOs)

Base: ICC A117.1 Section 704.4

================================================================================
SECTION 10: DWELLING AND SLEEPING UNITS
================================================================================

10.1 MULTI-FAMILY RESIDENTIAL
------------------------------
Type A units (fully accessible):
- Minimum 2% of total units (>20 units per building)
- All rooms/spaces on accessible route
- Accessible kitchen, bathrooms
- Reinforced grab bar locations

Type B units (adaptable):
- Minimum 5% of total units (>4 units per building, ≥1 elevator)
- Primary entrance on accessible route
- Accessible route through unit
- Usable kitchens/bathrooms
- Reinforced grab bar locations (not installed)

Distribution:
- Mix of unit types (studio, 1BR, 2BR, 3BR)
- Proportional to general occupancy

Pakistan context:
- Apartments (flats): Growing trend in Karachi, Lahore, Islamabad
- Bahria Town, DHA developments: Accessibility provisions minimal
- Accessible housing shortage critical

Base: IBC 2024 Section 1107
ICC A117.1 Sections 1003, 1004

10.2 HOTELS AND TRANSIENT LODGING
---------------------------------
Minimum accessible guest rooms:

Total rooms | Accessible rooms | Roll-in showers
1-25        | 1                | 0
26-50       | 2                | 0
51-75       | 4                | 1
76-100      | 5                | 1
101-150     | 7                | 2
151-200     | 8                | 3
201-300     | 10               | 4
301-400     | 12               | 5
401-500     | 13               | 6
501-1,000   | 3% of total      | 10% of accessible
1,001+      | 30 + 2 per 100   | 10% of accessible

Communication features:
- Visual alarms (smoke, fire)
- Visual notification (door knock, phone ring)
- TTY or equivalent

Dispersion:
- Accessible rooms distributed among room types, rates, amenities
- Not all on ground floor (dignity)

Pakistan hotels:
- Marriott, Serena, PC Hotels: International accessibility standards
- Local hotels: Limited compliance
- Murree, Nathia Gali resorts: Steep terrain challenges

Base: IBC 2024 Section 1107.6
ICC A117.1 Section 805

SECTION 11: PAKISTAN-SPECIFIC IMPLEMENTATION
================================================================================

11.1 MOSQUE ACCESSIBILITY
--------------------------
Prayer halls:
- Accessible route to prayer area (men's and women's sections)
- Wheelchair-accessible spaces: Front rows (elderly/disabled priority)
- Minimum 2% of worshipper capacity

Wudu (ablution) areas:
- Accessible sinks: Knee clearance, lever faucets
- Seats for foot washing (elderly, mobility impairments)
- Grab bars for balance

Minbar (pulpit):
- Platform lift or ramp access for imam
- Existing mosques: Retrofits required

Footwear storage:
- Accessible shoe racks (380-1,220 mm height)

Pakistan examples:
- Faisal Mosque, Islamabad: Partial accessibility (ramps, elevators)
- Badshahi Mosque, Lahore: Historic structure, accessibility limited
- New mosque construction (2020+): Accessibility code compliance mandatory

Base: Punjab Barrier-Free Access Rules 2021 Section 9
Pakistan Green Building Code 2023 Appendix D

11.2 EDUCATIONAL FACILITIES
----------------------------
Schools and universities:
- Accessible route to all classrooms, labs, libraries
- Accessible toilet rooms on each floor
- Elevators/ramps for multi-story buildings
- 5% accessible seating in lecture halls, auditoriums

Science labs:
- Adjustable-height lab benches (selected stations)
- Clear floor space for wheelchair access
- Emergency shutoffs within reach

Playgrounds:
- Accessible routes to play equipment
- Ground-level play components
- Transfer platforms for elevated equipment

Pakistan challenges:
- Public schools: Poor infrastructure, no accessibility
- Private schools: Variable (Roots, Beaconhouse, City School improving)
- Universities: LUMS, NUST, IBA retrofitting older buildings

Base: Punjab Barrier-Free Access Rules 2021 Section 7

11.3 HEALTHCARE FACILITIES
---------------------------
Hospitals:
- Accessible entrance (emergency + main)
- Accessible route to all departments
- 10% accessible patient rooms
- Accessible examination rooms, imaging, therapy

Medical equipment:
- Adjustable-height examination tables (480-610 mm range)
- Wheelchair-accessible scales
- Accessible diagnostic equipment (mammography, X-ray)

Waiting areas:
- 5% wheelchair seating spaces (dispersed)
- Accessible queuing/ticketing systems

Pakistan context:
- Public hospitals (DHQ, THQ): Limited accessibility
- Private hospitals (Shaukat Khanum, Aga Khan, Shifa): Better compliance
- Rural health centers: Accessibility almost non-existent

Base: Punjab Barrier-Free Access Rules 2021 Section 8
