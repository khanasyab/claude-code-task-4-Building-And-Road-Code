SECTION 1: GENERAL PROVISIONS
================================================================================

1.1 SCOPE (BNR 1994 Para 3.1)
   - Government-owned buildings
   - Government-rented buildings
   - Residential quarters
   - Office accommodations
   - Special buildings (rest houses, circuit houses, dak bungalows)

1.2 OFFICER ACCOUNTABILITY (Para 3.1-3.3)
   - Officer-in-charge responsible for condition
   - Fire safety compliance
   - Insurance maintenance
   - Annual inspection mandatory
   - Defect reporting within 7 days

1.3 BUILDING REGISTERS (Para 3.28-3.29)
   - Register of buildings maintained
   - Details: Location, area, value, occupant
   - Updated quarterly
   - Digital database integration (2025)

================================================================================
SECTION 2: FIXTURES AND FURNITURE
================================================================================

2.1 DISTINCTION (BNR 1994 Para 3.4-3.8)

A. FIXTURES (B&R Department Responsibility)
   - Punkahs (ceiling fans - modern interpretation)
   - Fixed heaters and cooling systems
   - Built-in cupboards and shelving
   - Attached bathroom fittings
   - Window blinds and curtains (fixed)
   - Kitchen built-in equipment
   - Fixed lighting installations

B. FURNITURE (Occupying Department Responsibility)
   - Movable tables, chairs, desks
   - Portable equipment
   - Non-fixed cabinets
   - Movable appliances
   - Loose furnishings

2.2 SPECIAL PROVISIONS

A. GOVERNOR'S RESIDENCES (Para 3.6)
   - Military Secretary administration
   - Special maintenance standards
   - Enhanced security provisions

B. MINISTERIAL RESIDENCES (Para 3.7)
   - Protocol Department coordination
   - Higher maintenance standards
   - Rapid response maintenance

C. DAK BUNGALOWS AND REST HOUSES (Para 3.8)
   - Furniture included as fixtures
   - Maintained by B&R Department
   - Caretaker arrangements
   - Booking systems

================================================================================
SECTION 3: ACQUISITION AND DISPOSAL
================================================================================

3.1 PURCHASE OF BUILDINGS (BNR 1994 Para 3.9)

A. PRE-PURCHASE REQUIREMENTS
   - Government approval mandatory
   - Technical valuation report
   - Structural assessment
   - Title verification
   - Cost-benefit analysis

B. VALUATION METHODOLOGY
   - Current market rates
   - Structural condition assessment
   - Location and accessibility
   - Future maintenance costs
   - Depreciation calculation

3.2 SALE OF BUILDINGS (Para 3.10)

A. DISPOSAL CONDITIONS
   - Government approval required
   - Other departments offered first
   - Public auction if no departmental interest
   - Demolition statement (if demolishing)
   - Environmental clearance for demolition

B. DEMOLITION PROCEDURES
   - Salvage material assessment
   - Safe demolition plan
   - Asbestos survey (if pre-1990 building)
   - Waste disposal plan
   - Site restoration

================================================================================
SECTION 4: RENTAL ACCOMMODATIONS
================================================================================

4.1 HIRE OF OFFICE ACCOMMODATION (BNR 1994 Para 3.11)

A. PRE-REQUISITES
   - Non-accommodation certificate mandatory
   - Certifies no government building available
   - Rental reasonableness verification
   - Market survey (3 comparable properties)

B. LEASE PROCEDURES
   - Lease deed execution
   - Government approval (above Rs. 500,000 annual)
   - Maximum 3-year term (renewable)
   - Sub-letting approval if required

C. RENTAL ASSESSMENT
   - Market rate surveys
   - Location premium/discount
   - Condition adjustment
   - Facilities included
   - Municipal tax treatment

4.2 RENTING OF GOVERNMENT BUILDINGS (Para 3.12-3.22)

A. RENTAL CALCULATION (Para 3.13-3.14)
   [Pakistan FR 45-A Integration]

   Standard Rent Formula:
   - 10% of capital value per annum (basic principle)
   - Adjusted for age and condition
   - Market rate comparison
   - Municipal taxes separately charged
   - Furniture charges (if furnished)

B. RENTAL CLASSES (Para 3.15-3.16)
   - Class A: Prime locations, excellent condition
   - Class B: Good locations, good condition
   - Class C: Average locations, fair condition
   - Special rates for ceremonial buildings

C. RENT RECOVERY (Para 3.17-3.22)
   - Monthly deduction from salary (government employees)
   - Advance payment (non-government tenants)
   - Penalty for late payment: 10% per annum
   - Disconnection of utilities after 2 months default

4.3 RESIDENTIAL BUILDINGS FOR OFFICIALS (BNR 1989 Section B, 3.31-3.49)

A. CLASSIFICATION
   - Type I: Senior officers (>Grade 19)
   - Type II: Mid-level officers (Grade 17-18)
   - Type III: Junior officers (Grade 14-16)
   - Type IV: Supporting staff (Grade 1-13)

B. ALLOCATION CRITERIA
   - Seniority in grade
   - Family size
   - Station of posting
   - Availability
   - Waiting list maintained

C. MAINTENANCE
   - Major repairs: B&R Department
   - Minor repairs: Occupant responsibility
   - Boundary walls and drainage: B&R Department
   - Internal decoration: Occupant (or B&R by recovery)

SECTION 5: MAINTENANCE STANDARDS
================================================================================
[Enhancement: USA IBC Property Maintenance Code]

5.1 STRUCTURAL REQUIREMENTS

A. FOUNDATIONS AND STRUCTURE
   - Free from deterioration
   - No structural cracks requiring repair
   - Settlement monitoring
   - Annual structural inspection

B. EXTERIOR WALLS
   - Weather-tight and rodent-proof
   - Painted/finished to prevent decay
   - Drainage away from building

C. ROOFS
   - Water-tight and weather-proof
   - Gutters and downspouts functional
   - Roof covering intact
   - Chimney stacks safe

5.2 INTERIOR REQUIREMENTS

A. FLOORS, WALLS, CEILINGS
   - Sound and good repair
   - Sanitary condition
   - No dampness
   - Painted/finished appropriately

B. STAIRWAYS AND RAILINGS
   - Safe and sound
   - Handrails secure
   - Adequate lighting
   - Non-slip surfaces

5.3 FACILITIES REQUIREMENTS

A. SANITATION (Para 3.23-3.24)
   - Adequate toilet facilities
   - Ratio: 1 WC per 10 persons (offices)
   - Separate facilities for men/women
   - Hot water in bathrooms (cold climates)
   - Proper ventilation

B. WATER SUPPLY (Para 3.25)
   - Potable water available
   - Adequate pressure
   - Hot water system maintained
   - Storage tanks cleaned bi-annually

C. ELECTRICAL INSTALLATIONS (Para 3.26)
   - Safe and adequate
   - Circuit breakers functional
   - Proper earthing
   - Emergency lighting (public buildings)
   - Energy-efficient fixtures (2025 standard)

D. DRAINAGE (Para 3.23)
   - Proper drainage system
   - Septic tanks/sewerage connection
   - No foul odors or blockages
   - Soakage pits properly maintained

5.4 SPACE STANDARDS (Para 3.30)
   - Minimum 54 sq ft per person (offices)
   - Adequate ceiling height: 10 ft minimum
   - Natural light and ventilation
   - Workspace ergonomics

SECTION 6: TAXES AND CHARGES
================================================================================

6.1 MUNICIPAL TAXES (BNR 1994 Para 3.27)

A. TAX RESPONSIBILITY
   - Property tax: Government (if owner)
   - Water charges: Occupant department
   - Sewerage charges: Government
   - Conservancy: Occupant department

B. RECOVERY PROCEDURES
   - Deducted from rent (if rented to government)
   - Direct billing (if rented to private)
   - Annual reconciliation

SECTION 7: INSPECTION AND REPORTING
================================================================================

7.1 INSPECTION FREQUENCY (Para 3.28-3.29)

A. ROUTINE INSPECTIONS
   - Monthly: Building supervisor
   - Quarterly: Sub-Divisional Officer
   - Annually: Divisional Officer

B. SPECIAL INSPECTIONS
   - After natural disasters (earthquake, floods)
   - Before VIP occupancy
   - After major repairs
   - Complaint-based

7.2 DEFECT REPORTING
   - Immediate: Life-safety issues
   - Within 7 days: Structural concerns
   - Monthly report: Minor defects
   - Photographic documentation

7.3 BUILDING CONDITION RATING
   [Enhancement: International standards]
   - Excellent: As new, no defects
   - Good: Minor defects, routine maintenance
   - Fair: Moderate defects, programmed repairs needed
   - Poor: Significant defects, urgent repairs needed
   - Very Poor: Structural concerns, major rehabilitation required

================================================================================
SECTION 8: FIRE SAFETY AND EMERGENCY
================================================================================

8.1 FIRE SAFETY REQUIREMENTS (Para 3.2)

A. FIRE PREVENTION
   - Fire extinguishers (appropriate type and quantity)
   - No combustible storage in prohibited areas
   - Electrical safety compliance
   - Smoking restrictions enforced
   - Kitchen fire safety (offices with pantries)

B. FIRE FIGHTING EQUIPMENT
   - Portable extinguishers: 1 per 200 sq m
   - Fire blankets in kitchens
   - Fire hose reels (buildings >10m height)
   - Hydrant connections maintained

C. EVACUATION PLANNING
   - Emergency exits clearly marked
   - Evacuation plans displayed
   - Annual fire drills
   - Assembly points designated

8.2 INSURANCE (Para 3.3)

A. MANDATORY INSURANCE
   - Fire insurance for all government buildings
   - Sum insured: Replacement value
   - Annual renewal mandatory
   - Claims processed promptly

B. ADDITIONAL COVERAGE
   - Earthquake insurance (seismic zones)
   - Flood insurance (flood-prone areas)
   - Terrorism coverage (high-security buildings)

SECTION 9: SPECIAL BUILDINGS
================================================================================

9.1 REST HOUSES AND DAK BUNGALOWS (Para 3.8)

A. CLASSIFICATION
   - Circuit Houses (for officers Grade 18+)
   - Inspection Houses (for officers Grade 17+)
   - Dak Bungalows (for all government servants)
   - Rest Rooms (for travelers)

B. FACILITIES PROVIDED
   - Furnished accommodation
   - Caretaker services
   - Basic utilities included
   - Kitchen facilities

C. BOOKING AND CHARGES
   - Advance booking system (online 2025)
   - Priority: Government officials on duty
   - Charges per room per night
   - Extra charges: Meals, special services

9.2 WORKSHOPS AND STORES (Chapter IV reference)
   - Adequately lit and ventilated
   - Fire safety compliance
   - Secure storage
   - Proper material segregation

SECTION 10: SUSTAINABLE BUILDING MANAGEMENT
================================================================================
[Enhancement: 2025 Sustainability Standards]

10.1 ENERGY EFFICIENCY
   - LED lighting retrofits (ongoing program)
   - Solar water heaters (where feasible)
   - Energy audits (buildings >5000 sq m)
   - Smart thermostats and controls

10.2 WATER CONSERVATION
   - Low-flow fixtures
   - Rainwater harvesting (new construction)
   - Gray water reuse (feasible locations)
   - Leak detection programs

10.3 WASTE MANAGEMENT
   - Segregated waste bins
   - Recycling programs
   - E-waste collection
   - Composting (where applicable)
