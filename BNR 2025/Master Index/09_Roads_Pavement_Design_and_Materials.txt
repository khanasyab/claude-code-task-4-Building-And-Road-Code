    - Aging resistance specifications

3.2 BITUMINOUS MIX DESIGNS
    --------
    - Marshal Method
    - Superpave Method
    - Gyratory compaction specifications

    - Voids in mineral aggregate (VMA)
    - Voids filled with bitumen (VFB)
    - Rutting and cracking resistance
    - Moisture susceptibility (ATSM-T283)

    - Water-cement ratio control
    - Aggregate specifications (coarse and fine)
    - Maximum aggregate size limitations
    - Slump requirements for construction
    - Compressive strength requirements (28-day)
    - Flexural strength specifications
    - Shrinkage control measures
    - Joint specifications and spacing
    - Load transfer efficiency (dowels/aggregates)
    - Air entrainment for frost protection
    - Alkali-silica reaction (ASR) prevention

3.4 BASE COURSE MATERIALS

4. MODERN PAVEMENT TECHNOLOGIES & INNOVATIONS
================================================================================

⭐ 4.1 WARM MIX ASPHALT (WMA)
    --------
    - Definition: Asphalt mixes produced and placed at lower temperatures
    - Benefits:
        - Reduced energy consumption (10-30% lower temperatures)
        - Lower greenhouse gas emissions
        - Improved workability and paving conditions
        - Extended haul distances
        - Better safety for workers
        - Enhanced durability

    - Technologies:
        - Foaming-based (injection of water, steam)
        - Additive-based (waxes, emulsions, adhesive promoters)
        - Organic/synthetic polymers

    - Performance specifications:
        - Temperature reduction: 15-30°C below conventional HMA
        - Compaction standards similar to conventional mixes
        - Rutting resistance comparable or superior
        - Moisture damage performance equivalent or better
        - Fatigue cracking resistance maintained or improved

    - Environmental benefits:
        - CO2 emissions reduction: 15-20% per ton
        - Fuel consumption savings
        - Worker heat stress reduction
        - Reduced volatile organic compound (VOC) emissions

⭐ 4.2 RECYCLED MATERIALS IN PAVEMENTS
    --------
    - Reclaimed Asphalt Pavement (RAP):
        - Incorporation rates: 0-100% depending on specifications
        - Testing of RAP binder properties
        - Blending design with virgin materials
        - Performance impact assessment
        - Long-term durability evaluation
        - Cost-benefit analysis
        - Supply chain development
        - Quality consistency requirements

    - Recycled Asphalt Shingles (RAS):
        - Incorporation in asphalt mixes
        - Binder extraction and testing
        - Blending compatibility
        - Performance expectations
        - Environmental benefits

    - Recycled Plastic in Pavements (RP):
        - Plastic-modified binders
        - Aggregate coating with plastic
        - Plastic aggregate replacement
        - Performance improvements
        - Sustainability benefits
        - Commercial product developments

    - Recycled Concrete Aggregate (RCA):
        - Use as base/sub-base material
        - Recycled concrete material (RCM) specifications
        - CBR and strength characteristics
        - Durability testing
        - Environmental impact reduction
        - Cost effectiveness

⭐ 4.3 PERMEABLE PAVEMENTS
    --------
    - Porous Asphalt Pavements:
        - Open-graded mix design (8-12% air voids)
        - Binder selection and requirements
        - Permeability testing standards
        - Clogging prevention measures

        - Lifespan considerations

    - Pervious Concrete:

    - Permeable Pavers:

5. TESTING AND QUALITY ASSURANCE STANDARDS
================================================================================

5.1 BITUMINOUS MATERIAL TESTING
    --------
    - Binder Tests:
        - Penetration (ASTM D5)
        - Softening Point (ASTM D36)
        - Ductility (ASTM D113)
        - Viscosity (ASTM D4402)
        - Flash Point (ASTM D92)

    - Mix Design Tests:
        - Marshall Stability (ASTM D1559)
        - Superpave Mix Design
        - Gyratory Compaction
        - Resilient Modulus (AASHTO T307)
        - Dynamic Modulus (AASHTO TP 62)
        - Moisture Susceptibility (AASHTO T283)
        - Raveling and Stripping

    - Field Testing:
        - Density and void determination
        - Core sampling and analysis
        - Deflection measurements (FWD, Benkelman Beam)
        - Texture depth (Sand Patch, UTM)
        - Skid resistance (Skid Tester)
        - Rut depth measurement
        - Pavement distress surveys

5.2 CONCRETE MATERIAL TESTING
    --------
    - Fresh Concrete:
        - Slump (ASTM C143)
        - Compaction factor
        - VeBe consistency
        - Air content determination
        - Temperature monitoring

    - Hardened Concrete:
        - Compressive strength (28-day, early strength)
        - Flexural strength (modulus of rupture)
        - Tensile strength (split cylinder test)
        - Modulus of elasticity
        - Drying shrinkage (ASTM C157)
        - ASR expansion (ASTM C1260)
        - Rapid chloride permeability test (RCPT)
        - Durability studies

    - Field Testing:
        - Schmidt hammer (rebound number)
        - Ultrasonic pulse velocity
        - Falling weight deflectometer (FWD)
        - Ground-penetrating radar (GPR)
        - Laser crack measurement

5.3 AGGREGATE TESTING
    --------
    - Grading and Size:
        - Sieve analysis (ASTM C136)
        - Gradation verification

    - Strength:
        - Los Angeles Abrasion (ASTM C131)
        - Soundness (magnesium sulfate, ASTM C88)
        - Aggregate Crushing Value (ACV)

    - Shape:
        - Flakiness Index (IS 2386 Part 1)
        - Elongation Index (IS 2386 Part 1)

    - Surface Properties:
        - Polished Stone Value (PSV)
        - Friction Angle
        - Micro-Deval abrasion test

    - Durability:
        - Water absorption (ASTM C127/C128)
        - Organic matter content
        - Clay and dust content (wash test)

5.4 SOIL AND SUBGRADE TESTING
    --------
    - Classification Tests:
        - Atterberg limits (liquid limit, plastic limit)
        - Grain size analysis
        - Soil classification (USCS, AASHTO)

    - Strength and Compaction:
        - California Bearing Ratio (CBR) - ASTM D1883
        - Modified Proctor compaction test
        - Standard Proctor compaction test
        - Maximum dry density
        - Optimum moisture content

    - Durability:
        - Expansion tests
        - Soaked CBR
        - Repeat load triaxial test
        - Resilient modulus determination
