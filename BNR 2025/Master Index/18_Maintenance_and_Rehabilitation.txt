SECTION 1: MAINTENANCE MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

1.1 LEVELS OF MAINTENANCE
--------------------------
ROUTINE MAINTENANCE:
- Frequency: Daily, weekly, monthly
- Activities: Cleaning drains, filling potholes, grass cutting, sign repair
- Objective: Keep road operational, prevent rapid deterioration
- Responsibility: NHA regional offices, district highway divisions
- Cost: 1-2% of replacement cost per year

PERIODIC MAINTENANCE:
- Frequency: 3-7 years (flexible pavement), 10-15 years (rigid)
- Activities: Surface resealing, overlay (50-75 mm), joint sealing
- Objective: Restore surface condition, extend service life
- Cost: 5-10% of replacement cost

REHABILITATION:
- Frequency: 10-20 years
- Activities: Major overlay (>100 mm), pavement reconstruction (full-depth)
- Objective: Restore structural capacity, serviceability
- Cost: 30-50% of replacement cost

RECONSTRUCTION:
- Frequency: End of service life (20-30 years)
- Activities: Complete removal and replacement
- Cost: 100% replacement cost

Pakistan backlog:
- Estimated 40% of network requires periodic maintenance (deferred)
- 15% requires rehabilitation (severe deterioration)
- Funding constraints: Maintenance vs. new construction trade-off

Base: IRC:SP:51-2014 Section 2
NHA Road Asset Management Plan 2015

1.2 PAVEMENT MANAGEMENT SYSTEM (PMS)
-------------------------------------
Components:

INVENTORY DATABASE:
- Road sections (chainage, length, width)
- Pavement type (flexible, rigid, composite)
- Construction history (year, layers, materials)
- Traffic (AADT, vehicle composition)

CONDITION ASSESSMENT:
- Visual survey: Distress type, severity, extent
- Roughness: IRI (International Roughness Index) - profilometer
- Structural capacity: FWD (Falling Weight Deflectometer), Benkelman Beam
- Surface properties: Skid resistance, texture depth
- Frequency: Annual (main network), biennial (low-volume roads)

PERFORMANCE MODELING:
- Predict deterioration (pavement condition over time)
- Factors: Age, traffic, climate, maintenance history
- Models: HDM-4 (Highway Development and Management), empirical curves

PRIORITIZATION:
- Ranking: Worst-first (condition-based) or optimize (budget allocation)
- Criteria: Traffic volume, economic importance, safety, equity

WORK PROGRAMMING:
- Multi-year plan (5-10 year horizon)
- Budget scenarios (allocate funds to treatments)
- Output: Annual work program (sections, treatments, costs)

Pakistan PMS status:
- NHA: PMS implemented 2005 (HDM-4 software), updated 2015
- Data collection: Limited (manual surveys, few automated equipment)
- Provincial highways: No systematic PMS (ad-hoc repairs, political influence)
- Need: Strengthened PMS (better data, regular updates, enforced prioritization)

Base: IRC:SP:51-2014 Chapter 3
World Bank PMS Guidelines

SECTION 2: PAVEMENT CONDITION ASSESSMENT
================================================================================

2.1 VISUAL DISTRESS SURVEY
---------------------------
FLEXIBLE PAVEMENT DISTRESSES:

CRACKING:
- Alligator cracking: Interconnected cracks (fatigue, weak base/subgrade)
  - Severity: Low (<10% area), Medium (10-30%), High (>30%)
  - Treatment: Patch (low), overlay (medium), reconstruction (high)

- Longitudinal cracking: Parallel to centerline (thermal, construction joint)
  - Treatment: Crack sealing (width <6 mm), patching (width >6 mm)

- Transverse cracking: Perpendicular to centerline (thermal shrinkage)
  - Treatment: Crack sealing, fog seal

- Block cracking: Regular blocks (thermal cycles, aged binder)
  - Treatment: Overlay, surface treatment

SURFACE DEFORMATION:
- Rutting: Longitudinal depressions in wheel paths (plastic deformation)
  - Severity: <10 mm (low), 10-20 mm (medium), >20 mm (high) - safety concern (water ponding, hydroplaning)
  - Causes: Weak asphalt mix, high traffic, high temperatures
  - Treatment: Mill and replace surface (medium/high), overlay (low)

- Corrugation/Washboarding: Transverse waves (unstable surface, braking/acceleration)
  - Treatment: Mill and replace

- Shoving: Localized bulging (shear failure near intersections, braking zones)
  - Treatment: Remove and replace affected area

SURFACE DEFECTS:
- Potholes: Bowl-shaped holes (water infiltration + traffic)
  - Pakistan: Major safety hazard, very common (poor drainage, delayed repairs)
  - Treatment: Patching (temporary - cold mix, permanent - hot mix)

- Raveling/Weathering: Loss of aggregate from surface (binder aging, poor compaction)
  - Treatment: Fog seal (light), chip seal (moderate), overlay (severe)

- Bleeding: Excess bitumen on surface (over-application, hot climate)
  - Treatment: Sand blotting (light), mill surface (severe)

- Polishing: Loss of skid resistance (aggregate wear, traffic)
  - Treatment: Micro-surfacing, thin overlay

EDGE DEFECTS:
- Edge breaks: Shoulder erosion, pavement edge cracking
  - Treatment: Edge repair, shoulder reconstruction

RIGID PAVEMENT DISTRESSES:

CRACKING:
- Corner breaks: Diagonal cracks from corner (load transfer loss, weak foundation)
  - Treatment: Partial-depth repair (surface crack), full-depth replacement (through-slab)

- Longitudinal/Transverse cracks: Not at joints (thermal stress, subgrade settlement)
  - Treatment: Routing and sealing (width <3 mm), full-depth repair (width >3 mm)

JOINT DEFECTS:
- Joint faulting: Vertical offset at joint (pumping, slab settlement)
  - Severity: <5 mm (low), 5-10 mm (medium), >10 mm (high) - safety/ride quality
  - Treatment: Grinding/slab jacking (medium), joint reconstruction (high)

- Spalling: Concrete breakdown at joint edges (poor joint sealing, freeze-thaw, D-cracking)
  - Treatment: Partial-depth repair (shallow), full-depth (deep spalling)

SURFACE DEFECTS:
- Scaling: Surface deterioration (freeze-thaw - rare Pakistan, poor finishing)
  - Treatment: Overlay, thin bonded concrete

- Polishing: Loss of skid resistance (aggregate wear)
  - Treatment: Grooving, diamond grinding

LOAD TRANSFER:
- Pumping: Subgrade ejection at joints (water + traffic → slab support loss)
  - Signs: Staining at joints, faulting
  - Treatment: Undersealing (grout injection), slab replacement

Pakistan condition (typical):
- National Highways: IRI 3-6 m/km (fair to poor), 30-50% cracked area
- Provincial highways: IRI 6-12 m/km (poor to very poor), 50-80% distressed
- Monsoon worsens: Potholes multiply after rainfall (water infiltration)

Base: IRC:SP:16-2019
ASTM D6433 (PAVER pavement condition index)

2.2 ROUGHNESS MEASUREMENT
--------------------------
International Roughness Index (IRI):
- Units: mm/km or m/km (ride quality indicator)
- Measurement: Profilometer (laser-based), bump integrator (cheaper, less accurate)

IRI classification:

IRI (m/km) | Condition     | Ride quality
<2         | Very good     | Smooth
2-4        | Good          | Comfortable
4-6        | Fair          | Acceptable
6-8        | Poor          | Uncomfortable
>8         | Very poor     | Unacceptable

Pakistan standards (NHA):
- New construction: IRI <2.5 m/km (acceptance threshold)
- Maintenance trigger: IRI >4.0 m/km (periodic maintenance required)
- Rehabilitation trigger: IRI >6.0 m/km

Frequency:
- Main highways: Annual (100% network coverage)
- Secondary roads: Biennial

Equipment:
- Automated: Laser profiler (mounted vehicle, 80-100 km/h speed)
- Manual: Bump integrator, walking profilometer (low-volume roads)

Base: IRC:SP:16-2019
World Bank IRI guidelines

2.3 STRUCTURAL EVALUATION
--------------------------
BENKELMAN BEAM DEFLECTION (BBD):
- Principle: Measure pavement deflection under moving truck (rear axle unloading)
- Equipment: Beam with dial gauge, probe rests on pavement
- Load: 8,160 kg single axle (standard)
- Output: Deflection in mm (0.01 mm accuracy)

Interpretation:
- Low deflection (<1.0 mm): Strong pavement
- Medium (1.0-2.0 mm): Adequate, monitor
- High (>2.0 mm): Weak, overlay/rehabilitation needed

Frequency: 50 m intervals (both wheel paths)

FALLING WEIGHT DEFLECTOMETER (FWD):
- Principle: Drop weight creates impulse load, measure deflection (multiple geophones)
- Advantages: Faster than BBD (stationary test, 3-5 min per point), more data (deflection bowl)
- Output: Deflection vs. distance (backcalculate layer moduli)

Pakistan usage:
- BBD more common (cheaper equipment, simpler)
- FWD limited (NHA major projects, CPEC routes)

Base: IRC:81-1997
ASTM D4695 (FWD testing)

2.4 SKID RESISTANCE
--------------------
Measurement methods:
- British Pendulum Tester (BPT): Portable, spot measurements
- Locked-wheel skid tester: Continuous, towed trailer at 50 km/h
- Grip Tester: Continuous, self-contained vehicle

Skid Resistance Value (SRV):
- BPT: 0-100 scale (friction coefficient × 100)

Classification:

SRV    | Skid resistance | Action
>65    | Very good       | None
55-65  | Good            | Monitor
45-55  | Moderate        | Treatment within 1 year
35-45  | Poor            | Immediate treatment (safety risk)
<35    | Very poor       | Urgent treatment (high crash risk)

Locations requiring high skid resistance:
- Approaches to signals, intersections (braking zones)
- Sharp curves, steep grades
- Pedestrian crossings

Treatment:
- Surface dressing: High-friction aggregate (calcined bauxite, crushed rock)
- Micro-surfacing: Polymer-modified emulsion + aggregate
- Thin overlay: Open-graded or gap-graded mix

Pakistan practice:
- Skid resistance testing rare (few equipment, not routinely done)
- Polished pavements common (wet weather accidents)

Base: DMRB CS 230
IRC:SP:16-2019 Appendix

SECTION 3: PAVEMENT MAINTENANCE TREATMENTS
================================================================================

3.1 ROUTINE MAINTENANCE TREATMENTS
-----------------------------------
POTHOLE PATCHING:

Temporary (emergency):
- Material: Cold mix asphalt (pre-mixed, no heating required)
- Procedure: Clean hole, remove loose material, fill, compact (hand tamper)
- Life: 3-6 months (monsoon damage common)
- Pakistan: Very common (reactive repairs, backlog accumulates)

Permanent:
- Material: Hot mix asphalt
- Procedure: Square cut edges (saw/jackhammer), clean, tack coat, fill (compacted in layers ≤50 mm), compact (vibratory roller)
- Life: 2-5 years (if properly done)

Throw-and-roll (emergency):
- Dump cold mix, allow traffic to compact (poor quality, short life)
- Not recommended (waste of material) but common Pakistan

CRACK SEALING:
- Cracks <6 mm width
- Material: Hot-pour bituminous sealant (rubberized)
- Procedure: Clean crack (compressed air, wire brush), heat (crack dry), apply sealant (pour box, minimum 5 mm depth)
- Life: 2-3 years

FOG SEAL:
- Thin application of diluted asphalt emulsion (SS-1, diluted 1:1 with water)
- Purpose: Seal small cracks, bind loose aggregate (raveling prevention)
- Application rate: 0.5-1.0 L/m²
- Life: 2-3 years

Pakistan challenges:
- Delayed repairs (potholes left for weeks/months - expand rapidly)
- Poor-quality materials (cold mix inferior, sealant not rubberized)
- Lack of equipment (crack sealing guns rare, manual application ineffective)

Base: IRC:SP:51-2014 Section 5

3.2 PERIODIC MAINTENANCE TREATMENTS
------------------------------------
SURFACE DRESSING (CHIP SEAL):
- Single layer: Bitumen spray (0.8-1.2 kg/m²) + aggregate chips (10-14 mm, 8-12 kg/m²)
- Double layer: Two applications (coarse + fine aggregate)
- Equipment: Bitumen pressure distributor, aggregate spreader, pneumatic roller
- Life: 5-7 years
- Cost: Rs 150-250 per m² (economical for low-volume roads)

SLURRY SEAL:
- Mixture: Emulsion (10-16%) + aggregate (0-6 mm grading) + filler + water
- Application: Squeegee box (3-6 mm thickness)
- Curing: 2-4 hours (traffic opening)
- Life: 3-5 years

MICRO-SURFACING:
- Similar to slurry seal but polymer-modified emulsion (quick-set)
- Application: Paver box, <12 mm thickness
- Can fill ruts (multiple passes, build up)
- Life: 5-7 years

THIN OVERLAY (40-50 mm):
- Milling: Remove existing surface (10-20 mm) for texture, level correction
- Overlay: Dense Bituminous Concrete (DBC) or Semi-Dense Bituminous Concrete (SDBC)
- Tack coat: Emulsion (0.25-0.35 kg/m²) ensure bond
- Life: 7-10 years
- Cost: Rs 800-1,200 per m²

JOINT SEALING (Rigid pavement):
- Remove old sealant (router, grinder)
- Clean joint (compressed air, vacuum)
- Backer rod: Compressible foam (diameter 1.25× joint width, depth control)
- Sealant: Hot-pour (bituminous) or cold-pour (polysulfide, polyurethane)
- Shape factor: Width:depth ratio 2:1 (proper adhesion)
- Life: 5-10 years

Pakistan application:
- Surface dressing underused (prefer overlay - higher cost but longer life)
- Joint sealing neglected (rigid pavements deteriorate prematurely)

Base: IRC:SP:51-2014 Section 6

3.3 REHABILITATION TREATMENTS
------------------------------
THICK OVERLAY (75-150 mm):
- Structural overlay: Restore pavement capacity
- Design: Based on deflection (BBD or FWD), remaining life
- Milling: Optional (maintain vertical clearances - bridges, underpasses)
- Life: 10-15 years

FULL-DEPTH RECLAMATION (FDR):
- Pulverize existing pavement (reclaimer/pulverizer 200-300 mm depth)
- Stabilize: Add cement (3-5%) or lime (2-4%), mix, compact
- New surface: Bituminous or concrete overlay
- Benefits: Reuse material, improve base strength, cost-effective
- Life: 15-20 years (with proper surface)

Pakistan usage: Limited (equipment not widely available, technique unfamiliar)

RECONSTRUCTION:
- Remove existing pavement (full-depth)
- Reconstruct: New sub-base, base, surface (per original design or upgraded)
- Required: Pavement failed, overlay not feasible (structural failure, poor drainage)
- Cost: Highest (Rs 5,000-10,000 per m² depending on thickness, materials)

Base: IRC:81-1997
IRC:SP:51-2014 Section 7

SECTION 4: DRAINAGE MAINTENANCE
================================================================================

4.1 SURFACE DRAINAGE
---------------------
CLEANING:
- Frequency: Before monsoon (April-May), during monsoon (monthly)
- Activities:
  - Remove silt, debris, vegetation from roadside drains
  - Clear culvert inlets/outlets (blockage → overtopping, road flooding)
  - Clean catch basins, grates (urban roads)

Equipment:
- Manual: Shovels, wheelbarrows (labor-intensive, common Pakistan)
- Mechanical: Vacuum trucks, excavators (faster, expensive)

REPAIRS:
- Masonry drains: Re-pointing joints, replace displaced stones
- Concrete drains: Repair cracks, spalled sections
- Erosion control: Riprap, vegetation (channel bed/sides)

Pakistan monsoon issues:
- Blocked drains common (siltation, encroachments - garbage dumping)
- Flash flooding (road inundation, pavement undermining)
- Delayed cleaning (after monsoon - damage already occurred)

Base: IRC:SP:51-2014 Section 5.3

4.2 SUBSURFACE DRAINAGE
------------------------
INSPECTION:
- Longitudinal drains: Check outlets (flowing or blocked)
- Cross-drains: Walk through (if large enough) or flush test

MAINTENANCE:
- Flushing: High-pressure water (remove sediment)
- Rodding: Push rod through pipe (clear blockages)
- Replacement: If collapsed, severely damaged

Pakistan challenges:
- Subsurface drains often not provided (cost-cutting during construction)
- Where provided, maintenance neglected (out of sight, out of mind)

Base: IRC:SP:51-2014 Section 5.3

SECTION 5: BRIDGE INSPECTION AND MAINTENANCE
================================================================================

5.1 BRIDGE INSPECTION PROGRAM
------------------------------
ROUTINE INSPECTION:
- Frequency: Annual (all bridges)
- Scope: Visual walkover (accessible areas)
- Personnel: Bridge inspector (trained engineer/technician)
- Checklist: Deck, superstructure, bearings, substructure, foundations, appurtenances
- Output: Inspection report (defects noted, photos, condition rating)

PRINCIPAL INSPECTION:
- Frequency: 5 years (normal bridges), 3 years (critical/old bridges)
- Scope: Detailed examination (close-up, access equipment - ladder, snooper, boat)
- Personnel: Senior bridge engineer
- Testing: Concrete cover, carbonation, chloride content (corrosion assessment)
- Output: Detailed report (structural assessment, repair recommendations, cost estimates)

SPECIAL INSPECTION:
- Triggered by: Event (flood, earthquake, vehicle impact), unusual defect
- Scope: Specific issue investigation (e.g., bearing malfunction, crack propagation)
- Personnel: Specialist (structural, geotechnical, materials)

Pakistan status:
- NHA: Inspection program established (2005), ~14,500 bridges
- Provincial bridges: Irregular inspections (lack of trained personnel, equipment)
- Backlog: Many bridges not inspected for 10+ years

Base: IRC:SP:35-2014
AASHTO Manual for Bridge Evaluation

5.2 BRIDGE CONDITION RATING
----------------------------
Rating scale (0-9):

Rating | Condition          | Description
9      | Excellent          | No defects
7-8    | Good               | Minor defects (cosmetic, no structural impact)
5-6    | Fair               | Moderate defects (requires monitoring, minor repairs)
3-4    | Poor               | Significant defects (major repairs needed)
1-2    | Critical           | Advanced deterioration (strengthening, replacement required)
0      | Failed             | Closed to traffic (unsafe)

Component ratings:
- Deck
- Superstructure
- Substructure
- Overall bridge rating (lowest of three)

Pakistan bridge condition (2023 estimate):
- Good (7-9): 40%
- Fair (5-6): 35%
- Poor (3-4): 20%
- Critical (1-2): 5% (~700 bridges structurally deficient, load-restricted or closed)

Base: IRC:SP:35-2014 Section 4

5.3 BRIDGE MAINTENANCE ACTIVITIES
----------------------------------
DECK MAINTENANCE:
- Crack sealing: Epoxy injection (structural cracks), bituminous sealant (surface cracks)
- Spall repair: Concrete patching (remove loose concrete, clean rebar, apply repair mortar)
- Wearing coat replacement: Mill old asphalt, apply new (40-50 mm BC)

BEARING MAINTENANCE:
- Elastomeric bearings: Inspect for cracks, bulging, debonding (replace if defective, 20-25 year life)
- Pot bearings: Lubrication (sliding surface), seal replacement
- Cleaning: Remove debris (prevent bearing lockup, unintended stress)

EXPANSION JOINT MAINTENANCE:
- Seal replacement: Remove old seal, clean, install new (similar to pavement joint sealing)
- Joint cleaning: Remove debris (prevent jamming)
- Armoring repair: Replace damaged steel angles (nosing)

PAINTING (Steel bridges):
- Surface preparation: Remove rust (wire brushing, sandblasting)
- Primer: Anti-corrosive (zinc-rich, epoxy)
- Finish coats: 2-3 coats (total dry film thickness 200-300 microns)
- Frequency: 7-10 years (coastal - 5 years due to salt air)

Pakistan challenges:
- Deferred maintenance (cost constraints)
- Bearing replacements delayed (require temporary support, traffic closure - complex)
- Painting often cosmetic only (no surface prep, rust returns quickly)

SCOUR COUNTERMEASURES:
- Scour monitoring: Underwater inspection (divers, sonar)
- Riprap placement: Dumped stone around piers (if scour detected)
- Guide bunds: Deflect flow (reduce attack angle)

Pakistan scour issues:
- 2010/2022 floods: Many bridges collapsed due to scour (foundation undermining)
- Monitoring weak (no routine underwater inspections)

Base: IRC:SP:35-2014 Sections 6-7

SECTION 6: MONSOON AND EMERGENCY MAINTENANCE
================================================================================

6.1 PRE-MONSOON PREPARATIONS
-----------------------------
Timing: April-May (before monsoon onset June-September)

Activities:
- Drainage cleaning: All roadside drains, culverts cleared
- Vulnerable sections: Reinforce (sandbags, gabions at erosion-prone locations)
- Stockpiling: Materials (aggregate, cement, sandbags), equipment pre-positioned
- Inspection: Identify weak points (cracks, edge breaks, drainage deficiencies)
- Repairs: Critical repairs completed (potholes, erosion gullies)

Coordination:
- District administration: Emergency response plan (equipment, personnel, shelters)
- Military (if needed): Heavy equipment, bridging (CPEC routes, critical highways)

Base: NHA Monsoon Preparedness Guidelines

6.2 EMERGENCY REPAIRS
----------------------
ROAD WASHOUTS:
- Temporary access: Earthen ramps, steel plates (restore traffic quickly)
- Permanent repair: Reconstruct embankment (properly compacted, drainage provided)

LANDSLIDES (KPK, Northern Areas, Kashmir):
- Clearance: Remove debris (excavators, bulldozers)
- Stabilization: Retaining walls, rock bolts, shotcrete (long-term)
- Monitoring: Inclinometers, crack gauges (movement detection)

BRIDGE DAMAGE:
- Minor damage: Temporary repairs (shoring, steel plates over deck holes)
- Major damage (span collapse): Bailey bridge (temporary, quick installation)
- Replacement: Design and construct permanent bridge (6-12 months)

Pakistan emergency response:
- NHA: Emergency units (regional machinery pools)
- FWO (Frontier Works Organization): Northern Areas (KKH, Gilgit-Baltistan)
- Army: Mobilized for major disasters (2005 earthquake, 2010/2022 floods)

Response time:
- Main highways: 24-48 hours (partial restoration)
- Full restoration: 1-4 weeks (depending on damage extent)

Base: NHA Emergency Response Procedures

SECTION 7: PERFORMANCE-BASED MAINTENANCE CONTRACTS
================================================================================

7.1 TRADITIONAL VS. PERFORMANCE-BASED
--------------------------------------
TRADITIONAL (METHOD-BASED):
- Payment: Per activity (Rs/m² patching, Rs/m crack sealing)
- Risk: Client (if treatment fails, pay again for re-repair)
- Quality: Variable (contractor focus on quantity, not durability)

PERFORMANCE-BASED (OUTPUT-BASED):
- Payment: Lump sum annual fee (fixed road section, specified condition standards)
- Risk: Contractor (must maintain condition, regardless of cost)
- Quality: Better (contractor incentivized for durable repairs - reduce future costs)

Performance standards:
- Potholes: Repaired within 24 hours (diameter >200 mm), 7 days (diameter <200 mm)
- IRI: Maintained ≤4.0 m/km
- Drainage: No flooding (all drains functional)
- Markings: Visible (retroreflectivity ≥100 mcd/lux/m²)

Pakistan pilots:
- NHA: Performance-based contracts (2012-2015) - 500 km trial (M-1, M-2 sections)
- Results: Mixed (better condition, but higher cost than traditional - 20-30% premium)
- Challenges: Enforcement (penalties for non-performance not applied, weak monitoring)

Future:
- World Bank recommendation: Expand performance-based contracts (NHA considering)
- PPP (Public-Private Partnership): Long-term (10-20 years) - operator collects tolls, maintains

Base: World Bank Performance-Based Road Management Contracts
NHA PPP Policy 2010

SECTION 8: ASSET MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

8.1 ASSET VALUATION
--------------------
Replacement cost:
- Calculate: Current unit rates × quantities (pavement area, bridge deck area, structures)
- NHA network: Estimated Rs 3,000-4,000 billion replacement value (2023)

Depreciation:
- Straight-line: Annual depreciation = Replacement cost / Design life
- Example: Rs 10 million road (20-year design life) → Rs 500,000/year depreciation

Asset registry:
- Database: All infrastructure (roads, bridges, buildings, equipment)
- Attributes: Location, type, dimensions, construction date, condition
- Valuation: Updated annually (inflation, condition deterioration)

Pakistan status:
- NHA: Asset register maintained (GIS-based, updated irregularly)
- Provincial: Limited asset registers (no systematic valuation)

Base: GASB 34 (Governmental Accounting Standards - Infrastructure Assets)

8.2 LIFECYCLE COSTING
----------------------
Total cost of ownership:
- Initial cost: Construction (Year 0)
- Maintenance costs: Routine (annual), periodic (every 5-7 years), rehabilitation (every 15 years)
- User costs: Vehicle operating costs, time delays (during maintenance)
- Salvage value: Residual value at end of analysis period

Present value:
- Discount rate: 8-12% (Pakistan)
- NPV (Net Present Value) = Σ [Cost_t / (1+r)^t]

Economic analysis:
- Compare alternatives: Flexible vs. rigid pavement, different maintenance strategies
- Select: Lowest lifecycle cost (or highest benefit-cost ratio)

Example (simplified):

Option A (Flexible): Initial Rs 5M, overlay Rs 2M (Year 10), maintenance Rs 100k/year
Option B (Rigid): Initial Rs 10M, joint sealing Rs 500k (Year 10), maintenance Rs 50k/year

NPV calculation (20-year period, 10% discount):
- Option A: Higher lifecycle cost (more frequent interventions)
- Option B: Lower lifecycle cost (but higher upfront) - if budget available, preferable

Pakistan practice:
- Lifecycle costing rarely used (focus on initial cost, ignore long-term)
- Result: False economy (cheap construction, expensive maintenance)

Base: IRC:SP:30-2019 (Guidelines on Cement Concrete Road Construction) Appendix
HDM-4 Lifecycle Costing Module
