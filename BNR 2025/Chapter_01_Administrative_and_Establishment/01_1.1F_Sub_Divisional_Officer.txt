PART A: FOUNDATIONAL BNR 1994 PROVISIONS
================================================================================

1.1 PARA 1.70 - SUB-DIVISIONAL OFFICER DEFINITION AND ROLE

**EXACT TEXT FROM BNR 1994:**

"The division is divided in to Sub-Divisions Incharge of Sub-Divisional Officers
who may be Executive Engineers, Assistant Executive Engineers, Assistant
Engineers or where no such officers are available, Engineer Subordinates; they
are responsible to the Divisional Officer for the management and execution of
works within their Sub-Divisions and are his assistants.

No. Sub Division can be constituted in the first instance with out the sanction
of Government."

**KEY PRINCIPLES:**

A. ORGANIZATIONAL UNIT
   - Sub-Division: Fundamental field unit within a Division
   - Sub-Divisional Officer: Head of Sub-Division
   - Responsible to Divisional Officer (direct supervisor)
   - Role: Assistant to Divisional Officer for field-level operations

B. RANK FLEXIBILITY
   - May be Executive Engineers (senior level)
   - May be Assistant Executive Engineers (common level)
   - May be Assistant Engineers (junior level)
   - May be Engineer Subordinates (when gazetted officers unavailable)
   - Rank depends on workload, complexity, and availability

C. FUNCTIONAL RESPONSIBILITY
   - Management of works within Sub-Division
   - Execution of works within Sub-Division
   - Field-level implementation of divisional policies
   - Direct interface with contractors and site activities

D. ESTABLISHMENT AUTHORITY
   - Government sanction required for initial constitution of Sub-Division
   - Cannot be created unilaterally by Divisional or Superintending Engineer
   - Reflects importance of Sub-Division as permanent organizational structure

1.2 RELATED BNR 1994 PROVISIONS

**Para 1.53 (Divisional Officer Context):**

"Assistant Executive and Assistant or temporary Engineers may be placed in
charge of Divisions, either to fill short temporary vacancies or because
Executive Engineer is available; they exercise the powers and bear the
responsibilities of an Executive Engineer while in such charge."

**Application to Sub-Divisional Officers:**
- When placed in temporary charge of Division, Sub-Divisional Officer exercises
  full Divisional Officer powers
- Demonstrates career progression pathway from Sub-Division to Division
- Emphasizes importance of SDO competency development

**Para 1.57 (Transfer Authority):**

"Divisional Officers may transfer establishment other than Sub-Divisional
Officers, Divisional Accountants and within their respective Divisions without
reference to higher authority."

**Implication for Sub-Divisional Officers:**
- SDOs cannot be transferred by Divisional Officer alone
- Transfer requires higher authority approval (Superintending Engineer or above)
- Reflects importance and stability of Sub-Divisional Officer position
- Protection from arbitrary or frequent transfers

**Para 1.62 (Supervisory Relationship):**

"The relative position of a Divisional Accountant to the Divisional Officer in
respect of accounts is analogous to that of a Sub-Divisional Officer to a
Divisional Officer in respect of works and the responsibilities of the latter
for the work of the Divisional Accountant are similar to those which attach to
him respect of the execution of works in the charge of other subordinates."

**Key Points:**
- Sub-Divisional Officer to Divisional Officer relationship is supervisory, not
  independent
- Divisional Officer ultimately responsible for Sub-Divisional Officer's work
- Same accountability framework as Divisional Accountant for financial matters
- Clear hierarchy and responsibility chain

**Para 1.87 (Training and Professional Development):**

"An officer appointed to the Department as Assistant Executive Engineer,
Assistant Engineer, or Temporary Engineer shall be required, during the first
tow years of his service, to write up the accounts of a Sub-Division himself
for three months, and he shall shortly, thereafter, be posted to a divisional
accounts are dealt with there and consolidated in the accounts of the Division."

**Training Implications:**
- Sub-Division is primary training ground for new engineers
- Sub-Divisional Officer responsible for mentoring junior engineers
- Hands-on experience in field operations and accounting
- Foundation for future Divisional Officer roles

1.3 OPERATIONAL CONTEXT FROM BNR 1994

**Field-Level Authority:**

While BNR 1994 does not extensively detail Sub-Divisional Officer specific
powers, the following principles are established:

A. DELEGATION OF DIVISIONAL OFFICER DUTIES
   - Sub-Divisional Officer acts under delegated authority from Divisional
     Officer
   - Responsible for day-to-day field operations within Sub-Division
   - Reports progress, issues, and irregularities to Divisional Officer
   - Implements Divisional Officer's directions and policies

B. SITE SUPERVISION
   - Direct supervision of works execution
   - Quality control at field level
   - Contractor coordination and monitoring
   - Measurement and verification of work done

C. MEASUREMENT AND CERTIFICATION
   - Record measurements in Measurement Books
   - Initial certification of work completed
   - Recommend payments based on field verification
   - Ensure accuracy of quantities and quality

D. RESOURCE MANAGEMENT
   - Manage labor, materials, and equipment at Sub-Division level
   - Coordinate with Divisional Officer for resource allocation
   - Ensure efficient utilization of divisional resources
   - Monitor stocks and stores within Sub-Division

E. REPORTING OBLIGATIONS
   - Regular progress reports to Divisional Officer
   - Immediate reporting of accidents, irregularities, or unusual occurrences
   - Submit site inspection reports
   - Provide technical data for divisional reporting

2.1 FIELD INSPECTOR AND DEPUTY BUILDING OFFICIAL FRAMEWORK

**Source:** International Building Code (IBC) 2024, Chapter 1, Sections 103.3,
104, 110

The Sub-Divisional Officer role aligns with the IBC 2024 concept of a
field-level inspector or deputy building official who operates under delegated
authority from the Building Official (analogous to Divisional Officer).

**Section 103.3 - Deputies:**

"In accordance with the prescribed procedures of this jurisdiction and with the
concurrence of the appointing authority, the building official shall have the
authority to appoint a deputy building official, other related technical
officers, inspectors and other employees. Such employees shall have powers as
delegated by the building official."

**Application to Sub-Divisional Officer:**
- Sub-Divisional Officer = Deputy or Technical Officer with delegated authority
- Powers exercised as delegated by Divisional Officer
- Clear scope of delegation defined in writing
- Authority limited to Sub-Division geographic and functional scope

2.2 INSPECTION AUTHORITY AND PROTOCOLS (IBC 2024, Section 110)

**Section 110.1 - General Inspection Authority:**

"Construction or work for which a permit is required shall be subject to
inspection by the building official and such construction or work shall remain
visible and able to be accessed for inspection purposes until approved."

**Sub-Divisional Officer Inspection Duties:**

A. REQUIRED INSPECTIONS (IBC 2024, Section 110.3)

   The Sub-Divisional Officer shall conduct or ensure the following inspections:

   1. **Footing and Foundation Inspection**
      - After excavation complete and reinforcing steel in place
      - Forms in place for concrete
      - Materials on job (except ready-mix concrete)
      - Verify foundation depth, bearing capacity, reinforcement

   2. **Concrete Slab and Under-Floor Inspection**
      - After reinforcing steel and building service equipment in place
      - Before concrete placed or floor sheathing installed
      - Check embedments, drainage, vapor barriers

   3. **Lowest Floor Elevation (Flood Hazard Areas)**
      - Upon placement of lowest floor before further vertical construction
      - Elevation certification must be submitted
      - Compliance with flood zone requirements

   4. **Frame Inspection**
      - After roof deck/sheathing and all framing in place
      - Fire-blocking and bracing complete
      - Pipes, chimneys, vents to be concealed are complete
      - Rough electrical, plumbing, heating wires, pipes, ducts approved

   5. **Types IV-A, IV-B and IV-C Connection Protection Inspection**
      [Note: Limited applicability to Pakistan context - applicable primarily
       to wood-based construction types]
      - After wood cover installed
      - Before other coverings or finishes applied
      - Applies to specific wood-cover fire-rating provisions per IBC Section
        2304.10.1

   6. **Lath and Gypsum Panel Product Inspection**
      - After lathing and gypsum products in place
      - Before plastering applied or joints/fasteners finished

   7. **Weather-Exposed Balcony and Walking Surface Waterproofing**
      - Impervious moisture barrier not concealed until inspected

   8. **Fire and Smoke-Resistant Penetrations**
      - Joint and penetration protection not concealed until inspected

   9. **Energy Efficiency Inspections**
      - Compliance with energy code requirements
      - Thermal envelope insulation R/U-values
      - Fenestration U-value, duct system R-value
      - HVAC and water-heating equipment efficiency

   10. **Other Inspections**
       - Building official authorized to require additional inspections
       - Beyond those listed in Sections 110.3.1 through 110.3.9
       - To ascertain compliance with code and other enforced laws
       - Provides flexibility for Sub-Divisional Officer to inspect as needed

   11. **Special Inspections**
       - Coordinate special inspections per Chapter 17
       - Review special inspector qualifications
       - Monitor special inspection compliance

   12. **Final Inspection**
       - After all work required by permit completed
       - Verify compliance with approved plans
       - Confirm all previous inspection findings addressed

B. INSPECTION PROCEDURES

   **Section 110.2 - Preliminary Inspection:**
   - Examine buildings, structures, sites before permit issuance
   - Verify site conditions match application
   - Identify potential code compliance issues

   **Section 110.5 - Inspection Requests:**
   - Permit holder notifies when work ready for inspection
   - Sub-Divisional Officer schedules and conducts inspection within
     reasonable time
   - Provide access and means for inspection

   **Section 110.6 - Approval Required:**
   - Work shall not proceed beyond inspection point without approval
   - Inspector indicates satisfactory portions and non-compliant portions
   - Non-compliant work not concealed until approved

2.3 RIGHT OF ENTRY AND ENFORCEMENT (IBC 2024, Section 104.4)

**Section 104.4 - Right of Entry:**

"Where it is necessary to make an inspection to enforce the provisions of this
code, or where the building official has reasonable cause to believe that there
exists in a structure or on a premises a condition that is contrary to or in
violation of this code that makes the structure or premises unsafe, dangerous
or hazardous, the building official is authorized to enter the structure or
premises at all reasonable times to inspect or to perform the duties imposed by
this code. If such structure or premises is occupied, the building official
shall present credentials to the occupant and request entry. If such structure
or premises is unoccupied, the building official shall first make a reasonable
effort to locate the owner, the owner's authorized agent or other person having
charge or control of the structure or premises and request entry. If entry is
refused, the building official shall have recourse to every remedy provided by
law to secure entry."

**Sub-Divisional Officer Entry Procedures:**

A. STANDARD ENTRY PROTOCOL
   - Present proper identification credentials
   - Request entry from occupant or owner
   - Explain purpose of inspection
   - Conduct inspection during reasonable hours

B. UNOCCUPIED PREMISES
   - Make reasonable effort to locate owner or agent
   - Leave notice of attempted entry
   - Schedule inspection with owner/agent

C. ENTRY REFUSED
   - If owner refuses entry, do not force entry
   - Report refusal to Divisional Officer
   - Obtain inspection warrant through legal process
   - After warrant obtained, owner cannot refuse entry

**Section 104.5 - Identification:**

"The building official shall carry proper identification when inspecting
structures or premises in the performance of duties under this code."

**Sub-Divisional Officer Credentials:**
- Official identification card with photo
- Department seal and authorizing signature
- Clear indication of authority and jurisdiction
- Contact information for verification

2.4 NOTICES AND ORDERS (IBC 2024, Section 104.6)

**Section 104.6 - Notices and Orders:**

"The building official shall issue necessary notices or orders to ensure
compliance with this code."

**Sub-Divisional Officer Notice Authority:**

A. NOTICE OF VIOLATION (Section 114.2)
   - Serve written notice on person responsible for violation
   - Direct discontinuance of illegal action or condition
   - Specify abatement measures required
   - Provide reasonable time for compliance

B. STOP WORK ORDERS (Section 115)

   **Section 115.1 - Authority:**
   "Where the building official finds any work regulated by this code being
   performed in a manner contrary to the provisions of this code or in a
   dangerous or unsafe manner, the building official is authorized to issue a
   stop work order."

   **Sub-Divisional Officer Stop Work Authority:**
   - Issue stop work order for code violations
   - Issue stop work order for unsafe conditions
   - Written order given to owner, agent, or person performing work
   - Work ceases immediately upon issuance
   - State reason and conditions for resumption

   **Section 115.3 - Emergency Authority:**
   - In emergencies, written notice not required prior to stopping work
   - Verbal stop work order followed by written confirmation
   - Immediate action to prevent injury or damage

C. UNSAFE STRUCTURE NOTICES (Section 116)

   **Section 116.1 - Unsafe Conditions:**
   - Identify unsafe structures or equipment
   - Structures dangerous to human life or public welfare
   - Inadequate egress, fire hazards, structural deficiencies

   **Section 116.3 - Notice of Unsafe Condition:**
   - Written notice describing unsafe condition
   - Specify required repairs or demolition
   - Stipulate reasonable time for compliance
   - Proper service per Section 116.4 (personal delivery, certified mail, or
     posting)

2.5 OFFICIAL RECORDS AND DOCUMENTATION (IBC 2024, Section 104.7)

**Section 104.7 - Official Records:**

The Sub-Divisional Officer must maintain and contribute to official records:

A. INSPECTION RECORDS (Section 104.7.2)
   - Record of each inspection made
   - Notices and orders issued
   - Findings and disposition of each inspection
   - Minimum 5-year retention
   - Available for Divisional Officer review

B. CONTEMPORANEOUS DOCUMENTATION
   - Document inspections on same day conducted
   - Photograph deficiencies and compliance
   - GPS coordinates for site locations
   - Weather conditions affecting work

C. DIGITAL RECORD-KEEPING
   - Use approved digital inspection platforms
   - Real-time synchronization with divisional office
   - Backup and redundancy protocols
   - Audit trail for all entries

2.6 QUALIFICATIONS AND COMPETENCY (IBC APPENDIX A)

**Source:** IBC 2024, Appendix A, Section A101.3

**Section A101.3 - Inspector and Plans Examiner:**

"The building official shall appoint or hire such number of officers,
inspectors, assistants and other employees as shall be authorized by the
jurisdiction. A person who has fewer than 5 years of experience as a
contractor, engineer, architect, or as a superintendent, foreman or competent
mechanic in charge of construction shall not be appointed or hired as inspector
of construction or plans examiner. The inspector or plans examiner shall be
certified through a recognized certification program for the appropriate trade."

**Sub-Divisional Officer Minimum Qualifications:**
- Minimum 5 years' experience as contractor, engineer, architect, or
  superintendent/foreman
- Certification through recognized certification program for appropriate trade
- Combination of roles acceptable to meet 5-year requirement

**Competency Requirements:**
- Technical knowledge of construction practices
- Understanding of code provisions
- Ability to identify code violations
- Communication and documentation skills
- Judgment and decision-making under field conditions

2.7 LIABILITY PROTECTION (IBC 2024, Section 104.8)

**Section 104.8 - Liability:**

"The building official, member of the board of appeals or employee charged with
the enforcement of this code, while acting for the jurisdiction in good faith
and without malice in the discharge of the duties required by this code or other
pertinent law or ordinance, shall not thereby be rendered personally liable,
either civilly or criminally, and is hereby relieved from personal liability for
any damage accruing to persons or property as a result of any act or by reason
of any act or omission in the discharge of official duties."

**Sub-Divisional Officer Liability Protection:**
- Good faith enforcement protected
- No personal liability for honest mistakes
- Malicious or negligent actions not protected
- Department provides legal defense
- Document all decisions and basis for actions

3.1 PROJECT MANAGER ROLE (UK DMRB GG 102)

**Source:** UK DMRB GG 102 - Quality management systems for highway works,
Revision 0, May 2019

**Section 7.1.1 - Key Roles:**

"The quality plan shall identify key roles and the competencies that are
necessary for the delivery of the product / project. Key roles should include:
1) project managers;
2) technical specialists;
3) lead assessors and inspectors;
4) audit and assurance assessors; and
5) signatories."

**Sub-Divisional Officer as Project Manager:**

The Sub-Divisional Officer functions as the project manager for works within
the Sub-Division, responsible for:

A. OPERATIONAL PLANNING (Clause 8.2)
   - Detail operational planning and control processes
   - Deliver quantity, timing, and technical quality
   - Identify internal and external stakeholders
   - Monitor performance against resource and competence requirements
   - Take control action per defined criteria
   - Manage scope of work, programme, and budget
   - Manage change control processes
   - Control outsourced processes

B. RESOURCE MANAGEMENT
   - Assess and assign competent individuals to activities
   - Monitor resource availability and utilization
   - Coordinate with Divisional Officer for additional resources
   - Optimize deployment across multiple projects

C. QUALITY PLANNING
   - Develop Sub-Division quality plan aligned with divisional QMS
   - Define inspection and testing requirements
   - Establish hold points and witness points
   - Specify quality records and documentation

3.2 SITE SUPERVISION AND OVERSEEING ORGANISATION

**Source:** UK DMRB CG 300 - Technical approval of highway structures, Version 0.2.2 (30 September 2025)

**Overseeing Organisation (OO) Concept:**

The Overseeing Organisation monitors construction on behalf of the client to
ensure compliance with approved designs and specifications.

**Sub-Divisional Officer as OO Representative:**

A. CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
   - Monitor contractor compliance with approved drawings
   - Verify materials meet specification requirements
   - Witness critical construction activities
   - Review contractor quality control procedures
   - Escalate non-conformances to Divisional Officer

B. TECHNICAL COMPLIANCE
   - Ensure construction follows approved design intent
   - Review proposed changes for technical acceptability
   - Coordinate design clarifications with designer
   - Document as-built conditions

C. SAFETY OVERSIGHT
   - Monitor contractor health and safety compliance
   - Ensure temporary works designed and approved
   - Verify lifting operations and equipment safety
   - Report safety incidents and near-misses

3.3 COMPETENCE MANAGEMENT (UK DMRB GG 102, Clause 7.3)

**Clause 7.3 - Competence Assessment:**

"The quality plan shall identify the role or position of the individual with
the assigned responsibility for assessing technical competency and for
assigning competent individuals to project activities."

**Sub-Divisional Officer Competence Responsibilities:**

A. SELF-COMPETENCE
   - Maintain technical competence through CPD
   - Undertake relevant training and certifications
   - Stay current with code changes and best practices
   - Demonstrate competence in assigned work types

B. TEAM COMPETENCE ASSESSMENT
   - Assess competence of Assistant Engineers and Junior Engineers
   - Verify contractor personnel qualifications
   - Assign tasks commensurate with competence levels
   - Identify training needs and development opportunities

C. COMPETENCE RECORDS
   - Maintain competence records for all personnel
   - Document training completed
   - Track certifications and licenses
   - Update competence matrices regularly

4.1 SUB-DIVISIONAL OFFICER DEFINITION (CPWD MANUAL 2022)

**Source:** CPWD Works Manual 2022, Definitions

**Sub-Divisional Officer:**

"The Officer in-charge of a Sub-Division, usually of the rank of Assistant
Executive Engineer, responsible to the Divisional Officer for execution of
works within the Sub-Division."

**Key Characteristics:**

A. USUAL RANK: ASSISTANT EXECUTIVE ENGINEER (AEE)
   - Entry-level gazetted engineering position
   - Bridge between field staff and divisional management
   - Hands-on involvement in construction supervision
   - Career development pathway to Executive Engineer

B. GEOGRAPHIC SCOPE: SUB-DIVISION
   - Smaller geographic area than Division
   - Typically covers 1-3 tehsils or municipalities
   - Manageable span of control for direct supervision
   - Usually 3-5 Sub-Divisions per Division

C. REPORTING RELATIONSHIP
   - Reports directly to Divisional Officer (Executive Engineer)
   - Receives instructions and guidance from Divisional Officer
   - Implements divisional policies and procedures
   - Accountable for Sub-Division performance

4.2 EXECUTION RESPONSIBILITIES (CPWD MANUAL 2019)

**Source:** CPWD Works Manual 2019, Chapter 5 (Execution of Works)

**Work Execution:**

"The Engineer in Charge and his representatives shall be responsible for
execution of works and contract management according to the drawings, design,
specifications, quality, and agreement etc."

**Sub-Divisional Officer as Representative:**

A. CONTRACT MANAGEMENT
   - Day-to-day contract administration within Sub-Division
   - Monitor contractor performance and progress
   - Review and recommend contractor payment certificates
   - Enforce contract terms and specifications
   - Report contractor defaults to Divisional Officer

B. QUALITY CONTROL (CPWD Manual 2019, Chapter 5)

   **Responsibility for Quality:**
   "The officer who records/test checks the measurements for an item of work
   will be responsible for the quality, quantity/measurements and dimensional
   accuracy of that item of work."

   **Sub-Divisional Officer Quality Duties:**
   - Record measurements in Measurement Books
   - Test check measurements of subordinates
   - Responsible for quality of measured items
   - Ensure materials and workmanship compliance
   - Coordinate testing with approved laboratories

C. PERIODIC INSPECTION
   - Inspect works frequently per SOP 5/12
   - Ensure work per quality, design, drawings, specifications
   - Identify and correct deficiencies promptly
   - Document inspection findings
   - Report serious quality issues to Divisional Officer

4.3 TECHNICAL SANCTION AND ESTIMATES

**Technical Sanction Authority:**

While Divisional Officer (Executive Engineer) typically accords technical
sanction, Sub-Divisional Officer plays critical role:

A. ESTIMATE PREPARATION
   - Prepare detailed estimates for new works
   - Prepare estimates for repairs and maintenance
   - Calculate quantities from drawings
   - Apply current market rates or schedule of rates
   - Include all necessary components and departmental charges

B. DESIGN REVIEW
   - Review designs prepared by Assistant Engineers
   - Check for constructability and practicality
   - Identify design deficiencies or errors
   - Recommend design modifications
   - Submit estimates with recommendations to Divisional Officer

C. SITE ASSESSMENT
   - Conduct site surveys for new works
   - Assess existing conditions for repairs
   - Identify site-specific constraints
   - Recommend suitable designs and methods
   - Provide technical input for feasibility studies

4.4 MEASUREMENT AND PAYMENT (CPWD MANUAL 2019)

**Source:** CPWD Works Manual 2019, Chapter 14 (Supply of Materials) and
Chapter 5

**Payment Processing:**

"All the stores that are received are verified, and measurements and details
thereof are to be recorded in the Measurement Book if the purchase is for
specific work. Payments are to be made by the Executive Engineer on the basis
of the entries recorded in the Measurement Book, according to the terms of the
payment as finalised in the supply order."

**Sub-Divisional Officer Payment Certification:**

A. MEASUREMENT BOOK ENTRIES
   - Record all measurements in Measurement Books (MB)
   - Measurements taken jointly with contractor representative
   - Clear description of item and location
   - Dimensions, quantities, and calculations shown
   - Date, signature of SDO and contractor

B. VERIFICATION PROCESS
   - Verify materials received on site
   - Check quality and specifications compliance
   - Confirm quantities match delivery receipts
   - Witness testing where required
   - Document any deficiencies or rejections

C. PAYMENT RECOMMENDATIONS
   - Prepare running account bill (RAB) based on MB entries
   - Calculate payment per contract rates
   - Apply deductions (advance recovery, taxes, LD if applicable)
   - Recommend payment certificate to Divisional Officer
   - Ensure supporting documents complete

D. RECORDS RETENTION
   - Maintain all Measurement Books systematically
   - File payment certificates and supporting documents
   - Preserve records per retention schedule (minimum 10 years for works
     records)
   - Make records available for audit

4.5 COMPLETION PROCEDURES (CPWD MANUAL 2019)

**Source:** CPWD Works Manual 2019, Chapter 5 (Execution of Works)

**Provisional Completion:**

"On substantial Completion of any work which has been completed to such an
extent that the intended purpose of the work is met and is ready for use, then
a provisional Completion Certificate with a list of outstanding balance items
of work that need to be completed in accordance with the provisions of the
contract shall be recorded by the Executive Engineer and T/S Authority."

**Sub-Divisional Officer Role:**

A. COMPLETION INSPECTION
   - Conduct thorough inspection when contractor claims completion
   - Verify all major works substantially complete
   - Test functionality of systems (if applicable)
   - Prepare list of outstanding items (snag list)
   - Assess if work ready for intended use

B. PROVISIONAL COMPLETION RECOMMENDATION
   - Recommend provisional completion to Divisional Officer
   - Provide detailed snag list with timelines
   - Confirm substantial compliance with contract
   - Document condition of work
   - Initiate defects liability period

C. DEFECTS LIABILITY MONITORING
   - Monitor defects during defects liability period (typically 12 months)
   - Record all defects identified
   - Notify contractor to rectify defects
   - Verify defect rectification
   - Report persistent defects to Divisional Officer

D. FINAL COMPLETION INSPECTION
   - Conduct joint inspection with Divisional Officer/T/S Authority
   - Verify all snag items completed
   - Verify all defects rectified
   - Confirm work complete in all respects
   - Recommend final completion certificate

5.1 POSITION DEFINITION AND SCOPE

**Sub-Divisional Officer - Pakistan Building and Roads Department:**

A. DUAL TERMINOLOGY
   - **Pakistan Convention:** "Sub-Divisional Officer (SDO)" per BNR 1994
   - **India Equivalent:** "Assistant Executive Engineer (AEE)" per CPWD
   - Both terms refer to same position
   - BNR 2025 uses "Sub-Divisional Officer" as primary term

B. ORGANIZATIONAL POSITION
   - Reports To: Divisional Officer / Executive Engineer
     **Note:** "Divisional Officer (DO)" and "Executive Engineer (EE)" are
     interchangeable terms for the same position - head of a Division who
     supervises Sub-Divisional Officers. BNR 2025 uses "Divisional Officer"
     as primary term per BNR 1994 convention.
   - Supervises: Assistant Engineers, Junior Engineers, Sub-Engineers, Work
     Supervisors, field staff
   - Coordinates With: Superintendent of Works (quality matters), contractors,
     consultants, other SDOs
   - Peer Level: Other Sub-Divisional Officers within division

C. GEOGRAPHIC AND FUNCTIONAL SCOPE
   - Heads a Sub-Division (field unit within Division)
   - Geographic area: Typically 1-3 tehsils or equivalent
   - Functional scope: All buildings and roads works within Sub-Division
   - Sub-Division size: Based on workload, typically Rs. 200-500 million annual
     budget
   - Usually 3-5 Sub-Divisions per Division

D. TENURE AND APPOINTMENT
   - Appointed by Divisional Officer on posting orders from Superintending
     Engineer
   - Minimum tenure: 2 years (to ensure project continuity and local knowledge)
   - Transfer requires Superintending Engineer approval
   - Succession planning: Identify potential successor 3 months before transfer

5.2 QUALIFICATION REQUIREMENTS

A. EDUCATIONAL QUALIFICATIONS (MINIMUM)
   - Bachelor's degree in Civil Engineering from HEC-recognized university
   - Registration with Pakistan Engineering Council (PEC) as Registered
     Engineer (RE)
   - Good academic standing (minimum 2nd Division or equivalent GPA)

B. EXPERIENCE REQUIREMENTS
   - Minimum 5 years post-graduation experience in civil engineering
   - At least 2 years in supervisory capacity (as Assistant Engineer or
     equivalent)
   - Experience in both building and road construction desirable
   - Prior field experience in construction supervision mandatory
   - Experience in measurement and contract administration

C. PROFESSIONAL QUALIFICATIONS (PREFERRED)
   - Professional Engineer (PE) registration with PEC (10 years experience +
     CPD) - for senior SDOs
   - Post-graduate diploma in Construction Management or related field
   - Training in project management (basic level)
   - Training in contract administration and FIDIC contracts

D. TECHNICAL COMPETENCIES
   - Proficiency in quantity surveying and measurement
   - Knowledge of BNR 2025, NHA specifications, Building Code of Pakistan
   - Understanding of IS codes, ASTM standards
   - Material testing and quality control procedures
   - Contract administration and payment certification
   - Cost estimation and rate analysis

E. TECHNOLOGY SKILLS
   - Proficiency in AutoCAD for reading and markup of drawings
   - Spreadsheet software (MS Excel) for calculations and reporting
   - Basic project management software
   - Digital measurement and inspection tools
   - GPS and GIS applications for site location
   - Digital photography and documentation

5.3 CORE RESPONSIBILITIES

A. FIELD SUPERVISION AND EXECUTION
   - Direct supervision of all works within Sub-Division
   - Daily site visits to active construction sites
   - Monitor contractor performance and progress
   - Ensure compliance with drawings and specifications
   - Coordinate multiple projects simultaneously
   - Resolve site-level issues and obstructions

B. QUALITY ASSURANCE AND CONTROL
   - Primary responsibility for quality at field level
   - Inspect materials before incorporation in work
   - Witness critical construction activities

   - Materials received
   - Tests conducted

   - Issues and resolutions
   - Visitors and inspections

7.2 WORK INSPECTION AND APPROVAL

A. INSPECTION STAGES

   **1. Before Covering/Concealment:**
   - Foundations (before backfilling)
   - Reinforcement (before concreting)
   - Formwork (before concreting)
   - Underground services (before covering)
   - Waterproofing (before protection layer)
   - Structural elements (before finishes)

   **2. During Construction:**
   - Concrete placing and compaction
   - Masonry construction (plumbness, bonding)
   - Earthwork compaction (layer-wise)
   - Pavement layer construction
   - Welding and fabrication
   - MEP installations

   **3. After Completion (Before Acceptance):**
   - Dimensional accuracy
   - Finishes quality (uniformity, defects)
   - Functionality of systems
   - Cleanliness and punch list items
   - Testing and commissioning

B. APPROVAL DOCUMENTATION
   - Inspection Request Form (IRF) from contractor
   - Inspection conducted within 24-48 hours
   - Inspection report prepared (approve/reject/revise)
   - If approved: Allow next stage of work
   - If rejected: Issue non-conformance notice, require corrective action
   - Photograph approved work
   - Record in daily diary and inspection register

C. HOLD POINTS AND WITNESS POINTS
   - **Hold Points:** Work cannot proceed without SDO approval (e.g.,
     reinforcement before concreting)
   - **Witness Points:** SDO should witness if available (e.g., concrete pour,
     compaction test)
   - Clearly communicated to contractor in pre-construction meeting
   - Documented in quality plan

7.3 MATERIAL INSPECTION AND APPROVAL

   - Contractor submits Material Approval Request (MAR)

B. SITE MATERIAL INSPECTION
   - Inspect materials upon delivery to site

   - Reject substandard or damaged materials

C. MATERIAL TESTING COORDINATION

   - Review test results against acceptance criteria

   - Maintain testing records and certificates

   - Conduct independent verification
   - Witness critical activities
   - Review test results and take action

   - Conduct internal quality audits quarterly
   - Audit contractor QC procedures
   - Verify documentation and records
   - Identify systemic issues
   - Implement corrective and preventive actions (CAPA)
   - Report audit results to Divisional Officer

8.2 TESTING AND LABORATORY COORDINATION

A. TESTING REQUIREMENTS
   - Material acceptance testing (cement, steel, aggregate, bitumen)
   - In-process testing (concrete strength, compaction, pavement thickness)
   - Final acceptance testing (load tests, functionality tests)
   - Frequency per specifications (e.g., 1 concrete cube per 10 m)

B. LABORATORY MANAGEMENT
   - Use only approved/accredited laboratories
   - Maintain list of approved labs with scope
   - Monitor lab turnaround time
   - Verify lab accreditation status periodically
   - Address delays or quality concerns with lab
   - Consider lab rotation to avoid complacency

C. SAMPLING PROCEDURES
   - Random sampling per ASTM/IS/BS standards
   - Joint sampling with contractor representative
   - Proper sample collection, labeling, and preservation
   - Tamper-proof sealing
   - Chain of custody maintained
   - Traceability to work location and batch

D. TEST RESULT MANAGEMENT
   - Review test results promptly (within 24 hours of receipt)
   - Compare against acceptance criteria
   - **If Pass:** Clear for next stage, file result
   - **If Marginal:** Investigate, consider additional testing, engineering
     judgment
   - **If Fail:** Issue non-conformance, stop related work, investigate root
     cause, decide on corrective action (remove/replace, repair, accept with
     concession)
   - Maintain test result register with status

8.3 NON-CONFORMANCE MANAGEMENT

A. NON-CONFORMANCE IDENTIFICATION
   - Identified through: inspections, testing, audits, observation
   - Types: Material non-conformance, workmanship defect, dimensional
     deviation, specification breach

B. NON-CONFORMANCE REPORTING
   - Issue Non-Conformance Report (NCR) immediately
   - NCR includes: description, location, photographs, reference to
     specification, severity (major/minor)
   - Distribute to contractor and Divisional Officer
   - Log in NCR register

C. CORRECTIVE ACTION
   - Contractor proposes corrective action
   - SDO reviews and approves/rejects proposal
   - Options: Remove and replace, Repair, Accept with concession (requires
     Divisional Officer approval), Engineering analysis and acceptance
   - Monitor implementation of corrective action
   - Verify effectiveness (re-inspection, re-testing)
   - Close NCR only after satisfactory correction

D. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
   - For repeated or major non-conformances, conduct root cause analysis
   - Use tools like 5 Whys, Fishbone Diagram
   - Identify systemic issues (training, supervision, materials, equipment)
   - Implement preventive actions to avoid recurrence
   - Share lessons learned with team

8.4 COMPLIANCE MANAGEMENT

A. CODE AND STANDARD COMPLIANCE
   - Ensure compliance with BNR 2025
   - Apply Building Code of Pakistan for building works
   - Use NHA specifications for road works
   - Reference IS, ASTM, BS, or specified standards
   - Keep updated copies of codes and standards available
   - Train team on applicable requirements

B. CONTRACT COMPLIANCE
   - Ensure work per contract drawings and specifications
   - Monitor compliance with contractual obligations (program, quality, safety)
   - Enforce contract terms fairly and consistently
   - Document non-compliance and actions taken
   - Escalate persistent non-compliance to Divisional Officer

C. REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

   - Labor laws (minimum wages, working hours, child labor prohibition)
   - Local bylaws and permits
   - Utility regulations (NOCs from WAPDA, SNGPL, etc.)

D. AUDIT AND INSPECTION PREPAREDNESS

   - Respond to internal audit findings promptly
   - Facilitate external audits (Auditor General, NAB, etc.)
   - Provide requested documents and information
   - Address audit observations within specified timeframes
   - Implement audit recommendations

B. INTERPERSONAL SKILLS DEVELOPMENT

   **Relationship Building:**
   - Build rapport with contractors (firm but fair)
   - Earn trust of team through consistency and integrity
   - Network with peers in other departments and agencies
   - Maintain professional relationships with superiors
   - Engage respectfully with public and communities

   **Conflict Resolution:**
   - Address conflicts early before escalation
   - Understand all perspectives
   - Find win-win solutions where possible
   - Escalate when necessary to Divisional Officer
   - Document and learn from conflicts

   **Negotiation:**
   - Negotiate constructively with contractors (variations, claims)
   - Balance departmental interests with fairness
   - Prepare thoroughly with facts and alternatives
   - Seek mutually acceptable outcomes
   - Know when to compromise and when to stand firm

9.5 WORK-LIFE BALANCE AND WELL-BEING

A. CHALLENGES FOR FIELD ENGINEERS
   - Long and irregular working hours
   - Travel and site visits in difficult terrain
   - High stress and responsibility
   - Pressure from superiors, contractors, and public
   - Risk of burnout

B. STRATEGIES FOR BALANCE
   - Set boundaries (e.g., site visits during daylight, emergency-only after
     hours)
   - Delegate and empower team members
   - Efficient time management (avoid firefighting mode)
   - Take leave and rest (not a sign of weakness)
   - Maintain hobbies and family time
   - Physical fitness and healthy lifestyle
   - Seek support when overwhelmed (supervisor, peers, counseling)

C. STRESS MANAGEMENT
   - Identify stress triggers and early signs
   - Develop coping strategies (exercise, meditation, hobbies)
   - Maintain perspective (not every problem is catastrophic)
   - Build support network (professional and personal)
   - Recognize when to seek professional help

================================================================================
PART J: COMMUNITY COORDINATION AND PUBLIC SERVICE
================================================================================

10.1 PUBLIC INTERFACE AND STAKEHOLDER ENGAGEMENT

A. ROLE AS PUBLIC SERVANT
   - Sub-Divisional Officer is face of department to public at field level
   - Responsible for professional, courteous, and responsive public service
   - Address public concerns and complaints
   - Provide information and transparency
   - Build public trust in department

B. STAKEHOLDER IDENTIFICATION
   - Primary: Local communities and residents affected by projects
   - Secondary: Local government (tehsil, district), elected representatives
   - Tertiary: NGOs, civil society, media
   - Institutional: Utility agencies, regulatory authorities, law enforcement
   - Project: Contractors, consultants, suppliers

C. STAKEHOLDER ENGAGEMENT STRATEGY
   - Identify stakeholders and their interests/concerns
   - Develop engagement plan (frequency, methods, responsibilities)
   - Proactive communication (before issues arise)
   - Two-way dialogue (listen and respond)
   - Manage expectations realistically
   - Build collaborative relationships

10.2 PUBLIC COMPLAINT AND GRIEVANCE HANDLING

A. COMPLAINT RECEPTION
   - Receive complaints through multiple channels:
     * In-person at Sub-Division office
     * Written complaints (letter, email)
     * Phone calls
     * Online grievance portals (Pakistan Citizen Portal, departmental portal)
     * Through elected representatives or local government
   - Acknowledge receipt immediately
   - Register in complaints register

B. COMPLAINT INVESTIGATION
   - Investigate complaint promptly (within 3 working days)
   - Visit site if necessary
   - Gather facts and evidence
   - Consult with team and contractor if relevant
   - Determine validity and merit of complaint
   - Document investigation process

C. COMPLAINT RESOLUTION
   - If valid: Take corrective action, inform complainant, close complaint
   - If partially valid: Address valid aspects, explain others, seek
     understanding
   - If invalid: Explain reasons politely and patiently, close complaint
   - Escalate to Divisional Officer if beyond SDO authority or complex
   - Target resolution within 7 working days
   - Follow-up to ensure satisfaction

D. COMPLAINT REPORTING
   - Maintain complaints register (complainant, date, issue, action, status)
   - Monthly summary to Divisional Officer
   - Identify recurring issues and systemic solutions
   - Use complaints as improvement opportunities

10.3 COMMUNITY COORDINATION AND PARTICIPATION

A. COMMUNITY CONSULTATION
   - For projects affecting communities, conduct community consultations
   - Pre-construction: Inform about project, timeline, impacts, benefits
   - During construction: Regular updates, address concerns
   - Post-construction: Handover and maintenance briefing
   - Methods: Public meetings, focus groups, door-to-door outreach

B. SOCIAL AND ENVIRONMENTAL SAFEGUARDS
   - Implement Social Management Plan (if required by project)
   - Minimize displacement and impacts on livelihoods
   - Ensure fair compensation and resettlement (if applicable)
   - Protect cultural and religious sites
   - Gender-sensitive approach (women's participation, safety)
   - Vulnerable groups consideration (disabled, elderly, poor)

C. LOCAL EMPLOYMENT AND PROCUREMENT
   - Encourage contractors to hire local labor (where skilled available)
   - Source materials locally where feasible and economical
   - Support local economy and build ownership
   - Balance local preference with quality and economy

D. COMMUNITY FEEDBACK AND PARTICIPATION
   - Establish community liaison mechanism
   - Appoint community focal person for project
   - Regular community meetings during construction
   - Community monitoring and social audits (where applicable)
   - Incorporate community feedback in execution

10.4 COORDINATION WITH LOCAL GOVERNMENT AND ELECTED REPRESENTATIVES

A. LOCAL GOVERNMENT COORDINATION
   - Coordinate with Tehsil and District administration
   - Obtain necessary NOCs and permits
   - Coordinate road closures and traffic management
   - Inform about project progress and issues
   - Seek support for land acquisition, law and order

B. ELECTED REPRESENTATIVES LIAISON
   - MNAs, MPAs, local councilors often interested in projects in constituency
   - Provide regular updates proactively
   - Respond to queries and requests professionally
   - Invite to inaugurations and milestones
   - Manage expectations (technical vs. political considerations)
   - Maintain political neutrality and professionalism

C. PROTOCOL AND ETIQUETTE
   - Understand protocol for dealing with officials and representatives
   - Appropriate respect and courtesy
   - Timely and accurate information
   - Avoid commitments beyond authority
   - Escalate policy or political matters to Divisional Officer

10.5 MEDIA RELATIONS

A. MEDIA INTERACTION GUIDELINES
   - Generally, media interaction handled by Divisional Officer or designated
     spokesperson
   - SDO may interact for routine project information
   - Stick to facts, avoid speculation or opinion
   - Positive and professional demeanor
   - Avoid "no comment" (provide basic info or refer to spokesperson)

B. PROACTIVE MEDIA ENGAGEMENT
   - Invite media to project inaugurations and milestones
   - Provide press releases (through Divisional Officer)
   - Use media to communicate project benefits and progress
   - Build positive public image of department

C. CRISIS COMMUNICATION
   - In case of accident, failure, or controversy:
   - Inform Divisional Officer immediately
   - Coordinate response with DO and departmental spokesperson
   - Provide accurate information, avoid cover-up
   - Express concern and commitment to resolution
   - Avoid blame or defensive statements

10.6 TRANSPARENCY AND ACCOUNTABILITY

A. PROACTIVE DISCLOSURE
   - Display project information boards at sites (cost, timeline, contractor,
     SDO contact)
   - Publish progress on departmental website/portal
   - Make project documents available (per Right to Information Act)
   - Explain expenditures and progress to public

B. CITIZEN FEEDBACK MECHANISMS
   - Provide multiple channels for feedback (helpline, email, online portal)
   - Respond to feedback promptly and courteously
   - Act on valid feedback
   - Report back on actions taken

C. SOCIAL AUDIT AND COMMUNITY MONITORING
   - Where applicable (e.g., PSDP projects), facilitate social audits
   - Community monitoring committees inspect projects
   - Provide information and site access
   - Address concerns raised
   - Build trust through openness

D. ACCOUNTABILITY TO PUBLIC
   - Recognize duty to public as taxpayers and beneficiaries
   - Execute projects with integrity and value for money
   - Maintain highest professional and ethical standards
   - Subject to accountability (departmental, legal, public)
   - Demonstrate responsible stewardship of public resources

CRITICAL PROVISIONS SUMMARY
================================================================================

**PART A: BNR 1994 FOUNDATION (PARA 1.70)**
- Sub-Division: Field unit within Division, headed by Sub-Divisional Officer
- SDO ranks: Executive Engineer, Assistant Executive Engineer, Assistant
  Engineer, or Engineer Subordinate
- Responsible to Divisional Officer for management and execution of works in
  Sub-Division
- Role: Assistant to Divisional Officer for field-level operations
- Government sanction required for initial constitution of Sub-Division
- SDO transfer requires higher authority approval (not Divisional Officer alone)
- Primary training ground for new engineers
- SDO to DO relationship analogous to Divisional Accountant to DO (supervisory)

**PART B: USA ENHANCEMENTS (IBC 2024)**
- Deputy Building Official / Inspector role with delegated authority
- Comprehensive inspection requirements (10 types: foundation, slab, frame,
  energy, special, final, etc.)
- Right of entry for inspections with proper procedures (credentials, warrants)
- Authority to issue notices, stop work orders, unsafe structure notices
- Official records maintenance (inspections, notices, 5-year retention)
- Minimum qualifications: 5 years experience, certification
- Liability protection for good faith actions

**PART C: UK ENHANCEMENTS (DMRB)**
- Project Manager role for Sub-Division works
- Operational planning: scope, programme, budget, stakeholders, resources
- Quality plan development and implementation
- Overseeing Organisation representative: monitor construction compliance
- Competence assessment: self and team
- Site supervision and safety oversight

**PART D: INDIA ENHANCEMENTS (CPWD)**
- Sub-Divisional Officer = Assistant Executive Engineer (AEE) rank typically
- Responsible for field execution and contract management
- Quality responsibility: officer recording measurements responsible for quality
- Measurement Book entries and payment recommendations
- Completion procedures: provisional and final (with DO/T/S Authority)
- Testing coordination and quality control

**PART E: PAKISTAN-SPECIFIC CONTEXT**
- Qualifications: Bachelor's Civil + PEC RE + 5 years experience (2 supervisory)
- Reports to Divisional Officer, supervises AEs/JEs/field staff
- Geographic scope: 1-3 tehsils, typically Rs. 200-500M annual budget
- Technical authority: Recommend TS up to Rs. 5M, accord petty repairs up to
  Rs. 500K
- Financial powers: Certify payments up to Rs. 10M/month, petty expenditure Rs.
  100K
- Contractual authority: Site instructions, variations up to 2%/Rs. 1M, time
  extensions 30 days
- Emergency powers: Sanction up to Rs. 2M (report within 48 hours)
- Reporting: Daily diary, weekly progress, monthly Sub-Division report

**PART F: MODERN SUBDIVISION MANAGEMENT**
- Digital tools: Mobile inspection apps, e-measurement books, drones, PMIS, GIS
- Data-driven decisions: KPI dashboards, predictive analytics
- Lean construction: Waste elimination, pull planning, visual management
- Sustainability: Green construction, environmental compliance, waste management
- Continuous improvement: Kaizen, innovation, lessons learned

**PART G: FIELD OPERATIONS AND SITE SUPERVISION**
- Daily site visits to all active sites, comprehensive checklist
- Daily diary maintenance with all site activities
- Inspection stages: before covering, during construction, after completion
- Material inspection and approval process
- Progress monitoring: physical, financial, S-curve analysis
- Site meetings: daily toolbox, weekly progress, monthly review

**PART H: QUALITY MANAGEMENT AND COMPLIANCE**
- Quality assurance framework: policy, planning, control, audits
- Testing and laboratory coordination: approved labs, proper sampling
- Non-conformance management: NCR, corrective action, root cause analysis
- Compliance: codes, contracts, regulations, audit preparedness

**PART I: PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT AND ETHICS**
- PEC Code of Ethics: public safety paramount, competence, integrity, fairness
- CPD requirements: 15 hours annually, formal education, on-the-job learning
- Career pathway: Management track (SDODOSEREOCE) or Specialist track
- Leadership skills: communication, team building, problem-solving, emotional
  intelligence
- Work-life balance and stress management

**PART J: COMMUNITY COORDINATION AND PUBLIC SERVICE**
- Public interface: face of department, professional and courteous service
- Complaint handling: receive, investigate, resolve within 7 days
- Community consultation and participation: pre, during, post-construction
- Local government and elected representatives coordination
- Transparency: project boards, proactive disclosure, social audits
