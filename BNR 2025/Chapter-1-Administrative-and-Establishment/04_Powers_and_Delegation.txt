SECTION D: POWERS AND DELEGATION
================================================================================

Based on: BNR 1989/1994 + CPWD Powers (India) + DMRB Approval Framework (UK)

================================================================================
1.0 INTRODUCTION
================================================================================

This section defines the powers vested in various authorities for administration,
technical approval, financial sanction, and enforcement of the Building and Road
Code. It establishes clear delegation of authority to ensure efficient decision-
making while maintaining accountability and oversight.

Key Principles:
- Delegation based on competence, experience, and project complexity
- Financial powers linked to position and accountability
- Technical powers based on professional qualifications
- Checks and balances to prevent abuse of authority
- Transparency and audit trail for all decisions
- Emergency powers for urgent situations

================================================================================
2.0 ADMINISTRATIVE AND FINANCIAL POWERS
================================================================================

2.1 CHIEF ENGINEER - ADMINISTRATIVE POWERS

   General Administrative Powers:
   - Overall superintendence and control of building and road code administration
   - Formulation of policies and procedures within code framework
   - Approval of organizational structure and staffing
   - Transfer and posting of officers up to XEN level
   - Approval of annual work plans and budgets
   - Establishment of quality standards and performance metrics
   - Issuance of circulars, instructions, and interpretations of code provisions
   - Coordination with other government departments and agencies

   Financial Powers - Expenditure:
   - Administrative Approval: Up to PKR 100 million (per project)
   - Technical Sanction: Unlimited (all values)
   - Contract Award: Up to PKR 100 million (single contract)
   - Contingent Expenditure: Up to PKR 5 million (per occasion)
   - Purchase of Equipment: Up to PKR 50 million
   - Emergent Repairs: Up to PKR 25 million
   - Write-off of losses: Up to PKR 10 million

   Financial Powers - Revenue:
   - Remission of fees: Up to PKR 1 million (per case)
   - Waiver of penalties: Up to PKR 500,000 (per case)
   - Refund of deposits: Up to PKR 5 million

   Enforcement Powers:
   - Stop-work orders for any project
   - Revocation of permits for serious violations
   - Demolition orders for illegal/unsafe structures
   - Prosecution for code violations
   - Blacklisting of contractors/consultants (up to 3 years)
   - Imposition of penalties as per Section I

   Delegation Powers:
   - May delegate powers to SE/XEN within prescribed limits
   - Delegation must be in writing with clear scope and conditions
   - Delegation does not absolve CE of oversight responsibility

2.2 SUPERINTENDING ENGINEER - ADMINISTRATIVE POWERS

   General Administrative Powers:
   - Superintendence of circle/region
   - Implementation of policies and directives from CE
   - Coordination among divisions within circle
   - Transfer and posting of officers up to SDO level
   - Approval of divisional work plans
   - Performance monitoring and evaluation of subordinate offices
   - Public grievance redressal at circle level

   Financial Powers - Expenditure:
   - Administrative Approval: Up to PKR 50 million
   - Technical Sanction: Up to PKR 500 million
   - Contract Award: Up to PKR 50 million
   - Contingent Expenditure: Up to PKR 2 million
   - Purchase of Equipment: Up to PKR 20 million
   - Emergent Repairs: Up to PKR 10 million
   - Write-off of losses: Up to PKR 2 million

   Financial Powers - Revenue:
   - Remission of fees: Up to PKR 500,000
   - Waiver of penalties: Up to PKR 200,000
   - Refund of deposits: Up to PKR 2 million

   Enforcement Powers:
   - Stop-work orders within circle jurisdiction
   - Show-cause notices for violations
   - Permit suspension (subject to appeal to CE)
   - Fines and penalties up to PKR 5 million
   - Approval of alternate materials/methods (subject to technical justification)

   Reporting:
   - Monthly reports to Chief Engineer
   - Quarterly performance review meetings

2.3 EXECUTIVE ENGINEER - ADMINISTRATIVE POWERS

   General Administrative Powers:
   - Administration of division
   - Day-to-day operations and management
   - Supervision of sub-divisions
   - Assignment of work to subordinate officers
   - Approval of leave for staff (except XENs)
   - Divisional planning and budgeting
   - Coordination with local authorities and stakeholders

   Financial Powers - Expenditure:
   - Administrative Approval: Up to PKR 10 million
   - Technical Sanction: Up to PKR 100 million
   - Contract Award: Up to PKR 10 million
   - Contingent Expenditure: Up to PKR 500,000
   - Purchase of Equipment: Up to PKR 5 million
   - Emergent Repairs: Up to PKR 5 million
   - Petty Works (combined AA and TS): Up to PKR 5 million annually
   - Write-off of losses: Up to PKR 500,000

   Financial Powers - Revenue:
   - Remission of fees: Up to PKR 200,000
   - Waiver of penalties: Up to PKR 100,000
   - Refund of deposits: Up to PKR 1 million

   Permit and Plan Approval Powers:
   - Building permits: Commercial/residential (low-rise, 7-23m height)
   - Structural design approval: Up to 5 stories (standard construction)
   - Road projects: Local roads and streets (not highways)
   - Drainage and water supply projects: Local systems
   - Amendments to approved plans (within 10% scope change)

   Enforcement Powers:
   - Stop-work notices
   - Violation notices and correction orders
   - Permit suspension (temporary, pending SE review)
   - Fines and penalties up to PKR 1 million
   - Initiation of prosecution proceedings (with SE approval)

   Reporting:
   - Monthly reports to Superintending Engineer
   - Quarterly project status updates

2.4 SUB-DIVISIONAL OFFICER - ADMINISTRATIVE POWERS

   General Administrative Powers:
   - Management of sub-division
   - Field supervision and enforcement
   - Inspection scheduling and oversight
   - Supervision of inspectors and field staff
   - Complaints investigation
   - Public interface and customer service

   Financial Powers - Expenditure:
   - Technical Sanction: Up to PKR 10 million
   - Petty Works: Up to PKR 1 million (combined AA and TS)
   - Contingent Expenditure: Up to PKR 100,000
   - Emergent Repairs: Up to PKR 1 million

   Financial Powers - Revenue:
   - Fee collection and accounting
   - Refund of deposits: Up to PKR 250,000

   Permit and Plan Approval Powers:
   - Building permits: Residential (single/two-family, <7m height)
   - Minor commercial: Shops, offices (up to 500 sq m)
   - Residential additions and alterations
   - Temporary structures and permits
   - Certificates of Occupancy (after final inspection)

   Inspection Powers:
   - Schedule and conduct all required inspections
   - Foundation inspections (before concrete pour)
   - Structural frame inspections
   - MEP rough-in inspections
   - Final inspections
   - Complaint-based inspections
   - Random quality audits

   Enforcement Powers:
   - Stop-work notices (immediate, verbal; written confirmation within 24 hours)
   - Violation notices with correction deadlines
   - Re-inspection fees for non-compliance
   - Recommendation for permit revocation (to XEN)
   - Fines and penalties up to PKR 200,000

   Reporting:
   - Weekly reports to Executive Engineer
   - Monthly inspection statistics and violation summaries

2.5 ASSISTANT ENGINEER - ADMINISTRATIVE POWERS

   General Administrative Powers:
   - Supervision of assigned area or project
   - Field inspections and quality control
   - Assistance to SDO in plan review and enforcement
   - Documentation and record keeping

   Financial Powers:
   - Petty expenditure: Up to PKR 100,000 (with SDO approval)

   Permit and Plan Approval Powers:
   - Minor residential permits: Single-family dwellings (simple construction)
   - Residential alterations: Interior renovations, additions <50 sq m
   - Temporary structures: Tents, sheds (non-permanent)
   - Recommendation for approval to SDO (for larger projects)

   Inspection Powers:
   - All types of inspections (foundation, structural, MEP, final)
   - Site visits and quality checks
   - Material testing coordination
   - Documentation of conditions and deficiencies

   Enforcement Powers:
   - Verbal warnings for minor violations
   - Written violation notices (countersigned by SDO)
   - Stop-work recommendations to SDO (for serious safety issues)

   Reporting:
   - Daily inspection logs
   - Weekly reports to SDO

2.6 SUB-ENGINEER / OVERSEER - FIELD POWERS

   Inspection Powers:
   - Routine site inspections under supervision
   - Material verification and sampling
   - Measurement and quantity checks
   - Photographic documentation

   Enforcement Powers:
   - Observation of violations (report to AE/SDO)
   - No independent enforcement authority (acts under supervision)

   Reporting:
   - Daily field reports to AE

3.0 TECHNICAL APPROVAL POWERS
================================================================================

3.1 STRUCTURAL DESIGN APPROVAL AUTHORITY

   Chief Engineer:
   - All high-rise buildings (>60m or >20 stories)
   - Special structures (stadiums, auditoriums, long-span structures)
   - Critical facilities (hospitals, emergency response centers)
   - Innovative/unconventional structural systems
   - Structures in high seismic zones (Zone 4) ⭐
   - Peer review and technical audit recommendations

   Superintending Engineer:
   - Mid-rise buildings (23-60m or 7-20 stories)
   - Bridges and highway structures (all types)
   - Industrial structures (warehouses, factories)
   - Assembly occupancies (>1,000 occupants)
   - Retaining structures >6m height
   - Structures in seismic Zone 3 ⭐

   Executive Engineer:
   - Low-rise commercial/residential (7-23m or 3-7 stories)
   - Standard construction (conventional framing systems)
   - Small bridges and culverts (span <12m)
   - Retaining structures 3-6m height
   - Standard foundation systems (shallow footings, grade beams)
   - Structures in seismic Zones 1 and 2A ⭐

   Sub-Divisional Officer:
   - Low-rise residential (<7m or 1-2 stories)
   - Simple construction (load-bearing masonry, light framing)
   - Residential additions and alterations (non-structural)
   - Retaining structures <3m height

3.2 MEP SYSTEMS APPROVAL AUTHORITY ⭐

   Chief Engineer (or designated ME/EE):
   - Complex MEP systems (large commercial, high-rise)
   - Central chilled water plants >500 TR
   - High voltage systems (>11 kV)
   - Emergency power systems >1 MVA
   - District cooling/heating systems
   - Renewable energy systems >500 kW ⭐

   Superintending Engineer (with ME/EE input):
   - Standard commercial MEP systems
   - HVAC systems 100-500 TR
   - Electrical systems 415V-11kV
   - Fire protection systems (all types)
   - Building automation and controls ⭐

   Executive Engineer (with ME/EE review):
   - Residential and small commercial MEP
   - Split AC systems and package units (<100 TR)
   - Low voltage electrical (230/415V)
   - Standard plumbing and drainage
   - Solar PV systems <100 kW ⭐

3.3 ROAD AND HIGHWAY DESIGN APPROVAL AUTHORITY

   Chief Engineer (Roads):
   - Highways and expressways (all classes)
   - Major urban arterials and ring roads
   - Grade-separated interchanges
   - Tunnels and major earthworks
   - Pavement design for heavy traffic (>5,000 AADT)

   Superintending Engineer (Roads):
   - Collector roads and secondary highways
   - Urban main streets and avenues
   - At-grade intersections and roundabouts
   - Pavement design for medium traffic (1,000-5,000 AADT)
   - Road rehabilitation and overlay projects

   Executive Engineer (Roads):
   - Local roads and streets
   - Rural access roads
   - Pavement design for low traffic (<1,000 AADT)
   - Road maintenance and minor improvements

3.4 BRIDGE DESIGN APPROVAL AUTHORITY

   Chief Engineer (or Additional CE - Bridges):
   - Major bridges (span >60m or total length >300m)
   - Cable-stayed, suspension, arch bridges
   - Bridges in high seismic zones or over major rivers
   - Railway over-bridges and flyovers

   Superintending Engineer (Bridges):
   - Medium bridges (span 30-60m)
   - Standard girder bridges (RCC, pre-stressed)
   - Box culverts and pipe culverts (all sizes)
   - Pedestrian bridges and underpasses

   Executive Engineer:
   - Small bridges and culverts (span <30m)
   - Standard slab and girder bridges
   - Hume pipe culverts and minor drainage structures

4.0 ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTY POWERS
================================================================================

4.1 STOP-WORK ORDER AUTHORITY

   Immediate Stop-Work (Verbal + Written Confirmation within 24 hours):
   - Any technical officer (AE and above) for imminent safety hazards
   - Examples: unsafe excavation, overloaded structure, collapse risk

   Formal Stop-Work Order:
   - Sub-Divisional Officer: For violations of approved plans, missing inspections,
     working without permit
   - Executive Engineer: For serious code violations, structural safety concerns
   - Superintending Engineer: For repeated violations, unsafe structures
   - Chief Engineer: For major public safety threats, illegal construction

   Lifting of Stop-Work Order:
   - Same authority that issued order (or higher)
   - Only after violations corrected and verified by inspection
   - May require engineering assessment and certification

4.2 PERMIT REVOCATION AUTHORITY

   Temporary Suspension (pending correction):
   - Executive Engineer: Up to 90 days
   - Superintending Engineer: Up to 180 days

   Permanent Revocation:
   - Chief Engineer: All cases
   - Grounds: Fraud, misrepresentation, serious violations, public safety threat

   Appeal Process:
   - Appeal to next higher authority within 30 days
   - Final departmental appeal to Chief Engineer
   - Judicial review through courts

4.3 DEMOLITION ORDER AUTHORITY

   For illegal or unsafe structures:

   Executive Engineer:
   - Unauthorized minor structures (<50 sq m)
   - Temporary structures without permits
   - Structures in immediate danger of collapse

   Superintending Engineer:
   - Unauthorized structures (50-500 sq m)
   - Structures in violation of setbacks/zoning
   - Unsafe structures after engineering assessment

   Chief Engineer:
   - Major unauthorized structures (>500 sq m)
   - Structures in protected/prohibited areas
   - Structures posing significant public safety risk

   Due Process:
   - Show-cause notice with opportunity to respond (15-30 days)
   - Engineering assessment and documentation
   - Demolition order with appeal rights
   - Demolition carried out after appeal period or rejection of appeal
   - Cost recovery from owner

4.4 PENALTY AND FINE IMPOSITION

   Sub-Divisional Officer:
   - Fines: Up to PKR 200,000
   - Violations: Working without inspection, minor code violations

   Executive Engineer:
   - Fines: Up to PKR 1 million
   - Violations: Unauthorized construction, plan deviations, safety violations

   Superintending Engineer:
   - Fines: Up to PKR 5 million
   - Violations: Serious safety violations, repeated offenses, fraudulent submissions

   Chief Engineer:
   - Fines: Unlimited (as per code provisions and provincial laws)
   - Violations: Major public safety threats, willful violations, professional negligence

   Payment and Appeal:
   - Payment within 30 days of notice
   - Appeal to next higher authority
   - Non-payment: Prosecution and recovery as arrears of land revenue

5.0 ALTERNATE MATERIALS AND METHODS APPROVAL ⭐
================================================================================

5.1 APPROVAL PROCESS

   For materials, designs, or methods not explicitly covered in code:

   Application:
   - Submitted by design professional or applicant
   - Technical justification and supporting documentation
   - Evidence of equivalent/superior performance
   - Test data and certifications
   - Proposed conditions and monitoring

   Review:
   - Executive Engineer: Minor variations (e.g., material substitutions within same class)
   - Superintending Engineer: Moderate variations (e.g., alternative structural systems
     for low-rise)
   - Chief Engineer + Technical Advisory Committee: Major variations (e.g., innovative
     systems, new materials, performance-based design)

   Approval Authority:
   - XEN: Up to 10% variation from standard specifications
   - SE: 10-25% variation or new materials with established track record
   - CE: >25% variation, completely new systems, or research/experimental applications

   Conditions:
   - Enhanced inspections and testing
   - Third-party review and monitoring
   - Performance guarantees and warranties
   - Documentation and reporting requirements
   - Pilot project restrictions (if applicable)

5.2 PERFORMANCE-BASED DESIGN APPROVAL ⭐

   For projects using performance-based approaches (instead of prescriptive code):

   Allowed for:
   - Fire protection and life safety design
   - Energy efficiency and sustainability (beyond prescriptive requirements)
   - Structural design for special structures
   - Alternative compliance pathways

   Requirements:
   - Peer review by independent third-party experts
   - Computational modeling and simulation
   - Factor of safety considerations
   - Acceptance criteria and performance objectives
   - Approval by Chief Engineer + Technical Advisory Committee

6.0 EMERGENCY POWERS
================================================================================

6.1 NATURAL DISASTER OR EMERGENCY SITUATIONS

   Chief Engineer - Emergency Powers:
   - Waive normal approval processes for emergency repairs/reconstruction
   - Expedited permit processing (within 48 hours)
   - Relaxation of documentation requirements (temporary)
   - Emergency procurement without normal tendering (up to PKR 50 million)
   - Deployment of staff and resources
   - Coordination with disaster management authorities

   Duration: Up to 3 months from declaration of emergency (extendable by government)

   Post-Emergency Regularization:
   - All emergency approvals/expenditures documented
   - Formal approvals obtained within 90 days of emergency end
   - Audit and review by oversight authorities

6.2 PUBLIC SAFETY EMERGENCIES

   Any Engineer (AE and above):
   - Immediate action to protect public safety (evacuations, barricading, emergency
     shoring, etc.)
   - Notification to supervisory officer within 4 hours
   - Written report and recommendations within 24 hours

   Executive Engineer and above:
   - Emergency demolition of imminently dangerous structures
   - Temporary closure of roads/buildings
   - Emergency repairs to public infrastructure
   - Cost recovery from responsible parties

7.0 DELEGATION FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

7.1 PRINCIPLES OF DELEGATION

   - Delegation must be specific, clear, and in writing
   - Delegatee must have requisite qualifications and experience
   - Delegation does not absolve delegator of oversight responsibility
   - Periodic review and reporting required
   - Delegation can be withdrawn at any time
   - Emergency or unusual cases referred to delegator

7.2 TEMPORARY DELEGATION

   During absence or vacancy:
   - CE powers: Temporarily to senior-most SE
   - SE powers: To another SE in same region or senior XEN
   - XEN powers: To SDO (with limitations)

   Notification:
   - Formal order specifying scope, duration, and limitations
   - Communicated to all concerned officers and public

7.3 SPECIAL PROJECT DELEGATION ⭐

   For major projects (e.g., mega infrastructure, public-private partnerships):
   - Special Project Director appointed (SE or XEN rank)
   - Enhanced financial and technical powers for project duration
   - Direct reporting to CE
   - Dedicated team and resources
   - Performance-based accountability

8.0 INTER-AGENCY COORDINATION POWERS ⭐
================================================================================

8.1 COORDINATION WITH UTILITIES

   Chief Engineer:
   - MoU and coordination agreements with WASA, DISCOs, Gas Companies
   - Resolution of inter-agency disputes
   - Joint inspection protocols for major projects

   Executive Engineer:
   - Routine coordination for utility connections
   - NOC verification and compliance
   - Joint inspections at site level

8.2 COORDINATION WITH DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITIES

   For projects in areas under development authority jurisdiction:
   - Joint review committees (CE level)
   - Harmonization of standards and requirements
   - Mutual recognition of approvals (where applicable)
   - Dispute resolution mechanisms

8.3 COORDINATION WITH FIRE DEPARTMENT ⭐

   Superintending Engineer (or designated Fire Protection Officer):
   - Joint fire safety inspections for high-rise and special occupancies
   - Review of fire protection system designs
   - Approval of fire safety plans
   - Coordination on fire safety training and drills

8.4 COORDINATION WITH ENVIRONMENTAL AUTHORITIES

   Chief Engineer / Superintending Engineer:
   - EPA clearance coordination for scheduled projects
   - Environmental compliance monitoring
   - Green building and sustainability initiatives

9.0 APPELLATE AND REVIEW POWERS
================================================================================

9.1 ADMINISTRATIVE APPEALS

   First Appeal:
   - Against AE/SDO decision: To Executive Engineer (within 30 days)
   - Against XEN decision: To Superintending Engineer (within 30 days)
   - Against SE decision: To Chief Engineer (within 30 days)

   Final Departmental Appeal:
   - To Chief Engineer (final authority within department)

   Judicial Review:
   - High Court (if exhausted departmental appeals)

   Timeline for Appeal Decisions:
   - XEN: 15 days
   - SE: 30 days
   - CE: 45 days

9.2 TECHNICAL REVIEW AND AUDIT

   Superintending Engineer:
   - Random audit of divisional approvals and inspections (10% sample)
   - Quality control inspections
   - Review of XEN technical sanctions and approvals

   Chief Engineer:
   - Third-party technical audits for major projects
   - Peer review of complex/innovative designs
   - Investigation of structural failures or major defects

9.3 FINANCIAL AUDIT

   Departmental Accounts Officer:
   - Monthly financial reconciliation
   - Audit of expenditures and revenues
   - Compliance with financial rules

   Auditor General:
   - Annual audit of department
   - Special audits as directed by government

10.0 BLACKLISTING AND DEBARMENT POWERS
================================================================================

10.1 CONTRACTORS AND CONSULTANTS

   Grounds for Blacklisting:
   - Fraudulent practices or misrepresentation
   - Serious quality deficiencies or safety violations
   - Abandonment of project without cause
   - Repeated failures to meet contractual obligations
   - Conviction for bribery or corruption

   Authority:
   - Superintending Engineer: Blacklisting up to 1 year (circle level)
   - Chief Engineer: Blacklisting up to 3 years (province-wide)
   - Secretary/Government: Blacklisting >3 years or permanent

   Process:
   - Show-cause notice with opportunity to respond
   - Inquiry and hearing (if requested)
   - Blacklisting order with specific grounds and duration
   - Appeal to next higher authority

10.2 DESIGN PROFESSIONALS

   Grounds for Action:
   - Negligent or incompetent design
   - Code violations or safety lapses
   - False certification or fraudulent submissions
   - Professional misconduct

   Authority:
   - Chief Engineer: Debarment from departmental projects (up to 5 years)
   - PEC Ethics Committee: Professional disciplinary action (warning to de-registration)

11.0 RECORDS AND TRANSPARENCY ⭐
================================================================================

11.1 DECISION DOCUMENTATION

   All decisions involving exercise of powers must be documented with:
   - Unique reference number and date
   - Clear statement of decision and authority
   - Rationale and supporting information
   - Conditions and limitations (if any)
   - Appeal rights and procedures

11.2 PUBLIC ACCESS TO INFORMATION

   Under Right to Information laws:
   - Approved building permits and plans (redacted for privacy)
   - Administrative approval and technical sanction summaries
   - Enforcement actions and penalties
   - Contractor/consultant blacklisting
   - Departmental policies and circulars

   Exemptions:
   - Security-sensitive information
   - Personal information (addresses, etc.)
   - Ongoing investigations or litigation

11.3 DIGITAL RECORDS AND E-GOVERNANCE ⭐

   - All approvals, permits, and enforcement actions recorded digitally
   - Online tracking and status updates
   - Digital signatures and authentication
   - Audit trail and version control
   - Backup and archival as per record retention policy

================================================================================
12.0 ACCOUNTABILITY AND OVERSIGHT
================================================================================

12.1 PERFORMANCE MONITORING

   - Key performance indicators (KPIs) for each position
   - Monthly/quarterly performance reports
   - Customer satisfaction surveys
   - Third-party performance audits

12.2 COMPLAINTS AND GRIEVANCES

   Public Complaint Mechanisms:
   - Online complaint portal (e-Complaint System) ⭐
   - Toll-free helpline
   - Walk-in complaint centers
   - Investigation and resolution within 30 days
   - Escalation to higher authority if not resolved

12.3 DISCIPLINARY ACTION FOR MISUSE OF POWERS

   For officers misusing or exceeding delegated powers:
   - Minor misconduct: Warning, reprimand, adverse remarks
   - Serious misconduct: Suspension, demotion, dismissal
   - Criminal conduct: Referral to law enforcement

   Process:
   - Inquiry by senior officer
   - Opportunity for defense
   - Decision by competent authority (as per service rules)
   - Appeal rights
