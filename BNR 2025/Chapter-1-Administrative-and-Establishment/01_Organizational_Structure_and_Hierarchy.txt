CHAPTER 1 - ADMINISTRATIVE AND ESTABLISHMENT
SECTION A: ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE AND HIERARCHY
================================================================================

Based on: BNR 1989/1994 + IBC 2024 (USA) + DMRB (UK) + CPWD (India) + BNBC 2020 (Bangladesh) + UDA (Sri Lanka)
Adapted for Pakistan Context - Pakistan Engineering Council (PEC) Framework

================================================================================
1.0 INTRODUCTION
================================================================================

This section establishes the organizational structure for the administration and
enforcement of the Building and Road Code in Pakistan. The structure blends the
foundational framework of BNR 1989/1994 with modern international best practices
to ensure efficient, transparent, and professional delivery of public works and
building regulation services.

The organizational hierarchy is designed to:
- Maintain clear lines of authority and responsibility
- Enable effective decision-making at appropriate levels
- Ensure technical competence at all positions
- Facilitate coordination between departments and agencies
- Support digital transformation and modern service delivery ⭐

================================================================================
2.0 HIERARCHICAL STRUCTURE - BUILDING DEPARTMENT
================================================================================

2.1 CHIEF ENGINEER (CE) / BUILDING OFFICIAL ⭐

   Position Overview:
   - Apex technical and administrative authority for building regulation
   - Reports to: Secretary, Communications & Works Department
   - Qualifications: BE/BS in Civil Engineering + 25 years experience
   - PEC Registration: Required (Registered Engineer - Civil)

   Primary Responsibilities:
   a) Overall administration and enforcement of Building and Road Code
   b) Policy formulation and strategic planning
   c) Approval of major projects (>PKR 500 million)
   d) Inter-departmental coordination
   e) Budget preparation and financial management
   f) Performance monitoring and quality assurance
   g) Final authority on code interpretations
   h) Disaster preparedness and emergency response coordination ⭐

   Powers:
   - Grant or deny building permits for complex/high-risk structures
   - Issue stop-work orders and revocation of permits
   - Modify code requirements through alternate materials/methods process
   - Impose penalties for violations (as per Section I)
   - Delegate authority to subordinate officers
   - Establish inspection protocols and quality standards

2.2 SUPERINTENDING ENGINEER (SE) / DEPUTY BUILDING OFFICIAL ⭐

   Position Overview:
   - Circle/regional level authority
   - Reports to: Chief Engineer
   - Qualifications: BE/BS in Civil Engineering + 18 years experience
   - PEC Registration: Required (Registered Engineer - Civil)

   Primary Responsibilities:
   a) Supervision of multiple divisions within a circle
   b) Technical review and approval (PKR 100-500 million projects)
   c) Quality control and audit of subordinate offices
   d) Contract administration and dispute resolution
   e) Resource allocation within circle
   f) Monthly progress reporting to Chief Engineer
   g) Public grievance redressal at circle level

   Territorial Jurisdiction:
   - Each SE typically oversees 3-5 divisions
   - Circle may be provincial, regional, or specialized (e.g., Buildings Circle,
     Roads Circle, Bridges Circle)

2.3 EXECUTIVE ENGINEER (XEN) / PLANS EXAMINER ⭐

   Position Overview:
   - Division level authority
   - Reports to: Superintending Engineer
   - Qualifications: BE/BS in Civil Engineering + 10 years experience
   - PEC Registration: Required (Registered Engineer - Civil)

   Primary Responsibilities:
   a) Division-level administration and enforcement
   b) Plan review and permit issuance (PKR 10-100 million projects)
   c) Inspection scheduling and quality oversight
   d) Contract management and site supervision
   e) Preparation of estimates and technical specifications
   f) Maintenance of divisional records and documentation
   g) Coordination with local authorities and stakeholders
   h) Monthly divisional performance reports

   Territorial Jurisdiction:
   - Division typically covers a district or major municipality
   - May have specialized divisions (e.g., Building Plans Division, Road
     Construction Division, Maintenance Division)

2.4 SUB-DIVISIONAL OFFICER (SDO) / BUILDING INSPECTOR ⭐

   Position Overview:
   - Sub-division level authority
   - Reports to: Executive Engineer
   - Qualifications: BE/BS in Civil Engineering + 5 years experience
   - PEC Registration: Required (Registered Engineer - Civil)

   Primary Responsibilities:
   a) Sub-divisional administration and day-to-day enforcement
   b) Plan review and permit issuance for routine projects (<PKR 10 million)
   c) Site inspections (foundation, structural, MEP, final)
   d) Monitoring of ongoing construction activities
   e) Complaints investigation and violation notices
   f) Preparation of sub-divisional estimates and reports
   g) Supervision of field staff (Sub-Engineers, Inspectors)
   h) Coordination with municipal authorities

   Inspection Authority:
   - Foundation inspections (before concrete pour)
   - Structural frame inspections (beam/column reinforcement)
   - MEP rough-in inspections
   - Final inspections and certificate of occupancy issuance

2.5 ASSISTANT ENGINEER (AE) / ASSISTANT BUILDING INSPECTOR ⭐

   Position Overview:
   - Field supervision and inspection
   - Reports to: Sub-Divisional Officer
   - Qualifications: BE/BS in Civil Engineering + 2 years experience
   - PEC Registration: Required (Registered Engineer - Civil)

   Primary Responsibilities:
   a) Field inspections and quality control
   b) Assistance in plan review (simple residential/commercial)
   c) Site supervision and measurement
   d) Documentation and record keeping
   e) Preparation of inspection reports
   f) Support to SDO in enforcement activities
   g) Public interface and customer service

   Areas of Specialization (may be assigned):
   - Structural inspections
   - Plumbing and drainage inspections ⭐
   - Electrical inspections ⭐
   - HVAC and mechanical inspections ⭐
   - Fire protection systems inspections ⭐

2.6 SUB-ENGINEER (SE) / FIELD INSPECTOR

   Position Overview:
   - Field-level technical staff
   - Reports to: Assistant Engineer or Sub-Divisional Officer
   - Qualifications: Diploma in Civil Engineering (DAE) + 3 years experience
   - PEC Registration: Required (Registered Sub-Engineer)

   Primary Responsibilities:
   a) Routine site inspections
   b) Measurement and quantity verification
   c) Material testing coordination
   d) Site documentation and photography
   e) Assistance in enforcement actions
   f) Maintenance of site inspection logs

2.7 JUNIOR ENGINEER (JE) / ENTRY-LEVEL INSPECTOR

   Position Overview:
   - Entry-level technical position
   - Reports to: Sub-Engineer or Assistant Engineer
   - Qualifications: Diploma in Civil Engineering (DAE)
   - PEC Registration: Required (Registered Sub-Engineer)

   Primary Responsibilities:
   a) Basic site inspections under supervision
   b) Data collection and documentation
   c) Assistance in measurements and surveys
   d) Record maintenance and filing
   e) Public assistance and information provision

3.0 SPECIALIZED TECHNICAL DIVISIONS ⭐
================================================================================

3.1 STRUCTURAL ENGINEERING DIVISION

   Headed by: Executive Engineer (Structural) or Senior Structural Engineer
   Qualifications: MS in Structural Engineering or BE + 15 years experience

   Responsibilities:
   - Review of structural designs for complex buildings (>5 stories, special
     occupancies, seismic zones 2B-4)
   - Peer review of critical structures (hospitals, schools, stadiums)
   - Approval of alternate structural systems
   - Seismic design review and compliance ⭐
   - Post-disaster structural assessments ⭐

3.2 FIRE PROTECTION AND LIFE SAFETY DIVISION ⭐

   Headed by: Fire Protection Engineer or Deputy Chief Fire Officer
   Qualifications: BE in Fire Protection Engineering or Civil + Fire Safety
                   Certification (NFPA, ICC)

   Responsibilities:
   - Review of fire protection and life safety systems
   - Egress analysis and means of escape verification
   - Fire alarm and suppression system approval
   - High-rise building fire safety compliance
   - Coordination with Fire Department and Rescue Services
   - Fire safety inspections and testing

3.3 ELECTRICAL AND MECHANICAL ENGINEERING DIVISION ⭐

   Headed by: Executive Engineer (Electrical/Mechanical)
   Qualifications: BE in Electrical or Mechanical Engineering + 10 years experience

   Responsibilities:
   - Review of electrical installations and power systems
   - HVAC system design review
   - Elevator and escalator safety compliance
   - Energy code compliance (Chapter 6) ⭐
   - Renewable energy system approvals (solar PV, etc.) ⭐
   - Building automation and control systems ⭐

3.4 PLUMBING AND SANITARY DIVISION ⭐

   Headed by: Senior Engineer (Public Health Engineering)
   Qualifications: BE in Civil/Public Health Engineering + 8 years experience

   Responsibilities:
   - Plumbing system design review
   - Water supply and drainage system approval
   - Rainwater harvesting system compliance ⭐
   - Wastewater treatment and disposal systems
   - Cross-connection control and backflow prevention
   - Coordination with WASA/water utilities

3.5 ACCESSIBILITY AND UNIVERSAL DESIGN DIVISION ⭐

   Headed by: Accessibility Coordinator/Senior Architect
   Qualifications: Architecture/Civil Engineering + Universal Design Certification

   Responsibilities:
   - Accessibility compliance review (Chapter 7)
   - Universal design consultation
   - Barrier-free access verification
   - Assistive technology integration
   - Coordination with disability advocacy groups

4.0 ROADS AND HIGHWAYS DEPARTMENT STRUCTURE
================================================================================

4.1 CHIEF ENGINEER (ROADS & HIGHWAYS)

   Position Overview:
   - Apex authority for road network planning, design, construction, and maintenance
   - Reports to: Secretary, Communications & Works Department
   - Qualifications: BE/BS in Civil Engineering + 25 years experience
   - PEC Registration: Required (Registered Engineer - Civil)

   Primary Responsibilities:
   a) Strategic planning for road network development
   b) Major highway project approvals (>PKR 1 billion)
   c) Pavement management and asset management systems ⭐
   d) Traffic safety and road safety audits ⭐
   e) Coordination with National Highway Authority (NHA)
   f) Budget allocation and financial oversight
   g) Policy formulation and standards development

4.2 ADDITIONAL CHIEF ENGINEER (PLANNING & DESIGN)

   Responsibilities:
   - Road network planning and feasibility studies
   - Geometric design standards and specifications
   - Bridge and structure design review
   - Traffic engineering and management ⭐
   - Environmental impact assessments
   - GIS and road inventory systems ⭐

4.3 ADDITIONAL CHIEF ENGINEER (CONSTRUCTION & MAINTENANCE)

   Responsibilities:
   - Construction project management
   - Quality control and assurance programs
   - Road maintenance and rehabilitation
   - Emergency response and disaster management
   - Contract administration
   - Material testing and quality certification

4.4 SUPERINTENDING ENGINEERS (ROADS)

   Specialized Circles:
   - Provincial Highways Circle
   - District Roads Circle
   - Bridges and Structures Circle
   - Road Maintenance Circle
   - Traffic Engineering Circle ⭐

4.5 DIVISIONAL STRUCTURE (ROADS)

   Each division typically handles:
   - Road construction projects (new alignments)
   - Road improvement and widening
   - Pavement rehabilitation and overlay
   - Bridge construction and maintenance
   - Drainage and earthworks
   - Traffic engineering and safety improvements

5.0 COORDINATION AND INTEGRATION MECHANISMS ⭐
================================================================================

5.1 INTER-DEPARTMENTAL COORDINATION COMMITTEE

   Composition:
   - Chief Engineer (Buildings) - Chairman
   - Chief Engineer (Roads & Highways) - Co-Chairman
   - Representative from Environment Protection Agency (EPA)
   - Representative from Fire Department
   - Representative from Development Authorities (LDA, RDA, CDA, etc.)
   - Representative from WASA/Water Utilities
   - Representative from Electric Supply Companies (DISCOs)
   - Representative from Gas Utilities
   - Representative from Traffic Police

   Meeting Frequency: Quarterly

   Functions:
   - Coordination on major development projects
   - Resolution of inter-departmental issues
   - Policy harmonization and standardization
   - Information sharing and best practices
   - Joint inspections for complex projects

5.2 TECHNICAL ADVISORY COMMITTEE

   Composition:
   - Senior professors from engineering universities (3-5 members)
   - PEC representatives (2 members)
   - Industry experts (3 members from consulting firms)
   - Representatives from professional bodies (Pakistan Institute of Architects,
     Institution of Engineers Pakistan)

   Functions:
   - Technical guidance on code updates
   - Review of complex/innovative designs
   - Resolution of technical disputes
   - Research and development recommendations
   - International best practice adoption

5.3 ONE-WINDOW FACILITATION CENTERS ⭐

   Established at District/Division level to provide:
   - Single point of contact for applicants
   - Integrated permit processing
   - Multi-agency coordination
   - Status tracking and transparency
   - Grievance redressal

6.0 STAFFING NORMS AND ORGANIZATIONAL CAPACITY
================================================================================

6.1 STAFFING RATIOS (BUILDING DEPARTMENT)

   For urban areas with active construction:
   - 1 Plans Examiner (XEN/AE) per 500 permit applications annually
   - 1 Building Inspector (SDO/AE) per 250 active construction sites
   - 1 Specialized Inspector (Fire/Electrical/Plumbing) per 1,000 permits annually

   Minimum staff for Division:
   - 1 Executive Engineer
   - 2-3 Sub-Divisional Officers
   - 4-6 Assistant Engineers
   - 8-10 Sub-Engineers
   - Support staff (draftsmen, clerks, data entry operators)

6.2 STAFFING RATIOS (ROADS DEPARTMENT)

   Based on road network length and traffic volume:
   - 1 Division per 500-800 km of road network
   - 1 Sub-Division per 150-250 km
   - Maintenance staff: 1 crew (10-15 workers) per 50 km of paved roads

6.3 PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT REQUIREMENTS ⭐

   All technical staff must complete:
   - Minimum 40 hours continuing education annually
   - Code update training (when new code editions adopted)
   - Specialized certification programs (ICC, NFPA, etc.) for inspectors
   - Digital systems training (e-permitting, GIS, BIM) ⭐
   - Customer service and communication skills

7.0 PERFORMANCE METRICS AND ACCOUNTABILITY ⭐
================================================================================

7.1 KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS (KPIs)

   Building Department:
   - Average plan review time: ≤30 days for commercial, ≤15 days for residential
   - Inspection request response time: ≤48 hours
   - Permit processing time: ≤7 days after plan approval
   - Customer satisfaction score: ≥80%
   - Code violation correction rate: ≥85%
   - Digital permit adoption rate: ≥60% by 2027 ⭐

   Roads Department:
   - Project completion rate: ≥90% within scheduled time
   - Quality compliance rate: ≥95% of test results meeting specifications
   - Road condition index: ≥70% of network in good/fair condition
   - Safety audit completion: 100% of major projects
   - Maintenance response time: ≤24 hours for emergencies

7.2 PERFORMANCE MONITORING SYSTEMS ⭐

   - Quarterly performance reviews by supervisory officers
   - Annual performance evaluations linked to promotion eligibility
   - Public dashboard for permit processing metrics (transparency)
   - Third-party audits for quality assurance
   - Citizen feedback mechanisms (online surveys, complaint portals)

================================================================================
8.0 TERRITORIAL JURISDICTION AND APPLICABILITY
================================================================================

8.1 PROVINCIAL JURISDICTION

   This organizational structure applies to:
   - All provincial government projects (buildings and roads)
   - Cantonment areas (coordination with Cantonment Boards)
   - Areas not under development authority jurisdiction

8.2 DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITIES

   Major development authorities maintain parallel structures:
   - Lahore Development Authority (LDA)
   - Rawalpindi Development Authority (RDA)
   - Capital Development Authority (CDA)
   - Karachi Development Authority (KDA)
   - Multan Development Authority (MDA)
   - Faisalabad Development Authority (FDA)

   Coordination Mechanism: Joint review committees for projects at boundaries

8.3 MUNICIPAL CORPORATIONS

   For smaller municipalities and towns:
   - May adopt simplified organizational structure
   - Minimum requirement: 1 qualified Building Inspector (PEC registered)
   - Technical support from district/division level offices
   - Option to outsource plan reviews to empaneled consultants ⭐
