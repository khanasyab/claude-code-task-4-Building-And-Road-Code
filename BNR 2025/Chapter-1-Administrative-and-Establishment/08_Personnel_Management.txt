1.0 INTRODUCTION
================================================================================

This section establishes the personnel management framework for the Building
and Road Code administration, covering recruitment, training, performance
management, career development, and employee welfare to ensure a competent,
motivated, and professional workforce.

Key Objectives:
- Attract and retain qualified technical professionals
- Ensure competency through training and development
- Merit-based recruitment and promotion
- Fair and transparent performance management
- Employee motivation and job satisfaction
- Succession planning and knowledge management
- Work-life balance and employee welfare

================================================================================
2.0 ORGANIZATIONAL STAFFING STRUCTURE
================================================================================

2.1 SANCTIONED POSTS (TYPICAL DIVISION)

   Technical Staff:
   - Executive Engineer (XEN): 1 post (BS-18)
   - Sub-Divisional Officer (SDO): 2-3 posts (BS-17)
   - Assistant Engineer (AE): 4-6 posts (BS-16)
   - Sub-Engineer (SE): 8-12 posts (BS-11-14)
   - Junior Engineer (JE)/Overseer: 10-15 posts (BS-7-9)

   Supporting Staff:
   - Draftsman/CAD Operator: 2-4 posts
   - Survey Assistant: 2-3 posts
   - Work Inspector: 3-5 posts
   - Laboratory Technician: 1-2 posts (if lab exists)

   Administrative Staff:
   - Accountant: 1 post
   - Clerk/Office Assistant: 3-5 posts
   - Computer Operator/Data Entry: 1-2 posts
   - Driver: 2-3 posts
   - Naib Qasid/Chowkidar: 2-3 posts

   Total Strength: Approximately 50-80 persons per division (varies by workload)

2.2 SPECIALIZED POSITIONS ⭐

   For Circle/Provincial Level:
   - Fire Protection Engineer (SE/XEN level)
   - Structural Engineer (XEN level)
   - Bridge Engineer (XEN level)
   - Traffic Engineer (XEN level)
   - Geotechnical Engineer (XEN level)
   - Electrical Engineer (XEN level)
   - Mechanical Engineer (XEN level)
   - IT Manager (BS-17)
   - Quality Assurance Manager (XEN level)
   - Safety Officer (BS-16)

2.3 STAFFING NORMS

   Based on Workload:
   - Building permits: 1 Plans Examiner per 500 applications/year
   - Inspections: 1 Inspector per 250 active sites
   - Road construction: 1 XEN per 500-800 km road network
   - Maintenance: 1 crew per 50 km paved roads

   Vacancy Management:
   - Target: ≤10% vacancy rate
   - Priority filling of critical positions (XEN, SDO, specialized engineers)
   - Temporary arrangements (additional charge, acting) until permanent filling

3.0 RECRUITMENT AND SELECTION
================================================================================

3.1 RECRUITMENT AUTHORITY

   - Provincial Public Service Commission (PPSC): For BS-16 and above
     (Gazetted posts: XEN, SDO, AE)
   - Departmental Selection Committee: For BS-1 to BS-15 (Non-gazetted posts:
     Sub-Engineers, supporting staff)

3.2 MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS (AS PER SECTION C)

   Chief Engineer:
   - BE/BS Civil Engineering + 25 years experience
   - PEC Registered Professional Engineer (RPE)

   Superintending Engineer:
   - BE/BS Civil Engineering + 18 years experience
   - PEC Registered Professional Engineer (RPE)

   Executive Engineer:
   - BE/BS Civil Engineering + 10 years experience
   - PEC Registered Engineer (RE)

   Sub-Divisional Officer:
   - BE/BS Civil Engineering + 5 years experience
   - PEC Registered Engineer (RE)

   Assistant Engineer:
   - BE/BS Civil Engineering + 2 years experience
   - PEC Registered Engineer (RE)

   Sub-Engineer:
   - DAE (Diploma of Associate Engineer) in Civil Engineering
   - PEC Registered Sub-Engineer (RSE)

   Junior Engineer/Overseer:
   - DAE in Civil Engineering (fresh graduates eligible)

3.3 RECRUITMENT PROCESS

   Advertisement:
   - Published in national/provincial newspapers and PPSC/department website
   - Details: Number of posts, qualifications, experience, age limit, etc.

   Application and Screening:
   - Online application submission
   - Initial screening for eligibility

   Written Test (for AE and below):
   - Technical knowledge (civil engineering, building code, design)
   - General knowledge and current affairs
   - English and Urdu
   - Passing marks: Typically 50-60%

   Interview:
   - For short-listed candidates (top scorers in written test)
   - Panel: Senior officers (CE, SE, XEN) + PPSC representative
   - Assessment: Technical competence, communication, personality

   Final Selection:
   - Merit list based on combined score (written test + interview)
   - Offers issued to top candidates as per vacancies
   - Background verification and medical examination

   Probation Period:
   - Fresh appointees: 1-2 years probation
   - Confirmation after satisfactory performance
   - Extension or termination if unsatisfactory

3.4 RESERVED QUOTAS AND AFFIRMATIVE ACTION ⭐

   As per Government Policy:
   - Merit: 90-95%
   - Reserved quotas (5-10%):
     * Women: Typically 5-10% of posts
     * Minorities: 5% (as per Constitution)
     * Disabled persons: 2-3%
     * Special areas (FATA, Balochistan, etc.): As per provincial rules

   Implementation:
   - Separate merit lists for reserved categories
   - If quota not filled, may convert to general merit

4.0 INDUCTION AND ORIENTATION ⭐
================================================================================

4.1 INDUCTION PROGRAM

   Duration: 2-4 weeks for new technical staff

   Components:
   - Organizational overview (history, structure, vision/mission)
   - Building and Road Code introduction (overview, key provisions)
   - Departmental policies, procedures, and rules
   - Ethics and anti-corruption (zero tolerance policy)
   - IT systems training (iBRCMS, office applications)
   - Safety and occupational health
   - Service rules and employee benefits

   Delivery:
   - Classroom sessions (1 week)
   - Field visits to ongoing projects (1 week)
   - Shadowing experienced officers (1-2 weeks)

4.2 MENTORSHIP PROGRAM ⭐

   For Fresh Graduates:
   - Assigned a mentor (senior AE or SDO)
   - Mentor provides guidance, support, and feedback
   - Regular check-ins (weekly/biweekly)
   - Duration: 6-12 months

   Benefits:
   - Faster learning curve
   - Integration into organizational culture
   - Career guidance and development

6.0 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

6.1 PERFORMANCE APPRAISAL SYSTEM

   Annual Performance Evaluation (ACR - Annual Confidential Report):
   - For all government employees
   - Period: July 1 - June 30 (financial year)
   - Initiating Officer: Immediate supervisor (XEN for SDO, SE for XEN, etc.)
   - Reviewing Officer: One level above
   - Countersigning Officer: Two levels above (for senior positions)

   Performance Criteria (out of 100):
   - Job knowledge and technical competence: 20 points
   - Quality of work and accuracy: 20 points
   - Timeliness and productivity: 15 points
   - Initiative and innovation: 10 points
   - Leadership and teamwork: 10 points
   - Communication and interpersonal skills: 10 points
   - Integrity and ethical conduct: 10 points
   - Overall rating: 5 points

   Rating Scale:
   - Outstanding (90-100): Exceptional performance, far exceeds expectations
   - Very Good (75-89): Consistently exceeds expectations
   - Good (60-74): Meets expectations, satisfactory performance
   - Average (50-59): Partially meets expectations, needs improvement
   - Poor (<50): Below expectations, requires corrective action

6.2 PERFORMANCE OBJECTIVES AND KPIs ⭐

   Individual Performance Objectives:
   - Set at beginning of year (aligned with departmental goals)
   - SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound
   - Examples for SDO (Building Inspector):
     * Complete 95% of inspection requests within 48 hours
     * Achieve 85% first-inspection pass rate
     * Conduct 100 site inspections per month
     * Attend 40 hours of training/CPD
     * Zero corruption complaints

   Mid-Year Review:
   - Progress review at 6 months
   - Course correction if needed
   - Feedback and support from supervisor

6.3 PERFORMANCE-RELATED CONSEQUENCES

   High Performers (Outstanding/Very Good):
   - Recognition and commendation (certificates, awards)
   - Financial incentive (performance allowance, if budget allows)
   - Fast-track promotion consideration
   - Priority for training and development opportunities
   - Assignment to high-profile projects

   Average Performers (Good/Average):
   - Standard career progression
   - Developmental feedback and training
   - Support to improve performance

   Poor Performers (Poor rating):
   - Counseling and performance improvement plan (PIP)
   - Additional training and supervision
   - Extension of probation (for probationers)
   - Adverse remarks (impact on promotion)
   - Disciplinary action if gross negligence or misconduct

6.4 360-DEGREE FEEDBACK (OPTIONAL) ⭐

   For senior positions (SE and above):
   - Feedback from multiple sources:
     * Self-assessment
     * Supervisor
     * Peers
     * Subordinates
     * Customers/stakeholders
   - Anonymous and confidential
   - Used for developmental purposes (not punitive)
   - Helps identify blind spots and leadership effectiveness

7.0 CAREER DEVELOPMENT AND PROMOTION
================================================================================

7.1 CAREER PROGRESSION PATH

   Typical Career Ladder (Technical):
   - Junior Engineer (BS-7-9): Entry level
   - Sub-Engineer (BS-11-14): 3-5 years as JE
   - Assistant Engineer (BS-16): BE/BS degree + PEC registration
   - Sub-Divisional Officer (BS-17): 2-3 years as AE + promotion exam
   - Executive Engineer (BS-18): 5-7 years as SDO
   - Superintending Engineer (BS-19): 8-10 years as XEN
   - Chief Engineer (BS-20): 7-10 years as SE

   Lateral Entry:
   - Fresh graduates: Direct entry as AE (BS-16)
   - Experienced engineers: May enter at XEN level (through PPSC)

7.2 PROMOTION CRITERIA

   Time-Based Progression (Non-gazetted):
   - Sub-Engineer to higher grades: Time scale (3-5 years per grade)
   - Based on satisfactory ACRs and no adverse remarks

   Competitive Promotion (Gazetted):
   - AE to SDO: Departmental promotion exam + ACR + seniority (40-30-30 weightage)
   - SDO to XEN: ACR + seniority + interview (50-30-20 weightage)
   - XEN to SE: ACR + seniority (70-30 weightage, no exam)
   - SE to CE: Selection by government based on merit and seniority

   Promotion Exam (for AE to SDO):
   - Technical: Civil engineering, building code, design (60%)
   - Management: Contract, finance, administration (30%)
   - General: Current affairs, English (10%)
   - Passing marks: 50-60%

7.3 CAREER DEVELOPMENT PLANNING ⭐

   Individual Development Plan (IDP):
   - Created by employee with supervisor input
   - Identifies:
     * Career goals (short-term and long-term)
     * Skill gaps and training needs
     * Action plan (training, assignments, certifications)
   - Reviewed annually and updated

   Job Rotation:
   - Exposure to different divisions, circles, or specializations
   - Broadens experience and skills
   - Typical rotation: 3-5 years per posting for XEN/SDO

   Succession Planning:
   - Identification of high-potential employees
   - Grooming for senior positions
   - Knowledge transfer from retiring officers

8.0 COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS
================================================================================

8.1 SALARY STRUCTURE

   Basic Pay Scales (BPS):
   - As per provincial pay scales (revised periodically)
   - Example (indicative):
     * BS-20 (CE): PKR 150,000 - 300,000
     * BS-19 (SE): PKR 120,000 - 250,000
     * BS-18 (XEN): PKR 100,000 - 200,000
     * BS-17 (SDO): PKR 80,000 - 150,000
     * BS-16 (AE): PKR 60,000 - 120,000
     * BS-11-14 (Sub-Engineer): PKR 30,000 - 80,000

   Annual Increments:
   - 5% annual increment (or as per government policy)
   - Additional increment for outstanding performance (if budget allows)

8.2 ALLOWANCES

   Technical Allowance:
   - For engineering staff (typically 25-40% of basic pay)
   - Higher for specialized positions

   Conveyance/Transport Allowance:
   - For official travel (field visits, inspections)
   - As per entitlement (varies by grade)

   Housing/Accommodation:
   - Government accommodation (if available)
   - OR House Rent Allowance (HRA): 30-45% of basic pay

   Medical Allowance:
   - Fixed monthly amount or reimbursement of medical expenses
   - Health insurance for employee and family ⭐

   Hardship Allowance:
   - For postings in remote or difficult areas (5-20% of basic pay)

   Other Allowances:
   - Dearness Allowance (cost of living)
   - Utilities (electricity, gas)
   - Entertainment (for senior officers)

8.3 BENEFITS AND PERQUISITES

   Leave:
   - Casual Leave: 15 days per year
   - Earned Leave: 30 days per year (accumulates)
   - Medical Leave: As per medical certificate
   - Maternity Leave: 90 days (for female employees)
   - Paternity Leave: 7-15 days (for male employees) ⭐
   - Study Leave: For higher education (as per rules)

   Provident Fund:
   - Government contributes matching amount to GP Fund
   - Retirement benefit (lump sum)

   Pension:
   - Defined benefit pension after retirement (typically after 25-30 years service)
   - Amount: Based on last drawn pay and length of service

   Gratuity:
   - Lump sum payment at retirement
   - Calculation: One month's basic pay for each completed year of service

   Travel and Transportation:
   - Official vehicle for senior officers (SE and above)
   - Fuel and maintenance for official use
   - Travel allowance for field staff

   Training and Development:
   - Sponsored training, certifications, conferences (domestic and international)

8.4 PERFORMANCE-BASED INCENTIVES ⭐

   (If budget and policy allow)
   - Annual performance bonus (5-10% of basic pay for outstanding performers)
   - Project completion bonus (for successful project delivery)
   - Innovation awards (for process improvements, cost savings)

================================================================================

9.1 HEALTH AND SAFETY

   Occupational Health:
   - Annual medical checkup for all employees
   - Health insurance (employee + family)
   - Accidental insurance for field staff

   Workplace Safety:
   - Safe working conditions (office and field)
   - Personal protective equipment (PPE) for site visits
   - First aid facilities and training

9.2 WORK-LIFE BALANCE

   Working Hours:
   - Standard: 8 hours/day, 5 days/week (Monday-Friday)
   - Flexible timing (within reason, with supervisor approval) ⭐
   - Overtime: Compensatory time off or overtime pay (as per rules)

   Remote Work/Work from Home: ⭐
   - For suitable roles (e.g., design, plan review, administration)
   - Policy-based implementation (post-COVID consideration)
   - Requires approval and performance monitoring

   Family-Friendly Policies:
   - Day care facilities (at major offices, if feasible)
   - Flexible leave for family emergencies
   - Support for work-life integration

9.3 EMPLOYEE ASSISTANCE PROGRAM ⭐

   Counseling Services:
   - Stress management and mental health support
   - Career counseling
   - Financial planning assistance

   Grievance Redressal:
   - Internal complaint mechanism
   - Ombudsperson or grievance committee
   - Confidential and fair process
   - Protection against retaliation

9.4 RETIREMENT PLANNING

   Pre-Retirement Counseling:
   - Financial planning (pension, gratuity, investments)
   - Health and lifestyle guidance
   - Post-retirement opportunities (consulting, advisory roles)

   Retirement Benefits:
   - Pension (monthly)
   - Gratuity (lump sum)
   - Commutation of pension (partial lump sum option)
   - Medical benefits (continued for retirees)

   Farewell and Recognition:
   - Formal farewell function
   - Certificate of appreciation
   - Acknowledgment of contributions

10.0 DISCIPLINE AND CONDUCT
================================================================================

10.1 CODE OF CONDUCT

   Expected Behavior:
   - Professionalism and courtesy
   - Integrity and honesty
   - Punctuality and discipline
   - Respect for colleagues and public
   - Compliance with laws, rules, and policies
   - Confidentiality of official information
   - Avoidance of conflict of interest

   Prohibited Conduct:
   - Corruption, bribery, or kickbacks
   - Nepotism and favoritism
   - Harassment (sexual or otherwise)
   - Misuse of official position or resources
   - Negligence or dereliction of duty
   - Insubordination or misconduct

10.2 DISCIPLINARY PROCESS

   Minor Misconduct:
   - Verbal warning
   - Written warning (entered in service record)
   - Withholding of increment
   - Censure or reprimand

   Major Misconduct:
   - Inquiry by senior officer or committee
   - Show-cause notice and opportunity to explain
   - Hearing (if requested)
   - Penalties:
     * Reduction in rank
     * Compulsory retirement
     * Dismissal from service
     * Prosecution (if criminal conduct)

   Appeal:
   - Right to appeal to higher authority
   - Representation to departmental appeals committee
   - Final recourse: Service tribunal or courts

10.3 ANTI-CORRUPTION MEASURES

   Declaration of Assets:
   - Annual declaration by officers BS-17 and above
   - Disclosure of assets, liabilities, and business interests
   - Verification by anti-corruption authorities

   Transparency and Accountability:
   - Digitalization of processes (reduces human discretion)
   - Audit and oversight mechanisms
   - Whistleblower protection
   - Penalties: Dismissal, prosecution, blacklisting

12.0 DIVERSITY AND INCLUSION ⭐
================================================================================

12.1 EQUAL OPPORTUNITY

   Non-Discrimination:
   - Merit-based recruitment and promotion
   - No discrimination based on gender, religion, ethnicity, disability
   - Compliance with constitutional and legal provisions

   Affirmative Action:
   - Reserved quotas for women, minorities, disabled persons (as per policy)

   Women in Leadership:
   - Mentorship and career development for women

   - Assistive technology (screen readers, etc.)
   - Flexible work arrangements

   - 2-3% quota for disabled persons (as per government policy)
