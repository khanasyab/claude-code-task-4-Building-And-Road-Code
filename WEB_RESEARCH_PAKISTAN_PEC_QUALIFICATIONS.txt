PAKISTAN ENGINEERING COUNCIL (PEC) PROFESSIONAL REGISTRATION AND EXAMINATION SYSTEM
Web Research Report - November 2024/January 2025
==================================================================================

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
The Pakistan Engineering Council (PEC) operates under the PEC Act 1976 (amended up to December 1, 2016)
and regulates the engineering profession in Pakistan through professional registration, accreditation,
and continuing professional development programs. The system includes three main individual categories
(RE, PE, CE) and firm registration for consulting engineers.

==================================================================================
1. PEC REGISTRATION CATEGORIES AND REQUIREMENTS
==================================================================================

1.1 REGISTERED ENGINEER (RE)
-----------------------------
Definition: "A person who holds an accredited engineering qualification, whether working privately
or in the employment of an engineering public organization and is registered as such by the Council."

Eligibility:
- Bachelor's degree in engineering from PEC-accredited institution
- Engineers graduated after January 15, 2008 from Pakistani or foreign universities/institutions
- Must possess accredited engineering qualification

Key Limitations:
- Cannot independently sign off designs
- Can perform all professional engineering works except independent design signing

Registration Process:
- Submit completed application form
- Educational certificates and transcripts (verified against PEC-accredited institutions)
- CNIC/Passport copies
- Photographs
- Professional experience certificates (if applicable)
- Payment of registration fees (variable by category)

Annual Requirements:
- CPD Credit Points: 9 points in first 3-year cycle, 12 points in next 3-year cycle,
  5 points per year thereafter
- Annual renewal fees
- Maintain compliance with professional conduct standards

1.2 PROFESSIONAL ENGINEER (PE)
-----------------------------
Definition: "A person who holds an accredited engineering qualification and after obtaining
professional experience of five years, whether working privately or in the employment of
an engineering public organization, has passed the prescribed engineering practice examination
and is registered as such by the Council."

Requirements to Become PE:
1. Must be registered as RE with PEC (primary pathway)
2. Minimum 5 years of relevant professional engineering experience after graduation
3. Pass the Engineering Practice Examination (EPE) conducted by PEC
4. Earn minimum 17 CPD points since August 2010
5. Meet annual CPD requirements (3 credit points per year)

Pathways to PE Registration (as of 2024):
- Category-I: RE with EPE qualification
- Category-II: RE or engineering graduate (graduated on/before January 15, 2008) through
  assessment/interview
- Category-III: Possessing professional title of C.Eng or CP.Eng from internationally
  recognized professional bodies

CPD Requirements for PE:
- Mandatory 3 credit points per year for renewal of registration
- Effective from July 2010
- Must maintain compliance throughout professional career

1.3 CONSULTING ENGINEER REGISTRATION
------------------------------------
Note: This refers to FIRM registration, not individual registration as CE.

Firm Structure Requirements:
- Minimum 51% of shares/interest must be owned by Professional Engineers
- Remaining 49% can be owned by a "qualified person"
- Qualified person: Bachelor degree from HEC-recognized university with minimum 5 years experience
  OR any legal entity

Registration Procedure for Pakistani Firms:
- Firm name must be unique (check availability with PEC)
- Submit application on prescribed form
- PEC scrutinizes application and may request clarifications
- Registration granted within 1 month of application submission for initial registration or renewal
- Application for renewal: December 1 to end of year based on consultant status as of November 31

Foreign Consulting Firms:
- Must register for specified projects
- Projects must require specialized expertise not available in Pakistan
- Must enter into joint venture with Pakistani consulting engineer

1.4 CONTRACTOR/OPERATOR REGISTRATION
------------------------------------
- Available for construction companies and engineering operators
- Subject to specific byelaws and requirements
- Subject to recent amendments in February 2024 regarding credit points and license fees

2. PEC EXAMINATION SYSTEM - ENGINEERING PRACTICE EXAMINATION (EPE)
==================================================================================

2.1 ENGINEERING PRACTICE EXAMINATION (EPE) - PROFESSIONAL ENGINEER PATHWAY
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose: Examination conducted by PEC to grant RE the status of PE

Frequency: Twice per year

Exam Structure:
- Part-I: Common for all Engineering Disciplines
  • Tests general engineering knowledge and professional practice
  • Covers engineering ethics, professional responsibilities, codes of conduct
  • Covers Pakistan's engineering regulations and building codes

- Part-II: Discipline-Specific
  • Based on breadth and depth subjects of specific engineering discipline
  • Tests technical competency in specialized field

Passing Criteria:
- Minimum 60% marks required in EACH part
- Both parts must be passed to qualify for PE registration
- Individual part failures can be retaken in subsequent examinations

Subject Coverage (General Areas):
- Professional Practice and Ethics
- Engineering Management (project management, contract administration)
- Pakistan's Engineering Regulations and Building Codes
- Relevant PEC Act provisions
- Technical Knowledge (discipline-specific)

Available Disciplines for Part-II:
- Civil Engineering
- Electrical Engineering
- Mechanical Engineering
- Chemical Engineering
- Other recognized engineering disciplines

Eligibility to Sit EPE:
- Currently registered as RE with PEC
- Minimum 5 years professional engineering experience post-graduation
- Minimum 17 CPD points earned since August 2010
- Payment of examination fee

3. CONTINUING PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT (CPD) SYSTEM
==================================================================================

3.1 CPD FRAMEWORK OVERVIEW
------------------------
Established Under: Professional Development of Engineers Byelaws-2008
Effective Date: July 10, 2010
Mandatory: Yes, for all RE and PE registration renewals

3.2 CPD CREDIT POINT REQUIREMENTS
-------------------------------
For Registered Engineers (RE):
- First 3-year cycle: 9 credit points
- Second 3-year cycle and beyond: 12 credit points per 3-year cycle
- Ongoing: 5 credit points per year thereafter

For Professional Engineers (PE):
- 3 credit points per year
- Mandatory for registration renewal
- Newly registered and existing PE must comply

For New PE Eligibility:
- Minimum 17 CPD points earned since August 2010
- Required before appearing for EPE exam

4. FPSC AND PPSC ENGINEERING EXAMINATION SYSTEMS
==================================================================================

4.1 FPSC (FEDERAL PUBLIC SERVICE COMMISSION) - CSS EXAM
-------------------------------------------------------
Authority: Independent federal body responsible for civil service recruitment
Exam: Central Superior Services (CSS) Examination

CSS Exam Structure:
- Multi-stage assessment process
- Written examination (compulsory and optional subjects)
- Medical examination
- Psychological testing
- Interview stage

Passing Requirements:
- Minimum 40% in compulsory subjects
- Minimum 33% in optional subjects
- Minimum aggregate 50% to proceed to interview stage

Grade Level: Grade BPS-17 (entry point)
Salary: PKR 70,000 to PKR 80,000 per month (approximate starting)

CSS Occupational Groups (12 Groups):
1. Pakistan Administrative Service (PAS)
2. Foreign Service of Pakistan (FSP)
3. Police Service
4. Pakistan Customs Service
5. Commerce & Trade Group
6. Postal Group
7. Information Group
8. Office Management Group (OMG)
9. Railways
10. Pakistan Audit and Accounts Service
11. Inland Revenue Service
12. [Additional groups as per current notification]

Important Note on Engineering:
- NO separate "Engineering Service" as dedicated CSS occupational group
- Engineering graduates CAN appear in CSS with engineering-related optional subjects
- Engineering backgrounds can leverage technical knowledge

CSS Optional Subjects Available for Engineering Graduates:
- Computer Science (200 marks)
- Physics (200 marks)
- Chemistry (200 marks)
- Applied Mathematics (100 marks) - Most relevant for engineers
- Pure Mathematics (100 marks)
- Statistics (100 marks)
- Geology (100 marks)

Applied Mathematics Syllabus Content (Engineering-Relevant):
- Vector calculus
- Statics and dynamics
- Ordinary differential equations
- Fourier series and partial differential equations
- Numerical methods

CSS Pass Rate:
- 2020: 1.962% pass rate
- 2021: 2.11% pass rate (364 out of 17,240 candidates)
- Reputation: Extremely competitive and rigorous exam

5.3 ENGINEERING SERVICE STRUCTURE IN PAKISTAN CIVIL SERVICE
----------------------------------------------------------
- Engineering positions integrated within existing CSS groups
- No standalone centralized "Engineering Service" (unlike pre-1976 All-India Service model)
- Engineering recruitment distributed across:
  * Federal ministries and departments
  * Provincial services (via PPSC equivalents)
  * Autonomous bodies (WAPDA, NHA, etc.)
  * Public authorities

Grade Structure:
- Entry: BPS-17 (Junior Engineer)
- Senior: BPS-18-21 (Senior Engineer, Chief Engineer)
- Executive: BPS-22+ (Chief Executive, Secretary Engineering)

8. SUMMARY OF KEY REQUIREMENTS BY REGISTRATION CATEGORY
==================================================================================

REGISTERED ENGINEER (RE):
------------------------
Minimum Education: Bachelor degree in engineering (PEC-accredited)
Experience Required: None (entry-level post-graduation)
Examination: None
CPD: 9 pts (3 years), then 12 pts (3 years), then 5 pts/year
Annual Renewal: Yes
Cost: Registration and annual renewal fees (variable)
Professional Scope: All engineering works except independent design signing

PROFESSIONAL ENGINEER (PE):
-------------------------
Minimum Education: Bachelor degree in engineering (PEC-accredited)
Experience Required: 5 years professional engineering experience
Examination: Engineering Practice Examination (EPE) - Pass Parts I & II (60% each)
CPD: 3 credit points per year (17 points minimum to sit exam)
Annual Renewal: Yes
Cost: Registration, exam, and annual renewal fees
Professional Scope: All professional engineering works including design signing

CONSULTING ENGINEER FIRM:
-----------------------
Ownership: 51% Professional Engineers + 49% Qualified persons/entities
Registration: Firm-level (not individual)
Experience: Partners must meet PE requirements
Examination: Organization must meet firm standards
CPD: Organizational CPD compliance
Renewal: Annual (December 1 end of year)
Cost: Registration and annual renewal fees
Scope: Consulting and design services, joint ventures for foreign projects
