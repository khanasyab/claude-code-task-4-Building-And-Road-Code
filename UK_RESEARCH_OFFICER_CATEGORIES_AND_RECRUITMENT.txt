SECTION 1: UK DMRB QUALITY MANAGEMENT AND COMPETENCY FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

REFERENCE: GG 102 - Quality Management Systems for Highway Works
REFERENCE: GC 101 - General Requirements for Specification for Highway Works
REFERENCE: GG 101 - Introduction to the Design Manual for Roads and Bridges

1.1 ORGANIZATIONAL ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES (GG 102, Section 5.3)

The UK framework requires organizations to:

A. IDENTIFY ALL ORGANIZATIONAL ROLES
   - Quality plan shall identify all roles
   - Position of individual fulfilling responsibilities must be documented
   - Clear hierarchy and reporting structure required

B. IDENTIFY KEY ROLES
   According to GG 102 Section 7.1.1, key roles must include:

   1) Project Managers
      - Responsible for project delivery and coordination
      - Must be identified at initiation stage

   2) Technical Specialists
      - Subject matter experts in specific disciplines
      - Specialized knowledge in their field

   3) Lead Assessors and Inspectors
      - Quality assurance personnel
      - Independent verification capability

   4) Audit and Assurance Assessors
      - Internal and external audit roles
      - Compliance verification

   5) Authorized Signatories
      - Product and document approval authority
      - Release authorization responsibility

C. INDIVIDUAL ASSIGNMENT
   - Quality plan must identify individuals in key roles
   - May be sourced from external providers
   - Individual competence must be verified

1.2 COMPETENCE MANAGEMENT AND ASSESSMENT (GG 102, Section 7 & GC 101, Section 8)

REFERENCE STANDARD: ISO 10018:2015 - Quality management. Guidelines on people
                    involvement and competence

A. COMPETENCE DEFINITION AND ASSESSMENT

Competence shall be assessed on basis of:
   1) Knowledge - Theoretical and technical understanding
   2) Training - Formal education and skill development
   3) Experience - Practical application and proven capability

B. COMPETENCE ASSESSMENT CRITERIA (GG 102, Section 7.6-7.7)

The assessment of staff competence shall include:

   1) TECHNICAL CAPABILITIES
      - Ability to perform technical work
      - Understanding of standards and regulations
      - Problem-solving capability

   2) BEHAVIOURS (Soft Skills)
      - Communication and interpersonal skills
      - Professional conduct
      - Teamwork and collaboration
      - Reliability and accountability
      - Adaptability and learning agility

   3) FIT FOR INTENDED USE
      - Ability to deliver products meeting Overseeing Organisation requirements
      - Understanding of customer needs
      - Quality mindset

   4) TECHNICAL COMPETENCY REQUIREMENTS
      - Knowledge of relevant DMRB documents
      - Understanding of other Overseeing Organisation documents
      - EU and National legislation compliance knowledge

   5) QUALITY SCHEMES KNOWLEDGE (GG 102, Section 7.7.1)
      - National Highways Sector Schemes (NHSS)
      - Equivalent quality frameworks
      - Industry standards and best practices

C. COMPETENCY BALANCING AND RISK MANAGEMENT (GG 102, Section 7.5)

Competence level must be balanced against:
   - Risks associated with failure of the product/work
   - Criticality of the role
   - Consequences of non-performance
   - Complexity of the work

D. DOCUMENTED EVIDENCE REQUIREMENT (GC 101, Section 8.2-8.4)

Competence verification requires:
   - Documented evidence of each person's competence
   - Records demonstrating knowledge, training, and experience
   - Submission when requested by Overseeing Organisation
   - Evidence must demonstrate necessary competence for work undertaken

1.3 COMPETENCE RESPONSIBILITY AND ASSIGNMENT (GG 102, Section 7.3-7.4)

A. ASSIGNED RESPONSIBILITY

Quality plan shall identify the individual with responsibility for:
   - Assessing technical competency
   - Assigning competent individuals to project activities
   - Ongoing competence monitoring

B. COMPETENCE MANAGEMENT APPROACH

Competence shall be managed in accordance with ISO 10018:2015:
   - Identification of competency requirements
   - Assessment of existing competency levels
   - Development and training planning
   - Verification of competence achievement
   - Ongoing competence maintenance

SECTION 2: UK CIVIL SERVICE GRADING AND CLASSIFICATION SYSTEM
================================================================================

NOTE: UK Civil Service operates under generic grading structures based on:
- Civil Service Code of Practice
- Civil Service Competency Frameworks
- EspaC (HR system) grading structures
- Pay levels and responsibility bands

2.1 GENERIC UK CIVIL SERVICE GRADE STRUCTURE

The UK public sector uses the following generic grade structure:

A. ADMINISTRATIVE/OPERATIONAL GRADES

   1) ADMINISTRATIVE ASSISTANT / SUPPORT WORKER
      - Entry level clerical/administrative support
      - Basic administrative procedures knowledge
      - Limited independent decision-making
      - Supervision required

   2) ADMINISTRATIVE OFFICER
      - Clerical and administrative work
      - Routine decision-making within guidelines
      - Supervision of clerical staff
      - Higher responsibility than Assistant level

   3) HIGHER ADMINISTRATIVE OFFICER
      - Significant administrative responsibility
      - Procedural and policy implementation
      - Limited supervisory responsibility
      - Support to senior management

B. PROFESSIONAL/SPECIALIST GRADES

   1) PROFESSIONAL SPECIALIST / TECHNICAL OFFICER
      - Professional qualification required (e.g., degree or equivalent)
      - Subject matter expertise
      - Technical decision-making
      - May supervise technicians

   2) SENIOR SPECIALIST / PRINCIPAL PROFESSIONAL
      - Advanced expertise level
      - Project or programme management
      - Technical guidance and quality assurance
      - Mentoring and training responsibility

   3) LEAD SPECIALIST / HEAD OF PROFESSION
      - Strategic technical leadership
      - Departmental head or equivalent
      - Policy development and implementation
      - Authority over specialist area

C. MANAGEMENT/LEADERSHIP GRADES

   1) MANAGER / OPERATIONS MANAGER
      - Team management responsibility
      - Delivery of service/function
      - People management and development
      - Budget responsibility

   2) SENIOR MANAGER / PROGRAMME MANAGER
      - Significant programme/project responsibility
      - Multiple team management
      - Strategic planning
      - Performance accountability

   3) SENIOR LEADER / DEPARTMENT HEAD
      - Departmental leadership
      - Strategic responsibility
      - Government facing
      - Policy influence

2.2 UK CIVIL SERVICE COMPETENCY FRAMEWORKS

Standard UK Civil Service competencies include:

A. BEHAVIOURS (Cross-cutting)
   - Changing and Improving
   - Making Effective Decisions
   - Leadership and Leading Change
   - Communicating and Influencing
   - Working Together
   - Managing a Quality Service
   - Delivering at Pace

B. TECHNICAL/PROFESSIONAL COMPETENCIES (Role-specific)
   - Technical expertise and knowledge
   - Professional judgment
   - Continuous improvement orientation
   - Knowledge management

C. MANAGEMENT COMPETENCIES (For managers)
   - People management and development
   - Strategic planning
   - Budget and resource management
   - Team leadership

SECTION 3: PROFESSIONAL QUALIFICATIONS AND REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

REFERENCE: GG 102, GC 101, CD 350

3.1 PROFESSIONAL ENGINEERING QUALIFICATIONS IN UK

A. CHARTERED STATUS (Highest Level)

   1) CHARTERED ENGINEER (CEng)
      - Granted by Engineering Council UK
      - Requirements:
        * Degree in engineering or equivalent
        * Further training and experience (typically 4+ years)
        * Demonstrated professional competence
        * Knowledge of professional standards and ethics
        * CPD (Continuing Professional Development) commitment
      - Institution Membership:
        * ICE (Institution of Civil Engineers)
        * IStructE (Institution of Structural Engineers)
        * IMechE (Institution of Mechanical Engineers)
        * Others by discipline

   2) INCORPORATED ENGINEER (IEng)
      - Lower than Chartered but substantial responsibility
      - Requirements:
        * HND/HNC qualification or degree
        * Experience (typically 3+ years)
        * Demonstrable practical competence
      - Institution membership varies by discipline

B. ENGINEERING TECHNICIAN (EngTech)
   - Technical specialist level
   - Requirements:
     * NVQ Level 3 or equivalent
     * Practical experience (typically 2+ years)
     * Technical competence in specialist area

C. TECHNICIAN LEVEL
   - Operational technical role
   - Requirements:
     * Relevant technical qualifications
     * Apprenticeship or equivalent
     * Practical competence

3.2 INSTITUTION MEMBERSHIPS (UK)

A. MAJOR ENGINEERING INSTITUTIONS

   1) ICE - Institution of Civil Engineers
      - Roads and highways specialization
      - Standards development
      - Professional standards setting
      - Competency assessment frameworks

   2) IStructE - Institution of Structural Engineers
      - Structural design and assessment
      - Professional standards
      - Qualification pathways

   3) CIHT - Chartered Institution of Highways and Transportation
      - Transport engineering specialization
      - Roads and highways focus
      - Professional qualification (FCIHT, MCIHT)

   4) EIC - Engineering Council UK
      - Oversees professional qualifications
      - Sets standards for CEng, IEng, EngTech
      - Professional conduct standards

B. QUALITY AND MANAGEMENT INSTITUTIONS

   1) ISO - International Organization for Standardization
      - Quality standards (ISO 9001)
      - Competence standards (ISO 10018)
      - Various technical standards

   2) UKAS - United Kingdom Accreditation Service
      - Accreditation of certification bodies
      - Quality scheme accreditation
      - Testing and inspection accreditation

C. PROFESSIONAL MEMBERSHIP BENEFITS

   - Continuous Professional Development (CPD)
   - Professional indemnity insurance
   - Professional conduct standards
   - Knowledge sharing and networking
   - Skill development pathways

3.3 QUALIFICATION HIERARCHY FOR ROAD WORKS PROFESSIONALS

Based on DMRB and UK practice:

LEVEL 1: SENIOR PROFESSIONAL/MANAGEMENT
   - Chartered Engineer (CEng) preferred
   - 10+ years experience
   - Strategic responsibility
   - Project leadership
   - Examples: Chief Engineer, Consulting Principal

LEVEL 2: ESTABLISHED PROFESSIONAL
   - Chartered Engineer (CEng) or Incorporated Engineer (IEng)
   - 5-10 years experience
   - Technical leadership
   - Quality assurance responsibility
   - Examples: Senior Structural Designer, Principal Inspector

LEVEL 3: QUALIFIED PROFESSIONAL
   - Bachelor's degree in relevant discipline
   - 2-5 years experience
   - Technical competence
   - Work under supervision of Level 2
   - Examples: Graduate Engineer, Design Technician

LEVEL 4: TECHNICAL SPECIALIST
   - HND/HNC or equivalent
   - Engineering Technician (EngTech)
   - 1-3 years experience
   - Specialized technical skills
   - Examples: CAD Technician, Survey Technician

LEVEL 5: TECHNICAL/OPERATIONAL
   - Level 3 National Vocational Qualification (NVQ)
   - Apprenticeship or equivalent
   - Practical competence
   - Specialized technical tasks
   - Examples: Construction Supervisor, Test Technician

SECTION 4: QUALITY MANAGEMENT SCHEME FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

REFERENCE: GC 101, Section 7 (Quality Management Schemes)
REFERENCE: GG 102, Section 7

4.1 QUALITY MANAGEMENT SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS

A. MANDATORY REQUIREMENTS

Organizations must:

   1) Implement Quality Management System (QMS)
      - ISO 9001:2015 compliance
      - Assessed and registered
      - Accredited certification body assessment
      - UKAS accreditation of certifier

   2) Operate QMS Actively
      - Documented procedures
      - Record maintenance
      - Continuous monitoring
      - Regular reviews and updates

   3) Submit Registration Certificate
      - Prior to works commencement
      - Maintained throughout works
      - Scope must include specified work

B. QUALITY PLAN REQUIREMENTS (GC 101, Section 6)

Quality plan must include:

   1) ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE
      - All roles and responsibilities
      - Key personnel identification
      - Competency requirements
      - Individual assignments

   2) COMPETENCY ASSURANCE
      - Competency assessment criteria
      - Evidence documentation process
      - Assessment methodology
      - Ongoing monitoring

   3) RESOURCE MANAGEMENT
      - Personnel resources
      - Equipment and materials
      - Budget allocation
      - Risk management

   4) OPERATIONAL CONTROL
      - Quality procedures
      - Work execution standards
      - Inspection and testing
      - Non-conformity management

   5) PERFORMANCE MEASUREMENT
      - Key performance indicators
      - Monitoring frequency
      - Recording and reporting
      - Corrective action process

4.2 QUALITY MANAGEMENT SCHEME FEATURES (GC 101, Section 7)

A. SCHEME GOVERNANCE

   1) TECHNICAL ADVISORY COMMITTEE
      - Advises UKAS and certification bodies
      - Representatives from:
        * Clients and client bodies
        * Trade associations
        * Training organizations
        * Certification bodies
        * Professional bodies
        * National accreditation body

   2) DOCUMENTED REQUIREMENTS
      - Scheme documentation (publicly available)
      - Competence requirements
      - Quality management features
      - Assessment procedures

   3) SCHEME ADMINISTRATION
      - Documented, publicly available list of registrants
      - Regular review (minimum annually)
      - Non-commercial operation
      - Third-party accreditation

B. COMPETENCY ASSESSMENT IN QUALITY SCHEMES

   1) JUDGED AGAINST SCHEME CRITERIA
      - Competency assessed against scheme competency requirements
      - Scheme requirements must be equivalent to Section 8 (GC 101)
      - Evidence documentation required

   2) SCOPE OF ASSESSMENT
      - Technical capability assessment
      - Behavioural assessment
      - Experience and knowledge verification
      - Relevance to specific work activities

4.3 ACCREDITATION AND CERTIFICATION BODY REQUIREMENTS

A. CERTIFICATION BODY QUALIFICATIONS

   1) PROFESSIONAL COMPETENCE
      - In-house expertise in assessed areas
      - Knowledge of technical requirements
      - Understanding of industry best practice

   2) ACCREDITATION
      - UKAS accreditation required
      - Accreditation scope includes:
        * ISO/IEC 17021-1:2015 compliance
        * Relevant quality management scheme scope
        * ISO/IEC 17065:2012 for product certification schemes

   3) MULTI-LATERAL AGREEMENTS
      - UKAS Multi-Lateral Agreement (MLA) recognized
      - European Accreditation Organisation equivalent
      - International Accreditation Forum MLA signatories

SECTION 5: RECRUITMENT AND SELECTION PROCEDURES
================================================================================

5.1 UK PUBLIC SECTOR RECRUITMENT BEST PRACTICE

Based on Civil Service Commission Standards and UK Government:

A. RECRUITMENT PROCESS PRINCIPLES

   1) MERIT-BASED SELECTION
      - Open and fair competition
      - Best qualified applicants selected
      - Evidence-based assessment
      - Transparent procedures

   2) ROLE DEFINITION
      - Clear job descriptions
      - Competency requirements specified
      - Qualification requirements detailed
      - Experience requirements defined

   3) APPLICATION AND SHORTLISTING
      - Open advertising (minimum 2 weeks)
      - Standardized application process
      - Consistent shortlisting criteria
      - Documented decision rationale

B. ASSESSMENT METHODS

   1) COMPETENCY-BASED ASSESSMENT
      - Structured interviews
      - Technical assessments
      - Work samples/simulations
      - References and background checks

   2) ASSESSMENT PANELS
      - Multiple assessors (minimum 2-3)
      - Diverse panel composition
      - Documented scoring
      - Independent assessment

   3) COMPETENCY EVALUATION
      - Against role competency framework
      - Behavioural demonstration
      - Evidence collection
      - Moderated assessment

C. SELECTION DOCUMENTATION

   - Job description with competency framework
   - Recruitment plan and timetable
   - Scoring sheets and assessment records
   - Audit trail of decisions
   - Feedback documentation

5.2 ROLE-SPECIFIC RECRUITMENT CRITERIA

A. SENIOR MANAGEMENT POSITIONS

   Typical Requirements:
   - 10+ years relevant experience
   - Leadership experience
   - Strategic thinking capability
   - Managing teams and budgets
   - Degree/professional qualification (normally required)
   - Professional registration (CEng, FCIHT, etc., if applicable)
   - Demonstrated track record of successful delivery

B. PROFESSIONAL TECHNICAL POSITIONS

   Typical Requirements:
   - 5-10 years relevant experience
   - Degree in relevant discipline
   - Professional qualification (CEng, IEng, or in progress)
   - Technical competence in specialist area
   - Project experience
   - Quality assurance capability
   - Communication skills

C. SPECIALIST/TECHNICIAN POSITIONS

   Typical Requirements:
   - 2-5 years relevant experience
   - HND/HNC or technical qualification
   - Specialist technical skills
   - Training and development capability
   - Attention to detail
   - Team working

D. OPERATIONAL/SUPPORT POSITIONS

   Typical Requirements:
   - 1-3 years relevant experience (may be entry-level)
   - Relevant qualification (NVQ Level 2-3)
   - Procedural knowledge
   - Reliability and attention to detail
   - Basic technical competence

5.3 COMPETENCY ASSESSMENT IN RECRUITMENT

A. COMPETENCY FRAMEWORK APPLICATION

   Assess against:
   - Technical competency (knowledge, skills, experience)
   - Behavioral competencies (communication, teamwork, decision-making)
   - Professional standards (conduct, ethical behavior)
   - Development potential and learning orientation

B. EVIDENCE GATHERING

   - Educational qualifications
   - Professional registrations/certifications
   - Work experience and achievements
   - References from previous employers/supervisors
   - Interview performance
   - Technical assessments
   - Project examples and case studies

SECTION 6: DIVERSITY, EQUALITY AND INCLUSION STANDARDS
================================================================================

REFERENCE: GG 101, Section 1.8-1.10 (Equality, Diversity and Inclusion)

6.1 EQUALITY IMPACT ASSESSMENT (EqIA) REQUIREMENTS

A. MANDATORY EqIA SCREENING

For any activity with potential impact on people with protected characteristics:

   1) SCREENING PROCESS
      - Determine if full EqIA required
      - Identify affected groups
      - Document screening decision

   2) PROTECTED CHARACTERISTICS
      Under UK Equality Act 2010:
      - Age
      - Disability
      - Gender reassignment
      - Marriage and civil partnership
      - Pregnancy and maternity
      - Race
      - Religion or belief
      - Sex
      - Sexual orientation

B. FULL EqIA REQUIREMENTS

If screening indicates full EqIA needed:

   1) ASSESSMENT SCOPE
      - Identify potential disadvantage or additional risk
      - Analyse impact on protected groups
      - Consider differential impact

   2) MITIGATION PLANNING
      - Propose solutions to mitigate negative impact
      - Consultation with affected groups
      - Evidence collection

   3) ENGAGEMENT AND CONSULTATION
      - Engagement with affected communities
      - Group consultations
      - Feedback incorporation
      - Transparency in decision-making

6.2 DIVERSITY IN RECRUITMENT AND SELECTION

A. RECRUITMENT PRINCIPLES

   1) INCLUSIVE ADVERTISING
      - Wide publication of vacancies
      - Accessible application processes
      - Transparent selection criteria
      - Encourage diverse applicants

   2) FAIR ASSESSMENT
      - Bias-free evaluation methods
      - Diverse assessment panels
      - Consistent application of criteria
      - Documented decisions

   3) ACCESSIBILITY SUPPORT
      - Reasonable accommodations for applicants
      - Flexible working arrangements
      - Accessible assessment formats
      - Support for people with disabilities

B. INCLUSIVE WORKPLACE STANDARDS

   - Equal pay for equal value
   - Non-discrimination policies
   - Harassment and bullying prevention
   - Flexible working support
   - Career development equality
   - Mentoring and sponsorship programmes
