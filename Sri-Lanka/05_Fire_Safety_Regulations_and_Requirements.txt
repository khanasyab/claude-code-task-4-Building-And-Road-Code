COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE TO FIRE PROTECTION IN SRI LANKA

DOCUMENT INFORMATION
---------------------
Title: Fire Safety Regulations and Requirements in Sri Lanka
Legal Basis: Construction Industry Development Authority (CIDA) Act No. 33 of 2014
Implementing Standards: ICTAD Fire Regulations
Reference Standards: National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) Standards
Enforcement: CIDA, Municipal Councils, Fire Service Department
Status: Mandatory for specified building types

======================================================================
CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION AND LEGAL FRAMEWORK
======================================================================

1.1 OVERVIEW

Fire safety in Sri Lanka is governed by regulations contained under the
Construction Industry Development Authority (CIDA) Act No. 33 of 2014,
which provides minimum requirements for fire protection design and
construction of new buildings and renovation of existing buildings
throughout Sri Lanka.

1.2 REGULATORY AUTHORITIES

PRIMARY AUTHORITIES:

CONSTRUCTION INDUSTRY DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY (CIDA):
- Fire safety regulation development
- Standards and code enforcement
- Fire safety plan approval
- Technical guidance

FIRE SERVICE DEPARTMENT:
- Fire safety inspections
- Emergency response
- Fire prevention education
- Enforcement of fire safety measures

COLOMBO MUNICIPAL COUNCIL (CMC):
- Fire Service Department operations in Colombo
- Building fire safety approval
- Regular inspections
- Fire NOC issuance

URBAN DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY (UDA):
- Integration of fire safety in building approvals
- Coordination with fire authorities
- Planning regulations

MUNICIPAL COUNCILS AND LOCAL AUTHORITIES:
- Local enforcement
- Fire safety inspections
- Approval processes
- Coordination with fire services

1.3 APPLICABLE STANDARDS

Fire safety regulations reference:
- ICTAD Fire Regulations
- NFPA (National Fire Protection Association) Standards
- British Standards (BS) for fire safety
- SLS standards (where applicable)
- International building codes

1.4 APPLICABILITY

MANDATORY FIRE SAFETY APPROVAL REQUIRED FOR:

a) ALL NON-RESIDENTIAL BUILDINGS:
   - Commercial buildings
   - Industrial facilities
   - Institutional buildings
   - Public assembly areas

b) RESIDENTIAL BUILDINGS:
   - Four stories (G+3) and above
   - Apartment complexes
   - High-rise residential towers

c) SPECIAL OCCUPANCIES:
   - Hotels and lodging
   - Hospitals and healthcare facilities
   - Educational institutions
   - Shopping complexes
   - Theaters and cinemas

d) RENOVATIONS AND ALTERATIONS:
   - Major renovations affecting fire safety
   - Change of occupancy
   - Structural modifications

CHAPTER 2: CURRENT STATUS AND GAPS
======================================================================

2.1 REGULATORY COVERAGE

Research studies (2020) indicate:
- Local fire safety codes cover only 7.14% of international standards
- Remaining 92.86% requires improvements
- Significant gaps in high-rise building provisions
- Limited enforcement capacity

2.2 COMPLIANCE CHALLENGES

- Minimum compliance mentality prevalent
- Poor fire safety performance in most high-rise buildings
- Lack of comprehensive local standards
- Limited public awareness
- Economic constraints

2.3 IMPROVEMENT INITIATIVES

Ongoing efforts to:
- Develop comprehensive fire codes
- Enhance enforcement mechanisms
- Increase professional capacity
- Public education campaigns
- International collaboration

CHAPTER 4: MEANS OF EGRESS
======================================================================

4.1 GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLES:
- Minimum two independent means of egress
- Clear and unobstructed exit routes
- Adequate exit capacity
- Protected exit routes
- Emergency lighting
- Exit signage

4.2 EXIT CAPACITY

CALCULATION BASIS:
- Occupant load determination
- Exit width per person
- Door capacity
- Stairway capacity

OCCUPANT LOAD FACTORS:

Assembly without fixed seats: 0.5 m² per person
Assembly with fixed seats: Number of seats
Standing space: 0.3 m² per person
Offices: 10 m² per person
Educational classrooms: 2 m² per person
Retail sales areas: 3 m² per person
Storage areas: 30 m² per person
Industrial: Variable based on use

MINIMUM EXIT WIDTHS:
- Single door: 900mm minimum
- Main exit doors: 1100mm minimum (preferred)
- Corridors: 1200mm minimum
- Emergency stairways: 1100mm minimum

4.3 TRAVEL DISTANCE

MAXIMUM TRAVEL DISTANCES:

Dead-end corridors:
- General: 15 meters maximum
- Sprinklered buildings: 20 meters maximum

To nearest exit:
- Non-sprinklered: 30 meters maximum
- Sprinklered buildings: 45 meters maximum
- Open-air routes: 60 meters maximum

4.4 EXIT DOORS

DOOR REQUIREMENTS:
- Side-hinged swinging doors
- Swing in direction of egress (occupant load >50)
- Panic hardware (assembly occupancies)
- Self-closing devices (fire doors)
- Emergency exit signs (illuminated)
- No locks preventing egress

DOOR DIMENSIONS:
- Minimum clear width: 800mm
- Minimum clear height: 2000mm
- Maximum leaf width: 1200mm

4.5 EXIT STAIRWAYS

STAIRWAY DESIGN:

Dimensions:
- Minimum width: 900mm (preferably 1100mm)
- Maximum riser height: 190mm
- Minimum tread depth: 250mm
- Consistent riser and tread within flight
- Headroom clearance: 2100mm minimum

Handrails:
- Both sides required
- Height: 900mm from pitch line
- Continuous within flight
- Graspable profile

Construction:
- Non-combustible materials
- Fire-resistant enclosure (1-2 hour rating)
- Ventilation provisions
- Pressurization (high-rise buildings)

Landings:
- Minimum depth equal to stair width
- Maximum 3.6m vertical rise between landings
- Level landings at doors

4.6 EMERGENCY LIGHTING

REQUIREMENTS:
- Automatic activation on power failure
- Battery backup duration: Minimum 90 minutes
- Illumination levels: 1 lux minimum at floor level
- Coverage of all exit routes
- Exit signs illuminated

EXIT SIGNAGE:
- "EXIT" signs at all exits
- Directional signs at decision points
- Visible from all points in exit route
- Pictographic signs (international symbols)
- Photoluminescent or electrically illuminated

CHAPTER 5: FIRE-RESISTANT CONSTRUCTION
======================================================================

5.1 FIRE RESISTANCE RATINGS

Buildings and building elements require fire resistance ratings based on:
- Building height
- Occupancy type
- Floor area
- Fire load

COMMON FIRE RESISTANCE PERIODS:
- 30 minutes (0.5 hour)
- 60 minutes (1 hour)
- 90 minutes (1.5 hours)
- 120 minutes (2 hours)
- 180 minutes (3 hours)
- 240 minutes (4 hours)

5.2 STRUCTURAL ELEMENTS

LOAD-BEARING WALLS:
Low-rise buildings (up to 15m): 1 hour
Medium-rise buildings (15-30m): 2 hours
High-rise buildings (>30m): 3 hours

COLUMNS:
Low-rise: 1 hour
Medium-rise: 2 hours
High-rise: 3-4 hours

BEAMS AND FLOORS:
Low-rise: 1 hour
Medium-rise: 2 hours
High-rise: 2-3 hours

ROOFS:
Non-combustible construction: 30 minutes minimum
Above assembly areas: 1 hour minimum

5.3 FIRE COMPARTMENTATION

COMPARTMENT REQUIREMENTS:
- Maximum compartment size: 2000-3000 m² (varies by occupancy)
- Fire-resistant walls and floors
- Protected openings (fire doors, dampers)
- Service penetration sealing

COMPARTMENT WALLS:
- Fire resistance: 1-2 hours
- Floor to floor (or roof) construction
- Smoke-tight construction
- Openings protected with fire doors/shutters

FIRE DOORS:

Ratings:
- FD30: 30-minute fire door
- FD60: 60-minute fire door
- FD90: 90-minute fire door
- FD120: 120-minute fire door

Requirements:
- Self-closing devices
- Intumescent seals
- Smoke seals (where required)
- Vision panels (fire-rated glass)
- Certification and labeling

5.4 FIRE-RESISTANT MATERIALS

CONCRETE:
- Inherent fire resistance
- Cover to reinforcement critical
- Minimum dimensions for ratings

MASONRY:
- Solid blocks/bricks
- Minimum thickness for ratings
- Plastered finish improves rating

FIRE-RATED BOARDS:
- Gypsum boards
- Calcium silicate boards
- Specifications and installation

INTUMESCENT COATINGS:
- Applied to steel structures
- Expands when heated
- Thickness for required rating

CHAPTER 6: FIRE DETECTION AND ALARM SYSTEMS
======================================================================

6.1 SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS

AUTOMATIC FIRE DETECTION:
Required in:
- Buildings >1500 m² floor area
- High-rise buildings (all)
- Assembly occupancies (>300 persons)
- Hotels and guesthouses
- Hospitals and care facilities
- Educational institutions (boarding)

6.2 DETECTION DEVICES

SMOKE DETECTORS:
Types:
- Ionization type
- Photoelectric type
- Beam type (large spaces)
- Aspirating systems (high-value areas)

Locations:
- Corridors and common areas
- Sleeping areas
- Mechanical and electrical rooms
- Elevator lobbies and shafts
- Storage areas

Coverage:
- Maximum 100 m² per detector (corridors)
- Maximum 80 m² per detector (rooms)
- Adjusted for ceiling height

HEAT DETECTORS:
Types:
- Fixed temperature
- Rate-of-rise
- Combination

Applications:
- Kitchens
- Boiler rooms
- Smoking areas
- Areas unsuitable for smoke detection

6.3 MANUAL FIRE ALARM

MANUAL CALL POINTS:
Location:
- All exit routes
- Near exit doors
- Maximum 30m travel distance to call point
- 1.4m height from floor

Requirements:
- Break-glass or push-button type
- Clearly visible and accessible
- Labeled "FIRE ALARM"

6.4 ALARM NOTIFICATION

AUDIBLE ALARMS:
- Sounders throughout building
- Minimum 65 dBA at all points
- Distinctive sound (continuous/pulsing)
- Audible above ambient noise

VISUAL ALARMS:
- Required in noisy areas
- Accessibility for hearing impaired
- Strobe lights
- Minimum candela rating

6.5 FIRE ALARM CONTROL PANEL

LOCATION:
- Near main entrance
- Accessible to fire service
- Secure but accessible

FEATURES:
- Zone indication
- Fault monitoring
- Mains and battery power
- Silence/reset controls
- Test facilities
- Log/event recording

6.6 POWER SUPPLY

PRIMARY POWER:
- Dedicated electrical supply
- Circuit protection
- Surge protection

SECONDARY POWER:
- Battery backup
- Minimum 24 hours standby + 30 minutes alarm
- Automatic changeover
- Battery monitoring

6.7 SYSTEM STANDARDS

Design and installation per:
- NFPA 72: National Fire Alarm and Signaling Code
- BS 5839: Fire detection and alarm systems
- System certification required

CHAPTER 7: FIRE SUPPRESSION SYSTEMS
======================================================================

7.1 AUTOMATIC SPRINKLER SYSTEMS

REQUIREMENT:
Mandatory for:
- High-rise buildings (>30m)
- Large commercial buildings (>2000 m²)
- Assembly occupancies (>500 persons)
- Hotels (>20 rooms)
- Basements (>200 m²)
- Storage areas with high fire load

7.2 SPRINKLER SYSTEM DESIGN

DESIGN STANDARDS:
- NFPA 13: Standard for Installation of Sprinkler Systems
- BS EN 12845: Fixed firefighting systems - Sprinkler systems
- Hydraulic calculations required
- Appropriate hazard classification

HAZARD CLASSIFICATION:
Light Hazard:
- Offices, educational facilities
- Density: 2.25 mm/min over 84 m²

Ordinary Hazard Group 1:
- Parking structures, restaurants
- Density: 5 mm/min over 144 m²

Ordinary Hazard Group 2:
- Retail, warehouses
- Density: 6.1 mm/min over 144 m²

Extra Hazard:
- Industrial processes, high-fire-load storage
- Higher densities and areas

7.3 SPRINKLER SYSTEM COMPONENTS

SPRINKLER HEADS:
Types:
- Pendant
- Upright
- Sidewall
- Concealed

Temperature ratings:
- 68°C (Ordinary)
- 79°C (Intermediate)
- 93°C (High)
- 141°C (Extra High)

Coverage:
- Maximum 9-21 m² per head (varies by hazard)
- Specific spacing requirements
- Obstruction rules

PIPING:
- Steel pipes (galvanized or black)
- CPVC (residential applications)
- Sizing per hydraulic calculation
- Support and bracing requirements

WATER SUPPLY:
Requirements:
- Adequate pressure and flow
- Reliability (two sources preferred)
- Duration: 30-90 minutes (varies by hazard)

Sources:
- Municipal water supply
- Underground tanks with pumps
- Elevated tanks
- Pressure tanks

CONTROL VALVES:
- Main control valve (OS&Y or butterfly)
- Floor/zone control valves
- Supervised (tamper switches)
- Testing and inspection provisions

ALARM DEVICES:
- Water flow switches
- Pressure switches
- Alarm bells/gongs
- Connection to building fire alarm

7.4 STANDPIPE AND HOSE SYSTEMS

FIRE HOSE REELS:
Requirements:
- Maximum 30m coverage radius
- 25mm or 38mm hose
- Sufficient water pressure (250 kPa minimum)
- Located in protected positions

WET RISERS (Hose Stations):
- Vertical water supply pipes
- Outlet valves at each floor
- 65mm connections
- For buildings up to 45m height

DRY RISERS:
- For fire service use
- Buildings above 18m (where wet risers not provided)
- 100mm diameter
- Inlet at ground level (Siamese connection)
- Outlets at each floor

7.5 PORTABLE FIRE EXTINGUISHERS

TYPES:

Water extinguishers:
- Class A fires (ordinary combustibles)
- 9 liters capacity (common)

Foam extinguishers:
- Class A and B fires
- 6-9 liters capacity

Dry powder extinguishers:
- Class A, B, C, and electrical fires
- Multi-purpose (ABC type)
- 4-9 kg capacity

CO₂ extinguishers:
- Electrical fires, Class B
- 2-5 kg capacity
- Computer rooms, electrical panels

DISTRIBUTION:

Travel distance:
- Class A hazards: 23m maximum
- Class B hazards: 15m maximum

Locations:
- Near exits
- At fire risk points
- Visible and accessible
- Mounting height: 1.0-1.5m

Signage:
- "FIRE EXTINGUISHER" sign
- Type indication
- Operating instructions

MAINTENANCE:
- Monthly visual inspection
- Annual service and testing
- Recharge after use
- Pressure gauge monitoring

7.6 SPECIAL SUPPRESSION SYSTEMS

KITCHEN HOOD SYSTEMS:
- Wet chemical systems
- Automatic activation
- Manual pull stations
- Integration with gas shutoff

GASEOUS SUPPRESSION:
Applications:
- Server rooms
- Data centers
- Control rooms
- Museums and archives

Agents:
- FM-200 (HFC-227ea)
- Novec 1230
- CO₂ (limited applications)

WATER MIST SYSTEMS:
- Alternative to sprinklers
- Fine water droplets
- Suitable for special applications
- Lower water damage

======================================================================
CHAPTER 8: HIGH-RISE BUILDING REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

8.1 DEFINITION

High-rise buildings generally considered as:
- Buildings with occupied floors >30m above ground
- Or buildings with 10 or more stories

8.2 ENHANCED FIRE SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

FIRE-RESISTANT CONSTRUCTION:
- Higher fire resistance ratings (3-4 hours)
- Non-combustible construction
- Protected vertical shafts
- Compartmentation

MULTIPLE ESCAPE ROUTES:
- Minimum two separated stairways
- Scissor stairs or spatially separated
- Protected enclosures
- Pressurization systems

PRESSURIZED STAIRWAYS:
- Positive air pressure
- Prevents smoke infiltration
- Maintains tenable conditions
- Automatic activation

FIRE LIFTS:
- At least one fire service lift
- Protected shaft and lobby
- Fire-resistant power supply
- Communication system
- Suitable car size (minimum 1.1m x 2.1m)

FIREFIGHTING SHAFTS:
- Combined stairway and fire lift
- Fire-resistant lobby
- Fire-fighting equipment
- Dry/wet riser
- Communication point

AUTOMATIC SPRINKLER SYSTEMS:
- Throughout building
- Appropriate system design
- Reliable water supply
- Fire service inlet

FIRE ALARM AND DETECTION:
- Building-wide automatic detection
- Voice communication system
- Phased evacuation capability
- Fire control center

EMERGENCY POWER:
- Generator backup
- Critical system support:
  * Fire pumps
  * Fire alarm
  * Emergency lighting
  * Fire lifts
  * Pressurization fans
  * Smoke control

FIRE COMMAND CENTER:
Location:
- Ground floor
- Near main entrance
- Protected construction

Equipment:
- Fire alarm control panel
- Communication systems
- CCTV monitors
- Building systems control
- Fire service telephone
- Emergency plans

CHAPTER 9: SPECIAL OCCUPANCY REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

9.1 ASSEMBLY OCCUPANCIES

Examples: Theaters, cinemas, auditoriums, places of worship

SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS:
- Enhanced egress capacity
- Multiple exits (minimum 3 for large assemblies)
- Panic hardware on exit doors
- Emergency lighting
- Exit signage
- Seating arrangements (aisle widths)
- Automatic sprinklers (large occupancies)
- Voice evacuation system

9.2 EDUCATIONAL INSTITUTIONS

REQUIREMENTS:
- Clear egress routes
- Fire drills and evacuation procedures
- Fire alarm systems
- Emergency lighting
- Fire extinguishers
- Assembly area provisions
- Boarding facilities (enhanced requirements)

9.3 HEALTHCARE FACILITIES

Hospitals, nursing homes, care facilities

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- Defend-in-place strategy (limited evacuation)
- Horizontal evacuation (to adjacent compartments)
- Enhanced compartmentation
- Automatic sprinklers throughout
- Redundant fire alarm systems
- Emergency power (extended duration)
- Smoke control systems
- Staff training

9.4 HOTELS AND GUEST HOUSES

REQUIREMENTS:
- Fire alarm systems (audible in all guest rooms)
- Smoke detectors in guest rooms
- Emergency lighting
- Exit signage
- Escape route plans in rooms
- Staff training
- Sprinklers (larger hotels)
- Fire extinguishers on each floor

9.5 INDUSTRIAL OCCUPANCIES

CONSIDERATIONS:
- Hazard assessment
- Appropriate suppression systems
- Separation of high-hazard areas
- Emergency shutdown procedures
- Specialized detection systems
- Employee training
- Hot work permits

9.6 SHOPPING COMPLEXES

REQUIREMENTS:
- Comprehensive fire detection and alarm
- Automatic sprinklers
- Smoke control (atriums)
- Multiple exits
- Travel distance compliance
- Emergency lighting
- Public address system
- Management procedures

CHAPTER 10: FIRE SERVICE ACCESS
======================================================================

10.1 ACCESS ROADS

REQUIREMENTS:
- Minimum width: 3.7m (preferably 6m)
- Minimum height clearance: 3.7m
- Surface: Hard standing, all-weather
- Gradient: Maximum 10%
- Turning radius: Minimum 9m
- Weight capacity: 17 tonnes (fire appliance)
- Access within 45m of all points of building

DEAD-END ROADS:
- Maximum 20m length (must provide turning)
- Turning circle or hammerhead

10.2 FIRE SERVICE VEHICLE POSITIONING

HARD STANDINGS:
- Adjacent to building
- Suitable for aerial appliances (high-rise)
- Level ground (maximum 5% slope)
- Clear of overhead obstructions

10.3 FIRE HYDRANTS

EXTERNAL HYDRANTS:
- Minimum 75mm connection
- Maximum 135m spacing along roads
- Maximum 90m from building entrance
- Adequate water pressure and flow
- Clear identification and access

10.4 FIRE SERVICE INLETS

SPRINKLER/RISER INLETS:
- Located at accessible position
- Ground level or near
- Clearly labeled
- 65mm (landing valve) or 100mm (dry riser)
- Siamese connection for dual supply

CHAPTER 11: FIRE SAFETY MANAGEMENT
======================================================================

11.1 FIRE SAFETY PLAN

REQUIREMENTS:
- Documented fire safety management plan
- Evacuation procedures
- Staff responsibilities
- Maintenance schedules
- Training programs
- Emergency contact information

11.2 FIRE DRILLS

- Regular fire drills (at least annually)
- Evacuation time recording
- Identification of issues
- Staff familiarity with procedures
- Visitor management

11.3 MAINTENANCE AND TESTING

FIRE DETECTION AND ALARM:
- Weekly testing (sounders)
- Monthly testing (detectors - sample)
- Annual service and certification

SPRINKLER SYSTEMS:
- Weekly visual inspection
- Quarterly alarm testing
- Annual full system test
- Five-year internal inspection (pipes)

FIRE EXTINGUISHERS:
- Monthly visual check
- Annual servicing
- Pressure testing (as scheduled)

EMERGENCY LIGHTING:
- Monthly function test (brief)
- Annual duration test (full 90 minutes)

FIRE DOORS:
- Monthly check (closing, seals)
- Annual service (self-closers)

11.4 RECORD KEEPING

Maintain records of:
- System tests and inspections
- Maintenance activities
- Fire drills
- Training sessions
- Modifications
- Incident reports

CHAPTER 12: APPROVAL PROCESS
======================================================================

12.1 FIRE SAFETY PLAN SUBMISSION

REQUIRED FOR:
- New construction (applicable buildings)
- Major renovations
- Change of occupancy
- Installation of fire systems

SUBMISSION TO:
- Construction Industry Development Authority (CIDA)
- Municipal Council
- UDA (for urban development areas)

12.2 REQUIRED DOCUMENTS

- Architectural drawings (showing fire safety features)
- Fire safety plan
- System design calculations
- Equipment specifications
- Professional certifications
- Compliance statement

12.3 REVIEW AND APPROVAL

TIMELINE:
- Fire Safety Recommendations issued within 3 working days (CMC)
- Technical review period varies

APPROVAL OUTCOME:
- Fire NOC (No Objection Certificate)
- Conditions (if any)
- Validity period

12.4 INSPECTION

STAGES:
- During construction (system installation)
- Upon completion (final inspection)
- Commissioning (system testing)

CERTIFICATE:
- Fire Safety Certificate
- Required before occupancy
- Periodic renewals

CONCLUSION
======================================================================

Fire safety is a critical aspect of building design and management in
Sri Lanka. While current regulations provide a foundation, continuous
improvement is needed to address gaps and enhance protection.

Key principles:
- Prevention through design
- Early detection and warning
- Effective suppression
- Safe evacuation
- Fire service access
- Ongoing maintenance
- Regular training

Compliance with fire safety regulations ensures:
- Protection of life and property
- Legal compliance
- Insurance validity
- Business continuity
- Public confidence

All building owners, designers, and managers must prioritize fire safety
and ensure full compliance with applicable regulations.
