CHAPTER 1: OVERVIEW OF NWSDB
======================================================================

1.1 ORGANIZATION BACKGROUND AND MANDATE

The National Water Supply and Drainage Board (NWSDB) is the National Organization
responsible for the provision of safe drinking water and facilitating the provision
of sanitation to the people in Sri Lanka.

ESTABLISHMENT:
- Established under NWSDB Act No. 2 of 1974
- Subsequent amendments strengthening powers and responsibilities
- Operates under Ministry of Water Supply
- Coverage: Urban and semi-urban areas across Sri Lanka

PRIMARY FUNCTIONS:

a) Water Supply:
   - Planning, design, and construction of water supply schemes
   - Operation and maintenance of water treatment plants
   - Distribution of potable water
   - Quality assurance and monitoring
   - Customer service and billing

b) Sewerage and Drainage:
   - Sewerage system planning and implementation
   - Wastewater treatment plants
   - Storm water drainage (in coordination with local authorities)
   - Sanitation facilities

c) Standards and Regulation:
   - Setting technical standards for water supply systems
   - Specifications for materials and equipment
   - Design guidelines and manuals
   - Quality control procedures

d) Research and Development:
   - Water resource management
   - Treatment technologies
   - Non-revenue water reduction
   - Capacity building

1.2 SERVICE COVERAGE

WATER SUPPLY COVERAGE:
- Urban water supply schemes: Major cities and towns
- Rural water supply: In coordination with provincial councils
- Population coverage: Approximately 40-45% of national population
- Service connections: Over 1.2 million connections

SEWERAGE COVERAGE:
- Limited to selected urban areas
- Greater Colombo sewerage scheme
- Other town sewerage projects
- Expanding coverage gradually

SERVICE STANDARDS:
- Water quality: Compliance with SLS 614:2013 standard for drinking water
- Supply hours: 24-hour supply in most urban areas
- Pressure: Minimum 15 m head at customer connection
- Response time: Emergency repairs within 24-48 hours

1.3 REGULATORY STANDARDS

STANDARDS COMPLIANCE:

Sri Lanka Standards (SLS):
- SLS 614:2013: Specification for potable water (physical and chemical requirements)
- SLS standards for pipes, fittings, and materials
- Microbiological standards for drinking water

British Standards (where SLS not available):
- BS 6700: Design, installation, testing, and maintenance of services supplying
  water for domestic use
- BS 8301: Code of practice for building drainage
- BS EN standards for materials and components

World Health Organization (WHO) Guidelines:
- Guidelines for drinking water quality
- International best practices

Central Environmental Authority (CEA) Standards:
- Discharge standards for wastewater
- Environmental protection requirements

CHAPTER 2: WATER SUPPLY SYSTEM DESIGN STANDARDS
======================================================================

2.1 WATER DEMAND ESTIMATION

DOMESTIC CONSUMPTION:

Per Capita Demand:
- Urban areas: 120-150 liters per capita per day (lpcd)
- Semi-urban areas: 100-120 lpcd
- Rural areas: 60-80 lpcd
- High-end urban: 180-200 lpcd

Variations:
- Climate and temperature
- Socioeconomic status
- Water availability and reliability
- Cultural practices and habits

Household Consumption:
- Average family size: 4-5 persons
- Monthly consumption: 15-25 m³ per household
- Peak factor: 1.5-2.0 times average demand

NON-DOMESTIC CONSUMPTION:

Commercial Establishments:
- Offices: 45-60 liters per employee per day
- Restaurants: 50-70 liters per meal served
- Hotels: 200-400 liters per guest per day
- Shopping centers: 15-25 liters per m² per day

Institutional:
- Schools: 30-45 liters per student per day
- Hospitals: 300-500 liters per bed per day
- Hostels: 120-150 liters per person per day

Industrial:
- Light industry: 20-40 liters per employee per day
- Manufacturing: Variable depending on process
- Cooling water: Significant demand for certain industries

DESIGN FACTORS:

Peak Demand Factors:
- Peak hour factor: 1.5-2.5 times average daily demand
- Peak day factor: 1.2-1.5 times average daily demand
- Minimum night flow: 0.3-0.5 times average demand

Future Demand:
- Design period: 20-30 years for infrastructure
- Population growth rate: 1-2% per annum (varies by region)
- Increase in per capita consumption: 1-1.5% per annum
- Industrial and commercial growth projections

Losses and Wastage:
- Non-Revenue Water (NRW): 15-25% (target), actual may be higher
- Leakage, theft, and meter inaccuracies
- Design factor: 1.15-1.25 times net demand

2.2 WATER SOURCE DEVELOPMENT

WATER SOURCES:

Surface Water:
- Rivers and streams
- Reservoirs and impoundments
- Tanks and lakes
- Treatment required before supply

Groundwater:
- Wells (dug wells and tube wells)
- Borewells and deep wells
- Spring water
- May require minimal treatment

Rainwater:
- Rainwater harvesting for supplementary supply
- Rooftop collection systems
- Storage and filtration

INTAKE STRUCTURES:

River Intakes:
- Located upstream of pollution sources
- Provision for different water levels (low flow and flood)
- Screening chambers
- Pumping stations

Reservoir Intakes:
- Multiple level intakes for water quality control
- Tower intake or submerged intake
- Screens and trash racks

Well and Borehole:
- Proper construction and casing
- Sanitary seal and wellhead protection
- Submersible pumps

2.3 WATER TREATMENT STANDARDS

TREATMENT PROCESSES:

Conventional Treatment Train:
1. Screening (coarse and fine screens)
2. Coagulation and flocculation
3. Sedimentation
4. Filtration (rapid sand filters)
5. Disinfection (chlorination)
6. pH adjustment (if required)
7. Storage

Coagulation:
- Alum (aluminum sulfate): 15-50 mg/L
- Ferric chloride or PAC (alternative coagulants)
- Rapid mixing: 1-3 minutes
- Flocculation: 20-30 minutes

Sedimentation:
- Detention time: 2-4 hours
- Surface loading rate: 15-30 m³/m²/day
- Clarifier or settling tank

Filtration:
- Rapid gravity sand filters
- Filter media: Sand (0.6-0.8 mm effective size), gravel support
- Filtration rate: 5-10 m³/m²/hour
- Backwashing frequency: 24-48 hours or when head loss excessive

Disinfection:
- Chlorination (most common): Free residual chlorine 0.2-0.5 mg/L at consumer end
- Contact time: Minimum 30 minutes
- Chlorine dose: 1-3 mg/L (depending on raw water quality)
- Alternative: Chlorine dioxide, UV treatment

WATER QUALITY STANDARDS:

Potable Water Requirements (SLS 614:2013):

Physical Parameters:
- Turbidity: < 2 NTU (Nephelometric Turbidity Units)
- Color: < 15 TCU (True Color Units)
- pH: 6.5-8.5
- Total dissolved solids: < 500 mg/L (desirable), < 1,000 mg/L (maximum)
- Temperature: Ambient

Chemical Parameters:
- Calcium (Ca): < 100 mg/L
- Magnesium (Mg): < 30 mg/L
- Iron (Fe): < 0.3 mg/L
- Manganese (Mn): < 0.1 mg/L
- Chloride (Cl): < 250 mg/L
- Sulfate (SO₄): < 250 mg/L
- Nitrate (NO₃): < 50 mg/L
- Fluoride (F): 0.6-1.0 mg/L (optimum), < 1.5 mg/L (maximum)
- Arsenic (As): < 0.01 mg/L
- Lead (Pb): < 0.01 mg/L
- Cadmium (Cd): < 0.003 mg/L

Microbiological Parameters:
- E. coli: 0 CFU/100 mL (zero tolerance)
- Total coliform: 0 CFU/100 mL (in 95% of samples)
- Fecal coliform: 0 CFU/100 mL

Disinfection Residual:
- Free residual chlorine: 0.2-0.5 mg/L at consumer tap

TESTING FREQUENCY:

Regular Monitoring:
- Daily: Turbidity, pH, residual chlorine
- Weekly: Bacteriological tests at treatment plant
- Monthly: Full physical and chemical analysis
- Consumer complaints: Immediate testing

Sampling Points:
- Raw water source
- Treated water (clear water tank)
- Distribution system (multiple points)
- Consumer taps (random sampling)

2.4 TRANSMISSION AND DISTRIBUTION SYSTEM DESIGN

PIPELINE DESIGN:

Pipe Material Selection:

Common Materials:
- uPVC (unplasticized Polyvinyl Chloride): Most common, PN 10-16 bar
- DI (Ductile Iron): Main transmission, PN 25-40 bar
- HDPE (High Density Polyethylene): Increasing use, flexible
- GI (Galvanized Iron): Older systems, being replaced
- AC (Asbestos Cement): Old systems, no longer used for new installations
- Mild Steel: Large diameter transmission mains

Material Standards:
- uPVC: SLS or BS standards
- DI pipes: ISO 2531, BS EN 545
- HDPE: ISO 4427, PE 80 or PE 100
- GI pipes: BS 1387 (for service connections)

Pipe Sizing:

Velocity Limits:
- Minimum velocity: 0.3-0.5 m/s (prevent sedimentation)
- Maximum velocity: 2.0-3.0 m/s (prevent erosion and water hammer)
- Typical design velocity: 0.6-1.5 m/s

Hazen-Williams Formula (commonly used):
V = 0.85 × C × R^0.63 × S^0.54
Where: V = velocity, C = roughness coefficient, R = hydraulic radius, S = slope

Hazen-Williams C values:
- uPVC, HDPE: 140-150
- DI with lining: 120-130
- GI pipes: 100-120 (new), 60-80 (old)

Head Loss Calculation:
- Friction losses in pipes (Hazen-Williams or Darcy-Weisbach)
- Minor losses at fittings, valves, bends
- Total head = Static head + Friction head + Minor losses + Residual pressure

Pressure Requirements:
- Minimum pressure at consumer tap: 15 m head (1.5 bar)
- Maximum pressure: 60 m head (6.0 bar)
- Pressure reducing valves where pressure exceeds limits

DISTRIBUTION NETWORK CONFIGURATION:

Network Types:
- Grid/Loop system: Preferred for urban areas, reliable supply
- Branch/Tree system: Used in low-density areas, economical
- Combination system: Mixed approach

Design Considerations:
- Minimize dead ends
- Adequate fire flow capacity
- Provision for future expansion
- Sectionalization with valves for maintenance
- Pressure zones for hilly areas

STORAGE FACILITIES:

Purpose of Storage:
- Balance supply and demand variations
- Provide reserve for emergencies
- Maintain system pressure
- Enable treatment plant operation at constant rate

Storage Capacity:
- Service reservoir: 25-30% of average daily demand
- Overhead tanks: 1/3 to 1/2 of peak hourly demand for the zone
- Elevated storage: Provides gravity pressure

Types of Storage:
- Ground level service reservoirs (RCC construction)
- Elevated tanks (overhead tanks on towers)
- Underground reservoirs (where space limited)

Construction Standards:
- Reinforced concrete (most common)
- Water-tightness critical
- Proper inlet, outlet, overflow arrangements
- Access manholes and ventilation
- Draining and cleaning provisions
- Security fencing and protection

PUMPING STATIONS:

Pump Selection:
- Centrifugal pumps (most common)
- Submersible pumps (for wells and lift stations)
- Pump capacity: Peak hourly demand + losses
- Number of pumps: Minimum 2 (1 duty + 1 standby)

Pump Performance:
- Operating point on pump curve
- Efficiency: > 70% at operating point
- NPSH (Net Positive Suction Head) requirements

Power Supply:
- Reliable power supply essential
- Standby generator for critical pumping stations
- Variable frequency drives (VFDs) for energy efficiency
- SCADA systems for monitoring and control

CHAPTER 3: SERVICE CONNECTION STANDARDS
======================================================================

3.1 WATER SERVICE CONNECTION SPECIFICATIONS

CONNECTION TYPES:

Domestic Connection:
- Standard sizes: 12.5 mm (½"), 19 mm (¾"), 25 mm (1")
- Connection from distribution main to customer premises
- Includes ferrule, gooseneck, meter, and stop valve

Commercial Connection:
- Larger sizes: 25 mm (1") to 50 mm (2") or more
- Based on demand calculation
- Separate meter for fire hydrant (if required)

Bulk/Institutional Connection:
- Large diameter: 50 mm (2") to 150 mm (6") or more
- Direct connection from transmission main (if justified)
- Bulk meters and metering chambers

CONNECTION COMPONENTS:

Ferrule (Saddle):
- Brass or gunmetal ferrule
- Clamped on distribution main
- Sizes: 12.5, 19, 25 mm
- Tapping done under pressure (wet tapping)

Gooseneck/Service Pipe:
- Flexible connection from main to meter
- HDPE or GI pipe
- Length: 1.5-3.0 m (as required)
- Buried at minimum 600 mm depth

Water Meter:
- Multi-jet meters (common for domestic)
- Single-jet meters (smaller connections)
- Woltman meters (larger connections > 50 mm)
- Meter sizes: 12.5, 19, 25, 40, 50, 80, 100 mm
- Accuracy class: B or better

Stop Valve:
- Before meter: NWSDB control valve (locked)
- After meter: Consumer control valve
- Brass or gunmetal valve

Meter Chamber:
- Precast concrete meter box
- Dimensions: Appropriate for meter size
- Cast iron cover with "NWSDB" marking
- Located outside property boundary or as agreed
- Minimum 300 mm × 300 mm × 300 mm (for small meters)

3.2 INTERNAL PLUMBING STANDARDS

PIPE MATERIALS FOR INTERNAL DISTRIBUTION:

Permitted Materials:
- CPVC (Chlorinated Polyvinyl Chloride): Hot and cold water
- PEX (Cross-linked Polyethylene): Hot and cold water
- PPR (Polypropylene Random Copolymer): Hot and cold water
- Copper pipes: Premium installations
- uPVC: Cold water only
- GI pipes: Older installations (corrosion issues)

Pipe Sizing for Buildings:
- Based on fixture units and demand
- Minimum size for distribution: 19 mm (¾")
- Branch to fixtures: 12.5-19 mm
- Riser pipes in multi-story: 25-50 mm

INSTALLATION STANDARDS:

Pipe Laying:
- Pipes run concealed in chases or suspended from ceiling
- Adequate support and bracketing
- Expansion provision for plastic pipes
- Isolation valves at each floor and branch
- Protection from physical damage

Water Tank Installation:
- Overhead tank: RCC, fiber glass, or food-grade plastic
- Capacity: 1-2 days consumption for building
- Height: Provide minimum 15 m head at highest fixture
- Inlet with ball valve and overflow
- Access manhole with cover
- Cleaning provision
- Overflow and ventilation

Hot Water System:
- Solar water heaters (encouraged)
- Electric geysers or boilers
- Separate hot water distribution
- Insulation of hot water pipes
- Tempering valves where required

Fixtures and Fittings:
- Wash basins, sinks, toilets, showers
- Water-efficient fixtures (low-flow)
- Good quality taps and valves
- Proper drainage connections

3.3 WATER CONSERVATION MEASURES

MANDATORY CONSERVATION:

Rainwater Harvesting:
- Mandatory for buildings > 500 m² roof area (per UDA regulations)
- Harvesting from roof surface
- First flush diverter
- Storage tank: 10-20 liters per m² of roof area
- Filtration before use
- Use: Toilet flushing, gardening, washing

Water-Efficient Fixtures:
- Low-flow taps: 4-6 liters per minute
- Dual-flush toilets: 3/6 liter flush
- Showers with flow restrictors: 8-10 liters per minute
- Waterless urinals or low-flush urinals

Leak Detection and Prevention:
- Regular inspection of plumbing
- Repair of leaks promptly
- Quality materials and installation
- Pressure regulation to prevent damage

Reuse and Recycling:
- Greywater reuse (treated wastewater from sinks, showers)
- Use for toilet flushing and irrigation
- Separate plumbing for recycled water
- Proper treatment and disinfection

TARIFF STRUCTURE FOR CONSERVATION:

NWSDB Tariff (Progressive):
- First 10 m³: Low rate (encourage basic access)
- 11-20 m³: Moderate rate
- 21-40 m³: Higher rate
- Above 40 m³: Highest rate (discourage wastage)

Non-Domestic Tariff:
- Higher rates for commercial and industrial
- Bulk users: Negotiated rates

CHAPTER 4: SEWERAGE AND WASTEWATER MANAGEMENT
======================================================================

4.1 SEWERAGE SYSTEM TYPES

TYPES OF SEWERAGE SYSTEMS:

Separate System:
- Sanitary sewers (wastewater only)
- Storm drains (rainwater only)
- Preferred system for new developments
- Advantages: Smaller sewer size, easier treatment, no overflow during rain

Combined System:
- Single pipe for sewage and storm water
- Common in older urban areas
- Disadvantages: Large pipe size, combined sewer overflows (CSO), complex treatment

Partially Separate:
- Combination approach
- Common in transitional areas

ON-SITE SANITATION:

Where Public Sewerage Not Available:

Septic Tank System:
- Most common on-site system
- Primary treatment of sewage
- Effluent to soak pit or drain field
- Design based on daily sewage flow

Design Criteria:
- Capacity: 1 day sewage retention + sludge accumulation space
- Dimensions: Length:Width = 2:1 to 3:1
- Depth: 1.2-1.5 m (liquid depth)
- Minimum size: 1.5 m³ (for small households)
- Construction: RCC or brick masonry, plastered, water-tight

Soak Pit/Leach Pit:
- Receives effluent from septic tank
- Percolation into soil
- Design based on soil percolation rate
- Minimum 3 m from buildings, 30 m from wells

Alternative Systems:
- Biogas plants (combined waste treatment and energy)
- Constructed wetlands
- Package treatment plants (for institutions)

4.2 PUBLIC SEWERAGE SYSTEM DESIGN

SEWAGE FLOW ESTIMATION:

Domestic Sewage:
- Approximately 80% of water supply
- Per capita sewage flow: 100-120 liters per day
- Infiltration allowance: 10-20%
- Peak factor: 2-3 times average flow

Non-Domestic Sewage:
- Based on water consumption
- Commercial: 80-90% of water supply
- Industrial: Variable (may include process wastewater)

Design Flow:
Q = Average flow × Peak factor + Infiltration

SEWER DESIGN STANDARDS:

Pipe Sizes:
- Minimum diameter: 150 mm (6") for lateral sewers
- Main sewers: 200-300 mm (8"-12")
- Trunk sewers: 300 mm+ (12"+)

Pipe Materials:
- uPVC sewers: Most common, SN 4 or SN 8 class
- Concrete pipes: Large diameter sewers
- DI pipes: Pumping mains
- HDPE: Special applications

Gradients and Velocities:

Minimum Velocity:
- Self-cleansing velocity: 0.6-0.8 m/s at peak flow
- Prevents solid deposition

Maximum Velocity:
- 3.0 m/s (to prevent pipe erosion)

Minimum Gradient:
- 150 mm pipe: 1:100 (1%)
- 200 mm pipe: 1:150 (0.67%)
- 250 mm pipe: 1:200 (0.5%)
- Larger pipes: Flatter gradients permissible

Flow Depth:
- Proportional flow: D/D pipe = 0.5-0.75 at peak flow
- Provides capacity for surge and future growth
- Full bore flow avoided (no ventilation)

SEWER APPURTENANCES:

Manholes:
- Access for inspection, maintenance, cleaning
- Location: Changes in direction, gradient, or pipe size; junctions
- Spacing: Maximum 80-100 m for 150-300 mm sewers
- Construction: Precast concrete or brick masonry
- Manhole depth: Based on sewer invert level
- Manhole cover: Cast iron, load-rated for traffic

Drop Manholes:
- Where incoming sewer has high invert relative to outgoing
- Inside or outside drop pipe
- Cushions energy of falling sewage

Sewer Junctions:
- Smooth transitions
- Oblique junctions preferred (minimize turbulence)
- Invert matching or drop where required

Ventilation:
- Fresh air inlets at upstream ends
- Ventilating shafts in long sewers
- Natural ventilation through manholes

4.3 WASTEWATER TREATMENT

TREATMENT LEVELS:

Preliminary Treatment:
- Screening (coarse and fine)
- Grit removal
- Removes large solids and inorganic material

Primary Treatment:
- Sedimentation
- Removes 50-60% of suspended solids
- 30-40% BOD removal

Secondary Treatment:
- Biological treatment (activated sludge, trickling filters, oxidation ponds)
- Removes 85-95% BOD
- Reduces pathogens

Tertiary Treatment:
- Advanced treatment for specific contaminants
- Nutrient removal (nitrogen, phosphorus)
- Disinfection (chlorination, UV)

COMMON TREATMENT PROCESSES IN SRI LANKA:

Waste Stabilization Ponds:
- Simple, low-cost technology
- Series of anaerobic, facultative, and maturation ponds
- Long retention time (20-40 days)
- Suitable for tropical climate
- Large land area required

Activated Sludge Process:
- Aeration tank with suspended biomass
- Secondary settling tank
- Return sludge and waste sludge
- Efficient treatment, smaller footprint
- Higher operational cost

Trickling Filters:
- Attached growth biological treatment
- Filter media (stone or plastic)
- Natural ventilation
- Moderate efficiency

Upflow Anaerobic Sludge Blanket (UASB):
- Anaerobic treatment
- Biogas production
- Suitable for warm climate
- Pre-treatment before aerobic treatment

EFFLUENT DISCHARGE STANDARDS:

Central Environmental Authority (CEA) Standards:

Discharge to Inland Waters:
- BOD₅: < 30 mg/L
- COD: < 250 mg/L
- Suspended solids: < 50 mg/L
- pH: 6.0-8.5
- Oil and grease: < 10 mg/L

Discharge to Marine Waters:
- BOD₅: < 100 mg/L
- COD: < 250 mg/L
- Suspended solids: < 150 mg/L
- Fecal coliform: < 400 MPN/100 mL

Reuse Standards:
- Treated wastewater for irrigation
- Unrestricted irrigation: Stricter standards (coliform < 200/100mL)
- Restricted irrigation: Moderate standards (coliform < 1000/100mL)

SLUDGE MANAGEMENT:

Sludge Treatment:
- Thickening (reduce volume)
- Digestion (anaerobic or aerobic)
- Dewatering (drying beds, mechanical)
- Stabilization

Sludge Disposal:
- Land application (as fertilizer)
- Composting
- Landfill disposal
- Incineration (rarely used)

CHAPTER 5: DRAINAGE STANDARDS
======================================================================

5.1 STORM WATER DRAINAGE

SURFACE DRAINAGE:

Design Storm:
- Return period: 2-5 years (residential)
- Return period: 5-10 years (commercial/industrial)
- Return period: 10-25 years (major roads and critical areas)

Rainfall Intensity:
- Based on local rainfall data
- Intensity-Duration-Frequency (IDF) curves
- Storm duration: Time of concentration

Runoff Calculation:

Rational Method:
Q = C × I × A / 360
Where: Q = runoff (m³/s), C = runoff coefficient, I = intensity (mm/hr), A = area (ha)

Runoff Coefficients:
- Roofs: 0.85-0.95
- Concrete/asphalt surfaces: 0.80-0.90
- Gravel areas: 0.50-0.70
- Lawns and gardens: 0.10-0.30
- Unimproved areas: 0.20-0.40

DRAINAGE SYSTEM DESIGN:

Surface Drains:
- Open drains along roads and plots
- Trapezoidal or rectangular section
- Lined with concrete or stone pitching
- Minimum gradient: 0.3-0.5%

Underground Storm Drains:
- uPVC or concrete pipes
- Minimum diameter: 200 mm (for laterals)
- Main drains: 300-600 mm
- Trunk drains: 600 mm+

Catch Basins/Gully Traps:
- Collect surface runoff
- Trap sediment and debris
- Cast iron grating
- Spacing: 25-50 m along roads

Outfall:
- Discharge to natural watercourse, canal, or sea
- Erosion protection at outfall
- Environmental clearance for major outfalls

ON-SITE DRAINAGE:

Building Drainage:
- Slope paved areas away from building (minimum 1-2%)
- Perimeter drains around foundations
- Downspouts from roof gutters
- Discharge to storm drain or soak pit

Soak Pits for Storm Water:
- Percolation pit for roof runoff
- Dry wells filled with rubble
- Prevents flooding and recharges groundwater
- Size based on catchment area and soil permeability

Sustainable Drainage Systems (SuDS):
- Permeable pavements
- Rain gardens and bio-swales
- Detention and retention ponds
- Infiltration trenches
- Green roofs

5.2 BUILDING SANITARY DRAINAGE

INTERNAL DRAINAGE SYSTEM:

Drainage Pipes:
- uPVC pipes for sanitary drainage (most common)
- Cast iron pipes for critical applications
- Minimum sizes:
  * Toilet outlet: 100 mm
  * Sink/wash basin: 40-50 mm
  * Bath/shower: 50 mm
  * Floor drains: 50-75 mm

Traps:
- P-traps or S-traps for fixtures
- Water seal: 50-75 mm
- Prevents sewer gas entry
- Accessible for cleaning

Vent Pipes:
- Ventilate drainage system
- Prevent siphonage and maintain trap seals
- Minimum vent pipe diameter: 50 mm
- Terminate above roof level (minimum 150 mm above roof)

Inspection Openings:
- Cleanouts at changes in direction
- Accessible for rodding and maintenance

SOIL AND WASTE SYSTEM:

Single Stack System:
- Combined soil and waste stack
- Common in residential buildings
- Adequate venting required

Two-Pipe System:
- Separate soil stack (toilets) and waste stack (other fixtures)
- Better ventilation and maintenance

Horizontal Drains:
- Minimum gradient: 1:40 to 1:60 (2.5% to 1.67%)
- Smooth internal surface
- Minimize bends and junctions

UNDERGROUND DRAINAGE:

Inspection Chambers:
- Precast concrete or brick masonry
- Manhole covers
- Depth: Based on drain invert
- Size: 600 mm × 600 mm (minimum)

Connection to Public Sewer:
- Through inspection chamber at property boundary
- Anti-flooding device (where risk of surcharge)
- Compliance with NWSDB connection standards

Disconnection Chamber:
- Separate surface water and foul water
- Intercepting trap (optional, not commonly used in Sri Lanka)
