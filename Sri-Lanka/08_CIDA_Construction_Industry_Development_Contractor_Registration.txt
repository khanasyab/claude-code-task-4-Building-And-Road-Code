CHAPTER 1: OVERVIEW OF CONSTRUCTION INDUSTRY DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY
======================================================================

1.1 ESTABLISHMENT AND MANDATE

HISTORICAL BACKGROUND:

Institute for Construction Training and Development (ICTAD):
- Established in 1989 as a training and development body
- Introduced Central Registration Scheme for contractors in 1989
- Revised registration schemes in 1993, 1995, 2008
- Published standard specifications and bidding documents
- Developed fire safety regulations for buildings

Construction Industry Development Authority (CIDA):
- Established under Construction Industry Development Act No. 33 of 2014
- CIDA is the successor to ICTAD with enhanced powers and mandate
- Statutory authority with regulatory functions
- Continues registration scheme under new regulations

FUNCTIONS AND POWERS:

Primary Functions (Section 5 of CIDA Act):

a) Industry Regulation:
   - Register and grade contractors
   - Issue licenses and permits
   - Monitor compliance with standards
   - Disciplinary actions for violations

b) Standards Development:
   - Develop and publish technical standards
   - Specifications for construction works
   - Standard bidding documents
   - Quality assurance frameworks

c) Training and Development:
   - Skill development programs for construction workers
   - Training for supervisors and managers
   - Certification programs
   - Continuous professional development

d) Industry Promotion:
   - Promote construction industry development
   - Facilitate technology transfer
   - Encourage innovation
   - Support local contractors

e) Research and Information:
   - Construction industry research
   - Statistical data collection and analysis
   - Best practices documentation
   - Industry publications

f) International Cooperation:
   - Participation in international forums
   - Collaboration with foreign counterparts
   - Adoption of international standards

1.2 CONSTRUCTION INDUSTRY DEVELOPMENT ACT NO. 33 OF 2014

KEY PROVISIONS:

Part I - Establishment of CIDA:
- Constitution and composition of Authority
- Powers and functions
- Financial provisions

Part II - Registration of Contractors:
- Mandatory registration for government contracts
- Registration requirements and procedures
- Grading system based on capability
- Suspension and cancellation provisions

Part III - Training and Development:
- Authority to conduct training programs
- Certification of construction workers
- Quality assurance in training

Part IV - Standards and Specifications:
- Development of technical standards
- Adoption and enforcement
- Fire safety regulations (Chapter 2-6 of ICTAD Fire Regulations)

Part V - Procurement:
- Standard bidding documents
- Procurement procedures for construction
- Prequalification requirements

Part VI - Enforcement:
- Inspections and investigations
- Penalties for violations
- Appeals process

REGULATIONS UNDER CIDA ACT:

Construction Industry Development (Registration of Contractors) Regulation No. 1 of 2017:
- Made under Sections 35(1), (2), (4) and 58 of CIDA Act
- Published in Gazette Extraordinary
- Detailed provisions for contractor registration and grading

1.3 IMPORTANCE OF CIDA REGISTRATION

MANDATORY REQUIREMENT:

For Government Contracts:
- CIDA registration (formerly ICTAD registration) is mandatory for obtaining
  government construction contracts
- Applies to central government, provincial councils, local authorities, and
  state-owned enterprises
- Contracts awarded based on registered grade/category

Benefits of Registration:
- Access to government construction projects
- Pre-qualification for tenders
- Industry recognition and credibility
- Capacity building support from CIDA
- Access to CIDA training programs
- Networking opportunities

Contractor Population:
- Over 2,500 construction contractors registered with CIDA
- Ranging from small-scale local contractors to large national contractors
- Continuous updating and re-grading

CHAPTER 2: CONTRACTOR REGISTRATION REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

2.1 ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

BASIC REQUIREMENTS:

Legal Status:
- Registered organization for construction business in Sri Lanka
- Registration with:
  * Registrar of Companies (under Companies Act), OR
  * Provincial Secretaries (for partnerships or sole proprietorships)

Ownership:
- Minimum 51% ownership by nationals of Sri Lanka
- Ensures promotion of local construction industry
- Foreign contractors may register in specific categories with approval

Business Activity:
- Business of Building/Civil Engineering construction
- Electro-Mechanical works
- Piling and specialized construction
- As evidenced in registration documents

MANDATORY DOCUMENTS:

For Registration Application:
1. Certificate of Incorporation or Business Registration
2. Certified copy of Articles of Association or Partnership Deed
3. List of Directors/Partners with NIC copies
4. Company profile and history
5. Audited financial statements (last 3 years)
6. Equipment list with ownership proof or lease agreements
7. Staff list with qualifications and experience
8. List of completed projects with certificates
9. Tax clearance certificate
10. EPF/ETF registration proof
11. Current registration certificate (for re-grading)
12. Registration fee payment

TECHNICAL PERSONNEL:

Minimum Staff Requirements (vary by grade/category):

Engineers:
- Graduate engineers (BSc Engineering or equivalent)
- Chartered Engineers (C.Eng from IESL) - for higher grades
- Relevant experience in construction
- Full-time employment with company

Technical Officers/Overseers:
- National Diploma or equivalent
- Construction site experience
- Supervisory roles

Quantity Surveyors:
- For larger contractors
- Estimating and contract management

Skilled Workers:
- Masons, carpenters, bar benders, electricians, plumbers
- Evidence of skills and experience

FINANCIAL CAPACITY:

Financial Strength Indicators:
- Paid-up capital
- Net worth (assets minus liabilities)
- Annual turnover
- Credit facilities available (bank overdrafts, letters of credit)
- Liquidity ratios

Verification:
- Audited financial statements
- Bank statements and credit letters
- Income tax returns

EQUIPMENT AND PLANT:

Owned or Leased Equipment:
- Construction machinery (excavators, loaders, compactors, cranes, etc.)
- Transport vehicles (trucks, tippers)
- Concrete mixers and batching plants
- Pumps and generators
- Surveying equipment
- Small tools and equipment

Documentation:
- Ownership documents (bill of sale, invoice)
- Lease agreements (for leased equipment)
- Photographs of major equipment
- Current location and condition

EXPERIENCE AND PAST PERFORMANCE:

Completed Projects:
- List of projects completed in last 5-7 years
- Project details: Name, location, value, scope, duration
- Client certificates and completion certificates
- Photographs of completed works

Performance Record:
- Quality of work
- Timely completion
- Client satisfaction
- No major disputes or legal issues

2.2 REGISTRATION CATEGORIES AND GRADES

CATEGORIES OF REGISTRATION:

Category 1 - Buildings:
- Building construction (residential, commercial, industrial, institutional)
- Includes all building trades (masonry, carpentry, plumbing, electrical, finishing)

Category 2 - Civil Engineering:
- Roads and highways
- Bridges and culverts
- Drainage and irrigation structures
- Earthworks and soil stabilization
- Dams and reservoirs
- Water supply and sewerage systems

Category 3 - Electro-Mechanical:
- Electrical installations (power, lighting, controls)
- Mechanical installations (HVAC, plumbing, fire protection)
- Elevators and escalators
- Specialized systems (BMS, security)

Category 4 - Piling:
- Driven piles (precast concrete, steel)
- Bored piles
- Diaphragm walls
- Ground improvement techniques

Category 5 - Specialized:
- Asphalt paving and road surfacing
- Structural steel fabrication and erection
- Waterproofing and damp-proofing
- Landscaping and horticulture
- Specialized facade systems
- Other niche construction activities

Multiple Category Registration:
- Contractor may register in multiple categories if eligible
- Separate assessment for each category

GRADING SYSTEM:

Grades Based on Capacity (for Building and Civil categories):

Grade C1 (Lowest):
- Project value: Up to Rs. 10 million
- Small-scale local contractors
- Simple projects with standard designs

Grade C2:
- Project value: Rs. 10-25 million
- Small to medium contractors
- Moderately complex projects

Grade C3:
- Project value: Rs. 25-50 million
- Medium-scale contractors
- Multi-trade projects

Grade C4:
- Project value: Rs. 50-100 million
- Medium to large contractors
- Complex projects requiring significant resources

Grade C5:
- Project value: Rs. 100-250 million
- Large contractors
- Major projects with advanced technology

Grade C6:
- Project value: Rs. 250-500 million
- Very large contractors
- High-rise buildings, major infrastructure

Grade C7 (Highest):
- Project value: Rs. 500 million+
- Premier contractors
- Mega projects, complex undertakings

(Note: Values are indicative and subject to revision by CIDA)

Electro-Mechanical, Piling, Specialized:
- May have different grading scales
- Based on technical capacity, equipment, and experience

GRADING CRITERIA:

Registration and grading determined by evaluating:

1. Financial Capability (40-50% weightage):
   - Paid-up capital
   - Net worth
   - Annual turnover
   - Credit facilities
   - Liquidity

2. Technical Ability (30-40% weightage):
   - Qualified staff (engineers, technical officers)
   - Experience of key personnel
   - Organizational structure

3. Plant and Machinery (10-20% weightage):
   - Owned and leased equipment
   - Suitability for project types and sizes
   - Maintenance and operational status

4. Experience (10-20% weightage):
   - Projects completed
   - Value and complexity of projects
   - Performance record
   - Client feedback

Scoring System:
- Points allocated for each criterion
- Total score determines grade
- Minimum threshold for registration
- Higher scores achieve higher grades

2.3 REGISTRATION PROCESS

STEP 1: APPLICATION SUBMISSION

Process:
- Obtain application form from CIDA or download from website
- Complete form with all required information
- Compile all supporting documents
- Submit to CIDA Registration Division with application fee

Application Fee:
- Fee based on applied grade/category
- Non-refundable
- Payment by bank draft or online

STEP 2: PRELIMINARY ASSESSMENT

CIDA Review:
- Check completeness of application and documents
- Initial assessment of eligibility
- Request clarifications or additional documents if required

Timeline: 2-4 weeks for preliminary review

STEP 3: DETAILED EVALUATION

Evaluation Team:
- CIDA technical officers
- Experts in respective categories

Assessment:
- Detailed scrutiny of documents
- Verification of information
- Scoring against grading criteria
- May include interviews with applicant

Physical Verification:
- Site visits to verify equipment, offices, ongoing projects (for higher grades)
- Photographs and verification reports

STEP 4: COMMITTEE DECISION

CIDA Registration Committee:
- Reviews evaluation report
- Makes decision on registration and grade
- May approve, reject, or request further information

STEP 5: REGISTRATION CERTIFICATE ISSUANCE

Upon Approval:
- Registration certificate issued specifying:
  * Contractor name and registration number
  * Category and grade
  * Validity period (typically 2 years)
  * Maximum project value permitted

Certificate:
- Official document to be presented for tender pre-qualification
- Renewable upon expiry

STEP 6: ANNUAL UPDATING

During Validity:
- Annual update of information (staff, equipment, financial, projects)
- Submission of updated documents
- Monitoring of contractor performance

Timeline: Annual update deadline (e.g., March 31 each year)

2.4 RE-GRADING AND UPGRADING

REASONS FOR RE-GRADING:

Upgrading:
- Contractor grown in capacity (financial, technical, equipment, experience)
- Successfully completed larger projects
- Seeking to bid for higher-value projects

Downgrading:
- Financial difficulties or reduced capacity
- Loss of key personnel or equipment
- Poor performance or disputes

UPGRADING PROCESS:

Application:
- Submit upgrading application with updated documents
- Evidence of increased capacity
- Completed projects in current or higher grade

Evaluation:
- Similar to initial registration process
- Assessment against higher grade criteria
- Verification of claimed improvements

Decision:
- Approval for higher grade if criteria met
- Effective from date of approval

VALIDITY AND RENEWAL:

Registration Validity:
- Typically 2 years from date of issuance
- Expiry date indicated on certificate

Renewal Process:
- Apply for renewal before expiry (typically 3 months before)
- Submit updated information and documents
- Renewal fee payment
- Re-assessment (may maintain or change grade based on current status)

Consequences of Non-Renewal:
- Registration lapses
- Cannot bid for government contracts
- Must re-apply as new registration (more complex and time-consuming)

CHAPTER 3: PROCUREMENT AND TENDERING
======================================================================

3.1 STANDARD BIDDING DOCUMENTS (SBDs)

ICTAD/CIDA STANDARD BIDDING DOCUMENTS:

Purpose:
- Standardized procurement documents for construction projects
- Ensure fairness, transparency, and efficiency
- Comply with government procurement regulations
- Align with international best practices

Categories of SBDs:

CIDA/SBD/1 - Procurement of Works:
- For major construction works
- Competitive bidding (open or limited)
- Detailed specifications and drawings
- Bill of Quantities (BOQ)
- Contract conditions (FIDIC-based)

CIDA/SBD/2 - Procurement of Works (Major):
- Very large and complex projects
- May include design-build or other delivery methods
- Enhanced pre-qualification requirements

CIDA/SBD/3 - Procurement of Works (Minor):
- Smaller works below specified threshold
- Simplified documentation
- Reduced pre-qualification requirements

CIDA/SBD/4 - Framework Contracts:
- For ongoing works over a period
- Unit rates for various work items
- Call-offs for specific tasks

Editions:
- Second Edition (January 2007) widely used
- Periodic updates and revisions
- Latest editions available from CIDA

International Equivalents:
- FIDIC Conditions of Contract for Construction (Red Book 1999)
- FIDIC MDB Harmonised Edition (Pink Book 2010)
- Used for donor-funded and large international projects

3.2 PRE-QUALIFICATION REQUIREMENTS

PURPOSE OF PRE-QUALIFICATION:

- Screen contractors before inviting bids
- Ensure only capable contractors participate
- Reduce risk of project failure
- Save time and cost (detailed bids only from qualified)

PRE-QUALIFICATION CRITERIA:

General Requirements:
- Valid CIDA registration in appropriate category and grade
- Financial capacity for project size
- Technical capability and experience
- Equipment and resources
- Legal and compliance (tax clearance, EPF/ETF, no blacklisting)

Specific to Project:
- Experience in similar projects (type, size, complexity)
- Experience in similar conditions (geographical, climatic)
- Availability of key personnel and equipment during project period
- Current workload and capacity to undertake additional project

Evaluation and Scoring:
- Points allocated to each criterion
- Minimum score required for qualification
- Short-listing of qualified bidders

3.3 BIDDING PROCESS

STANDARD PROCESS:

1. Advertisement:
   - Public advertisement of tender in newspapers and websites
   - Tender notice with project details, eligibility, submission deadline

2. Pre-Qualification (if applicable):
   - Submission of PQ documents
   - Evaluation and shortlisting

3. Tender Document Issue:
   - Purchase of tender documents by qualified bidders
   - Document fee

4. Site Visit and Pre-Bid Meeting:
   - Mandatory or optional site inspection
   - Pre-bid meeting to clarify queries
   - Minutes and addenda issued

5. Bid Submission:
   - Sealed bids submitted by deadline
   - In specified format and with required documents
   - Bid security (typically 1-2% of bid value)

6. Bid Opening:
   - Public opening of bids at specified time and place
   - Bids read out, prices recorded

7. Bid Evaluation:
   - Technical evaluation (compliance, methodology)
   - Financial evaluation (price competitiveness)
   - Evaluation report prepared

8. Post-Qualification (if required):
   - Verification of documents and claims of lowest bidder

9. Award:
   - Contract awarded to successful bidder
   - Award notification and contract signing
   - Performance security (typically 10% of contract value)

10. Contract Execution:
    - Mobilization and commencement
    - Site handover

BID VALIDITY:
- Typically 90-120 days from bid opening
- Extension may be requested

3.4 CONTRACT MANAGEMENT

CONTRACT TYPES:

Lump Sum (Fixed Price):
- Agreed total price for specified scope
- Contractor bears risk of cost overruns
- Suitable for well-defined scope

Unit Rate (Remeasurement):
- Payment based on quantities actually executed
- Unit rates agreed in BOQ
- Suitable where quantities uncertain

Cost Plus (Reimbursable):
- Actual cost plus agreed fee/percentage
- Used for urgent or undefined scope works
- Higher risk for employer

CONTRACT ADMINISTRATION:

Site Engineer/Resident Engineer:
- Represents employer on site
- Supervises construction
- Certifies progress payments
- Approves variations

Contractor's Site Management:
- Project manager
- Site engineer/supervisor
- Quality control
- Safety management
- Labor and material management

Progress Monitoring:
- Monthly progress reports
- Progress meetings
- Inspection and testing
- Quality assurance

Payments:
- Interim Payment Certificates (IPCs) - monthly or as per milestones
- Retention (typically 5-10% retained until completion)
- Final payment upon completion and defects liability period

Variations and Claims:
- Variation orders for changes in scope
- Claims for additional costs or time extensions
- Dispute resolution mechanisms

4.2 FIRE SAFETY REGULATIONS

ICTAD FIRE PROTECTION STANDARDS:

CIDA Act No. 33 of 2014 provides minimum requirements for fire protection design
and construction of new buildings and renovation of existing buildings throughout
Sri Lanka.

Chapters:

Chapter 2: Fire Escape
- Means of escape requirements
- Exit routes and stairways
- Travel distances
- Capacity of exits
- Emergency lighting

Chapter 3: Structural Fire Precautions
- Fire resistance of structural elements
- Compartmentation
- Fire stopping and sealing
- Protection of openings

Chapter 4: Fire Extinguishing Appliances, Detection, and Alarm Systems
- Fire extinguishers (types, locations, maintenance)
- Fire hose reels and hydrants
- Sprinkler systems
- Fire detection and alarm systems
- Emergency voice communication

Chapter 5: Special Uses, Occupancies, and Structures
- High-rise buildings
- Underground structures
- Hazardous occupancies
- Assembly buildings
- Healthcare facilities

Chapter 6: Access for Fire Fighting Vehicles
- Fire appliance access roads
- Width, gradient, turning radius
- Load capacity
- Clear height
- Hydrant locations

Compliance:
- Mandatory for building approval
- Fire Services Department clearance based on these regulations
- Enforced by local authorities and FSD

CHAPTER 6: ENFORCEMENT AND DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS
======================================================================

6.1 COMPLIANCE MONITORING

CIDA Inspections:
- Random inspections of registered contractors
- Verification of registration information
- Site visits to ongoing projects
- Review of compliance with standards

Reporting:
- Clients and consultants report contractor performance
- Complaints from public or stakeholders
- Self-reporting by contractors

6.2 NON-COMPLIANCE AND PENALTIES

GROUNDS FOR DISCIPLINARY ACTION:

- Providing false information in registration application
- Non-compliance with CIDA regulations
- Poor quality work and project failures
- Breach of contract terms
- Financial irregularities or bankruptcy
- Criminal convictions related to construction business
- Non-payment of statutory contributions (EPF, ETF, taxes)
- Safety violations resulting in accidents
- Environmental violations

DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS:

Warning:
- Official written warning for minor violations
- Opportunity to rectify

Suspension:
- Temporary suspension of registration (e.g., 6-12 months)
- Cannot bid for new contracts during suspension
- Must resolve issues before reinstatement

Downgrading:
- Reduction in grade due to reduced capacity or performance issues
- Limits value of projects contractor can bid for

Cancellation:
- Permanent cancellation of registration
- Serious violations or repeated offenses
- Blacklisting from government contracts

Appeals:
- Contractor may appeal decision
- Appeals process outlined in regulations
- Independent review

6.3 BLACKLISTING

PROCUREMENT BLACKLIST:

Maintained by:
- National Procurement Agency or CIDA
- Circulated to all government agencies

Reasons:
- Contract abandonment
- Fraud or corruption
- Serious quality failures
- Repeated violations

Effect:
- Cannot bid for or be awarded government contracts
- Period of blacklisting (e.g., 1-5 years or permanent)

Removal:
- After blacklist period expires
- If successfully appeals decision
- Resolution of issues leading to blacklisting
