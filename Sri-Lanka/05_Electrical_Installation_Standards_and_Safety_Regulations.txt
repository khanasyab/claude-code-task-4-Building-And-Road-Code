--------------------
Primary Authority: Ceylon Electricity Board (CEB)
Alternative Authority: Lanka Electricity Company (LECO) - within service area

CHAPTER 1: OVERVIEW OF ELECTRICAL REGULATIONS
======================================================================

1.1 LEGAL AND REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

ELECTRICITY LEGISLATION:

Historical Development:
- Electricity Act No. 19 of 1950: Original legislation
- Ceylon Electricity Board Act No. 17 of 1969: Established CEB
- Electricity Act No. 20 of 2009: Current primary legislation
- Regulations under Section 54 by Minister of Power and Energy

Regulatory Bodies:
- Public Utilities Commission of Sri Lanka (PUCSL): Economic and technical regulation
- Ceylon Electricity Board (CEB): Generation, transmission, distribution (bulk of country)
- Lanka Electricity Company (LECO): Distribution in Colombo and surrounding areas
- Sri Lanka Sustainable Energy Authority (SLSEA): Renewable energy promotion

1.2 APPLICABLE STANDARDS AND CODES

WIRING STANDARDS:

Primary Standards:
- IEE Wiring Regulations: Regulations of the Institution of Electrical Engineers,
  London, for Electrical Installations (historical reference)
- BS 7671:2008 (17th Edition): British Standard Requirements for Electrical
  Installations (widely referenced)
- BS 7671:2018+A2:2022 (18th Edition): Updated British Standard (latest)
- SLS 703:1985: Sri Lanka Standard Code of Practice for Electrical Installations

Application:
All electrical installations in Sri Lanka must comply with the IEE Wiring
Regulations and/or BS 7671. Internal wiring shall be completed in accordance
with standard rules and regulations (updated BS 7671).

ADDITIONAL STANDARDS:

Equipment and Materials:
- IEC Standards: International Electrotechnical Commission standards
- Sri Lanka Standards (SLS): For cables, switches, accessories
- British Standards (BS): Where SLS not available

Safety Standards:
- Electricity (Safety, Quality and Continuity) Regulations published by CEB
- Occupational safety standards
- Fire safety electrical requirements

1.3 ELECTRICITY SUPPLY IN SRI LANKA

SUPPLY CHARACTERISTICS:

Voltage and Frequency:
- Standard frequency: 50 Hz
- Domestic supply: 230 V (single-phase), 400 V (three-phase)
- Low voltage (LV): Up to 1 kV
- Medium voltage (MV): 11 kV, 33 kV
- High voltage (HV): 132 kV, 220 kV (transmission)

Supply Types:
- Single-phase: 230 V (for residential and small commercial)
- Three-phase: 400 V (for commercial, industrial, and large residential)
- High voltage: For large industrial consumers

Service Connection Capacity:
- Single-phase: Up to 10-12 kVA (approximately 42-50 A)
- Three-phase: 12 kVA to several MVA

SUPPLY QUALITY STANDARDS:

Voltage Tolerance:
- Nominal voltage: 230 V ± 6% (216-244 V acceptable range)
- Three-phase: 400 V ± 6%

Frequency Tolerance:
- 50 Hz ± 1% (49.5-50.5 Hz)

Power Quality:
- Voltage harmonics: Limited per IEC 61000 standards
- Voltage imbalance: < 2% for three-phase
- Interruptions: Minimize duration and frequency

Reliability:
- System Average Interruption Duration Index (SAIDI)
- System Average Interruption Frequency Index (SAIFI)
- Continuous monitoring and improvement

CHAPTER 2: ELECTRICAL SERVICE CONNECTION REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

2.1 APPLICATION FOR ELECTRICITY CONNECTION

CONNECTION PROCESS:

Step 1: Application Submission
Documents required:
- Application form (from CEB/LECO)
- Proof of ownership or tenancy agreement
- Copy of National Identity Card
- Detailed site plan showing location
- Building approval from local authority
- Wiring certificate from licensed electrical contractor (for new installations)

Step 2: Site Inspection
- CEB/LECO inspects premises
- Determines connection point and requirements
- Estimates cost of connection

Step 3: Payment and Agreement
- Connection charges payment
- Security deposit (refundable)
- Supply agreement execution

Step 4: Wiring Inspection
- Internal wiring by licensed contractor
- Inspection and approval by CEB/LECO
- Wiring test certificate submission

Step 5: Meter Installation and Energization
- Meter installation by CEB/LECO
- Service connection
- Supply energization

TIMELINES:
- Single-phase domestic (within existing infrastructure): 7-14 days
- Three-phase or requiring infrastructure extension: 30-90 days
- Delays possible if line extensions or transformer upgrades required

2.2 WIRING CERTIFICATE REQUIREMENTS

LICENSED ELECTRICAL CONTRACTORS:

Qualifications:
- Registered with CEB/LECO
- Trained and certified electricians
- Knowledge of wiring regulations
- Insurance and bonding

Responsibilities:
- Design and installation of internal wiring
- Compliance with standards and regulations
- Testing and certification
- Maintenance and repairs

WIRING CERTIFICATE:

Content:
- Details of installation (building address, type, capacity)
- Description of wiring system
- Cable sizes and types
- Protection devices (MCBs, RCCBs, etc.)
- Earthing system details
- Test results (insulation resistance, earth continuity)
- Declaration of compliance with regulations
- Contractor's signature and license number

Testing Requirements:
- Insulation resistance test: Minimum 1 MΩ (megohm) at 500 V DC
- Continuity test: Earthing and bonding conductors
- Polarity test: Correct phase and neutral connections
- RCD/RCCB operation test

Validity:
- Certificate valid for connection application
- Re-inspection if installation modified

2.3 INTERNAL WIRING STANDARDS

CONDUCTOR SIZING:

Current Carrying Capacity:
Based on:
- Load current
- Installation method (conduit, surface, buried)
- Ambient temperature
- Grouping of cables
- Diversity factor

Voltage Drop:
- Maximum voltage drop: 2.5% of nominal voltage (for lighting), 5% (total from supply point)
- Voltage drop = (mV/A/m) × Current (A) × Length (m) / 1000

Typical Cable Sizes for Single-Phase Domestic Installation:
- Main incoming: 10 mm² or 16 mm² (based on load)
- Lighting circuits: 1.5 mm²
- Power socket circuits: 2.5 mm²
- Air conditioner: 4 mm² to 10 mm² (based on capacity)
- Water heater: 4 mm² to 6 mm²
- Cooker/oven: 6 mm² to 10 mm²

CABLE TYPES:

Common Cables in Sri Lanka:

PVC Insulated Cables:
- Single-core or multicore
- 600/1000 V grade
- Suitable for general wiring
- Most economical

PVC/PVC Cables:
- PVC insulation and PVC sheath
- For conduit or surface installation
- Common sizes: 1.5, 2.5, 4, 6, 10, 16, 25, 35, 50 mm²

Aluminum Service Main Wire:
- For overhead service connection
- From meter to CEB distribution line
- Insulated aluminum conductor
- Sizes: 16, 25, 35, 50 mm²

Armored Cables:
- Steel wire armored (SWA) for mechanical protection
- Underground or outdoor installation
- Single-core or multicore

Flexible Cables:
- For portable appliances and equipment
- Three-core flex (phase, neutral, earth)

Color Coding (per BS 7671):
- Phase (Live): Brown (single-phase), Brown/Black/Grey (three-phase)
- Neutral: Blue
- Earth: Green/Yellow stripe

Old Color Coding (still encountered in old installations):
- Phase: Red (or Red/Yellow/Blue for three-phase)
- Neutral: Black
- Earth: Green or bare copper

INSTALLATION METHODS:

Conduit System:
- PVC conduits (most common)
- Concealed in walls or floor
- Surface mounted where required
- Conduit sizes: 16, 20, 25, 32, 40, 50 mm
- Maximum cable fill: 40-45% of conduit area

Surface Wiring:
- Cables in surface-mounted conduits or trunking
- Supported by cleats or clips at regular intervals
- Mainly for industrial or temporary installations

Concealed Wiring:
- Conduits embedded in walls during construction
- Cables drawn through after plastering
- Preferred method for residential and commercial

Underground Cables:
- Armored cables or cables in ducts
- Minimum depth: 450-600 mm
- Warning tape above cables
- Sand bedding and protection

Overhead Wiring:
- Service mains from pole to building
- Minimum height: 3.5 m (over pedestrian areas), 5.5 m (over vehicle areas)
- Adequate support and tensioning

CHAPTER 3: EARTHING AND PROTECTION SYSTEMS
======================================================================

3.1 EARTHING REQUIREMENTS

PURPOSE OF EARTHING:

Safety Functions:
- Protect against electric shock
- Provide path for fault currents
- Enable operation of protective devices
- Protect against lightning

Types of Earth Electrodes:

Plate Electrode:
- Copper or GI plate (600 mm × 600 mm × 3 mm minimum)
- Buried vertically at minimum 2.5 m depth
- Common in Sri Lankan residential installations

Pipe/Rod Electrode:
- GI pipe (38-50 mm diameter) or copper rod
- Length: 2.0-2.5 m minimum
- Driven vertically into ground
- Multiple rods bonded together if required

Strip Electrode:
- Copper or GI strip buried around building
- For large installations or poor soil resistivity areas

Earth Resistance:
- Target earth resistance: < 5 Ω (ohms) for domestic installations
- < 1 Ω for sensitive equipment and data centers
- Measured using earth tester
- Improved by parallel electrodes, chemical treatment of soil, longer electrodes

EARTHING SYSTEM CONFIGURATION:

Main Earth Terminal (MET):
- Central earthing point in installation
- Located near main switchboard
- All protective conductors connected to MET
- MET connected to earth electrode

Protective Conductors:
- Connect all exposed metallic parts to MET
- Green/Yellow insulated cable
- Size based on associated phase conductor
- Minimum size: 1.5 mm² for fixed wiring, 2.5 mm² (often used)

Bonding:
- Main bonding: Metallic services (water pipes, gas pipes) bonded to MET
- Supplementary bonding: In bathrooms and wet areas
- Bond conductor sizes: Minimum 6 mm² (main bonding), 2.5-4 mm² (supplementary)

Earthing of Equipment:
- All Class I appliances and equipment earthed
- Socket outlets with earth pin
- Fixed equipment earthing connection

3.2 PROTECTION AGAINST ELECTRIC SHOCK

BASIC PROTECTION (Direct Contact):

Insulation:
- Live parts fully insulated
- Insulation integrity maintained
- Regular inspection and testing

Barriers and Enclosures:
- Electrical equipment in proper enclosures
- IP rating appropriate for environment
- Covers secured (tools required to open)

Placing Out of Reach:
- High voltage equipment at safe height
- Restricted access to electrical rooms

FAULT PROTECTION (Indirect Contact):

Automatic Disconnection of Supply:
- Most common method in Sri Lanka
- Circuit breaker or fuse operates on fault
- Disconnection time: < 0.4 seconds (final circuits), < 5 seconds (distribution)

Earth Fault Loop Impedance:
- Low impedance path from fault point through earth to supply
- Ensures sufficient fault current to operate protective device
- Calculated and verified: Zs × Ia ≥ Uo (where Zs = loop impedance, Ia = operating current,
  Uo = nominal voltage to earth)

Residual Current Devices (RCDs):

RCCB (Residual Current Circuit Breaker):
- Detects imbalance between phase and neutral currents
- Trips when residual current exceeds rated sensitivity
- Common ratings: 30 mA (socket outlets, wet areas), 100 mA, 300 mA (larger circuits)
- Tripping time: < 30 ms at 5 × rated current

RCBO (Residual Current Breaker with Overcurrent Protection):
- Combines RCD and MCB functions
- Protects against earth faults, overload, and short circuits
- Saves space in distribution board

Application:
- 30 mA RCD mandatory for socket outlets likely to supply portable equipment outdoors
- RCD protection for bathroom circuits
- RCD protection for all socket outlets (best practice)

3.3 OVERCURRENT AND SHORT CIRCUIT PROTECTION

MINIATURE CIRCUIT BREAKERS (MCBs):

Characteristics:
- Thermal element: Overload protection (slow response)
- Magnetic element: Short circuit protection (fast response)
- Breaking capacity: 6 kA, 10 kA (domestic/commercial), higher for industrial

MCB Types (Trip Curves):
- Type B: 3-5 × rated current (general purpose, residential)
- Type C: 5-10 × rated current (motors, transformers)
- Type D: 10-20 × rated current (heavy inductive loads)

Common Ratings:
- Lighting circuits: 6 A, 10 A
- Socket circuits: 16 A, 20 A
- Air conditioners: 16 A, 20 A, 32 A (based on capacity)
- Main incomer: 32 A, 40 A, 63 A, 80 A (single-phase domestic)

FUSES (Less Common in New Installations):

Types:
- Rewirable fuses (very old installations, being replaced)
- Cartridge fuses (HRC - High Rupturing Capacity)

Application:
- Main service fuse (sealed by CEB/LECO)
- Some industrial applications

DISTRIBUTION BOARDS:

Main Distribution Board (MDB):
- Receives supply from meter
- Main switch or MCCB
- RCDs and outgoing circuit breakers
- Suitable for current rating of installation

Sub-Distribution Boards (SDBs):
- Distribute power to specific areas/floors
- Fed from main DB through sub-main cables
- Local isolation and protection

Enclosure:
- Metal or insulated material
- IP rating: IP30 minimum (indoor), IP54+ (outdoor/wet areas)
- Proper labeling of circuits
- Circuit schedule displayed

CHAPTER 4: SPECIAL INSTALLATIONS AND LOCATIONS
======================================================================

4.1 BATHROOMS AND WET AREAS

ZONING SYSTEM:

Zone 0: Interior of bathtub or shower basin
- No electrical equipment except: Low voltage (max 12 V) suitable equipment
- IPX7 rating minimum

Zone 1: Above bathtub/shower to 2.25 m height
- Water heaters (if suitable)
- Instantaneous water heaters rated IPX4 minimum
- No switches or socket outlets

Zone 2: Area extending 0.6 m beyond Zone 1, up to 2.25 m height
- Luminaires and extractors (IPX4 minimum)
- Shaver supply units (isolating transformer)
- No socket outlets (except shaver socket)

Outside Zones (beyond Zone 2):
- Normal installation rules apply
- RCD protection mandatory

SUPPLEMENTARY BONDING:

Requirements:
- Bond all metallic pipes, taps, bath/shower trays to earth
- Bond to equipotential bonding conductor
- Conductor size: Minimum 4 mm²
- May be omitted if all circuits RCD protected (30 mA) and other conditions met

4.2 SWIMMING POOLS AND FOUNTAINS

ZONING FOR POOLS:

Zone 0: Inside pool basin
- No equipment except SELV (< 12 V AC) pool equipment

Zone 1: Above and around pool to 2 m from edge, up to 2.5 m height
- Only pool equipment rated for Zone 1
- SELV or equipment rated IPX4/IPX5 minimum

Zone 2: 1.5 m beyond Zone 1 boundary
- Socket outlets via RCD (30 mA) and isolating transformer
- Equipment rated IPX4 minimum

All metallic parts bonded together and earthed.

4.3 EXTERNAL LIGHTING AND OUTDOOR INSTALLATIONS

Requirements:
- Cables suitable for outdoor use (UV resistant, armored)
- Equipment with appropriate IP rating (IP44 minimum, IP65 for exposed)
- RCD protection mandatory
- Adequate earthing
- Lamp posts with internal earth
- Buried cables at minimum 450 mm depth with marking tape

4.4 SOLAR PV INSTALLATIONS

REGULATIONS:

Net Metering and Net Accounting:
- Regulations by PUCSL for grid-connected PV systems
- System capacity limits for different consumer categories
- Approval process through CEB/LECO

System Components:
- PV panels (modules)
- Inverter (grid-tie or hybrid)
- Mounting structure
- DC and AC cabling
- Protection devices
- Net meter or bidirectional meter

Safety Requirements:
- DC side isolation
- AC side protection (MCB, surge protection)
- Anti-islanding protection
- Earthing of panels and structure
- Fire safety compliance

Standards:
- IEC 61215: PV module qualification
- IEC 61730: PV module safety
- IEC 62116: Inverter anti-islanding
- SLS standards (where available)

Installation:
- By licensed electrical contractor with PV experience
- Approval from CEB/LECO before connection
- Inspection before energization
- Commissioning tests

CHAPTER 5: ELECTRICAL SAFETY REGULATIONS
======================================================================

5.1 ELECTRICAL SAFETY IN BUILDINGS

GENERAL SAFETY REQUIREMENTS:

Accessibility:
- Electrical equipment accessible for operation and maintenance
- Adequate working space around switchboards (minimum 1 m clearance)
- Lighting in electrical rooms
- Warning signs and labels

Isolation:
- Means of isolation for every circuit
- Lockable main switch
- Local isolation for fixed equipment
- Visible break (or indicator) required

Emergency Switching:
- Emergency stop for machinery
- Easily accessible and identifiable
- Red color, mushroom type

Fire Safety:
- Fire-resistant cables where required (escape routes, high-rise buildings)
- Proper cable sizing to prevent overheating
- Cables not exceeding current carrying capacity
- Use of fire barriers and seals in cable penetrations

Electrical Rooms and Substations:
- Dedicated rooms for electrical equipment (buildings > certain size)
- Adequate ventilation
- Fire extinguishers (CO₂ or dry powder, not water)
- Restricted access (authorized personnel only)
- Safety signage

5.2 SAFETY DURING ELECTRICAL WORK

PERMIT TO WORK SYSTEM:

For High Voltage and Critical Installations:
- Formal written permission before work
- Isolation verified
- Earthing applied where necessary
- Clear communication between control and site

LIVE WORKING:
- Avoided wherever possible
- Only by trained and authorized personnel
- Proper tools and equipment
- Insulated tools and gloves
- Second person present

LOCKOUT/TAGOUT (LOTO):
- Isolate supply before work
- Lock isolation device in open position
- Tag indicating work in progress
- Key held by person doing work

PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE):

For Electrical Work:
- Insulated gloves (rated for voltage)
- Safety shoes (electrical hazard rated)
- Safety glasses or face shield
- Insulated tools
- Flame-resistant clothing (for high-energy work)

5.3 ELECTRICAL INSPECTIONS AND TESTING

PERIODIC INSPECTION AND TESTING:

Frequency:
- New installations: Before energization
- Existing installations: Every 5 years (domestic), 3 years (commercial),
  annually (industrial)
- After alterations or additions
- Following fault or incident

Inspection:
- Visual examination of installation
- Compliance with regulations
- Condition of equipment and cables
- Correct labeling and identification
- Earthing and bonding integrity

Testing:
- Continuity of protective conductors
- Insulation resistance (minimum 1 MΩ)
- Earth fault loop impedance
- RCD operation and tripping time
- Polarity verification
- Voltage and phase sequence (three-phase)

Test Certificate:
- Results recorded on standard form
- Deviations and remedial work noted
- Certificate retained for record

THERMAL IMAGING:
- Detect hot spots in connections
- Preventive maintenance tool
- Identify loose connections, overloading

CHAPTER 6: ENERGY EFFICIENCY AND CONSERVATION
======================================================================

6.1 ENERGY EFFICIENT ELECTRICAL DESIGN

LIGHTING:

Efficient Lamp Types:
- LED lamps: Highest efficiency (80-120 lumens/watt), long life
- CFL lamps: Moderate efficiency (50-70 lumens/watt)
- T5 fluorescent tubes: Good for commercial
- Avoid incandescent lamps (low efficiency, 10-15 lumens/watt)

Lighting Controls:
- Motion sensors for corridors, stairs, toilets
- Daylight sensors for areas with natural light
- Timers for outdoor and security lighting
- Dimmers where appropriate
- Zone control for large spaces

Lighting Design:
- Task lighting rather than general high levels
- Utilize natural daylight
- Light-colored walls and ceilings (better reflectance)
- Lux levels appropriate for task

AIR CONDITIONING:

Efficient Systems:
- Inverter technology air conditioners (energy efficiency rating A or higher)
- Variable Refrigerant Flow (VRF) systems for commercial buildings
- Proper sizing (avoid oversizing)
- Regular maintenance (cleaning filters, coils)

Controls:
- Programmable thermostats
- Temperature setpoint: 24-25°C (avoid excessive cooling)
- Time controls (switch off when unoccupied)
- Zone control

Design Considerations:
- Building envelope insulation
- Solar shading and orientation
- Natural ventilation where feasible
- Minimize heat gains (efficient lighting, insulated roofs, low-E glass)

MOTORS AND DRIVES:

Efficiency:
- High-efficiency motors (IE2, IE3 class)
- Variable Speed Drives (VSDs) for varying loads
- Proper motor sizing
- Power factor correction

APPLIANCES AND EQUIPMENT:

Selection:
- Energy-efficient models (check energy labels)
- Right size for need
- Quality brands with proven performance

Usage Practices:
- Switch off when not in use
- Avoid standby power consumption
- Regular maintenance

6.2 POWER FACTOR CORRECTION

POWER FACTOR:

Definition:
- Power factor (PF) = Real power / Apparent power
- Low PF (< 0.85) indicates reactive power consumption
- Causes: Inductive loads (motors, transformers, discharge lamps)

Issues with Low Power Factor:
- Higher current for same real power
- Increased losses in cables and transformers
- Penalties from CEB/LECO (for industrial/commercial)
- Reduced system capacity

Improvement:
- Capacitor banks (parallel compensation)
- Automatic power factor correction (APFC) panels
- Individual correction at equipment (motor capacitors)
- Target PF: > 0.9 (ideally 0.95-0.98)

Benefits:
- Reduced electricity cost (avoided penalties, reduced demand charges)
- Improved voltage regulation
- Increased capacity in existing installation

6.3 RENEWABLE ENERGY INTEGRATION

SOLAR PHOTOVOLTAIC (PV):

Rooftop Solar:
- Most common renewable energy in Sri Lanka
- Grid-connected systems with net metering/net accounting
- System sizes: 1-10 kW (residential), 10-1000+ kW (commercial/industrial)

Battery Storage:
- Hybrid systems with battery backup
- Energy storage for time-shifting and backup
- Lithium-ion or advanced lead-acid batteries

WIND ENERGY:
- Limited to suitable locations with good wind resource
- Small wind turbines for individual buildings (rare)
- Larger utility-scale wind farms

MICRO-HYDRO:
- Small hydroelectric systems in hilly areas
- Run-of-river or small storage
- Local off-grid or grid-connected

BIOGAS:
- Organic waste to biogas
- Gas engines for electricity generation
- Combined heat and power (CHP)
