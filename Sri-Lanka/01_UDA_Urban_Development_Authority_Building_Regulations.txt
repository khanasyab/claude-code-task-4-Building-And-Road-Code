CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION AND LEGAL FRAMEWORK
======================================================================

1.1 OVERVIEW OF UDA

The Urban Development Authority (UDA) is a corporate entity established
under the Urban Development Authority Law, No. 41 of 1978. The UDA
functions with the mandate to promote integrated planning and
implementation of economic, social, and physical development of
designated urban development areas in Sri Lanka.

The Authority is responsible for:
- Integrated urban planning and development
- Building plan approval and permitting
- Enforcement of planning and building regulations
- Urban development project implementation
- Coordination with local authorities
- Setting development standards and guidelines

1.2 LEGAL MANDATE

The Urban Development Authority Law empowers the UDA to:

a) Prepare development plans for declared urban development areas
b) Regulate and control development activities
c) Approve building plans and issue development permits
d) Enforce compliance with planning regulations
e) Coordinate with local authorities and other agencies
f) Promote orderly and sustainable urban development

1.3 DECLARED URBAN DEVELOPMENT AREAS

The UDA has jurisdiction over several declared urban development areas
across Sri Lanka, including:

- Colombo Urban Development Area
- Kandy Urban Development Area
- Galle Urban Development Area
- Negombo Urban Development Area
- Anuradhapura Urban Development Area
- Kurunegala Urban Development Area
- Other declared areas through Government Gazette

CHAPTER 2: PLANNING AND BUILDING REGULATIONS
======================================================================

2.1 GAZETTE NOTIFICATION

The primary UDA Planning and Building Regulations were stipulated by
Gazette of the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka Extraordinary
No. 392/9 dated 10th March 1986. Subsequent amendments and updates have
been issued through various gazette notifications.

Latest updates include:
- Gazette Notification dated 2021 (Updated Regulations)
- Public comment period extended to May 30th, 2025 for proposed amendments

2.2 APPLICABILITY

These regulations apply to:
- All new building construction
- Alterations and additions to existing buildings
- Change of use of buildings
- Subdivision and amalgamation of land
- Development activities in declared urban areas
- Temporary structures and installations

2.3 EXEMPTIONS

Certain minor works may be exempted from full approval requirements:
- Minor repairs and maintenance
- Internal alterations not affecting structure
- Boundary walls below specified height
- Agricultural buildings in designated zones
- Specific exemptions granted by the Authority

CHAPTER 3: DEVELOPMENT PERMIT REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

3.1 REQUIREMENT FOR DEVELOPMENT PERMIT

Section 4 of the UDA Law states:
"No development activity shall be carried out in a declared urban
development area without the written approval of the Urban Development
Authority."

Development activities requiring a permit include:
- Construction of new buildings
- Structural alterations or extensions
- Change of use of land or buildings
- Subdivision or amalgamation of land
- Earth moving and site preparation
- Installation of outdoor advertising
- Establishment of industries or commercial activities

3.2 TYPES OF PERMITS

The UDA issues various types of permits:

PLANNING PERMIT (Land Use Clearance):
- Ensures compliance with zoning regulations
- Verifies land use compatibility
- Confirms plot coverage and setback requirements
- Validates access and parking provisions

DEVELOPMENT PERMIT (Building Plan Approval):
- Authorizes construction activities
- Confirms structural safety
- Verifies compliance with building regulations
- Ensures adherence to approved plans

SUBDIVISION/AMALGAMATION APPROVAL:
- Permits division of land parcels
- Authorizes combination of multiple lots
- Ensures compliance with minimum lot sizes
- Confirms adequate infrastructure provision

3.3 VALIDITY OF PERMITS

Development permits are subject to validity periods:
- Standard validity: One (1) year from date of issue
- Extension possible upon application before expiry
- Lapsed permits require fresh application
- Changes to approved plans require revised approval

CHAPTER 4: APPLICATION AND APPROVAL PROCESS
======================================================================

4.1 PRE-APPLICATION REQUIREMENTS

Before submitting an application, applicants must:

a) Obtain clear title to the property
b) Verify property boundaries through survey
c) Check zoning and land use requirements
d) Consult with relevant utility providers
e) Engage registered professionals (architects/engineers)
f) Prepare required documents and drawings

4.2 REQUIRED DOCUMENTS

Standard application requires submission of:

OWNERSHIP DOCUMENTS:
- Original deeds or certified copies
- Clear title for minimum 35 years
- Survey plan (approved, less than 10 years old)
- Tax receipts and clearances

TECHNICAL DRAWINGS:
- Site plan (minimum 1:500 scale)
- Floor plans (all levels)
- Elevations (all facades)
- Sections (minimum two)
- Structural drawings (foundation, framing, details)
- MEP drawings (mechanical, electrical, plumbing)
- Landscape plan
- Drainage and sanitation plan

PROFESSIONAL CERTIFICATIONS:
- Architect's certificate and stamp
- Structural engineer's certificate
- Quantity surveyor's estimate
- Soil investigation report (for major buildings)

REGULATORY CLEARANCES:
- Fire safety recommendations (buildings G+3 or above)
- Environmental clearance (if required)
- Road Development Authority clearance (road frontage)
- Utility agency clearances
- Solid waste management approval (buildings >1500 sqm)

4.3 NUMBER OF COPIES

Submit nine (09) complete sets of:
- Building application forms
- All drawings and plans
- Supporting documents
- Professional certificates

4.4 REVIEW AND APPROVAL TIMELINE

COLOMBO MUNICIPAL COUNCIL AREA:
- Technical review: 5 working days
- Total processing time: 7 working days

NON-CMC AREAS:
- Clearance processing: 10 working days
- Total processing time: 12 working days

FACTORS AFFECTING TIMELINE:
- Completeness of application
- Complexity of project
- Need for additional clearances
- Committee meeting schedules

4.5 APPROVAL OUTCOME

Upon approval, applicants receive:
- Development Permit in owner's name
- Three (03) copies of approved plans
- Stamped and signed drawings
- Conditions of approval (if any)
- Validity period notification

CHAPTER 5: ZONING AND LAND USE REGULATIONS
======================================================================

5.1 ZONING CLASSIFICATIONS

Urban development areas are divided into zones:

RESIDENTIAL ZONES:
- Low Density Residential (R1)
- Medium Density Residential (R2)
- High Density Residential (R3)
- Mixed Residential

COMMERCIAL ZONES:
- Neighborhood Commercial (C1)
- General Commercial (C2)
- Central Business District (CBD)
- Mixed Commercial

INDUSTRIAL ZONES:
- Light Industrial (I1)
- General Industrial (I2)
- Heavy Industrial (I3)
- Special Economic Zones

INSTITUTIONAL ZONES:
- Educational
- Healthcare
- Religious
- Government/Administrative

SPECIAL ZONES:
- Recreation and Open Space
- Conservation Areas
- Heritage Zones
- Coastal Zone
- Agricultural Reserve

5.2 PERMITTED USES

Each zone specifies:
- Permitted uses (as of right)
- Conditional uses (requiring special approval)
- Prohibited uses
- Accessory uses

5.3 DEVELOPMENT STANDARDS BY ZONE

Standards vary by zone and include:
- Minimum lot size
- Maximum building height
- Floor Area Ratio (FAR)
- Plot coverage ratio
- Setback requirements
- Parking requirements
- Open space requirements

======================================================================
CHAPTER 6: BUILDING HEIGHT AND COVERAGE REGULATIONS
======================================================================

6.1 PLOT COVERAGE

Plot coverage is the percentage of land area covered by buildings:

RESIDENTIAL ZONES:
- Low Density: 35-40% maximum
- Medium Density: 45-50% maximum
- High Density: 50-60% maximum

COMMERCIAL ZONES:
- Neighborhood: 50-60% maximum
- General Commercial: 60-70% maximum
- CBD: 70-80% maximum

INDUSTRIAL ZONES:
- Light Industrial: 50-60% maximum
- General Industrial: 60-70% maximum

6.2 FLOOR AREA RATIO (FAR)

FAR is the ratio of total floor area to plot area:

RESIDENTIAL ZONES:
- Low Density: 0.75 - 1.5
- Medium Density: 1.5 - 2.5
- High Density: 2.5 - 4.0

COMMERCIAL ZONES:
- Neighborhood: 1.5 - 2.5
- General Commercial: 2.5 - 4.0
- CBD: 4.0 - 8.0

6.3 BUILDING HEIGHT RESTRICTIONS

Height limits vary by location and zone:

GENERAL LIMITS:
- Low-rise: Up to 4 stories (G+3)
- Medium-rise: 5 to 10 stories
- High-rise: Above 10 stories

SPECIFIC RESTRICTIONS:
- Proximity to airports (obstacle limitation surfaces)
- Heritage areas (compatible with surrounding)
- Coastal areas (specific limits apply)
- View corridors (protected sight lines)

BONUS HEIGHT:
Additional height may be permitted for:
- Provision of public amenities
- Green building certification
- Exceptional architectural merit
- Urban regeneration projects

======================================================================
CHAPTER 7: SETBACK REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

7.1 FRONT SETBACK

Distance from building to front property line:

RESIDENTIAL:
- Main roads: 6-10 meters
- Secondary roads: 4-6 meters
- Access lanes: 3-4 meters

COMMERCIAL:
- Main roads: 4-8 meters (may be reduced in CBD)
- Secondary roads: 3-6 meters

7.2 SIDE SETBACKS

Distance from building to side property lines:

RESIDENTIAL:
- Single side: 1.5-3 meters
- Both sides: Total 3-6 meters
- Corner plots: Larger setback on secondary frontage

COMMERCIAL/INDUSTRIAL:
- Single side: 2-4 meters
- Both sides: Total 4-8 meters

7.3 REAR SETBACK

Distance from building to rear property line:

RESIDENTIAL: 3-6 meters (varies by building height)
COMMERCIAL: 3-6 meters
INDUSTRIAL: 4-8 meters

7.4 SETBACK MODIFICATIONS

Setbacks may be modified for:
- Covered parking areas (specific provisions)
- Projecting architectural features (limited projection)
- Underground basements (within property)
- Boundary walls and fences (separate regulations)

======================================================================
CHAPTER 8: PARKING REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

8.1 RESIDENTIAL PARKING

SINGLE FAMILY DWELLINGS:
- Minimum 1 space per dwelling unit
- Preferred 2 spaces per unit

APARTMENTS:
- Studio/1 BHK: 1 space per unit
- 2-3 BHK: 1.5 spaces per unit
- 4+ BHK: 2 spaces per unit
- Visitor parking: 0.25 spaces per unit

8.2 COMMERCIAL PARKING

OFFICE BUILDINGS:
- 1 space per 40-60 sqm of floor area

RETAIL:
- Small shops: 1 space per 30-50 sqm
- Supermarkets: 1 space per 25-40 sqm

RESTAURANTS:
- 1 space per 10-15 sqm dining area
- Fast food: 1 space per 8-12 sqm

HOTELS:
- 0.75-1 space per guest room
- Additional spaces for banquet/conference facilities

8.3 INSTITUTIONAL PARKING

EDUCATIONAL:
- Schools: 1 space per classroom + staff
- Colleges/Universities: Varies by enrollment

HOSPITALS:
- 1 space per 3-5 beds
- Additional for staff and visitors

PLACES OF WORSHIP:
- 1 space per 10-15 seats

8.4 PARKING DESIGN STANDARDS

PARKING SPACE DIMENSIONS:
- Standard space: 2.5m x 5.0m
- Parallel parking: 2.4m x 6.0m
- Disabled parking: 3.6m x 5.0m

CIRCULATION:
- One-way aisle: 3.5-4.0m width
- Two-way aisle: 6.0-7.0m width

ACCESSIBLE PARKING:
- Minimum 2% of total spaces
- Located near building entrance
- Proper signage and marking

======================================================================
CHAPTER 9: INFRASTRUCTURE AND SERVICES
======================================================================

9.1 ROAD ACCESS

All plots must have:
- Legal access to public road
- Minimum access width: 3.0 meters
- Paved access for commercial/industrial
- Emergency vehicle access for large developments

9.2 WATER SUPPLY

REQUIREMENTS:
- Connection to public water supply (where available)
- Private wells (approved by health authorities)
- Adequate storage capacity (minimum 2 days)
- Fire fighting water reserve (for large buildings)

STANDARDS:
- Conform to National Water Supply and Drainage Board standards
- Meet SLS 614:2013 (Potable Water Standard)

9.3 DRAINAGE AND SEWERAGE

SURFACE WATER DRAINAGE:
- Separate storm water drainage system
- Connection to public drains (where available)
- Proper grading and site drainage
- Retention/detention for large sites

SEWERAGE:
- Connection to public sewer (mandatory where available)
- Septic tank systems (approved design)
- Treatment plants (for large developments)
- Comply with environmental standards

9.4 SOLID WASTE MANAGEMENT

For buildings >1500 sqm or G+3:
- Dedicated waste storage area
- Separate collection for recyclables
- Proper ventilation and drainage
- Collection schedule coordination
- Approval required from CMC/LA

9.5 ELECTRICITY

- Connection to national grid
- Adequate service capacity
- Underground cables (in specified areas)
- Compliance with Ceylon Electricity Board requirements
- Energy efficiency measures (as required)

======================================================================
CHAPTER 10: SPECIAL PROVISIONS
======================================================================

10.1 HIGH-RISE BUILDINGS

Buildings above specified height require:

ADDITIONAL APPROVALS:
- Airport authority clearance
- Civil Aviation Authority approval
- Detailed wind and seismic analysis
- Fire safety engineering report

ENHANCED REQUIREMENTS:
- Multiple fire escapes
- Pressurized stairwells
- Fire fighting systems
- Emergency power supply
- Passenger and fire service lifts

10.2 HERITAGE AND CONSERVATION AREAS

In declared heritage zones:
- Preservation of architectural character
- Restrictions on demolition
- Design compatibility requirements
- Special approval process
- Archaeological clearances (if required)

10.3 COASTAL ZONE

Buildings in coastal areas subject to:
- Coast Conservation Department approval
- Coastal Zone Management Plan compliance
- Setback from High Water Line
- Environmental impact assessment
- Special structural requirements

10.4 SUBDIVISIONS

Land subdivision requires:
- Minimum lot sizes per zone
- Adequate access for all lots
- Infrastructure provision
- Public space dedication (for large subdivisions)
- Survey and legal documentation

======================================================================
CHAPTER 11: INSPECTION AND ENFORCEMENT
======================================================================

11.1 CONSTRUCTION INSPECTIONS

Mandatory inspections during construction:

FOUNDATION STAGE:
- Excavation and soil condition
- Foundation layout and depth
- Reinforcement placement
- Approval before concreting

STRUCTURAL FRAME:
- Column and beam placement
- Reinforcement details
- Concrete strength verification
- Approval before subsequent work

FINAL INSPECTION:
- Completion as per approved plans
- Building services installation
- Fire safety measures
- Occupancy readiness

11.2 CERTIFICATE OF CONFORMITY

Upon satisfactory completion:
- Final inspection by UDA/LA
- Certificate of Conformity issued
- Permit for occupancy
- Required for utility connections

11.3 VIOLATIONS AND PENALTIES

TYPES OF VIOLATIONS:
- Construction without permit
- Deviation from approved plans
- Unauthorized change of use
- Violation of setbacks/coverage

ENFORCEMENT ACTIONS:
- Stop work order
- Removal/demolition order
- Fines and penalties (as per UDA Law)
- Legal prosecution
- Refusal of Certificate of Conformity

11.4 RECTIFICATION

For violations:
- Notice to rectify issued
- Specified timeline for compliance
- Revised plans if structural changes needed
- Penalty fees may apply
- Regular permits required for legalization

CHAPTER 13: PROFESSIONAL REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

13.1 REGISTERED ARCHITECTS

Design architects must be:
- Bachelor of Architecture from recognized university
- Registered with UDA as Design Architect
- Member of Institute of Architects Sri Lanka (IASL)
- Minimum experience as specified
- Current practicing certificate

13.2 STRUCTURAL ENGINEERS

Structural engineers must be:
- Degree in Civil Engineering
- Chartered Engineer (C.Eng) status
- Member of Institution of Engineers Sri Lanka (IESL)
- Registered with UDA as Structural Engineer
- Relevant design experience

13.3 QUANTITY SURVEYORS

QS professionals should be:
- Qualified Quantity Surveyor
- Member of Institute of Quantity Surveyors Sri Lanka (IQSSL)
- Registered with UDA (for cost estimates)

13.4 PROFESSIONAL LIABILITY

Professionals are responsible for:
- Accuracy of design and documentation
- Compliance with regulations
- Professional indemnity insurance
- Signing and sealing documents
- Ethical practice standards

planned and orderly development of urban areas in Sri Lanka. Compliance

- Environmental conservation
- Quality of urban life

All developers, property owners, and professionals must familiarize
themselves with these regulations and ensure full compliance in all
