CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION TO CIDA
======================================================================

1.1 HISTORICAL BACKGROUND

ICTAD (Institute for Construction Training and Development):
- Established in 1989
- Developed standard construction specifications
- Implemented contractor registration scheme
- Provided training and development

TRANSITION TO CIDA:
- Construction Industry Development Authority Act No. 33 of 2014
- CIDA assumed responsibilities of ICTAD
- Continued and enhanced regulatory functions
- Expanded scope and authority

1.2 CIDA MANDATE AND FUNCTIONS

The Construction Industry Development Authority is responsible for:

a) INDUSTRY REGULATION:
   - Registration and grading of contractors
   - Registration of consultants
   - Monitoring industry performance
   - Enforcing standards and regulations

b) STANDARDS DEVELOPMENT:
   - Development of construction specifications
   - Maintenance of SCA series documents
   - Technical guideline publication
   - Best practice promotion

c) CAPACITY BUILDING:
   - Training programs for industry professionals
   - Skill development initiatives
   - Technology transfer
   - Research and development

d) POLICY FORMULATION:
   - Industry policy recommendations
   - Regulatory framework development
   - Stakeholder coordination
   - International collaboration

e) FIRE SAFETY REGULATION:
   - Fire protection standards
   - Building fire safety requirements
   - Approval and inspection
   - Enforcement

CHAPTER 2: SCA SERIES OVERVIEW
======================================================================

2.1 WHAT IS SCA?

SCA stands for "Standard Construction Specifications and Conditions"
These are comprehensive technical specifications for various types of
construction works in Sri Lanka, covering materials, workmanship,
testing, and contract conditions.

2.2 PURPOSE OF SCA SPECIFICATIONS

- Standardize construction practices
- Define quality requirements
- Establish testing procedures
- Provide basis for contracts
- Ensure consistency across projects
- Facilitate cost estimation
- Support dispute resolution

2.3 APPLICABILITY

SCA specifications are used for:
- Government construction projects (mandatory)
- Public sector developments
- Private sector projects (recommended)
- Donor-funded projects
- Infrastructure developments

2.4 LEGAL STATUS

For government projects:
- Mandatory compliance required
- Contractual obligations
- Payment basis
- Dispute reference

For private projects:
- Voluntary adoption (highly recommended)
- Industry best practice
- Professional standard

CHAPTER 8: CONTRACTOR REGISTRATION SYSTEM
======================================================================

8.1 REGISTRATION REQUIREMENT

All contractors undertaking construction projects require:
- Registration with CIDA
- Appropriate grading based on capacity
- Current registration certificate
- Compliance with regulations

8.2 CONTRACTOR GRADES

Contractors classified into grades based on:
- Financial capacity
- Technical competency
- Past performance
- Project value limits

GRADING SYSTEM:
- Grade C1: Major projects (unlimited value)
- Grade C2: Large projects
- Grade C3: Medium projects
- Grade C4: Small projects
- Grade C5: Minor projects

8.3 FIELDS OF REGISTRATION

Contractors registered in specific fields:
- Building Construction (B)
- Roads and Bridges (R)
- Water Supply and Drainage (W)
- Electrical Works (E)
- Mechanical Works (M)
- Other specialized fields

8.4 REGISTRATION PROCESS

APPLICATION:
- Submit application form
- Provide required documents
- Pay application fees

EVALUATION:
- Financial assessment
- Technical capability
- Past experience
- Equipment and resources

APPROVAL:
- Committee review
- Grade determination
- Certificate issuance
- Database entry

RENEWAL:
- Annual renewal required
- Updated information submission
- Performance review
- Fee payment
