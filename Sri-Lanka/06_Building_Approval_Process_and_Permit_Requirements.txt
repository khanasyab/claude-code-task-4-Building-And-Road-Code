======================================================================
BUILDING APPROVAL PROCESS AND PERMIT REQUIREMENTS
COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE TO OBTAINING BUILDING PERMITS IN SRI LANKA
======================================================================

DOCUMENT INFORMATION
---------------------
Title: Building Approval Process and Permit Requirements in Sri Lanka
Governing Authorities: Urban Development Authority (UDA), Municipal Councils, Local Authorities
Legal Framework: UDA Law No. 41 of 1978, Municipal Council Ordinances
Application Portal: applications.uda.lk (online applications)
Processing Time: 7-12 working days (varies by location)

======================================================================
CHAPTER 1: OVERVIEW OF APPROVAL SYSTEM
======================================================================

1.1 REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

Building approvals in Sri Lanka are governed by a multi-tiered system:

URBAN DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY (UDA):
- Jurisdiction: Declared urban development areas
- Role: Planning approval, development permits
- Coordinates with local authorities
- Sets planning standards

MUNICIPAL COUNCILS:
- Major cities (Colombo, Kandy, Galle, etc.)
- Building plan approval and permits
- Implementation of UDA regulations
- Inspections and enforcement

PRADESHIYA SABHAS (DIVISIONAL COUNCILS):
- Smaller urban areas
- Building approval authority
- Local regulation enforcement

GRAMA NILADHARI DIVISIONS:
- Rural areas
- Basic approvals for small constructions
- Coordination with local authorities

1.2 FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLE

"No development activity shall be carried out in a declared urban
development area without the written approval of the Urban Development
Authority."

This applies to all construction, alterations, and changes of use
within declared urban areas.

1.3 TYPES OF APPROVALS REQUIRED

PRIMARY APPROVALS:
1. Planning Permission (Land Use Clearance)
2. Development Permit (Building Plan Approval)

ADDITIONAL CLEARANCES (as applicable):
3. Fire Safety Clearance
4. Environmental Clearance
5. Road Authority Clearance
6. Archaeological Clearance
7. Coastal Conservation Clearance
8. Solid Waste Management Approval
9. Various Utility Clearances

======================================================================
CHAPTER 2: PRE-APPLICATION REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

2.1 LAND OWNERSHIP VERIFICATION

DEED REQUIREMENTS:
- Clear title to property
- Minimum 35 years clear deeds required
- Original deeds or certified copies
- No legal encumbrances
- Proper land registration

SURVEY PLAN:
- Approved survey plan required
- Must be less than 10 years old
- Signed and stamped by Licensed Surveyor
- Registered with Survey Department
- Shows accurate boundaries and dimensions

2.2 SITE ASSESSMENT

Before applying, verify:
- Zoning classification of land
- Applicable development regulations
- Setback requirements
- Height restrictions
- Plot coverage and FAR limits
- Access to public roads
- Utility availability
- Special restrictions (heritage, coastal, etc.)

2.3 ENGAGE QUALIFIED PROFESSIONALS

REQUIRED PROFESSIONALS:

Design Architect:
- Bachelor of Architecture from recognized university
- Registered with UDA
- Member of Institute of Architects Sri Lanka (IASL)
- Minimum 8 years experience (for major projects)
- Current practicing certificate

Structural Engineer:
- Civil engineering degree
- Chartered Engineer (C.Eng) status
- Member of Institution of Engineers Sri Lanka (IESL)
- Registered with UDA
- Minimum 5-8 years structural design experience

Quantity Surveyor:
- Qualified Quantity Surveyor
- Member of Institute of Quantity Surveyors Sri Lanka (IQSSL)
- Registered with UDA (for cost estimates)

Other Consultants (as required):
- MEP (Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing) Engineers
- Landscape Architects
- Environmental Consultants
- Fire Safety Engineers
- Traffic Engineers

2.4 PRELIMINARY INVESTIGATIONS

SOIL INVESTIGATION:
Required for:
- Buildings over 3 stories (G+2)
- Buildings on suspect soil conditions
- Buildings with basements
- Major structures

Report contents:
- Soil profile
- Bearing capacity
- Settlement prediction
- Foundation recommendations
- Water table information

TOPOGRAPHIC SURVEY:
- For sloping sites
- Large developments
- Detailed site planning

CHAPTER 3: APPLICATION DOCUMENTS
======================================================================

3.1 OWNERSHIP DOCUMENTS

Required documents:
- Original property deeds (or certified copies)
- Survey plan (approved, less than 10 years old)
- Tax receipts (up to date)
- Assessment tax clearance
- Land Registry extracts
- Deed of transfer (if recent purchase)

FOR SUBDIVIDED LANDS:
- Approved subdivision plan
- Individual lot survey plan
- Road vesting documentation

FOR AMALGAMATED LANDS:
- Approved amalgamation plan
- Updated survey plan
- Combined deed documentation

3.2 APPLICATION FORMS

STANDARD FORMS:
- Building application form (prescribed format)
- Owner's declaration and consent
- Professional certificates
- Contractor declaration (if known)
- Occupancy type declaration

INFORMATION REQUIRED:
- Owner details (name, address, NIC)
- Property details (address, plot number)
- Proposed use of building
- Construction details (area, height, stories)
- Estimated construction cost
- Proposed commencement and completion dates

3.3 ARCHITECTURAL DRAWINGS

REQUIRED DRAWINGS (9 copies):

SITE PLAN (Scale 1:500 or 1:250):
- Property boundaries
- Building location
- Setbacks from boundaries
- Access roads and driveways
- Parking layout
- Drainage pattern
- Existing trees and features
- North orientation
- Site dimensions
- Adjacent properties

FLOOR PLANS (Scale 1:100 or 1:50):
- All floor levels (basement, ground, upper floors, roof)
- Room layouts and dimensions
- Room names and uses
- Door and window locations
- Wall thicknesses
- Stairways and elevators
- Sanitary fixtures
- Built-in furniture
- Floor levels and heights
- Area calculations

ELEVATIONS (Scale 1:100):
- All four sides (North, South, East, West)
- Building height
- Floor levels
- Roof profile
- Material indications
- Window and door positions
- Architectural features

SECTIONS (Scale 1:100):
- Minimum two sections (longitudinal and cross)
- Through main features (stairs, significant spaces)
- Foundation details
- Floor-to-floor heights
- Roof structure
- Ground levels

ROOF PLAN (Scale 1:100):
- Roof layout
- Slopes and drainage
- Water tanks
- Access points
- Parapets

3.4 STRUCTURAL DRAWINGS

FOUNDATION PLAN:
- Foundation layout
- Foundation types and sizes
- Soil bearing capacity
- Reinforcement details
- Ground beam details

STRUCTURAL FRAMING PLANS:
- Column and beam layout (each floor)
- Member sizes
- Reinforcement schedules
- Slab details

STRUCTURAL DETAILS:
- Column details and schedules
- Beam details and schedules
- Slab reinforcement details
- Staircase details
- Connection details
- Foundation details

STRUCTURAL CALCULATIONS:
- Design basis and codes used
- Load calculations
- Member design calculations
- Foundation design
- Professional certification

3.5 MEP DRAWINGS

ELECTRICAL PLANS:
- Distribution board locations
- Lighting layout
- Power outlet layout
- Electrical load calculations
- Single line diagrams
- Earthing and lightning protection

PLUMBING PLANS:
- Water supply layout
- Drainage and sewerage layout
- Sanitary fixture locations
- Pipe sizes and materials
- Water tank details
- Septic tank/treatment plant (if applicable)

MECHANICAL PLANS (if applicable):
- HVAC layout
- Ventilation systems
- Fire fighting systems
- Lift installations

3.6 ADDITIONAL PLANS

LANDSCAPE PLAN:
- Landscaping layout
- Planting schedule
- Hard landscaping
- Boundary walls and gates
- External lighting

DRAINAGE PLAN:
- Surface water drainage
- Connection to public drains
- Soak pits (if applicable)
- Levels and gradients

3.7 PROFESSIONAL CERTIFICATES

ARCHITECT'S CERTIFICATE:
Content:
- Compliance with UDA regulations
- Building bye-laws compliance
- Proper design standards
- Professional seal and signature
- Registration number
- Date

STRUCTURAL ENGINEER'S CERTIFICATE:
Content:
- Structural design compliance
- Code references (Eurocodes/SLS-EN)
- Structural safety assurance
- Soil investigation consideration
- Professional seal and signature
- Registration number

QUANTITY SURVEYOR'S ESTIMATE:
- Detailed cost estimate
- Item-wise breakdown
- Total construction value
- Professional certification

3.8 SUPPORTING REPORTS

SOIL INVESTIGATION REPORT:
- For buildings requiring foundation design
- From recognized laboratory
- Recommendations for foundation type

ENVIRONMENTAL REPORT (if required):
- Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA)
- For large projects or sensitive areas
- Central Environmental Authority (CEA) approval

TRAFFIC IMPACT ASSESSMENT:
- For large commercial developments
- Access arrangements
- Parking adequacy

CHAPTER 4: SPECIAL CLEARANCES
======================================================================

4.1 FIRE SAFETY CLEARANCE

REQUIRED FOR:
- All non-residential buildings
- Residential buildings G+3 (4 stories) and above
- Buildings with floor area >1500 m²
- Hotels, hospitals, schools, assembly buildings

SUBMITTED TO:
- Fire Service Department (via Municipal Council)
- CIDA (for fire safety plan approval)

DOCUMENTS:
- Fire safety plan showing:
  * Fire escape routes
  * Fire extinguisher locations
  * Fire detection and alarm system
  * Fire suppression systems
  * Fire service access
  * Emergency lighting
- Fire safety system specifications
- Professional certification

PROCESSING TIME:
- 3 working days (Colombo Municipal Council)
- Varies for other areas

OUTCOME:
- Fire Safety Recommendations
- Fire NOC (No Objection Certificate)

4.2 ENVIRONMENTAL CLEARANCE

REQUIRED FOR:
- Projects listed in CEA regulations
- Large developments
- Projects in sensitive areas
- Industries

AUTHORITY:
- Central Environmental Authority (CEA)

LEVELS:
1. Environmental Recommendation (ER)
   - Minor environmental impact
   - Quick approval process

2. Initial Environmental Examination (IEE)
   - Moderate environmental concerns
   - Detailed assessment required

3. Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA)
   - Major projects
   - Comprehensive study
   - Public consultation
   - Longer approval process

PROCESSING TIME:
- ER: Few weeks
- IEE: 2-3 months
- EIA: 6-12 months

4.3 ROAD DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY CLEARANCE

REQUIRED FOR:
- Buildings fronting main roads (A, B grade roads)
- Access to main roads
- Development within road reservation

SUBMITTED TO:
- Road Development Authority (RDA)
- Provincial Road Development Authority (PRDA)

CONSIDERATIONS:
- Building setback from road
- Access point approval
- Road widening reservations
- Traffic safety

4.4 COASTAL CONSERVATION CLEARANCE

REQUIRED FOR:
- Development within coastal zone (300m from mean high water line)
- Any construction on beach areas
- Coastal infrastructure

AUTHORITY:
- Coast Conservation and Coastal Resources Management Department

REQUIREMENTS:
- Coastal Zone Management Plan compliance
- Setback from High Water Line
- Environmental considerations
- Special approvals for coastal structures

4.5 ARCHAEOLOGICAL CLEARANCE

REQUIRED FOR:
- Development in archaeological reserves
- Areas with historical significance
- Ancient city areas (Anuradhapura, Polonnaruwa, etc.)

AUTHORITY:
- Department of Archaeology

PROCESS:
- Site inspection
- Archaeological assessment
- Conditions for development
- Monitoring requirements

4.6 SOLID WASTE MANAGEMENT APPROVAL

REQUIRED FOR:
- Buildings G+3 (4 stories) or above
- Buildings with floor area >1500 m²
- Large commercial developments

SUBMITTED TO:
- Municipal Council (Solid Waste Management Unit)

REQUIREMENTS:
- Waste storage facilities plan
- Waste management plan
- Collection arrangements
- Recycling provisions

PROCESSING TIME:
- 5 working days (CMC)

4.7 AVIATION AUTHORITY CLEARANCE

REQUIRED FOR:
- Buildings within airport vicinity
- High-rise buildings
- Tall structures (towers, masts)

AUTHORITY:
- Civil Aviation Authority of Sri Lanka (CAASL)

ASSESSMENT:
- Obstacle Limitation Surfaces
- Height restrictions
- Aviation safety

CHAPTER 5: APPLICATION SUBMISSION
======================================================================

5.1 NUMBER OF COPIES

Submit NINE (9) complete sets of:
- All drawings
- Application forms
- Certificates
- Supporting documents

DISTRIBUTION:
Copies distributed to various departments for review:
- Building department
- Fire services
- Public health
- Engineering services
- Planning department
- Other relevant agencies

5.2 SUBMISSION METHODS

MANUAL SUBMISSION:
- Physical submission at authority office
- During office hours
- Receipt provided

ONLINE SUBMISSION:
- UDA Online Application Portal: applications.uda.lk
- Digital documents upload
- Online payment
- Application tracking
- Status notifications

5.3 APPLICATION FEES

Fees based on:
- Type of building
- Floor area
- Construction value
- Location

TYPICAL FEE STRUCTURE:
- Application processing fee
- Plan scrutiny fee (per square meter)
- Inspection fees
- Certificate fees

PAYMENT METHODS:
- Bank deposit
- Online payment (for online applications)
- Payment receipt required

5.4 APPLICATION ACKNOWLEDGMENT

Upon submission:
- Application number issued
- Receipt provided
- Expected timeline communicated
- Contact information for inquiries

======================================================================
CHAPTER 6: REVIEW AND APPROVAL PROCESS
======================================================================

6.1 TECHNICAL REVIEW

REVIEW STAGES:

INITIAL SCREENING:
- Document completeness check
- Application form verification
- Fee payment confirmation
- Professional certificates validation

TECHNICAL EVALUATION:
- Zoning compliance
- Building regulation compliance
- Structural adequacy
- Fire safety provisions
- Drainage arrangements
- Parking adequacy
- Accessibility compliance

INTER-DEPARTMENTAL REVIEW:
Circulation to:
- Building department
- Fire services
- Public health inspector
- Engineering department
- Planning department

EXTERNAL AGENCY CLEARANCES:
As applicable:
- Fire Safety (CIDA/Fire Service)
- Environmental (CEA)
- Road Authority (RDA)
- Coast Conservation
- Archaeology
- Others

6.2 QUERIES AND CLARIFICATIONS

If issues identified:
- Query letter issued
- Specific deficiencies listed
- Timeline to respond
- Revised drawings may be required

APPLICANT RESPONSE:
- Address all queries
- Submit revised documents
- Provide clarifications
- Additional information (if requested)

RESUBMISSION:
- Corrected drawings
- Additional documents
- Query response letter

6.3 COMMITTEE REVIEW

For major projects:
- Building approval committee
- Multi-departmental representation
- Site inspection (if needed)
- Decision making

6.4 PROCESSING TIMELINE

COLOMBO MUNICIPAL COUNCIL AREA:
- Technical review: 5 working days
- Clearance processing: 2 working days
- Total processing time: 7 working days

NON-CMC AREAS (OTHER MUNICIPAL COUNCILS):
- Technical review: 7-10 working days
- Clearance processing: 2-5 working days
- Total processing time: 12 working days

FACTORS AFFECTING TIMELINE:
- Completeness of application
- Complexity of project
- Need for special clearances
- Number of queries raised
- Committee meeting schedules
- External agency approvals

FAST-TRACK OPTIONS:
Some authorities offer expedited processing:
- Additional fees may apply
- Criteria for eligibility
- Reduced timelines

6.5 APPROVAL OUTCOME

APPROVAL GRANTED:
- Development Permit issued
- Three (3) copies of approved plans returned
- Plans stamped and signed by authority
- Permit conditions listed
- Validity period specified

CONDITIONS OF APPROVAL:
May include:
- Compliance with specific requirements
- Additional submissions required
- Inspection schedules
- Conditions for occupancy

PERMIT VALIDITY:
- Standard validity: One (1) year
- Construction must commence within validity
- Extension possible before expiry
- Lapsed permits require fresh application

REFUSAL:
If approval not granted:
- Reasons provided
- Options for modification
- Appeal process available

======================================================================
CHAPTER 7: MODIFICATIONS AND AMENDMENTS
======================================================================

7.1 MINOR MODIFICATIONS

Minor changes during construction:
- Internal layout adjustments (non-structural)
- Finishing material changes
- Minor fixture relocations

PROCESS:
- Inform building inspector during inspection
- Documentation for records
- No formal approval if truly minor

7.2 MAJOR AMENDMENTS

Significant changes requiring approval:
- Structural modifications
- Additional floors or areas
- Change in use
- Facade alterations
- Building footprint changes

PROCESS:
- Submit revised plans
- Amendment application
- Technical review
- Approval required before implementation
- Amendment fees applicable

7.3 CHANGE OF USE

Converting building to different occupancy:
- New application required
- Compliance with new use requirements
- Fire safety reassessment
- Parking adequacy
- Additional clearances may be needed

======================================================================
CHAPTER 8: CONSTRUCTION PHASE
======================================================================

8.1 COMMENCEMENT

BEFORE STARTING CONSTRUCTION:
- Obtain approved permit
- Display permit at site
- Engage registered contractor (for applicable projects)
- Notify authority of commencement date
- Ensure site safety measures

SITE BOARD:
Display board showing:
- Project details
- Owner information
- Architect and engineer details
- Contractor information
- Permit number and date
- Expected completion date

8.2 INSPECTIONS DURING CONSTRUCTION

MANDATORY INSPECTIONS:

COMMENCEMENT INSPECTION:
- Site verification
- Setting out check
- Temporary works approval

FOUNDATION INSPECTION:
- After excavation, before concreting
- Foundation depth and width
- Soil bearing verification
- Reinforcement check
- Approval before concreting

PLINTH LEVEL INSPECTION:
- Ground floor level verification
- Plumbing installations check
- Damp proofing

STRUCTURAL FRAME INSPECTION:
- Floor-by-floor inspection (for multi-story)
- Column and beam placement
- Reinforcement verification
- Concrete quality check

ROOF LEVEL INSPECTION:
- Final height verification
- Structural completion
- Roof construction

DRAINAGE INSPECTION:
- Drainage system installation
- Connection to public drains
- Testing

FINAL INSPECTION:
- Overall completion check
- Compliance with approved plans
- All systems functional
- Fire safety measures in place
- Ready for occupancy

8.3 INSPECTION REQUESTS

PROCESS:
- Request inspection in advance
- Allow 2-3 days notice
- Inspector visits site
- Inspection report prepared
- Approval for next stage (or corrections required)

RECORD KEEPING:
- Inspection reports filed
- Progress documented
- Any deviations noted

8.4 DEVIATIONS FROM APPROVED PLANS

If deviations identified:
- Stop work order may be issued
- Explanation required
- Corrective action needed
- Regularization process
- Possible penalties

======================================================================
CHAPTER 9: COMPLETION AND OCCUPANCY
======================================================================

9.1 CERTIFICATE OF CONFORMITY

Upon satisfactory completion:

APPLICATION:
- Request final inspection
- Submit completion documents
- Ensure all systems tested
- Clear any outstanding issues

FINAL INSPECTION:
Authority verifies:
- Construction as per approved plans
- All mandatory inspections completed
- Building services functional
- Fire safety measures in place
- Drainage connections complete
- No unauthorized deviations

CERTIFICATE ISSUANCE:
- Certificate of Conformity (CoC) issued
- Also called "Completion Certificate"
- Required for occupancy
- Necessary for utility connections

TIMELINE:
- Final inspection within 1-2 weeks of request
- Certificate issued within few days after satisfactory inspection

9.2 OCCUPANCY CERTIFICATE

Some authorities issue separate Occupancy Certificate:
- After Certificate of Conformity
- Permits occupation of building
- Required for business licenses
- Insurance purposes

9.3 UTILITY CONNECTIONS

With Certificate of Conformity:
- Apply for permanent electricity connection (CEB)
- Water connection (NWSDB or local authority)
- Telephone and internet services
- Other utilities

ELECTRICITY CONNECTION:
- Application to Ceylon Electricity Board
- Wiring inspection by CEB
- Load approval
- Meter installation

WATER CONNECTION:
- Application to NWSDB or local authority
- Connection feasibility
- Payment of connection fees
- Meter installation

9.4 AS-BUILT DRAWINGS

Good practice:
- Prepare as-built drawings
- Document actual construction
- Include all systems and layouts
- Keep for future reference
- Required for some authorities

======================================================================
CHAPTER 10: SPECIAL CASES
======================================================================

10.1 RENOVATION AND ALTERATION

MINOR RENOVATIONS:
- Interior painting and finishing
- Fixture replacements
- Minor repairs
- Generally no approval required

MAJOR ALTERATIONS:
Requiring approval:
- Structural changes
- Adding floors or areas
- Facade changes
- Changes affecting fire safety
- Plumbing or electrical major works

PROCESS:
- Submit alteration application
- Show existing and proposed layouts
- Structural certification (if applicable)
- Approval before work commences

10.2 HERITAGE BUILDINGS

Additional requirements:
- Heritage conservation guidelines
- Approval from archaeology department
- Restrictions on alterations
- Material and design compatibility
- Special monitoring

10.3 ILLEGAL CONSTRUCTIONS

For unauthorized construction:
- Authority inspection
- Violation notice issued
- Options:
  1. Regularization (if possible)
  2. Modification to comply
  3. Demolition (severe cases)
- Penalties and fines
- Legal action possible

REGULARIZATION:
If building can be made compliant:
- Submit plans showing actual construction
- Demonstrate compliance (or modifications)
- Payment of penalty fees
- Approval process
- Compliance certificate

======================================================================
CHAPTER 11: SMALL BUILDINGS AND EXEMPTIONS
======================================================================

11.1 SMALL RESIDENTIAL BUILDINGS

Simplified process in some areas:
- Single-family houses (small size)
- Limited floor area
- Maximum height restrictions
- Simpler documentation
- Faster approval

11.2 AGRICULTURAL BUILDINGS

In rural areas:
- Farm buildings
- Livestock shelters
- Agricultural storage
- May have simplified requirements

11.3 TEMPORARY STRUCTURES

Short-term structures:
- Temporary sheds
- Event structures
- Construction site facilities
- Limited approval validity
- Removal date specified

11.4 BOUNDARY WALLS AND GATES

Height restrictions:
- Front boundary: 1.2m solid, up to 2m with openings
- Side and rear: 2m typical maximum
- Some areas: Simple approval or exemption

======================================================================
CHAPTER 12: APPEALS AND DISPUTES
======================================================================

12.1 APPEAL PROCESS

If approval denied or conditions unacceptable:

INTERNAL APPEAL:
- Request reconsideration
- Provide additional information
- Address concerns raised
- Review by senior officials or committee

FORMAL APPEAL:
- Appeal to higher authority
- UDA Appeals Committee
- Ministry level (in some cases)
- Legal representation possible

TIMELINE:
- Appeal within specified period (typically 30 days)
- Response time varies

12.2 DISPUTE RESOLUTION

For disputes with authority:
- Negotiation and clarification
- Technical expert opinions
- Professional body mediation
- Legal recourse (last resort)

CHAPTER 14: TIPS FOR SMOOTH APPROVAL
======================================================================

14.1 PREPARATION

- Understand applicable regulations before designing
- Engage qualified professionals early
- Verify all property documents
- Check zoning and restrictions
- Consult with authority informally (if possible)

14.2 DOCUMENTATION

- Ensure complete documentation
- Professional drawings and details
- All required certificates
- Clear and accurate information
- Sufficient copies
- Proper binding and labeling

14.3 COMPLIANCE

- Design to comply with all regulations
- Don't assume variations will be granted
- Address setbacks, height, coverage
- Adequate parking provision
- Fire safety from design stage
- Drainage properly planned

14.4 PROFESSIONAL ENGAGEMENT

- Hire experienced professionals
- Ensure proper registration and credentials
- Good professional-authority relationship
- Professional follow-up on application

14.5 COMMUNICATION

- Respond promptly to queries
- Provide complete information
- Maintain communication with authority
- Track application progress
- Follow up appropriately

14.6 PATIENCE AND PLANNING

- Allow sufficient time in project schedule
- Don't commence without approval
- Plan for potential delays
- Budget for fees and potential modifications

- Understanding the regulatory framework
- Complete and accurate documentation
- Engagement of qualified professionals
- Compliance with all applicable regulations
- Patience and proper follow-up

Key success factors:
- Thorough preparation
- Professional design and documentation
- Complete application submission
- Timely response to queries
- Compliance with regulations

Benefits of proper approval:
- Legal authorization to construct
- Protection from enforcement actions
- Structural and fire safety assurance
- Utility connection facilitation
- Property value and saleability
- Peace of mind

Always obtain necessary approvals before commencing construction and
ensure full compliance with approved plans during construction.
