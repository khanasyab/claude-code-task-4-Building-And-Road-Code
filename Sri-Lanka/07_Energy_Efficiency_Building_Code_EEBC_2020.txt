ENERGY EFFICIENCY BUILDING CODE OF SRI LANKA 2020
COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE TO ENERGY EFFICIENT BUILDING DESIGN
======================================================================

DOCUMENT INFORMATION
---------------------
Title: Energy Efficiency Building Code of Sri Lanka 2020
Authority: Sri Lanka Sustainable Energy Authority (SLSEA)
Ministry: Ministry of Power and Energy
Official Publication: 2020 (Updated version of 2008 code)
Website: www.energy.gov.lk
Previous Version: Code of Practice for Energy Efficient Buildings in Sri Lanka - 2008
Legal Status: Mandatory for specified building types
National Goal: Carbon neutral Sri Lanka by 2050

======================================================================
CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION
======================================================================

1.1 BACKGROUND AND PURPOSE

The Energy Efficiency Building Code (EEBC) of Sri Lanka 2020 was
developed to promote energy efficient design and construction of
buildings in Sri Lanka, supporting the nation's goal to become carbon
neutral by 2050.

OBJECTIVES:
- Introduce energy efficient design for commercial buildings
- Enable design under minimal energy consumption
- Maintain building function and occupant comfort
- Reduce greenhouse gas emissions
- Promote sustainable construction practices
- Set minimum energy efficiency standards
- Encourage designs exceeding minimum standards

1.2 HISTORICAL DEVELOPMENT

EVOLUTION:
- First EEBC developed by Ceylon Electricity Board (CEB) in 2000
- Code of Practice compiled by SLSEA in 2008 (revised version)
- EEBC 2020 - Current updated comprehensive code
- Continuous improvement based on experience and technology

1.3 SCOPE AND APPLICABILITY

MANDATORY APPLICATION:
The code applies to:
- New commercial buildings
- Industrial facilities
- Large-scale housing schemes
- Major renovations (affecting >50% of building systems)

BUILDING TYPES COVERED:
- Office buildings
- Retail and shopping complexes
- Hotels and restaurants
- Hospitals and healthcare facilities
- Educational institutions
- Theaters and assembly buildings
- Industrial buildings with conditioned spaces
- Multi-family residential (large complexes)

EXEMPTIONS:
- Single-family residences
- Small buildings (<500 m²)
- Agricultural buildings
- Temporary structures
- Special purpose buildings (as specified)

1.4 DESIGN INTENT

The code enables:
- Energy-efficient building envelope design
- Efficient HVAC system design
- Effective lighting design
- Integration of renewable energy
- Indoor environmental quality
- Sustainable building operation

CHAPTER 2: CLIMATE AND DESIGN CONDITIONS
======================================================================

2.1 SRI LANKA CLIMATE ZONES

Sri Lanka has distinct climate zones affecting building design:

ZONE 1: LOW COUNTRY WET ZONE
Locations: Colombo, Galle, Kalutara, Ratnapura
Characteristics:
- High rainfall (>2500mm annually)
- High humidity (75-85%)
- Moderate temperature (25-30°C)
- Two monsoon seasons

ZONE 2: LOW COUNTRY DRY ZONE
Locations: Hambantota, Puttalam, Trincomalee
Characteristics:
- Low rainfall (<1750mm annually)
- Lower humidity (60-75%)
- Higher temperatures (27-35°C)
- Hot and arid conditions

ZONE 3: HILL COUNTRY (UP-COUNTRY)
Locations: Kandy, Nuwara Eliya, Badulla
Characteristics:
- Moderate to high rainfall
- Moderate humidity
- Cool temperatures (15-25°C depending on elevation)
- Pleasant climate

ZONE 4: INTERMEDIATE ZONE
Locations: Kurunegala, Matale
Characteristics:
- Moderate rainfall (1750-2500mm)
- Transitional characteristics
- Moderate temperatures (24-28°C)

2.2 OUTDOOR DESIGN CONDITIONS

RECOMMENDED DESIGN CONDITIONS FOR EACH ZONE:

The code specifies outdoor design conditions including:
- Dry bulb temperature (for various percentiles)
- Wet bulb temperature
- Relative humidity
- Solar radiation values
- Wind speed and direction

DATA SOURCES:
- Meteorological Department of Sri Lanka
- Climatologic Tables
- SWERA (Solar and Wind Energy Resource Assessment)
- TMY2 (Typical Meteorological Year) data
- Acceptable to SLSEA for software simulations

2.3 INDOOR DESIGN CONDITIONS

STANDARD INDOOR CONDITIONS FOR AIR-CONDITIONED SPACES:

Temperature: 25°C ± 1.5°C (23.5°C to 26.5°C)
Relative Humidity: 55% ± 5% (50% to 60%)

ACCEPTABLE RANGES BY SPACE TYPE:

Offices:
- Temperature: 24-26°C
- Relative Humidity: 50-60%

Retail:
- Temperature: 24-26°C
- Relative Humidity: 50-60%

Hotels (Guest Rooms):
- Temperature: 23-25°C
- Relative Humidity: 50-60%

Hospitals:
- Temperature: 22-24°C (patient areas)
- Relative Humidity: 40-60%
- Special requirements for operating theaters, ICU

Schools:
- Temperature: 24-27°C
- Relative Humidity: 50-70%

NATURALLY VENTILATED SPACES:
- Comfort based on adaptive thermal comfort model
- Indoor temperature can be higher than AC spaces
- Air movement and humidity control important

======================================================================
CHAPTER 3: BUILDING ENVELOPE REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

3.1 OVERVIEW

The building envelope is the physical separator between conditioned and
unconditioned spaces, significantly affecting energy consumption.

ENVELOPE ELEMENTS:
- Exterior walls
- Roof/ceiling assemblies
- Windows and glazing
- Doors
- Floors (if over unconditioned space)

3.2 OVERALL THERMAL TRANSFER VALUE (OTTV)

DEFINITION:
OTTV is a measure of average heat gain through the building envelope,
calculated in Watts per square meter (W/m²).

FORMULA:
OTTV = α × (1-WWR) × Uw × TDeq + (WWR × Uf × ΔT) + (WWR × SC × SF)

Where:
α = Solar absorptance of opaque wall
WWR = Window-to-Wall Ratio
Uw = Thermal transmittance of opaque wall (W/m²·K)
TDeq = Equivalent temperature difference (°C)
Uf = Thermal transmittance of fenestration (W/m²·K)
ΔT = Temperature difference between indoor and outdoor
SC = Shading Coefficient of glass
SF = Solar Factor (W/m²)

MAXIMUM OTTV VALUES:

For Air-Conditioned Buildings:
- Commercial buildings: 50 W/m² (maximum)
- Industrial buildings: 60 W/m² (maximum)

TARGET VALUES (RECOMMENDED):
- High performance: <40 W/m²
- Best practice: <35 W/m²

3.3 OPAQUE WALL REQUIREMENTS

THERMAL TRANSMITTANCE (U-VALUE):

Maximum U-values for walls:
- Above grade walls: 1.5 W/m²·K
- Recommended: <1.2 W/m²·K
- High performance: <0.8 W/m²·K

WALL CONSTRUCTION OPTIONS:

Standard masonry wall (230mm brick, plastered):
- U-value: Approximately 2.0 W/m²·K
- Requires improvement for compliance

Insulated wall options:
- Cavity wall with insulation: U = 0.6-0.8 W/m²·K
- External insulation: U = 0.5-0.7 W/m²·K
- Insulated concrete formwork: U = 0.4-0.6 W/m²·K

INSULATION MATERIALS:
- Expanded polystyrene (EPS)
- Extruded polystyrene (XPS)
- Mineral wool
- Polyurethane foam
- Reflective insulation
- Natural materials (coconut fiber, etc.)

SOLAR ABSORPTANCE:

Light colors (white, cream): α = 0.2-0.4 (recommended)
Medium colors (pastels): α = 0.4-0.6
Dark colors: α = 0.6-0.9 (avoid for energy efficiency)

3.4 ROOF REQUIREMENTS

THERMAL TRANSMITTANCE:

Maximum U-values for roofs:
- Standard: 1.0 W/m²·K
- Recommended: <0.8 W/m²·K
- High performance: <0.5 W/m²·K

ROOF CONSTRUCTION OPTIONS:

Uninsulated concrete slab roof:
- U-value: 3.0-4.0 W/m²·K (non-compliant)

Insulated options:
- Concrete roof with ceiling and insulation: 0.4-0.6 W/m²·K
- Roof with reflective insulation: 0.5-0.7 W/m²·K
- Green roof: 0.3-0.5 W/m²·K

ROOF COLOR:
- Light colors required (white, light gray)
- Solar reflectance index (SRI): >78 recommended
- Cool roof coatings beneficial

ROOF INSULATION:
Minimum insulation R-value: 2.0 m²·K/W
Recommended R-value: 3.0 m²·K/W or higher

3.5 FENESTRATION (WINDOWS AND GLAZING)

WINDOW-TO-WALL RATIO (WWR):

Maximum WWR by orientation:
- North and South: 50%
- East and West: 40%
- Overall building average: 40%

GLASS SPECIFICATIONS:

SHADING COEFFICIENT (SC):
Maximum SC: 0.60
Recommended: <0.50
High performance: <0.40

SOLAR HEAT GAIN COEFFICIENT (SHGC):
Maximum: 0.40
Recommended: <0.35
High performance: <0.25

VISIBLE LIGHT TRANSMITTANCE (VLT):
Minimum: 0.40 (40%)
Recommended: >0.50 for view glazing

U-VALUE (THERMAL TRANSMITTANCE):
Maximum: 3.3 W/m²·K (single glazing)
Recommended: <2.8 W/m²·K
High performance: <2.0 W/m²·K (double glazing)

GLASS OPTIONS:

Single clear glass (6mm):
- SC: 0.95, SHGC: 0.82, U: 5.7 W/m²·K (not recommended)

Tinted glass (6mm):
- SC: 0.70-0.80, SHGC: 0.60-0.69, U: 5.7 W/m²·K

Reflective glass:
- SC: 0.40-0.60, SHGC: 0.35-0.52, U: 5.5 W/m²·K

Low-E glass:
- SC: 0.50-0.70, SHGC: 0.43-0.60, U: 3.8-4.5 W/m²·K

Double glazed units:
- SC: 0.50-0.75, U: 1.8-2.8 W/m²·K (varies by type)

Performance glazing (low-E double):
- SC: 0.30-0.40, SHGC: 0.26-0.35, U: 1.6-2.0 W/m²·K

3.6 EXTERNAL SHADING

SHADING DEVICES:
Highly recommended for east and west facing windows:
- Horizontal overhangs (effective for south)
- Vertical fins (effective for east/west)
- Combination devices
- Operable shading (louvers, blinds)
- Vegetation (deciduous trees, vines)

OVERHANG DESIGN:
Depth calculated based on:
- Window height
- Solar angles
- Latitude (7°N for Sri Lanka)
- Required shading period

3.7 DAYLIGHTING

DESIGN STRATEGIES:
- Maximize north and south glazing (with shading)
- Minimize east and west glazing
- Light shelves to improve daylight penetration
- Skylights with proper shading
- Light-colored interior surfaces
- Integration with artificial lighting controls

DAYLIGHT FACTOR:
Target: 2-5% in occupied spaces
Benefits:
- Reduced lighting energy
- Improved occupant well-being
- Natural connection

======================================================================
CHAPTER 4: BUILDING HVAC SYSTEMS
======================================================================

4.1 GENERAL PROVISIONS

HVAC systems often account for 50-60% of building energy consumption
in commercial buildings in Sri Lanka.

DESIGN REQUIREMENTS:
- Proper load calculations
- Appropriate system selection
- Efficient equipment
- Effective controls
- Adequate commissioning

4.2 MANDATORY PROVISIONS

MINIMUM EQUIPMENT EFFICIENCY:

PACKAGE AIR CONDITIONERS:
Cooling capacity <12 kW:
- Energy Efficiency Ratio (EER): ≥2.9 W/W
- Coefficient of Performance (COP): ≥2.9

Cooling capacity 12-40 kW:
- EER: ≥2.7 W/W
- COP: ≥2.7

Cooling capacity >40 kW:
- EER: ≥2.6 W/W
- COP: ≥2.6

SPLIT AIR CONDITIONERS:
- Residential/light commercial: EER ≥2.9 W/W
- Commercial: EER ≥2.7 W/W

WATER-COOLED CHILLERS:
Centrifugal chillers:
- COP: ≥5.0 (full load)
- IPLV: ≥6.0 (integrated part load value)

Screw chillers:
- COP: ≥4.2 (full load)
- IPLV: ≥5.0

AIR HANDLING UNITS:
- Fan power limitation: <2.0 W/(L/s)
- Filter pressure drop: <250 Pa

COOLING TOWERS:
- Efficiency: ≥65 L/s per kW heat rejection

PUMPS:
- Pump system power: <22 W/gpm (primary pumps)
- Variable speed drives for pumps >5 kW

4.3 LOAD CALCULATION

REQUIREMENTS:
- Accurate cooling load calculation mandatory
- Based on actual building design
- Use recognized calculation methods (ASHRAE, CIBSE)
- Account for:
  * Envelope heat gains
  * Solar radiation
  * Internal gains (occupants, lighting, equipment)
  * Ventilation loads
  * Infiltration
  * Safety factors (reasonable, not excessive)

LOAD CALCULATION SOFTWARE:
- Building simulation tools acceptable
- Must account for Sri Lankan climate
- Use appropriate weather data

4.4 SYSTEM DESIGN

VENTILATION:

Outdoor air requirements:
- As per ASHRAE Standard 62.1-2007
- Ventilation for Acceptable Indoor Air Quality

Typical outdoor air rates:
- Offices: 8-10 L/s per person
- Retail: 7.5 L/s per person
- Classrooms: 8 L/s per person
- Hotel guest rooms: 12 L/s per person
- Restaurants: 10-12 L/s per person

Demand-controlled ventilation:
- CO₂ sensors for variable occupancy spaces
- Energy savings potential

DUCTWORK:

Duct construction:
- According to SMACNA standards (1995)
- Proper sealing of joints
- Leak testing requirements

Duct insulation:
- Minimum R-value: 1.9 m²·K/W (supply ducts)
- Return ducts: 1.2 m²·K/W (if outside conditioned space)

PIPING:

Pipe insulation:
- Chilled water pipes: Minimum 25mm thickness
- Condenser water pipes: Minimum 13mm thickness
- Hot water pipes: Minimum 25mm thickness

ZONING:

Separate zones for:
- Perimeter and interior spaces
- Different orientations
- Different occupancy schedules
- Different thermal requirements

CONTROLS:

Thermostats:
- Programmable thermostats required
- Temperature range: 23-26°C (adjustable)
- Time-based control for unoccupied periods
- Setback/setup temperatures

Occupancy sensors:
- For intermittently occupied spaces
- Automatic temperature adjustment

Building Management System (BMS):
- Required for buildings >2,000 m² conditioned area
- Centralized monitoring and control
- Scheduling capabilities
- Trend logging
- Alarm functions

4.5 COMMISSIONING

For systems >350 kW cooling capacity:

COMMISSIONING REQUIREMENTS:
- As per ASHRAE Guideline 1-1996
- HVAC Commissioning Process

COMMISSIONING ACTIVITIES:
- Design review
- Submittal review
- Installation verification
- Functional performance testing
- Systems manual
- Training

4.6 ALTERNATIVE DESIGN APPROACH

PERFORMANCE-BASED COMPLIANCE:

Instead of prescriptive requirements:
- Whole building energy simulation
- Demonstrate proposed design uses ≤85% of baseline energy
- Baseline per reference building design
- Approved simulation software required
- Documentation and verification

SIMULATION REQUIREMENTS:
- Detailed building model
- Actual building geometry
- Proposed envelope and systems
- Typical occupancy and operation
- Compare to code-compliant baseline

======================================================================
CHAPTER 5: LIGHTING SYSTEMS
======================================================================

5.1 GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

Lighting accounts for 20-30% of building energy in commercial buildings.

DESIGN OBJECTIVES:
- Adequate illumination for tasks
- Energy-efficient lighting technologies
- Effective controls
- Integration with daylighting
- Occupant comfort and well-being

5.2 LIGHTING POWER DENSITY (LPD)

DEFINITION:
Maximum lighting power per unit floor area (W/m²)

MAXIMUM LPD BY SPACE TYPE:

Office spaces: 12 W/m²
Corridors and circulation: 5 W/m²
Conference rooms: 13 W/m²
Classroom/Lecture halls: 13 W/m²
Retail sales areas: 15 W/m²
Hotel guest rooms: 10 W/m²
Restaurant dining: 11 W/m²
Hospital patient rooms: 8 W/m²
Library reading areas: 12 W/m²
Lobby/reception: 12 W/m²
Stairways: 6 W/m²
Parking garage: 2 W/m²
Warehouse/Storage: 8 W/m²

CALCULATION:
LPD = Total installed lighting power (W) / Floor area (m²)

Includes:
- All interior lighting
- Excludes emergency lighting, exit signs

5.3 LAMP AND LUMINAIRE REQUIREMENTS

MINIMUM LAMP EFFICACY:

Linear fluorescent lamps:
- T5 (14W-28W): ≥90 lumens/Watt
- T8 (18W-36W): ≥75 lumens/Watt

Compact fluorescent lamps (CFL):
- ≥55 lumens/Watt

LED lamps and luminaires:
- ≥75 lumens/Watt (general lighting)
- ≥65 lumens/Watt (directional lighting)

High intensity discharge (HID):
- Metal halide: ≥70 lumens/Watt
- High pressure sodium: ≥80 lumens/Watt

LAMP TECHNOLOGIES:

RECOMMENDED:
- LED (most efficient, long life)
- T5 fluorescent
- Induction lamps (long life applications)

ACCEPTABLE:
- T8 fluorescent with electronic ballasts
- Compact fluorescent (CFL)

NOT RECOMMENDED:
- T12 fluorescent
- Incandescent (except where necessary)
- Magnetic ballasts

BALLASTS:

Electronic ballasts required:
- High-frequency (25-40 kHz)
- Low losses
- Flicker-free operation
- Compatible with controls

5.4 LIGHTING CONTROLS

MANUAL CONTROLS:
- Local switches for individual spaces
- Multi-level switching for large spaces
- Accessible to occupants

AUTOMATIC CONTROLS (REQUIRED):

Occupancy sensors:
- Private offices
- Conference rooms
- Restrooms
- Storage areas
- Other intermittently occupied spaces

Time scheduling:
- For buildings >500 m²
- Automatic shut-off during unoccupied hours
- Override capability (timed, e.g., 30 minutes)

Daylighting controls:
- For spaces with adequate daylight
- Continuous dimming or step switching
- Photocell sensors
- Integrated with artificial lighting

EXTERIOR LIGHTING CONTROLS:
- Photocell or time clock
- Automatic on at dusk, off at dawn
- Bi-level control (reduce intensity after hours)

5.5 TASK LIGHTING

STRATEGY:
- Reduce ambient lighting levels
- Provide task lighting where needed
- Individual control
- Energy savings: 20-40%

5.6 ILLUMINATION LEVELS

RECOMMENDED ILLUMINANCE (lux):

General office work: 300-500 lux
Computer work: 300-400 lux
Technical drawing: 750 lux
Conference rooms: 300-500 lux
Corridors: 100-200 lux
Stairways: 150-200 lux
Retail display: 500-750 lux
Classroom/Lecture: 300-500 lux
Hospital patient rooms: 100-300 lux
Library reading: 500 lux

DESIGN CONSIDERATIONS:
- Provide required light where needed
- Avoid over-lighting
- Uniformity appropriate to task
- Glare control
- Color rendering

CHAPTER 6: RENEWABLE ENERGY INTEGRATION
======================================================================

6.1 SOLAR ENERGY

SOLAR PHOTOVOLTAIC (PV) SYSTEMS:

Benefits in Sri Lanka:
- Abundant solar resource (4.5-5.5 kWh/m²/day)
- Offset building electricity consumption
- Grid-connected or standalone
- Net metering available (CEB)
- Rapid payback (especially with feed-in tariffs)

Design considerations:
- Roof orientation and tilt
- Shading analysis
- Structural capacity for panels
- System sizing
- Inverter selection
- Grid connection requirements

SOLAR WATER HEATING:

Applications:
- Hotels (guest room hot water)
- Hospitals
- Residential
- Industrial processes

System types:
- Flat plate collectors
- Evacuated tube collectors
- Thermosyphon or pumped systems

Benefits:
- High efficiency (50-70%)
- Reduce electrical water heating
- Short payback period

6.2 WIND ENERGY

Limited application in Sri Lanka:
- Coastal areas with suitable wind
- Small-scale wind turbines
- Hybrid systems (wind + solar)

6.3 BIOMASS

Applications:
- Industrial processes
- District heating
- Combined heat and power (CHP)

Sri Lanka context:
- Agricultural waste (rice husk, coconut shell)
- Waste-to-energy
- Biogas from organic waste

6.4 INTEGRATION WITH CODE

The EEBC encourages:
- Provision for future renewable energy systems
- Roof design for PV installation
- Electrical infrastructure for grid-tied systems
- Space for solar water heating systems
- Consideration in energy performance calculations

CHAPTER 7: WATER EFFICIENCY
======================================================================

7.1 WATER CONSERVATION

Though primarily energy-focused, EEBC addresses water efficiency:

EFFICIENT FIXTURES:

Low-flow faucets:
- Maximum flow: 6-8 L/min
- Aerators for reduced flow
- Automatic sensors (public areas)

Water-efficient toilets:
- Dual-flush toilets: 3/6 liters
- Low-flow toilets: 6 liters per flush
- Waterless urinals (where acceptable)

Showerheads:
- Maximum flow: 9 L/min
- Low-flow showerheads: 6-7 L/min

7.2 WATER HEATING EFFICIENCY

- Solar water heating (preferred)
- Heat pump water heaters
- Condensing gas water heaters
- Insulated storage tanks (minimum R-10)
- Pipe insulation
- Point-of-use heaters (minimize distribution losses)

7.3 RAINWATER HARVESTING

Encouraged for:
- Landscape irrigation
- Toilet flushing
- Cooling tower makeup water
- Non-potable uses

System components:
- Collection (roof catchment)
- Filtration and treatment
- Storage tanks
- Distribution system

7.4 GREYWATER REUSE

Applications:
- Landscape irrigation
- Toilet flushing (with proper treatment)

Requirements:
- Appropriate treatment
- Separate plumbing
- Signage and identification
- Health authority approval

CHAPTER 8: COMPLIANCE AND VERIFICATION
======================================================================

8.1 COMPLIANCE PATHS

PRESCRIPTIVE PATH:
- Meet all mandatory provisions
- Meet prescriptive requirements for:
  * Building envelope (OTTV)
  * HVAC systems (equipment efficiency)
  * Lighting (LPD and controls)
- Simpler approach for standard buildings

PERFORMANCE PATH:
- Whole building energy simulation
- Demonstrate energy performance
- More flexibility in design
- Suitable for innovative designs

TRADE-OFFS:
Some trade-offs possible:
- Enhanced envelope for relaxed equipment efficiency
- Must be justified through calculation
- Overall energy performance maintained or improved

8.2 DOCUMENTATION

DESIGN STAGE:
- Envelope calculations (OTTV)
- Equipment specifications
- Lighting power calculations
- Control system descriptions
- Professional certifications

AS-BUILT:
- Installed equipment documentation
- Commissioning reports
- Test and balance reports
- Control system settings
- Operations and maintenance manuals

8.3 CERTIFICATION

PROCESS:
- Submit design compliance documents to SLSEA
- Review and approval
- Site inspections during construction
- Final verification
- Certificate of compliance

VALIDITY:
- Required for building occupancy permit
- Maintained with building documents

8.4 ENFORCEMENT

AUTHORITY:
- Sri Lanka Sustainable Energy Authority (SLSEA)
- Municipal authorities
- UDA (for urban areas)

NON-COMPLIANCE:
- Design modifications required
- Occupancy permit withheld
- Penalties as per regulations

CHAPTER 9: OPERATION AND MAINTENANCE
======================================================================

9.1 BUILDING OPERATION

ENERGY MANAGEMENT:
- Building energy manager designation
- Energy monitoring and tracking
- Performance benchmarking
- Continuous improvement

OCCUPANT BEHAVIOR:
- Energy awareness programs
- Training for building users
- Feedback on energy consumption
- Incentives for energy conservation

9.2 MAINTENANCE

HVAC SYSTEMS:
- Regular filter changes
- Coil cleaning
- Refrigerant charge checks
- Calibration of controls
- Motor and belt maintenance

LIGHTING SYSTEMS:
- Lamp replacement (group relamping)
- Luminaire cleaning
- Sensor calibration
- Control system checks

BUILDING ENVELOPE:
- Seal maintenance
- Weatherstripping
- Roof inspection
- Infiltration reduction

9.3 MONITORING

ENERGY METERING:
- Whole building metering
- Submetering by major loads
- Real-time monitoring
- Identify anomalies and inefficiencies

BENCHMARKING:
- Compare performance over time
- Compare to similar buildings
- Set improvement targets

CHAPTER 10: BENEFITS OF ENERGY EFFICIENCY
======================================================================

10.1 ECONOMIC BENEFITS

- Reduced energy bills (30-50% savings possible)
- Lower operating costs
- Higher property values
- Improved return on investment
- Protection against energy price increases
- Incentives and rebates

10.2 ENVIRONMENTAL BENEFITS

- Reduced greenhouse gas emissions
- Contribution to national carbon neutrality goals
- Reduced air pollution
- Conservation of natural resources
- Sustainable development

10.3 SOCIAL BENEFITS

- Improved indoor environmental quality
- Enhanced occupant comfort and productivity
- Health benefits
- Corporate social responsibility
- Leadership in sustainability

CONCLUSION
======================================================================

The Energy Efficiency Building Code of Sri Lanka 2020 provides
comprehensive requirements for designing energy-efficient buildings.

Compliance ensures:
- Reduced energy consumption
- Lower operating costs
- Environmental responsibility
- Occupant comfort
- Sustainable development

All building designers, developers, and owners should embrace energy
efficiency as an integral part of building design and operation,
contributing to Sri Lanka's goal of carbon neutrality by 2050.
