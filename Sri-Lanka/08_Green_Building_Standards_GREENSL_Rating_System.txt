CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION TO GREEN BUILDING COUNCIL SRI LANKA
======================================================================

1.1 OVERVIEW

The Green Building Council of Sri Lanka (GBCSL) is the national
organization promoting sustainable building practices and green
building certification in Sri Lanka.

MISSION:
To transform the building and construction industry towards
sustainability through education, advocacy, and market transformation

VISION:
A sustainable built environment for all Sri Lankans

1.2 ESTABLISHMENT AND RECOGNITION

- Established to promote green building practices
- Develops national green building rating systems
- Provides training and certification
- Advocates for sustainable construction
- Collaborates with international green building organizations

1.3 NATIONAL GOALS

ALIGNMENT WITH NATIONAL OBJECTIVES:
- Carbon neutrality by 2050
- Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)
- Climate change mitigation and adaptation
- Resource conservation
- Environmental protection

TARGETS FOR SRI LANKA:
- 30% of buildings to be carbon neutral
- 50% to achieve Platinum, Gold or Silver green building certification
- 100% of buildings to achieve minimum green certification

CHAPTER 3: RATING CATEGORIES AND POINT STRUCTURE
======================================================================

3.1 EVALUATION CATEGORIES

The GREENSL® Rating System evaluates buildings across EIGHT major
categories with total 100 points available:

1. MANAGEMENT (4 points)
2. SUSTAINABLE SITES (25 points)
3. WATER EFFICIENCY (14 points)
4. ENERGY AND ATMOSPHERE (22 points)
5. MATERIALS AND RESOURCES (14 points)
6. INDOOR ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY (13 points)
7. INNOVATION AND DESIGN PROCESS (4 points)
8. SOCIAL AND CULTURAL AWARENESS (4 points)

TOTAL: 100 points

3.2 CERTIFICATION LEVELS

Based on total points achieved:

CERTIFIED: 40-49 points
- Basic green building performance
- Meets fundamental sustainability criteria
- Entry-level certification

SILVER: 50-59 points
- Good sustainability performance
- Enhanced environmental features
- Recognized market leader

GOLD: 60-79 points
- Excellent sustainability performance
- Advanced green features
- Significant environmental benefits
- Industry leader

PLATINUM: 80-100 points
- Outstanding sustainability performance
- Exceptional environmental stewardship
- Innovation and leadership
- Best-in-class building

3.3 MINIMUM REQUIREMENTS

PREREQUISITES:
All projects must meet mandatory prerequisites in certain categories
before points can be awarded. These ensure fundamental sustainability
performance.

CHAPTER 5: SUSTAINABLE SITES (25 POINTS)
======================================================================

5.1 OVERVIEW

Sustainable Sites addresses:
- Site selection and planning
- Development density and connectivity
- Transportation and access
- Stormwater management
- Heat island effect
- Light pollution
- Ecology and biodiversity

5.2 SITE SELECTION

APPROPRIATE SITE SELECTION:
Avoid development on:
- Prime agricultural land
- Previously undeveloped land within 15m of water bodies
- Land within 30m from wetlands
- Endangered species habitats
- Public parkland
- Flood plains

PREFERRED SITES:
- Previously developed sites
- Brownfield remediation
- Infill development
- Greyfield redevelopment

5.3 DEVELOPMENT DENSITY AND CONNECTIVITY

URBAN DEVELOPMENT:
- High-density development encouraged
- Mixed-use developments
- Proximity to existing infrastructure
- Pedestrian and community connectivity
- Access to public transportation

DENSITY CALCULATIONS:
- Floor Area Ratio (FAR)
- Building footprint to land area
- Residential density (units per hectare)

5.4 TRANSPORTATION

ALTERNATIVE TRANSPORTATION:

Public transport access:
- Within 400m of bus route (2+ lines)
- Within 800m of rail station
- Frequent service (daily)

Bicycle facilities:
- Bicycle storage (5% of occupants minimum)
- Secure bicycle racks/rooms
- Changing facilities and showers
- Bicycle-friendly site access

Preferred parking:
- Low-emitting and fuel-efficient vehicles (5% of spaces)
- EV charging stations (2% of spaces minimum)
- Accessible prime parking

Parking capacity:
- Reduced parking (below zoning requirements)
- Shared parking arrangements
- Carpool parking (5% of spaces)

5.5 SITE DEVELOPMENT

SITE DISTURBANCE MINIMIZATION:
- Limit site disturbance to 40m beyond building perimeter
- Preserve existing vegetation
- Protect sensitive habitats
- Minimize grading and clearing

LANDSCAPING:

Native and adapted plants:
- Use 50% native or adapted species
- Climate-appropriate landscaping
- Low water requirement plants
- Drought-tolerant species

NO INVASIVE SPECIES:
- Eliminate invasive plant species
- Use indigenous species
- Promote biodiversity

5.6 STORMWATER MANAGEMENT

QUANTITY CONTROL:
- Implement stormwater management plan
- Reduce runoff volume and rate
- Infiltration systems
- Retention/detention basins
- Permeable paving
- Green roofs
- Rain gardens

QUALITY CONTROL:
- Remove 80% of Total Suspended Solids (TSS)
- Filter and treat stormwater
- Oil and grease separators
- Bioswales and wetlands
- Vegetated filters

5.7 HEAT ISLAND EFFECT

NON-ROOF SURFACES:
Options:
- Shade hardscape (within 5 years)
- Light-colored paving (SRI ≥29)
- Open grid pavement
- Covered/underground parking

Target: 50% of non-roof hardscape

ROOF SURFACES:
Options:
- High Solar Reflectance Index (SRI ≥78)
- Green roof (vegetated)
- Solar panels
- Combination strategies

Target: 75% of roof area

5.8 LIGHT POLLUTION REDUCTION

REQUIREMENTS:
- Reduce site light trespass
- Improve night sky access
- Improve nighttime visibility
- Reduce glare and light pollution

STRATEGIES:
- Fully shielded luminaires
- Cut-off fixtures
- Appropriate light levels
- No uplighting
- Lighting controls (timers, sensors)
- Dark-sky compliant design

CHAPTER 6: WATER EFFICIENCY (14 POINTS)
======================================================================

6.1 OVERVIEW

Water Efficiency category promotes:
- Reduced potable water consumption
- Efficient fixtures and fittings
- Water recycling and reuse
- Rainwater harvesting
- Efficient irrigation
- Wastewater treatment

6.2 WATER USE REDUCTION

INDOOR WATER USE:

Target: 30-50% reduction from baseline

Low-flow fixtures:
- WC: Dual flush 3/6 L or 6 L per flush (baseline: 9 L)
- Urinals: Waterless or 2 L per flush (baseline: 4 L)
- Faucets: 6 L/min maximum (baseline: 8 L/min)
- Showerheads: 7.5 L/min maximum (baseline: 9.5 L/min)

Sensor-based controls:
- Automatic faucets
- Automatic flush valves
- Occupancy-based operation

High-efficiency equipment:
- Dishwashers (commercial)
- Washing machines
- Cooling towers (cycles of concentration)

6.3 OUTDOOR WATER USE

LANDSCAPING WATER REDUCTION:

Target: 50-100% reduction from baseline

Strategies:
- Drought-tolerant plants (xeriscaping)
- Efficient irrigation system (drip irrigation)
- Weather-based irrigation controllers
- Moisture sensors
- Zoned irrigation
- Mulching
- Rainwater for irrigation
- No permanent irrigation (for high performance)

BASELINE CALCULATION:
- Reference evapotranspiration (ETo)
- Landscape coefficient
- Irrigation efficiency factor

6.4 RAINWATER HARVESTING

DESIGN AND IMPLEMENTATION:
- Collection from roof areas
- First-flush diversion
- Filtration system
- Storage tanks (sized appropriately)
- Distribution system

USES:
- Landscape irrigation (primary)
- Toilet flushing (with proper treatment)
- Cooling tower makeup water
- Washing and cleaning
- Fire protection reserve

SIZING:
- Based on rainfall data (Sri Lanka 1000-5000 mm/year)
- Catchment area
- Storage capacity (15-30 days typically)
- Demand patterns

6.5 GREYWATER REUSE

SOURCES:
- Hand wash basins
- Showers
- Laundry (non-fecal)
- Kitchen sinks (with proper treatment)

TREATMENT:
- Filtration (sand filters)
- Biological treatment
- Disinfection
- Storage

USES:
- Landscape irrigation
- Toilet flushing
- Cooling towers

REQUIREMENTS:
- Separate plumbing systems
- Appropriate treatment
- Signage and identification
- Compliance with health regulations

6.6 WASTEWATER TREATMENT

ON-SITE TREATMENT:
- Sewage treatment plant (STP)
- Constructed wetlands
- Biodigester systems
- Effluent quality meeting standards

EFFLUENT REUSE:
- Treated wastewater for irrigation
- Groundwater recharge
- Reduce sewerage load

6.7 WATER METERING

METERING AND MONITORING:
- Main water meter (mandatory)
- Sub-meters for major uses:
  * Irrigation
  * Cooling towers
  * Kitchen/F&B
  * Domestic water
  * Process water
- Leak detection system
- Monitoring and reporting

CHAPTER 7: ENERGY AND ATMOSPHERE (22 POINTS)
======================================================================

7.1 OVERVIEW

Energy and Atmosphere is the highest-weighted category addressing:
- Energy performance optimization
- Renewable energy
- Enhanced commissioning
- Ozone depletion prevention
- Energy monitoring

7.2 ENERGY PERFORMANCE

MINIMUM ENERGY PERFORMANCE (PREREQUISITE):
- Comply with Energy Efficiency Building Code (EEBC) 2020
- Demonstrate 15% improvement over ASHRAE 90.1 baseline
- Whole building energy simulation
- Envelope, HVAC, lighting, equipment

OPTIMIZE ENERGY PERFORMANCE (POINTS):

Points awarded for percentage improvement above baseline:
- 20% improvement: 2 points
- 25% improvement: 4 points
- 30% improvement: 6 points
- 35% improvement: 8 points
- 40% improvement: 10 points
- 45% improvement: 12 points
- 50% improvement: 14 points
- 55% improvement: 16 points
- 60% or more: 18 points

PERFORMANCE MODELING:
- Use approved simulation software
- Detailed building model
- Actual design parameters
- Typical operation and occupancy
- Compare to reference building

7.3 RENEWABLE ENERGY

ON-SITE RENEWABLE ENERGY:

Target: Percentage of building energy from renewables

Solar photovoltaic:
- Rooftop PV systems
- Building-integrated PV (BIPV)
- Ground-mounted (if space available)
- Grid-connected or standalone

Solar thermal:
- Solar water heating systems
- Solar air heating
- Solar cooling (absorption chillers)

Other renewables:
- Wind (coastal/suitable sites)
- Biomass/biogas
- Micro-hydro (limited applications)
- Geothermal (limited in Sri Lanka)

POINTS AWARDED:
- 3% renewable: 1 point
- 6% renewable: 2 points
- 9% renewable: 3 points
- 12% renewable: 4 points
- 15% or more: 5 points

OFF-SITE RENEWABLE ENERGY:
- Green power purchase
- Renewable Energy Certificates (RECs)
- Reduced points compared to on-site

7.4 REFRIGERANT MANAGEMENT

ODS AND GWP REDUCTION:

Phase out CFCs:
- No CFC-based refrigerants
- Zero Ozone Depletion Potential (ODP = 0)

Minimize Global Warming Potential:
- Low-GWP refrigerants
- Calculate total GWP impact
- Minimize refrigerant charge

STRATEGIES:
- Natural refrigerants (ammonia, CO₂, hydrocarbons)
- HFO refrigerants (low GWP)
- Efficient systems (reduce refrigerant quantity)
- District cooling (shared systems)
- Non-vapor compression cooling

7.5 MEASUREMENT AND VERIFICATION

ENERGY METERING:

Whole building meter (prerequisite)

Sub-metering (points):
- HVAC systems
- Interior lighting
- Exterior lighting
- Plug loads
- Process loads (if significant)

MONITORING:
- Real-time energy monitoring
- Building Management System (BMS)
- Data logging and analysis
- Benchmarking
- Continuous improvement

CHAPTER 8: MATERIALS AND RESOURCES (14 POINTS)
======================================================================

8.1 OVERVIEW

Materials and Resources category promotes:
- Sustainable materials selection
- Waste reduction
- Recycled and recyclable materials
- Locally sourced materials
- Rapidly renewable materials
- Life cycle assessment

8.2 CONSTRUCTION WASTE MANAGEMENT (PREREQUISITE)

REQUIREMENTS:
- Waste management plan
- Separate collection for recyclables
- Documentation of waste streams
- Target: Divert >50% from landfill

RECYCLABLE MATERIALS:
- Concrete and masonry
- Steel and metals
- Wood and timber
- Cardboard and paper
- Plastics
- Glass

8.3 BUILDING REUSE

EXISTING BUILDING REUSE:
- Maintain existing walls, floors, roof
- Reuse existing structural elements
- Adaptive reuse of buildings
- Renovation over new construction

POINTS:
- 50% reuse (by surface area): 1 point
- 75% reuse: 2 points
- 95% reuse: 3 points

8.4 RECYCLED CONTENT

MATERIALS WITH RECYCLED CONTENT:
- Post-consumer recycled content
- Post-industrial recycled content

Examples:
- Steel (recycled content common)
- Aluminum
- Glass
- Insulation (recycled paper, glass)
- Concrete (fly ash, slag)
- Ceiling tiles
- Carpets and flooring

TARGET:
- 15% of material cost: 1 point
- 30% of material cost: 2 points

CALCULATION:
(Post-consumer + 0.5 × post-industrial) / total material cost

8.5 REGIONAL MATERIALS

LOCALLY SOURCED MATERIALS:
- Extracted AND manufactured within 500 km of site
- Support local economy
- Reduce transportation impacts

Sri Lankan materials:
- Clay bricks and tiles
- Cement (local production)
- Aggregates (sand, metal)
- Timber (sustainably harvested)
- Coconut fiber products
- Granite and stone
- Ceramics

TARGET:
- 20% of material cost (local): 1 point
- 40% of material cost (local): 2 points

8.6 RAPIDLY RENEWABLE MATERIALS

DEFINITION:
Materials from plants harvested ≤10-year cycle

Examples:
- Bamboo (products and structures)
- Coconut timber and products
- Cork
- Natural linoleum
- Straw boards
- Natural fibers (coir, sisal)

TARGET:
- 5% of material cost: 1 point

8.7 CERTIFIED WOOD

SUSTAINABLE FORESTRY:
- FSC (Forest Stewardship Council) certified
- Responsible forest management
- Chain of custody certification
- Legal harvest verification

TARGET:
- 50% of wood from certified sources: 1 point

8.8 LOW-EMITTING MATERIALS

Addressed primarily in Indoor Environmental Quality
(See Chapter 9)

8.9 LIFE CYCLE ASSESSMENT

ENVIRONMENTAL PRODUCT DECLARATIONS (EPDs):
- Consider life cycle impacts
- Select materials with lower environmental footprint
- Use of EPDs in material selection
- LCA software and databases

CHAPTER 9: INDOOR ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY (13 POINTS)
======================================================================

9.1 OVERVIEW

Indoor Environmental Quality (IEQ) addresses:
- Indoor air quality
- Thermal comfort
- Daylighting and views
- Acoustic comfort
- Occupant control

9.2 MINIMUM IAQ PERFORMANCE (PREREQUISITE)

VENTILATION RATES:
- Meet ASHRAE 62.1-2007 minimum requirements
- Outdoor air delivery monitoring
- Ventilation rates by space type

For naturally ventilated buildings:
- ASHRAE 62.1 natural ventilation requirements
- Operable windows
- Cross ventilation
- Stack effect utilization

9.3 ENVIRONMENTAL TOBACCO SMOKE (ETS)

NO SMOKING POLICY:
- Indoor smoking prohibited
- Outdoor smoking >7.5m from entries, intakes
- Signage
- Enforcement

9.4 OUTDOOR AIR DELIVERY MONITORING

MONITORING SYSTEMS:
- CO₂ sensors in densely occupied spaces
- Direct outdoor air measurement
- Alarm when ventilation inadequate
- Automated response

9.5 LOW-EMITTING MATERIALS

VOC LIMITS:

Adhesives and sealants:
- Meet or exceed VOC limits
- Low-emitting products

Paints and coatings:
- VOC limits (< 50 g/L for interior paints)
- Zero-VOC paints preferred

Flooring:
- Low-emitting carpet and cushion
- Low-emitting resilient flooring
- Certified products (Green Label Plus, FloorScore)

Composite wood and agrifiber:
- No added urea-formaldehyde resins
- CARB compliant
- Low-emitting certified

9.6 INDOOR CHEMICAL AND POLLUTANT SOURCE CONTROL

ENTRYWAY SYSTEMS:
- Grilles, grates, or mats
- Minimum 3m length
- Trap dirt and contaminants
- Regular cleaning

INTERIOR CROSS-CONTAMINATION:
- Exhaust hazardous areas (copy rooms, janitor closets)
- Separate high-polluting areas
- Negative pressure
- Self-closing doors

9.7 THERMAL COMFORT

DESIGN FOR COMFORT:
- Meet ASHRAE 55-2004 requirements
- Temperature and humidity control
- Air movement
- Radiant temperature

OCCUPANT CONTROL:
- Individual temperature control (at least 50% of occupants)
- Operable windows (naturally ventilated buildings)
- Zoning for control

COMFORT MONITORING:
- Temperature and humidity sensors
- Automated controls
- Occupant feedback system

9.8 DAYLIGHTING AND VIEWS

DAYLIGHT:
Options to achieve points:

Daylight simulation:
- Demonstrate 50-75% of spaces achieve 300-3000 lux
- 75% or more of spaces
- During occupied hours

Prescriptive measures:
- Glazing area calculations
- Daylighting zone definitions
- Sidelighting and toplighting

VIEWS:
- 75-90% of regularly occupied spaces with views
- Direct line of sight to outside
- View factors (multiple lines of sight, distance)

GLARE CONTROL:
- Shading devices
- Adjustable blinds
- Interior light shelves
- Appropriate glazing

9.9 ACOUSTIC COMFORT

SOUND CONTROL:
- Background noise levels (HVAC)
- Sound insulation (walls, floors)
- Reverberation control
- Speech privacy
- External noise mitigation

TARGETS:
- Meet local standards or ASHRAE 189.1
- Background noise: NC 30-40 (depending on space)

CHAPTER 15: OTHER GREEN BUILDING RATING SYSTEMS IN SRI LANKA
======================================================================

15.1 LEED (LEADERSHIP IN ENERGY AND ENVIRONMENTAL DESIGN)

- U.S. Green Building Council (USGBC) system
- Internationally recognized
- Several versions (BD+C, O+M, ID+C, ND)
- Used in Sri Lanka for some projects
- Certification levels: Certified, Silver, Gold, Platinum

15.2 EDGE (EXCELLENCE IN DESIGN FOR GREATER EFFICIENCIES)

- IFC (International Finance Corporation) system
- Focus on emerging markets
- Resource efficiency (energy, water, materials)
- 20% savings minimum
- Certification levels: EDGE, EDGE Advanced, EDGE Zero Carbon
- Used in residential and commercial projects

15.3 BREEAM (BUILDING RESEARCH ESTABLISHMENT ENVIRONMENTAL ASSESSMENT METHOD)

- UK-based system
- Internationally used
- Comprehensive assessment
- Less common in Sri Lanka

15.4 BEAM (BUILDING ENVIRONMENTAL ASSESSMENT METHOD)

- Hong Kong system

CONCLUSION
======================================================================

The GREENSL® Rating System provides a comprehensive framework for
sustainable building design, construction, and operation in Sri Lanka.

Certification benefits:
- Environmental responsibility
- Economic advantages
- Social benefits
- Market recognition
- Contribution to national sustainability goals

All developers, designers, and building owners are encouraged to pursue
green building certification as part of Sri Lanka's journey towards a
sustainable built environment and carbon neutrality by 2050.
