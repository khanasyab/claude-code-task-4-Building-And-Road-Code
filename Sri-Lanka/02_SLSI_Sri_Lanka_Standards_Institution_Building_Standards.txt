CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION TO SLSI
======================================================================

1.1 OVERVIEW

The Sri Lanka Standards Institution (SLSI) is the National Standards
Body of Sri Lanka, responsible for the preparation, promotion, and
implementation of standards in the country. Originally established as
the Bureau of Ceylon Standards in 1964, it was reconstituted as SLSI
under Act No. 6 of 1984.

1.2 FUNCTIONS AND RESPONSIBILITIES

Primary functions of SLSI include:

a) STANDARDIZATION:
   - Develop and publish Sri Lanka Standards
   - Adopt international standards
   - Harmonize national standards with international practices
   - Review and revise existing standards

b) CERTIFICATION:
   - Product certification programs
   - Quality management system certification (ISO 9001)
   - Environmental management system certification (ISO 14001)
   - Occupational health and safety certification (ISO 45001)
   - Food safety certification (ISO 22000)

c) TESTING AND CALIBRATION:
   - Laboratory testing services
   - Calibration of measuring instruments
   - Material testing and analysis
   - Accreditation to ISO/IEC 17025

d) TRAINING:
   - Quality management training
   - Standards awareness programs
   - Technical training courses
   - Capacity building initiatives

e) INFORMATION SERVICES:
   - Standards documentation
   - Technical library
   - Online standards database
   - Consultation services

1.3 GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE

SLSI operates through:
- Council (governing body)
- Director General (chief executive)
- Technical Committees
- Sectoral Committees
- Working Groups

1.4 SECTORAL COMMITTEES

The Sectoral Committee on Building and Construction Materials is
responsible for developing and approving standards related to:
- Building materials
- Construction methods
- Structural design standards
- Building services and installations

======================================================================
CHAPTER 2: SRI LANKA STANDARDS (SLS) OVERVIEW
======================================================================

2.1 STANDARD NUMBERING SYSTEM

Sri Lanka Standards are designated as:
- SLS [NUMBER]: [YEAR] - Title
- Example: SLS 107:2015 - Specification for Ordinary Portland Cement

2.2 TYPES OF STANDARDS

PRODUCT STANDARDS:
- Specifications for manufactured products
- Quality requirements
- Testing methods
- Packaging and marking

PROCESS STANDARDS:
- Production methods
- Quality control procedures
- Manufacturing practices

PERFORMANCE STANDARDS:
- Functional requirements
- Performance criteria
- Testing protocols

TESTING STANDARDS:
- Test methods and procedures
- Sampling techniques
- Laboratory protocols

2.3 VOLUNTARY VS MANDATORY STANDARDS

VOLUNTARY STANDARDS:
- Majority of SLS standards (approximately 1,968)
- Recommended practices
- Market-driven compliance
- Certification available

MANDATORY STANDARDS:
- 32 standards made mandatory by regulation
- Focus on public safety and health
- Legal requirement for compliance
- Categories include:
  * Building materials
  * Household electrical items
  * Food products
  * Consumer protection items

2.4 ADOPTION OF INTERNATIONAL STANDARDS

SLSI adopts standards from:
- ISO (International Organization for Standardization)
- IEC (International Electrotechnical Commission)
- European Standards (EN)
- British Standards (BS)
- ASTM (American Society for Testing and Materials)

Adopted standards designated as:
- SLS ISO [NUMBER]
- SLS IEC [NUMBER]
- SLS EN [NUMBER]

CHAPTER 3: BUILDING MATERIALS STANDARDS
======================================================================

3.1 CEMENT AND CONCRETE

SLS 107:2015 - ORDINARY PORTLAND CEMENT (OPC)
- Constituents and composition requirements
- Mechanical properties (compressive strength)
- Physical properties (setting time, soundness)
- Chemical properties (limits on harmful substances)
- Four strength classes: 32.5, 42.5, 52.5
- Testing methods and quality control
- Packaging and marking requirements

SLS 1247 - PORTLAND POZZOLANA CEMENT
- Blended cement specifications
- Pozzolanic material requirements
- Performance characteristics
- Applications and uses

SLS 1256 - PORTLAND SLAG CEMENT
- Blast furnace slag content
- Strength development
- Durability characteristics

SLS 855 - RAPID HARDENING PORTLAND CEMENT
- High early strength requirements
- Applications in urgent construction

CONCRETE SPECIFICATIONS:

SLS 1144 - CONCRETE CUBE CRUSHING TEST
- Standard test method for compressive strength
- Cube preparation and curing
- Testing procedures
- Result interpretation
- Laboratory requirements (ISO 17025 accredited)

SLS 1500 (SERIES) - CONCRETE PRODUCTION AND TESTING
- Concrete mix design
- Quality control in production
- Field testing methods
- Acceptance criteria

3.2 STEEL AND REINFORCEMENT

SLS 26 - PLAIN STEEL BARS FOR CONCRETE REINFORCEMENT
- Dimensional requirements
- Mechanical properties (yield strength, tensile strength)
- Chemical composition
- Manufacturing standards
- Marking and identification

SLS 375 - RIBBED (DEFORMED) STEEL BARS FOR CONCRETE REINFORCEMENT
- Rib pattern specifications
- Bond characteristics
- Mechanical properties:
  * Yield strength
  * Tensile strength
  * Elongation
- Testing standards
- Applications in structural concrete

SLS 978 - HIGH STRENGTH DEFORMED STEEL BARS
- Enhanced strength characteristics
- Seismic design applications
- Special detailing requirements
- Equivalent to BS 4449

TENSILE TESTING:
- Conducted according to ISO 6892
- Laboratory accreditation required
- Test specimen preparation
- Loading rates and procedures
- Calculation of results

STRUCTURAL STEEL SECTIONS:

SLS 949 (PARTS 1-5) - HOT ROLLED STEEL BARS
- Part 1: Round bars
- Part 2: Square bars
- Part 3: Hexagonal bars
- Part 5: Flat bars
- Dimensional tolerances
- Mechanical properties
- Surface finish requirements

SLS 907 - HOT ROLLED STRUCTURAL STEEL SECTIONS
- U sections (Channels)
- L sections (Angles)
- T sections (Tees)
- I sections (Beams)
- H sections (Columns)
- Dimensional standards
- Material properties
- Applications in construction

3.3 MASONRY MATERIALS

CHAPTER 5: PRODUCT CERTIFICATION
======================================================================

5.1 SLS CERTIFICATION MARK

The SLS Mark indicates:
- Conformity to Sri Lanka Standards
- Quality assurance
- Regular surveillance
- Consumer confidence

5.2 CERTIFICATION PROCESS

Steps for obtaining SLS certification:

1. APPLICATION:
   - Submit application form
   - Provide product specifications
   - Identify applicable standards

2. INITIAL ASSESSMENT:
   - Factory inspection
   - Quality system evaluation
   - Product testing

3. EVALUATION:
   - Test result review
   - System audit findings
   - Compliance verification

4. GRANT OF LICENSE:
   - License agreement
   - Right to use SLS Mark
   - Validity period (typically 1 year)

5. SURVEILLANCE:
   - Regular factory inspections
   - Market surveillance
   - Sample testing
   - System audits

5.3 MANDATORY CERTIFICATION

Products requiring mandatory SLS certification:
- Portland cement (SLS 107)
- Steel reinforcement bars
- Electrical cables and wires
- Switches and socket outlets
- Circuit breakers
- Selected building materials

5.4 VOLUNTARY CERTIFICATION

Available for:
- Non-mandatory products
- Enhanced market credibility
- Export requirements
- Customer specifications
