CHAPTER 1: OVERVIEW OF OCCUPATIONAL SAFETY AND HEALTH LEGISLATION
======================================================================

1.1 LEGAL FRAMEWORK

FACTORIES ORDINANCE (NO. 45 OF 1942):

Scope:
The Factories Ordinance is the primary legislation outlining Occupational Health
and Safety (OHS) provisions for workplaces deemed "factories" in Sri Lanka.

Definition of Factory:
- Premises where 15 or more workers are employed, OR
- Premises where mechanized processes are carried out (regardless of number of workers)
- Includes construction sites meeting these criteria

Provisions:
- Cleanliness and waste disposal
- Overcrowding prevention
- Ventilation requirements
- Adequate lighting
- Sanitary facilities
- Safety precautions for machinery
- Fire prevention and fire-fighting equipment
- Welfare facilities (drinking water, first aid, rest areas)

SUBSEQUENT AMENDMENTS AND REGULATIONS:

Over the years, various amendments and supplementary regulations have been introduced:
- Regulations for specific hazards (asbestos, lead, etc.)
- Construction-specific safety regulations
- Machinery safety standards
- Personal protective equipment requirements

NATIONAL OCCUPATIONAL SAFETY AND HEALTH POLICY (2012):

Purpose:
Establishes comprehensive guidelines on OHS risk prevention, management, and
promotion of safety culture in Sri Lanka.

Key Principles:
- Prevention of occupational accidents and diseases
- Safe and healthy work environment as fundamental right
- Employer responsibility for worker safety
- Worker participation in safety management
- Continuous improvement
- Compliance with international standards (ILO conventions)

Objectives:
- Reduce occupational accidents, injuries, and diseases
- Strengthen institutional framework for OHS
- Improve inspection and enforcement
- Promote awareness and training
- Establish compensation and rehabilitation systems

INTERNATIONAL STANDARDS:

ISO 45001:2018:
- International standard for Occupational Health and Safety Management Systems (OHSMS)
- Adopted by many organizations in Sri Lanka voluntarily
- Framework for systematic OHS management

ILO Conventions:
- International Labour Organization conventions ratified by Sri Lanka
- Standards for safety, health, working conditions

1.3 CONSTRUCTION INDUSTRY SAFETY CHALLENGES

HIGH-RISK SECTOR:

Construction is inherently hazardous due to:
- Work at height
- Heavy machinery and equipment
- Manual handling and physical strain
- Electrical hazards
- Excavation and confined spaces
- Temporary structures
- Weather exposure
- Changing work environment
- Diverse workforce with varying skill levels

COMMON ACCIDENTS:

Falls from Height:
- Scaffolding collapses
- Ladder accidents
- Working near edges without protection
- Most common cause of fatalities

Struck by Objects:
- Falling materials and tools
- Swinging loads from cranes
- Collapse of structures or formwork

Electrocution:
- Contact with overhead power lines
- Defective electrical equipment
- Lack of proper earthing

Machinery Accidents:
- Caught in/between machinery
- Overturning of equipment
- Inadequate guarding

Collapse and Structural Failure:
- Trenches and excavations
- Temporary structures
- Inadequate shoring

SAFETY CULTURE ISSUES:

Challenges in Sri Lanka:
- Limited safety awareness among workers and supervisors
- Cost-cutting measures compromising safety
- Informal labor practices
- Inadequate training
- Poor enforcement and compliance
- Reactive rather than proactive approach
- People tend to fulfill only minimum requirements

RESEARCH FINDINGS:

Studies on construction safety in Sri Lanka have identified:
- Need for improved site conditions
- Better supervision and management
- Enhanced worker training
- Stricter enforcement
- Industry-wide safety culture development

2.1 SITE ORGANIZATION AND MANAGEMENT

SAFETY MANAGEMENT PLAN:

Content:
- Safety policy statement
- Organization and responsibilities
- Hazard identification and risk assessment
- Safe work procedures
- Training programs
- Emergency response plans
- Incident investigation procedures
- Monitoring and review

Responsibilities:

Employer/Principal Contractor:
- Provide safe workplace
- Ensure competent supervision
- Supply necessary safety equipment
- Conduct risk assessments
- Provide training
- Maintain accident records
- Comply with regulations

Site Manager/Engineer:
- Implement safety management plan
- Daily safety inspections
- Toolbox talks
- Coordinate with subcontractors
- Monitor compliance

Safety Officer (for large sites):
- Dedicated safety professional
- Full-time on site
- Conduct safety audits
- Training and awareness
- Incident investigation

Supervisor/Foreman:
- Direct supervision of workers
- Enforce safe work practices
- Ensure PPE use
- Report hazards

Workers:
- Follow safety procedures
- Use PPE provided
- Report hazards and near-misses
- Participate in training

SITE ACCESS AND SECURITY:

Perimeter Fencing:
- Secure boundary around site
- Prevent unauthorized access
- Height: Minimum 2 m
- Warning signs displayed

Gates and Access Control:
- Controlled entry points
- Security personnel
- Visitor management (sign-in, induction, PPE, escort)

Signage:
- Site identification board
- Safety signs (mandatory, warning, prohibition, emergency)
- Contact information for emergencies
- Site layout showing assembly points, first aid, etc.

WELFARE FACILITIES:

Sanitary Facilities:
- Adequate toilets (ratio based on number of workers)
- Clean and maintained
- Hand washing facilities
- Separate for men and women (if both employed)

Drinking Water:
- Clean, cool drinking water
- Sufficient supply
- Cups or dispensing method

Rest Areas:
- Shaded area for breaks
- Seating
- Protection from weather

Storage:
- Secure storage for workers' belongings
- Tool storage
- Material storage areas

First Aid:
- First aid box with supplies
- First aider trained
- Emergency contact numbers displayed
- Access to medical care

2.2 PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE)

BASIC PPE FOR CONSTRUCTION:

Safety Helmet (Hard Hat):
- Protects head from falling objects and impacts
- Mandatory for all on site
- Properly fitted with chin strap
- Replacement after impact or every 2-3 years

Safety Footwear:
- Steel-toed boots
- Slip-resistant sole
- Ankle support
- Mandatory for all workers

High Visibility Vest:
- Fluorescent color (orange, yellow, lime green)
- Reflective stripes
- Ensures visibility, especially near vehicles and machinery

Eye Protection:
- Safety goggles or glasses
- For grinding, cutting, chipping, dust
- Side shields for full protection

Hand Protection (Gloves):
- Leather gloves for general handling
- Rubber gloves for concrete and wet work
- Cut-resistant gloves for sharp materials
- Insulated gloves for electrical work
- Appropriate for task

SPECIALIZED PPE:

Fall Protection:
- Full body harness
- Lanyard and shock absorber
- Anchor points
- For work at height > 2 m without edge protection

Respiratory Protection:
- Dust masks (N95 or FFP2) for dust
- Respirators for chemicals, fumes, confined spaces
- Fit testing and training required

Hearing Protection:
- Ear plugs or ear muffs
- For noise levels > 85 dB(A)
- Noisy operations (jack hammers, concrete breakers, etc.)

Face Shields:
- For grinding, cutting where flying particles
- Used with safety glasses

PPE MANAGEMENT:

Provision:
- Employer provides PPE free of charge
- Correct size and type for task
- Adequate quantities

Training:
- Proper use and care of PPE
- Limitations of PPE
- Inspection before use

Maintenance:
- Regular cleaning
- Repair or replacement of damaged PPE
- Storage in clean, dry place

Enforcement:
- Supervisors ensure PPE worn
- Disciplinary action for non-compliance
- Role model by management

2.3 WORK AT HEIGHT SAFETY

SCAFFOLDING:

Design and Erection:
- Design by competent person for loads and configurations
- Erected by trained scaffolders
- Adequate foundation (base plates, sole boards)
- Tied to structure at regular intervals
- Vertical spacing: 4 m, Horizontal: 6 m (typical)

Components:
- Standards (vertical members)
- Ledgers (horizontal members along length)
- Transoms (horizontal members across width)
- Bracing (diagonal members for stability)
- Base plates and adjustable jacks
- Couplers (right-angle, swivel, putlog)

Working Platforms:
- Minimum width: 600 mm (preferably 900 mm for material storage)
- Boards: Scaffold boards (38-50 mm thick, 225 mm wide)
- No gaps > 25 mm between boards
- Secured against displacement
- Even surface, no projecting nails

Guardrails:
- Top rail: 900-1150 mm height
- Mid rail: Approximately 500 mm
- Toe board: 150 mm minimum height
- Prevents falls and falling objects

Access:
- Ladder access at each level
- Ladder extended 1 m above platform
- Tied and secured

Inspection:
- Daily by competent person before use
- After any modification or weather event
- Tag system (green = safe, red = unsafe)

LADDERS:

Requirements:
- Good condition (no broken rungs, splits)
- Secured at top and bottom
- Angle: 75° (1:4 ratio, 1 unit out for 4 units up)
- Extended 1 m above landing
- Three points of contact while climbing
- Not for heavy material transport

Step Ladders:
- Fully opened and locked
- Not overreaching
- Platform or top step not to be stood on (unless designed for it)

EDGE PROTECTION:

Where Work Near Unprotected Edges:
- Guardrails (top, mid, toe board)
- Covers over openings (secure, marked, load-rated)
- Safety nets (where guardrails not feasible)

FALL ARREST SYSTEMS:

Components:
- Full body harness (not belt)
- Lanyard with shock absorber
- Anchor point (rated > 15 kN per person)
- Connectors (snap hooks, carabiners)

Use:
- Attached at all times when at height
- Minimize free fall distance
- Inspection before each use

Rescue Plan:
- Procedure to rescue fallen worker
- Suspension trauma can be fatal within minutes
- Rapid response essential

ELEVATED WORK PLATFORMS (EWPs):

Types:
- Scissor lifts
- Boom lifts (articulating, telescopic)
- Mobile towers

Requirements:
- Operator training and certification
- Pre-operation inspection
- Guardrails on platform
- Harness attachment point (use harness on boom lifts)
- Ground conditions suitable
- Weather conditions considered
- No overloading

ROOF WORK:

Precautions:
- Assess roof strength (fragile roofs)
- Crawling boards or roof ladders
- Edge protection or fall arrest
- Openings covered or guarded
- Weather considered (wind, rain makes slippery)

2.4 EXCAVATION AND TRENCHING SAFETY

HAZARDS:

- Cave-in and collapse (most serious)
- Falls into excavation
- Struck by excavated material or equipment
- Hazardous atmosphere (gas, lack of oxygen)
- Water ingress
- Underground services (utilities)

PRECAUTIONS:

Planning:
- Locate underground utilities before digging (electricity, water, gas, telecom)
- Soil investigation and assessment
- Design of shoring and support
- Access and egress
- Dewatering plan

Excavation Depth:

< 1.2 m:
- May not require shoring if stable soil
- Daily inspection
- Edge protection

1.2 - 4.0 m:
- Shoring or sloping required (unless rock)
- Safe slope angle: 45° (1:1) or flatter depending on soil
- Inspection by competent person
- Ladder access every 8 m

> 4.0 m:
- Engineered shoring system
- Design by qualified engineer
- Strict supervision
- Continuous monitoring

Shoring Systems:
- Timber shoring (traditional, labor-intensive)
- Trench boxes (steel or aluminum, reusable)
- Sheet piling (for deep or long excavations)
- Hydraulic shores

Edge Protection:
- Barriers at least 1 m from edge
- Prevent persons, materials, equipment from falling in

Spoil Piles:
- At least 1 m from edge (2 m preferable)
- Prevent surcharge on excavation walls

Inspection:
- Daily before work starts
- After rainfall or disturbance
- Competent person
- Record of inspections

Atmosphere Testing:
- Test for oxygen deficiency, flammable gases, toxic gases
- Ventilation if required
- Continuous monitoring for deep excavations

Access and Egress:
- Ladder within 8 m of any worker
- Extend 1 m above surface
- Secure

2.5 MACHINERY AND EQUIPMENT SAFETY

GENERAL REQUIREMENTS:

Maintenance:
- Regular servicing as per manufacturer
- Daily pre-operation checks
- Defects repaired before use
- Records maintained

Guarding:
- Moving parts guarded (gears, belts, chains, shafts)
- Emergency stop accessible
- Interlocks on guards

Operator Competence:
- Training and certification for specific machine
- Authorization to operate
- Supervision of learners

CRANES AND LIFTING EQUIPMENT:

Tower Cranes:
- Design and erection by specialist
- Foundation design for loads
- Regular inspection and testing (statutory)
- Operator license
- Signaler for blind lifts
- Load charts followed
- Weather restrictions (wind)

Mobile Cranes:
- Operator license and experience
- Ground conditions suitable (may require outrigger mats)
- Lift plan for complex lifts
- Proximity to power lines (maintain clearance or isolate)
- Load charts and radius
- Tag lines to control load

Hoists and Lifts:
- Material hoist for vertical material transport
- Enclosed on all sides
- Gates at loading levels
- Regular inspection
- Operator present

Lifting Accessories:
- Chains, slings, shackles rated and marked
- Inspection before use (wear, damage)
- Proper rigging techniques
- Never exceed Safe Working Load (SWL)

Lifting Operations:
- Lift plan for non-routine lifts
- Competent lift supervisor
- Exclusion zone under load
- Communication signals standardized
- Weather conditions

CONCRETE MIXERS:

Precautions:
- Guards on rotating parts
- Emergency stop accessible
- Stable positioning
- Electrical safety (earthing, RCD)
- Cleaning with mixer off

POWER TOOLS:

Hand-Held Tools:
- Grinders, drills, saws, breakers
- Proper guards in place
- Two-handed operation where designed
- RCD protection (electrical tools)
- Compressed air tools: Secure hose connections

Precautions:
- Training before use
- Eye and respiratory protection
- Secure work piece
- Correct tool for job

VEHICLES AND MOBILE PLANT:

Excavators, Loaders, Dozers, Dump Trucks:
- Licensed/trained operators
- Seatbelts worn
- Reversing alarms and beacons
- Visibility (mirrors, cameras)
- Segregation of pedestrians and vehicles
- Traffic management plan
- Parking on level ground, lowered attachments

COMPRESSED GASES:

Cylinders (Oxygen, Acetylene, LPG):
- Stored upright and secured
- Separate storage for different gases
- Away from heat and ignition sources
- Regulators and hoses in good condition
- Flashback arrestors on oxy-acetylene equipment
- No oil or grease on oxygen equipment

2.6 ELECTRICAL SAFETY ON SITE

TEMPORARY ELECTRICAL INSTALLATIONS:

Distribution:
- Main distribution board fed from supply
- Weatherproof enclosures (IP65)
- Sub-distribution boards at work areas
- Proper cable sizing and protection
- Secure and protected routing

Protection:
- RCDs (30 mA for tools and portable equipment)
- Overcurrent protection (MCBs)
- Earthing system
- Regular testing

Cables and Leads:
- Heavy-duty flexible cables (rubber or approved)
- Protected from damage (vehicles, sharp edges)
- Elevated or covered where crossing walkways
- No joins or repairs with tape

Socket Outlets:
- Industrial type (weatherproof)
- 110 V supply preferred (safer than 230 V)
- RCD protection

Inspection and Testing:
- Weekly visual inspection
- Portable Appliance Testing (PAT) periodic
- Records maintained

OVERHEAD POWER LINES:

Hazard:
- Electrocution risk for cranes, scaffolding, concrete pumps
- Arcing can occur without direct contact

Precautions:
- Identify all power lines before work
- Maintain safe clearance distances:
  * Low voltage (< 1 kV): 3 m minimum
  * High voltage (11-33 kV): 6 m minimum
  * High voltage (132 kV+): 8 m minimum
- If clearance cannot be maintained:
  * Request de-energization and earthing by utility, OR
  * Goal post barriers erected, OR
  * Use of insulated tools and equipment
- Trained spotters and banksmen
- Warning signs and barriers

PORTABLE TOOLS AND EQUIPMENT:

Requirements:
- Class II (double insulated) preferred
- Or Class I with proper earthing and RCD
- 110 V center-tapped to earth system (55 V to earth) for hand-held tools
- Inspection before use

2.7 FIRE SAFETY ON CONSTRUCTION SITES

FIRE HAZARDS:

- Flammable materials (timber, formwork, packaging)
- Hot works (welding, cutting, grinding)
- Electrical faults
- Smoking
- Fuel and chemical storage
- Heating equipment

FIRE PREVENTION:

Good Housekeeping:
- Remove waste regularly
- Store materials in organized manner
- Keep escape routes clear

Hot Work Permit:
- Written permit for welding, cutting, etc.
- Fire extinguisher present
- Area cleared of flammables
- Fire watch during and after work
- Hot work prohibited in certain areas

Flammable Liquids:
- Minimum quantities on site
- Store in fire-resistant cabinets or areas
- Away from ignition sources
- Bunding to contain spills
- No smoking signs

Smoking:
- Designated smoking areas only
- Dispose of cigarette butts properly

Electrical:
- Regular inspection and maintenance
- No overloading
- Disconnect power when not in use

FIRE PROTECTION:

Fire Extinguishers:
- Suitable types for hazards (CO₂ for electrical, foam/water for general, dry powder multi-purpose)
- Located at strategic points
- Maximum travel distance: 25-30 m
- Inspected regularly
- Training for use

Fire Alarms:
- Air horns or sirens
- Method of raising alarm known to all

Water Supply:
- Adequate for fire fighting
- Hydrants or tanks

EMERGENCY RESPONSE:

Fire Emergency Plan:
- Alarm raising procedure
- Evacuation routes and assembly point
- Roll call
- Contact fire brigade (Tel: 110)
- Do not fight large fires (evacuate and call brigade)

Evacuation Drills:
- Periodic practice drills
- Ensure all know routes and assembly point

CHAPTER 3: HEALTH HAZARDS AND MANAGEMENT

3.1 OCCUPATIONAL HEALTH HAZARDS IN CONSTRUCTION

PHYSICAL HAZARDS:

Noise:
- Exposure to high noise levels from machinery, tools, processes
- Hearing damage (temporary or permanent)
- Limits: 85 dB(A) for 8-hour exposure (hearing conservation required)
- Higher levels require hearing protection and exposure time limits

Vibration:
- Hand-arm vibration from power tools (drills, grinders, breakers)
- Whole body vibration from vehicles and machinery
- Health effects: Vibration white finger, back problems
- Control: Use vibration-damped tools, limit exposure time

Heat Stress:
- Tropical climate and physical work
- Symptoms: Fatigue, heat exhaustion, heat stroke
- Control: Adequate breaks in shade, hydration, work-rest schedule

CHEMICAL HAZARDS:

Dusts:
- Concrete and cement dust (irritant, respiratory)
- Silica dust from cutting, grinding, drilling (silicosis, lung disease)
- Wood dust (respiratory sensitization)
- Asbestos (in old buildings during demolition) - serious hazard, cancer

Control:
- Wet methods to suppress dust
- Ventilation and extraction
- Respiratory protection (masks, respirators)
- Hygiene (washing before eating)

Cement and Concrete:
- Alkaline, causes skin burns and dermatitis
- Eye damage if splashed
- Control: Waterproof gloves, barrier creams, eye protection, washing facilities

Solvents and Chemicals:
- Paints, thinners, adhesives
- Inhalation hazards, skin contact, fire hazard
- Control: Ventilation, gloves, minimize exposure, substitution with less hazardous

Welding Fumes:
- Metal fumes (depending on welding process and metals)
- Control: Ventilation, fume extraction, respiratory protection

BIOLOGICAL HAZARDS:

- Contaminated soil or water (leptospirosis, tetanus)
- Insect and snake bites
- Mold and fungi
- Control: Vaccinations (tetanus), first aid, hygiene, protective clothing

ERGONOMIC HAZARDS:

Manual Handling:
- Lifting, carrying heavy materials
- Musculoskeletal injuries (back pain, strains)
- Control: Mechanical aids (cranes, hoists, trolleys), team lifting, training in proper technique, reduce load sizes

Repetitive Tasks:
- Repetitive strain injuries
- Control: Task rotation, tool design, rest breaks

Awkward Postures:
- Working in confined spaces, overhead work, kneeling
- Control: Proper tools, work platforms, limit duration

PSYCHOSOCIAL HAZARDS:

- Work pressure and long hours
- Stress and fatigue
- Control: Reasonable work schedules, breaks, support

3.2 HEALTH SURVEILLANCE AND MEDICAL CHECKS

PRE-EMPLOYMENT MEDICAL:
- Fitness for work assessment
- Baseline health status
- Identify pre-existing conditions

PERIODIC MEDICAL EXAMINATIONS:
- Annual or as required for specific exposures (noise, dusts, etc.)
- Monitor health changes
- Early detection of occupational diseases

RECORD KEEPING:
- Confidential medical records
- Exposure records

3.3 HYGIENE AND WELFARE

SANITARY FACILITIES:
- Clean toilets and washing facilities
- Maintained regularly
- Hand washing before eating

DRINKING WATER:
- Clean, cool water accessible
- Adequate supply especially in hot weather

EATING AREAS:
- Separate from work areas
- Clean area to eat
- Prevent contamination of food

WASHING FACILITIES:
- Showers if exposure to contaminants
- Soap and towels

CHAPTER 4: ACCIDENT INVESTIGATION AND REPORTING
======================================================================

4.1 ACCIDENT REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

LEGAL REQUIREMENTS (Factories Ordinance):

Notifiable Accidents:
- Fatal accidents
- Serious injuries (hospitalization, amputation, fracture, serious burns)
- Dangerous occurrences (collapse, explosion, fire, crane/hoist failure)

Reporting:
- Immediate notification to Department of Labour (by phone/email)
- Written report within 7 days
- Accident register maintained on site

4.2 ACCIDENT INVESTIGATION

PURPOSE:
- Determine root causes
- Prevent recurrence
- Learn lessons
- Legal and insurance requirements

INVESTIGATION PROCESS:

Immediate Actions:
- Ensure area safe
- Provide first aid and medical care
- Secure scene
- Notify relevant persons

Investigation Team:
- Safety officer
- Supervisor
- Worker representative
- Independent person (serious accidents)

Data Collection:
- Interview witnesses separately
- Examine equipment and location
- Photographs and measurements
- Documents (procedures, training records)

Analysis:
- Sequence of events
- Direct causes (unsafe acts, unsafe conditions)
- Root causes (management systems, training, design, etc.)
- Contributing factors

Report:
- Factual description
- Analysis
- Conclusions
- Recommendations for corrective actions

Follow-up:
- Implement recommendations
- Communicate lessons learned
- Monitor effectiveness

4.3 INCIDENT STATISTICS AND ANALYSIS

INCIDENT PYRAMID:

- Fatal/Serious injuries: Very few
- Lost Time Injuries (LTI): Few
- Medical Treatment Injuries (MTI): More
- First Aid Injuries: Many
- Near-misses: Very many

Importance of Near-Miss Reporting:
- Identify hazards before serious accident
- Learning opportunities
- Culture of safety

KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS (KPIs):

Lost Time Injury Frequency Rate (LTIFR):
LTIFR = (Number of LTIs × 1,000,000) / Total man-hours worked

Total Recordable Injury Frequency Rate (TRIFR):
TRIFR = (Number of recordable injuries × 1,000,000) / Total man-hours

Benchmarking:
- Compare with industry standards
- Track trends over time
- Set targets for improvement

6.1 ISO 45001:2018 STANDARD

OVERVIEW:
International standard for Occupational Health and Safety Management Systems (OHSMS),
replacing OHSAS 18001.

Benefits:
- Systematic approach to OHS
- Integration with other management systems (ISO 9001, ISO 14001)
- Continuous improvement
- Legal compliance
- Reduced accidents and costs
- Improved company reputation

STRUCTURE (High-Level Structure):

1. Scope
2. Normative References
3. Terms and Definitions
4. Context of the Organization
5. Leadership and Worker Participation
6. Planning
7. Support
8. Operation
9. Performance Evaluation
10. Improvement

Key Elements:
- Leadership commitment
- Worker consultation and participation
- Risk-based thinking
- Legal and other requirements
- Objectives and planning
- Competence and awareness
- Documented information
- Operational control
- Emergency preparedness
- Monitoring, measurement, audit
- Management review
- Continual improvement

6.2 IMPLEMENTING AN OHSMS

Steps:
1. Management commitment and policy
2. Gap analysis (current vs. requirements)
3. Planning (objectives, programs)
4. Implementation (procedures, training, communication)
5. Monitoring and measurement
6. Internal audit
7. Management review
8. Continual improvement

Certification:
- External audit by accredited certification body
- Demonstrates compliance with ISO 45001

6.3 CONTRACTOR MANAGEMENT

LARGE PROJECTS:
- Multiple contractors and subcontractors
- Coordination essential

Contractor Selection:
- Pre-qualification based on safety record
- Safety plans submitted
- Competence verification

Coordination:
- Joint safety committee
- Daily coordination meetings
- Clear communication
- Interface management

Monitoring:
- Audits of contractor compliance
- Incident reporting
- Performance measurement
