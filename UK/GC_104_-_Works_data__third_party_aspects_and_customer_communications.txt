Foreword
Foreword
This document gives requirements for the construction of works for the Overseeing Organisation.
This document forms part of the works specification.
The works specification is made up of both the Specification for Highway Works and the Works Specific
Requirements completed by the Specifier.
This document is applicable to contracts throughout the UK, complemented by the additional
specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.
Users are responsible for applying all appropriate documents applicable to their contract.
Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

1. Works data
1.
Works data
Setting Out
1.1
The data and information to be used for setting out shall be as stated in GC 104/WSR/001.
1.2
Setting out shall be carried out to timescales that meet the requirements of the Contractor's programme
and all programming constraints stated in the specification.
1.3
Setting out shall be carried out to ensure work being undertaken by third parties, including those for
utility services and suppliers, are located correctly.
1.4
Specific requirements for setting out shall be as detailed in GC 104/WSR/001.
Permanent Ground Markers and Permanent Benchmarks
1.5
Permanent ground markers (PGMs) and permanent benchmarks (PBMs) shall be as detailed in
GC 104/WSR/001.
1.6
Verification shall be undertaken for all PGMs and PBMs to check their levels and coordinates, unless
otherwise stated in GC 104/WSR/001.
1.7
The requirements for Verification GC 101 Section 14 shall apply to the checking of levels and
coordinates for all PGMs and PBMs.
1.8
Verification checking of levels and coordinates of PGMs and PBMs shall be undertaken within three
weeks of the commencement of the works and then periodically throughout the works where the
maximum period between checks is six weeks.
1.9
The following documentation shall be submitted for the checking of levels and coordinates for all PGMs
and PBMs: a record of the checking; details of any markers that are missing or not in the stated
location.
1.10
The requirements for Documentation GC 101 [Ref 4.N] Section 2 shall apply to the record of checking
levels and coordinates of all PGMs and PBMs and to the details of any markers that are missing or not
in the stated location.
1.11
Documentation for the checking of levels and coordinates of PGMs and PBMs shall be submitted within
24 hours of the checking being carried out.
1.12
Where PGMs or PBMs are found to be missing alternative control points shall be established and
added to those in the schedule for periodic verification.
Survey of existing aspects
Survey of existing features and areas of site
1.13
Survey of stated features and/or areas of site shall be undertaken to record the required data.
1.14
Survey of stated features and/or areas of site shall be undertaken prior to the commencement of works
to the feature or area of site.
1.15
The features and/or areas of site to be surveyed, the required data and survey specification shall be as
detailed in GC 104/WSR/001.
1.16
The following Documentation shall be submitted for survey of stated features/areas of site prior to the
commencement of works to the stated features or area of site: records of the survey including the
required data.
1.17
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 4.N] shall apply to the records of the
survey.
1.18
The location coordinates, level and dimensions of existing features that are to be removed and
replaced as part of the works shall be recorded.

1. Works data
1.19
The following Documentation shall be submitted for features that are to be removed and replaced as
part of the works prior to the commencement of the removal work: records of the location coordinates,
level and dimensions.
1.20
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 4.N] shall apply to the records of the
location coordinates, level and dimensions of features that are to be removed and replaced as part of
the works.
Existing ground levels
1.21
Verification shall be undertaken for existing ground levels to check them against the setting out data
and information detailed in paragraph 1.1 of this Section.
1.22
The frequency of the check on existing ground levels shall be once, prior to the commencement of work
to the ground being checked.
1.23
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 4.N] shall apply to the check on
existing ground levels.
1.24
The following Documentation shall be submitted for the check on existing ground levels prior to the
commencement of work to the ground being checked: records of the check and levels for areas where
there is discrepancy with the setting out data and information.
1.25
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 4.N] shall apply to records of the
check on existing ground levels and levels for areas where there is discrepancy with the setting out
data and information.

2. Utility services and supplies
2.
Utility services and supplies
2.1
For the purposes of the specification 'utility services and supplies' shall include all statutory
undertakers' services and supplies, other publicly or privately owned services and supplies and all
associated apparatus, including those for highway purposes.
E/2.2
Utility services shall include the National Roads Telecommunications Services.
2.3
The position of utility services and supplies which are affected, or possibly affected, by the works shall
be established.
2.4
Utility services and supplies shall not be damaged by the works.
2.5
Throughout the progress of the works measures shall be taken to support and fully protect utility
services and supplies which are affected, or possibly affected, by the works.
2.6
Utility services and supplies shall not be interrupted without prior arrangement with the utility service or
supply company, owner or operating agent.
2.7
Utility services and supplies to individual properties shall not be interrupted without liaison with the
owners or occupiers of the individual property.
2.8
Works to roadside technology and communications shall be undertaken in accordance with TC 131
[Ref 8.N] Roadside technology and communications.
2.9
The known details and locations of utility services and supplies shall be as stated in GC 104/WSR/002.
Diversionary works to utility services and supplies
2.10
Where utility services or supplies are subject to diversionary works liaison shall be undertaken with the
utility service or supply company, owner or operating agent.
2.11
Where utility services or supplies are subject to diversionary works the liaison with the utility service or
supply owner or operating agent shall include establishing arrangements and programming for
undertaking the diversionary works.
2.12
Diversionary works for utility services or supplies to be undertaken by third parties, that require to be
programmed for and liaison for shall be as detailed in GC 104/WSR/002.
2.13
Diversionary works for utility services or supplies that involve work being undertaken by the Contractor
for all or part of the utility works shall be as detailed in GC 104/WSR/002.
Disconnected utility services and supplies apparatus
2.14
Disconnected utility services or supplies apparatus that is to be removed and disposed of shall be as
detailed in GC 104/WSR/002.

4. Customer communications
4.
Customer communications
Scheme billboards
4.1
Scheme billboards detailed under this section shall be in addition to temporary traffic information
signing required for the works and that detailed in GC 106 [Ref 9.N].
4.2
Scheme billboards to be supplied and erected, and subsequently removed shall be as detailed in
GC 104/WSR/004.
4.3
Scheme billboards shall be in accordance with GG 906 [Ref 2.N].
4.4
Road restraint systems intended to protect road users from impacting scheme billboards shall be in
accordance with CC 400 [Ref 7.N].
4.5
Removal of scheme billboards shall include removal of mounting posts and foundations, and
reinstatement of the ground.
Visual identity
E/4.6
Scheme billboards and all customer written and electronic communications shall be in accordance with
the National Highways Specification for Branding ( NH Branding [Ref 5.N]), where customer
communications are all communications to the general public and/or travelling public.
Contractor's advertising boards
E/4.7
Contractor's advertising boards shall not be permitted on highways open to traffic.
Customer communications general
E/4.8
All responses to customer contacts about to the works shall be in accordance with GG 901 [Ref 3.N],
where customer contacts is all communications from the general public and/or travelling public.
E/4.9
All customer communications about the works shall be in accordance with National Highways
document Normal not formal [Ref 6.N].
E/4.10
All information required for the Overseeing Organisation to be able to meet customer communications
obligations detailed in GG 902 [Ref 1.N] shall be provided to meet the time frames detailed in GG 902
[Ref 1.N].
