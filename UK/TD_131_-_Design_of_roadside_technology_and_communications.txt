Introduction
Introduction
Background
The purpose of roadside technology is to contribute to safe and reliable journeys and to assist
customers by informing them about traffic conditions. The technology used on motorways and
all-purpose trunk roads contributes to these objectives by:
1)
monitoring - observing the network to detect incidents that could have an impact on the safety of
customers or their journey times;
2)
informing - providing customers with the information they need to make safe and informed
decisions whilst using the network;
3)
directing - controlling the flow of traffic on the network, and where appropriate enabling
enforcement activities.
This document references other documents that provide the criteria for provision and design
requirements for the roadside technology equipment and systems.
Assumptions made in the preparation of this document
The assumptions made in GG 101 [Ref 10.N] apply to this document.
Where the design of the roadside technology and communications equipment has additional testing
requirements (such as contract- specific requirements), it is assumed that those requirements are to be
recorded in the works information.

1. Scope
1.
Scope
Aspects covered
1.1
This document provides general requirements that shall be followed for the design and implementation
of roadside technology and communications on motorways and all-purpose trunk roads.
1.2
This document shall apply to the design of all roadside technology including; electrical and
communications systems, cabinets, ducts and chambers for roadside technology and communications,
variable message sign and signal systems, traffic detection systems, permanent traffic cameras,
emergency roadside telephones, traffic enforcement systems and ramp metering.
1.2.1
This document should be read in conjunction with the general requirements in GG 102 [Ref 15.N], GG
104 [Ref 19.N], GD 304 [Ref 9.N] and with all other DMRB documents relevant to the design of the
particular works to be undertaken.
Implementation
1.3
This document shall be implemented forthwith on all schemes involving roadside technology and
communications on the Overseeing Organisation's motorways and the all-purpose trunk roads
according to the implementations of GG 101 [Ref 10.N].
Use of GG 101
1.4
The requirements contained in GG 101 [Ref 10.N] shall be followed in respect of activities covered by
this document.

4)
design for maintenance arrangements:

b)
form of construction of the hardstanding maintenance working areas;
c)
slope dimensions of hardstanding maintenance working areas; and
d)
location of access steps and handrails.
5)
types and locations of roadside technology equipment to be removed, recovered, reinstated and
retained;
6)
maximum period of acceptable disruption to the service provided by roadside technology
equipment;
7)
types and locations of roadside technology equipment to remain operational during the works;

Physical security for roadside technology and communications
2.4
Physical security threats to the roadside technology equipment shall be assessed, documented,
mitigated and managed in accordance with the Overseeing Organisation's security procedure.
NOTE
Examples of physical security threats to roadside technology equipment can include compromised
roadside equipment and metal theft.
2.4.1
The TSP may request security measures on selected roadside technology equipment that are deemed
to be at risk of physical security threats.
2.4.2
Security mitigation measures may include security straps on cabinets and lockable chamber covers for
roadside technology and communications.
Designing for maintenance for roadside technology and communications
2.5
The roadside technology and communications design shall promote and enable safe site maintenance
and inspection.
NOTE
Examples to enable safe site maintenance and inspection can include: maintenance access points,
pathways, steps, handrails and hardstanding maintenance working areas.
2.5.1
The following should be taken into account when considering safe site maintenance and inspection of
roadside technology equipment:
1)
distance of the equipment from the maintenance access point;
2)
position of the equipment relative to the carriageway and any vehicle restraint;
3)
types and frequency of maintenance activities;
4)
whole life cost of providing a maintenance access point versus benefits of reduced temporary
traffic management deployments.
2.6
The Overseeing Organisation and maintainers shall be consulted on the design for maintenance for
roadside technology equipment.
2.7
Pathways between maintenance access points and roadside technology equipment sites shall be
continuous and a minimum of 600 mm in width.
2.8
Hardstanding maintenance working areas for roadside technology and communications shall be
designed and positioned to enable the maintainer to work safely and unencumbered.
2.8.1
A 600 x 600 mm hardstanding may provide a suitable safe working area for maintenance purposes for
roadside technology and communications.

At cabinet sites for roadside technology and communications the hardstanding should be measured

2.9

Specification and supply of roadside technology equipment
2.10
The specification for each roadside technology equipment shall be determined and documented.
NOTE 1
The Operational Technology Specifications Library OTSL [Ref 13.N] provides performance
specifications for roadside technology equipment.
NOTE 2
Traffic Open Products and Specifications TOPAS [Ref 24.N] provides performance specifications for
traffic signalling systems.
2.11
The responsibility for the supply of roadside technology equipment shall be determined using the
procurement procedures of the Overseeing Organisation.
NOTE
Procurement procedures can determine whether the equipment is procured by the Overseeing
Organisation or by the installation contractor.
Environmental control for roadside technology and communications
2.12
The environmental protection requirements for the roadside technology equipment shall be determined.
2.12.1
The environmental protection requirements for roadside technology equipment should be based on the
local environment.
NOTE
Particularly harsh environments include tunnels, high altitudes, near marine environments and
extremes of temperatures.
Siting of roadside technology equipment
2.13
Other than for the maintenance renewal of existing roadside technology equipment, roadside
technology equipment shall not be sited in the central reserve.
2.13.1
Roadside technology equipment should be relocated from the central reserve.
2.14
Roadside technology equipment shall be co-located, where design allows.
NOTE
Co-locating roadside technology equipment can support maintenance activities and minimise whole life
costs.
2.15
Roadside technology equipment shall be located so as not to interfere with, or be interfered by,
co-located roadside technology and communications.
NOTE
Communications interference includes radio, electromagnetic and physical interference, and
interference with third party systems.
2.16
Any roadside technology equipment sites that are at risk from flooding shall be positioned above the
flood level in accordance with CG 501 [Ref 4.N]and LA 113 [Ref 20.N].
2.17
The siting tolerances that have been used in the design of roadside technology and communications
shall be stated.
