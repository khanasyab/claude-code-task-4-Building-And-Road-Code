1. Steel safety barriers and transitions
1.
Steel safety barriers and transitions
Units
1.1
The units of measurement shall be:
a)
steel safety barrier ............ linear metre.
b)
steel safety barrier on structures ............ linear metre.
c)
transition ............ number.
d)
removable barrier sections ............ linear metre.
Measurement
1.2
The measurement of steel safety barriers shall be the developed length along the centre line for the
containment level class between the following points: the end of each safety barrier and the interface
with connections, transitions and terminals.
1.3
The measurement of steel safety barriers on structures shall be the developed length along the centre
line for the containment level class, for steel safety barriers located on bridge decks and reinforced
concrete structures only.
1.4
The measurement of removable safety barrier sections shall be the developed length along the centre
line for the containment level class between the following points: the end of each removable safety
barrier section and the interface with non-removable safety barrier, connections, transitions and
terminals.
1.5
The measurement of transitions shall be the complete installation.
1.6
The measurement of the overall length of steel safety barrier including transitions shall be the length of
need shown on the contract drawings and/or that specified in CC 400 [Ref 11.N].
Note
The actual length over which various VRS reach full containment can vary depending on the system
used.

1.7

steel safety barriers on structures in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 3.N] and

2. Concrete safety barrier and transitions
Measurement
2.7
The measurement of concrete safety barrier shall be the developed length along the centre line for the
containment level class, between the interface with transitions, emergency or maintenance crossing
points and steel step barrier to span manholes.
2.8
The measurement of concrete safety barrier on structures shall be the developed length along the
centre line for the containment level class for concrete safety barrier located on bridge decks and
reinforced concrete structures only.
2.9
The measurement of dual concrete safety barrier shall be the developed length along the centre line for
the containment level class of each length of concrete safety barrier measured separately, between the
interface with transitions, emergency or maintenance crossing points and steel step barrier to span
manholes.
2.10
The measurement of transitions shall be the complete installation.
Note
Bifurcations are a type of transition.
2.11
The measurement of an emergency or maintenance crossing point shall be the complete installation
with the length stated.
2.12
The measurement of a steel step barrier to span a manhole shall be the complete installation with the
length stated.
2.13
The measurement of the overall length of concrete safety barrier including transitions shall be the
length of need shown on the contract drawings and/or that specified in CC 400 [Ref 11.N].
Note
The actual length over which various VRS reach full containment can vary depending on the system
used.
Itemisation
2.14
Separate items shall be provided for concrete safety barrier and transitions in accordance with clauses
2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 3.N] and the following: (Chapter II p.3 & p.4)

7. Pedestrian parapets and pedestrian guardrails
7.
Pedestrian parapets and pedestrian guardrails
Definitions
7.1
Any reference to "pedestrian" shall be deemed to include equestrian and cyclist.
Units
7.2
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
pedestrian parapets, pedestrian guardrails ............ linear metre.
Measurement
7.3
The measurement of pedestrian parapets and pedestrian guardrails shall be the developed length
along the centre line.
7.4
Curves which are made up from individual straight lengths shall not be considered as curved elements
but as straight guardrails or handrails.

described by the specific radius.
