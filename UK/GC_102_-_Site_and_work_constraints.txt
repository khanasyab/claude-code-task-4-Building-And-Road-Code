Site and work constraints
(formerly Clauses 107, 109, 113, 121 and 124 of Series 100)
Version E/1.0.0
Summary
This document gives requirements with respect to construction programme constraints, site
security and site extent, the control of noise and vibration, health and safety restrictions and
reporting of incidents.
This document incorporates specific requirements for National Highways. Alternative versions of this document are
available for other Overseeing Organisations.
Feedback and Enquiries
Users of this document are encouraged to raise any enquiries and/or provide feedback on the content and usage of
this document to the dedicated National Highways team. The online feedback form for all enquiries and feedback
can be accessed at: www.standardsforhighways.co.uk/feedback.
This is a controlled document.

Foreword
Foreword
This document gives requirements for the construction of works for the Overseeing Organisation.
This document forms part of the works specification.
The works specification is made up of both the Specification for Highway Works and the Works Specific
Requirements completed by the Specifier.
This document is applicable to contracts throughout the UK, complemented by the additional
specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.
Users are responsible for applying all appropriate documents applicable to their contract.
Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

1. Construction programme constraints
1.
Construction programme constraints
1.1
Subject to and without prejudice to the conditions of contract the Contractor's programme for the works
shall comply with the constraints detailed in the specification including those detailed under this section.
1.2
The Contractor's programme shall comply with the constraints detailed under the following sections and
their associated Works Specific Requirements:
1)
temporary traffic management, Section 1 of GC 106 [Ref 9.N];
2)
work to utility services and supplies, Section 2 of GC 104 [Ref 10.N];
3)
site extent and limitations on use in Section 2 of this document;
4)
control of noise and vibration in Section 3 of this document;
5)
health and safety restrictions in Section 4 of this document;
6)
work embargo periods in Section 6 of this document;
7)
technical approval of highway structures in Section 18 of GC 101 [Ref 5.N].
8)
accommodation works in Section 3 of GC 104 [Ref 10.N].
9)
other identified third party works including those detailed under TC 131 [Ref 8.N] Roadside
technology and communications with respect to works for and by the Telecommunications
Service Provider.
1.3
Further constraints to be accommodated by the Contractor's programme shall be as described in
GC 102/WSR/001.
1.4
The Contractor's programme shall comply with requirements identified in GC 103 [Ref 4.N] including
the Environmental management plan, Cultural heritage assessment and mitigation, Habitat regulations
assessment, and Materials assets and waste management assessment.
1.5
The Contractor's programme shall comply with the requirements for submission of documentation and
other communications including submission timescales.

2. Site security, site extent and limitations o...
2.
Site security, site extent and limitations on use
Site security
2.1
Measures shall be taken to ensure that the site is secure and unauthorised access is restricted to
prevent theft and malicious damage.
2.2
Measures shall be taken to ensure access to hazardous areas is restricted.
2.3
Measures for site security shall ensure livestock from adjacent farm land cannot enter the site and
works or become a hazard.
2.4
Measures for site security shall comply with requirements of the Environmental management plan,
Cultural Heritage assessment and mitigation, and Habitat regulations assessment, including the
containment of animal species identified therein.
2.5
Measures for site security for containment of livestock and/or to comply with the Environmental
management plan, Cultural heritage assessment and mitigation and Habitat regulations assessment
shall not be hazardous to the livestock or wildlife.
Site specific security measures
2.6
Site specific security measures, in addition to those taken for general site security, shall be as
described in GC 102/WSR/002.
2.7
The following Documentation shall be submitted for site security measures prior to the commencement
of the works: details of the measures to be used for site security for each part of the site.
2.8
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 5.N] shall apply to details of site
security measures.
Site extent
2.9
The extent of the site shall be as stated in GC 102/WSR/002.
2.10
The site shall include areas of highway carriageway and verge used for locating temporary traffic
management signing and coning, and locating vehicle recovery plant and accommodation.
Limitations on use of the site
2.11
Limitations on the use of the site shall be as stated in GC 102/WSR/002.
2.12
The site, or any part of the site, shall not be used for any purpose other than for providing the works.
2.13
The existing highway carriageway or highway verge shall not be used for the storage of construction
equipment or materials outside the working hours for the works.
Adjacent Land Access
2.14
All existing public and private accesses to adjacent land shall be maintained throughout the works for
all travel modes and vehicles using the accesses.
2.15
Where the work affects public and private accesses to adjacent land, alternative access arrangements
shall be made for all travel modes and vehicles using the accesses, including for landowners, occupiers
and their visitors and suppliers.
2.16
Specific access requirements for adjacent land shall be as detailed in GC 102/WSR/002.

3. Control of noise and vibration
3.
Control of noise and vibration
Control of noise and vibration general requirements
3.1
Measures shall be determined and taken to reduce the impact of construction noise and vibration on
sensitive receptors.
3.2
Noise sensitive and vibration sensitive receptors shall be as identified in this section and as identified in
LA 111 [Ref 6.N] including sensitive buildings.
3.3
The known site specific details with respect to the control of construction noise and vibration shall be as
stated in GC 102/WSR/003.
3.4
Measures for the control of construction noise and vibration impacts identified by the noise and
vibration assessment undertaken in accordance with LA 111 [Ref 6.N] and/or identified in the
Environmental Management Plan shall be implemented.
3.5
The Environmental Management Plan shall be as detailed in "Environmental, cultural and habitat
management" in Section 1 of GC 103 [Ref 4.N].
3.6
Measures and restrictions for the control of construction noise and vibration, in addition to those
determined by the Contractor, shall be as stated in GC 102/WSR/003.
3.7
Details of work items or activities where requirements with respect to construction noise and vibration
are exceptional from those stated in GC 102 Section 3 shall be as stated in GC 102/WSR/003.
LA 111 construction noise and vibration assessment
3.8
The details of the construction noise and vibration assessment shall be as stated in GC 102/WSR/003.
3.9
Where a construction noise and vibration assessment has not been undertaken prior to the works a
construction noise and vibration assessment shall be undertaken in accordance with LA 111 [Ref 6.N],
unless otherwise stated in GC 102/WSR/003.
3.10
The following Documentation shall be submitted for the construction noise and vibration assessment
where one is required prior to the commencement of the works: the construction noise and vibration
assessment report.
3.11
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 5.N] shall apply to the construction
noise and vibration assessment report.
3.12
Where the impact of construction noise and/or vibration is anticipated to have a significant disturbance
impact of moderate or major as defined in LA 111 [Ref 6.N] for any sensitive receptor measures shall
be identified to reduce the impact to minor or negligible.
Construction noise and vibration control and consultation
3.13
The code of practice stated in BS 5228-1 [Ref 2.N] shall be followed for the control of construction
noise caused by the works.
3.14
The code of practice stated in BS 5228-2 [Ref 1.N] shall be followed for the control of vibration caused
by the works.
3.15
Where construction noise and/or vibration levels have, or are anticipated to have, a significant
disturbance impact of moderate or major as defined in LA 111 [Ref 6.N] and mitigation measures are
not anticipated to reduce the levels to minor or negligible the local authority shall be consulted and
consent under Section 61 of the of UKPGA 1974/40 [Ref 3.N] sort.
3.16
Where construction noise and/or vibration levels have, or are anticipated to have, a significant
disturbance impact of moderate or major as defined in LA 111 [Ref 6.N] and mitigation measures are
not anticipated to reduce the levels to minor or negligible consultation shall be undertaken with the
people affected by the disturbance impact.

3. Control of noise and vibration
3.17
The following Documentation shall be submitted for where construction noise and/or vibration levels
have, or are anticipated to have, a significant disturbance impact of moderate or major prior to the
commencement of the works that would cause the disturbance impact: evidence of the consultations
with the local authority and people affected by the disturbance impact.
3.18
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 5.N] shall apply to the evidence of
the consultations with the local authority and people affected by the disturbance impact.
3.19
The following documentation shall be submitted for any and all complaints received regarding
construction noise and/or vibration: details and records of the complaints including details of the person
or organisation making the complaint, the time and date of the incident precipitating the complaint and
the area(s) of site involved.
3.20
The requirements for Documentation GC 101 Section 2 shall apply to the details and records of
complaints.
Verification monitoring of noise and vibration levels and affects
Verification monitoring general requirements
3.21
Verification shall be undertaken for construction noise and vibration levels by monitoring measurement
at sensitive receptors.
3.22
The frequency of construction noise and vibration levels monitoring measurement shall be daily during
works that are likely to cause a disturbance impact.
3.23
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 5.N] shall apply to the monitoring
measurements of construction noise and vibration levels.
3.24
The following Documentation for the monitoring measurement of construction noise and vibration levels
shall be submitted as continuous records: monitoring measurement records.
3.25
The requirements of "Records" in Section 3 of GC 101 [Ref 5.N] shall apply to the monitoring
measurement noise and vibration levels records.
3.26
Verification and monitoring of construction noise and vibration levels identified in the assessment
undertaken in accordance with LA 111 [Ref 6.N] and/or the Environmental Management Plan shall be
undertaken.
3.27
Verification shall be undertaken for construction noise and vibration mitigation measures to ensure that
they have been implemented by inspection and reporting.
3.28
The frequency of the implementation of construction noise and vibration mitigation measures inspection
and reporting shall be once prior to the commencement of the works that are likely to cause
disturbance.
3.29
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 5.N] shall apply to the implementation
of construction noise and vibration mitigation measures inspection and reporting.
3.30
Verification monitoring of construction noise and vibration levels shall be assessed against the potential
disturbance affects and levels identified in the assessment in accordance with LA 111 [Ref 6.N] and/or
the Environmental Management Plan.
3.31
Where the measured construction noise and/or vibration levels are above those identified in the
assessment in accordance with LA 111 [Ref 6.N] and/or the Environmental Management Plan and/or
specified restrictions further measures shall be implemented and the verification monitoring activities
repeated.
3.32
Where the measured construction noise and/or vibration levels are above those identified in the
assessment in accordance with LA 111 [Ref 6.N] and/or the Environmental Management Plan and/or
specified restrictions the work causing the disturbance shall be ceased until such time as the further
measures are implemented.

3. Control of noise and vibration
Additional site specific monitoring
3.33
Site specific construction noise and vibration verification monitoring requirements in addition to those
identified in an assessment in accordance with LA 111 [Ref 6.N] and/or the Environmental
Management Plan shall be as specified in GC 102/WSR/003.
3.34
The following Documentation for site specific construction noise and vibration verification monitoring
shall be submitted as continuous records: records of the verification monitoring.
3.35
The requirements of "Records" in Section 3 of GC 101 [Ref 5.N] shall apply to records of the verification
monitoring.
Building condition surveys
3.36
Where the assessment carried out under requirement 3.8 identifies buildings as having an expected
moderate or major magnitude of vibration impact affect verification shall be undertaken for building
condition by survey.
3.37
Where a building condition survey is required this shall be undertaken prior to the commencement of
the works that are likely to cause the vibration affect.
3.38
The requirements for Verification in Section 14 of GC 101 shall apply to the building condition surveys.

5.
Reporting incidents, events and undesirable circumstances
5.1
In addition to legal reporting obligations, details of the following incidents, events and undesirable
circumstances occurring during the works shall be submitted: security, health, safety, well-being,
structural and environmental.

E/6. Work embargo periods
E/6.
Work embargo periods
E/6.1
Work on existing highways shall be restricted during the stated embargo periods.
E/6.2
During stated embargo periods temporary traffic management shall be removed or altered such that the
existing highways have the traffic flow capacity which existed before the work commenced, unless
otherwise stated in GC 102/WSR/006.
E/6.3
Provision of traffic flow capacity during stated embargo periods shall include the same number of traffic
lanes open to traffic as before works commenced.
E/6.4
Removal and/or alteration to temporary traffic management during stated embargo periods shall
comply with "Temporary traffic management" in Section 1 of GC 106 [Ref 9.N].
E/6.5
Embargo periods shall be those periods stated in the works specific requirements.
E/6.6
Site specific embargo periods shall be as stated in GC 102/WSR/006.
