Foreword
Foreword
This document gives requirements for the construction of works for the Overseeing Organisation.
This document forms part of the works specification.
The works specification is made up of both the Specification for Highway Works and the Works Specific
Requirements completed by the Specifier.
This document is applicable to contracts throughout the UK, complemented by the additional
specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.
Users are responsible for applying all appropriate documents applicable to their contract.
Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

1. Temporary accommodation and equipment for the...
1.
Temporary accommodation and equipment for the Overseeing
Organisation
1.1
The specified temporary accommodation, furnishings, fixtures, fittings and equipment shall be
provided, serviced, maintained and subsequently removed.
1.2
Provision, servicing, maintenance and removal of temporary accommodation shall include provision,
servicing, maintenance and removal of accesses for pedestrians and vehicles to each of the
accommodation spaces specified.
1.3
All temporary accommodation, furnishings, fixtures, fittings and equipment shall be fully serviceable for
the whole required time period.
1.4
Where no time period is stated, the required time period for the temporary accommodation, furnishings,
fixtures, fittings and equipment shall be from seven days prior to the commencement of the works until
four weeks after completion of the works.
1.5
All specified temporary accommodation, furnishings, fixtures, fittings, equipment and consumable items
shall be for the sole use of the Overseeing Organisation.
1.6
Unless otherwise stated, all temporary accommodation, furnishings, fixtures, fittings and equipment
shall be returned to the Contractor after the required time period.
1.7
All temporary accommodation, furnishings, fixtures, fittings and equipment shall comply with
Government Buying Standards for Sustainable Procurement DEFRA (GBS) [Ref 4.N].
1.8
All temporary accommodation spaces shall have 240 Volt power supplies, unless otherwise stated in
GC 105/WSR/001.
1.9
All welfare facilities shall have an uninterrupted mains water supply, unless otherwise stated in
GC 105/WSR/001.
1.10
Servicing of temporary accommodation, furnishings, fixtures and fittings shall include cleaning to the
standard defined in HSE L24 [Ref 9.N].
Temporary accommodation for the Overseeing Organisation
Overseeing Organisation Staff temporary accommodation
1.11
Temporary accommodation to be provided for the use of the Overseeing Organisation shall be as
described in GC 105/WSR/001.
Welfare provision for the Overseeing Organisation
1.12
Welfare provisions shall be in accordance with HSE Guidance for employers on welfare provisions HSE
WaW [Ref 7.N].
1.13
The welfare provisions shall be sufficient for the number of permanent staff plus the number of transient
staff.
1.14
The minimum welfare provision shall be as specified in GC 105/WSR/001.
1.15
Shower and toilet spaces provided for welfare shall have doors that are lockable from the inside.
Provision of furnishings, fixtures and fittings to temporary accommodation for the Overseeing
Organisation
1.16
Furnishings, fixtures and fittings shall be appropriate for their intended use.
1.17
Furnishings, fixtures and fittings for the office space(s) shall be as specified in GC 105/WSR/001.
1.18
A workstation for office space shall consist of an office desk and chair suitable for display screen
equipment (DSE) usage with a minimum of two 240 V power sockets, desk minimum size 1.5 m x 0.8
m, the chair to be fully adjustable and suitable for at least 8 hours DSE usage, unless otherwise stated
in GC 105/WSR/001.

2. Vehicles for the Overseeing Organisation
2.
Vehicles for the Overseeing Organisation
2.1
The specified vehicles shall be provided, serviced and maintained for the whole of the required time
period.
2.2
The vehicles shall be for the sole use of the Overseeing Organisation.
2.3
Where no time period is stated, the required time period for the provision of vehicles shall be from
seven days prior to the commencement of the works until four weeks after completion of the works.
2.4
All vehicles provided shall meet the minimum mandatory buying standards of the Government Buying
Standards for Transport (Vehicles) DEFRA (GBS T(V)) [Ref 5.N] for vehicles either directly purchased
or contracted under leasing / renting systems.
2.5
Vehicles to be provided for the use of the Overseeing Organisation shall be as stated in
GC 105/WSR/002.
2.6
Vehicle types 'car' and 'small van' shall be fully electric vehicles, unless otherwise stated in
GC 105/WSR/002.
2.7
Fuel or battery charging shall be provided for vehicles.
2.8
The vehicle shall be fit for the required use of the vehicle with the model, engine size and transmission
able to provide the acceleration and manoeuvrability required for the site working conditions.
2.9
The vehicles shall be fit for use on the public highway including meeting all legal requirements for the
use of the vehicle on the public highway.
2.10
The vehicles shall have comprehensive insurance cover for any qualified driver authorised by the
Overseeing Organisation and for the carriage of passengers, equipment, goods and samples.
2.11
The following Documentation shall be submitted for vehicles prior to the commencement of use of the
vehicle: copies of insurance certificates, copies of MOT certificates.
2.12
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to the insurance
certificates and MOT certificates.
Facilities and maintenance for Overseeing Organisation vehicles
2.13
Parking and charging points shall be provided at Overseeing Organisation accommodation locations as
specified in GC 105/WSR/002.
2.14
Electrical vehicle charging points shall be compatible for use for the electric vehicles provided.
2.15
The vehicles shall be maintained in clean and roadworthy condition in conformity with the vehicle
manufacturer's recommendations.
2.16
Where a vehicle is out of service for more than two hours a replacement shall be provided.
2.17
The inside and outside of vehicles shall be cleaned.
2.18
The minimum frequency for cleaning of vehicles shall be as stated in GC 105/WSR/002.
Markings for Overseeing Organisation vehicles
2.19
Vehicles shall have conspicuity markings and roof mounted amber lights in compliance with TSM
Chapter 8 [Ref 6.N].
2.20
Red retroreflective material used for vehicle conspicuity markings shall have a minimum coefficient of
retroreflection of RA Class RA2 as defined in BS EN 12899-1 [Ref 1.N].
2.21
Vehicles shall be free from markings identifying any company other than the Overseeing Organisation.
Equipment for Overseeing Organisation vehicles
2.22
Each vehicle shall be provided with a first aid kit and hazard warning triangle.

3. Communications systems for the Overseeing Org...
3.
Communications systems for the Overseeing Organisation
3.1
The specified communications systems shall be provided, maintained and subsequently removed.
3.2
All communications systems shall be fully serviceable for the whole required time period.
3.3
Where no time period is stated, the required time period for the communications systems shall be from
seven days prior to the commencement of the works until four weeks after completion of the works.
3.4
All specified communications systems shall be for the sole use of the Overseeing Organisation.
3.5
All communications systems shall be returned to the Contractor after the required time period.
Data communications for the Overseeing Organisation
3.6
Data communications to be provided for the use of the Overseeing Organisation shall be as specified in
GC 105/WSR/003.
Telephones for the Overseeing Organisation
3.7
Telephone requirements shall be in addition to requirements for data communications.
3.8
Landline telephone requirements shall be as specified in GC 105/WSR/003.
3.9
Mobile telephone requirements shall be as specified in GC 105/WSR/003.
3.10
Mobile telephones provided shall include the handset, operative sim card and charger/charger lead and
plug.
Radio communication system for the Overseeing Organisation
3.11
The provision of a radio communications system for the Overseeing Organisation shall be as stated in
GC 105/WSR/003.
3.12
The radio communications system for the Overseeing Organisation shall be as specified in
GC 105/WSR/003.
3.13
The radio communications system shall have an operating coverage or range that gives reception
throughout the site and for the extent of the temporary traffic management.
3.14
Each radio handset shall be provided with a charging unit and spare battery.
3.15
If the radio communications system is inoperative for any reason it shall be repaired or replaced within
6 hours.
Radio communication system licences
3.16
All necessary radio licences shall be obtained.
3.17
The following Documentation shall be submitted for the radio communications system prior to the
commencement of use of the radio communications system: copies of radio licences.
3.18
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to the copies of
radio licences.

4. Operatives for the Overseeing Organisation
4.
Operatives for the Overseeing Organisation
4.1
Operatives shall be provided for the specified tasks for the whole stated time periods.
4.2
Requirements for operatives for the Overseeing Organisation shall be as stated in GC 105/WSR/004.
4.3
Operatives shall be competent to undertake the specified tasks.
4.4
Where the specified tasks include driving the operatives shall have valid driving licences and suitable
driving experience for the vehicles being used.
4.5
The following Documentation shall be submitted for operatives for the Overseeing Organisation
engaged in driving vehicles prior to the commencement of the vehicle driving: copies of driving licences.
4.6
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to the copies of the
operatives' driving licences.

5. Progress images
5.
Progress images
5.1
Progress images of the works shall be captured as stills and/or video and submitted at the specified
times and time intervals.
Still images and video format
5.2
Still image resolution shall achieve a minimum of 300ppi when printed as A4.
5.3
Still images shall be submitted in an uncompressed (RAW) image file format and JPEG file format.
5.4
Video resolution shall be at least 720p (1280 x 720 pixels).
5.5
Video shall be provided in MP4 format to ISO/IEC 14496-14 [Ref 3.N].
5.6
The EXIF metadata for each image and video shall include: reference number, date, time, camera used
and geolocation.
Works progress images - location and subject
5.7
Requirements for progress images of the works shall be as specified in GC 105/WSR/005.
5.8
The following Documentation for progress images shall be submitted as continuous records: progress
images and/or video sets.
5.9
The requirements of "Records" in Section 3 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to the progress images
and/or video sets.
