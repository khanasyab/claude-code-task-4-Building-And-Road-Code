Summary
This document gives requirements for temporary traffic signs, including self-supported traffic
signs, road markings, road studs, cones, portable barriers, cylinders and delineators, portable

available for other Overseeing Organisations.

this document to the dedicated National Highways team. The online feedback form for all enquiries and feedback
can be accessed at: www.standardsforhighways.co.uk/feedback.

1.
General requirements for temporary traffic signs, road markings
and road studs
Scope of this document for temporary traffic signs, road markings and road
studs
1.1
This document shall apply to:
1)
temporary traffic signs, including self-supported traffic signs; road markings; road studs; cones,
portable barriers, cylinders and delineators; portable traffic signals and variable message traffic
signs, which are not intended to remain in position on completion of the permanent works; and
2)
removal of both permanent and temporary road markings.
Design of temporary traffic signs, road markings and road studs
1.2
The selection, positioning, installation, inspection, maintenance and removal of temporary traffic signs,
road markings and road studs, and the removal of permanent road markings shall be Contractor design
items, unless otherwise stated in CC 130/WSR/001.
1.3
The design of temporary traffic signs, road markings and road studs, and the removal of permanent
road markings shall be in accordance with CD 130 [Ref 3.N].
1.4
The requirements for "Contractor design" in Section 17 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N] shall apply to temporary
traffic signs, road markings and road studs, and the removal of permanent road markings.
Electrical installations for temporary traffic signs and road markings
1.5
Electrical installations for temporary traffic signs, road markings and road studs shall comply with
"General requirements for electrical work for road lighting and illuminated traffic signs" in Section 1 of
TC 501 [Ref 4.N].
Installation and removal of temporary traffic signs, road markings and road
studs
1.6
The installation and removal of temporary traffic signs, road markings and road studs, and the removal
of permanent road markings shall be carried out so that disturbance to road users is minimised.
1.7
The installation of temporary traffic signs, road markings and road studs shall be carried out by
organisations registered to and operating in compliance with a quality management scheme in
accordance with "Quality management schemes" in Section 7 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N].
E/1.8
Temporary traffic signs, road markings and road studs must be removed when they cease to be needed
for preventing danger to traffic, regulating traffic or for warning traffic of danger in accordance with
Section 174 of the Highways Act 1980 ( UKPGA 1980/66 [Ref 7.N]).
Inspection and maintenance of temporary traffic signs and road markings
1.9
Temporary traffic signs, road markings and road studs shall be inspected and maintained to assure that
they continue to fulfil their intended purpose, remain in place and damage free.
1.10
All damaged and displaced temporary traffic signs, road markings and road studs shall be replaced as
soon as it is safe to do so.

3.6
Temporary road markings shall be formed from paint, thermoplastic, cold plastic or preformed materials.

[Ref 12.N].
3.12

performance characteristics as stated in table 3.12.

4. Temporary road studs
4.
Temporary road studs
General requirements for temporary road studs
4.1
Temporary retroreflecting road studs specified by the Overseeing Organisation shall be as described in
CC 130/WSR/004.
4.2
Temporary non-retroreflecting road studs specified by the Overseeing Organisation shall be as
described in CC 130/WSR/004.
4.3
Temporary retroreflecting road studs shall be replaced at or before 3 months post installation unless
they are temporary road studs which are intended by the manufacturer to have a service life of greater
than 3 months and are confirmed during each inspection of temporary traffic management to be free of
damage and suitable for continued use.
4.4
Temporary retroreflecting road studs shall not be reused unless they are temporary road studs which
are intended by the manufacturer to be reusable and have been inspected and confirmed during each
inspection of temporary traffic management to be free of damage and suitable for reuse.
4.5
If permanent road studs are used for temporary works then they shall comply with "Permanent road
studs" in Section 8 of CC 120 [Ref 2.N].
Product requirements for temporary road studs
4.6
The performance of temporary retroreflecting road studs shall be compliant with CD 131 [Ref 18.N].
Installation requirements for temporary road studs
4.7
Temporary road studs shall be installed in accordance with the manufacturer's instructions.
Removal requirements for temporary road studs
4.8
The removal of temporary road studs shall be in accordance with the manufacturer's instructions.
4.9
The removal of temporary road studs shall not result in residue, voids, protrusions or loss of structural
integrity in the carriageway.

7. Portable traffic light signals
7.
Portable traffic light signals
General requirements for portable traffic light signals
E/7.1
Portable traffic light signals must only be placed on the highway when permission has been given by
the traffic authority in accordance with Schedule 14 of the Traffic Signs Regulations and General
Directions 2016 ( UKSI 2016/362 [Ref 19.N]).
7.2
The following Documentation shall be submitted for portable light traffic signals prior to the
commencement of installation of portable traffic light signals: evidence of permission to place portable
traffic light signals on the road by the traffic authority where such permission is required by the
applicable traffic signs regulations.
7.3
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N] shall apply to portable traffic
light signals.

7.4

7.5
