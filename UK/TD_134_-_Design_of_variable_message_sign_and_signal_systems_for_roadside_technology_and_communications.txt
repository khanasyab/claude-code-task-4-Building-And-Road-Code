Introduction
Background

and inform drivers where there are changing traffic conditions, traffic routes and statutory provisions.

The assumptions made in GG 101 [Ref 3.N] apply to this document.

Terms and definitions
Terms and definitions
Terms
Term
Definition
Advanced motorway
indicator
A type of electronic light emitting signal that can display mandatory
and advisory aspects.
Information model
As defined in GG 184 [Ref 10.N].
Motorway signal mark 4
A type of electronic light emitting variable message sign that can
display text, pictograms and speed aspects.
Variable message signs
Variable message signs are technology devices, within the definition
of Schedule 16 of the Traffic Signs Regulations and General
Directives, capable of displaying, at different times, two or more
defined legends or messages.
NOTE: Variable message signs can be in the form of light emitting or
non light emitting, electrical or electro-mechanical devices.

1. Scope
1.
Scope
Aspects covered
1.1
This document shall be used for the design of electrical, telecommunications and supporting
infrastructure for variable message signs and signal installations for roadside technology and
communications on motorways and all-purpose trunk roads.
1.1.1
This document should be read in conjunction with the general requirements in GG 102 [Ref 6.N], GG
104 [Ref 8.N], GD 304 [Ref 2.N]and with all other DMRB documents relevant to the design of the
particular works to be undertaken.
Implementation
1.2
This document shall be implemented forthwith on all schemes involving variable message signs and
signals on the Overseeing Organisations' motorways and all-purpose trunk roads according to the
implementation requirements of GG 101 [Ref 3.N].
Use of GG 101
1.3
The requirements contained in GG 101 [Ref 3.N] shall be followed in respect of activities covered by
this document.

2.
General requirements for variable message signs and signals
Design of supports and foundations for variable message signs and signals for
roadside technology and communications
2.1
The design of supports and foundations for variable message signs and signals for roadside technology
and communications shall be in accordance with CD 354 [Ref 1.N] design of minor structures.
Design of gantries for variable message signs and signals for roadside
technology and communications
2.2
The design of gantries and gantry mounting arrangements for variable message signs and signals for
roadside technology and communications shall be in accordance with CD 365 [Ref 4.N] portal and
cantilever signs/signals gantries.
Design of road restraint systems for variable message signs and signals for
roadside technology and communications
2.3
The design of road restraint systems for variable message signs and signals for roadside technology
and communications shall be in accordance with CD 377 [Ref 7.N] requirements for road restraint
systems.
Outputs from the design of variable message signs and signals for roadside
technology and communications
2.4
The outputs from the design of variable message signs and signals for roadside technology and
communications shall include all details for the construction of the supporting infrastructure and the
installation of variable message signs and signals.
2.4.1
Variable message signs and signals for roadside technology and communications installation design
outputs should include:
1)
the variable message sign and signal type
2)
the installation location, using a formal location referencing system;
3)
the installation location of inertia switches;
4)
the installation location and installation requirements for DLCBs and gantry isolation switches;
5)
details of associated connection equipment for variable message signs and signals;
6)
layouts showing the location of signals, signal alignment and associated equipment on the gantry
7)
alignment and setting out details for each variable message sign and signal;
8)
tolerance of heights and angles for setting out dimensions;
9)
signal wiring diagrams;
10)
mounting arrangement;
11)
support structures: posts, gantries;
12)
foundations for support structures, and methods of fixing to support structures;
13)
electrical and telecommunications installations, including cables, chambers and ducting; housings
and enclosures; housing lock types; numbers of keys for locks; and switchgear and wiring;
14)
road restraint systems; and
15)
position and orientation of all components of the proposed installation relative to other items of
existing and proposed road infrastructure, including the edge of the carriageway, so that the
following can be identified:
a)
the environment in which the installation is to take place;
b)
the factors that can influence the construction methodology and scheduling of the proposed
installation; and

3. Variable message signs and signals for roadsi...
3.
Variable message signs and signals for roadside technology and
communications
General design requirements for variable message signs and signals
3.1
Positioning of variable message signs and signals for roadside technology and communications shall
be in accordance with CD 146 [Ref 5.N].
3.2
Variable message sign and signal alignments for roadside technology and communications shall be in
accordance with Annex N, BS EN 12966 [Ref 9.N].
3.3
Cabling and cable terminations for variable message signs and signals for roadside technology and
communications shall be sited and designed to facilitate maintenance activities.
NOTE
Interface boxes such as a data link connection box are used to provide connectivity to variable
message signs and signals.
Variable message signs and signals on gantries for roadside technology and
communications
3.4
The layout of variable message signs and signals and associated equipment on a gantry for roadside
technology and communications shall be determined by the quantity and type of variable messages
signs and signals being positioned.
3.4.1
Typical layouts and wiring diagrams for variable message signs and signals installed on a gantry for
roadside technology and communications should be based on the figures in Appendix A.
3.5
Where a gantry spans both carriageways, all its signals for roadside technology and communications
shall be connected to cabinet-mounted roadside controller equipment located on the same side of the
highway as the transmission system cabling.
3.5.1
Associated roadside controller equipment for roadside technology and communications may be
mounted in the communications cabinet.
General design requirements for post mounted signals for roadside technology
and communications
3.6
Post mounted signals for roadside technology and communications shall be orientated so that the
enclosure doors are located downstream of the signal to allow operatives to view the oncoming traffic.
3.7
Post mounted signals for roadside technology and communications shall allow for telecommunications
service provider equipment.
NOTE
The service delivery point and associated cable joint enclosures can be in the signal post cabinet.
3.7.1
Typical wiring for post mounted signals for roadside technology and communications should be based
on the figures provided in Appendix A.
Ambient light monitors for variable message signs and signals for roadside
technology and communications
3.8
Where the brightness levels of variable signs and signals for roadside technology and communications
cannot be controlled automatically by the display device, an ambient light monitor shall be provided.
NOTE
Ambient light monitors can be mounted on accessible gantries or on hinged columns adjacent to
gantries.
