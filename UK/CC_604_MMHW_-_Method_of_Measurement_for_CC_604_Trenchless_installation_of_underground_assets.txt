1. Trenchless crossings - Scope and definitions
1.
Trenchless crossings - Scope and definitions
Scope
1.1
This method of measurement shall apply to the installation by trenchless techniques of highway drains,
service ducts and culverts of internal diameters 2m or less.
1.2
This method of measurement covers the trenchless or minimum dig installation and the associated
temporary works. All other items of the permanent works shall be measured under CC 500/MMHW
[Ref 7.N].
1.3
For the purposes of this measurement the launch and reception pits shall be considered temporary
works and not measured separately.
Definitions
1.4
The Earthworks Outline is defined in clauses 1.1, 1.2, 1.3, 1.4, 1.5, 1.6, and 1.7 of CC 601/MMHW [Ref
8.N] inclusive and shall apply equally to this method of measurement for trenchless crossings.
1.5
Any reference to chambers shall be deemed to include manholes, catchpits, oil interceptors and
drawpits.
Note
Pipe run is the term used to describe a specific trenchless crossing which has a unique 'Trenchless
crossing identification number'.

2. Establishment and removal of plant
2.
Establishment and removal of plant
Units
2.1
The units of measurement for establishment and removal of plant shall be:
a)
establishment and removal of plant ............ item.
Measurement
2.2
The establishment and removal of plant shall be measured once only to each pipe run.
2.3
Any additional establishment and removal of plant to suit the Contractor's method of working shall not
be measured.
Itemisation
2.4
Separate items shall be provided for plant in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref
4.N] and the following:
Table 2.4 Itemisation for Establishment and removal of plant
Group
Feature
I
1 Establishment and removal of plant.
II
1 Pipe run reference.
Item Coverage - Establishment and removal of plant
2.5
The items for the establishment of trenchless installation and plant shall be in accordance with clause
2.1 of GP 113 [Ref 3.N] and include for:
a)
bringing plant and equipment to the site of the pipe run;
b)
setting up plant and equipment at each pipe run;
c)
dismantling and removing plant and equipment on completion of the pipe run;
d)
construction of launch and reception pits and all reinstatement;
e)
thrust and winch blocks and their removal on completion of the pipe run;
f)
site preparation and access arrangements;
g)
lead-in trenches and their reinstatement;

ground stabilisation treatment;

j)
construction and reinstatement of launch and reception eyes;

alteration and reinstatement of existing chambers used as launch and reception pits;
l)
protection of existing or completed pipes in chambers;

n)

3. Trenchless crossings
3.
Trenchless crossings
Units
3.1
The unit of measurement for trenchless crossings shall be:
a)
pipe run ............ linear metre.
Measurement
3.2
The measurement of a pipe run shall be the lengths measured along the centre line of the pipe
between any of the following:
a)
the internal faces of permanent chambers;
b)
the external faces of headwalls;
c)
the position of commencement and termination shown in the Contract.
3.3
The measurement of drains and service ducts shall be for the complete construction irrespective of the
number of drains or ducts contained within any one bore.
3.4
Where more than one drain or duct is laid in a bore then the number of drains or ducts shall be stated in
the item description.
3.5
The measurement of pipe runs shall include for rehabilitation methods such as slip lining and
cured-in-place lining.
Itemisation
3.6
Separate items shall be included for each pipe run in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of
GP 112 [Ref 4.N] and the following:

1 Pipe run reference.

4. Trenchless crossings - Lateral connections
4.
Trenchless crossings - Lateral connections
Units
4.1
The unit of measurement for lateral connections shall be:
a)
lateral connections ............ number.
Measurement
4.2
The depth of lateral connection shall be the vertical measurement between the invert of the lateral pipe
and the Existing Ground Level.
4.3
Where the Earthworks Outline is below the Existing Ground Level the measurement of lateral
connection shall be taken to the Earthworks Outline.
Itemisation
4.4
Separate items shall be included for lateral connections in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of
GP 112 [Ref 4.N] and the following:

III
1 On-line replacement.
2 Rehabilitation.
IV
1 Cured in-place lining.

V
1 Different internal diameters of lateral pipes.
VI
1 Different internal diameters of main pipe run.
VII
1 Depth to invert not exceeding 2 metres.
2 Depth to invert exceeding 2 metres but not exceeding 3 metres and so on in
steps of 1 metre.
VIII
1 Lateral connection reference.
Note: Group IV Features 1 and 2 shall only apply to Group III Feature 2.
Item coverage - Lateral connections

5.
Trenchless installation of underground assets designed by the
Contractor
Item Coverage - Trenchless installation of underground assets designed by the Contractor
5.1
The respective coverage rules for trenchless installation of underground assets above shall be
replaced and/or supplemented with the items below where trenchless installation of underground
assets are to be designed by the Contractor.
5.2
The items for trenchless installation of underground assets designed by the Contractor, shall be in
accordance with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref 3.N] and include for:
a)
design in accordance with CD 622 [Ref 5.N], WSR/001 of CC 604 [Ref 10.N], Sections 17 and 18
of GC 101 [Ref 2.N];
b)
reports and certificates;
c)
provision of data and drawings;
d)
re-submissions and modifications;
e)
amendments to the Works;
f)
obtaining approvals;
g)
obtaining geotechnical certification ( CD 622 [Ref 5.N]);
h)
everything necessary for the design, fabrication, installation and verification of the Works.
