Quantities and their method of measurement.

1. Surface treatments for asphalt pavement surfa...
1.
Surface treatments for asphalt pavement surfaces
Units
1.1
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
surface treatments for asphalt pavement surfaces ............ square metre.
Measurement
1.2
The measurement of surface treatments for asphalt pavement surfaces shall be calculated using the
width of the top surface to be treated with no deductions made for openings of 1 square metre or less.
Itemisation
1.3
Separate items shall be provided for surface treatments for asphalt pavement surfaces, in accordance
with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 4.N] and the following:
Table 1.3 Itemisation for Surface treatments for asphalt pavement surfaces
Group
Feature
I
1 High friction surfacing.
2 Cold applied ultra-thin surfacing.
3 Asphalt surface preservation systems.
4 Emergency area surface treatment.
5 Slurry surfacing.
6 Surface dressing.
II
1 Different types.
III
1 Different colours.
IV
1 Different thicknesses.
V
1 Stated minimum Polished Stone Value (PSV).
VI
1 Stated maximum Aggregate Abrasion Value (AAV).
VII
1 Different rates of spread.
Item Coverage - Surface treatments for asphalt pavement surfaces
1.4
The items for surface treatments for asphalt pavement surfaces shall be in accordance with clause 2.1
of GP 113 [Ref 3.N] and include for:
a)
spreading and rolling deposited materials;
b)
bond coat as clause 3.6 of CC 202/MMHW [Ref 5.N];
c)
flexible pavement construction as clause 1.5 of CC 202/MMHW [Ref 5.N];
d)
measures required for aftercare and opening road to traffic.

2. Longitudinal diamond grinding of concrete pav...
2.
Longitudinal diamond grinding of concrete pavement surfaces
Units
2.1
The measurement shall be:
a)
longitudinal diamond grinding of concrete pavement surfaces ............ square metre.
Measurement
2.2
The measurement of longitudinal diamond grinding of concrete pavement surfaces shall be undertaken
across the full width of each lane.
2.3
The longitudinal grooves shall be no more than 5 mm deep, 2 to 4 mm wide, with spacings 1.75 to 3.25
mm wide.
Itemisation
2.4
Separate items shall be provided for longitudinal diamond grinding of concrete pavement surfaces in
accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 4.N] and the following:
Table 2.4 Itemisation for Longitudinal diamond grinding of concrete pavement surfaces
Group
Feature
I
1 Longitudinal diamond grinding.
II
1 Groove width.
III
1 Groove depth.
IV
1 Blade spacing.
Item Coverage - Longitudinal diamond grinding of concrete pavement surfaces
2.5
The items for longitudinal diamond grinding of concrete pavement surfaces shall be in accordance with
clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref 3.N] and include for:
a)
design in accordance with CD 227 [Ref 1.N] and Section 17 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N];
b)
reports and certificates;
c)
provision of data and drawings;
d)
re-submissions and modifications;
e)
amendments to the Works;
f)
obtaining approvals;
g)
everything necessary for the design, fabrication, installation and verification of the Works.

3. Fine milling of concrete pavement surfaces
3.
Fine milling of concrete pavement surfaces
Units
3.1
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
fine milling ............ square metre.
Measurement
3.2
The measurement of fine milling of concrete pavement surfaces shall be calculated using the width
stated in the Contract with no deductions made for openings of 1 square metre or less.
Itemisation
3.3
Separate items shall be provided for fine milling of concrete pavement surfaces in accordance with
clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 4.N] and the following:
Table 3.3 Itemisation for Fine milling of concrete pavement surfaces
Group
Feature
I
1 Fine milling
Item Coverage - Fine milling of concrete pavement surfaces
3.4
The items for fine milling of concrete pavement surfaces shall be in accordance with clause 2.1 of
GP 113 [Ref 3.N] and include for:
a)
re-shaping and rolling;
b)
shaping to cambers, falls and crowns;
c)
multiple handling of material;
d)
loading into transport;
e)
disposal of unacceptable earthworks material as clause 7.6 of CC 601/MMHW [Ref 6.N];
f)
working around drainage channels, chamber covers, gully gratings, expansion joints and the like;
g)
removing road studs not required for re-use;
h)
surface preparation and cleaning;
i)
cutting out and removal of material by other means;
j)
water supply and damping down;
k)
taking precautions to avoid damage to ironwork.
