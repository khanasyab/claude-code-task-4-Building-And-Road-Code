Introduction
Introduction
Background
This document provides the requirements for the design of the road lighting and associated electrical
asset. It specifically addresses the high level approach for lighting and electrical requirements as well
as the competencies of those involved in this area of the service.
The development of this document draws on the European Commission Green Public Procurement
Criteria for Street Lighting and Traffic Signals GPP [Ref 15.N] to inform the design process in order that
the lighting provision takes account of the task and performance criteria throughout its operational life,
and is designed, maintained and operated to be energy and sustainability efficient based upon a
whole-life costing and asset condition assessment.
This document supersedes TD 23/99, TD 34/07, TA 49/07 and IAN 167/12.
Assumptions made in the preparation of this document
The assumptions made in GG 101 [Ref 19.N] apply to this document.
This document is written for the competent lighting professional / practitioner in order that lighting
installations are designed that are suitable for motorway and all-purpose trunk roads.
Mutual recognition
Where there is a requirement in this document for compliance with any part of a "British Standard" or
other technical specification, that requirement may be met by compliance with the Mutual Recognition
clause in GG 101 [Ref 19.N].

Terms and definitions
Terms and definitions
Term
Definition
Competent practitioner
Typically qualified professionals able to work independently in
relevant fields, who has training and experience relevant to the
matter being addressed and an understanding of the requirements of
the particular task being approached, who are expected to apply
their own skill and judgement when making decisions involving the
information that the DMRB contains.
Conflict area
Conflict areas are typically junctions, intersections, roundabouts and
pedestrian crossings, where significant streams of motorised traffic
intersect with each other or with other road users such as
pedestrians and cyclists.
Feeder pillar
Defined within BS 7671 [Ref 36.N] Part 7. A door giving access to
electrical equipment and located less than 2.50 m above ground
level, locked with a key or requires a tool for access. This includes
pillars and electrical compartments in street lighting equipment such
as the base compartment of a column.
Geometry
Interrelated linear dimensions and characteristics of the road lighting
system, i.e. spacing, mounting height, transverse position and
arrangement.
Light pollution / Obtrusive
light
A term that describes the release of light that serves no useful
purpose as it falls outside the required area.
Light source
A component of the luminaire whose primary function is to produce
visible or near-visible radiant energy for general illumination and
includes LEDs and lamps.
Mounting height
Nominal vertical distance between the photometric centre of a road
lighting luminaire and the surface of the road.
OPDDS (O-PEN)
Open combined protective and neutral (PEN) conductor detection
devices (OPDDS) also termed Open protective earth neutral
(O-PEN) devices Open Protective Earth Neutral device.
Photobiological risk
The term applied to risk associated to the eye when exposed to blue
and short wavelength light sources.
Road lighting
A fixed lighting installation intended to provide good visibility to users
of outdoor public traffic areas during the hours of darkness to support
traffic safety, traffic flow and public security.
Road lighting zone
Section of the highway with a defined lighting performance criteria.
Stopping sight distance
Distance needed to bring a vehicle, driving at design speed, to a
complete standstill. Stopping sight distances are given in CD 109
[Ref 17.N] using the one step below desirable minimum distances.

1. Scope
1.
Scope
Aspects covered
1.1
Where the need for lighting has been established in TA 501 [Ref 39.N] this document shall be followed
for the design of new and replacement road lighting and short tunnels on motorways and all-purpose
trunk roads.
NOTE 1
The requirements for the assessment of the need for road lighting can be found in TA 501 [Ref 39.N].
NOTE 2
This document does not cover the requirements related to the design of lighting for long road tunnels
(refer to BS 5489-2 [Ref 3.N] and CD 352 [Ref 5.I]).
NOTE 3
For the requirements relating to equipment specification not directly related to photometric
performance, refer to the relevant clauses from TC 501 [Ref 10.N].
NOTE 4
A tunnel of length 200 m or less is a short tunnel.
NOTE 5
This document does not cover the design of road lighting power distribution networks.
Implementation
1.2
This document shall be implemented forthwith on all schemes involving the design of road lighting on
the Overseeing Organisations' motorway and all-purpose trunk roads according to the implementation
requirements of GG 101 [Ref 19.N].
Use of GG 101
1.3
The requirements contained in GG 101 [Ref 19.N] shall be followed in respect of activities covered by
this document.
Health and safety
1.4
To reduce the artificial optical radiation (AOR) hazard (photo-biological) risk only light sources with a
Risk Group rating of RG0 or RG1 in accordance with BS EN 62471 [Ref 30.N]shall be used.
Competency
1.5
All organisations and personnel delivering the road lighting design service shall be registered with an
appropriate professional institution, the Engineering Council (ECUK) and hold the competencies
relating to their discipline and role with a record made available to the Overseeing Organisation.
1.6
The lead in the lighting design process shall be at least Member of the Institution of Lighting
Professionals (MILP or equivalent), Incorporated Engineer (IEng) and competent in the design and
implementation of road lighting systems and associated infrastructure.
NOTE
Mutual recognition of professional qualifications is governed by UKPGA 2022/20 [Ref 34.N].

2. General requirements for road lighting design
2.
General requirements for road lighting design
Performance requirements for road lighting design
2.1
Lighting performance shall fulfil the requirements of BS 5489-1 [Ref 2.N], BS EN 13201-2 [Ref 42.N],
PD CEN/TR 13201-1 [Ref 14.I] and the requirements set out in this document.
2.2
National guidance relating to road lighting design shall be followed.
NOTE
National guidance includes guidance notes and professional lighting guides such as those published by
the Institution of Lighting Professionals (ILP), Society of Light & Lighting (SLL), Bat Conservation Trust
etc.
2.3
Lighting on motorway and all-purpose trunk roads shall mitigate obtrusive lighting, sky glow effects and
adverse impacts on fauna and flora in accordance with BS 5489-1 [Ref 2.N] road lighting and good
practice.
NOTE
Good practice guidance includes CIE 150 [Ref 10.I] Guide on the limitation of the effects of obtrusive
light from outdoor lighting installations, CIE 126 [Ref 11.I] Guidelines for minimising sky glow and ILP
GN01 [Ref 7.I] ILP Guidance notes for the reduction of obtrusive light.
2.4
Ecological surveys to mitigate potential fauna and flora impacts shall be undertaken in accordance with
LD 118 [Ref 1.N].
2.5
Luminaire design tilt shall be zero degrees achieving a optical G4 to G6 rating.
Core principles for road lighting design
2.6
The development and delivery of road lighting shall follow the European Commission Green Public
Procurement (GPP) Criteria for Street Lighting and Traffic Signals GPP [Ref 15.N].
2.7
The required task lighting performance and related environmental impact mitigation requirements
relating to the road lighting shall be established.
2.8
Luminaire circular economy compliance shall be determined against CIBSE TM66 [Ref 5.N].
NOTE
The required minimal TM66 CEAM Make value can be obtained from the Overseeing Organisation.
2.9
The lighting design shall be developed using a life cycle assessment coupled with a whole life costing
analysis.
2.10
Whole life costing shall follow BS ISO 15686-1 [Ref 1.I]based upon a study period equating to the
fatigue design life of a column.
2.11
The correct lighting system shall be chosen for the intended application and the installation optimised
for energy efficiency parameters in accordance with the requirements of this document and TA 501 [Ref
39.N].
NOTE
The most sustainable lighting installation when considering whole life costing is unlikely to be the most
cost effective to construct.
Non-quantifiable performance requirements for road lighting design
2.12
Installation appearance shall be pertinent to the landscape in which it is located.
NOTE
Installation appearance can be influenced by the choice of light source, luminaire optics at night and
