Introduction
Introduction
Background
The requirements and advice given in this document are based upon the guidance in BS EN 1295-1
[Ref 25.N]. Further guidance for the structural design of buried pipelines in the UK can be found in BS
9295 2010 [Ref 11.N].
BS EN 1295-1 [Ref 25.N] contains a National Annex A which states the vehicle loadings to be used for
pipeline design. These loadings were derived from BS 5400-2 2006 [Ref 24.N], which has since been
revised. The loadings reported in BS 5400-2 2006 [Ref 24.N] are still employed in pipeline design as
they give a more conservative result than loadings derived from newer standards.
The charts and tables given at the appendices are based on BS 5400-2 2006 [Ref 24.N] and BS 9295
2010 [Ref 11.N]. These charts and tables provide a simplified method for defining the pipe and bedding
combinations required for permitted vehicle loading requirements and to be compliant with the range of
permitted combinations given in CC 500 [Ref 8.N] and Section 4 of this document.
CC 500 [Ref 8.N] permits a range of pipes of different material properties to be used for drainage works
in highways. This document also allows a choice in the method of bedding pipes where the degree of
structural support given to the pipe when it is laid in a trench varies. Not all possible combinations of
pipe and bedding selected from the permitted options will be suitable for a particular design solution.
Permitted combinations are therefore specified for each contract. The charts given in the appendices to
this document are intended for a wide range of pipe bedding combinations for a range of manufactured
pipe thicknesses, possible trench widths and bedding factors.
This document supersedes the figures and requirements for pipe bedding previously shown in the
Highway Construction Details, F series (HCDs F-series).
This document provides requirements and advice used to develop the design outputs required to
deliver the works in accordance with CC 500 [Ref 8.N].
Assumptions made in the preparation of this document
The assumptions made in GG 101 [Ref 12.N] apply to this document.

Terms and definitions
Terms and definitions
Term
Definition
Bedding factor
Bedding factor ( fm ) is a multiple by which stresses in the pipe are reduced
by the load spreading properties of the bedding.
Flexible pipes
Pipes that deform to a significant extent before collapse
HA and HB loading
HA loading is loading representing normal traffic in Great Britain. HB loading
is an abnormal vehicle unit loading (source: DMRB CS 454 [Ref 1.I]).
Main road loading
Main road loading consists of 45 units of Type HB loading to BS 5400-2 2006
[Ref 24.N]. Field loading consists of two wheels of 60 kN with contact
pressure 400 kN/m2 . See Young & O'Reilly (TRRL) [Ref 1.N]
Rigid pipes
Pipes that fracture before significant deformation occurs

Scope
Aspects covered
1.1
The design and selection of type of pipe and bedding to meet the loading requirements from the
permitted alternatives in CC 500 [Ref 8.N] shall be in accordance with:
1)
the requirements in BS 9295 2010 [Ref 11.N]; or,
2)
the requirements in Sections 2, 3 and 4.
NOTE
The requirements presented in Sections 2, 3 and 4, in addition to the associated design charts and
guidance presented in Appendix A to this document, provide a simplified method for selecting pipe and
bedding combinations.
1.2
This document shall apply to the following type of pipes:
1)
vitrified clay pipes to BS EN 295-1 [Ref 30.N] to classes L 95, 120, 160 and 200, See BS 65 [Ref
31.N];
2)
precast concrete pipes classes to the following standards:
a)
BS 5911-1 [Ref 4.N];
b)
BS 5911-5 [Ref 3.N] (pre-stressed non pressure pipes and fittings); and,
c)
BS EN 1916 [Ref 5.N];
3)
thermoplastic pipes to the following standards:
a)
BS 4660 [Ref 28.N] (size 110 mm & 160 mm diameter);
b)
BS EN ISO 1452-1 [Ref 20.N];
c)
BS EN 1401-1 [Ref 13.N];
d)
BS EN 1852-1 [Ref 15.N];
e)
BS EN 12666-1 [Ref 14.N];
f)
BS EN 13476-1 [Ref 16.N];
g)
BS EN 13476-2 [Ref 17.N]; and,
h)
BS EN 13476-3 [Ref 18.N]
4)
ductile-iron pipes to BS EN 598 [Ref 9.N];
5)
cast-iron pipes to BS 437 [Ref 22.N]; and,
6)
glass reinforced plastics (GRP) pipes to BS EN ISO 23856 [Ref 19.N]
7)
corrugated steel pipes to BS EN 10346 [Ref 6.N] and AASHTO M274 [Ref 23.N].
NOTE
Refer to Appendix A for Figures A.1, A.2 and A.3 which detail bedding/pipe combinations.
1.3
Hydraulic design requirements for pipes are not covered in this document and shall be in accordance
with DMRB CG 501 [Ref 7.N].
1.4
This document shall apply to pipes up to 900 mm internal diameter.
1.5
Pipes greater than 900 mm diameter, or multi-cellular pipe structures, shall be designed with reference
to DMRB CG 300 [Ref 26.N].

requirements of GG 101 [Ref 12.N].

3.1.1
The design should include the full selection of alternative pipe and bedding combinations determined in
accordance with this document.
3.1.2
The impact of the roughness of an individual section of pipe on the flow should be included in the
system wide calculation.
3.1.3
Pipe diameters should be adjusted accordingly in order to accommodate changes in pipe material with
differing roughness coefficients.
3.1.4
One pipe type or material should be used within any individual drain between consecutive chambers.
NOTE 1
Pipes can be made of materials that deflect relatively little under load before cracking (rigid pipes) or of
materials that will tolerate large deflections under load before inward buckling occurs (flexible pipes).
NOTE 2
For pipe materials refer to this document and CC 500 [Ref 8.N].
NOTE 3
Pipe type is a carrier drain, filter drain or culvert.
NOTE 4
For surface level detail for filter drains refer to CD 524 [Ref 10.N].
NOTE 5
For geotextile requirements refer to CD 524 [Ref 10.N].
3.2

shall be assessed and the results set out within the report describing the technical basis of the design.

3.3.1
The maximum length of pipe between flexible joints may have to be limited where considerable
movement is expected.
3.3.2
Flexible and watertight joints should be specified in the following instances:
1)
in and under embankments;
2)
if differential settlement is expected in compressible soils subject to non-uniform loading.
3.3.3
Joints should have a smooth inner transition for rodding purposes.
NOTE 1
Rigid joints are made solid by caulking the sockets, or bolting together flanges integral with the pipes.
NOTE 2
Flexible joints are made with deformable rings or gaskets held between pipe spigots and sockets,
sleeves or collars.
NOTE 3
Consideration can be given to certain proprietary wrap type joints that are available.
NOTE 4
Most watertight joints will be flexible joints although rigid joints are occasionally used on clay pipes.
NOTE 5
For details of testing refer to CC 500 [Ref 8.N].
3.4
Joints in surface water drains shall be watertight or partly watertight.
3.4.1
Joints in pipes in soils that are predominantly fine sands or coarse silts should have watertight joints to
prevent soil particles passing through the joint into the pipe leaving voids on the outside of the pipe.
3.5
All drainage pipes on bridge structures shall be watertight and sealed.

General
4.1
A distinction shall be made between the requirements of bedding, haunching and surrounding, and
those of backfilling.
NOTE 1
Bedding, haunching and surrounding comprise all operations of trench fill up to a level 300 mm above
the top of the barrel of the pipe.
NOTE 2
Backfilling constitutes the remaining operations up to ground level in verges and open ground, and up
to formation or sub-formation level under carriageways.
NOTE 3
Work above formation level constitutes construction or reinstatement of the pavement.
4.2
Bed type materials shall be specified in accordance with CC 500 [Ref 8.N].

coefficient should be taken as 0.2.
4.35.4
Withdrawing trench support systems after the trench is completely backfilled should be avoided.
