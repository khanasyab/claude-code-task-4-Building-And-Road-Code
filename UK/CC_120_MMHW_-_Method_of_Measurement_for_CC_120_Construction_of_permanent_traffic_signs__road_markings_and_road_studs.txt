Foreword

Quantities and their method of measurement.

measurement, specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.

management system.

Measurement
4.2
The removal of permanent road markings shall only be measured where specifically required by the
Contract.
4.3
The marking and removal of permanent solid areas shall only be measured for the solid infilling
between converging lines, with the enclosing lines themselves measured as lines.
4.4
Permanent road markings which form part of a traffic signal installation or a controlled or uncontrolled
crossing shall not be separately measured.
4.5
Permanent road markings which require enhanced reflectorised marking materials stated in CC 120
[Ref 1.N] shall be identified and measured separately.

4. Permanent road markings
E/4.6
Permanent road markings other than those measured under clauses E/4.1 a) and E/4.1 c) of this
document shall be measured as lines and grouped together according to width.
NI/4.6
Permanent road markings other than those measured under clauses NI/4.1 a) and NI/4.1 c) of this
document shall be measured as lines and grouped together according to width.
S/4.6
Permanent road markings other than those measured under clauses S/4.1 a) and S/4.1 c) of this
document shall be measured as lines and grouped together according to width.
W/4.6
Permanent road markings other than those measured under clauses W/4.1 a) and W/4.1 c) of this
document shall be measured as lines and grouped together according to width.
4.7
In the case of permanent intermittent lines the measurement shall be of the marks only, with the length
of the mark and gap stated.
4.8
Permanent double lines shall be measured as two single lines.
4.9
Permanent diagonal lines between double lines and short transverse lines at the ends of any
longitudinal lines shall be measured with the lines of which they form part.
4.10
Permanent ancillary lines shall include lines forming hatched areas, chevrons, zigzag lines, boxed
areas and lines enclosing boxed areas.
4.11
In the case of hatched areas and chevrons the enclosing lines shall be measured as the longitudinal
line of which they form part.
4.12
The measurement of zigzag lines shall include any transverse or longitudinal lines at their ends.
4.13
The measurement of permanent circles with enclosing arrows (mini roundabouts) shall be for the
complete marking, the external diameter of the circle being stated, with distinctions made for all other
arrows between straight, curved, turning or double headed.
4.14
Permanent kerb markings shall be measured as a single item irrespective of the number of lines
forming any one marking.
4.15
Each permanent letter or numeral shall be separately measured with all letters or numerals grouped
together according to height.
4.16
Permanent symbols shall be measured separately and grouped together according to size.
4.17
The measurement of extra over preparation of concrete carriageway prior to the application of road
marking shall be the plan area of the size of the road markings to be applied.

10. Minimum visit charge
10.
Minimum visit charge
Units
10.1
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
minimum visit charge ............ shift.
Measurement
10.2
Minimum visit charges shall be measured once only for each day where the cumulative value of all
works to be undertaken by a Contractor during that period are less than the minimum visit charge for
that Contractor.
10.3
Where minimum visit charge is measured, all other works to be undertaken during that day shall not be
measured.
