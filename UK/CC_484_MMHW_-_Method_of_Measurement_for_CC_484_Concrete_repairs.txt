1. Establishment of plant for investigation of c...
1.
Establishment of plant for investigation of concrete condition
prior to concrete removal and substrate preparation
1.1
New concrete structures shall be measured under CC 482/MMHW [Ref 4.N].
1.2
Temporary scaffolding, access, encapsulation and support requirements shall be measured under GC
990/MMHW [Ref 6.N].
Units
1.3
The units of measurement shall be:
a)
establishment of plant for concrete condition investigation and testing ............ number.
Measurement
1.4
The measurement of establishment of plant for concrete condition investigation and testing shall be
measured once only to each structure to cover all non-destructive surveys and testing or all intrusive
surveys and testing prior to concrete removal and substrate preparation.
1.5
Any additional establishments to suit the Contractor's method of working shall not be measured.
Note
Due to the combined nature of investigations and tests, establishments are only measured once at
each structure and therefore there should be a maximum of two (non-destructive and intrusive) per
structure billed.
Itemisation
1.6
Separate items shall be provided for establishment of plant for a concrete condition investigation and
testing in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 3.N] and the following:
Table 1.6 Itemisation for Establishment of plant for concrete condition investigation and testing
Group
Feature
I
1 Establishment of plant for concrete condition investigation and testing.
II
1 For non-destructive surveys of concrete.
2 For intrusive surveys of concrete.
Item Coverage - Establishment of plant for concrete condition investigation and testing
1.7
The items for establishment of plant for concrete condition investigation and testing shall be in
accordance with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref 2.N] and include for:
a)
bringing plant and equipment to Site;
b)
obtaining access to the concrete area to be tested;
c)
setting up all plant and equipment and moving to different locations on site;
d)
dismantling and removing all plant and equipment from Site on completion.

4. Removal of concrete
4.
Removal of concrete
Units
4.1
The units of measurement shall be:
a)
trial areas ............ number.
b)
removal of concrete ............ litre or cubic metre.
Measurement
4.2
Measurement of the removal of concrete shall be the volume of the void obtained by the removal of
concrete required by the Contract or ordered by the Overseeing Organisation to be removed.
4.3
No deduction shall be made for:
a)
steel reinforcement;
b)
individual chamfers, splays, rebates, recesses, drips, grooves and the like of 100mm total girth or
less when measured overall the faces of the individual features formed in the concrete;
c)
in the case of concrete with a patterned face, any indentations of 100mm total girth or less when
measured overall the faces of the indentations formed in the concrete;
d)
holes, ducts, pipes, pockets, sockets, mortices, chambers, troughs and the like, not exceeding
0.15 cubic metres each in volume;
e)
cast in components not exceeding 0.15 cubic metres each.
Itemisation
4.4
Separate items shall be provided for removal of concrete in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4
of GP 112 [Ref 3.N] and the following:

10. Surface finish of concrete - Formwork for co...
10.
Surface finish of concrete - Formwork for concrete repairs
Note
Formwork associated with structural concrete used for concrete repairs such as pier strengthening
and/or replacement of structural elements should be measured under this section.
Units
10.1
The units of measurement shall be:
a)
formwork ............ square metre.
b)
void formers ............ linear metre.
Measurement
10.2
The measurement shall be the area of formwork which is in contact with the finished concrete but
measured over the face of openings of 1 square metre or less and features described in clause 10.3 c)
of this document below.
10.3
Formwork shall not be measured to:
a)
construction joints whether or not shown on the Drawings;
b)
holes, ducts, pockets, sockets, mortices and the like, not exceeding 0.15 cubic metres each in
volume;
c)
individual fillets, chamfers, splays, drips, rebates, recesses, grooves and the like of 100 mm total
girth or less when measured overall the faces in contact with the concrete;
d)
edges of blinding concrete 75 mm or less in thickness;
e)
upper surfaces of concrete inclined at an angle of less than 15° to the horizontal;
f)
unformed surfaces.
10.4
Where concrete, other than blinding concrete 75 mm or less in thickness, is placed in concrete
foundations, formwork shall be measured to the sides of such concrete foundations regardless of
whether or not any formwork is used, except where it is expressly stated on the Drawings that the
concrete is to be cast against the soil face.
10.5
The following shall apply for measurement of formwork:
a)
"horizontal" shall include formwork horizontal or inclined at any angle up to and including 5° to the
horizontal.
b)
"inclined" shall include formwork inclined at any angle more than 5° up to and including 85° to the
horizontal.
c)
"vertical" shall include formwork inclined at any angle more than 85° up to and including 90° to
the horizontal.
d)
"at any inclination" shall include formwork horizontal or inclined at any angle up to and including
90° to the horizontal.
10.6
The measurement of void formers shall be the length measured along the centre line of the void former,
and measured whether of a permanent or temporary nature.
