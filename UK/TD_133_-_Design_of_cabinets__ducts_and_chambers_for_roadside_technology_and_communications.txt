Introduction
Introduction
Background
Cabinets, ducts and chambers are used to support and house electrical and telecommunication
systems for roadside technology equipment, which supports operational services that monitor, inform
and direct customers, to enable safe and reliable journeys.
Assumptions made in the preparation of this document
The assumptions made in GG 101 [Ref 11.N] apply to this document.

Terms and definitions
Terms and definitions
Term
Definition
Asset owner
As defined in TD 132 [Ref 6.N].
Cabinet
A collective term for all cabinets for roadside technology and
communications, which includes electricity interface (EI) cabinets,
cabinets at the boundary, local power cabinets, communications
cabinets, transmission cabinets and power cabinets.
Cabinets at the boundary
A cabinet located at or within the highway boundary, used to
house interface equipment between distribution network
operators and third party telecommunications service providers.
Chamber
In this document, a chamber is an access point to
communications, power or other cable networks. Chambers on
the drainage network are not considered in this document.
NOTE: Examples of chambers are Type A, Type B, Type E and
jointing chamber, dimensions of which are defined in Section 5 of
this document.
Combined communications
cabinet
A variant of local power cabinet and communications cabinet
combined as a single cabinet.
NOTE: Examples of combined communications cabinets are the
Type 640 and Type 699 (in Scotland), combining functionality of a
Type 609 and Type 600 cabinet.
Communications
As defined in TD 132 [Ref 6.N].
Communications cabinet
Used to house the roadside (outstation) equipment and TSP
transmission and service delivery point equipment. It is a
weatherproof and environmentally controlled cabinet that can
accommodate a variety of equipment, including termination
frames, a power distribution unit, air conditioning, or heater and
fans.
Cross carriageway ducts (CCD)
Duct crossing a slip road or mainline carriageway, installed
underneath the road surface.
Ducts over gantries
Ducts over gantries comprise of both the buried ducts between
chambers and a gantry base and the above ground ducts routed
up and over the gantry, with access points at ground level to
facilitate the pulling of cables.
Duct
Ducts, sub-ducts or conduits used for installing the highway
communications cable network comprising communications,
power and other cables.
NOTE: Used as an overarching term for definitions contained with
BS EN 61386-1 [Ref 2.N].

Term
Definition
Ducts through structures (DTS)
Any duct installed within any structure, including:
1) bridge abutments;
2) bridge decks;
3) foundations for sign or signal gantries; or,
4) other technology equipment.
Electricity Interface (EI) cabinet
Interface point between the distribution network operator (DNO)
supply and the electrical distribution cables to road network.
Used to house the DNO cutout and main distribution board.
NOTE: In Scotland these are referred to as Termination Pillars.
Enforcement authority
As defined in CD 133 [Ref 8.N].
Flexible duct
Ducts of a construction that are designed to be coiled to a small
radius.
Hardened verge
Areas of verge where a duct is under a hard surface; for instance
a hard pavement or shallow encasement in concrete. This
applies where there are location-specific space restrictions, such
as retained cutting or where access pathways are provided.
Highway boundary
Edge of the land owned or managed by the Overseeing
Organisation.
Home Office Type Approval
As defined in CD 133 [Ref 8.N].
Information model
As defined in GG 184 [Ref 18.N].
Local duct
Duct connecting individual roadside technology equipment
between local cabinets and/or to end-device supporting structures
or to the longitudinal communications and power duct networks.
Local power cabinet
Used to house the sub-distribution board, providing power at
roadside technology equipment sites.
NOTE 1: In England these are referred to as rural or gantry.
NOTE 2: In Scotland these are referred to as Traffic Equipment
Distribution Pillars.
Longitudinal duct
Duct running along the length of the road to facilitate cables for
the transmission telecommunications service
Plinth
Used as a foundation to mount cabinets, typically made up of a
frame and skirt arrangement.
NOTE: An example of a plinth is the Type 610.
Power cabinet
A collective term for EI cabinets and local power cabinets, which
house the main distribution board and sub-distribution board.
Roadside technology equipment
As defined in TD 131 [Ref 17.N].

1. Scope
1.
Scope
Aspects covered
1.1
This document shall be used for the design of cabinets, ducts and chamber installations for roadside
technology and communications on motorways and all purpose trunk roads.
1.1.1
This document should be read in conjunction with the general requirements in GG 102 [Ref 13.N], GG
104 [Ref 15.N], GD 304 [Ref 9.N] and with all other DMRB documents relevant to the design of the
particular works to be undertaken.
Implementation
1.2
This document shall be implemented forthwith on all schemes involving cabinets, duct and chambers
for roadside technology and communications on the Overseeing Organisations' motorway and the
all-purpose trunk roads according to the implementations of GG 101 [Ref 11.N].
Use of GG 101
1.3
The requirements contained in GG 101 [Ref 11.N] shall be followed in respect of activities covered by
this document.

3.
Cabinets for roadside technology and communications
Provision of cabinets for roadside technology and communications
3.1
Cabinets shall be provided for housing roadside technology and communications equipment.
NOTE
Any requirements for housing of the TSP's transmission equipment is determined by the TSP, including
the internal environmental conditions, the amount of internal communications cabinet space required,
and the power supply and distribution requirements.
3.1.1
The combined communications cabinet for roadside technology and communications should be used
where site constraints allow to limit the number of cabinets deployed.
NOTE 1
Cabinet adaptation kits for power distribution in combined communications cabinets are described in
TR 2637 [Ref 1.I].
NOTE 2
The combined communications cabinet is referred to as the 640 cabinet or 699 cabinet in Scotland.
3.2
The shared use of cabinets for roadside technology and communications shall be agreed by all asset
owners and maintainers.
3.3
For cabinets at permanent traffic enforcement sites for roadside technology and communications, the
Overseeing Organisation and Enforcement Authority shall be consulted to determine the cabinet
requirements for the specific permanent traffic enforcement system.
3.4
Cabinets at permanent traffic enforcement sites for roadside technology and communications shall be
in accordance with the requirements of the Home Office Type Approval for the specific permanent
traffic enforcement system.
Siting of cabinets for roadside technology and communications
3.5
A local power cabinet and communications cabinet or enclosure housing roadside equipment for
roadside technology and communications shall be sited within view of, and readily accessible from, the
end device and any other communications cabinets it serves.
3.6
The siting of cabinets for roadside technology and communications that house TSP equipment and
SDPs shall be agreed with the TSP.
3.6.1
The proximity to and accessibility of longitudinal communications cables and cable joints should be
assessed when determining the siting of communications cabinets for roadside technology and
communications.
3.7
Cabinets shall be sited such that any communications cable connecting roadside technology equipment
and the TSP's SDP does not exceed 75% of the maximum cable length for the service supported.
3.8
Non-accessible faces of cabinet installations for roadside technology and communications, that are not
located at a boundary, shall have a minimum external clearance of one of the following options:
1)
Option 1: 250 mm clearance
2)
Option 2: 150 mm clearance.
3.8.1
Option 1 should be applied where site constraints allow.
3.8.2
Option 2 should only be applied at constraint sites with limited verge space where Option 1 cannot be
achieved.
3.9
Option 2 for shall be agreed with the TSP, asset owners and maintainers.
Cabinets at the boundary for roadside technology and communications
3.10
Cabinets at the boundary for roadside technology and communications shall be sited such that they are
accessible both from inside and outside the highway boundary.
NOTE 1
Access from within the highway boundary is required for the Overseeing Organisation's maintainers.

Cabinet internals for roadside technology and communications
3.22
Internal equipment layouts within cabinets for roadside technology and communications shall allow
access for installation and maintenance.
3.22.1
Access for installation and maintenance may include to the rear of the internal equipment within a
communications cabinet for roadside technology and communications.
3.23
Internal wiring and termination block arrangements shall allow for the connectivity of the equipment in
the communications cabinet for roadside technology and communications.
NOTE

3.23.1
Standardised cabinet internal wiring arrangements for roadside technology and communications should
be used throughout the scheme where possible.
3.24
Communications cabinets for roadside technology and communications shall be laid out, heated and
ventilated to maintain internal operating conditions within specified limits for all equipment contained
within the cabinet.
3.25

4.4

Where site conditions allow, ducts for roadside technology and communications should be sited within

4.4.2

4.7.1
For designs of new longitudinal infrastructure for roadside technology and communications where
overhead power lines, electric rail supplies or other power supply circuits run alongside a particular
verge, the longitudinal cable route should be designed in the opposite verge, where space is available

Provision of chambers for roadside technology and communications

replacement, modification and removal.

each end of a structure.

therefore chambers are required to allow cables to be installed through the structure. One chamber can
be used to accommodate the transition of ducts from the structure and one to accommodate the offset
to the line of the longitudinal ducts.
NOTE 2
Where there are chambers in structures, some structures can contain asbestos.
NOTE 3
The process and procedures for asbestos management are held within GG 105 [Ref 1.N].
NOTE 4
The requirements for a safety risk assessment are held within GG 104 [Ref 15.N].

one of the following options:

Option 1: Type A chamber;
2)
Option 2: Type B chamber;
3)
Option 3: Type E chamber;
4)
Option 4: Jointing chamber.

Option 1 should be selected where site constraints allow.
5.5.2
Option 2 should be selected at constraint sites and for Highways Agency Digital Enforcement and
Compliance System infrastructure requirements.
5.5.3
Option 3 may be used to interface with masts or poles to facilitate cable pulling.
NOTE
Type E chambers can be used to facilitate cable pulling where the end device is more than 30 m away

NOTE
Hazards include working in a confined space (in some locations toxic gas collects in the chamber) and
manual handling of chamber lids.
5.18.1
A standard design for each type of chamber for roadside technology and communications should be
used throughout the scheme.
5.19
Chambers for roadside technology and communications shall allow for the bending radius of power
cables and the containment of reduction joints at cabinet sites.
5.20
Bearers for cables and joints in chambers for roadside technology and communications shall be
provided for cable management.
5.21
Internal steps shall be provided for safe access where the invert depth exceeds 900 mm below the
finished surface of the carriageway or the adjacent ground.
5.22
A maintenance access area extending a minimum 600 mm from the side of the chamber lid for
roadside technology and communications shall be designed on all sides of chambers that do not have
a hinged lid.
5.23
Chambers with hinged lids for roadside technology and communications shall have a minimum 100 mm
clearance from the edge of the lid to any obstruction throughout the opening radius.
NOTE
Objects and the deflection zone of vehicle restraint system are examples of obstructions.
5.24
Drainage for all chambers for roadside technology and communications shall be designed such that
chambers and ducts remain free from standing water at all times.
5.24.1
A sump of 150 mm x 225 mm should be used unless determined otherwise by the ground conditions.
Type A chambers for roadside technology and communications
5.25
The maximum depth of a type A chamber for roadside technology and communications from the
underside of the cover to the internal base of the chamber excluding the sump, shall be 2000 mm.
5.26
Type A chambers for roadside technology and communications containing a communications cable
joint shall be equipped with 2 x 100 mm interrupter ports for network bypass cables.
5.27
Type A chambers or a group of Type A chambers for roadside technology and communications at a site
located on the longitudinal side of the carriageway shall be equipped with 2 x 100 mm interrupter ports
for network bypass cables.
5.28
Type A chambers for roadside technology and communications containing a power or communications
cable joint shall be a minimum 900 mm deep from the underside of the cover to the internal base of the
chamber, excluding the sump, for the design of cable supports.
5.29
Type A chambers for roadside technology and communications containing a power or communications
cable joint shall be provided with a cable joint bearer system.
5.30
A maximum of three cable joint enclosures shall be designed in any one chamber for roadside
technology and communications.
5.30.1
Where more than three cable joint enclosures are required, an additional type A chamber for roadside
technology and communications should be provided.

New cross-carriageway ducts for roadside technology and communications shall comprise a minimum
