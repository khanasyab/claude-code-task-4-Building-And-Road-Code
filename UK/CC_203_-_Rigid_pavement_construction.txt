Foreword
This document gives requirements for the construction of works for the Overseeing Organisation.
This document forms part of the works specification.
The works specification is made up of both the Specification for Highway Works and the Works Specific
Requirements completed by the Specifier.
This document is applicable to contracts throughout the UK, complemented by the additional
specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.
Users are responsible for applying all appropriate documents applicable to their contract.
Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

1.
General requirements for rigid pavement construction
1.1
Rigid pavement construction shall be as specified in CC 203/WSR/001.
1.2
Rigid pavement options shall be as described in CC 203/WSR/001.
1.3
Pavement course materials shall be as described in CC 203/WSR/001.
Surface levels requirements and verification of rigid pavement courses
1.4
On completion of each layer, the surface level of each rigid pavement course shall be at the design
level subject to the relevant tolerances stated in Table 1.4.
Table 1.4 Permitted deviation from design level for rigid pavement courses
Rigid pavement course
Permitted deviation from design
level
Surface slab
± 6 mm
Surface slab placed adjacent to existing surface water
channel
- 0 / + 10 mm
Base
± 15 mm
1.5
Verification shall be undertaken for the surface level of each rigid pavement course by measurement
against the datum level on a grid of points not more than 2 m spacing transversely and not more than
10 m spacing longitudinally.
1.6
The frequency of measurement of surface levels shall be once prior to overlay or opening to traffic.
1.7
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 15.N] shall apply to the measurement
of the surface level of each rigid pavement course.
1.8
Following the completion of surface slabs, surface water shall flow into the surface drainage system
without ponding.
Surface regularity requirements and verification of rigid pavement surface slabs
1.9
On the completion of the layer, the longitudinal regularity of surface slabs along any line parallel to the
edge of the pavement shall be within the limits in table 1.9.
Table 1.9 Longitudinal irregularity limits for surface slabs
Longitudinal irregularity
4 mm
7 mm
>10 mm
Number of permitted longitudinal irregularities per 300 m length
20
2
0
1.10
Where the length of surface slabs to be assessed against table 1.9 is less than 300 m, the number of
permitted longitudinal irregularities shall be that for a 300 m length, pro rata then rounded up to the
nearest whole number.
1.11
Verification shall be undertaken for the longitudinal regularity of surface slabs by testing continuously
along the entire length using a rolling straightedge or equivalent apparatus capable of measuring
irregularities over a 3 m length.
1.12
The frequency of longitudinal regularity testing shall be once prior to opening to traffic.
1.13
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 15.N] shall apply to the testing of the
longitudinal regularity of the surface slab.
1.14
Verification for the longitudinal regularity of surface slabs by testing shall be undertaken by an
accredited testing laboratory in compliance with "Accredited laboratory" in Section 16 of GC 101 [Ref
15.N].

1. General requirements for rigid pavement const...
1.15
On completion of the layer, the transverse regularity of surface slabs, measured as the difference
between the surface and the underside of a straightedge placed at right angles to the centre line of the
road, shall be not more than 3 mm.
1.16
Verification shall be undertaken for the transverse regularity of surface slabs by testing at 100 m
intervals using a 3 m straightedge and wedge in accordance with BS 8420 [Ref 23.N].
1.17
The frequency of transverse regularity testing shall be once prior to opening to traffic.
1.18
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 15.N] shall apply to the testing of
transverse regularity of the surface slab.
1.19
Verification for the transverse regularity of surface slabs by testing shall be undertaken by an accredited
testing laboratory in compliance with "Accredited laboratory" in Section 16 of GC 101 [Ref 15.N].
Surface preparation of rigid pavement layers prior to overlay with asphalt
1.20
Prior to overlay with asphalt, bond coat shall be applied to rigid pavement layers in accordance with BS
594987 [Ref 4.N] at a residual rate of not less than 0.35 kg/m2.
1.21
Verification shall be undertaken for the rate of spread of bond coat by testing in accordance with BS
594987 [Ref 4.N].
1.22
The frequency of rate of spread of bond coat testing shall be once per week.
1.23
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 15.N] shall apply to the testing of the
rate of spread of bond coat.
1.24
At the point of the application of bond coat to rigid pavement layers, the surface shall be free from
standing water, loose materials and foreign matter.
Horizontal alignments of rigid pavements
1.25
On completion, the horizontal alignment of the edge of the pavement surface shall be at the design
alignment within the following tolerances: ± 25 mm.
Rectification of rigid pavement layers
1.26
The depth of rectification of rigid pavement layers shall be to the full depth of the layer.
1.27
Where the full depth of the rigid pavement layer is to be removed and replaced for the purposes of
rectification, a minimum of a full lane width or full slab width shall be removed and replaced.
1.28
Where the full depth of the rigid pavement layer is to be removed and replaced for the purposes of
rectification, the rectification shall be a minimum of 15 m or a minimum of one slab in length.

2.2
Pavement quality concrete for CRCB and CRCP shall comply with "Pavement quality concrete" in

14. Filler board for concrete pavements
14.
Filler board for concrete pavements
General requirements and verification for filler board for concrete pavements
14.1
Filler board for concrete pavements used at expansion joints and isolation joints shall have a thickness
of 25 ± 1.5 mm, unless otherwise stated in CC 203/WSR/009.
14.2
Filler board for concrete pavements which has a thickness other than 25 mm shall be as specified in
CC 203/WSR/009.
14.3
Filler board for concrete pavements shall have a ridged top.
Resistance to weathering
14.4
Filler board for concrete pavements shall be free from disintegration or shrinkage after being subjected
to resistance to weathering testing.
14.5
Verification shall be undertaken for the resistance to weathering of filler board for concrete pavements
by testing in accordance with the procedure in Requirement 14.8.
14.6
The frequency of filler board resistance to weathering testing shall be one per source.
14.7
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 15.N] shall apply to the filler board
resistance to weathering testing.
14.8
The resistance to weathering of the filler board shall be assessed using the following procedure:
1)
Place three square 115 mm ± 2.5 mm specimens in a ventilated drying oven for 7 days at 55 ± 5
°C.
2)
Remove the specimens from the oven and immediately immerse in water at 20 ± 2 °C for 24
hours.
3)
Place the specimens vertically on edge in a water tight test pan with a ribbed bottom and slotted
lid, such that the specimens cannot fall onto their sides.
4)
Fill the pan with water so that half the depth of the specimens are submerged.
5)
Freeze the specimens so that they are at least -7 °C for 4 hours.
6)
Place the pan in a water bath at 28 ± 10 °C until 1 hour after the samples have thawed.
7)
Repeat Steps 5 to 6 five times.
8)
Remove the specimens from the pan and air dry at room temperature for 48 hours.
9)
Inspect the specimens for signs of disintegration or shrinkage.
Recovery from compression
14.9
Filler board for concrete pavements shall have a thickness recovery from compression of not less than
70% after being subjected to compression and recovery testing.
14.10
Filler board for concrete pavements shall have a reduction of mass of not more than 1% after being
subjected to compression and recovery testing.
14.11
Verification shall be undertaken for the recovery from compression of filler board for concrete
pavements by testing in accordance with the procedure in Requirement 14.17.
14.12
The frequency of filler board recovery from compression testing shall be one per source.
14.13
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 15.N] shall apply to the filler board
recovery from compression testing.
14.14
Verification shall be undertaken for the reduction of mass of filler board for concrete pavements by
testing in accordance with the procedure in Requirement 14.17.
14.15
The frequency of filler board reduction in mass testing shall be one per source.
