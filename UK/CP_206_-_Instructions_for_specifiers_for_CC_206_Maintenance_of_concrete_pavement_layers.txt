Foreword
This document provides specifier instructions for the production of the works specific requirements for
CC 206 Maintenance of concrete pavement layers.
This document does not form part of the works specification.
The works specification is made up of both the Specification for Highway Works and the works specific
requirements completed by the Specifier.
This document is applicable for contracts throughout the UK, complemented by the additional
specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.
Users are responsible for applying all appropriate documents applicable to their contract.
Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

3. Lower strength concrete for pavements
Installation requirements for lower strength concrete for pavements
3.20
The installation of reinforcement for lower strength concrete for pavements shall comply with
Installation requirements for reinforcement for concrete pavements in "Reinforcement for concrete
pavement layers" in Section 8 of CC 203 [Ref 13.N].
3.21
The cover to any reinforcement from the layer surface in lower strength concrete for pavements shall
be 50 ± 10 mm.
3.22
At the point of placement, the substrate to receive the lower strength concrete shall be free from foreign
matter, standing water and ice.
3.23
Lower strength concrete for pavements shall be placed, compacted and finished before the concrete
has set and while the concrete is still workable.
3.24
Lower strength concrete for pavements shall be compacted such that it is free from entrapped air.
Curing and protection of lower strength concrete for pavements
3.25
Immediately after finishing, the lower strength concrete layer shall be cured and protected in
accordance with BS EN 13670 [Ref 8.N].
3.26
The duration of the curing and protection of the lower strength concrete layer shall be in accordance
with the requirements of curing class 4 to BS EN 13670 [Ref 8.N].
3.27
Any curing compound that is applied to the lower strength concrete layer shall be removed before
overlay, unless the curing compound does not adversely affect the adhesion of the overlay to the lower
strength concrete layer.
3.28
The surface temperature of the lower strength concrete layer shall be prevented from falling below 0 °C
until the compressive strength has reached 5 MPa, when measured in accordance with BS EN 13670
[Ref 8.N].
3.29
The lower strength concrete layer shall not be trafficked until the compressive strength has reached
70% of the characteristic 28 day compressive strength.
3.30
Trafficking of the lower strength concrete layer shall be limited to construction equipment used to
construct the overlying layers.
Joints in lower strength concrete for pavements
3.31
Transverse joints shall be sawn in the hardened lower strength concrete layer before natural cracks
develop in the layer, at 3 m spacing ± 150 mm, unless otherwise stated in CC 206/WSR/003.
Works Specific Requirements to be provided within the WSR template where applicable are:
SI.3.31
Transverse joints in the hardened lower strength concrete layer shall [enter free text].
3.32
Where the lower strength concrete layer is placed in individual construction widths exceeding 4.75 m,
longitudinal joints shall be sawn, before natural cracks develop in the layer, to be located outside of
wheel track zones and not more than 4.75 m apart.
3.33
The joint grooves sawn into the lower strength concrete layer shall be not more than 5 mm wide.
3.34
The depth of the joint groove sawn into the lower strength concrete layer shall be 1/4 to 1/3 of the
nominal layer thickness.
End product requirements for lower strength concrete for pavements
3.35
At the point of the construction of the overlying layers, the surface level of the lower strength concrete
shall be in accordance with table 3.35.

15.7
Transverse saw-cuts in jointed reinforced concrete slabs prior to the crack and seat operation shall not
be made within 500 mm of existing transverse cracks or joints.
15.8
Transverse saw-cuts in jointed reinforced concrete slabs prior to the crack and seat operation shall be 3
mm ± 1 mm wide.
15.9
Transverse saw-cuts in jointed reinforced concrete slabs prior to the crack and seat operation shall be
not more than half the slab thickness.
15.10
The transverse saw-cuts in jointed reinforced concrete slabs prior to the crack and seat operation shall
sever the reinforcement.
15.11
Debris and slurry in the saw-cuts and on the slab surface produced by the saw-cutting operation shall
be removed prior to the crack and seat operation.
Requirements, verification and documentation for cracking of jointed reinforced
concrete slabs
15.12
Cracks shall be induced in the jointed reinforced concrete slab at the location of the saw-cuts through
one strike of the impact blade on the breaker equipment.
15.13
The edge of the impact blade on the breaker equipment during cracking shall be not less than 0.5 m
from the edge of the jointed reinforced concrete slab.
15.14
The induced cracks in the jointed reinforced concrete slab shall be to the full width of the bay.
15.15
Following induced cracking, the saw-cut arrises in the jointed reinforced concrete slab shall be free
from spalling.
15.16
Following induced cracking, cores extracted from the cracking locations shall have single induced
cracks extending from the base of the saw-cut to the bottom of the concrete slab.
15.17
Verification shall be undertaken for the crack pattern and saw-cut depth of the saw-cut and cracked
jointed concrete slab by assessment of 150 mm nominal diameter cores taken centred on the
transverse saw-cut and crack locations.
15.18
The frequency of crack pattern and saw-cut depth assessment from cores shall be 1 per 300 m2.
15.19
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 9.N] shall apply to the assessment of
the pattern and saw-cut depth of the saw-cut and cracked jointed concrete slab.
15.20
The following documentation shall be submitted following the completion of the saw-cut, crack and seat
of jointed reinforced concrete slabs: core logging report.
15.21
Following induced cracking, the jointed reinforced concrete slab shall be free from longitudinal cracks
situated in wheel track zones that intersect the transverse saw-cuts, where wheel track zones are 600
mm wide with the inside edges offset from the centre of the lane by 720 mm.
15.22
The following Documentation for saw-cut, crack and seat of jointed reinforced concrete slabs shall be
submitted as continuous records: for the cracking operation: Blade length and weight, drop height for
each crack.
15.23
The requirements of "Records" in Section 3 of GC 101 [Ref 9.N] shall apply to saw-cut, crack and seat
of jointed reinforced concrete slabs.
Requirements and documentation of seating of saw-cut and cracked jointed
reinforced concrete slabs
15.24
Following induced cracking, the jointed reinforced concrete slab shall be seated with not less than 6
passes from a pneumatic-tyred roller with a weight of not less than 20 tonnes.
15.25
The following Documentation for the seating of saw-cut and cracked jointed reinforced concrete slabs
shall be submitted as continuous records: for the seating operation: number of roller passes per area.

16.
Surface preparation of concrete pavement layers prior to asphalt
overlay
General requirements for surface preparation of concrete pavement layers prior
to asphalt overlay
16.1
Prior to an asphalt overlay, a bond coat shall be applied to the surface of concrete pavement layers in
accordance with BS 594987 [Ref 1.N] at a residual rate of not less than 0.35 kg/m2.
16.2
Verification shall be undertaken for the rate of spread of bond coat by testing in accordance with BS
594987 [Ref 1.N].
16.3
The frequency of rate of spread of bond coat testing shall be once per week.
16.4
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 9.N] shall apply to the testing of the
rate of spread of bond coat.
16.5
At the point of application of the bond coat, concrete pavement layers to be overlaid shall be free from
loose material, foreign matter and standing water.
