1.
General requirements for bridge deck expansion joints and
sealing of gaps in bridges and structures
1.1
Storage and installation of joints, jointing materials, sealants and other associated items shall be in
accordance with the manufacturer's recommendations.
1.2
The same joint system, seal or sealant shall continue across the full width of the deck including
footway, verge, hard strip, hard shoulder and central reserve.
1.3
Different joint systems shall not be combined at one end of a span.

2. Bridge deck expansion joints
2.
Bridge deck expansion joints
Bridge deck expansion joints general requirements
2.1
The bridge deck expansion joints shall be as specified in CC 490/WSR/002.
Bridge deck expansion joints product requirements
2.2
Bridge deck expansions joints shall be joint systems which are either compliant with ETAG 032 2013
[Ref 3.N] or have a legacy Departmental Type Approval.
2.3
Bridge deck expansion joint systems with a legacy Departmental Type Approval shall be compliant with
the performance characteristics of CD 357 [Ref 1.N].
2.4
Bridge deck expansion joints in accordance with ETAG 032 2013 [Ref 3.N] shall be compliant with the
performance characteristics as set in Appendix B of CD 357 [Ref 1.N].
2.5
The requirements for product compliance in Section 10 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to any
component of the joint system that falls within the scope of a designated standard.
2.6
Seals which are not held in metal runners shall remain in compression for the full length of the joint
movement.
Bridge deck expansion joints product verification
2.7
Bridge deck expansion joints in accordance with ETAG 032 2013 [Ref 3.N] shall be compliant with the
requirements for product conformity assessment and certification in Section 10 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N].
2.8
The following Documentation shall be submitted for compliance with the performance requirements for
bridge decks joints that have a legacy Departmental Type Approval prior to the commencement of the
joint installation: Manufacturer's joint registration certificate confirming the approval of the proposed
joint system.
2.9
The following Documentation shall be submitted for bridge deck expansion joints in accordance with
ETAG 032 2013 [Ref 3.N] prior to the commencement of bridge joint installation: Declaration of
Performance for the joint system as set out in Appendix B of CD 357 [Ref 1.N].
Bridge deck expansion joints installation requirements
2.10
Bridge deck expansion joints shall be installed in accordance with the manufacturer's requirements.
2.11
Holding down bolts or anchorage pockets shall be accurately located and secured in the concrete to
the correct line and level in accordance with the joint manufacturer's requirements.
2.12
Threaded parts of holding down bolts and anchorage systems shall be protected from damage, kept
clean and free from rust.
2.13
The concrete substrate of the bridge deck shall not be damaged by the installation of the bridge deck
expansion joint.
2.14
Surfacing on either side of the bridge deck expansion joint shall be cut over its full depth to the
alignment required for the joint.
2.15
Deck waterproofing shall be cut clean and to the alignment of the bridge deck joint.
2.16
Bridge deck expansion joints shall be installed on concrete surfaces which are free from laitance,
sound, clean and comply with the manufacturer's requirements.
2.17
The bridge deck expansion joint and the bridge deck waterproofing shall be formed so that a watertight
seal is provided to prevent the passage of water through the joint or waterproofing systems.
2.18
The two sides of the bridge deck joint gap shall be set parallel to each other.
2.19
Bridge deck expansion joints shall be set to a uniform width and alignment in accordance with
manufacturer's requirements.

2. Bridge deck expansion joints
2.20
Bridge deck expansion joints shall be finished and aligned with the profile of the adjacent surfacing over
its full length.
2.21
The maximum vertical displacement between the joint and adjacent surfacing shall be 1.3mm for buried
joints and 3mm for all other joint types in accordance with CD 357 [Ref 1.N].
2.22
Bridge deck expansion joints shall be undamaged during the works.
2.23
Bridge deck expansion joints shall not be loaded during installation.
2.24
Bridge deck expansion joints shall not be loaded upon completion of the joint installation until the joint
materials achieve the strength recommended by the manufacturer.
2.25
The drainage system for water management shall be installed in accordance with the joint
manufacturer's requirements.
2.26
The drainage system for water management shall be accessible for cleaning..
2.27
The drainage system for water management shall be free of debris and any obstruction upon
completion of the joint installation.
2.28
Surface preparation and protection of metal components of bridge deck expansion joints shall comply
with the requirements of CC 486 [Ref 8.N] and the joint manufacturer's instructions.
Bridge deck expansion joints installation verification
2.29
Verification shall be undertaken for the joint installation by inspection in accordance with CS 450 [Ref
4.N].
2.30
The frequency of joint installation inspection shall be two inspections the first inspection upon
completion and the second inspection 3 months after installation.
2.31
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to joint installation
inspections.
Bridge deck expansion joints documentation requirements
2.32
The following Documentation shall be submitted for the joint system prior to the commencement of the
joint installation: maintenance manual for the bridge deck expansion joint system.
2.33
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to the joint
maintenance manual.

3.
Sealing of gaps in bridges and structures
Sealing of gaps in bridges and structures general requirements
3.1
The sealing of gaps in bridges and structures shall be as specified in CC 490/WSR/003.
Sealing of gaps in bridges and structures product requirements
3.2
Joint filler and sealant materials shall be compliant with BS EN 14188-3 [Ref 5.N] BS EN 14188-2 [Ref
6.N] BS EN 14188-1 [Ref 7.N].
3.3
The joint filler and sealant materials shall meet the performance characteristics as stated in table 3.3.

3. Sealing of gaps in bridges and structures
3.10
Filler board for expansion joints shall have a recovered thickness of at least 70% of the original
thickness and a reduction of mass of not more than 1% after being subject to compression and
recovery testing.
3.11
Filler board for expansion joints shall have an extrusion of its free edge under compression of less than
6 mm.
Sealing of gaps in bridges and structures product verification requirements
Verification requirements for resistance to weathering
3.12
Verification shall be undertaken for the resistance to weathering of filler boards by testing in accordance
with the following procedure:.
1)
place three square 115mm +/- 2.5 mm specimens in a ventilated drying oven at 55 +/- 5 oC for 7
days;
2)
after 7 days, remove the specimens from the oven and immediately immerse in water at room
temperature of between 16oC and 20oC for 24 hours;
3)
place the specimens vertically on edge in a watertight test pan with a ribbed bottom and slotted
lid, such that the specimens cannot fall onto their sides;
4)
fill the test pan with water so that half the depth of the specimens are submerged;
5)
freeze the specimens to -7 oC or below for 4 at least hours after initial freezing of the water in the
test pan;
6)
place the test pan in a water bath maintained at 18oC to 38oC until 1 hour after the samples have
thawed;
7)
repeat steps 5 to 6 five times;
8)
remove the specimens from the pan and air dry at room temperature of between 16oC and 20oC
for 48 hours; and,
9)
inspect the specimens for signs of disintegration or shrinkage.
3.13
The frequency of testing of filler boards for resistance to weathering shall be one test per source.
3.14
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to resistance to
weathering for filler boards..
Verification requirements for compression recovery
3.15
Verification shall be undertaken for the recovery from compression of filler board for expansion joints by
testing in accordance with the following procedure:-.
1)
trim two of the specimens subjected to resistance to weathering testing, and two new specimens
to 100 mm x 100mm +/- 0.5mm;
2)
record the mass of the two new specimens;
3)
maintain the specimens at room temperature of between 16oC and 20oC for 24 hours;
4)
three times at 24 hours intervals, apply a compression load between 0.7 N/mm2 to 4 N/mm2
evenly across the specimens at a rate of strain of 1.3 mm per minute until the specimens have
been compressed to 50% of their thickness, immediately releasing the load when the degree of
compression is reached;
5)
allow the specimens to recover for 30 minutes;
6)
measure the recovered thickness of each specimen;
7)
record the recovered thickness as a percentage of the original thickness to an accuracy of 25
microns and,
8)
record the mass of the two new specimens.
3.16
The frequency of filler board recovery from compression shall be one per source.

3. Sealing of gaps in bridges and structures
3.17
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to the recovery from
compression testing.
Verification requirements for extrusion
3.18
Verification shall be undertaken for the extrusion under compression of the filler board for expansion
joints by testing in accordance with the following procedure:-.
1)
trim one of the specimens subjected to the resistance to weathering test to 100 mm x 100mm
+/- 0.5 mm;
2)
mount the specimen in an extrusion mould of dimensions 100 mm x 100 mm (+ 0.5mm, - 0 mm)
internally, that is open at one end;
3)
apply a uniform load between 0.7 N/mm2 evenly across the specimens at a rate of strain of 1.3
mm per minute to compress the filler board to 50% of its original thickness with three of its edges
restrained; and,
4)
record the extrusion of the filler board above the top of the mould to an accuracy of 25 microns.
3.19
The frequency of filler board extrusion under compression testing shall be one per source.
3.20
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to the testing of the
extrusion under compression of filler board for expansion joints.
Sealing of gaps in bridges and structures installation requirements
3.21
Joint filler and sealant materials shall be installed in accordance with the manufacturer's instructions.
3.22
Joint sealants shall not suffer from cracking upon installation.
3.23
Immediately before sealing, the sides of the joint gap shall be clean, dry and free from loose material.
3.24
The joint gap shall be free of concrete or other unintended material projections.
3.25
Where a bond breaker in the form of a compressible strip is placed between the filler board and the
sealant it shall be compatible with both materials.
3.26
All seals on a continuous length of joint shall be poured or fixed in one continuous length.
3.27
Joints proposed in the seal shall be located and formed in accordance with the manufacturer's
instructions.
3.28
Gunned or poured sealants shall not adhere to the exposed concrete surfaces either side of the joint.
3.29
The concrete shall be left in a clean and unstained state once the installation is complete.
Sealing of gaps in bridges and structures installation verification
3.30
Verification shall be undertaken for sealing of gaps by inspection in accordance with CS 450 [Ref 4.N].
3.31
The frequency of inspection shall be once for each length of gap sealed.
3.32
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to the sealing of gaps.
