Site preparation and clearance

Summary
This document gives requirements for site clearance; environmental and cultural heritage
protection and preservation; explosives and blasting; and hazards and hazardous materials.
This document incorporates specific requirements for National Highways. Alternative versions of this document are
available for other Overseeing Organisations.
Feedback and Enquiries
Users of this document are encouraged to raise any enquiries and/or provide feedback on the content and usage of
this document to the dedicated National Highways team. The online feedback form for all enquiries and feedback
can be accessed at: www.standardsforhighways.co.uk/feedback.
This is a controlled document.

Foreword
Foreword
This document gives requirements for the construction of works for the Overseeing Organisation.
This document forms part of the works specification.
The works specification is made up of both the Specification for Highway Works and the Works Specific
Requirements completed by the Specifier.
This document is applicable to contracts throughout the UK, complemented by the additional
specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.
Users are responsible for applying all appropriate documents applicable to their contract.
Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

1. Site clearance
1.
Site clearance
1.1
Site clearance extent shall be as stated in GC 109/WSR/001.
Management of material assets and waste
1.2
The mitigation and enhancement that applies to clearance shall comply with the mitigation as stated as
a result of the assessment carried out under "Management of material assets and waste" in Section 2
of GC 103 [Ref 6.N].
Clearance, demolition and removal
1.3
The extent and timing of the clearance of each part of the site shall be as specified in GC 109/WSR/001.
Above ground buildings, structures, and obstructions
1.4
The buildings, structures, and obstructions to be broken up, demolished and / or removed shall be as
specified in GC 109/WSR/001.
Underground structures, chambers and foundations
1.5
Superfluous and/or no longer required underground structures, chambers and foundations that are to
be broken up, demolished and removed shall be as specified in GC 109/WSR/001.
1.6
Superfluous and/or no longer required underground structures, chambers and foundations that are not
to be completely removed shall be cleaned out and filled using general fill material to comply with
"General earthworks construction" in Section 5 of CC 601 [Ref 5.N].
1.7
Material used to fill superfluous and / or no longer required underground structures, chambers and
foundations shall be compacted in accordance with "Compaction of earthworks fill" in Section 6 of
CC 601 [Ref 5.N].
Imported fill
1.8
Imported fill shall be in accordance with the requirements for fill in "Earthworks materials" in Section 2
of CC 601 [Ref 5.N].
Superfluous structures
1.9
Superfluous and/or no longer required underground structures, chambers and foundations that are not
to be completely removed shall be free draining and not form a barrier to the horizontal or vertical
movement of water.
1.10
Superfluous and/or no longer required underground structures, chambers and foundations that are not
removed shall be perforated with holes made at 500mm centres to facilitate drainage unless stated in
GC 109/WSR/001.
Disused soil and surface water drains, sewers, cables and ducts
1.11
All disused soil and surface water drains, sewers, cables and ducts together with any bed, haunch or
surround within 1 m of formation level shall be be removed unless otherwise stated in
GC 109/WSR/001.
1.12
The ends of existing drains and sewers arising as a result of alterations of the drainage layout shall be
sealed to comply with "Drainage pipes" in Section 1 of CC 500 [Ref 4.N].
1.13
Disused larger pipes, culverts, chambers or other voids where there is a risk of collapse and / or
subsidence shall be either excavated and the trench backfilled as stated in GC 109/WSR/001.
1.14
Disused larger pipes, culverts, chambers or other voids where there is a risk of collapse and/or
subsidence that are to be excavated and the trench backfilled shall be excavated and backfilled in
accordance with CC 601 [Ref 5.N].

1. Site clearance
1.15
Disused larger pipes, culverts, chambers or other voids where there is a risk of collapse and / or
subsidence that are to be grouted shall be grouted in accordance with "Drainage pipes" in Section 1 of
CC 500 [Ref 4.N].
1.16
All trenches that are no longer required shall be back-filled to comply with Class 1, 2, or 3 fill material
unless otherwise stated in GC 109/WSR/001.
1.17
Class 1, 2, 3 fill material shall comply with "Acceptable earthwork material classes, properties, and
frequency of testing" in Section 3 of CC 601 [Ref 5.N].
Superficial obstructions
1.18
Superficial obstructions that are in the way or otherwise affected by the permanent works shall be be
broken up, demolished and removed down to ground level unless otherwise stated in
GC 109/WSR/001.
Adjoining buildings and structures
1.19
Buildings or structures adjoining those to be demolished shall be preserved and protected to maintain
their integrity during and after demolition.
1.20
Additional measures identified as necessary to maintain building / structures integrity during and after
demolition of buildings, or structures adjoining those to be demolished shall be as specified in
GC 109/WSR/001.
1.21
The additional treatment(s) to preserve buildings / structures adjoining those to be demolished shall be
as specified in GC 109/WSR/001.
Treatment of terminations of existing boundaries
1.22
Where the line of an existing fence, hedge or wall is cut by the site boundary, the severance shall be be
made good; either by the continuation of the fence,hedge or wall in a different direction, or by its
termination unless otherwise stated in GC 109/WSR/001.
1.23
Where the existing fence is a strained wire or chain link fence, a straining post shall be installed and the
fence re-strained.
Asbestos
1.24
A refurbishment and demolition survey in accordance with HSG 264 [Ref 2.N] and GG 105 [Ref 1.N]
shall be carried out prior to the commencement of any refurbishment, demolition or intrusive work.
1.25
The following Documentation shall be submitted for the presence of asbestos prior to the
commencement of all clearance works: A refurbishment and demolition survey in accordance with
HSG 264 [Ref 2.N] and GG 105 [Ref 1.N].
1.26
Buildings and structures that are to be demolished or partially demolished and are subject to control of
asbestos regulations UKSI 2012/632 [Ref 9.N] shall be as described in GC 109/WSR/001.
E/1.27
All works in locations identified in the Asbestos Management Plan shall be carried out in accordance
with the Asbestos Management Plan and the control of asbestos regulations ( UKSI 2012/632 [Ref
9.N]).
Statutory Undertakers & publicly owned services
1.28
All redundant drains, services and supplies shall be disconnected and properly sealed off in
accordance with the measures required by the Statutory Undertaker, the management of other publicly
owned services, or owners of privately owned services or supplies unless stated in GC 109/WSR/001.
Material arisings
1.29
All materials arising from site clearance which are not designated for reuse, not required, or
unacceptable for use in the permanent works shall be disposed of unless stated in GC 109/WSR/001.

1. Site clearance
1.30
Any materials arising from site clearance that are deemed required or acceptable for reuse that are
damaged during removal shall be replaced with materials of equivalent quality to the original materials.
1.31
Voids left by items that have been removed shall be back-filled unless otherwise stated in
GC 109/WSR/001.
1.32
Backfill for voids shall comply with CC 601 [Ref 5.N].
Topsoil
1.33
Topsoil excavated on site shall be reserved for re-use in accordance with CC 601 [Ref 5.N].
1.34
As the final part of site clearance, the excavated topsoil shall be spread over the disturbed ground, any
surplus being disposed in accordance "General earthworks construction" in Section 5 of CC 601 [Ref
5.N].
Road markings
1.35
All existing road markings and road studs on carriageways open to traffic that are either inconsistent
with scheme carriageway layout or a hazard to traffic shall be removed as soon as they become
superfluous or a hazard to traffic unless otherwise stated in GC 109/WSR/001.
1.36
Where existing road markings and/or road studs on the carriageway have been removed, the
carriageway shall be reinstated, unless otherwise stated in GC 109/WSR/001.
1.37
The removal of road markings shall comply with "Temporary road markings" in Section 3 of CC 130
[Ref 3.N].

2. Environmental and cultural heritage protectio...
2.
Environmental and cultural heritage protection and preservation
Quality management schemes
2.1
Archaeological, cultural, ecological and environmental preservation shall be carried out by
organisations registered to and operating in compliance with a quality management scheme in
accordance with "Quality management schemes" in Section 7 of GC 101 [Ref 7.N].
2.2
The following Documentation shall be submitted for all works prior to the commencement of
archaeological, cultural, ecological and environmental preservation works: proof of registration or
evidence of membership of an appropriate quality management scheme, such as NHSS 18.
2.3
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 7.N] shall apply to quality
management scheme for archaeological, cultural, ecological and environmental preservation works.
Environmental management plans
2.4
Environmental site clearance measures and restrictions shall be as stated in the environmental
management plans and mitigation requirements of "Environmental, cultural and habitat management"
in Section 1 of GC 103 [Ref 6.N].
Cultural heritage assessment and mitigation requirements
2.5
Cultural heritage assessment and mitigation of clearance works shall be as stated in the cultural
heritage assessment and mitigation requirements of "Environmental, cultural and habitat management"
in Section 1 of GC 103 [Ref 6.N].
Habitat regulations
2.6
Mitigation measures identified under the Habitat Regulations shall be as stated in the habitat
regulations assessment and mitigation requirements of "Environmental, cultural and habitat
management" in Section 1 of GC 103 [Ref 6.N].
Other mitigation measures and restrictions
2.7
Other environmental, ecological and archaeological measures and restrictions, shall be as stated in
GC 109/WSR/002.
Preservation of habitats, trees, shrubs, features and other areas.
2.8
Any area of habitat such as grassland or woodland, individual tree or shrub, that are required to be
preserved, eg. for landscaping purposes or because of Tree Preservation Orders shall be as listed in
GC 109/WSR/002.
2.9
Trees, bushes and hedges shall be that are to be preserved are listed in GC 109/WSR/002.
2.10
The following Documentation shall be submitted for any areas of habitat, trees covered by Tree
Preservation Orders, individual trees, shrubs, features and other areas listed for preservation prior to
the commencement of clearance works: detailing the measures to be taken to preserve and protect
them while carrying out the works.
2.11
Any areas of habitat, trees, shrubs, features and other areas designated for preservation which are
killed, removed or damaged during the course of the works shall be reinstated with plants of the same
species and equal in size to those killed, removed or damaged and the ground made good in
accordance with good arboricultural practice.
Clearance of existing vegetation including trees, bushes and hedges
2.12
Trees shall be defined as a woody plant greater than 2m in height and a bush as a woody plant of 2m
height or shorter.

3. Explosives and blasting
3.
Explosives and blasting
Site limitations for blasting
3.1
Blasting shall be not be permitted except where stated in GC 109/WSR/003.
3.2
Explosives and blasting shall be carried out in accordance with "General earthworks requirements" in
Section 1 of CC 601 [Ref 5.N].
3.3
Blasting shall be in accordance with "General earthworks construction" in Section 5 of CC 601 [Ref 5.N].
Unexploded ordnance
3.4
Sites shall have a preliminary risk unexploded ordnance (UXO) (Stage 1) assessment carried out in
accordance with CIRIA C681 [Ref 10.N].
3.5
The following Documentation shall be submitted for the assessment of the risk of unexploded ordnance
prior to the commencement of all works: Preliminary risk (Stage 1) assessment in accordance with
CIRIA C681 [Ref 10.N].
3.6
Sites where the risk of unexploded ordnance has been identified through the Stage 1 assessment shall
have a further detailed risk assessment and mitigation in accordance with CIRIA C681 [Ref 10.N]
carried out.
3.7
The following Documentation shall be submitted for sites where the risk of unexploded ordnance has
been identified prior to the commencement of all works: Detailed risk assessment, and where the risk is
deemed medium or high, include proposed risk mitigation and UXO risk mitigation plan.
3.8
Sites where the risk of unexploded ordnance has been identified shall have an emergency response
plan.

4.3
