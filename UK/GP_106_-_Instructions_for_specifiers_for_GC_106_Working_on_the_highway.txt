Summary
This document gives instructions for specifiers with respect to temporary traffic management,
routing of construction vehicles, vehicle recovery, temporary CCTV systems for the monitoring of
traffic and the construction of temporary highways.
This document incorporates specific requirements for National Highways. Alternative versions of this document are
available for other Overseeing Organisations.
Feedback and Enquiries
Users of this document are encouraged to raise any enquiries and/or provide feedback on the content and usage of
this document to the dedicated National Highways team. The online feedback form for all enquiries and feedback
can be accessed at: www.standardsforhighways.co.uk/feedback.
This is a controlled document.

Foreword
Foreword
This document provides specifier instructions for the production of the works specific requirements for
GC 106 Working on the highway.
This document does not form part of the works specification.
The works specification is made up of both the Specification for Highway Works and the works specific
requirements completed by the Specifier.
This document is applicable for contracts throughout the UK, complemented by the additional
specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.
Users are responsible for applying all appropriate documents applicable to their contract.
Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

1. Temporary traffic management
1.
Temporary traffic management
1.1
Temporary traffic management (TTM) required for undertaking the works shall be planned, designed,
programmed, provided, implemented, maintained and removed, unless otherwise stated in
GC 106/WSR/001.
Works Specific Requirements to be provided within the WSR template where applicable are:
SI.1.1
Details of alternative arrangements for temporary traffic management where they are different from GC
106 [enter free text].
1.2
TTM measures and arrangements shall maximise workforce and general public safety.
1.3
TTM measures and arrangements shall minimise road user disruption and disruption to other parties.
1.4
TTM shall only be implemented on the highway where and when the works require it.
1.5
TTM measures and arrangements shall provide for all road users and modes of transport that use the
highways, pedestrian routes, cycle routes and rights of way affected by the TTM and ensure
maintenance of accessibility arrangements for all road users.
1.6
All existing highways, pedestrian routes, cycling routes and rights of way shall be kept open to traffic
unless their closure is detailed under this Section or the procedures for closure detailed under this
Section are followed.
Temporary traffic management planning
1.7
Liaison with the police and highway authority for each highway affected by the works shall be
undertaken with respect to the planning, design and programming of TTM.
E/1.8
Liaison with the Traffic Officer Service for each highway affected by the works shall be undertaken with
respect to the planning, design and programming of TTM.
1.9
Liaison with the police and highway authority for each highway affected by the works shall be
undertaken before commencement and throughout the duration of the works.
E/1.10
Liaison with the Traffic Officer Service for each highway affected by the works shall be undertaken
before commencement and throughout the duration of the works.
TTM design
1.11
TTM shall be designed in accordance with CD 130 [Ref 4.N].
1.12
TTM shall be designed using TSM Chapter 8 [Ref 13.N].
1.13
TTM planning and design shall be undertaken by a competent TTM designer.
1.14
Competence assessment for undertaking TTM design shall include assessment of knowledge, training
and experience of TTM on the type of highway(s) involved in the works.
1.15
The following Documentation shall be submitted for individuals undertaking TTM design for the works
prior to the commencement of the works: evidence to demonstrate individuals have the suitable
competence to design TTM for the type of highway(s) involved in the works.
1.16
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N] shall apply to the evidence to
demonstrate TTM design competence.
TTM Proposals
1.17
The following Documentation shall be submitted for TTM prior to the commencement of the works:
detailed TTM proposals.
1.18
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N] shall apply to the detailed TTM
proposals.

The detailed TTM proposals submission shall include the following:

Traffic Management Manager and Traffic Safety Supervisors
1.55
A Traffic Management Manager (TMM) and one or more Traffic Safety Supervisors (TSS) shall be
appointed.
1.56
The term Traffic Safety Supervisor (TSS) shall also be read as Traffic Safety and Control Officer
(TSCO).
1.57
The TMM and TSS(s) shall be competent to undertake their TTM management and control functions.
1.58
The TMM and TSS(s) shall meet the training and qualifications requirements of the quality
management scheme for their TTM management and control functions.
1.59
The following Documentation shall be submitted for the competencies of the TMM and TSS(s) prior to
the commencement of the works: evidence to demonstrate the competencies of the TMM and TSS(s).
1.60
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N] shall apply to the evidence to
demonstrate the competencies of the TMM and TSS(s).
1.61
The TMM shall undertake the following functions:
1)
management of the TTM planning, programming and design;
2)
management of planning liaison for TTM with the police and highway authorities;
3)
appoint and assess competencies for each TSS; and
4)
ensure competence assessments are carried out for all TTM designers and operatives;
1.62
The TSS(s) shall undertake the following functions:
1)
control and supervision of TTM operations;
2)
ensure TTM measures and operations are implemented in accordance with the submitted TTM
proposals and agreements made through liaison with the police and highway authorities;
3)
be the point of contact for TTM operations with the Overseeing Organisation, the police and other
highway authorities;
4)
coordinate TTM operations with instructions given by police or traffic officers acting under their
statutory powers;
5)
undertake a programme of supervisory inspections of TTM measures and operations;
6)
manage liaison and coordination of TTM with vehicle recovery operations;
7)
undertake competence assessments for TTM operatives;
8)
ensure monitoring of traffic flows; and
9)
ensure recording and logging of incidents.
1.63
At all times that TTM is in operation at least one TSS shall be on site and be readily available for
dealing with TTM matters.

5.
Construction of temporary highways for traffic
5.1
Temporary highways for traffic shall include the following where these are constructed in order to
provide the works, or part of the works:
1)
temporary carriageway or part of carriageway onto which vehicular traffic is diverted;
2)
temporary footway, cycleway, bridleway or other route onto which pedestrian, cycling, equestrian
or other non-vehicular traffic is diverted;
3)
a combination of these; and
4)
a temporary means of access onto which traffic is diverted from a private means of access.
5.2
Requirements for existing highways that are subject to temporary closure and are diverted onto existing
highways are not subject to this section and shall be as stated in "Temporary traffic management" in
Section 1 of this document.
5.3
Temporary highways for traffic shall be fully constructed prior to their use by traffic.
5.4
Temporary highways for traffic shall be provided and maintained to be suitable for the class or classes
of traffic using the temporary highway, including the pavement and structures construction, highway
cross section and alignment.
5.5
Temporary highways for traffic shall be removed and reinstatement undertaken as soon as the
temporary highway is not required.
