Foreword
This document provides specifier instructions for the production of the works specific requirements for
CC 487 Maintenance painting of steelwork.
This document does not form part of the works specification.
The works specification is made up of both the Specification for Highway Works and the works specific
requirements completed by the Specifier.
This document is applicable for contracts throughout the UK, complemented by the additional
specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.
Users are responsible for applying all appropriate documents applicable to their contract.
Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

2. Surface preparation for maintenance painting ...
2.
Surface preparation for maintenance painting of steelwork
General requirements for surface preparation for maintenance painting of
steelwork
2.1
The minimum substrate standard following surface preparation for the types listed below shall be as
defined in CM 431 [Ref 5.N].
1)
sound paint;
2)
sound metal coating; and
3)
sound galvanised steel;
2.2
The minimum surface cleanliness following preparation of steel substrates, for the grades listed below,
shall be as defined in BS EN ISO 8501-1 [Ref 36.N].
1)
Sa2½
2)
Sa3
3)
St3
2.3
The surface roughness following preparation of steel substrates, for the profiles listed below, shall be
as defined in BS EN ISO 8503-1 [Ref 31.N].
1)
Fine
2)
Medium
3)
Coarse
2.4
Sound paint or sound metal coating at the interface with a prepared surface shall be feathered in.
2.5
The interface between different surface preparation types shall not be at an edge or within 50 mm of an
edge, measured perpendicular from the edge.
2.6
At the time of application of each coat of paint, the surfaces to be painted shall comply with all of the
following:.
1)
not have a residual dust level worse than Class 1 in accordance with BS EN ISO 8502-3 [Ref
32.N];
2)
meet the required substrate standard following surface preparation;
3)
be free from contamination, where contamination is defined in CM 431 [Ref 5.N]; and
4)
be free from moisture.
Cleaning requirements for surface preparation for maintenance painting of
steelwork
2.7
Cleaning solvents, other than water, shall not be used during the cleaning process, unless otherwise
stated in CC 487/WSR/002.
Works Specific Requirements to be provided within the WSR template where applicable are:
SI.2.7
The locations where cleaning solvents other than water are permitted, and the requirements on their
use, shall be as follows: [enter free text].
2.8
Where cleaning solvents, other than water, are permitted to be used during the cleaning process,
following their use, all traces of solvent shall be removed.
2.9
Inhibitors and salt extraction products shall not be used during any cleaning process.
Surface preparation of steel substrate for maintenance painting of steelwork
Surface cleanliness and roughness of steel substrate for maintenance painting of steelwork
2.10
The substrate standard, or cleanliness and roughness, following surface preparation shall be as stated
in CC 487/WSR/001.

2. Surface preparation for maintenance painting ...
2.11
Verification shall be undertaken for the surface roughness following blast-cleaning through testing with
a calibrated surface profile gauge in accordance with BS EN ISO 8503-5 [Ref 30.N] or ASTM D4417
[Ref 43.N] methods B or C.
2.12
The frequency of testing of the surface roughness following blast-cleaning shall be in accordance with
ASTM D4417 [Ref 43.N] prior to the application of paint.
2.13
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 3.N] shall apply to testing of the
surface roughness achieved by blast-cleaning for maintenance painting of steelwork.
2.14
The following Documentation for testing of the surface roughness following blast-cleaning shall be
submitted as continuous records: test reports in accordance with BS EN ISO 8503-5 [Ref 30.N] or
ASTM D4417 [Ref 43.N].
2.15
The requirements of "Records" in Section 3 of GC 101 [Ref 3.N] shall apply to test reports for
verification of the surface roughness following blast-cleaning.
Soluble salts on steel substrates for maintenance painting of steelwork
2.16
The presence of soluble salts on any steel substrate on which protective coatings are to be used shall
not exceed 20 mg/m2 in accordance with M-501 [Ref 7.N] or as per the coating manufacturer's data
sheets, whichever is lower.
2.17
Verification shall be undertaken for steel substrates to confirm that soluble salts are below the specified
limit by sampling in accordance with BS EN ISO 8502-6 [Ref 33.N] and measuring in accordance with
BS EN ISO 8502-9 [Ref 34.N].
2.18
The frequency of sampling and subsequent measurement of soluble salts on steel substrates shall be
one sample per 50 m2 of bare steel, prior to application of protective coatings.
2.19
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 3.N] shall apply to sampling and
measuring of soluble salts on steel substrates prepared for maintenance painting of steelwork.
2.20
The following Documentation for sampling and measuring of soluble salts on steel substrates shall be
submitted as continuous records: test report in accordance with BS EN ISO 8502-6 [Ref 33.N] and test
report in accordance with BS EN ISO 8502-9 [Ref 34.N].
2.21
The requirements of "Records" in Section 3 of GC 101 [Ref 3.N] shall apply to test reports for sampling
and measuring of soluble salts on steel substrates prepared for maintenance painting of steelwork.
Surface preparation of galvanised steel for maintenance painting of steelwork
2.22
The sound galvanised steel surface shall be prepared for painting by one of the following methods:.
1)
Item No 155 ('T' Wash Adhesion promoter) in accordance with CG 303 [Ref 38.N];
2)
Item No 157 (adhesion promoting primer) in accordance with CG 303 [Ref 38.N]; or
3)
sweep blast in accordance with ASTM D6386 [Ref 40.N].
Surface preparation of metal coating for maintenance painting of steelwork
2.23
The visual cleanliness of sound metal coating after surface preparation shall be free from corrosion
products and embedded abrasive particles.
2.24
Areas of sound metal coating to be retained shall not be exposed to moisture.
Surface preparation of sound paint for maintenance painting of steelwork
2.25
Sound paint that is to be overcoated or lapped over shall be free from any visible gloss that can
compromise adhesion.

2. Surface preparation for maintenance painting ...
Surface preparation of joints, plies, and fasteners for maintenance painting of
steelwork
2.26
Fasteners shall be prepared to the same standard as the adjacent main surfaces, unless otherwise
stated in CC 487/WSR/002.
Works Specific Requirements to be provided within the WSR template where applicable are:
SI.2.26
The requirements for surface preparation of fasteners, and the locations where the requirements apply,
shall be [enter free text].
2.27
After surface preparation and cleaning, any gaps around the perimeter of joints, plies, and fasteners
shall be dried and subsequently sealed prior to the application of any overall paint coat using one of the
following methods:.
1)
by successive applications of the first paint coat; or
2)
for wider gaps, by application of a sealant compatible with the paint to be applied over it.
2.28
Where a sealant is used to seal gaps around the perimeter of joints, plies, and fasteners, and the
curing time of the sealant exceeds the exposure times for the steel substrate or metal coating defined
in this section, the first coat of the paint system shall be applied before the sealant is applied.
Abrading for surface preparation for maintenance painting of steelwork
2.29
Hard grinding wheels shall not be used for abrading.
2.30
Abrading shall not result in a polished surface in accordance with BS EN ISO 12944-4 [Ref 10.N].
2.31
Abrading shall be carried out in a multi-directional motion.
Blast-cleaning for surface preparation for maintenance painting of steelwork
2.32
Metallic blast-cleaning abrasives for surface preparation for maintenance painting of steelwork shall
comply with BS EN ISO 11124-1 [Ref 17.N], and any of BS EN ISO 11124-2 [Ref 19.N], BS EN ISO
11124-3 [Ref 20.N], BS EN ISO 11124-4 [Ref 18.N], or BS EN ISO 11124-5 [Ref 16.N].
2.33
Non-metallic blast-cleaning abrasives for surface preparation for maintenance painting of steelwork
shall comply with BS EN ISO 11126-1 [Ref 22.N], and any of BS EN ISO 11126-3 [Ref 28.N], BS EN
ISO 11126-4 [Ref 21.N], BS EN ISO 11126-5 [Ref 24.N], BS EN ISO 11126-6 [Ref 23.N] BS EN ISO
11126-7 [Ref 29.N], BS EN ISO 11126-8 [Ref 25.N], BS EN ISO 11126-9 [Ref 26.N], or BS EN ISO
11126-10 [Ref 27.N].
2.34
Verification shall be undertaken for cleanliness of blast-cleaning abrasives for surface preparation for
maintenance painting of steelwork through testing in accordance with ASTM D4940 [Ref 41.N].
2.35
The frequency of cleanliness testing of blast-cleaning abrasives for surface preparation for
maintenance painting of steelwork shall be once for each blasting pot prior to each shift.
2.36
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 3.N] shall apply to cleanliness testing
of blast-cleaning abrasives for surface preparation for maintenance painting of steelwork.
2.37
The following Documentation for cleanliness of blast-cleaning abrasives for surface preparation for
maintenance painting of steelwork shall be submitted as continuous records: test report in accordance
with ASTM D4940 [Ref 41.N].
2.38
The requirements of "Records" in Section 3 of GC 101 [Ref 3.N] shall apply to test reports for
cleanliness of blast-cleaning abrasives for surface preparation for maintenance painting of steelwork.
2.39
Non-metallic blast-cleaning abrasives for surface preparation for maintenance painting of steelwork
shall not be reused.
2.40
During wet abrasive blast-cleaning for surface preparation for maintenance painting of steelwork, the
air/water/abrasive shall be thoroughly mixed and projected onto the surface to be cleaned through a
single bore nozzle/tube.

9.
Storage requirements and keeping periods for paints for
maintenance painting of steelwork
9.1
All paint shall be stored in accordance with the paint manufacturer's data sheets.
9.2
Paint in containers, painters' kettles, or airless spray containers that reaches temperatures outside the
storage temperature range given by the paint manufacturer shall not be used for maintenance painting
of steelwork.
9.3
Paint which has not been used within the shelf life specified by the paint manufacturer or within 24
months of the date of manufacture, whichever is the lesser, shall not be used for maintenance painting
of steelworks.
9.4
Unmixed paint shall not be retained for use in another shift where either of the following apply:.
1)
more than 50% of the original volume of paint has been decanted; or
2)
the paint container has been left unsealed for more than 2 minutes after decanting.
9.5
Mixed paint shall be discarded on expiry of the pot life or at the end of the working shift whichever is the
sooner.
