Foreword
This document is for incorporation into contracts by reference. It defines coverage of items in Bills of
Quantities and their method of measurement.
This document is applicable to contracts throughout the UK, complemented by the additional method of
measurement, specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.
Users are responsible for applying all appropriate documents applicable to their contract.
Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

1. Definitions
1.
Definitions
1.1
Any reference to 'drain' shall be deemed to include sewers and piped culverts.
1.2
Drains exceeding 900 mm internal diameter, box culverts, piped culverts and all associated chambers,
headwalls, outfall works and concrete bagwork shall be measured in accordance with the Section 18 of
CC 500/MMHW [Ref 16.N].
1.3
Trenches and ducts in connection with electrical work for road lighting and traffic signs cabling shall be
measured in accordance with TC 501 [Ref 4.N].
1.4
Trenches and ducts in connection with motorway communications cabling shall be measured in
accordance with TC 131 [Ref 18.N].
1.5
The Earthworks Outline is defined in clauses 1.1, 1.2, 1.3, 1.4, 1.5, 1.6, and 1.7 of CC 601/MMHW [Ref
17.N] inclusive and shall apply equally to CC 500/MMHW [Ref 16.N].
1.6
Where the ground level has been subjected to treatment, under the Contract, in respect of ground
improvement, mine workings, swallow holes and the like, for the purposes of CC 500/MMHW [Ref
16.N]Existing Ground Level shall be the level obtained upon completion of any such treatment of the
areas affected.
1.7
Any reference to chambers shall include manholes, catchpits and draw pits.
Note a
Sub-soil Level is defined as the level of the ground after the removal of topsoil required by the Contract.
Note b
Surcharge is defined as material placed on embankments for the purpose of loading the embankment
for the periods stated in the Contract.
Note c
For the purpose of measurement, a 'soft spot' is defined as formation material not meeting the
minimum formation strength requirements.

2. Drains and service ducts (excluding filter dr...
2.
Drains and service ducts (excluding filter drains, narrow filter
drains and fin drains)
Units
2.1
The unit of measurement for drains and service ducts shall be:
a)
drains, service ducts ............ linear metre.
Measurement
2.2
The measurement of drains and service ducts shall be the summation of their individual lengths
measured along the centre lines of the pipes between any of the following:
a)
the internal faces of chambers;
b)
the external faces of headwalls;
c)
the intersections of the centre lines at pipe junctions;
d)
the centre of gully gratings (or where no grating is provided, the centre of the gully);
e)
the position of terminations shown in the Contract;
f)
the point of change of stage depth.
2.3
The depth of drains and service ducts shall be the vertical measurement between the invert and the
following:
a)
where the invert is below the Existing Ground Level - the Existing Ground Level except that
where the Earthworks Outline is below the Existing Ground Level the measurement shall be
taken to the Earthworks Outline;
b)
where the invert is at or above the Existing Ground Level - the datum stated in the Contract, or
where none is stated, the Earthworks Outline.
2.4
Notwithstanding clause 2.3 of this document, where in the Contract a commencing level or a minimum
level of cover is stated from which excavation shall commence, then the depth is to be taken to that
stated level.
2.5
The average depth to invert shall be the calculated arithmetic mean of the depths taken at intervals of
10 metres along the pipelines starting from the outfall end.
2.6
For terminal lengths and pipelines less than 10 metres long the measurement of depths shall be taken
at their ends.
2.7
The measurement of service ducts shall be for the complete construction irrespective of the number of
ducts contained within any one trench.
2.8
Where more than one duct is laid in a trench then the number of ducts shall be stated in the item
description.
2.9
Drains and service ducts required to be designed by the Contractor shall be measured in accordance
with Section 18 of CC 500/MMHW [Ref 16.N].
2.10
The measurement of drainage shall be based upon the thinnest construction permitted for any of the
alternative Types of Pavement irrespective of the Type of Pavement chosen by the Contractor.
Note
Adjustments, and nearest average depths to inverts where shown, are at the discretion of and to be
confirmed by the document compiler.
Itemisation

and fin drains) in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 7.N] and the following:

3. Filter drains
3.
Filter drains
Units
3.1
The units of measurement for filter drains shall be:
a)
filter drains ............ linear metre.
b)
filter material contiguous with filter drains ............ cubic metre.
c)
subbase material ............ cubic metre.
d)
lightweight aggregate infill ............ cubic metre.
e)
excavate and replace filter material ............ cubic metre.
f)
piped counterfort drains ............ linear metre.
g)
un-piped counterfort drains ............ linear metre.
Measurement
3.2
The measurement of filter drains, excluding narrow filter drains, shall be the summation of their
individual lengths measured along the centre lines of the pipe (or trench where no pipe is provided),
between any of the following:
a)
the internal faces of chambers;
b)
the external faces of headwalls;
c)
the intersection of centre lines at junctions;
d)
the centre of gully gratings (or where no grating is provided the centre of the gully);
e)
the position of terminations shown in the Contract;
f)
the point of change of stage depth.
3.3
The depth of filter drains shall be the vertical measurement between the invert (or the centre line of the
trench bottom where no pipe is provided) and the following:
a)
where the invert is below the Existing Ground Level - the Existing Ground Level or the
Earthworks Outline whichever is the lower, except that where the finished level of the filter
material is above the Existing Ground Level the measurement shall be taken to the finished level
of the filter material;
b)
where the invert is at or above the Existing Ground Level - the datum stated in the Contract, or
where none is stated, the finished level of the filter material.
3.4
The calculation of average depth to invert of filter drains shall be as clause 2.5 of this document taken
along the centre line of the filter drain.
3.5
Narrow filter drains shall be measured in accordance with clauses 4.1, 4.2, 4.3, 4.4, and 4.5 of this
document.
3.6
The measurement of contiguous filter material shall be the volume of the material occupying the void
between the filter drain and the adjacent carriageway hard shoulder and hard strip.
3.7
The side of the contiguous filter material next to the filter drain shall be taken as the vertical extension
of the trench side above capping or where no capping is provided above subgrade level.
3.8
The measurement of subbase material shall be the volume of the subbase material within
non-pavement verge or central reserve adjacent to the carriageway, hard shoulder and hard strip filled
to the outline stated in the Contract.
3.9
The measurement of lightweight aggregate infill shall be the volume of the lightweight aggregate infill
above the filter drain filled to the outline stated in the Contract.

Protection of existing drains, sewers, piped culverts, service

Units

service ducts shall be:
a)
protection of existing drains, sewers, piped culverts, service mains and service ducts ............
linear metre.
Measurement
19.2
The measurement of protection of existing drains, sewers, piped culverts, service mains and service
ducts shall be the summation of their individual lengths measured along the centre line between any of
the following:
a)
the external face of chambers;
b)
the rear faces of headwalls and outfalls;
c)
the intersections of the centre lines at pipe junctions;
d)
the external faces at gullies;
e)
the positions of terminations shown in the Contract;
f)
the point of change of stage depth.
19.3
The protection of existing drains, sewers, piped culverts, service mains and service ducts shall only be
measured where specifically stated in the Contract or ordered by the Overseeing Organisation.
19.4
The depth of existing drains, sewers, piped culverts, service mains and service ducts to be protected
shall be the vertical measurement between the invert and the existing ground level.
19.5
The average depth to invert shall be the calculated arithmetical mean of the depths taken at intervals of
10 metres along the lengths measured.
19.6
For lengths less than 10 metres the measurement of depths shall be taken at the ends.
19.7
The measurement of protection of existing drains, sewers, piped culverts, service mains and service
ducts shall be the complete protection irrespective of the number uncovered in any one trench.
19.8
Where more than one drain, sewer, piped culvert, service main or service duct exists in any one trench
then the number shall be that stated in the item description.
19.9
The invert used to measure the depth of a configuration of ducts to be protected shall be the lowest
duct invert in that configuration.
