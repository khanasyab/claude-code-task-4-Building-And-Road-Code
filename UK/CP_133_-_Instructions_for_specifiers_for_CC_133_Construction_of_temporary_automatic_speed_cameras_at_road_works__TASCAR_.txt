Foreword
This document provides specifier instructions for the production of the works specific requirements for
CC 133 Construction of temporary automatic speed cameras at road works (TASCAR).
This document does not form part of the works specification.
The works specification is made up of both the Specification for Highway Works and the works specific
requirements completed by the Specifier.
This document is applicable for contracts throughout the UK, complemented by the additional
specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.
Users are responsible for applying all appropriate documents applicable to their contract.
Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

1.
General requirements for temporary automatic speed cameras at
road works (TASCAR)
Scope of this document for temporary automatic speed cameras at road works
(TASCAR)
1.1
This document shall apply to temporary automatic speed cameras at road works (TASCAR) which are
used for the enforcement of mandatory speed limits and which are not intended to remain in position on
the completion of permanent works or on conclusion of the event requiring temporary enforcement of
mandatory speed limits.
Provision of TASCAR
1.2
The provision of TASCAR shall include design, supply, installation, commissioning, verification,
maintenance and removal of all equipment and services needed to form a complete TASCAR system.
1.3
The provision of TASCAR shall not include enforcement operations, which are to be undertaken by the
enforcement authority until removal of the TASCAR system.
1.4
The enforcement authority shall be as specified in CC 133/WSR/001.

1. General requirements for temporary automatic ...
1.7
The design of TASCAR shall be in accordance with CD 133 [Ref 1.N].
1.8
The requirements for "Contractor design" in Section 17 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to TASCAR.
Design documentation for TASCAR
1.9
The following Documentation shall be submitted for TASCAR prior to the commencement of the works:
as stated in Table 1.9.
Table 1.9 Design documentation for TASCAR
Item
Description of design documentation
1
The Home Office Type Approval (HOTA) certificate for the selected TASCAR product(s)
2
Details of all installation dimensions and parameters mandated in the HOTA for the
selected TASCAR product(s)
3
TASCAR design proposals
4
TASCAR commissioning and calibration plan
5
TASCAR inspection, measurement and testing plan
6
TASCAR traffic signs verification plan
1.10
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to the detailed
TASCAR design proposals.
Product requirements for TASCAR
1.11
Devices used to function as a speedmeter in TASCAR systems shall have obtained Home Office Type
Approval (HOTA) before being selected for use during the works.
1.12
Support structures and fixings, mountings, base plates and other items used to secure the TASCAR
equipment to a support structure shall be as required by the HOTA for the TASCAR.
Installation requirements for TASCAR
1.13
The TASCAR product(s), including detection and measuring devices; camera recording devices; flash
or other ancillary lighting units; posts; housings; power supplies; and ancillary items shall be installed
and commissioned in accordance with the TASCAR manufacturer's instructions and any specific
installation requirements upon which HOTA is reliant.
1.14
Electrical installations for TASCAR shall be compliant with BS 7671 [Ref 3.N].
Verification requirements for TASCAR
Commissioning and calibration of TASCAR
1.15
Verification shall be undertaken for TASCAR by commissioning and calibration in accordance with the
TASCAR commissioning and calibration plan.
1.16
The frequency of commissioning and calibration shall be once on initial installation; once for every
subsequent modification or relocation of an installation; and once for every failure to meet the
acceptance criteria in the TASCAR commissioning and calibration plan.
1.17
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to commissioning and
calibration of TASCAR.

1. General requirements for temporary automatic ...
Verification of TASCAR following commissioning and calibration
1.18
Verification shall be undertaken for TASCAR by inspection, measurement and testing in accordance
with the TASCAR inspection, measurement and testing plan.
1.19
The frequency of inspection, measurement and testing shall be once on initial installation; once for
every subsequent modification or relocation of an installation; and once for every failure to meet the
acceptance criteria in the TASCAR inspection, measurement and testing plan.
1.20
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to inspection,
measurement and testing of TASCAR.
Independent inspection, measurement and testing of TASCAR
1.21
Where required by CC 133/WSR/001, independent inspection and testing services for TASCAR shall
be procured from an independent, third party organisation that holds accreditation specifically for the
purposes of assuring the installation of speed measuring devices.
1.22
Verification shall be undertaken for TASCAR by independent inspection and testing of whether the
TASCAR installation complies with its HOTA as stated in CC 133/WSR/01.
1.23
The frequency of independent inspection and testing for TASCAR shall be as stated in CC
133/WSR/001.
1.24
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to independent
inspection and testing of TASCAR.
Verification of traffic signs associated with TASCAR
1.25
Verification shall be undertaken for the traffic signs associated with TASCAR by inspection and
checking in accordance with the TASCAR traffic signs verification plan.
1.26
The frequency of inspection and checking of the traffic signs associated with TASCAR shall be in
accordance with the TASCAR traffic signs verification plan.
1.27
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to inspection and
checking of the traffic signs associated with TASCAR.
Installation, commissioning and verification documentation for TASCAR
1.28
The following Documentation shall be submitted for the installation, commissioning and verification of
each TASCAR installation prior to the commencement of TASCAR operation by the enforcement
authority: as stated in Table 1.28.

1. General requirements for temporary automatic ...
Table 1.28 Installation, commissioning and verification documentation for TASCAR
Item
Description of installation, commissioning and verification documentation for each
TASCAR installation
1
A copy of the statutory order or temporary traffic regulation order in accordance with which
the TASCAR installation has been designed and installed.
2
An as-installed location plan for all on-road TASCAR equipment, including location datum
points for enforcement purposes in the form of latitude and longitude.
3
Electrical installation certificate(s), including schedules of electrical inspections and test
results, in accordance with BS 7671 [Ref 3.N].
4
An end-to-end TASCAR system commissioning report, signed and dated by the person
responsible for its preparation, in accordance with the TASCAR commissioning and
calibration plan.
5
A TASCAR system calibration certificate, signed and dated by the person responsible for
its preparation, in accordance with the TASCAR commissioning and calibration plan.
6
Records of all inspections, measurements and tests, signed and dated by the persons
responsible for their preparation, in accordance with the TASCAR inspection, measurement
and testing plan.
7
A certificate confirming compliance of the TASCAR installation with its HOTA and the
accuracy of the documentation listed in items 1-6 of this table, signed and dated by person
responsible for its preparation.
1.29
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to the installation,
commissioning and verification documentation for TASCAR.
Documentation from the independent inspection, measurement and testing of TASCAR
1.30
The following Documentation shall be submitted for the independent inspection and testing of whether
the TASCAR installation complies with its HOTA, where such activities are required by CC
133/WSR/001, prior to the commencement of TASCAR operation by the enforcement authority:
independently produced inspection and test results; and certification, signed and dated by the person
responsible for its preparation, confirming whether the TASCAR installation is compliant with its HOTA.
1.31
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 2.N] shall apply to independently
produced certification confirming whether the TASCAR installation is compliant with its HOTA.
Documentation from the verification of traffic signs associated with TASCAR
1.32
The following Documentation shall be submitted for the inspection and checking of the traffic signs
associated with TASCAR throughout operation of TASCAR and prior to the commencement of removal
of TASCAR: documentation from the inspection and checking of traffic signs in accordance with the
TASCAR traffic signs verification plan.
1.33
Documentation for the inspection and checking of the traffic signs associated with TASCAR shall be
submitted to the Overseeing Organisation and the enforcement authority in accordance with the
timescales stated in the TASCAR traffic signs verification plan.
Operations and maintenance requirements for TASCAR
1.34
TASCAR equipment shall operate and be maintained in accordance with the TASCAR manufacturer's
instructions and any specific operations and maintenance requirements upon which HOTA is reliant.

1. General requirements for temporary automatic ...
1.35
All evidential data and evidential data storage media from TASCAR shall become and remain the
property of the enforcement authority.
1.36
Where a defect or fault causes a TASCAR installation to stop working or affects the enforcement
authority's use of the TASCAR system, the Overseeing Organisation and the enforcement authority
shall be notified of the defect or fault and the plans and timescales for rectifying the defect or fault.
1.37
Where a defect or fault causes a TASCAR installation to stop working or affects the enforcement
authority's use of the TASCAR system, the defect or fault shall be rectified in accordance with the
timescales in Table 1.37.

Item
Location of defect or fault
Maximum timescale for defect or fault
rectification after the defect or fault occurs
1
TASCAR equipment placed on and
adjacent to the road
96 hours
2
All other locations
48 hours
Decommissioning requirements for TASCAR
1.38
Following the removal of TASCAR, electrical installations, verges, paved areas and carriageways shall
be reinstated in accordance with the outputs from the design of TASCAR.
