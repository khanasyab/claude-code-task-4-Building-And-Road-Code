Revision 2.1.0 (Publication: Date September 2024) The term 'expressway' has been replaced by 'enhanced
all-purpose dual carriageway'. The new term better reflects the nature of the requirements/advice prescribed by
GD 300, which represent enhancements over the conventional all-purpose dual carriageway baseline. Revision 2
(April 2020) This revision is for minor administrative changes to the England NAA. See the England NAA revision
release notes for more details. Revision 1 (March 2020) This revision includes updates to the Highways England
National Application Annex focused on the provision of additional advice for level 1 to 4 schemes. Superseded
references have also been updated. Revision 0 (May 2019) GD 300 document created to outline design
requirements and advice for new and upgraded all-purpose trunk roads (expressways). This full document has
been written to comply with the new Highways England drafting rules.

1. Scope
1.
Scope
Aspects covered
1.1
This document shall be used for the design of enhanced all-purpose dual carriageways.
NOTE
National requirements and advice for the design of enhanced all-purpose dual carriageways are
provided in the National Application Annexes of this document. Currently, enhanced all-purpose dual
carriageways are only applicable in England.
Implementation
1.2
This document shall be implemented forthwith on all enhanced all-purpose dual carriageway schemes
and schemes applying previous versions of this document on the Overseeing Organisations' motorway
and all-purpose trunk roads according to the implementation requirements of GG 101 [Ref 1.N].
Use of GG 101
1.3
The requirements contained in GG 101 [Ref 1.N] shall be followed in respect of activities covered by
this document.

Introduction
Introduction
Background
The Department for Transport's (DfT) Road Investment Strategy RIS 1 [Ref 4.I] set out the intention for
the busiest all-purpose roads to be upgraded to improve safety and operational performance. This
document (GD 300) was first published in May 2019 in response to this initiative.
At the core of the GD 300 concept is the intention to enable improvements to safety, efficiency and user
experience through the implementation of infrastructure, technology and operational practices.
The original intention for GD 300 was to outline the requirements for what is referred to in this
document as 'level 4' only, which includes the application of motorway regulations to the route. In the
development of the GD 300 concept however, it was acknowledged that the level of provision
associated with the application of motorway regulations would not be practical for all routes. As a
result, guidance was developed for what is referred to in this document as levels 1, 2 and 3. Each level
(starting at level 1) represents increasing levels of enhancements over the conventional all-purpose
dual carriageway baseline.
Regardless of the level selected, the intention is that all schemes designed using GD 300 positively
contribute to National Highways' commitments to safety through:
1)
working towards the 'Zero Harm' goal of bringing the number of people killed or seriously injured
to a level approaching zero by 2040; and
2)
delivering schemes with international road assessment programme IRAP [Ref 2.I] minimum Star
Rating of 3.
As part of this revision 2.1.0 of the document, the term 'enhanced all-purpose dual carriageway' has
replaced 'expressway'. The term enhanced all-purpose dual carriageway better reflects the levelled
nature of the enhancements prescribed by this document that are over and above the conventional
all-purpose dual carriageway baseline.
Building on the foundations of revision 2.1.0, work on a more major revision (revision 3) of GD 300 is
underway. Revision 3 will remove level 4 requirements/advice for motorway designation and instead
focus on the provision of enhancements to all-purpose dual carriageways that retain all-purpose trunk
road designation. As part of this initiative, revision 3 will provide more flexibility in the way
enhancements can be delivered in order to better account for the greater variability in the nature of
non-motorway dual carriageways.
Document structure and levels
The requirements for level 4 enhanced all-purpose dual carriageways are set out in the main body of
this document in sections E/1 to E/10.
Advice for levels 1 to 3 is provided in Appendix E/A. It should be noted that Appendix E/A represents
guidance only and does not preclude levels 1 and 2 schemes from adopting design components from
level 3 should a scheme specific assessment identify the benefits of doing so.
Nature of an enhanced all-purpose dual carriageway
General
An enhanced all-purpose dual carriageway is intended to be implemented in circumstances where
additional road capacity and/or safety enhancements are required. This can be achieved through the
modification of an existing route (which can comprise online or offline works, or a combination of both)
or through the development of a new route.
The most appropriate level of enhanced all-purpose dual carriageway will vary from scheme to scheme.
An overview of the recommended design components for levels 1 to 3 compared to level 4 is provided
in Table E/A.3 in Appendix E/A. The main differences are:

Introduction
1)
Level 1 provides a high-quality dual carriageway, with junctions that are full grade separated only,
and with no central reserve gaps.
2)
Level 2 builds upon level 1, with the added provision of a rigid central reserve barrier.
3)
Level 3 further builds upon level 1 and 2, with the addition of all-purpose trunk road (APTR)
emergency areas, technology (including stopped vehicle detection) and traffic officer provision,
whilst removing lay-bys and introducing prohibitions for walkers, cyclists and horse-riders (WCH)
and certain vehicle types.
4)
Level 4 includes all of the above and adds motorway designation.
WCH and prohibited vehicles
Providing alternative provision for WCH allows for these users to be separated from the high-speed
dual carriageway and for the introduction of a prohibition of WCH on the mainline.
The level of alternative provision to be provided for WCH is intended to reflect the range of potential
users, key destinations and latent demand.
Alternative provision could involve making use of, or upgrading, existing routes, through to introducing
new, dedicated WCH paths. Existing routes could be part of the Overseeing Organisation's network, a
local highway authority's network, or a mixture of both. It may also be necessary to provide new
bridges and/or underpasses to enable the crossing of the enhanced all-purpose dual carriageway to
avoid severance and make sure that the ability to access communities and facilities is not negatively
impacted.
Prohibiting certain vehicles allows for more reliable journeys due to a reduction in the potential for
materially disparate speeds across users of the enhanced all-purpose dual carriageway. This adds
further benefits to the safety risk profile of the scheme. For upgrades to existing routes, suitable
alternative provision for those users newly prohibited will need to be factored into the scheme design.
Due to the potential scope of providing alternative provision for WCH and prohibited vehicles, it is
important that liaison with local authorities and key stakeholders is undertaken from the outset of
scheme development. It may also be more challenging to introduce WCH and vehicle prohibitions on
schemes involving the upgrade of an existing route to an enhanced all-purpose dual carriageway. The
practicality of introducing these prohibitions will be a material factor in deciding the appropriate level of
enhanced all-purpose dual carriageway to be introduced.
Benefits of enhanced all-purpose dual carriageways
The benefits of enhanced all-purpose dual carriageways are acknowledged in the 'Road Investment
Strategy 2' (see RIS 20-25 [Ref 6.I]). The implementation of an enhanced all-purpose dual carriageway
can deliver these benefits with alignment to the six performance outcomes presented in National
Highways' Strategic Business Plan: 2020-2025 (see HE SBP 2020-2025 [Ref 7.I]:
1)
improving safety for all;
2)
providing fast and reliable journeys;
3)
a well maintained and resilient network;
4)
delivering better environmental outcomes;
5)
meeting the needs of all users; and,
6)
achieving efficient delivery.
The level of benefit achieved against each of the above performance outcomes will vary depending on
the level of enhanced all-purpose dual carriageway implemented.
