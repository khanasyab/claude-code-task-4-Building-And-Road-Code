Quantities and their method of measurement.

Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

1. Waterproofing
1.
Waterproofing
Units
1.1
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
waterproofing ............ square metre.
Measurement
1.2
The measurement shall be the area of surface covered by the waterproofing with no deductions made
for openings of 1 square metre or less.
Itemisation
1.3
Separate items shall be provided for waterproofing in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of
GP 112 [Ref 2.N] and the following:
Table 1.3 Itemisation for Waterproofing
Group
Feature
I
1 Waterproofing for concrete decks of highway structures.
2 Waterproofing below ground concrete surfaces.
II
1 Different systems.
III
1 More than 300 mm wide horizontal or at any inclination up to and including 30o
to the horizontal.
2 More than 300 mm wide at any inclination more than 30o up to and including
90o to the horizontal.
3 300 mm wide or less at any inclination.
4 Domed.
Item Coverage - Waterproofing
1.4
The items for waterproofing shall be in accordance with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref 1.N] and include for:
a)
preparing, cleaning and drying;
b)
priming and bonding agents;
c)
laying to cambers, falls and crowns;
d)
protective layers including sand carpet, ST1 concrete or tiles set in bitumen;
e)
additional protective layers (APL);
f)
formwork as clause 3.9 of CC 482/MMHW [Ref 3.N];
g)
additional base or binder course required as a result of the Contractor's choice of waterproofing;
h)
nibs, angle fillets, external angles, mitres, stops and the like;
i)
sealing and making good at edges and chases, around interruptions and projections and up to
abutting surfaces including cleaning and priming;
j)
cutting out and rectifying imperfections;
k)
joints and laps;
l)
preparing surfaces at gullies and the like;
m)
masking and other measures to protect adjacent untreated areas;
n)
complying with any special or manufacturer's requirements in respect of ambient conditions and
for intervals between successive operations and applications;

2. Surface protection systems for structural con...
2.
Surface protection systems for structural concrete
Units
2.1
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
hydrophobic impregnation, anti-graffiti coatings, anti-carbonation coatings of structural concrete
............ square metre.
Measurement
2.2
The measurement of hydrophobic impregnation shall be the area of the surface to be impregnated of
any width or at any inclination with no deductions made for openings of 1 square metre or less.
2.3
The measurement of anti-graffiti coatings and anti-carbonation coatings shall be the area of the surface
to be treated of any width or at any inclination with no deductions made for openings of 1 square metre
or less.
2.4
For patterned surfaces the measurement shall be the flat undeveloped area and measured over the
face of individual fillets, chambers, splays, drips, rebates, recesses, grooves and the like, not forming
part of the pattern and of 100 mm total girth or less when measured overall the faces in contact with the
concrete.
2.5
Surfaces shall be measured once only, irrespective of the number of applications specified in the
treatment.
Itemisation
2.6
Separate items shall be provided for surface protection systems for structural concrete in accordance
with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 2.N] and the following:
Table 2.6 Itemisation for Surface protection systems for structural concrete
Group
Feature
I
1 Hydrophobic impregnation.
2 Anti-graffiti coatings.
3 Anti-carbonation coatings.
II
1 Patterned surfaces.
2 Plain surfaces.
III
1 Different systems.
IV
1 Different structural elements.
Item Coverage - Surface protection systems for structural concrete
2.7
The items for surface protection systems for structural concrete shall be in accordance with clause 2.1
of GP 113 [Ref 1.N] and include for:
a)
preparing, cleaning, removal of graffiti and drying;
b)
protection from precipitation and spray;
c)
masking and other measures to protect adjacent untreated materials;
d)
disposal of unacceptable earthworks material as clause 7.6 of CC 601/MMHW [Ref 4.N];
e)
facilities and assistance for inspection by the Overseeing Organisation;
f)
complying with any special requirements in respect of ambient conditions and for intervals
between successive operations and applications;

3. Removal of existing waterproofing
3.
Removal of existing waterproofing
Units
3.1
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
removal of existing waterproofing ............ square metre.
Measurement
3.2
The measurement of removal of existing waterproofing shall be the area of surface covered by the
existing waterproofing with no deductions made for openings of 1 square metre or less.
Itemisation
3.3
Separate items shall be provided for removal of existing waterproofing in accordance with clauses 2.2,
2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 2.N] and the following:
Table 3.3 Itemisation for Removal of existing waterproofing
Group
Feature
I
1 Removal of existing waterproofing.
II
1 More than 300mm wide horizontal or at any inclination up to and including 30o to
the horizontal.
2 More than 300mm wide at any inclination more than 30o up to and including 90o
to the horizontal.
3 300mm wide or less at any inclination.
4 Domed.
Item Coverage - Removal of existing waterproofing
3.4
The items for removal of existing waterproofing shall be in accordance with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref
1.N] and include for:
a)
marking out the areas of waterproofing to be removed;
b)
removal of all protective layers;
c)
removal of primer;
d)
preparation of existing surfaces to receive new waterproofing;
e)
disposal of unacceptable earthworks materials as clause 7.6 of CC 601/MMHW [Ref 4.N];
f)
measures to prevent damage to existing surfaces, property, structures, drains and services;
g)
measures to prevent dust, chemicals, waterproofing debris or other such materials used in the
removal of waterproofing encroaching the site boundary and effecting either the health and safety
of the general public or the environment;
h)
allow time for deck concrete testing and remedial works;
i)
replacing items damaged during the foregoing operations;
