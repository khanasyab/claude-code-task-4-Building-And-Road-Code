Foreword
Foreword
This document is for incorporation into contracts by reference. It defines coverage of items in Bills of
Quantities and their method of measurement.
This document is applicable to contracts throughout the UK, complemented by the additional method of
measurement, specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.
Users are responsible for applying all appropriate documents applicable to their contract.
Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

1. Fabrication and erection of steelwork
1.
Fabrication and erection of steelwork
Units
1.1
The units of measurement shall be:
a)
fabrication, permanent erection ............ tonne.
b)
trial erection at the place of fabrication ............ item.
Measurement
1.2
The measurement shall be the computed mass of the finished member comprising plates, rolled
sections, shear connectors, stiffeners, cleats, packs, splice plates and all fittings, without allowance for
tolerances for rolling margin and other permissible deviations from standard masses or nominal
dimensions, and excluding the masses of welds, bolts, nuts, washers, rivets and protective coatings.
1.3
No deductions shall be made for notches, cope holes, bolt and rivet holes, and the like, which are each
less than 0.03 square metres.
1.4
The computed mass of rolled and cast steel and cast iron shall be determined from the dimensions
shown on the Drawings on the following basis:
a)
rolled or cast steel, 7850 kilogrammes per cubic metre;
b)
cast iron, 7210 kilogrammes per cubic metre.
1.5
Deck panels shall be measured separately only when the deck panel is not integral with the main
member.
1.6
Bracings, external diaphragms and the like shall be measured separately as subsidiary steelwork only
when they are not integral with main members or deck panels.
1.7
Main members and deck panels shall be inclusive of connectors, stiffeners, internal diaphragms and
other integral components.
Fabrication of steelwork
Itemisation
1.8
Separate items shall be provided for fabrication of steelwork in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and
2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 2.N] and the following:
Table 1.8 Itemisation for Fabrication of steelwork
Group
Feature
I
1 Fabrication.
II
1 Main members.
2 Deck panels.
3 Subsidiary steelwork.
III
1 Rolled sections.
2 Plated rolled sections.
3 Plated girders.
4 Box girders.
IV
1 Comprised of different combinations of grades of steel.
V
1 Curved on plan or elevation.
VI
1 Tapering.

1. Fabrication and erection of steelwork
Item Coverage - Fabrication of steelwork
1.9
The items for fabrication shall be in accordance with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref 1.N] and include for:
a)
examining and checking for segregation, laminations, cracks and surface flaws and carrying out
any remedial measures required by the Overseeing Organisation in respect of such defects;
b)
cutting, marking off, drilling, notching, machining, treatment of outside arrises, smoothing to
slopes, form fitting, end and edge preparation and cambering;
c)
riveting, bolting, assembling and pre-heating;
d)
welds, packing plates, rivets, bolts (including holding down bolts), nuts and washers required to
fabricate and to complete the erection and installation on Site, together with spares and service
bolts, drifts, draw up cleats and the like;
e)
pre-production procedural trials;
f)
welder approval trials and provision of certificates;
g)
allowance for rolling margins, over-runs and other permissible deviations;
h)
checking of deviations in plate panels and rolled and built-up sections and of alignment at joints,
including taking measurements and observations and recording and supplying one copy of the
records to the Overseeing Organisation;
i)
preparation and supply of marked erection drawings, marking members for identification and
delivery in matching sequence;
j)
in the case of weathering steel blast cleaning after fabrication.
Erection of steelwork
Itemisation
1.10
Separate items shall be provided for erection of steelwork in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4
of GP 112 [Ref 2.N] and the following:
Table 1.10 Itemisation for Erection of steelwork
Group
Feature
I
1 Trial erection at the place of fabrication.
2 Permanent erection.
II
1 Different forms of steel substructure construction.
2 Different forms of steel superstructure construction.
Item Coverage - Trial erection at the place of fabrication
1.11
The items for trial erection at the place of fabrication shall be in accordance with clause 2.1 of GP 113
[Ref 1.N] and include for:
a)
temporary bracing or stays to prevent displacement including the provision and removal of
temporary attachments;
b)
proving dimensions, cambers and profiles and alignment at joints;
c)
match-marking members as required for permanent erection;
d)
dismantling;
e)
modifications and refitting of members as a result of the trial erection;
f)
bolts, nuts, washers, sockets, base plates, anchorage assemblies, drilling or forming holes and
pockets and casting in.

1. Fabrication and erection of steelwork
Item Coverage - Permanent erection
1.12
The items for permanent erection shall be in accordance with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref 1.N] and
include for:
a)
temporary bracing or stays to prevent displacement including the provision and removal of
temporary attachments;
b)
welder approval trials and provision of certificates;
c)
procedural trials;
d)
permanent bolted, riveted and welded connections including the provision of preheat and shelters

including taking measurements and observations and recording and supplying one copy of the
records to the Overseeing Organisation;
f)
drilling or forming holes and pockets and casting in bars, sockets, base plates and anchorage
assemblies;
g)
adhesives and epoxy or polyester mortar, bedding mortar or grout.

2. Miscellaneous metalwork
2.
Miscellaneous metalwork
Definition
2.1
Miscellaneous metalwork comprises items of metalwork incorporated into structures and shall include
ladders, brackets, handrails, access covers and frames, mesh panels, walkway panels, screens, grilles
and the like.
Units
2.2
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
miscellaneous metalwork ............ number.
Measurement
2.3
Items of metalwork which are included in the computed weight of fabricated items, other measured
items or separately measured elsewhere, shall not be measured as miscellaneous metalwork.
Itemisation
2.4
Separate items shall be provided for miscellaneous metalwork in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and
2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 2.N] and the following:
Table 2.4 Itemisation for Miscellaneous metalwork
Group
Feature
I
1 Miscellaneous metalwork.
II
1 Different types.
III
1 Different materials.
IV
1 Different sizes.
Item Coverage - Miscellaneous metalwork
2.5
The items for miscellaneous metalwork shall be in accordance with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref 1.N] and
include for:
a)
fabrication as clause 1.9 of this document;
b)
erection as clauses 1.11 and 1.12 of this document;
c)
protective system as clause 1.6 of CC 486/MMHW [Ref 4.N];
d)
structural concrete as clause 1.4 of CC 482/MMHW [Ref 3.N];
e)
formwork as clause 3.9 of CC 482/MMHW [Ref 3.N].

3. Corrugated steel buried structures
3.
Corrugated steel buried structures
Note
This measurement section is intended for situations where the Contractor is not designing the work and
pricing as a lump sum in accordance with CC 605/MMHW [Ref 5.N].
Units
3.1
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
corrugated steel buried structures (stating the length) ............ number.
Measurement
3.2
The length stated shall be the extreme length of the corrugated steel buried structure.
Itemisation
3.3
Separate items shall be provided for corrugated steel buried structures in accordance with clauses 2.2,
2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 2.N] and the following:
Table 3.3 Itemisation for Corrugated steel buried structures
Group
Feature
I
1 Corrugated steel buried structures.
II
1 Different types.
III
1 Different sizes.
IV
1 Different thicknesses or gauge.
Item Coverage - Corrugated steel buried structures
3.4
The items for corrugated steel buried structures shall be in accordance with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref
1.N] and include for:
a)
cutting, marking off, drilling, notching, bevels, skews, bends, edge preparation, cambering,
riveting, bolting and fabricating;
b)
marking members for identification and delivery in matching sequence;
c)
laps, rivets, bolts, nuts, washers and the like, spares and service bolts, drifts, draw up cleats and
the like;
d)
protective system as clause 1.6 of CC 486/MMHW [Ref 4.N];
e)
channels;
f)
awaiting the results of tests;
g)
paved invert.

4.
Metal facing units for reinforced earth structures
Note
This measurement section is intended for situations where the Contractor is not designing the work and
pricing as a lump sum in accordance with CC 606/MMHW [Ref 6.N].
Units
4.1
The units of measurement shall be:
a)
metal facing units ............ square metre.
b)
metal capping units and the like ............ linear metre.
Measurement
4.2
The measurement of metal facing units shall be the plane undeveloped area of the facing.
4.3
The measurement of metal capping units and the like shall be the measurement along the centre line.
Itemisation
4.4
Separate items shall be provided for metal facing and capping units in accordance with clauses 2.2,
2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 2.N] and the following:

4.5

6. Provision of welding team to carry out struct...
6.
Provision of welding team to carry out structural steel repairs
Units
6.1
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
provision of welding team to carry out structural steel repairs ............ hour or day.
Measurement
6.2
The provision of welding team to carry out structural steel repairs including welding, shot peening,
mechanical repairs and the like, shall only be measured where the task order expressly states to this
effect.
6.3
The measurement of provision of welding team to carry out structural steel repairs shall be each hour
during which the Contractor is required to Provide the Service as agreed with the Service Manager and
subject to a minimum measurement of two hours.
6.4
Measurement of part hours shall be rounded up to the nearest hour.
6.5
Periods when the welding team are not providing the service shall not be measured.
Itemisation
6.6
Separate items shall be provided for the provision of a welding team to carry out structural steel repairs
in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 2.N] and the following:
Table 6.6 Itemisation for Provision of welding team to carry out structural steel repairs
Group
Feature
I
1 Different welding teams to carry out structural steel repairs.
II
1 Internal location on bridge.
2 External location on bridge.
Item Coverage - Welding team to carry out structural steel repairs
6.7
The items for provision of a welding team to carry out structural steel repairs shall be in accordance
with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref 1.N] and include for:
a)
trial areas and trials;
b)
chemical composition testing;
c)
welder certification;
d)
welding procedures, including welding test joints;
e)
working at height;
f)
preparation and maintenance of the surfaces;
g)
marking;
h)
electrical equipment, wiring and connections;
i)
provision of all consumables including gases, welding rods and the like;
j)
provision of electrical power feeds by mains connection or generators;
k)
electrical power;
l)
recording & reporting;
m)
inspection and testing of completed weld;
n)
compliance with all legislation not limited to legislation affecting the use of vehicles on the public
highway and the like;
