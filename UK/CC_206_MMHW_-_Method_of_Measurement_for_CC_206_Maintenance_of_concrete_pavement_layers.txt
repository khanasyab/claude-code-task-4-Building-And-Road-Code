1. Reinstatement of trenches and openings in con...
1.
Reinstatement of trenches and openings in concrete pavements
Units
1.1
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
reinstatement of trenches and openings in concrete pavements ............ square metre.
Measurement
1.2
The measurement of reinstatement of trenches and openings in concrete pavements shall be
calculated using the width of the top surface to be reinstated excluding sides and edges with no
deductions made for openings of 1 square metre or less.
1.3
The top surface for the following features shall be the widths or areas described below unless stated
otherwise:
a)
for drains, sewers, piped culverts, service ducts and filter drains - the width shall be the internal
diameter of the pipe plus 600 mm;
b)
for kerbs, channels, edgings, combined kerb drainage systems, linear drainage channel systems
and the like - the width of the foundations;
c)
for chambers, gullies, traffic signs, traffic signals, road lighting columns and the like - the
horizontal area of the base slab or where no base slab is required the area of the bottom of the
excavation.
Itemisation
1.4
Separate items shall be provided for reinstatement of trenches and openings in concrete pavements in
accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 4.N] and the following:
Table 1.4 Itemisation for Reinstatement of trenches and openings in concrete pavements
Group
Feature
I
1 Reinstatement of each type of concrete pavement.
II
1 Different thicknesses or depths.
Item Coverage - Reinstatement of trenches and openings in concrete pavements
1.5
The items for reinstatement of trenches and openings in concrete pavements shall be in accordance
with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref 3.N] and include for:
a)
determination of the extent of the reinstatement and agreement with the Overseeing Organisation;
b)
unbound and bound subbase for pavement foundation construction as clause 1.13 of
CC 201/MMHW [Ref 5.N];
c)
rigid pavement construction as clause 1.5 of CC 203/MMHW [Ref 6.N];
d)
surface treatment for asphalt pavement surfaces as clause 1.4 of CC 204/MMHW [Ref 7.N];
e)
kerb units, special kerb units, channels, edgings, combined kerb drainage systems, and linear
drainage channel systems as clause 2.7 of CC 207/MMHW [Ref 9.N];
f)
footways, cycle tracks and paved areas as clause 1.6 of CC 207/MMHW [Ref 9.N];
g)
scarifying;
h)
milling as clause 5.6 of CC 205/MMHW [Ref 8.N];
i)
drilling holes;
j)
bringing to correct levels and surface regularity following settlement.

2. Full depth repairs to concrete pavement layer...
2.
Full depth repairs to concrete pavement layers
Units
2.1
The units of measurement shall be:
a)
full depth repairs, bay replacement repairs ............ square metre.
b)
reinstatement of subbase ............ cubic metre.
Measurement
2.2
The measurement of full depth repairs and bay replacement repairs shall be the summation of the
individual areas to be repaired as stated in the Contract.
Itemisation
2.3
Separate items shall be provided for full depth repairs and bay replacement repairs to concrete
pavement layers in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 4.N] and the following:
Table 2.3 Itemisation for Full depth repairs to concrete pavement layers
Group
Feature
I
1 Full depth repairs.
2 Bay replacement repairs.
3 Reinstatement of subbase.
II
1 Each pavement type.
III
1 In unreinforced slabs.
2 In reinforced slabs.
IV
1 Different thicknesses of slabs.
Item Coverage - Full depth repairs to concrete pavement layers
2.4
The items for full depth repairs and bay replacement repairs to concrete pavement layers shall be in
accordance with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref 3.N] and include for:
a)
saw cutting and drilling to full depth;
b)
excavation of acceptable earthworks material as clause 3.5 of CC 601/MMHW [Ref 11.N];
c)
excavation of unacceptable earthworks material as clause 3.6 of CC 601/MMHW [Ref 11.N];
d)
excavation in Hard Material as clause 4.4 of CC 601/MMHW [Ref 11.N];
e)
disposal of unacceptable earthworks material as clause 7.6 of CC 601/MMHW [Ref 11.N];
f)
completion of formation as clause 10.5 of CC 601/MMHW [Ref 11.N];
g)
dowel bars and tie bars including drilling and supports, cleaning, plugging with resin mortar and
de-bonding and compressive discs;
h)
unbound and bound subbase for pavement foundation construction as clause 1.13 of
CC 201/MMHW [Ref 5.N];
i)
joint filler board;
j)
rigid pavement construction as clause 1.5 of CC 203/MMHW [Ref 6.N];
k)
removal of loose material, standing water and debris.

4. Thin bonded repairs to concrete pavement laye...
4.
Thin bonded repairs to concrete pavement layers
Units
4.1
The units of measurement shall be:
a)
thin bonded repairs ............ square metre.
b)
joint repairs ............ linear metre.
c)
saw-cutting grooves ............ linear metre.
d)
sealing grooves ............ linear metre.
Measurement
4.2
The measurement of thin bonded repairs shall be calculated using the plan area of the top surface of
each repair patch excluding areas of joint sealant.
4.3
Thin bonded repairs and joint repairs shall only be measured separately when areas and lengths to be
repaired are stated in the Contract.
4.4
The measurement of saw-cutting grooves shall be the summation of the lengths of saw-cutting grooves
stated in the Contract.
4.5
The measurement of sealing grooves shall be the summation of the lengths of the sealed grooves
stated in the Contract.
Itemisation
4.6
Separate items shall be provided for thin bonded repairs and joint repairs in accordance with clauses
2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 4.N] and the following:

m).

1 Different thicknesses or depths.

plan and so in steps of 1 square metre.
