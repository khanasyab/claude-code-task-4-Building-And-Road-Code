Foreword
Foreword
This document is for incorporation into contracts by reference. It defines coverage of items in Bills of
Quantities and their method of measurement.
This document is applicable to contracts throughout the UK, complemented by the additional method of
measurement, specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.
Users are responsible for applying all appropriate documents applicable to their contract.
Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

1. Definitions
1.
Definitions
1.1
Unless otherwise stated in the contract the items in GC 109 [Ref 7.N] shall include for the removal of
superficial obstructions down to existing ground level.
1.2
With the exception of items measured under clause 4.3 of this document and those including for the
removal of stumps and roots, work below existing ground level in the demolition and removal of
foundations, drains and sewers specified in GC 109 [Ref 7.N], chambers, cellars, ground slabs,
carriageways, kerbs, pavings, backfilling and the like shall be measured under CC 601/MMHW [Ref
6.N].
1.3
Lowering (milling) of carriageway levels shall be measured under CC 205/MMHW [Ref 5.N].
1.4
The measurement of general site clearance shall include for the removal of superficial obstructions
down to existing ground level.
1.5
If no general site clearance item is measured and the contract requires items to be taken to tip, these
shall be measured within clauses 4.2, 4.3, 4.4, and 4.5 of this document.
1.6
All references to 'tip' shall mean a licensed facility which is either a licensed disposal facility or a
licensed recycling facility.

2. Site clearance
2.
Site clearance
Units
2.1
The units of measurement shall be:
a)
general site clearance ............ hectare.
b)
vegetation clearance ............ square metre.
c)
demolition of individual or groups of buildings or structures ............ item or cubic metre.
d)
demolition of vertical concrete barrier ............ linear metre.
e)
partial demolition of individual structures ............ item or cubic metre.
Measurement
2.2
The measurement of general site clearance shall be the plan area with no deduction made for
buildings, structures, carriageways and the like.
2.3
The measurement of partial demolition shall be as stated in the Contract.
2.4
The measurement of vegetation clearance shall be the plan area.
Itemisation
2.5
Separate items shall be provided for site clearance in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of
GP 112 [Ref 4.N] and the following:

4.
Take up or down and set aside for re-use or remove to store or tip
off site
Measurement
Note
Take up or down and set aside for re-use should only be used for those materials or items that are
required to be stored on site by the Contractor prior to re-use. Take up or down and remove to store off
site is for those materials or items which are required to be taken off site to a store designated and
described in the contract. These requirements should be detailed in clause SI.1.4f of GC 109 [Ref 7.N]
of the specification including the distance to the store.
4.1
The item(s) for take up or down and remove to tip off site shall be used only when no item for general
site clearance has been included in the Bill of Quantities for that particular area. In this case it is
essential that all items to be removed to tip off site are measured separately in accordance with clauses
4.2, 4.3, 4.4, and 4.5 of this document.
Units
4.2
The units of measurement shall be: Take up or down and set aside for re-use or remove to store or tip
off site the following:
a)
blockwork and stonework ............ cubic metre.
b)
paved areas and the like including brick paviours ............ square metre.
c)
brickwork ............ square metre.
d)
kerbs, channels, edgings, combined drainage and kerb blocks, linear drainage channel systems,
fencing, safety barriers, vehicle parapets, transitions, terminals, pedestrian guardrails and
pedestrian parapets and the like, copings, string courses and the like ............ linear metre.
e)
cable ............ linear metre.
f)
road lighting columns, brackets and wall mountings, traffic signs, posts and foundations, road
studs, cored thermoplastic node markers, gates, stiles, street furniture, feeder pillars,
communications cabinets, brackets, signal indicators, shelves, racking, frames, electronic units
and the like ............ number.
g)
chamber covers and frames, gully gratings and frames and the like ............ number.
h)
individual blocks, features or stones ............ number.
i)
extra over for excavation and disposal of concrete foundations to traffic signs ............ number.
Measurement
