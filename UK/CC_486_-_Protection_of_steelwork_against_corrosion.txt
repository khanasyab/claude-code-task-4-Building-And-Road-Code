1. General requirements for protection of steelw...
1.
General requirements for protection of steelwork against
corrosion
1.1
Protection of steelwork against corrosion, either on site or in the workshop shall be carried out in
accordance with this document.
1.2
The work specific requirements for protection of steelwork against corrosion for bridges, parapets and
other highway structures, except bearings and minor structures shall be as specified in
CC 486/WSR/001.
1.3
The work specific requirements for protection of steelwork against corrosion for bearings shall be as
specified in CC 486/WSR/001.
1.4
The work specific requirements for protection of steelwork against corrosion for minor structures shall
be as specified in CC 486/WSR/001.
1.5
The protection of steelwork against corrosion for traffic sign posts shall be in accordance with CC 120
[Ref 2.N].
Lighting requirements for protection of steelwork against corrosion
1.6
Work for protection of steelwork against corrosion shall only be carried out when the light intensity at
the workface is above 500 Lux over at least 1 m2, unless otherwise stated in CC 486/WSR/001.
1.7
Verification shall be undertaken for light intensity at the workface for protection of steelwork against
corrosion through testing with a light meter, complying with BS ISO/CIE 19476 [Ref 1.N].
1.8
The frequency of testing for light intensity at the workface for protection of steelwork against corrosion
shall be at least once prior to starting surface preparation, application of paint or paint inspection.
1.9
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N] shall apply to testing for light
intensity at the workface for protection of steelwork against corrosion.
1.10
The following Documentation for light intensity at the workface for protection of steelwork against
corrosion shall be submitted as continuous records: record of light intensity readings and the calibration
certificate for the light meter.
1.11
The requirements of "Records" in Section 3 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N] shall apply to records of light intensity
readings at the workface for protection of steelwork against corrosion and the calibration certificate for
the light meter.
Corrosion Protection of Ferrous Materials by Industrial Coatings for protection
of steelwork against corrosion
1.12
Corrosion Protection of Ferrous Materials by Industrial Coatings shall be undertaken by organisations
registered to and operating in compliance with a quality management scheme in accordance with
"Quality management schemes" in Section 7 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N].

2. Surface preparation for protection of steelwo...
2.
Surface preparation for protection of steelwork against corrosion
General requirements for surface preparation for protection of steelwork
against corrosion
2.1
The minimum substrate standard following surface preparation for the types listed below shall be as
defined in CM 431 [Ref 9.N].
1)
sound paint;
2)
sound metal coating; and
3)
sound galvanised steel;
2.2
The minimum surface cleanliness following preparation of steel substrates, for the grades listed below,
shall be as defined in BS EN ISO 8501-1 [Ref 42.N].
1)
Sa2½
2)
Sa3
3)
St3
2.3
The surface roughness following preparation of steel substrates, for the profiles listed below, shall be
as defined in BS EN ISO 8503-1 [Ref 35.N].
1)
Fine
2)
Medium
3)
Coarse
2.4
At the time of application of each coat of paint, the surfaces to be painted shall comply with all of the
following:.
1)
not have a residual dust level worse than Class 1 in accordance with BS EN ISO 8502-3 [Ref
36.N];
2)
meet the required substrate standard following surface preparation;
3)
be free from contamination, where contamination is defined in CM 431 [Ref 9.N]; and
4)
be free from moisture.
Cleaning requirements for surface preparation for protection of steelwork
against corrosion
2.5
The final workshop coats on external surfaces shall be wet cleaned on site, unless otherwise stated in
CC 486/WSR/002.
2.6
Cleaning solvents, other than water, shall not be used during the cleaning process, unless otherwise
stated in CC 486/WSR/002.
2.7
Where cleaning solvents, other than water, are permitted to be used during the cleaning process,
following their use, all traces of solvent shall be removed.
2.8
Inhibitors and salt extraction products shall not be used during any cleaning process.
Surface preparation of steel substrate for protection of steelwork against
corrosion
2.9
All prepared steel surfaces, except for edges, shall achieve preparation grade P2 in accordance with
BS EN ISO 8501-3 [Ref 41.N].
2.10
All edges of prepared steel surfaces shall achieve preparation grade P3 in accordance with BS EN ISO
8501-3 [Ref 41.N].

2.11
Areas of prepared steelwork shall be protected by the primer and next coat of paint, including stripe
coats where specified, before any cleaning down or other preparation of adjacent surfaces is
undertaken.
Surface cleanliness and roughness of steel substrate for protection of steelwork against
corrosion
2.12
All defects, including rogue peaks, shelling, rollovers/laminations or inclusions, following blast-cleaning
of steel surfaces shall be removed by grinding or abrading followed by subsequent blast-cleaning to
achieve the required standard of profile and visual cleanliness, where the defects are defined in CM
431 [Ref 9.N].
2.13
The surface profile of all prepared steel surfaces after blast-cleaning shall be free from embedded
abrasive particles when viewed through a 10X illuminated magnifying glass.
2.14
Verification shall be undertaken for the absence of embedded abrasive particles on all prepared steel
surfaces after blast-cleaning through visual inspection with a 10X illuminated magnifying glass.
2.15
The frequency of visual inspection for embedded abrasive particles on all prepared steel surfaces shall
be once for all steel surfaces prepared by blast-cleaning, prior to application of protective coatings.
2.16
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N] shall apply to visual inspection for
embedded abrasive particles on all prepared steel surfaces for protection of steelwork against
corrosion.
2.17
Following blast-cleaning, the surface roughness of steel surfaces prepared for the application of
protective systems shall comply with Table 5.1, Table 6.1, or Table 7.1.
2.18
Verification shall be undertaken for the surface roughness achieved by blast-cleaning of steel surfaces
through testing with a calibrated surface profile gauge conforming to BS EN ISO 8503-5 [Ref 34.N], or
ASTM D4417 [Ref 49.N] methods B or C.
2.19
The frequency of testing of the surface roughness achieved by blast-cleaning shall be once for every
prepared steel surface, prior to application of protective coatings.
2.20
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N] shall apply to testing of the
surface roughness achieved by blast-cleaning on all prepared steel surfaces for protection of steelwork
against corrosion.
2.21
The following Documentation for testing of the surface roughness of steel surfaces prepared for
corrosion protection by blast-cleaning shall be submitted as continuous records: test reports in
accordance with BS EN ISO 8503-5 [Ref 34.N] or ASTM D4417 [Ref 49.N].
2.22
The requirements of "Records" in Section 3 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N] shall apply to test reports for
verification of the surface roughness following blast-cleaning.
Soluble salts on steel substrates for protection of steelwork against corrosion
2.23
The presence of soluble salts on any steel substrate on which protective coatings are to be used shall
not exceed 20 mg/m2 in accordance with M-501 [Ref 11.N] or as per coating manufacturer's data sheet,
whichever is lower.
2.24
Verification shall be undertaken for steel substrates to confirm that soluble salts are below the specified
limit by sampling in accordance with BS EN ISO 8502-6 [Ref 37.N] and measuring in accordance with
BS EN ISO 8502-9 [Ref 38.N].
2.25
The frequency of sampling and subsequent measurement of soluble salts on steel substrates shall be
one sample per 50 m2 of bare steel, prior to application of protective coatings.
2.26
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N] shall apply to sampling and
measuring of soluble salts on steel substrates prepared for protection against corrosion.
2.27
The following Documentation for sampling and measuring of soluble salts on steel substrates shall be
submitted as continuous records: test report in accordance with BS EN ISO 8502-6 [Ref 37.N] and test
report in accordance with BS EN ISO 8502-9 [Ref 38.N].

Surface preparation of paint for protection of steelwork against corrosion
2.29
Paint that is to be subsequently overcoated or lapped over shall be free from any visible gloss that can
compromise adhesion.
2.30
Any edges of paint coats that are to be subsequently overcoated or lapped over shall be stepped back
by a minimum 30 mm.
Abrading for surface preparation for protection of steelwork against corrosion
2.31
Hard grinding wheels shall not be used for abrading.
2.32
Abrading shall not result in a polished surface in accordance with BS EN ISO 12944-4 [Ref 14.N].
2.33
Abrading shall be carried out in a multi-directional motion.
Blast-cleaning for surface preparation for protection of steelwork against
corrosion
2.34
Metallic blast-cleaning abrasives for surface preparation for protection of steelwork against corrosion
shall comply with BS EN ISO 11124-1 [Ref 21.N] and any of BS EN ISO 11124-2 [Ref 23.N], BS EN
ISO 11124-3 [Ref 24.N], BS EN ISO 11124-4 [Ref 22.N] or BS EN ISO 11124-5 [Ref 20.N].
2.35
Non-metallic blast-cleaning abrasives for surface preparation for protection of steelwork against
corrosion shall comply with BS EN ISO 11126-1 [Ref 26.N] and any of BS EN ISO 11126-3 [Ref 32.N],
BS EN ISO 11126-4 [Ref 25.N], BS EN ISO 11126-5 [Ref 28.N], BS EN ISO 11126-6 [Ref 27.N], BS
EN ISO 11126-7 [Ref 33.N], BS EN ISO 11126-8 [Ref 29.N], BS EN ISO 11126-9 [Ref 30.N] or BS EN
ISO 11126-10 [Ref 31.N].
2.36
Verification shall be undertaken for cleanliness of blast-cleaning abrasives for surface preparation for
protection of steelwork against corrosion through testing in accordance with ASTM D4940 [Ref 47.N].
2.37
The frequency of cleanliness testing of blast-cleaning abrasives for surface preparation for protection of
steelwork against corrosion shall be once for each blasting pot prior to each shift.
2.38
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N] shall apply to cleanliness testing
of blast-cleaning abrasives for surface preparation for protection of steelwork against corrosion.
2.39
The following Documentation for cleanliness of blast-cleaning abrasives for surface preparation for
protection of steelwork against corrosion shall be submitted as continuous records: test report in
accordance with ASTM D4940 [Ref 47.N].
2.40
The requirements of "Records" in Section 3 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N] shall apply to test reports for
cleanliness of blast-cleaning abrasives for surface preparation for protection of steelwork against
corrosion.
2.41
Non-metallic blast-cleaning abrasives for surface preparation for protection of steelwork against
corrosion shall not be reused.
2.42
During wet abrasive blast-cleaning for surface preparation for protection of steelwork against corrosion,
the air/water/abrasive shall be thoroughly mixed and projected onto the surface to be cleaned through a
single bore nozzle/tube.
2.43
Within 60 minutes of completing the wet blast-cleaning of a surface subject to protection against
corrosion, the whole of the cleaned surface shall be thoroughly washed with air and water to remove
any contamination, and any further deposits of abrasive on already rinsed surfaces removed in a
similar manner within 60 minutes of being deposited.
2.44
Surfaces subject to protection against corrosion that have been wet blast-cleaned shall be dry
blast-cleaned once they are free from surface moisture to remove flash rusting and achieve the
required surface profile.

General requirements for paint coatings for protection of steelwork against
corrosion
3.1
Paints for protection of steelwork against corrosion shall comply with CG 303 [Ref 44.N], where the
term paint refers to individual paint materials and protective paint coatings in general.
E/3.2
No nationally determined requirement is provided.
3.3
The paint Item Nos shall be as defined in CG 303 [Ref 44.N].
3.4
The requirements for "Product acceptance schemes" in Section 12 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N] shall apply to

4.
Hot dip galvanised coatings for protection of steelwork against
corrosion
General requirements for hot dip galvanised coatings for protection of
steelwork against corrosion
4.1
Hot dip galvanising shall be carried out by an organisation registered to and operating in compliance
with a quality management system complying with BS EN ISO 9001 [Ref 45.N].
4.2
The scope of registration to the quality management system for the organisation undertaking the hot
dip galvanising shall include the galvanising operations required for the works.
Product requirements for hot dip galvanised coatings for protection of
steelwork against corrosion
4.3
Hot dip galvanised coatings shall meet the requirements of BS EN ISO 1461 [Ref 8.N], unless
otherwise stated in CC 486/WSR/004.
4.4
The required hot dip galvanised coating thickness, where greater than that given in BS EN ISO 1461
[Ref 8.N], and surface preparation requirements shall be as stated in CC 486/WSR/004.
4.5
Prior to hot dip galvanising, steelwork shall be free from welding slag, oil or grease, paint, lacquers, and
contaminants as defined in CM 431 [Ref 9.N].
4.6
Hot dip galvanised surfaces shall not receive hexavalent chromate passivation treatments.
4.7
Vent holes drilled in hollow steel sections prior to hot dip galvanising shall be plugged after hot dip
galvanising and before any painting, in accordance with "Surface treatment (BS EN 1090-2 Section
10)" in Section 8 of CC 483 [Ref 51.N].
4.8
Steelwork shall not be distorted beyond the dimensional tolerances specified in CC 483 [Ref 51.N], as
a result of the hot dip galvanising process.
4.9
There shall be no liquid metal assisted cracking as a result of the hot dip galvanising process.
Requirements for hot dip galvanised surfaces to be painted for protection of steelwork against
corrosion
4.10
Hot dip galvanised surfaces to be painted shall not receive passivation treatments.
4.11
Where a hot dip galvanised coating is required on only part of a component it shall be applied before
the rest of the component receives paint.
4.12
Contamination of hot dip galvanised coatings which are to be painted shall be removed by wet cleaning
in accordance with "Surface preparation for protection of steelwork against corrosion " in Section 2 of
this document.
Verification requirements for hot dip galvanised coatings for protection of
steelwork against corrosion
4.13
Verification shall be undertaken for the thickness of galvanised coatings by measurement of the
galvanised coating thickness in accordance with BS EN ISO 1461 [Ref 8.N].
4.14
The frequency of measurement of galvanised coating thickness shall be as stated in BS EN ISO 1461
[Ref 8.N].
4.15
The requirements for "Verification" in Section 14 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N] shall apply to measurement of
galvanised coating thickness.
4.16
Verification shall be undertaken for the absence of distortion of galvanised steelwork through visual
inspection and measurement in accordance with CC 483 [Ref 51.N].
4.17
The frequency of visual inspection and measurement for distortion shall be at least once for all
galvanised components after quenching.

17.
Paint inspectors for protection of steelwork against corrosion
General requirements for paint inspectors for protection of steelwork against
corrosion
17.1
A paint inspector in accordance with this Section shall be appointed for the inspection and reporting of
the corrosion protection works, unless otherwise stated in CC 486/WSR/017.
17.2
The paint inspector shall be from an independent organisation.
17.3
The paint inspector shall hold a minimum level 2 coating inspection qualification certified by any of
ICorr/NACE/AMPP/Frosio.
17.4
The organisation that the paint inspector represents shall have a quality management system in place
and operating in accordance with "Quality management" in Section 5 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N].
Documentation requirements for paint inspectors for protection of steelwork
against corrosion
17.5
The following Documentation shall be submitted for each paint inspector prior to the commencement of
their appointment: name of paint inspector, evidence of paint inspector qualification and the following
details of the organisation they represent: name, address, and evidence of compliance with Quality
Management in Section 5 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N].
17.6
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 6.N] shall apply to evidence of paint
inspector qualification and details of the paint inspector and the organisation they represent.
