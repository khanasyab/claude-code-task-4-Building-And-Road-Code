CC 402
Temporary vehicle restraint systems
(formerly part of Series 400)
Version E/1.0.0
Summary
This document gives requirements for temporary vehicle restraint systems including temporary
safety barriers, terminals, transitions and crash cushions.
This document incorporates specific requirements for National Highways. Alternative versions of this document are
available for other Overseeing Organisations.
Feedback and Enquiries
Users of this document are encouraged to raise any enquiries and/or provide feedback on the content and usage of
this document to the dedicated National Highways team. The online feedback form for all enquiries and feedback
can be accessed at: www.standardsforhighways.co.uk/feedback.
This is a controlled document.

Foreword
This document gives requirements for the construction of works for the Overseeing Organisation.
This document forms part of the works specification.
The works specification is made up of both the Specification for Highway Works and the Works Specific
Requirements completed by the Specifier.
This document is applicable to contracts throughout the UK, complemented by the additional
specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.
Users are responsible for applying all appropriate documents applicable to their contract.
Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

1.
General requirements for temporary vehicle restraint systems
1.1
Temporary vehicle restraint systems shall comply with "General requirements for road restraint
systems" in Section 1 of CC 400 [Ref 4.N].
1.2
Temporary vehicle restraint systems shall comply with "General requirements for
non-harmonised/non-designated road restraint products" in Section 2 of CC 400 [Ref 4.N].
1.3
Temporary vehicle restraint systems shall comply with "Vehicle restraint systems" in Section 3 of
CC 400 [Ref 4.N].
Quality management requirements for temporary vehicle restraint systems
1.4
The supply, installation, maintenance and repair of temporary vehicle restraint systems shall be carried
out by organisations registered to and operating in compliance with a quality management scheme in
accordance with "Quality management schemes" in Section 7 of GC 101 [Ref 1.N].

2. Temporary safety barriers
2.
Temporary safety barriers
2.1
The Contractor shall provide, install and remove all temporary safety barriers.
2.2
The design of the temporary safety barrier installation shall be a Contractor design item, unless
otherwise stated in CC 402/WSR/002.
2.3
The design of temporary safety barrier installations shall be in accordance with CD 377 [Ref 6.N].
2.4
The requirements for "Contractor design" in Section 17 of GC 101 [Ref 1.N] shall apply to the design of
temporary safety barrier installations unless this is not a Contractor design item.
Product requirements for temporary safety barriers
2.5
Temporary safety barriers shall be compliant with BS EN 1317-1 [Ref 10.N] and BS EN 1317-2 [Ref
9.N].
2.6
The temporary safety barriers shall meet the performance characteristics stated in CC 402/WSR/002.
Schedule for temporary safety barriers
2.7
Temporary safety barriers shall be supplied, installed and removed as specified in CC 402/WSR/002.
Documentation requirements for temporary safety barriers
E/2.8
The following Documentation shall be submitted for temporary safety barriers prior to the
commencement of incorporation into the works: either.
1)
a declaration of performance in accordance with BS EN 1317-5 [Ref 5.N], or
2)
a letter of acceptance which:
a)
confirms that the product complies with BS EN 1317-1 [Ref 10.N] and BS EN 1317-2 [Ref
9.N]following a review of the certified results of impact testing;
b)
identifies any restrictions on the use of the product;
c)
is signed by one of the independent reviewers appointed by the Overseeing Organisation,
in accordance with CD 377 [Ref 6.N];
d)
includes a copy of the independent reviewer's letter of appointment, issued by the
Overseeing Organisation.
E/2.9
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 1.N] shall apply to a declaration of
performance or a letter of acceptance for temporary safety barriers.
2.10
The Documentation submitted shall demonstrate that the proposed product meets the specification
requirements for the temporary safety barriers.
2.11
Certified results of impact testing shall comprise of the following:.
1)
test result certificate from an accredited laboratory; and
2)
test report in accordance with BS EN 1317-1 [Ref 10.N] and BS EN 1317-2 [Ref 9.N]; and
3)
video or high speed film of impact test annotated showing date, test number and vehicle restraint
system performance; and
4)
still photographs of complete installation prior to impact including anchorage points; and
5)
still photographs of vehicle before and after impact and full drawings of tested items; and
6)
certification from the manufacturer that the item tested complies with the drawings supplied.

3. Temporary terminals
3.
Temporary terminals
3.1
The Contractor shall provide, install and remove all temporary terminals.
3.2
The design of the temporary terminal installation shall be a Contractor design item, unless otherwise
stated in CC 402/WSR/003.
3.3
The design of temporary terminal installations shall be in accordance with CD 377 [Ref 6.N].
3.4
The requirements for "Contractor design" in Section 17 of GC 101 [Ref 1.N] shall apply to the design of
temporary terminal installations.
Product requirements for temporary terminals
3.5
Temporary terminals shall be compliant with BS EN 1317-1 [Ref 10.N] and either DD ENV 1317-4 2002
[Ref 3.N] or PD CEN/TS 1317-7 [Ref 8.N].
3.6
The temporary terminals shall meet the performance characteristics stated in CC 402/WSR/003.
Schedule for temporary terminals
3.7
Temporary terminals shall be supplied, installed and removed as specified in CC 402/WSR/003.
Documentation requirements for temporary terminals
E/3.8
The following Documentation shall be submitted for temporary terminals prior to the commencement of
incorporation into the works: a letter of acceptance which:.
1)
confirms that the product complies with BS EN 1317-1 [Ref 10.N] and either DD ENV 1317-4
2002 [Ref 3.N] or PD CEN/TS 1317-7 [Ref 8.N] following a review of the certified results of impact
testing;
2)
identifies any restrictions on the use of the product;
3)
is signed by one of the independent reviewers appointed by the Overseeing Organisation, in
accordance with CD 377 [Ref 6.N];
4)
includes a copy of the independent reviewer's letter of appointment, issued by the Overseeing
Organisation.
E/3.9
The requirements for "Documentation" in Section 2 of GC 101 [Ref 1.N] shall apply to a letter of
acceptance for temporary terminals.
3.10
The Documentation submitted shall demonstrate that the proposed product meets the specification
requirements for temporary terminals.
3.11
Certified results of impact testing shall comprise of the following.
1)
test result certificate from an accredited laboratory; and
2)
test report in accordance with either DD ENV 1317-4 2002 [Ref 3.N] or PD CEN/TS 1317-7 [Ref
8.N]; and
3)
video or high speed film of impact test annotated showing date, test number and performance
class; and
4)
still photographs of complete installation prior to impact including anchorage points; and
5)
still photographs of vehicle before and after impact and full drawings of tested items; and
6)
certification from the manufacturer that the item tested complies with the drawings supplied.

4. Temporary transitions
4.
Temporary transitions
4.1
The Contractor shall provide, install and remove all temporary transitions.
4.2

Product requirements for temporary transitions
4.5
Temporary transitions shall be compliant with either BS EN 1317-1 [Ref 10.N] and either DD ENV
1317-4 2002 [Ref 3.N] and/or Assessment Method A1 or A2 in PD CEN/TR 1317-10 [Ref 7.N].
4.6
The temporary transitions shall meet the performance characteristics stated in CC 402/WSR/004.
Schedule for temporary transitions
4.7

of incorporation into the works: a letter of acceptance which:.
1)
confirms that the product complies with BS EN 1317-1 [Ref 10.N] and either DD ENV 1317-4
2002 [Ref 3.N] and/or Assessment Method A1 or A2 of PD CEN/TR 1317-10 [Ref 7.N] following a
review of the certified results of impact testing;
2)
identifies any restrictions on the use of the product;
3)
is signed by one of the independent reviewers appointed by the Overseeing Organisation, in
accordance with CD 377 [Ref 6.N];
4)
includes a copy of the independent reviewer's letter of appointment, issued by the Overseeing
Organisation

acceptance for temporary transitions.
4.10
The Documentation submitted shall demonstrate that the proposed product meets the specification
requirements for temporary transitions.
4.11

7.N]; and
2)
video or high speed film of impact test annotated showing date, test number and vehicle restraint
system performance; and
3)
still photographs of complete installation prior to impact including connected vehicle restraint
system/structure; and
4)
still photographs of vehicle before and after impact and full drawings of tested items; and
5)
certification from the manufacturer that the item tested complies with the drawings supplied.
