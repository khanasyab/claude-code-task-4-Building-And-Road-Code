Introduction
Introduction
Background
Traffic detection is an operational function that provides input data to systems, which process the data
to deliver particular outputs. Traffic detection systems are used to support:
1)
Safety - early and accurate detection of incidents that triggers appropriate operational responses;
2)
User satisfaction - early detection of events, enabling communication of issues affecting network
performance to customers, with appropriate alternative advice; and
3)
The smooth flow of traffic - incident resolution and network availability is enhanced by early and
accurate incident detection.
Assumptions made in the preparation of this document
The assumptions made in GG 101 [Ref 5.N] apply to this document.

1. Scope
1.
Scope
Aspects covered
1.1
This document shall be used for the provision, siting, coverage and design of traffic detection system
installations for roadside technology and communications on motorways and all-purpose trunk roads.
1.1.1
This document should be read in conjunction with the general requirements in GG 102 [Ref 7.N], GG
104 [Ref 9.N], GD 304 [Ref 3.N]and with all other DMRB documents relevant to the design of the
particular works to be undertaken.
1.2
This document shall not be used for the design of traffic detectors for traffic signalling systems.
Implementation
1.3
This document shall be implemented forthwith on all schemes involving traffic detection systems on the
Overseeing Organisations' motorways and all-purpose trunk roads according to the implementation
requirements of GG 101 [Ref 5.N].
Use of GG 101
1.4
The requirements contained in GG 101 [Ref 5.N] shall be followed in respect of activities covered by
this document.

Outputs from the design of traffic detectors for roadside technology and
communications
2.4
The outputs from the design of traffic detectors for roadside technology and communications shall
include all details for the construction of the supporting infrastructure and the installation of traffic
detectors.
2.4.1
For each traffic detector installation for roadside technology and communications the design outputs
should include:
1)
the detector type and outstation equipment
2)
the installation location, using a formal location referencing system;
3)
mounting arrangement;
4)
support structures: posts, gantries, and methods of fixing to support structures;
5)
foundations for support structures;
6)
design coverage and visibility splay drawings for stopped vehicle detection systems;
7)
the installation technique for the loops if the slots are to be cut after resurfacing;
8)
the number of turns for detector loops;
9)
termination locations for detectors;
10)
the installation requirements for below ground detector systems that are not loops;
11)
the site acceptance testing and commissioning requirements for other non loops detectors;
12)
test certificate format for other non loop systems
13)
electrical and telecommunications installations, including cables, chambers and ducting; housings
and enclosures; housing lock types; numbers of keys for locks; and switchgear and wiring;
14)
road restraint systems; and
15)
position and orientation of all components of the proposed installation relative to other items of
existing and proposed road infrastructure, including the edge of the carriageway, so that the
following can be identified:
a)
the environment in which the installation is to take place;
b)
the factors that can influence the construction methodology and scheduling of the proposed
installation; and
c)
the factors that can affect the operational performance of the proposed installation in the
future.

3. Detectors for motorway incident detection and...
NOTE
Primary sites for detectors are the first to be sited when designing a scheme. When the primary sites
have been identified, other sites can be located in relation to the primary sites in accordance with
spacing requirements.
3.2
Where detection functions for roadside technology and communications require multiple detector sites,
the spacing of detectors between primary sites shall meet operational requirements for detection
resolution.
NOTE
MIDAS-based incident detection uses a detection resolution of 500 m +100/-200 m for all types of
highway, with a scheme average tolerance of +50/-100 m.
3.3
One set of loop-based detectors for MIDAS detection equipment for roadside technology and
communications shall be used on both carriageways mid-link, when the majority of detectors to be
installed on the link are non-loop detectors.
NOTE 1
These non-loop detectors include (but are not limited to) side-fire radars and magnetometers.
NOTE 2
The midpoint loops-based MIDAS site are to be used as a reference point for the calibration of all other
types of detectors on the link.
3.4
A gap in traffic detection provision for roadside technology and communications shall not be created
between existing traffic detection locations or diverge connector roads adjacent to the scheme.
NOTE
For MIDAS-based incident detection, a gap of less than four signal sites creates a discontinuity in the
network detection provision.

4.
Stopped vehicle detection for roadside technology and

Provision of stopped vehicle detection for roadside technology and
communications
4.1
On all links comprising a motorway incorporating variable mandatory speed limits (VMSL) without a
hard shoulder, i.e. all lane running, stopped vehicle detection (SVD) for roadside technology and
communications shall be provided to meet the operational requirements of the Overseeing
Organisation.
NOTE 1
An all lane running carriageway link could have SVD provided throughout the length of that carriageway
link in accordance with these requirements, even if there were some sections of hard shoulder within
that link.
NOTE 2
A controlled motorway carriageway link could not have any SVD provision, in accordance with these
requirements, even if that link contained some sections without a hard shoulder.
NOTE 3
The operational regime and resultant SVD provision is determined separately for each carriageway.
4.2
On all intra-junction sections that are within the scheme extents of a motorway incorporating VMSL
without a hard shoulder, i.e. all lane running, SVD for roadside technology and communications shall
be provided to meet the operational requirements of the Overseeing Organisation.
NOTE
An intra-junction section in an all lane running scheme which retains hard shoulders would have SVD
provision in accordance with these requirements.
4.3
SVD for roadside technology and communications shall provide at least 95% coverage of mainline
running lanes per link per carriageway.
4.4
SVD for roadside technology and communications shall provide at least 95% coverage of each
mainline emergency area within the SVD coverage area.
4.5
SVD for roadside technology and communications shall provide at least 95% coverage of each
mainline place of relative safety within the coverage area.
NOTE
All safety benefits of SVD are only realised where there is traffic camera coverage of the SVD coverage
area.
4.6
SVD coverage on merge connector roads for roadside technology and communications shall
commence from the back of the merge connector road nose and extend downstream.
4.7
SVD coverage on diverge connector roads for roadside technology and communications shall extend
from the mainline to the back of the diverge connector road nose.
4.7.1
SVD coverage on the diverge connector road for roadside technology and communications may be
extended downstream of the back of the diverge connector road nose where;
1)
there is a location-specific safety hazard relating to stopped vehicles on a diverge connector
road, and
2)
where supported by a safety risk assessment that identifies SVD as a proportionate control
measure.
4.8
Connector roads with restricted access within a link which has VMSL without a hard shoulder, i.e. all
lane running, shall have SVD coverage in accordance with this section for roadside technology and
communications.
NOTE
Connector roads with restricted access include provision for depots, works units and turnaround points.
Both merge and diverge connector roads are included.

may be extended downstream of the back of the diverge connector nose or upstream of the merge
connector nose where:

4.10

4.11

of relative safety.
4.12

consideration of the wider highway and roadside technology design, such that they continuously
provide the required coverage throughout the scheme's design life.
NOTE

future vegetation growth or a planned change to the roadside assets or layout which could affect
detection reach or accuracy.
4.12.1

should be co-located with other roadside technology equipment for efficient construction and ongoing
maintenance.
4.12.2
SVD detectors for roadside technology and communications may be standalone or attached to gantry

Traffic counting detectors for roadside technology and

Provision of traffic counting detectors for roadside technology and
communications
5.1
Traffic counting detectors for roadside technology and communications shall be provided where traffic
monitoring capabilities are required.
NOTE
Traffic counting detectors are provided to monitor traffic flows, vehicle speeds and vehicle classification.
5.1.1
The Overseeing Organisation should be consulted to confirm the need for traffic monitoring for roadside
technology and communications.
