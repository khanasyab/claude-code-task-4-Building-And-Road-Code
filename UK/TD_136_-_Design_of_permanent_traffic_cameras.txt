Introduction
Introduction
Background
Permanent traffic cameras for roadside technology and communications are provided to assist with the
management of traffic on roads.
The primary users of the permanent traffic cameras are control centre operators. The Operators are
able to move and zoom the cameras to monitor and manage congestion and incidents. The cameras
give a bird's eye view of what is happening which helps the Operator to decide on the support needed.
Permanent traffic camera coverage is particularly beneficial in the following situations:-
1)
where there are high traffic flows with recurrent congestion or queues, particularly in urban areas
at terminal junctions, interchanges, or where the number of lanes reduces;
2)
at interchanges with high traffic flows where there are converging/diverging movements or
sub-standard construction features such as sharp bends;
3)
at locations such as tunnels where traffic congestion or accidents can create dangerous
environmental conditions;
4)
on exposed bridges or motorways at high elevations above sea level where inclement weather
conditions such as high cross winds frequently can occur and occur hazardous conditions.
5)
Verifying incident reports, such as from customers or system generated i.e. stopped vehicle
detection alerts.
Assumptions made in the preparation of this document
The assumptions made in GG 101 [Ref 4.N] apply to this document.

1. Scope
1.
Scope
Aspects covered
1.1
This document shall be used for the provision, siting and design of permanent traffic camera
installations on motorways and all-purpose trunk roads.
1.1.1
This document should be read in conjunction with the general requirements in GG 102 [Ref 6.N], GG
104 [Ref 8.N], GD 304 [Ref 3.N] and with all other DMRB documents relevant to the design of the
particular works to be undertaken.
1.2
This document shall not be used for the design of temporary traffic cameras.
Implementation
1.3
This document shall be implemented forthwith on all schemes involving permanent traffic cameras on
the Overseeing Organisations' motorways and all-purpose trunk roads according to the implementation
requirements of GG 101 [Ref 4.N].
Use of GG 101
1.4
The requirements contained in GG 101 [Ref 4.N] shall be followed in respect of activities covered by
this document.

2. General requirements for permanent traffic ca...
2.
General requirements for permanent traffic cameras for roadside
technology and communications
Design of supports and foundations for permanent traffic cameras for roadside
technology and communications
2.1
The design of supports and foundations for permanent traffic cameras for roadside technology and
communications shall be in accordance with CD 354 [Ref 1.N] design of minor structures.
Design of gantries for permanent traffic cameras for roadside technology and
communications
2.2
The design of gantries and gantry mounting arrangements for permanent traffic cameras for roadside
technology and communications shall be in accordance with CD 365 [Ref 5.N] portal and cantilever
signs/signals gantries.
Design of road restraint systems for permanent traffic cameras for roadside
technology and communications
2.3
The design of road restraint systems for permanent traffic cameras for roadside technology and
communications shall be in accordance with CD 377 [Ref 7.N].
Outputs from the design of permanent traffic cameras for roadside technology
and communications
2.4
The outputs from the design of permanent traffic cameras for roadside technology and communications
shall include all details for the construction of the supporting infrastructure and the installation of
permanent traffic cameras.
2.4.1
For each permanent traffic camera installation for roadside technology and communications the design
outputs should include:
1)
type and variant of each permanent traffic camera;
2)
the installation location, using a formal location referencing system;
3)
location of each permanent traffic camera outstation;
4)
mounting arrangement;
5)
support structures: posts, gantries, and methods of fixing to support structures;
6)
foundations for support structures;
7)
road restraint systems;
8)
design coverage and visibility splay drawings for each permanent traffic camera;
9)
installation requirements for the permanent traffic camera and traffic enforcement equipment;
10)
electrical and telecommunications installations, including cables, chambers and ducting; housings
and enclosures; housing lock types; numbers of keys for locks; switchgear and wiring; and
11)
position and orientation of all components of the proposed installation relative to other items of
existing and proposed road infrastructure, including the edge of the carriageway, so that the
following can be identified:
a)
the environment in which the installation is to take place;
b)
the factors that can influence the construction methodology and scheduling of the proposed
installation; and
c)
the factors that can affect the operational performance of the proposed installation in the
future.
2.5
Information models shall be produced to define the details and dimensions of each permanent traffic
camera installation for roadside technology and communications.
2.5.1
Drawings and non-graphical information should be produced to provide any additional details or views
needed for the construction of each permanent traffic camera installation for roadside technology and
communications.

Provision of permanent traffic cameras for roadside technology
and communications
Provision of permanent traffic cameras for roadside technology and
communications
3.1
Where an operational need has been established, permanent traffic cameras for roadside technology
and communications shall be provided on motorways and all-purpose trunk roads.
NOTE 1
Operational requirements includes maintenance and calibration of ramp metering equipment, which
requires visibility of the equipment.
NOTE 2
The operational benefits of permanent traffic cameras can be maximised by combining with variable
message signs to provide prompt information to drivers and thus reduce the frequency of secondary
accidents.
3.1.1
Permanent traffic cameras for general surveillance for roadside technology and communications should
be provided in the locations detailed in Table 3.1.1.

3. Provision of permanent traffic cameras for ro...
3.1.2
The control centre operators should be consulted regarding the proposed provision of permanent traffic
cameras for roadside technology and communications on schemes to confirm the operational need for
permanent traffic cameras.
NOTE
Local operational intelligence can provide insights on problematic locations which are not easily
identified through traditional data analysis.
3.1.3
Incident management system logs and road traffic collision data should be used to determine incident
hot-spots on existing roads.
3.2
On motorways incorporating variable mandatory speed limits where the hard shoulder is not used as a
running lane or where it is dynamically used as a running lane, permanent traffic camera coverage for
roadside technology and communications shall enable:
1)
operators to see in excess of 95% of the total scheme area and be able to interpret the images
correctly;
2)
view of a 1.75 m cube to represent a minimum of 5% of the image height at maximum zoom; and,
3)
coverage of emergency roadside telephones.
3.3
On motorways incorporating variable mandatory speed limits without a hard shoulder, permanent traffic
camera coverage for roadside technology and communications shall include the whole carriageway,
emergency areas and maintenance hard standings, where provided to enable:
1)
operators to see the entire mainline carriageway running lanes and the full extent of emergency
areas with no blind spots in all ambient lighting levels;
2)
view of a 1.75 m cube to represent a minimum of 5% of the image height at maximum zoom; and,
3)
views of emergency roadside telephones in emergency areas that represent a minimum of 10%
of the image height.
NOTE
A blind spot exists if a 1.75 m wide cube target (or the remaining visible area of this target when
obscured) does not represent at least 5% of image height and width.
3.4
On motorways where stopped vehicle detection is provided, permanent traffic camera coverage for
roadside technology and communications shall enable control centre operators to verify stopped
vehicle detection alerts.
3.5
Permanent traffic cameras for roadside technology and communications associated with an emergency
area shall include a pre-set position to view and zoom into the emergency area in addition to the home
position and any other positions agreed with the Overseeing Organisation.
3.6
Permanent traffic camera coverage of an emergency roadside telephone located at a new short section
of hard shoulder or hard shoulder defined as a place of relative safety shall represent a minimum of
10% of image height for roadside technology and communications.
3.7
Permanent traffic camera coverage drawings showing the viewing areas of each camera shall be
produced for roadside technology and communications.
NOTE 1
Permanent traffic camera coverage drawings provide plan view and cross-section splays.
NOTE 2
The permanent traffic camera coverage drawings can be used to verify coverage during installation and
once in operation.

4. Siting of permanent traffic cameras for roads...
4.
Siting of permanent traffic cameras for roadside technology and
communications
Siting of permanent traffic cameras for roadside technology and
communications
4.1
Permanent traffic cameras for roadside technology and communications shall be located and mounted
so that they provide the required coverage.
4.1.1
The siting and mounting of permanent traffic cameras for roadside technology and communications
should take coverage, environmental conditions, image stability and whole life costs into account.
4.1.2
Permanent traffic cameras for roadside technology and communications should be co-located with
other roadside technology equipment, for efficient construction and ongoing maintenance.
