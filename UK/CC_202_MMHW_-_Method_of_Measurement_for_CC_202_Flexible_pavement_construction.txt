Foreword

Quantities and their method of measurement.
This document is applicable to contracts throughout the UK, complemented by the additional method of
measurement, specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.
Users are responsible for applying all appropriate documents applicable to their contract.
Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

1. Flexible pavement construction
1.
Flexible pavement construction
Units
1.1
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
base course, lower base course, upper base course, binder course and surface course ............
square metre.
Measurement
1.2
The measurement of base course, lower base course, upper base course, binder course and surface
course shall be calculated using the width of the top surface of the course with no deductions made for
openings of 1 square metre or less.
1.3
The width of the "top surface" of the course shall be the width required by the Contract, excluding
sloping sides or edges.
Note
The adjustment items in Group X in clause 1.4 of this document are optional and to be determined on a
project specific basis only.

2. Regulating course
2.
Regulating course
Units
2.1
The units of measurement shall be:
a)
regulating course ............ tonne or cubic metre.
Measurement
2.2
The measurement of regulating course by tonne shall be calculated from the tonnage of material
certified by the Overseeing Organisation multiplied by the factor for the particular aggregate used.
2.3
The tonnage certified by the Overseeing Organisation shall be only that material included on delivery
tickets which is incorporated in the Permanent Works in the locations and to the extent and thickness
required by the Contract.
2.4
Material in excess of the requirements of the Contract and material used for any other purpose shall not
be included within the certified tonnage.
2.5
The measurement of regulating course by cubic metres shall be the volume of material measured to
the outlines required by the Contract.
Note
It is recommended that where a significant quantity of regulating material is anticipated to be required,
the regulating course be measured in cubic metres. Measurements by tonnage will only be used
exceptionally where there will be significant difficulties in measuring the volume.
Itemisation
2.6
Separate items shall be provided for regulating course in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of
GP 112 [Ref 2.N] and the following:
Table 2.6 Itemisation for Regulating course
Group
Feature
I
1 Each group or type of regulating course.
Note: Each item description which includes Group I Feature 1 shall relate to the tabulated list of the
different constituent aggregate types and their factors included in the Bill of Quantities.
Item Coverage - Regulating course
2.7
The items for regulating course shall be in accordance with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref 1.N] and include
for:
a)
flexible pavement construction as clause 1.5 of this document;
b)
weighing, tickets and copies.

3. Bond coat
3.
Bond coat
Units
3.1
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
bond coat ............ square metre.
Measurement
3.2
For the purposes of measurement any reference to bond coat shall be deemed to include tack coats.
3.3
Bond coat shall only be measured separately when the Contract requires a bond coat to be applied to
any existing (not new) bound substrate.
Note
Existing bound substrate can include planed or milled asphalt (inlay), un-planed or un-milled asphalt
(overlay) and rigid pavement construction whether planed or un-planed.
3.4
Bond coat which is a component part of any newly installed specified rigid or flexible pavement option
e.g. between asphalt layers or on newly installed concrete pavement, shall not be separately measured.
Itemisation
3.5
Separate items shall be provided for bond coat in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112
[Ref 2.N] and the following:
Table 3.5 Itemisation for Bond coat
Group
Feature
I
1 Bond coat.
II
1 Different types.
III
1 Different rates of spread.
Item Coverage - Bond coat
3.6
The items for bond coat shall be in accordance with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref 1.N] and include for:
a)
making good after sampling and testing;
b)
designing and verifying mixes;
c)
grading, measuring, mixing and depositing materials;
d)
making joints;
e)
cleaning surfaces;
f)
protection and masking and unmasking of kerb units, drainage channels, chamber covers, gully
gratings, expansion joints, road studs, road markings and the like and obtaining clean markings;
g)
cutting back, preparing and working on or up to adjacent faces, surfaces and features.
