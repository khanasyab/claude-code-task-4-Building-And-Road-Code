Summary
This document provides instructions for specifiers with respect to environmental, cultural and
habitat management, management of material assets and waste, timber products, carbon
management and reporting.
This document incorporates specific requirements for National Highways. Alternative versions of this document are
available for other Overseeing Organisations.
Feedback and Enquiries
Users of this document are encouraged to raise any enquiries and/or provide feedback on the content and usage of
this document to the dedicated National Highways team. The online feedback form for all enquiries and feedback
can be accessed at: www.standardsforhighways.co.uk/feedback.
This is a controlled document.

Foreword
Foreword
This document provides specifier instructions for the production of the works specific requirements for
GC 103 Environment, sustainability and carbon management.
This document does not form part of the works specification.
The works specification is made up of both the Specification for Highway Works and the works specific
requirements completed by the Specifier.
This document is applicable for contracts throughout the UK, complemented by the additional
specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.
Users are responsible for applying all appropriate documents applicable to their contract.
Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

1.
Environmental, cultural and habitat management
Quality management schemes
1.1
The establishment and maintenance of landscape and associated land-based activities shall be carried
out by organisations registered to and operating in compliance with a quality management scheme in
accordance with "Quality management schemes" in Section 7 of GC 101 [Ref 4.N].
Environmental management plan
1.2
The Environmental Management Plan (EMP) shall be as stated in GC 103/WSR/001.
Works Specific Requirements to be provided within the WSR template where applicable are:
SI.1.2
The availability of an environmental management plan shall be [select one from: Provided (provide link
to EMP developed under LA 120 [Ref 3.N]), required to be carried out, screening required for EMP, not
required].
Environmental Management Plan - First Iteration
1.3
Where the availability of an EMP has been flagged as "required to be carried out", an EMP shall be
produced in accordance with LA 120 [Ref 3.N].
1.4
Where the availability of an EMP has been flagged as "screening required for EMP", a screening
exercise in accordance with LA 102 [Ref 9.N] shall be carried out.
1.5
The following Documentation shall be submitted for screening in accordance with LA 102 [Ref 9.N]
where an environmental management plan has been flagged as "screening required for EMP" prior to
the commencement of all works: screening report in accordance with LA 102 [Ref 9.N].
1.6
Where the availability of an environmental management plan has been flagged as "screening required
for EMP", and the screening process that has been carried out in accordance with LA 102 [Ref 9.N]
indicates that an environmental plan is required, an environmental management plan shall be produced
in accordance with the requirements for the first iteration EMP in LA 120 [Ref 3.N] prior to the
commencement of the works.
Environmental management plan - Second iteration
1.7
Where the availability of an environmental management plan has been flagged as "Provided", the
environmental management plan shall be revised in accordance with the requirements for the second
iteration environmental management plan in LA 120 [Ref 3.N] prior to the commencement of the works.
Environmental management plan - First and second iteration record requirement
1.8
The following Documentation shall be submitted for all works falling within the scope of LA 120 [Ref
3.N] where an environmental plan is required prior to the commencement of all works: an
environmental management plan produced in accordance with LA 120 [Ref 3.N].
Environmental management plan - Third Iteration
1.9
Where there is an environmental management plan for the works in place, on completion of the works,
the environmental management plan shall be revised and updated in accordance with the requirements
for the third iteration environmental management plan in LA 120 [Ref 3.N].
1.10
The revised and updated environmental management plan shall be submitted in accordance with the
requirements for the third iteration environmental management plan in LA 120 [Ref 3.N].

2. Management of material assets and waste
2.
Management of material assets and waste
2.1
The management of construction and operational materials, site arisings and waste shall be as stated
in GC 103/WSR/002.
Works Specific Requirements to be provided within the WSR template where applicable are:
SI.2.1
The availability of the materials assets and waste assessment shall be [select one from: Provided
(provide link to materials asset and waste assessment developed under LA 110 [Ref 6.N]), required to
be carried out, none required].
2.2
Where there is no assessment of material assets and waste in place prior to the commencement of the
works, and the availability of a material assets and waste assessment has been flagged as "required to
be carried out", a material assets and waste assessment shall be carried out in accordance with LA 110
[Ref 6.N].
2.3
The following Documentation shall be submitted for where the need for a material assets and waste
assessment has been flagged as "required to be carried out" prior to the commencement of all works: a
material assets and waste assessment in accordance with LA 110 [Ref 6.N].
Management of site arisings and waste
2.4
The following Documentation shall be submitted for the management of construction and operational
materials, site arisings and waste in accordance with LA 110 [Ref 6.N] prior to the commencement of
the works: waste carrier registration, landfill licence and waste transfer documentation.
Monitoring of site arisings and waste
2.5
Site arisings and waste shall be monitored in accordance with LA 110 [Ref 6.N] and the materials
assets and waste assessment.
2.6
The following Documentation for the monitoring of site arisings and waste shall be submitted as
continuous records: monitoring documentation in accordance with LA 110 [Ref 6.N].
2.7
The requirements of "Records" in Section 3 of GC 101 [Ref 4.N] shall apply to site arisings and waste
monitoring documentation.

3. Timber and products containing wood supplied ...
3.
Timber and products containing wood supplied under the contract
3.1
All timber and wood-derived products for supply or use in the works shall be supplied in accordance
with UK Govt Timber Procurement Policies (TPP), the EU Timber Regulation (EUTR) or a FLEGT
Voluntary Partner Agreement (VPA) where supplied from a country outside of the UK or EU in
accordance with UK Govt Timber Procurement Advice Note UK Govt TPAN [Ref 12.N].
3.2
The following Documentation shall be submitted for timber and wood derived products supplied under
Forestry Certification Schemes approved as Category A evidence that meet the UK government's
criteria for sustainability and legality prior to the commencement of all works: FSC Certificates or
Programme for Endorsement of Forest Certification certificates.
3.3
The following Documentation shall be submitted for timber and wood derived products supplied under
Forestry Certification Schemes approved as Category B evidence that meet the UK Govt criteria for
sustainability and legality prior to the commencement of all works: Evidence required under UK Govt
TPP Framework for Evaluation Category B evidence DEFRA (TPP Cat B) [Ref 11.N].
3.4
The following Documentation shall be submitted for all other schemes other than certification schemes
that the forest source meets the UK government's criteria for sustainability and legality prior to the
commencement of all works: Forest Law Enforcement, Governance and Trade (FLEGT) Voluntary
Partnership Agreement (VPA).

4. Carbon management and reporting
4.
Carbon management and reporting
E/4.1
Carbon management shall be in accordance with PAS 2080 [Ref 1.N].
E/4.2
Embodied carbon for all materials, fuels, transport, waste and waste transport for all works shall be
assessed using the National Highways Carbon Emissions Calculation Tool ( NH CECT [Ref 7.N]) or
equivalent.
E/4.3
The following Documentation for embodied carbon shall be submitted as continuous records in
accordance with the National Highways Carbon Tool Guidance ( NH CT(G) [Ref 8.N]).
E/4.4
The requirements of continuous records in Section 3 of GC 101 [Ref 4.N] shall apply to embodied
carbon.
Environmental Product Declaration (EPD)
E/4.5
Where available, the following documentation shall be submitted for the environmental impact
assessed in accordance with BS EN 15804 [Ref 10.N] of products incorporated into the permanent
works prior to the commencement of the works: Environmental Product Declaration (EPD).
