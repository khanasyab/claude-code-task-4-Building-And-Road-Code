Introduction
Introduction
Background
This document represents the combination of two former advice documents HA 39/98 'Road edge
details' and HA 83/99 'Safety aspects of road edge drainage features'.
The document has rationalised any duplication between the two source documents and does not
include any information in the two source documents that is not related to road edge drainage features.
The document includes the figures and requirements for edge of pavement drains previously shown in
the B and F Series of HCD drawings in MCHW.
Section 3 of the document includes requirements and advice on surface water channels. Surface water
channels are described in further detail in document CD 521 [Ref 9.N]. In order to prevent duplication of
information, Section 3 of this document includes several cross-references to CD 521 [Ref 9.N].
Tables summarising scenarios for use and pros and cons of the types of drainage solution available are
presented in CG 501 [Ref 3.N].
This document supersedes the figures and requirements previously shown in the Highway Construction
Details (HCD), B series (all) and F series drawings F15, F16, F18, F19, F20 and F21.
This document provides requirements and advice used to develop the design outputs required to
deliver the works in accordance with CC 500 [Ref 5.N].
Assumptions in the preparation of this document
The assumptions made in GG 101 [Ref 10.N] apply to this document.

1. Scope
1.
Scope
Aspects covered
1.1
This document shall be used for the design of edge of pavement drainage details.
NOTE
The figures in this document depict pavement edge details dealing principally with drainage aspects of
highway verges and central reserves.
Implementation
1.2
This document shall be implemented forthwith on all schemes involving highway drainage on the
Overseeing Organisation's motorway and all-purpose trunk roads according to the implementation
requirements of GG 101 [Ref 10.N].
Use of GG 101
1.3
The requirements contained in GG 101 [Ref 10.N] shall be followed in respect of activities covered by
this document.
Health and safety
1.4
Unless protected from direct traffic, road edge drainage features shall be capable of withstanding the
structural loading imposed on them during normal usage and the structural loading that can be imposed
on them during lane closures and consequent trafficking of hard shoulders.
1.5
Road edge drainage features shall be designed to mitigate the effects on errant vehicles leaving the
carriageway.
NOTE
The road edge drainage features can present a potential hazard to errant vehicles leaving the
carriageway.
1.6
The selection of road edge drainage features shall be guided by the health and safety issues
associated with the maintenance needs of the features which is particularly important for central
reserve drainage where difficulty of access can exacerbate maintenance interventions.
NOTE 1
The road edge drainage feature plays a major part in road safety by removing surface water quickly.
NOTE 2
A suitable road edge drainage feature prevents damage to the structural foundations of the
carriageway. This, in turn, reduces the requirement for maintenance.
NOTE 3
Maintenance on motorways and other high speed roads involves a higher than normal degree of risk,
both to road users and maintenance operatives.
NOTE 4
Safety issues associated with rural roads differ from those associated with urban roads.

2. Combined surface and sub-surface filter drain...
Figure 2.3d Combined surface and sub-surface filter drain surface Type Y
NOTE 1
Combined surface and sub-surface filter drains with Type Y surfaces as shown on Figure 2.3d are more
suitable for roads with flatter gradients where flow along the surface into catchpits is less assured.
NOTE 2
Combined surface and sub-surface filter drains with Type W surfaces as shown on Figure 2.3b have a
greater fall towards the centre of the drain because the reduced permeability of the thick surface layer
can result in greater flows along the surface.
NOTE 3
Guidance on the requirements for the pipe, bedding and trench of a combined surface and sub-surface
filter drain is provided in CD 533 [Ref 4.N].
2.3.1
The use of surface and sub-surface filter drains in central reserves should be avoided in order to
reduce health and safety risks during maintenance interventions.
2.3.2
Where combined surface and sub-surface filter drains are used in cuttings they may serve as carrier
drains.
NOTE
Separate fin or narrow filter drains are not required where combined surface and sub-surface filter
drains in cuttings function as carrier drains.
2.4
Filter, sub-base and topsoil materials for combined surface and sub-surface filter drains shall be
extended to the edge of the pavement layer.
2.5
The surface treatment to the combined surface and sub-surface filter drain shall be either topsoil or
sub-base.
2.6
The geotextile shown in Figure 2.3a shall be fixed in a pavement layer to provide required topsoil
thickness.
2.7
Where the filter drain utilises Type B filter media, a geotextile membrane shall be provided between the
filter drain and overlaying layers for drain Types W, X and Y.
2.8
The combined surface and sub-surface filter drains shall not result in saturated downstream conditions.
2.8.1
The diameter of combined surface and sub-surface filter drains should be limited to 300mm.

2.9
Combined surface and sub-surface drains shall not be used in narrow verges subject to use by
equestrians.
Upper section of combined surface and sub-surface filter drains
2.10
The upper section of combined surface and sub-surface filter drains shall be designed to mitigate the
risk of stone scatter.
NOTE 1
Managing risks associated with stone scatter is described in DMRB CD 525 [Ref 1.I].
NOTE 2
The treatment options depicted in Figure 2.3 incorporate measures to reduce stone scatter.
2.10.1
Where possible, more then one type of upper section should be specified in the contract with the final
choice being left to the contractor.
2.11
A lip shall be provided between the edge of carriageway and the top of the combined surface and
sub-surface filter drain.
NOTE
The purpose of the lip is to enable continuous over the edge drainage to be maintained and to reduce
the effect of build-up of vegetation.
2.12
The depth of the lip between the edge of carriageway and the top of the combined surface and
sub-surface filter drain depends on the the upper section type selected and shall be as shown in Figure
2.3.
2.12.1
Where Type 7 chambers as shown in CD 536 [Ref 1.N]are used to collect surface flows from combined
surface and sub-surface filter drains the chamber top should be set as dimensioned below the depth of
the lip.
2.13
The top surface of a combined surface and sub-surface filter drain shall be dished to allow high flows to
be channelled along the surface and into catchpits.
2.13.1
Catchpit grating levels should be calculated to suit the falls toward the centre of the drain.
Lower section of combined surface and sub-surface filter drains
2.14
The lower section of combined surface and sub-surface filter drains shall be designed as filter drains
and generally in accordance with the drawings in DMRB CD 533 [Ref 4.N].
2.14.1
Where possible, more than one type of lower section should be specified in the contract with the final
choice being left to the contractor.
NOTE
The lower section of combined surface and sub-surface filter drains are generally formed with Type B
material.
2.14.2
To avoid migration of fine sands and silts into the filter material, the lower section of combined surface
and sub-surface drains laid in fine sands and silts should be Type J or K as shown in DMRB CD 533
[Ref 4.N].
2.14.3
Lightweight aggregate may be used as the filter material for combined surface and sub-surface filter
drains Type G, H and I as shown in DMRB CD 533 [Ref 4.N].
2.15
To prolong the life of the combined surface and sub-surface filter drain, a geotextile membrane shall be
placed between the upper and lower sections of the drain for drains Type G, H and I as detailed in
DMRB CD 533 [Ref 4.N].
NOTE
The upper and lower sections of the drain are terms that approximate to the upper and lower half of the
drain, and do not refer to the point where the filter material (or concrete) changes from one Type to
another.
Asset recording
2.16
Combined surface and sub-surface filter drains shall be recorded as continuous assets as detailed in
DMRB CD 535 [Ref 6.N].

3. Surface water channels
3.
Surface water channels
Profiles and dimensions

7. Edge-of-pavement drains
7.
Edge-of-pavement drains
7.1
Edge-of-pavement drains shall be included on all roads except the following:
1)
roads with combined surface and sub-surface drains;
2)
roads comprising rigid or flexible carriageways with over-the-edge drainage; nor,
3)
roads with free draining sub-grade.
NOTE
The purpose of edge-of-pavement drainage is to provide sub-surface drainage for the pavement layers,
sub-base and capping (if permeable). Sub-surface drainage ensures that the road does not fail
prematurely through water-related deterioration.
7.2
Edge-of-pavement drains shall be formed with fin drains or narrow filter drains.
NOTE 1
Typical fin drain and filter drain types are included within this section.
NOTE 2
The edge-of-pavement details are included within the figures in this document.
7.3
The fin drain and narrow filter drain installation details shall be modified according to the relevant edge
of pavement detail shown in this document.
7.3.1
The selection of a fin drain or filter drain system to drain pavement layers of a road in a cutting should be
influenced by considerations of possible groundwater, drainage during construction and whether or not
a longitudinal carrier drain facility is necessary to transport surface water runoff from the carriageways.
NOTE
Fin drains and narrow filter drains can be unsuitable for dealing with high groundwater flows or where a
longitudinal carrier drain is necessary to transport surface water runoff from the carriageways.
7.3.2
Where the subbase comprises cement bound granular mixture (CBGM) or where cement or lime
stabilisation is proposed, the drain selected should be less vulnerable to the build-up of calcium-based
deposits.
7.4
Narrow filter drains shall always be included on the low side of carriageways on embankments.
7.4.1
Drainage design should allow as wide a range of edge-of-pavement drain types as possible in order to
deliver the most effective solution.
7.5
Fin drains and narrow filter drains which form part of the permanent works shall not be used for the
disposal of surface water runoff during construction.
7.6
Fin drains and narrow filter drains shall be a minimum of 75mm from the edge of the surface water
channel.
7.7
Fin drains shall have one geotextile face in contact with the side of the excavation.
7.8
Narrow filter drains shall have one face in contact with the pavement construction.
7.9
The side of the fin drain with the greater permeability shall be facing towards and be in contact with the
pavement construction.

Appendix A. Fin drain testing
A1
Test method for the percentage contact area of drainage core
The test determines the area of one face of a drainage core which will be in contact with a geotextile
filter as a percentage
The apparatus required is to be as follows:
1)
loading device able to apply a compressive load of at least 2kN and having a flat steel base;
2)
flat steel loading plate 200 mm x 200 mm;
3)
printers ink and roller (or pad);
4)
sheet of thin compressible rubber; and,
5)
planimeter.
The test procedure is to be as follows:
1)
cut three representative test specimens 200 mm x 200 mm (Â±2 mm);
2)
apply ink to one 200 mm x 200 mm face of a specimen and cover with a sheet of plain paper and
a thin compressible rubber sheet;
3)
place the prepared specimen in to the loading device and gradually apply the load of 2 kN and
maintain for 5 minutes;
4)
release the load and remove the specimen and separate it from the paper;
5)
using the planimeter find the total area of the paper which has received an imprint;
6)
repeat for all specimens.
The percentage contact area = (total area of imprint/area of test specimen) x 100
The report is to include:
1)
a reference to this method;
2)
sample identification details;
3)
individual and mean percentage contact areas; and,
4)
details of any deviation from the specified test procedure.
Alternative methods of determining the percentage contact area may be employed with the prior
approval of the Overseeing Organisation.
A2
Test method for determining the thickness of the fin drain core under specified
normal and shear stresses
The test determines the thickness of the fin drain under sustained normal and shear stresses. A long
term thickness (at 100,000 hours) is calculated by extrapolation and a short term equivalent normal
load which produces the long term thickness is determined.
The apparatus required is as follows:
1)
a suitable compression testing machine, which is to have a vertical travel at least the nominal
thickness of the specimen. It is to be capable of sustaining the necessary loads to within 1%
accuracy for the duration of the test;
2)
the compression testing apparatus, which is to include a fixed base plate and parallel moveable
top plate with flat steel surface with sufficient friction to permit the development of the required
shear forces;
3)
a means of measuring the mean thickness of the specimen to an accuracy of 0.01mm.
As an alternative to 1) and 2) above, an appropriate inclined plane and kentledge system can be
employed to produce the normal and shear loads.
