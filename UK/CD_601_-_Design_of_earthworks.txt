Introduction
Introduction
Background
Earthworks design is heavily dependent on readily available external standards. However, design
outputs are not standardised across industry. This document aims to provide a standardised consistent
flow of requirements from the design (DMRB) to the relevant work specific requirements (MCHW).
This document provides requirements and advice to develop the design outputs to deliver works in
accordance with CC 601 [Ref 14.N] and CC 606 [Ref 39.N]. This document complements "Design
outputs for work specific items and activities" in section 9 of CD 622 [Ref 32.N].
For many earthworks design outputs there is no criteria, advice, or guidance given in this document.
The designer is expected to use their experience and expertise, backed up by reference to existing
standards, codes of practice, and industry guidance documents as referenced in CD 622 [Ref 32.N], to
make appropriate design decisions.
Whilst CD 601 [Ref 11.N] is a geotechnical design document, the designer is expected to liaise with all
relevant disciplines (such as drainage, structures, pavement, etc.) in the preparation of geotechnical
designs.
Capping materials
The outcomes, requirements, and the record of outputs required for the design of capping materials
(previously considered by MCHW Series 600 to be earthworks materials) are presented in CD 225 [Ref
9.N].
Assumptions made in the preparation of this document
The assumptions made in GG 101 [Ref 31.N] apply to this document.
Mutual Recognition
Where there is a requirement in this document for compliance with any part of a British Standard or
other technical specification, that requirement may be met by compliance with the Mutual Recognition
clause in GG 101 [Ref 31.N].

1. Scope
1.
Scope
Aspects covered
1.1
This document shall be used to define the outcomes, requirements, and the record of outputs required
from the design of earthworks on all schemes involving the construction, improvement, and
maintenance of geotechnical structures on the Overseeing Organisations' motorway and all-purpose
trunk roads.
1.2
This document shall apply to the design of all earthworks, including:
1)
excavation (including cuttings);
2)
fill (including embankments);
3)
special geotechnical measures (SGM);
4)
ground improvement;
5)
ground treatment (including of naturally occurring cavities and disused mine workings); and,
6)
reinforced soil and anchored earth structures.
NOTE
Special geotechnical measures are defined in CD 622 [Ref 32.N].
1.3
This document shall not be used for the design of capping materials for pavement foundations.
NOTE
The design for new pavement foundations is covered in CD 225 [Ref 9.N].
Implementation
1.4
This document shall be implemented forthwith on all schemes involving the construction, improvement,
and maintenance of geotechnical structures on the Overseeing Organisations' motorway and
all-purpose trunk roads according to the implementation requirements of GG 101 [Ref 31.N].
Use of GG 101
1.5
The requirements contained in GG 101 [Ref 31.N] shall be followed in respect of activities covered by
this document.

2. General earthworks requirements
2.
General earthworks requirements
Recording of the earthworks design
2.1
The design of earthworks shall be in accordance with CD 622 [Ref 32.N].
2.2
The earthworks design outputs shall be recorded in the Geotechnical Design Report in accordance with
CD 622 [Ref 32.N].
NOTE
Design outputs not applicable to the works or where the design utilises a default position do not need to
be presented in the Geotechnical Design Report.
2.3
Any earthwork, or part of earthwork, referred to by a design output shall be given a unique reference in
accordance with CD 622 [Ref 32.N].
Earthworks layout drawings or models
2.4
The design outputs for earthworks layout drawings or models shall include:
1)
the unique reference of the earthworks;
2)
the earthwork type;
3)
the earthwork layout drawing(s) or model reference where the earthwork is described; and,
4)
the earthwork layout drawing(s) or model title where the earthwork is described.
2.5
The requirements of geotechnical drawings stated in CD 622 [Ref 32.N] shall apply to the earthworks
layout drawings.
2.6
The earthwork type shall identify the specific earthwork item and activity.
NOTE
Example earthwork types are excavation, earthworks fill, gabion wall, ground anchor(s) etc.
Earthworks construction procedures documents for specific activities
2.7
Earthworks construction procedures documents shall present earthworks works specific requirements
where design outputs are too complex or varied to identify within this document.
2.8
Earthworks construction procedures documents shall include requirements on geometry, constructor
design, performance, activities, verification including method and frequency, documentation
submission, and limitations of construction activities.
NOTE 1
Multiple earthworks construction procedures documents can be presented within a single document.
NOTE 2
Earthworks construction procedures documents can make reference to earthworks layout drawings and
models.
2.9
Requirements given in an earthworks construction procedures document shall be:
1)
drafted in a form that gives contractual instruction to the constructor only;
2)
able to be complied with safely;
3)
drafted in a form that does not limit the application of the SHW and gives freedom to the specifier
to apply the SHW content to what is needed for a specific work; and,
4)
drafted to provide a clear distinction between product and installation (workmanship)
requirements.
NOTE 1
The Overseeing Organisation can provide guidance notes on document layout, style, and drafting rules
for consistent presentation and content of the MCHW, if requested.
NOTE 2
Earthworks construction procedures documents can take the form of an industry recognised and
appropriate specification, such as ICE SGT [Ref 6.I] etc.
2.10
Earthworks construction procedures documents shall be included in the Geotechnical Design Report.

2. General earthworks requirements
2.11
The design outputs for earthworks construction procedures documents for specific activities shall
include:
1)
the unique reference of the earthworks;
2)
the earthwork type;
3)
the earthworks construction procedure document reference; and,
4)
the earthworks construction procedure title.
Earthworks surface finished profile
2.12
The design outputs for earthworks surface finished profile shall include the vertical tolerances above
and below design profile for each earthwork, in units of mm.
NOTE 1
Tolerances for earthworks surface finished profile are based upon the end-use of the earthwork.
Tolerances stated for unloaded areas, typically including side slopes, can be greater than for pavement
or structural foundation formations, where tight control can be required.
NOTE 2
Surface level tolerances on formations beneath pavements are presented in CD 225 [Ref 9.N].
NOTE 3
Tolerances can take into account the material being worked, for example, when it is not be possible to
achieve strict tolerances in a ripped in situ rock.
Verification of earthworks surface finished profile
2.13
Requirements for verification of earthworks surface finished profile shall be presented in an earthworks
construction procedures document in accordance with this Section.
2.14
The method of verification of earthworks surface levels shall be stated in the earthworks construction
procedures document.
NOTE
Example methods of verification include land survey or visual inspection against spot target heights /
pegs etc.
Restrictions on temporary works for earthworks
2.15
The design of temporary works and construction practices shall be complimentary to and not have an
adverse affect on the design of the permanent works.
NOTE 1
The design of temporary works can include:
1)
the effects of adjacent works, such as methods of stockpiling materials, use of plant, or siting of
temporary buildings or structures, on the stability of excavations or earthworks fills;
2)
support to excavations during construction, and the timing and sequence of removal of this
support;
3)
lowering of groundwater to enable earthworks to be constructed;
4)
earthworks protection layer thickness;
5)
compaction at earthworks edges;
6)
effects on operative network;
7)
compaction plant around pipes; and/or
8)
excavation by blasting.
NOTE 2
Recommendations and guidance for the design of temporary works and construction practices for
earthworks is provided in BS EN 16907-3 [Ref 15.N], BS 5975 [Ref 6.N], BS 6031 [Ref 4.N] and HSE
(website) [Ref 23.N].
2.16
The design outputs for restrictions on temporary works for earthworks shall include any restrictions
resulting from the permanent works design.

General classification of earthworks materials
3.1
The earthworks design shall only use acceptable earthworks materials.

General requirements for the compaction of earthworks fill
6.1

NOTE 1
Control procedures for the compaction of earthworks fill includes method and end-product compaction

NOTE 2
Guidance on design of the compaction of fill materials for earthworks is provided in Parsons AW (1992)

NOTE 3
Earthworks material properties testing methods, including in situ density and water content, are
described in "Sampling and testing methods for earthworks materials" in Section 16 of this document.
NOTE 4
Alternative testing of in situ density and water content of compacted earthworks fill can include
continuous compaction control as described in PD CEN/TS 17006 [Ref 16.N].
Method compaction of earthworks fill
6.2
The design outputs for method compaction of earthworks fill shall include compaction of the finishing
earthworks fill layer beneath pavements.
NOTE
Additional compaction requirements, such as additional passes or limitations on plant, on the finishing
earthworks fill layer beneath pavements can provide potential benefit to the degree of compaction
achieved.
6.2.1
For Class 1A, Class 1B, Class 2A, Class 2B, Class 2C, or Class 2D earthworks materials, the minimum
number of passes when compacting the finishing earthworks fill layer beneath pavements should be
twice the stated value.
NOTE
The minimum number of passes for method compaction of earthworks fill is stated in Table 6.5 in
"Method compaction of earthworks fill" in Section 6 of CC 601 [Ref 14.N].
6.3
The design outputs for method compaction of earthworks fill shall include:
1)
the frequency for confirmatory testing;
2)
the test method for confirmatory testing; and,
3)
the frequency of compaction compliance testing.
NOTE
Method compaction has been developed to produce a minimum state of compaction equal to 10% air
voids at a water content at the dry limit for acceptability for the earthworks material being compacted.
For cohesive earthworks materials, the water content at which 10% air voids or less would be achieved
is roughly equivalent to a maximum moisture condition value of 12.5. For granular earthworks
materials, the water content at which 10% air voids or less would be achieved will be higher than for
cohesive earthworks materials; for example, for a well graded sand with moisture condition value of
14.5 is expected to achieve 10% air voids or less.
6.3.1
Confirmatory testing of method compaction should be included where there is a risk that the minimum
state of compaction will not be achieved.
NOTE
Occasions where confirmatory testing of method compaction of earthworks fill is used can include
where access for compaction plant is limited or an earthworks fill material displays a wide range of
results in investigative testing or is potentially sensitive to environmental conditions, e.g. rain, snow etc.
6.3.2
Confirmatory testing of method compaction should be by in situ density and water content testing of the
compacted earthworks fill.
6.3.3
Compaction compliance testing should be included in all instances of method compaction of
earthworks fill.
6.3.4
Compaction compliance testing should be by recording the type and category of plant used, measured
compacted layer thicknesses, and recorded number of passes made.

similar performance characteristics in similar ground conditions.
11.3
Requirements for ground improvement shall be presented in an earthworks construction procedures
document in accordance with "Earthworks construction procedures documents for specific activities" in
Section 2 of this document.
11.4
The requirements for ground improvement shall include:
1)
permitted methods of ground improvement;
2)
requirements for ground improvement; and,
3)
conditions or restrictions of site trials.
11.4.1
Requirements for ground improvement should include any known restrictions.
11.4.2
Restrictions to ground improvement should include physical site constraints, such as space limitations
or obstructions; geotechnical restrictions, such as known limitations of the existing ground or
restrictions on use of certain materials; and environmental restrictions, such as noise or vibration or
known environmental permit restrictions.
11.4.3
At least one site trial should be undertaken for each instance of ground improvement.
11.4.4

11.4.5

main ground improvement works.

improvement have been met by the construction procedures used in the site trial.

of the main ground improvement works commencing.

improvement works commencing.

15. Instrumentation and monitoring of earthworks
15.
Instrumentation and monitoring of earthworks
15.1
The design of instrumentation and monitoring of earthworks shall include the measurement and
acceptance criteria of the performance characteristics of earthworks during and post construction.
NOTE 1
Instrumentation used to measure performance characteristics of earthworks can comprise a wide range
of equipment, including simple methods such as monitoring pegs, to complex or elaborate
instrumentation such as vibrating wire pressure cells or strain gauges..
NOTE 2
Examples of measurement of performance without installation of instrumentation include where
measurement is achieved through comparison of land surveys undertaken at different times.
15.2
Design outputs for instrumentation and monitoring of earthworks shall be presented in an earthworks
construction procedures document in accordance with "Earthworks construction procedures documents
for specific activities" in Section 2 of this document.
15.3
The design outputs for instrumentation and monitoring of earthworks shall include:
1)
purpose of instrumentation and monitoring;
2)
performance characteristics;
3)
duration of monitoring;
4)
a monitoring action plan; and,
5)
instrument decommissioning.
15.3.1
The design outputs for instrumentation and monitoring of earthworks should include the following
specific details:
1)
instrumentation types;
2)
instrument locations;
3)
instrumentation installation requirements;
4)
monitoring frequency; and,
5)
calibration requirements.
NOTE
The specific details for instrumentation and monitoring of earthworks are not included in
pre-construction phase design where they are subject to construction phase design.
15.3.2
Performance characteristics for instrumentation and monitoring of earthworks should include the
parameters to be monitored, documentation requirements including format of any documentation, and
any other particular requirements or restrictions required by the design.
15.3.3
Monitoring of instrumentation for permanent earthworks should be designed to satisfy the design need
to demonstrate the performance characteristics have been met.
NOTE 1
The duration of the monitoring can be given in units of time or in terms of physical parameters.
NOTE 2
Examples of duration of the monitoring in terms of physical parameters includes 'until X mm of
settlement has occurred', 'until pore water pressures have stabilised', or 'until a material strength of X
has been achieved', etc.
15.3.4
Monitoring of instrumentation for permanent earthworks should be designed to be undertaken for
minimum 12 months following completion of the construction of the earthwork.
15.3.5
Instrument decommissioning for instrumentation and monitoring of earthworks should state whether the
instrumentation is to remain permanently, or whether and when the instrumentation is to be removed.
15.3.6
Instrument locations for monitoring of earthworks should be presented on earthworks layout drawings
or models in accordance with "Earthworks layout drawings or models" in Section 2 of this document.
15.3.7
Instrumentation for monitoring of earthworks should be installed in accordance with manufacturer's
instructions, where these exist.

NOTE 2
An example of the frequency of monitoring in terms of event is 'once earthworks fill has been placed
and compacted to X level'.
NOTE 3
The monitoring frequency can vary dependent on conditions. For example, an increase in the
frequency during critical activities, such as adjacent excavations, or reduction in frequency when no
works are being undertaken in the area.
15.4
The earthworks monitoring action plan shall state trigger or threshold levels for monitoring and actions
to be undertaken on breach of levels, including notification.
15.4.1
Trigger or threshold values should be based on risk assessment and take account of the sensitivity of
the design situation to the parameters being monitored, the anticipated performance of the earthwork
over time, and the sensitivity of the earthwork.
NOTE
Trigger or threshold values often adopt a tiered approach with actions increasing in severity as each
trigger or threshold value is passed; for example, green - continue, amber - notify and increase
monitoring, red - notify and implement further actions, black - make safe and cease works.
15.4.2
Notification for instrumentation and monitoring of earthworks stated in the earthworks monitoring action
plan should include description of when notification is to be made, persons to be notified, and
documentation to be provided with any notification.
NOTE 1
Examples of when notification can be made include a time-based report, such as weekly monitoring
reports, and following specific events, such as when certain trigger levels are met.
NOTE 2
Persons to be notified for instrumentation and monitoring can vary depending on the notification type or
reason, for example on achieving a specified trigger level.

Introduction
Introduction
Background
This National Application Annex gives the Welsh Government-specific requirements for the definition of
the outcomes, requirements, and the record of outputs required from the design of earthworks on all
schemes involving the construction, improvement, and maintenance of geotechnical structures on the
Overseeing Organisations' motorway and all-purpose trunk roads.
Assumptions made in the preparation of this document
The assumptions made in GG 101 [Ref 2.N] apply to this document.
Mutual Recognition
Where there is a requirement in this document for compliance with any part of a British Standard or
other technical specification, that requirement may be met by compliance with the Mutual Recognition
clause in GG 101 [Ref 2.N].

Contaminants in acceptable earthworks materials
W/1.1
The method and frequency of testing of contaminants in acceptable earthworks materials shall be
informed by risk assessment.
W/1.1.1
Identification of contaminant concentration, and the method and frequency of testing of earthworks
materials should be undertaken in accordance with the Environment Agency's Land contamination risk
management processes together with the requirements of Natural Resources Wales.
