Terms and definitions
Terms and definitions
Term
Definition
Cycle track
A track separate from the main carriageway for use by cyclists.
Footway
A way over which the public have a right of way on foot
Full reconstruction
Maintenance treatment that involves replacement of all of the
bound layers and extends into the foundation
High amenity footways and cycle
tracks
Surfaces specifically selected for their decorative qualities,
incorporating special colours, textures, or finishes, constructed
and maintained to a high standard.
Kerb unit
An overarching term referring to kerbs, edgings and channels
Partial reconstruction
Replacement of all of the bound layers
Shared use
A facility used by more than one type of user; for example
pedestrians and cyclists
Surface replacement
Replacement of an asphalt surface course.
Surface treatment
The application of a material to an existing surface to improve
characteristics such as skid resistance, macrotexture, noise
and service life.

1. Scope
1.
Scope
Aspects covered
1.1
The requirements in this document shall be followed to design pavement maintenance treatments for
footways and cycle tracks.
NOTE 1
The requirements for the design for pavement maintenance are covered in CD 227 [Ref 2.N].
NOTE 2
The requirements for the design of footways and cycle tracks are covered in CD 239 [Ref 7.N].
NOTE 3
The requirements for general maintenance including vegetation management of asset delivery contract
areas are covered in GM 701 [Ref 1.I].
NOTE 4
The requirements for general inspection of asset delivery contract areas are covered in GS 801 [Ref
1.N].
NOTE 5
The requirements for surface materials suitable for horse riding are covered in CD 143 [Ref 5.N].
Implementation
1.2
This document shall be implemented forthwith on all schemes involving the design of pavement
maintenance of footways and cycle tracks according to the implementation requirements of GG 101
[Ref 8.N].
Use of GG 101
1.3
The requirements contained in GG 101 [Ref 8.N]shall be followed in respect of activities covered by this
document.

2. Designing for pavement maintenance of footway...
2.
Designing for pavement maintenance of footways and cycle tracks
2.1
Data collected from visual inspections shall be used to assess the condition of footways and cycle
tracks.
NOTE
Information on visual inspections is given in GS 801 [Ref 1.N].
2.2
The following shall be determined to inform the design for maintenance of footways and cycle tracks:
1)
surfacing type present;
2)
location, nature, extent and severity of each defect;
3)
probable cause of each defect;
4)
whether the defect is confined to the surface or extends deeper into the pavement structure;
5)
treatment options;
6)
headroom at structures, gantries and overhead lines; and,
7)
consequential impact on ancillary assets.
NOTE
Information on headroom is given in CD 143 [Ref 5.N] for pedestrians and CD 195 [Ref 4.N] for cyclists.
2.3
Where deterioration of the footway or cycle track is confined to the surface layers, or limited to
cracking, surface only maintenance treatments shall be used.
NOTE
Surface only maintenance includes replacement/repair/reinstatement of the surface layer, crack sealing
and/or pavement surface treatment.
2.4
Where deterioration extends deeper than the surface layer, or is structural, partial or full reconstruction
shall be designed in accordance with CD 239 [Ref 7.N] to rectify the deterioration.
NOTE
Structural deterioration refers to physical and functional degradation, which includes heave, settlement
and root growth.
2.4.1
Where deterioration includes loss of edge, details should be designed in accordance with CD 239 [Ref
7.N].
2.4.2
Where vehicle overrun is found to have contributed to the deterioration, then partial or full
reconstruction design should either:
1)
be selected for the vehicle loading category in accordance with CD 239 [Ref 7.N]; or,
2)
include measures to prevent motor traffic access in accordance with CD 195 [Ref 4.N].
2.5
Where inadequate or ineffective drainage is found to have contributed to the deterioration, then repair or
improvement of drainage shall be designed in accordance with CG 501 [Ref 3.N] and CD 524 [Ref 6.N].
2.6
Where geometry is found to have contributed to the deterioration, then improvement of geometry shall
be designed in accordance with CG 501 [Ref 3.N] and Inclusive Mobility [Ref 2.I].
Designing for maintenance of asphalt surfaced footways and cycle tracks
2.7
Bituminous materials and thicknesses for the maintenance of footways and cycle tracks shall be
selected from the options in CD 239 [Ref 7.N].
2.8
The following shall be detailed for slurry surfacing:
1)
minimum PSV;
2)
maximum AAV;
3)
colour;
4)
nominal layer thickness; and,
5)
performance category.
2.9
The options for crack sealing of bituminous layers for maintenance of asphalt surfaced footways and
cycle tracks shall be selected from CD 227 [Ref 2.N].

2. Designing for pavement maintenance of footway...
Designing for maintenance of concrete-surfaced footways and cycle tracks
2.10
The design of repairs to concrete surfaced footways and cycle tracks shall be in accordance with
design of surface-only maintenance for pavements (with a concrete surface) in CD 227 [Ref 2.N].
2.10.1
Bituminous mixtures may be used as a repair material for the full depth of the concrete footways and
cycle tracks, unless there is evidence of vehicle overrun causing deterioration.
2.11
The design of full depth repairs to concrete-surfaced footways and cycle tracks with pavement quality
concrete shall detail:
1)
concrete compressive strength class and requirements for air entrainment;
2)
joint spacing and types;
3)
surface finish; and,
4)
any joint sealing.
NOTE 1
Load transfer devices are typically not used in concrete footways and cycle tracks.
NOTE 2
Joints are not typically sealed in concrete footways and cycle tracks.
2.11.1
Expansion joints should be included where there is evidence of compression failures as a result of
thermal expansion of the concrete.
Designing for maintenance of modular surfaced footways
2.12
Modular paving materials for the maintenance of footways shall be selected from the options in CD 239
[Ref 7.N].
2.12.1
Where modular surfaced footways are to be repaired, intact paving units may be recovered and reused
within the repair.
2.13
Materials shall be identified to match existing modular paving on registered high amenity footways and
cycle tracks.
Designing for maintenance of tactile paving
2.14
The selection of tactile paving materials shall be in accordance with CD 239 [Ref 7.N].
Designing for maintenance of kerb units
2.15
The selection and placement of kerb units shall be in accordance with CD 239 [Ref 7.N].
