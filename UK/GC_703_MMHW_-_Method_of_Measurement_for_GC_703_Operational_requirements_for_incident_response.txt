Foreword
This document is for incorporation into contracts by reference. It defines coverage of items in Bills of
Quantities and their method of measurement.
This document is applicable to contracts throughout the UK, complemented by the additional method of
measurement, specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.
Users are responsible for applying all appropriate documents applicable to their contract.
Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

1. Deliver incident response service basic facil...
1.
Deliver incident response service basic facility
Units
1.1
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
deliver incident response service basic facility ............ sum.
Note
An incident response service is typically priced at twelve monthly interventions.
Measurement
1.2
The measurement of deliver incident response service basic facility shall be for the whole period
required by the Service Manager.
1.3
Unless specified otherwise by the Service Information, the measurement of deliver incident response
service basic facility shall be twelve months for each year.
Itemisation
1.4
Separate items shall be provided for deliver incident response service basic facility in accordance with
clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 2.N] and the following:
Table 1.4 Itemisation for Deliver incident response service basic facility
Group
Feature
I
1 Deliver incident response service basic facility.
Item Coverage - Deliver incident response service basic facility
1.5
The items for deliver incident response service basic facility shall be in accordance with clause 2.1 of
GP 113 [Ref 1.N] and include for:
a)
collaborating with the Overseeing Organisation to develop a severe incident response plan;
b)
all charges and payments connected with the provision and use of vehicles, materials and
equipment;
c)
24 hour availability of operatives, vehicles, equipment and supervision together with a call centre
and contact details;
d)
issuing to the Service Manager call out rosters detailing names and contact details of resources
available for emergency call outs;
e)
provision of a responsible person to receive Service Manager's instructions and to ensure
compliance with any such instructions;
f)
mobilisation and management of resources to meet designated response times and undertake
required assessments.

2. Emergency call out team
2.
Emergency call out team
Units
2.1
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
emergency call out team ............ number.
b)
additional hours required at incident ............ hour.
Measurement
2.2
The measurement of emergency call out team shall be the number of call outs ordered by the Service
Manager.
2.3
The call out team shall include provision for up to two hours resource at the incident.
2.4
Additional resources required in excess of two hours at the incident shall be measured separately.
Itemisation
2.5
Separate items shall be provided for emergency call out team in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and
2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 2.N] and the following:
Table 2.5 Itemisation for Emergency call out team
Group
Feature
I
1 Emergency call out team.
2 Additional hours required at incident.
II
1 Stated time periods.
Item Coverage - Emergency call out team
2.6
The items for emergency call out team shall be in accordance with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref 1.N] and
include for:
a)
providing all measures, equipment and resources specified and detailed in the specification;
b)
mobilisation and management of resources to meet designated response times;
c)
continued management and deployment of resources;
d)
clear site of all materials and equipment after incident has closed;
e)
maintaining temporary barriers, cones, signs and the like for up to 48 hours until permanent
repairs have been carried out or further instructions are issued by the Service Manager.
