Foreword
Foreword
This document is for incorporation into contracts by reference. It defines coverage of items in Bills of
Quantities and their method of measurement.
This document is applicable to contracts throughout the UK, complemented by the additional method of
measurement, specification requirements and contractual changes of each Overseeing Organisation.
Users are responsible for applying all appropriate documents applicable to their contract.
Users are responsible for archiving contract documentation in accordance with the user's quality
management system.

1. Provision of environmental, cultural, habitat...
1.
Provision of environmental, cultural, habitat management plans,
screening and assessments
Units
1.1
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
management plans, screening and assessments ............ number.
Itemisation
1.2
Separate items shall be provided for environmental, cultural, habitat management plans, screening and
assessments in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 5.N] and the following:
Table 1.2 Itemisation for Environmental, cultural, habitat management plans, screening and
assessments
Group
Feature
I
1 Screening for the environmental management plan.
2 Environmental management plan.
3 Cultural heritage assessment and mitigation.
4 Conservation of habitats and species regulations assessment.
5 Material assets and waste assessment.
6 Other stated plans or assessments.
II
1 First iteration.
2 Second iteration.
3 Third iteration.
Note: Group II Features 1 - 3 shall apply only to Group I Feature 2.
Item coverage - Environmental, cultural, habitat management plans, screening and assessments
1.3
The items for environmental, cultural, habitat management plans, screening and assessments shall be
in accordance with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref 4.N] and include for:
a)
scoping;
b)
screening, consultation and reporting;
c)
develop design objectives;
d)
identify mitigation measures;
e)
provision of a constraints map;
f)
management plans;
g)
method statements;
h)
procedures and records;
i)
preparation of change register;
j)
investigation and monitoring reports;
k)
revisions and updates;
l)
compliance with all relevant requirements as set out in the applicable Design Manual of Roads
and Bridges (DMRB) document(s) LA 102 [Ref 7.N], LA 106 [Ref 1.N], LA 110 [Ref 6.N], LA 115
[Ref 3.N] and LA 120 [Ref 2.N] respectively.
