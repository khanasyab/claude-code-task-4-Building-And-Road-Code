1. Reinstatement of trenches and openings in fle...
1.
Reinstatement of trenches and openings in flexible pavements for
maintenance
Units
1.1
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
reinstatement of trenches and openings in flexible pavements ............ square metre.
Measurement
1.2
The measurement of reinstatement of trenches and openings in flexible pavements for maintenance
shall be calculated using the width of the top surface to be reinstated excluding sides and edges with
no deductions made for openings of 1 square metre or less.
1.3
The top surface for the following features shall be the widths or areas described below:
a)
for drains, sewers, piped culverts, service ducts and filter drains - the width shall be the internal
diameter of the pipe plus 600 mm;
b)
for kerb units, channels, edgings, combined kerb drainage systems, linear drainage channel
systems and the like - the width of the foundations;
c)
for chambers, gullies, traffic signs, traffic signals, road lighting columns and the like - the
horizontal area of the base slab or where no base slab is required the area of the bottom of the
excavation.
Itemisation
1.4
Separate items shall be provided for reinstatement of trenches and openings in flexible pavements for
maintenance in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 2.N] and the following:
Table 1.4 Itemisation for Reinstatement of trenches and openings in flexible pavements for
maintenance
Group
Feature
I
1 Reinstatement of each type of flexible pavement.
II
1 Different thicknesses or depths.
Item Coverage - Reinstatement of trenches and openings in flexible pavements for maintenance
1.5
The items for reinstatement of trenches and openings in flexible pavements for maintenance shall be in
accordance with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref 1.N] and include for:
a)
determination of the extent of the reinstatement and agreement with the Overseeing Organisation;
b)

CC 201/MMHW [Ref 3.N];
c)

d)

e)
kerb units, special kerb units, channels, edgings, combined kerb drainage systems, and linear
drainage channel systems as clause 2.7 of CC 207/MMHW [Ref 5.N];
f)
footways, cycle tracks and paved areas as clause 1.6 of CC 207/MMHW [Ref 5.N];
g)
scarifying;
h)
milling as clause 5.6 of this document;
i)
drilling holes;

3. Regulating course for maintenance
3.
Regulating course for maintenance
Units
3.1
The units of measurement shall be:
a)
regulating course ............ tonne or cubic metre.
Measurement
3.2
The measurement of regulating course for maintenance by tonne shall be calculated from the tonnage
of material certified by the Overseeing Organisation multiplied by the factor for the particular aggregate
used.
3.3
The tonnage certified by the Overseeing Organisation shall be only that material included on delivery
tickets which is incorporated in the Permanent Works in the locations and to the extent and thickness
required by the Contract.
3.4
Material in excess of the requirements of the Contract and material used for any other purpose shall not
be included within the certified tonnage.
3.5
The measurement of regulating course for maintenance by cubic metres shall be the volume of material
measured to the outlines required by the Contract.
Note
It is recommended that where a significant quantity of regulating material is anticipated to be required,
the regulating course be measured in cubic metres. Measurements by tonnage will only be used
exceptionally where there will be significant difficulties in measuring the volume.
Itemisation
3.6
Separate items shall be provided for regulating course for maintenance in accordance with clauses 2.2,
2.3, and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 2.N] and the following:
Table 3.6 Itemisation for Regulating course for maintenance
Group
Feature

Note: Each item description which includes Group I Feature 1 shall relate to the tabulated list of the
different constituent aggregate types and their factors included in the Bill of Quantities.
Item Coverage - Regulating course for maintenance
3.7
The items for regulating course for maintenance shall be in accordance with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref

4. Bond coat for maintenance
4.
Bond coat for maintenance
Units
4.1
The unit of measurement shall be:
a)
bond coat ............ square metre.
Measurement
4.2
For the purposes of measurement any reference to bond coat for maintenance shall be deemed to
include tack coats.
4.3
Bond coat for maintenance shall only be measured separately when the Contract requires a bond coat
to be applied to any existing (not new) bound substrate.
Note
Existing bound substrate can include planed or milled asphalt (inlay), un-planed or un-milled asphalt
(overlay) and rigid pavement construction whether planed or un-planed.
4.4
Bond coat which is a component part of any newly installed specified rigid or flexible pavement option
e.g. between asphalt layers or on newly installed concrete pavement, shall not be separately measured.
Itemisation
4.5
Separate items shall be provided for bond coat for maintenance in accordance with clauses 2.2, 2.3,
and 2.4 of GP 112 [Ref 2.N] and the following:
Table 4.5 Itemisation for Bond coat for maintenance

II
1 Different types.
III
1 Different rates of spread.
Item Coverage - Bond coat for maintenance
4.6
The items for bond coat for maintenance shall be in accordance with clause 2.1 of GP 113 [Ref 1.N]
and include for:
a)
making good after sampling and testing;
b)
