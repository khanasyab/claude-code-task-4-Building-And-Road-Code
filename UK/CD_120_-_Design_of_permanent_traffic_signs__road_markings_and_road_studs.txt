Design of permanent traffic signs, road

Summary
A DMRB document to be used for the design of permanent traffic signs, road markings and road
studs, including upright traffic signs, parallel and zebra crossing globes, refuge indicator lamps,
twin amber flashing lights, bollards, and marker posts, which are intended to remain in position
on completion of the permanent works.
National Variation
This document has associated National Application Annexes providing alternative or supplementary content to that
given in the core document, which is relevant to specific Overseeing Organisations. National Application Annexes
are adjoined at the end of this document.
Feedback and Enquiries
Users of this document are encouraged to raise any enquiries and/or provide feedback on the content and usage of
this document to the dedicated National Highways team. The online feedback form for all enquiries and feedback
can be accessed at: www.standardsforhighways.co.uk/feedback.
This is a controlled document.

Introduction
Introduction
Background
Traffic signs, road markings and road studs facilitate the safe use and effective operation of roads by
communicating directions, warnings, regulations and other information to road users.
This document is one of a suite of documents that, together, provide requirements and supporting
advice for the design, construction, inspection, maintenance and disposal of permanent traffic signs,
road markings and road studs. Section 2 of this document includes a list of documents relevant to the
design of permanent traffic signs, road markings and road studs; the other documents within the suite
are listed in the following table.

studs

1. Scope
1.
Scope
Aspects covered
1.1
This document shall be used for the design of permanent traffic signs, road markings and road studs,
including upright traffic signs, parallel and zebra crossing globes, refuge indicator lamps, twin amber
flashing lights, bollards, and marker posts, which are intended to remain in position on completion of
the permanent works.
1.2
This document shall not be used for the design of temporary traffic signs, temporary road markings or
temporary road studs.
NOTE
CD 130 [Ref 4.N] provides requirements and supporting advice for the design of temporary traffic signs,
temporary road markings and temporary road studs.
1.3
This document shall not be used for the design of coloured road surfacing.
NOTE
CD 236 [Ref 34.N] provides requirements and supporting advice for the design of coloured road
surfacing.
Implementation
1.4
This document shall be implemented forthwith on all schemes involving the design of permanent traffic
signs, road markings and road studs on the Overseeing Organisations' motorway and all-purpose trunk
roads according to the implementation requirements of GG 101 [Ref 17.N].
Use of GG 101
1.5
The requirements contained in GG 101 [Ref 17.N] shall be followed in respect of activities covered by
this document.

General design requirements for permanent location marker posts
Placement of permanent location marker posts
7.1
Permanent location marker posts, with location numerals, shall be provided in the verge of each:
1)
motorway:
a)
main carriageway; and
b)
connector road;
2)
all-purpose dual carriageway:
a)
main carriageway where an operational need has been established; and
b)
connector road where an operational need has been established.
3)
all-purpose single carriageway where an operational need has been established.
7.1.1
Where permanent location marker posts are to be provided, they should be provided:
1)
at 100-metre intervals, starting at:
a)
the start of the road; or
b)
an alternative local datum point;
2)
with location numerals that depict the approximate distance from the start of the road or the
alternative local datum point; and
3)
where an operational need has been established, with a carriageway or connector road identifier.
7.1.2
Where permanent location marker posts are to be provided, the start of the road or the alternative local
datum point from which the location marker posts are to start should be determined in consultation with
the Overseeing Organisation.
NOTE
The start of the road is typically the start of the main carriageway with the location numerals, "0/0."
7.1.3
Location numerals that depict the approximate distance from the start of the road or the agreed local
datum point should be:
1)
expressed in kilometres and tenths of kilometres; and
2)
determined from measurements along the centre-line of the road.
NOTE
Permanent location marker posts provide a unique locational reference which can be used by road

Appendix A. Guidance on applying for the authorisation of non-prescribed
permanent traffic signs
A1
Introduction
This appendix provides guidance on applying to the national authority for the authorisation of
non-prescribed permanent traffic signs.
A2
Legal framework
The applicable traffic signs regulations prescribe the design and conditions of use of traffic signs,
including road markings, traffic signals and pedestrian, cycle and equestrian crossings, on or near
roads. Traffic signs are required to be prescribed or authorised by the national authority.
If a traffic authority wishes to use a traffic sign that is not prescribed, an application for authorisation will
need to be made to the national authority. The design of such traffic signs should conform to
established principles. Non-prescribed traffic signs will generally only be authorised in exceptional
circumstances and where there is no prescribed alternative. This is essential in order to minimise the
number of traffic sign types required for the safe and efficient functioning of the road system.
Examples of non-prescribed traffic signs include the use of a non-standard distance on a
gantry-mounted traffic sign and a traffic sign for a through-about. Expiry of a previous non-prescribed
traffic sign authorisation can also dictate the need for a new authorisation.
A non-prescribed traffic sign authorisation is a legal document which authorises the use of a traffic sign
that falls outside the applicable traffic signs regulations. A completed and signed authorisation makes a
non-prescribed traffic sign lawful when the traffic sign is displayed, erected or placed in accordance
with the requirements in the same authorisation. The requirements of a traffic sign authorisation
typically include details of the siting and use of the non-prescribed traffic sign, and can include the
period of time for which the traffic sign can lawfully remain in place.
A3
Indicative step-by-step application process
This sub-section provides an indicative, step-by-step guide to applying for authorisation of a
non-prescribed traffic sign. The Overseeing Organisation should be consulted regarding any variations
to this process and the time it will take to complete the process.
Throughout the process of applying for a non-prescribed traffic sign authorisation, the responsibility for
engaging with the national authority rests with the Overseeing Organisation, unless otherwise agreed
with the Overseeing Organisation.
A3.1
Evaluation of options for prescribed traffic signs
An application for a non-prescribed traffic sign authorisation can only be made providing all prescribed
options, including permitted variants, have been evaluated and discounted.
A3.2
Agreement with the Overseeing Organisation that a non-prescribed traffic sign is justified
Following the evaluation of options, the need for a non-prescribed traffic sign authorisation should be
agreed with the Overseeing Organisation. Where appropriate, the Overseeing Organisation will seek
agreement-in-principle from the national authority to avoid any abortive design work on an application
that is unlikely to be accepted by the national authority.
A3.3
Preparation of the application and supporting evidence
The form of the application for non-prescribed traffic sign authorisation and the extent of the supporting
evidence to be provided with the application should be agreed with the Overseeing Organisation.
Designs for non-prescribed traffic signs should adhere to the principles and guidance set out in the
Traffic Signs Manual, including, in particular, TSM Chapter 7 [Ref 47.N].

2)

3)

4)
a statement of the purpose of the non-prescribed traffic sign;
5)
relevant supporting evidence; and
6)

1)

2)
road safety audit reports in accordance with GG 119 [Ref 29.N];
3)

4)

5)

1)

larger than A3.
2)

proposed traffic signs and road markings, and other relevant street furniture within the limits of
the proposed scheme.
3)
Detailed traffic sign design drawings are to have traffic sign images, which are equivalent in
quality to those published in the applicable traffic signs regulations and the Traffic Signs Manual,
and include both 'tiled outline' and 'colour fill' versions; dimensions in millimetres; relevant
x-height(s); method of illumination; the colours to be used and the mounting details.
4)
Drawings are:
a)
to have a border with drawing title, number and revision;
b)
to use a simple traffic sign referencing system which is common to all drawings;
c)
not to refer to other drawings that are not included in the non-prescribed traffic sign
authorisation application; and
d)
not to be labelled with "DRAFT," "FOR INFORMATION ONLY," "NOT FOR
CONSTRUCTION" or other similar statements.
5)
Statements of the purpose of the non-prescribed traffic sign are to include a brief justification of
the non-prescribed traffic sign compared to the prescribed options and demonstrate, using
evidence, that all other viable options have been considered. If the submission forms part of a
trial or pilot this is to be clearly stated.
6)
The responsibility for drafting non-prescribed traffic sign authorisation documents rests with the
Overseeing Organisation, unless otherwise agreed with the Overseeing Organisation.
