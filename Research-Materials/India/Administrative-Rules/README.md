## 1. CENTRAL CIVIL SERVICES (CONDUCT) RULES, 1964

### Legal Framework
- **Official Title:** Central Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1964
- **Short Form:** CCS (Conduct) Rules
- **Legal Basis:** Article 309 of the Constitution of India
- **Applicability:** All Central Government employees
- **Issuing Authority:** Department of Personnel & Training (DoPT)
- **Website:** dopt.gov.in

### Purpose
Foundational legal framework governing:
- Conduct of central government employees
- Discipline and responsibilities
- Code of ethics
- Public servant integrity
- Neutrality and impartiality
- Professional behavior

### Scope and Coverage
**Definition of "Workplace":**
Under the rules, "workplace" includes any:
- Department
- Organisation
- Undertaking
- Establishment
- Enterprise
- Institution
- Office, branch, or unit

That is:
- Established by Central Government
- Owned by Central Government
- Controlled by Central Government
- Wholly or substantially financed by funds provided directly or indirectly by Central Government

## 7. POWERS OF SANCTION

### Administrative Approval
**Definition:** Approval for undertaking a project

**Authority Levels:**
- Central Government (above specified limits)
- Administrative Department (within limits)
- Chief Engineer (within delegated limits)
- Lower authorities (as delegated)

**Requirements:**
- Justification and necessity
- Scope of work
- Estimated cost
- Budget provision
- Environmental clearances
- Other statutory approvals

### Technical Sanction
**Definition:** Approval of detailed estimates and designs

**Authority Levels:**
Based on project value:
- Chief Engineer: Up to specified limit
- Superintending Engineer: Up to lower limit
- Executive Engineer: Up to still lower limit
- Circle-level technical committees: For complex works

**Requirements:**
- Detailed estimates
- Drawings and designs
- Specifications
- Rate analysis
- Structural stability certificate
- Compliance with codes

### Financial Sanction
**Definition:** Approval for expenditure

**Integration:**
- Budget allocation
- Administrative approval
- Technical sanction
- Fund availability
