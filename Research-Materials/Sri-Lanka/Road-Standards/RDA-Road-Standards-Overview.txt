================================================================================
OVERVIEW
================================================================================

The Road Development Authority (RDA) is the premier government agency in
Sri Lanka responsible for the planning, development, construction, improvement,
and maintenance of the national road network. RDA develops and maintains
comprehensive design standards and specifications for road and highway projects
throughout Sri Lanka.

ESTABLISHMENT:
- Created to manage Sri Lanka's road infrastructure
- Successor to road functions of former Public Works Department (PWD)
- Operates under Ministry of Highways

JURISDICTION:
- National highways (Class A roads)
- Major arterial roads (Class B roads)
- Important inter-provincial roads
- Major bridges and structures
- Highway infrastructure

ROAD CLASSIFICATION SYSTEM
================================================================================

SRI LANKA ROAD NETWORK:

CLASS A ROADS (National Highways):
- Managed by RDA
- Major inter-city connections
- Strategic importance
- Higher design standards

CLASS B ROADS (National Roads):
- Also managed by RDA
- Important connectivity roads
- Regional significance
- Moderate design standards

CLASS C, D, E ROADS:
- Managed by Provincial Road Development Authority (PRDA)
- Local authorities
- Lower classification
- Local connectivity
