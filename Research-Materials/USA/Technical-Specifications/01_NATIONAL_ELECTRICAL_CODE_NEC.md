# NATIONAL ELECTRICAL CODE (NEC) - NFPA 70

## Overview
The National Electrical Code (NEC), officially known as NFPA 70, is the benchmark for safe electrical design, installation, and inspection to protect people and property from electrical hazards. It is the most widely adopted code in the United States and is used worldwide.

## Official Source
- **Publisher**: National Fire Protection Association (NFPA)
- **Designation**: NFPA 70
- **Website**: https://www.nfpa.org
- **Code Online**: https://www.nfpa.org/codes-and-standards/nfpa-70
- **First Edition**: 1897
- **Current Edition**: 2023 NEC (Next edition: 2026)

## Legal Authority
- Consensus standard developed by NFPA
- Adopted as law by states, counties, and municipalities
- Referenced in OSHA regulations (29 CFR 1910 and 1926)
- Approved as American National Standard (ANSI)

## Update Cycle
- **3-year revision cycle** (2023, 2026, 2029, etc.)
- Public input and comment process
- Technical committees review proposals
- NFPA membership votes on code
