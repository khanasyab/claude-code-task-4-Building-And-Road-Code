# INTERNATIONAL PLUMBING CODE (IPC)

## Overview
The International Plumbing Code (IPC) provides minimum regulations for plumbing facilities in terms of both performance and prescriptive objectives, and provides for the acceptance of new and innovative products, materials, and systems. It is coordinated with the family of International Codes (I-Codes) published by the International Code Council (ICC).

## Official Source
- **Publisher**: International Code Council (ICC)
- **Website**: https://www.iccsafe.org
- **Online Access**: https://codes.iccsafe.org (free viewing, registration required)
- **Current Edition**: 2024 IPC (updated every 3 years)
- **Previous Editions**: 2021, 2018, 2015, 2012, 2009, 2006, 2003, 2000

## Scope and Coverage
The IPC establishes minimum regulations for plumbing systems using prescriptive and performance-related provisions. It applies to:
- Installation of plumbing systems
- Materials used in plumbing
- Design of plumbing systems
- Alterations, repairs, replacement, renovation, and addition to plumbing systems

**Key Definitions:**
- **Approved**: Acceptable to the code official
- **Backflow**: Flow of water or other liquids into distributing pipes from any source other than intended
- **Drainage fixture unit (d.f.u.)**: Measure of probable discharge into drainage system
- **Fixture**: Receptacle or device connected to plumbing system
- **Potable water**: Water free from impurities in amounts sufficient to cause disease
- **Trap**: Fitting providing liquid seal to prevent sewer gas backup
- **Vent**: Pipe permitting flow of air to/from drainage system
- **Water supply fixture unit (w.s.f.u.)**: Measure of probable flow demand
