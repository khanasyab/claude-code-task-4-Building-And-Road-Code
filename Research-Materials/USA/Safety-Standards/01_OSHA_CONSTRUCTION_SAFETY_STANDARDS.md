# OSHA CONSTRUCTION SAFETY STANDARDS

## Overview
The Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) Construction Safety Standards are federal regulations that establish minimum safety and health requirements for construction work. These standards are legally enforceable and apply to most construction employers and workers in the United States.

## Official Source
- **Agency**: Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA)
- **Parent Agency**: U.S. Department of Labor
- **Website**: https://www.osha.gov
- **Construction Page**: https://www.osha.gov/construction
- **Regulations**: 29 CFR 1926 (Title 29, Code of Federal Regulations, Part 1926)

## Legal Authority
- **Occupational Safety and Health Act of 1970** (OSH Act)
- Public Law 91-596
- 29 USC 651 et seq.
- Applies to private sector employers in all 50 states

## Access to Standards
- **FREE**: Complete text available online at https://www.osha.gov

### Subpart M - Fall Protection (1926.500-1926.503)

**Most frequently cited OSHA standard**

#### 1926.500 - Scope, application, and definitions
- Application to construction work
- When fall protection is required
- Definitions (anchorage, body belt, body harness, etc.)

#### 1926.501 - Duty to have fall protection
- **6-foot rule**: Fall protection required when working at heights of 6 feet or more
- Leading edges: 6 feet
- Hoist areas: 6 feet
- Holes: Any depth
- Formwork and reinforcing steel: 6 feet
- Ramps, runways, and walkways: 6 feet
- Excavations: 6 feet
- Dangerous equipment: Any height
- Overhand bricklaying: 6 feet
- Roofing work: 6 feet (low-slope), 6 feet (steep slope)
- Precast concrete erection: 6 feet
- Residential construction: 6 feet
- Wall openings: Any height
- Walking/working surfaces: 6 feet

#### 1926.502 - Fall protection systems criteria and practices
- **Guardrail systems**:
  - Top rail: 42 inches (±3 inches)
  - Mid rail: midway between top rail and platform
  - Capable of withstanding 200 pounds
- **Safety net systems**:
  - Maximum 30 feet below working level
  - Extend 8 feet beyond edge
  - Mesh openings no greater than 36 square inches
- **Personal fall arrest systems (PFAS)**:
  - Full-body harness (body belts prohibited for fall arrest after 1998)
  - Deceleration distance: 3.5 feet maximum
  - Free fall distance: 6 feet maximum
  - Strength: 5,000 pounds
  - Anchorage: 5,000 pounds per person
- **Positioning device systems**
- **Warning line systems**
- **Controlled access zones**
- **Safety monitoring systems**
- **Covers** for holes and openings
- **Protection from falling objects**

#### 1926.503 - Training requirements
- Training program requirements
- Certification of training
- Retraining

## OSHA Enforcement

### Inspection Priorities
1. Imminent danger
2. Fatality/catastrophe
3. Worker complaints
4. Referrals
5. Follow-up
6. Programmed inspections

### Citation Types
- **Serious**: $1,000 - $16,131 per violation
- **Other-than-serious**: Up to $16,131
- **Willful**: $10,000 - $161,323 per violation
- **Repeat**: Up to $161,323 per violation
- **Failure to abate**: $16,131 per day beyond abatement date

### Penalty Multipliers
Penalties can be increased for:
- Large employers
- Willful violations
- Repeat violations
- Failure to abate

## Key OSHA Definitions

### Competent Person
Person capable of identifying existing and predictable hazards and who has authorization to take prompt corrective measures.

### Qualified Person
Person who, by possession of a recognized degree, certificate, or professional standing, or by extensive knowledge, training, and experience, has successfully demonstrated ability to solve or resolve problems.

### Authorized Person
Person approved or assigned by the employer to perform a specific type of duty or be at a specific location.
