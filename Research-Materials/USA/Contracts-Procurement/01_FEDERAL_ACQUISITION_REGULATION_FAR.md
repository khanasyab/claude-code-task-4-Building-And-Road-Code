# FEDERAL ACQUISITION REGULATION (FAR)

## Overview
The Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) is the primary regulation for use by all federal executive agencies in their acquisition of supplies and services with appropriated funds. It is the principal set of rules governing the federal procurement process.

## Official Source
- **Primary Authority**: Title 48 of the Code of Federal Regulations (CFR)
- **Website**: https://www.acquisition.gov
- **eCFR Online**: https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-48
- **Maintained By**:
  - Department of Defense (DoD)
  - General Services Administration (GSA)
  - National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA)

## Legal Authority
- Armed Services Procurement Act (1947)
- Federal Property and Administrative Services Act (1949)
- Office of Federal Procurement Policy Act (1974)
- Federal Acquisition Streamlining Act (1994)
- Competition in Contracting Act (1984)

## Current Status
- **Latest Edition**: Continuously updated
- **Format**: Title 48 CFR Chapter 1
- **Access**: FREE - publicly available online
- **Updates**: Published in Federal Register, incorporated into annual edition

## Important FAR Thresholds (2024)

### Simplified Acquisition Threshold (SAT)
- **$250,000** for most contracts
- **$800,000** for construction (CONUS)
- **$1,500,000** for construction (OCONUS)

### Micro-Purchase Threshold
- **$10,000** for supplies and services
- **$2,000** for construction

### Truth in Negotiations Act (TINA)
- **$2 million** threshold for cost or pricing data

### Small Business Subcontracting Plan
- **$750,000** for most contracts
- **$1.5 million** for construction
