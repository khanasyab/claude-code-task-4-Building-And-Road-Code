# AASHTO STANDARDS - AMERICAN ASSOCIATION OF STATE HIGHWAY AND TRANSPORTATION OFFICIALS

## Overview
AASHTO (American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials) is a standards-setting organization that publishes specifications, test protocols, and guidelines used in the design and construction of highways and bridges throughout the United States.

## Official Source
- **Organization**: American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials
- **Website**: https://www.aashto.org
- **Store**: https://store.transportation.org
- **Headquarters**: Washington, D.C.
- **Founded**: 1914

## Key Publications

### 1. AASHTO LRFD Bridge Design Specifications
**Full Title**: LRFD Bridge Design Specifications (Load and Resistance Factor Design)

#### Overview
- Primary design standard for highway bridges in the United States
- Uses Load and Resistance Factor Design (LRFD) methodology
- Updated annually with interim revisions

#### Current Edition
- **9th Edition** (2020) with annual interims
- Previous editions: 1st (1994) through 8th (2017)

#### Major Sections
1. **Section 1**: Introduction
2. **Section 2**: General Design and Location Features
3. **Section 3**: Loads and Load Factors
4. **Section 4**: Structural Analysis and Evaluation
5. **Section 5**: Concrete Structures
6. **Section 6**: Steel Structures
7. **Section 7**: Aluminum Structures
8. **Section 8**: Wood Structures
9. **Section 9**: Decks and Deck Systems
10. **Section 10**: Foundations
11. **Section 11**: Abutments, Piers, and Walls
12. **Section 12**: Buried Structures and Tunnel Liners
13. **Section 13**: Railings
14. **Section 14**: Joints and Bearings

#### Load Categories Covered
- Dead loads (DC, DW, EV)
- Live loads (LL, IM, CE, BR, PL, LS)
- Dynamic loads
- Wind loads (WL, WS)
- Seismic loads (EQ)
- Water loads (WA, FR, BL, IC)
- Earth pressure loads (EH, ES, DD)
- Temperature loads (TU, TG, SH, CR)
- Collision loads (CT, CV)
- Live load surcharge (LS)

#### Key Features
- Reliability-based design
- Multiple load combinations
- Service, strength, and extreme event limit states
- Comprehensive material specifications
- Seismic design provisions

### 2. AASHTO "Green Book" - Policy on Geometric Design
**Full Title**: A Policy on Geometric Design of Highways and Streets

#### Overview
- Definitive guide for highway geometric design
- Nicknamed the "Green Book"
- Used by state DOTs and design consultants nationwide

#### Current Edition
- **7th Edition** (2018)
- Updated approximately every 10 years

#### Major Topics
1. **Highway Functions and Design Controls**
   - Functional classification
   - Design vehicles
   - Design speed
   - Sight distance

2. **Elements of Design**
   - Cross section elements
   - Traveled way
   - Shoulders
   - Medians
   - Roadside design
   - Curbs

3. **Alignment**
   - Horizontal alignment
   - Vertical alignment
   - Combination of horizontal and vertical alignment
   - Superelevation

4. **Cross Section Elements**
   - Lane widths
   - Shoulder widths
   - Median widths
   - Slopes and drainage

5. **Local Roads and Streets**
   - Rural local roads
   - Urban local streets
   - Collector roads and streets

6. **Collector Roads and Streets**
   - Rural collectors
   - Urban collectors

7. **Rural and Urban Arterials**
   - Design criteria
   - Access management
   - Intersection design

8. **Freeways**
   - Mainline design
   - Ramp design
   - Interchange design

9. **Intersections**
   - At-grade intersections
   - Channelization
   - Turning roadways
   - Roundabouts

10. **Grade Separations and Interchanges**
    - Interchange types
    - Ramp design
    - Interchange spacing

#### Design Speeds
- Local streets: 20-40 mph
- Collectors: 30-60 mph
- Arterials: 40-70 mph
- Freeways: 50-80 mph

- **T 304**: Uncompacted Void Content of Fine Aggregate
- **T 307**: Tensile Strength of Geotextiles

## Adoption and Use

### Federal Requirements
- Federal Highway Administration (FHWA) requires use of AASHTO standards for federally-funded projects
- 23 CFR 625 mandates AASHTO design standards

### State Adoption
- All 50 states use AASHTO standards as basis
- States may modify or supplement
- State DOT manuals often reference AASHTO
- Local agencies follow state requirements
