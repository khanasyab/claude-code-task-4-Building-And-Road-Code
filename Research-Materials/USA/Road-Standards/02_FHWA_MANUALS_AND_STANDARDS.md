# FEDERAL HIGHWAY ADMINISTRATION (FHWA) MANUALS AND STANDARDS

## Overview
The Federal Highway Administration (FHWA) is a division of the U.S. Department of Transportation that supports state and local governments in the design, construction, and maintenance of the nation's highway system. FHWA publishes numerous manuals, guides, and standards that establish best practices for highway design, construction, and maintenance.

## Official Source
- **Agency**: Federal Highway Administration (FHWA)
- **Parent Agency**: U.S. Department of Transportation (USDOT)
- **Website**: https://www.fhwa.dot.gov
- **Headquarters**: Washington, D.C.

## Access to Documents
- **FREE**: All FHWA publications are publicly available
- **No cost**: Public domain
- **Online access**: Downloadable PDFs from FHWA website
- **Distribution**: Available through FHWA offices

## Major FHWA Publications

### 1. Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices (MUTCD)

#### Overview
The MUTCD defines the standards used by road managers nationwide to install and maintain traffic control devices on all streets, highways, and bicycle/pedestrian facilities.

#### Official Details
- **Full Title**: Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices for Streets and Highways
- **Current Edition**: 2009 MUTCD with Revisions 1 and 2 (2012)
- **Latest**: 11th Edition expected (currently in development)
- **Legal Authority**: 23 CFR Part 655
- **Status**: National standard (mandatory for federal-aid highways)

#### Structure
- **Part 1**: General
- **Part 2**: Signs
- **Part 3**: Markings
- **Part 4**: Highway Traffic Signals
- **Part 5**: Traffic Control Devices for Low-Volume Roads
- **Part 6**: Temporary Traffic Control
- **Part 7**: Traffic Controls for School Areas
- **Part 8**: Traffic Controls for Railroad and Light Rail Transit Grade Crossings
- **Part 9**: Traffic Controls for Bicycle Facilities
- **Part 10**: Traffic Control for Pedestrian, Hybrid Beacon, and Rectangular Rapid Flashing Beacon

#### Key Topics

**Part 2 - Signs:**
- Regulatory signs (R series)
- Warning signs (W series)
- Guide signs (D, E, F, G, I, M series)
- Sign sizes, colors, retroreflectivity
- Sign placement and mounting

**Part 3 - Markings:**
- Pavement markings
- Edge lines
- Center lines
- Lane lines
- Crosswalks
- Stop bars
- Arrow markings
- Word and symbol markings
- Yellow, white, red, blue marking colors

**Part 4 - Traffic Signals:**
- Traffic signal warrants
- Traffic signal operations
- Signal heads and displays
- Pedestrian signals
- Emergency vehicle signals
- Flashing beacons

**Part 6 - Temporary Traffic Control (Work Zones):**
- Work zone traffic control
- Typical applications
- Temporary traffic control devices
- Flagging procedures
- Temporary traffic control plans

#### Access
- **Free download**: https://mutcd.fhwa.dot.gov
- **Searchable online**: Full text available
- **State supplements**: States may have additional requirements
