OVERVIEW
================================================================================

The Department of Public Health Engineering (DPHE) is the national lead agency
responsible for the provision of water supply and sanitation services in rural
Bangladesh. DPHE assists municipalities and communities in building water supply
infrastructure throughout the country, except for the three largest urban areas
(Dhaka, Chittagong, and Khulna, which have separate water authorities).

JURISDICTION:
- Rural water supply
- Rural sanitation
- Small town water supply
- Municipal water supply assistance
- Water quality monitoring
- Community water management

WATER QUALITY STANDARDS
================================================================================

BANGLADESH STANDARDS:
DPHE maintains water quality parameters based on Bangladesh Standards and
WHO Guidelines.

KEY PARAMETERS AND STANDARDS:

CHEMICAL PARAMETERS:
- Aluminum: 0.2 mg/L (Bangladesh Standard)
- Arsenic: 0.05 mg/L (Bangladesh Standard) vs 0.01 mg/L (WHO Guideline)
  Note: Bangladesh has set a higher standard due to natural occurrence
- Barium: Standards specified
- Iron: Standards specified
- Manganese: Standards specified
- Lead: Standards specified
- Other heavy metals: As per standards

MICROBIOLOGICAL PARAMETERS:
- E. coli: Zero/100 mL sample (Bangladesh Standard)
- Total coliform: Standards specified
- Fecal coliform: Standards specified

PHYSICAL PARAMETERS:
- pH: Acceptable range specified
- Turbidity: Maximum limits
- Color: Acceptable limits
- Taste and odor: Standards
- Temperature: Guidelines

ARSENIC STANDARD:
Bangladesh standard for arsenic in drinking water: Below 50 ppb (0.05 mg/L)
Note: This is higher than WHO guideline of 10 ppb due to natural geological
conditions and feasibility considerations.

REGIONAL COMPARISON
================================================================================

SIMILARITIES TO PAKISTAN:
- Both countries face water quality challenges
- Similar PHE/PHED organizational structure
- Common South Asian water supply technologies
- Comparable rural water supply approaches

UNIQUE BANGLADESH CHALLENGES:
- Widespread arsenic contamination
- High population density
- Low-lying flood-prone geography
- Coastal salinity intrusion
- Climate vulnerability
