## Coverage

### Primary Regulations Covered:

1. **CDM 2015** - Construction (Design and Management) Regulations 2015
   - Duties of all construction dutyholders
   - Health and safety file requirements
   - Construction phase plans

2. **Work at Height Regulations 2005**
   - Scaffolding, working platforms
   - Fall prevention and protection
   - Inspection requirements

3. **LOLER 1998** - Lifting Operations and Lifting Equipment Regulations
   - Cranes, hoists, lifting equipment
   - Thorough examination requirements

4. **PUWER 1998** - Provision and Use of Work Equipment Regulations
   - All work equipment safety
   - Maintenance and inspection

5. **COSHH 2002** - Control of Substances Hazardous to Health
   - Silica dust, cement, asbestos
   - Hazardous substances control

6. **Control of Asbestos Regulations 2012**
   - Duty to manage asbestos
   - Asbestos surveys and removal
   - Licensing requirements

7. **Control of Noise at Work Regulations 2005**
   - Noise exposure limits
   - Hearing protection

8. **Control of Vibration at Work Regulations 2005**
   - Hand-arm and whole-body vibration
   - HAVS prevention

9. **PPE at Work Regulations 1992**
   - Personal protective equipment
   - All PPE types for construction

10. **RIDDOR 2013** - Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences
    - Incident reporting requirements

### Other Important Regulations:

- Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999
- Construction (Health, Safety and Welfare) Regulations 1996
- Electricity at Work Regulations 1989

All regulations and guidance freely available from:
- HSE Website: www.hse.gov.uk

### Key HSE Resources:

- **L Series** (Approved Codes of Practice - ACOPs) - Special legal status
  - L153: Managing health and safety in construction (CDM 2015)
  - L143: Managing and working with asbestos
  - L108: Controlling noise at work
  - Many others

- **INDG Series** (Information sheets) - Free brief guidance
- **HSG Series** (Health and Safety Guidance) - Detailed technical guidance
- **CIS Series** (Construction Information Sheets) - Construction-specific

## Enforcement

- **Health and Safety Executive (HSE)** - Construction sites
- **Local Authorities** - Some commercial premises
- **Penalties**: Unlimited fines, imprisonment, prohibition notices

## Competence

- CSCS (Construction Skills Certification Scheme)
- SMSTS, SSSTS (Site management/supervisor training)
- NEBOSH qualifications
- Professional CMIOSH status

## Major Hazards in Construction

1. Falls from height (leading cause of deaths)
2. Struck by moving vehicles/equipment
3. Struck by falling objects
4. Asbestos exposure
5. Silica dust
6. Manual handling
7. Noise and vibration
8. Electrical hazards
9. Excavations collapse
10. Contact with moving machinery

## Notes

- Construction remains high-risk industry
- Approximately 5,000 asbestos-related deaths annually in UK
- CDM 2015 applies to ALL construction work
- Competence essential for all roles
- Health and safety is legal requirement, not optional
