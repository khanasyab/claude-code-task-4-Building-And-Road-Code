## Coverage

### Standard Form Contracts:

#### JCT (Joint Contracts Tribunal) - Current Edition: JCT 2024

**Main Contracts**:
- Standard Building Contract (SBC) - Major projects, traditional
- Design and Build Contract (DB) - Single-point responsibility (most popular)
- Intermediate Building Contract (IC) - Medium projects
- Minor Works Building Contract (MW) - Small, simple projects
- Measured Term Contract (MTC) - Maintenance programs
- Management Building Contract (MC) - Large, complex projects
- Construction Management Agreement (CM) - Very large projects
- Target Cost Contract - Risk-sharing (new, due June 2025)
- Home Owner contracts - Domestic projects

**Use**: Dominant in UK building sector, especially private sector

#### NEC (New Engineering Contract) - Current Edition: NEC4 (2017)

**Main Contracts**:
- Engineering and Construction Contract (ECC) - Most used
- Professional Service Contract (PSC)
- Term Service Contract (TSC)
- Framework Contract (FC)
- Design, Build and Operate Contract (DBO)
- Alliance Contract (ALC)
- Short form variants

**Payment Options (ECC)**:
- Option A: Priced with activity schedule (lump sum)
- Option B: Priced with bill of quantities (measurement)
- Option C: Target with activity schedule (collaborative)
- Option D: Target with bill of quantities (collaborative)
- Option E: Cost reimbursable
- Option F: Management contract

**Use**: UK Government preferred form, public sector, infrastructure

#### FIDIC - Current Edition: FIDIC 2017

**Rainbow Books**:
- Red Book: Construction (employer designed)
- Yellow Book: Plant and Design-Build (contractor designed)
- Silver Book: EPC/Turnkey (maximum contractor risk)
- Green Book: Short Form
- Pink Book: MDB Harmonised (development bank projects)
- White Book: Client/Consultant services
- Gold Book: Design, Build and Operate (1999 version, withdrawn 2017)

**Use**: International projects, UK contractors working abroad

### UK Public Procurement:

#### Procurement Act 2023 (Effective 24 February 2025)

**Replaced**: Public Contracts Regulations 2015

**Key Features**:
- Single unified framework
- Greater flexibility
- Enhanced transparency (central digital platform)
- SME access improvements
- Stronger exclusion/debarment provisions
- Emphasis on contract management

**Thresholds (2025)**:
- Works: £5,336,937
- Supplies/Services: £139,688 (central gov) / £214,904 (others)
- Light Touch Regime: £663,540

**Procedures**:
- Open Procedure
- Competitive Flexible Procedure (most flexible, expected most popular)
- Limited Tendering (direct award - exceptional only)
- Dynamic Markets

**Key Principles**:
1. Public benefit
2. Value for money
3. Transparency
4. Integrity
5. Fair treatment
6. Non-discrimination

## Contract Selection Guide

**Choose JCT when**:
- UK building project
- Private sector
- Traditional procurement
- Industry familiarity important
- Extensive case law desired

**Choose NEC when**:
- UK public sector
- Infrastructure project
- Collaborative approach desired
- Flexibility in payment mechanisms needed
- Early warning and proactive management valued

**Choose FIDIC when**:
- International project
- UK contractor working abroad
- Major civil engineering
- International finance (World Bank, etc.)
- International arbitration expected

## Key Differences Summary

| Feature | JCT | NEC | FIDIC |
|---------|-----|-----|-------|
| **Origin** | UK (1931) | UK (1986) | International (1913) |
| **Philosophy** | Traditional | Collaborative | International |
| **Complexity** | Moderate-High | Moderate | High |
| **UK Use** | Very High (building) | High (public/infrastructure) | Low (international only) |
| **International** | Low | Growing | Very High |
| **Design** | Varies by form | Defined in Scope | Varies by book |
| **Payment** | Various methods | 6 options (A-F) | Measured or lump sum |
| **Variations** | Variation instructions | Compensation events | Variations |
| **Disputes** | Adjudication | Adjudication | DAAB then arbitration |
| **Programme** | Required | Contract document | Required |
| **Risk** | Traditional allocation | Flexible | Clear by book color |

## Notes

- All three contract families are high quality and suitable for major projects
- Selection depends on project type, location, parties, and procurement strategy
- Professional advice recommended for contract selection
- Training essential for effective contract administration
- Amendments and bespoke terms should be minimized to retain standard form benefits
