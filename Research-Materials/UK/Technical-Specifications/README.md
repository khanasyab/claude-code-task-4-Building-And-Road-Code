## Coverage

### Eurocodes (BS EN 1990-1999):

- **EN 1990**: Basis of structural design
- **EN 1991**: Actions on structures (10 parts)
- **EN 1992**: Design of concrete structures
- **EN 1993**: Design of steel structures (20 parts)
- **EN 1994**: Design of composite steel-concrete structures
- **EN 1995**: Design of timber structures
- **EN 1996**: Design of masonry structures
- **EN 1997**: Geotechnical design
- **EN 1998**: Earthquake resistance
- **EN 1999**: Design of aluminium structures

All with UK National Annexes providing country-specific parameters.

### Withdrawn British Standards (Historical):
- BS 5950 (Steel) - withdrawn 2010
- BS 8110 (Concrete) - withdrawn 2010
- BS 5268 (Timber) - partially withdrawn

### Complementary British Standards:
- BS EN 1090 (Execution of steel structures)
- BS 8000 (Workmanship series)
- BS 8500 (Concrete specification)
- BS EN 10025 (Structural steel products)
- Fire engineering standards (BS 9999, PD 7974)
- Accessibility standards (BS 8300)

### Electrical Standards (BS 7671):

Complete coverage of electrical installations including:
- Protection against electric shock
- Protection against thermal effects
- Protection against overcurrent and overvoltage
- Selection and erection of equipment
- Inspection and testing
- Special locations (bathrooms, construction sites, medical locations, etc.)
- Electric vehicle charging (Amendment 1:2020)
- Arc fault detection devices (Amendment 2:2022)
- Smart energy systems (Amendment 3:2024)

## Transition Information

**Second Generation Eurocodes**:
- First generation: 2002-2010 (most still current)
- Second generation: Being published 2023-2028
- BS EN 1990:2023 published (supersedes BS EN 1990:2002 and BS EN 1997-1)
- First generation withdrawal: 30 March 2028
- Coexistence period: 2023-2028

**BS 7671 Updates**:
- 18th Edition: Published July 2018, effective 1 January 2019
- Amendment 1: 2020 (EV charging)
- Amendment 2: 2022 (AFDDs, RCD changes, prosumer installations)
- Amendment 3: 2024 (Smart energy clarifications)
- Current version: BS 7671:2018+A3:2024

## Notes

### Eurocodes:
- UK has used Eurocodes since 2005
- All UK accredited engineering degrees teach Eurocodes
- Previous British Standards (BS 5950, BS 8110) withdrawn 2010
- Professional interpretation essential

### BS 7671:
- Not statutory law but satisfies statutory regulations
- Required for Building Regulations Part P compliance
- Competent person required
- Regular updates mean must verify current version
