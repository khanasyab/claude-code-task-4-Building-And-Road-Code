CHAPTER 1: GENERAL PROVISIONS
======================================================================

1.1 SCOPE AND PURPOSE
1.1.1 General Scope
Part 4 of BNBC 2020 establishes minimum requirements for fire prevention and
fire protection in buildings to:
a) Safeguard life from fire, smoke, and toxic fumes
b) Protect property from fire damage
c) Provide safe evacuation routes
d) Facilitate fire service operations
e) Minimize fire spread between buildings
f) Ensure adequate fire detection and suppression systems

1.1.2 Applicability
These provisions apply to:
- All new buildings and structures
- Existing buildings undergoing alteration
- Change of occupancy situations
- Buildings being repaired after fire damage
- Temporary structures for public assembly

1.2 FIRE SAFETY OBJECTIVES
1.2.1 Primary Objectives
a) Life Safety: Protect building occupants and emergency responders
b) Property Protection: Minimize fire damage to buildings and contents
c) Continuity: Maintain essential operations during and after fire
d) Environmental Protection: Prevent environmental damage from fire

1.2.2 Fire Safety Strategy
Comprehensive approach including:
- Prevention through design and material selection
- Detection through alarm systems
- Control through compartmentation and suppression
- Evacuation through adequate means of egress
- Fire service access for firefighting operations

1.3 TERMINOLOGY AND DEFINITIONS
1.3.1 Key Definitions

FIRE RESISTANCE RATING: The time in minutes or hours that a material or
assembly withstands exposure to fire without failing, measured by standard
test methods.

FIRE DOOR: A door assembly with specified fire resistance rating, including
frame, door leaf, and hardware, designed to resist fire passage.

FIRE COMPARTMENT: A space within a building enclosed by fire-resisting
construction to prevent fire spread.

MEANS OF EGRESS: A continuous and unobstructed path of travel from any point
in a building to a place of safety outside (public way).

FIRE LOAD: Total quantity of combustible materials in a building or fire
compartment, expressed in kg/m² or MJ/m².

OCCUPANT LOAD: Maximum number of persons that may occupy a building or
portion thereof at any time.

AUTOMATIC SPRINKLER SYSTEM: An integrated fire protection system including
water supply, distribution piping, and sprinkler heads that activate
automatically upon fire detection.

SMOKE CONTROL SYSTEM: Engineered system using mechanical fans and dampers to
control smoke movement within building during fire.

FIRE ALARM SYSTEM: System of devices that detect fire and alert occupants
through audible and visual signals.

1.4 FIRE TESTS AND FIRE RESISTANCE RATINGS
1.4.1 Standard Fire Tests
Fire resistance shall be determined by:
a) ASTM E119: Standard Test Methods for Fire Tests of Building Construction
   and Materials
b) BS 476: Fire tests on building materials and structures
c) ISO 834: Fire-resistance tests - Elements of building construction
d) BSTI approved equivalent tests

1.4.2 Fire Resistance Rating Requirements
Minimum fire resistance ratings (in hours):

For Load-Bearing Walls:
- Type 1 Construction (Fire-resistive): 3-4 hours
- Type 2 Construction (Non-combustible): 2-3 hours
- Type 3 Construction (Ordinary): 2 hours
- Type 4 Construction (Heavy timber): 1-2 hours
- Type 5 Construction (Wood frame): 1 hour

For Floor/Roof Structures:
- High-rise buildings (over 23m): 2 hours minimum
- Multi-storey buildings: 1.5-2 hours
- Low-rise buildings: 1 hour

For Fire Doors:
- Fire compartment separations: 1-2 hours
- Corridor/lobby doors: 0.5-1 hour
- Service shaft doors: 1 hour

1.4.3 Fire Testing Laboratories
Approved testing facilities in Bangladesh:
- BUET Fire Research Laboratory
- BSTI Testing Laboratories
- Approved international laboratories (UL, FM, BSI, etc.)

1.5 FIRE DRILL REQUIREMENTS
1.5.1 Mandatory Fire Drills
Building owners and occupants must conduct fire drills:
- Commercial buildings: Quarterly (every 3 months)
- Residential high-rises: Semi-annually (every 6 months)
- Schools and institutions: Monthly during session
- Hospitals and care facilities: Quarterly on each shift
- Factories and industrial: Quarterly

1.5.2 Fire Drill Procedures
Each drill shall include:
a) Activation of fire alarm
b) Orderly evacuation of all occupants
c) Use of designated escape routes
d) Assembly at designated safe areas
e) Accounting for all occupants
f) Timing and evaluation of drill
g) Documentation and record keeping

1.5.3 Fire Safety Training
Building management must provide training on:
- Fire prevention measures
- Use of fire extinguishers
- Evacuation procedures
- Emergency communication
- Assisting persons with disabilities
- Fire alarm system operation

CHAPTER 2: PRECAUTIONARY REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

2.1 OCCUPANCY CLASSIFICATION FOR FIRE SAFETY
2.1.1 Occupancy Groups
Buildings classified by fire risk based on use:

GROUP A - ASSEMBLY (High Risk)
A-1: Theaters, cinemas, concert halls (fixed seating)
A-2: Restaurants, nightclubs, bars
A-3: Churches, mosques, temples, community halls
A-4: Indoor sports arenas, swimming pools
A-5: Outdoor assembly venues
Fire Risk: High due to large occupant numbers

GROUP B - BUSINESS (Moderate Risk)
Offices, banks, post offices, professional services
Fire Risk: Moderate due to normal occupant awareness

GROUP C - EDUCATIONAL (Moderate to High Risk)
Schools, colleges, universities, training centers
Fire Risk: Higher for younger students, moderate for adults

GROUP D - INSTITUTIONAL (High Risk)
D-1: Hospitals, nursing homes
D-2: Prisons, detention facilities
D-3: Day care centers (over 5 children)
Fire Risk: High due to occupant limitations

GROUP E - RESIDENTIAL (Moderate Risk)
E-1: Hotels, motels, boarding houses
E-2: Apartment buildings, dormitories
E-3: Single and two-family dwellings
Fire Risk: Moderate, higher in high-density occupancies

GROUP F - MERCANTILE (Moderate to High Risk)
Retail stores, shopping centers, markets
Fire Risk: High in large malls, moderate in small shops

GROUP G - INDUSTRIAL (High Risk)
G-1: Moderate hazard factories
G-2: Low hazard factories
Fire Risk: Variable based on processes and materials

GROUP H - HIGH HAZARD (Very High Risk)
H-1: Explosives manufacturing
H-2: Flammable gas/liquid storage
H-3: Combustible materials processing
H-4: Toxic materials handling
Fire Risk: Very high, special provisions required

GROUP I - STORAGE (Variable Risk)
I-1: Moderate-hazard storage
I-2: Low-hazard storage
Fire Risk: Based on stored materials

2.2 CONSTRUCTION TYPES AND FIRE RESISTANCE
2.2.1 Classification of Construction Types

TYPE 1: FIRE-RESISTIVE CONSTRUCTION
- All structural elements non-combustible
- Highest fire resistance ratings (3-4 hours)
- Required for high-rise buildings (over 23m)
- Suitable for all occupancies
Materials: Reinforced concrete, protected steel

TYPE 2: NON-COMBUSTIBLE CONSTRUCTION
- Structural elements non-combustible
- Moderate fire resistance (1-3 hours)
- Suitable for multi-storey buildings
Materials: Concrete, protected steel, masonry

TYPE 3: ORDINARY CONSTRUCTION
- Exterior walls non-combustible
- Interior elements may be combustible
- Fire resistance: 1-2 hours
- Height limitations apply
Materials: Masonry walls, wood or steel interior

TYPE 4: HEAVY TIMBER CONSTRUCTION
- Large timber members with fire resistance
- Non-combustible exterior walls
- Fire resistance through mass
Materials: Large dimension timber, masonry

TYPE 5: WOOD FRAME CONSTRUCTION
- Combustible construction permitted
- Lowest fire resistance (0.5-1 hour)
- Height and area limitations
- Primarily for low-rise residential
Materials: Wood framing throughout

2.2.2 Construction Type Requirements by Occupancy

High-Rise Buildings (over 23m):
- Type 1 construction mandatory
- 2-hour minimum fire resistance

Assembly (Group A) over 300 occupants:
- Type 1 or 2 construction
- 2-hour fire separation

Hospitals and Institutional (Group D):
- Type 1 or 2 construction
- Enhanced fire protection systems

Residential High-Rise (Group E over 23m):
- Type 1 construction
- Automatic sprinklers mandatory

Industrial/Storage (Group G, H, I):
- Type based on hazard classification
- Special fire protection requirements

2.3 FIRE ZONES AND FIRE DISTRICTS
2.3.1 Fire Zone Classifications
Local authorities may designate fire zones:

FIRE ZONE 1 (Very High Density Urban):
- Central business districts
- High-value commercial areas
- Dense residential zones
- Type 1 or 2 construction required
- Combustible exterior finishes prohibited

FIRE ZONE 2 (High Density):
- Urban commercial and residential
- Type 2 or 3 construction minimum
- Fire separation requirements

FIRE ZONE 3 (Moderate Density):
- Suburban areas
- Mixed use zones
- Type 3 or better construction

FIRE ZONE 4 (Low Density):
- Rural areas
- Agricultural zones
- Type 5 construction permitted

2.3.2 Special Fire Districts
High-risk areas may be designated special fire districts:
- Old Dhaka congested areas
- Chittagong port area
- Industrial zones (Tongi, Gazipur)
- Market areas (New Market, Chawkbazar)
Special requirements: Enhanced fire protection, wider streets, mandatory
sprinklers, fire department access.

2.4 FIRE SEPARATION AND COMPARTMENTATION
2.4.1 Fire Compartment Requirements
Buildings shall be divided into fire compartments to limit fire spread:

Maximum Compartment Areas:
- High-rise buildings: 2000 m² per floor
- Hospitals: 1000 m² per compartment
- Hotels: 2000 m² per floor
- Offices: 3000 m² per floor
- Shopping centers: 2000 m² per tenant
- Warehouses: 5000 m² maximum

Fire compartments shall be separated by:
- Fire walls with 2-4 hour rating
- Fire doors with appropriate ratings
- Fire-rated penetration seals
- Automatic fire dampers in ducts

2.4.2 Fire Walls
Fire walls shall:
a) Extend from foundation to roof
b) Have minimum 3-hour fire resistance
c) Be constructed of non-combustible materials
d) Be structurally independent
e) Have no openings (or protected openings only)
f) Extend 600mm above roof surface

2.4.3 Fire Doors and Shutters
Fire doors in fire walls shall:
- Have rating equal to wall (up to 3 hours)
- Be self-closing or automatic closing
- Have approved hardware (panic bars for egress)
- Be labeled by approved testing agency
- Be maintained in working condition
- Not be propped open (unless magnetic hold-open with alarm release)

Fire shutters for large openings:
- Automatic closure upon alarm
- Manual override capability
- 2-hour minimum rating
- UL or equivalent approval

2.5 INTERIOR FINISH MATERIALS
2.5.1 Flame Spread Classification
Interior finish materials classified by flame spread index:

CLASS A (Flame Spread 0-25):
- Non-combustible materials
- Cement plaster, concrete
- Ceramic tiles, stone
- Gypsum board
- Required: Enclosed exit stairs, corridors in high-occupancy buildings

CLASS B (Flame Spread 26-75):
- Low flame spread materials
- Treated wood panels
- Some plastics and composites
- Permitted: Most occupancies, general use areas

CLASS C (Flame Spread 76-200):
- Moderate flame spread
- Untreated wood
- Some carpets and textiles
- Permitted: Low-risk occupancies only

CLASS D (Flame Spread over 200):
- High flame spread, combustible
- Prohibited in most buildings
- Special approval required if used

2.5.2 Application Requirements
Exit Stairs and Corridors:
- Walls and ceilings: Class A only
- Floors: Class B maximum

Assembly Occupancies (Group A):
- Walls and ceilings: Class A or B
- Floors: Class B
- Decorative materials: Flame-retardant treated

Residential (Group E):
- Walls: Class B maximum
- Ceilings: Class B maximum
- Floors: Class C permitted

2.5.3 Decorative Materials
Decorative materials and furnishings shall:
- Be flame-retardant treated
- Not exceed 10% of wall/ceiling area
- Meet flame spread requirements
- Be removed after temporary use
Special concern: Festival decorations in public buildings

2.6 ELECTRICAL, GAS AND MECHANICAL SERVICES
2.6.1 Electrical Fire Safety
Electrical installations shall comply with BNBC Part 8 and:
- Use approved wiring methods
- Proper circuit protection (MCBs, fuses)
- Grounding and earthing systems
- Emergency power systems for life safety
- Separation from combustible materials
- Regular inspection and maintenance

Fire Risk Areas:
- Garment factories: Enhanced wiring protection
- Electrical rooms: 2-hour fire-rated enclosure
- High-rise buildings: Protected vertical cable runs

2.6.2 Gas Systems
LPG and natural gas installations shall:
- Comply with BNBC Part 8, Chapter 8
- Have automatic shut-off valves
- Gas leak detection systems
- Proper ventilation
- Separation from ignition sources
- Regular leak testing

Special Requirements:
- Restaurants: Commercial kitchen hood suppression
- Hospitals: Medical gas system safety
- Industrial: Process gas safety systems

2.6.3 HVAC Systems
Heating, ventilation and air conditioning shall:
- Not contribute to fire spread
- Fire dampers in duct penetrations through fire-rated assemblies
- Smoke detectors in return air ducts
- Emergency shut-down capability
- Fire-rated equipment rooms
- Kitchen exhaust hood fire suppression

Smoke Control Systems:
- Required in atriums and large spaces
- Stairwell pressurization in high-rises
- Coordinated with fire alarm system
- Regular testing and maintenance

2.7 SMOKE AND HEAT VENTS
2.7.1 Requirements
Large single-storey buildings require smoke and heat vents:
- Warehouses over 5000 m²
- Factories over 3000 m²
- Shopping centers over 5000 m²
- Parking garages

2.7.2 Design Criteria
Smoke vents shall:
- Provide 2% of floor area as vent area (minimum)
- Automatic or manual operation
- Opening within 2 minutes of alarm
- Located at high points of building
- Weather-protected when closed
- Clear smoke to facilitate evacuation and firefighting

2.8 SPECIAL HAZARDS
2.8.1 Commercial Kitchens
Restaurant and hotel kitchens require:
- Type I hood with fire suppression system
- Automatic extinguishing for cooking equipment
- Grease duct fire protection
- Class K fire extinguishers
- Gas automatic shut-off
- Regular cleaning and maintenance

2.8.2 Garment Factories
Bangladesh garment industry special requirements:
- Enhanced fire detection throughout
- Multiple means of egress (minimum 2 per floor)
- Automatic sprinkler systems
- Emergency lighting and exit signs
- Fire-resistant storage for materials
- Lint control and housekeeping
- Regular fire drills (monthly)
- Fire safety officer on site

2.8.3 Hazardous Materials Storage
Storage of flammable/combustible materials:
- Separate fire-rated room or building
- Ventilation to exterior
- Explosion-proof electrical
- Spill containment
- Fire suppression systems
- Approved containers
- Maximum quantity limits

======================================================================
CHAPTER 3: MEANS OF EGRESS
======================================================================

3.1 GENERAL REQUIREMENTS
3.1.1 Fundamental Principles
Every building shall have adequate means of egress to permit safe evacuation
of all occupants in case of fire or emergency.

Means of egress consists of three parts:
1. EXIT ACCESS: Path from any occupied point to exit
2. EXIT: Protected path to exterior (stairs, doors)
3. EXIT DISCHARGE: Path from exit to public way

3.1.2 Design Criteria
Means of egress shall be:
a) Continuous and unobstructed
b) Adequate capacity for occupant load
c) Protected from fire and smoke
d) Properly illuminated
e) Clearly marked with exit signs
f) Maintained free of obstructions

3.2 OCCUPANT LOAD CALCULATION
3.2.1 Determination Method
Occupant load = Floor area / Occupant load factor

Occupant Load Factors (m² per person):

Assembly Uses:
- Fixed seating: Actual number of seats
- Assembly without fixed seating: 0.65 m²/person
- Standing space (waiting areas): 0.28 m²/person
- Stages and platforms: 1.4 m²/person
- Restaurants (dining area): 1.4 m²/person
- Kitchen (restaurant): 9.3 m²/person

Business Uses:
- Office space: 9.3 m²/person
- Bank: 4.6 m²/person

Educational Uses:
- Classrooms: 1.85 m²/person
- Shops and laboratories: 4.6 m²/person
- Assembly areas: 0.65 m²/person

Residential Uses:
- Hotels/dormitories: 18.5 m²/person
- Apartments: Actual occupants or 18.5 m²/person

Mercantile Uses:
- Retail sales (ground floor): 2.8 m²/person
- Upper floors: 5.6 m²/person
- Storage areas: 27.9 m²/person

Industrial:
- General: 9.3 m²/person
- Concentrated equipment: 4.6 m²/person

3.2.2 Mixed Occupancy
For buildings with multiple uses, calculate each area separately and sum the
occupant loads.

3.3 EXIT CAPACITY
3.3.1 Width Requirements
Minimum exit width based on occupant load:

Doors:
- Minimum clear width: 750mm (single leaf)
- Preferred width: 900mm
- Maximum leaf width: 1200mm
- Capacity: 1 person per 5mm width

Corridors and Exit Access:
- Minimum width: 1100mm
- Serving occupant load over 50: 1200mm minimum
- Capacity: 1 person per 5mm width

Stairs:
- Minimum width: 1100mm
- Serving over 50 persons: 1200mm minimum
- High-rise buildings: 1500mm minimum
- Capacity: 1 person per 7.6mm width

Exit Discharge/Exterior Exits:
- Width not less than exit served
- Multiple exits may converge if adequate width maintained

3.3.2 Minimum Number of Exits
Occupant Load → Minimum Number of Exits:
- 1-500 persons: 2 exits minimum
- 501-1000 persons: 3 exits minimum
- Over 1000 persons: 4 exits minimum

Special Requirements:
- High-rise buildings: Minimum 2 exit stairs per floor
- Assembly occupancies: Exits based on occupant load
- Each floor must have minimum 2 exits (except single dwelling units)

3.3.3 Exit Arrangement
Exits shall be:
a) Remote from each other (minimum 1/3 diagonal distance apart)
b) Accessible from any point in building
c) Not through high-hazard areas
d) Not through kitchens, storage, or restrooms
e) Arranged to minimize confusion

3.4 EXIT ACCESS
3.4.1 Travel Distance Limitations
Maximum travel distance to nearest exit:

Without Sprinklers:
- Assembly, Educational, Institutional: 30m
- Residential: 35m
- Business: 45m
- Factory (low hazard): 60m
- Storage (low hazard): 75m

With Automatic Sprinklers (add 25m):
- Assembly, Educational, Institutional: 55m
- Residential: 60m
- Business: 70m
- Factory (low hazard): 85m
- Storage (low hazard): 100m

Dead-End Corridors:
- Maximum length: 6m (unsprinklered)
- Maximum length: 15m (sprinklered)

3.4.2 Corridors
Exit access corridors shall:
- Minimum width: 1100mm (1200mm if over 50 occupants)
- Clear height: 2100mm minimum
- Fire-rated construction if required:
  * 1-hour rating for buildings over 4 storeys
  * 2-hour rating for high-rise buildings
- Protected by smoke detectors
- Emergency lighting provided
- Exit signs visible from all points

3.4.3 Doors in Means of Egress
Door Requirements:
- Swing in direction of egress (occupant load over 50)
- Panic hardware for assembly occupancies over 50
- Minimum clear width: 750mm
- Maximum leaf width: 1200mm
- No mirrors on egress side
- Door swing not obstruct corridor
- Self-closing or automatic closing
- Readily openable from inside without key

Prohibited Features:
- Revolving doors as required exits (may be supplementary)
- Turnstiles counting as exits
- Sliding doors (except special cases with approved hardware)
- Locks preventing egress
- Delayed egress (except with approved special locking)

3.5 EXITS (PROTECTED VERTICAL CIRCULATION)
3.5.1 Exit Stairs
Interior exit stairs shall:
- Be enclosed in fire-rated shaft:
  * 2 hours for buildings up to 4 storeys
  * 2 hours for buildings over 4 storeys
  * 2 hours minimum for high-rise
- Pressurized in high-rise buildings
- Have fire-rated doors (1-1.5 hours)
- Self-closing doors with panic hardware
- Emergency lighting
- Exit signs at each floor
- Communication system in high-rise

3.5.2 Stair Dimensions
Tread and Riser:
- Minimum tread depth: 250mm (280mm preferred)
- Maximum riser height: 190mm (175mm preferred)
- Uniform dimensions throughout flight
- Maximum variation: 5mm

Landings:
- Width not less than stair width
- Length not less than 1100mm
- At door openings: swing not reduce effective width

Handrails:
- Required both sides
- Height: 865-965mm above nosing
- Continuous along flight and landings
- Smooth gripping surface, 35-50mm diameter
- Extension 300mm beyond top and bottom
- Load capacity: 0.9 kN/m

3.5.3 Exterior Exit Stairs
When permitted:
- Open to exterior air
- Protected from weather (canopy/overhang)
- Non-combustible construction
- Proper drainage
- Non-slip surface
- Adequate lighting
- Not more than 50% of required exits

3.6 RAMPS
3.6.1 When Permitted
Ramps may be used as exits when:
- Slope not steeper than 1:12 (8.33%)
- Accessible route required
- Large assembly venues
- Parking structures

3.6.2 Design Requirements
- Maximum slope: 1:12 (1:20 preferred for accessibility)
- Minimum clear width: 1100mm
- Surface: Non-slip, uniform
- Landings: Every 9m of rise and at direction changes
- Landing length: 1500mm minimum
- Handrails: Both sides if rise over 150mm
- Fire rating: Same as stair if enclosed

3.7 EXIT SIGNS AND EMERGENCY LIGHTING
3.7.1 Exit Signs
Required at:
- All exit doors
- Exit access doors to stairs
- Changes of direction to exits
- Locations where exit path is not obvious

Sign Requirements:
- Word "EXIT" or "বহির্গমন" (Bengali)
- Letter height: 150mm minimum
- Visibility: 30m minimum in clear air
- Illuminated: Internally or externally
- Green color on white background (international standard)
- Battery backup: 90 minutes minimum
- Pictograms: ISO standard running man acceptable

3.7.2 Directional Exit Signs
Directional arrows required where:
- Exit not immediately visible
- Multiple directions possible
- Long corridors
Sign spacing: Every 30m maximum

3.7.3 Emergency Lighting
Required in:
- Exit stairs and corridors
- Exit doors
- Assembly areas
- All means of egress

Performance Requirements:
- Automatic activation on power failure
- Battery backup: 90 minutes minimum
- Illumination level: 10 lux minimum at floor
- Transition: Within 10 seconds of power loss
- Monthly testing required

3.8 SPECIAL EGRESS PROVISIONS
3.8.1 High-Rise Buildings (over 23m)
Additional requirements:
- Minimum 2 exit stairs
- Pressurized stairs
- Fire service elevator
- Emergency voice communication
- Refuge areas (every 15 floors in tall buildings)
- Enhanced emergency lighting
- Stair identification signs at each floor

3.8.2 Assembly Occupancies
- Multiple exits based on occupant load
- Exit capacity: All occupants in 4 minutes
- Panic hardware on all exit doors
- No turnstiles in exit path
- Aisles to exits with proper width
- Fixed seating: Aisle requirements

3.8.3 Hospitals and Care Facilities
- Horizontal exits (fire-rated corridors to separate compartment)
- Refuge areas with smoke protection
- Minimum 2 exits per floor
- Wider corridors: 2400mm minimum
- Automatic door openers
- Areas of rescue assistance

3.8.4 Schools
- Direct access to exterior from ground floor
- Multiple exits per classroom wing
- Assembly areas: Stadium egress
- Wider corridors and stairs
- Panic hardware
- Drill requirements: Monthly

CHAPTER 4: FIRE DETECTION AND ALARM SYSTEMS
======================================================================

4.1 GENERAL REQUIREMENTS
4.1.1 Where Required
Automatic fire alarm systems required in:
- High-rise buildings (all types)
- Hotels and dormitories over 3 storeys
- Hospitals and care facilities (all)
- Schools over 2 storeys
- Assembly occupancies over 300 persons
- Shopping centers over 500 m²
- Factories and warehouses over 2000 m²
- Underground areas over 200 m²

4.1.2 System Types
- Conventional Systems: Zone-based detection
- Addressable Systems: Individual device identification
- Analog Addressable: Level of detection reported
Preference: Addressable systems for buildings over 5 storeys

4.2 SYSTEM COMPONENTS
4.2.1 Control Panel
- Listed and approved system
- Located in fire command center or entry lobby
- Accessible to fire service
- Display zones/devices in alarm
- Manual evacuation control
- Backup power supply

4.2.2 Detection Devices
Smoke Detectors:
- Ionization or photoelectric type
- Ceiling-mounted in general areas
- Coverage: 80 m² per detector (open areas)
- Required in: corridors, stairs, mechanical rooms, elevator lobbies

Heat Detectors:
- Fixed temperature or rate-of-rise
- Kitchens, boiler rooms, attics
- Areas unsuitable for smoke detectors
- Coverage: 50 m² per detector

Manual Pull Stations:
- Located at exits
- Each floor near stairways
- Maximum travel distance: 60m
- Height: 1200-1500mm above floor
- Break-glass type

Beam Detectors:
- Large open spaces
- High ceilings (over 10m)
- Warehouses, atriums

4.2.3 Notification Devices
Audible Alarms:
- Bells, horns, sirens
- Sound level: 75 dB minimum at 3m
- 15 dB above ambient noise
- Distinct from other signals
- Temporal-3 pattern (ISO 8201)

Visual Alarms:
- Strobe lights
- Required in public areas and corridors
- Accessibility for hearing impaired
- Flash rate: 1-3 Hz

Voice Evacuation:
- Required in high-rise buildings
- Pre-recorded messages
- Live microphone capability
- Minimum 75 dB sound level
- Two-way communication in exit stairs

4.3 SYSTEM OPERATION
4.3.1 Alarm Sequence
Upon detection:
1. Control panel receives signal
2. Alarm verification (if programmed)
3. Audible and visual alarms activate
4. Elevator recall to ground floor
5. Door hold-open releases
6. Smoke control activates
7. Emergency lighting activates
8. Notification to fire service (automatic or manual)

4.3.2 Zoning
- Each floor separate zone
- Large floors: Multiple zones
- Zone size: Maximum 2000 m²
- Stairwells: Separate zones
- Mechanical spaces: Separate zones

4.4 POWER SUPPLY
4.4.1 Primary Power
- Connected to normal building power
- Dedicated circuit
- Located in fire-rated electrical room
- Protection from fire and water damage

4.4.2 Backup Power
- Battery backup: 24 hours standby + 15 minutes alarm
- Generator backup (high-rise)
- Automatic transfer
- Low battery alarm
- Regular testing

4.5 MONITORING AND MAINTENANCE
4.5.1 Central Monitoring
High-rise and high-risk buildings:
- 24-hour monitoring
- Connection to fire service
- Automatic signal transmission
- Response protocol established

4.5.2 Testing and Maintenance
- Weekly: Visual inspection, test functions
- Monthly: Battery testing, alarm testing
- Quarterly: Full system testing
- Annual: Comprehensive inspection by licensed technician
- Records maintained minimum 3 years
- Testing by BSTI approved agencies

CHAPTER 5: FIRE SUPPRESSION SYSTEMS
======================================================================

5.1 AUTOMATIC SPRINKLER SYSTEMS
5.1.1 Where Required
Mandatory automatic sprinklers in:
- High-rise buildings (all floors)
- Hotels over 3 storeys
- Hospitals and care facilities (all areas)
- Assembly occupancies over 1000 persons
- Shopping centers over 1000 m²
- Garment factories (all areas)
- Warehouses over 5000 m²
- Underground areas over 500 m²
- Atriums and large open spaces

5.1.2 System Types
Wet Pipe Systems:
- Pipes filled with water under pressure
- Immediate discharge when head activates
- Most common type for heated buildings

Dry Pipe Systems:
- Pipes filled with compressed air
- Water admitted after valve opens
- Unheated spaces, freezing risk areas

Pre-Action Systems:
- Requires detector activation before water flows
- Computer rooms, museums, libraries
- Prevents water damage from accidental activation

Deluge Systems:
- Open sprinkler heads
- Valve opens to flood area
- High-hazard areas, chemical storage

5.1.3 Design Standards
- NFPA 13: Automatic Sprinkler Systems
- Hydraulic calculations required
- Water supply adequate for demand
- Minimum 30-minute duration at design density

Sprinkler Spacing:
- Maximum coverage: 12 m² per head (ordinary hazard)
- Maximum spacing: 4.6m between heads
- Distance to wall: 2.3m maximum
- Minimum clearance below head: 450mm

5.1.4 Water Supply
Sources:
- Municipal water main (preferred)
- On-site water tanks with pumps
- Combined system

Tank Capacity (if used):
- High-rise buildings: 200,000 liters minimum
- Mid-rise buildings: 100,000 liters minimum
- Plus domestic water demand

Pressure:
- Minimum: 100 kPa at most remote head
- Fire pumps if municipal pressure insufficient
- Jockey pump to maintain pressure

5.2 STANDPIPE SYSTEMS
5.2.1 Requirements
Required in:
- Buildings over 4 storeys
- High-rise buildings (dry and wet standpipes)
- Enclosed stairs
- Stages over 100 m²
- Underground areas

5.2.2 Types
Class I (Fire Department Use):
- 65mm hose connections
- 380 liters/minute per connection
- Fire department connects own hoses

Class II (Occupant Use):
- 40mm hose stations
- 40 liters/minute
- Pre-connected hose (30m length)

Class III (Combined):
- 65mm connections
- 40mm adapters
- Both fire department and occupant use

5.2.3 Locations
- Enclosed exit stairs
- Exit landings
- Maximum 60m hose travel from any point
- Clearly marked "FIRE HOSE"

5.3 PORTABLE FIRE EXTINGUISHERS
5.3.1 General Requirements
Required in all buildings and occupancies.

5.3.2 Types and Classes
Class A: Ordinary combustibles (wood, paper, cloth)
- Water, foam, dry chemical extinguishers
- Red color band

Class B: Flammable liquids (oil, gasoline, solvents)
- CO2, dry chemical, foam extinguishers
- Yellow color band

Class C: Electrical fires
- CO2, dry chemical (non-conductive)
- Blue color band

Class D: Combustible metals
- Specialized dry powder
- Special training required

Class K: Kitchen fires (cooking oils)
- Wet chemical extinguishers
- Required in commercial kitchens

5.3.3 Distribution
Minimum Rating and Travel Distance:

Class A (Ordinary):
- Light hazard: 2A rating, 23m travel
- Moderate hazard: 2A rating, 15m travel
- High hazard: 4A rating, 11m travel

Class B (Flammable):
- Low hazard: 5B rating, 15m travel
- Moderate hazard: 10B rating, 9m travel
- High hazard: 20B rating, 9m travel

5.3.4 Installation
- Mounted on wall brackets or cabinets
- Height: 1.5m maximum to top (large), 1.5m for small
- Clearly visible
- Access not obstructed
- Signage: "FIRE EXTINGUISHER" in English and Bengali
- Annual inspection and recharging

5.4 SPECIAL SUPPRESSION SYSTEMS
5.4.1 Commercial Kitchen Hood Systems
Required for:
- Restaurants
- Hotels
- Institutional kitchens
- Any commercial cooking

System Components:
- Automatic detection (fusible links)
- Manual pull station
- Wet chemical suppression
- Discharge nozzles over cooking surfaces
- Automatic gas/power shut-off
- Mechanical ventilation shutdown

5.4.2 Clean Agent Systems
For computer rooms, data centers, museums:
- FM-200, Novec 1230, Inergen gases
- No water damage to equipment
- Safe for occupied spaces
- Pre-discharge alarm and delay
- Proper ventilation after discharge

5.4.3 Foam Systems
For flammable liquid storage:
- Gasoline storage facilities
- Chemical warehouses
- Airport hangars
- Foam concentrate and delivery system

5.5 FIRE DEPARTMENT ACCESS
5.5.1 Fire Service Vehicles
Site access requirements:
- Minimum access road width: 6m
- Turning radius: 12m minimum
- Vertical clearance: 4.5m minimum
- Load capacity: 40 tons
- Grade: Maximum 10%
- All-weather surface
- Clear access to building entrances

5.5.2 Fire Hydrants
- Spacing: Maximum 120m along streets
- 100m maximum to building entrance
- 65mm outlets (minimum 2)
- Minimum pressure: 250 kPa
- Color coded: Red for public hydrants
- Clear access maintained
- Regular testing and maintenance

5.5.3 Fire Department Connection (FDC)
Required for:
- Sprinkler systems
- Standpipe systems
- Combined systems

Location:
- Exterior wall near main entrance
- Within 30m of fire hydrant
- Visible from street
- Labeled "FIRE DEPARTMENT CONNECTION - SPRINKLER/STANDPIPE"
- 65mm connections
- Check valve to prevent backflow
- Protected caps

5.5.4 Fire Service Elevator
Required in high-rise buildings (over 23m):
- One elevator serving all floors
- Protected by 2-hour rated shaft
- Lobbies at each floor
- Operates on emergency power
- Fire service key override
- Communication system
- Capacity: Minimum 8 persons
- Opens directly into stair lobby

======================================================================
CHAPTER 6: SPECIAL PROVISIONS
======================================================================

6.1 HIGH-RISE BUILDINGS (OVER 23M)
6.1.1 Enhanced Requirements
- Type 1 (fire-resistive) construction mandatory
- Automatic sprinklers throughout
- Voice evacuation system
- Pressurized exit stairs
- Emergency generator
- Fire service elevator
- Fire command center
- Smoke control system
- Enhanced emergency lighting
- Two-way communication in stairs
- Standpipe systems
- Refuge areas (very tall buildings)

6.1.2 Fire Command Center
Required contents:
- Fire alarm control panel
- Sprinkler monitoring
- Elevator controls
- HVAC controls
- Communication system
- Emergency generator status
- Building plans
- Fire department access keys
- Located near main entrance
- 2-hour fire rated room

6.2 UNDERGROUND BUILDINGS
6.2.1 Definition
Buildings with floor level more than 10m below lowest exit discharge.

6.2.2 Special Requirements
- Smoke control system mandatory
- Enhanced fire alarm system
- Automatic sprinklers
- Emergency lighting with extended battery
- Multiple exit routes to surface
- Elevator emergency operation
- Ventilation system
- Fire resistant construction
- Regular fire drills

6.3 ATRIUMS
6.3.1 Definition
Large volume spaces connecting multiple floors.

6.3.2 Requirements
- Sprinklers throughout atrium
- Smoke control system
- Smoke detectors at all levels
- Fire-rated separation from other areas
- Travel distance limitations
- Interior finish: Class A only
- Glass walls: Fire rated if serving as separation

6.4 PARKING STRUCTURES
6.4.1 Open Parking
Naturally ventilated, open on sides:
- No sprinklers required if open
- Fire separation from other occupancies
- Standpipes required if over 4 levels
- Emergency vehicle access

6.4.2 Enclosed Parking
Mechanically ventilated:
- Automatic sprinklers required
- Smoke detection and ventilation
- Fire rating: 2 hours from other occupancies
- Exit stairs at each level
- Emergency lighting

6.5 GARMENT FACTORIES
6.5.1 Bangladesh Specific Requirements
Following Rana Plaza disaster, enhanced requirements:
- Automatic sprinkler systems throughout
- Multiple means of egress (minimum 2 per floor)
- Minimum 3 exit stairs for large factories
- Enhanced fire alarm system
- Emergency lighting with backup
- Fire extinguishers at 15m spacing
- Monthly fire drills mandatory
- Fire safety officer on premises
- Lint control and housekeeping
- No storage blocking exits
- Fire service inspection quarterly
- Worker fire safety training
- Maximum occupant load enforcement

6.5.2 Exit Width Calculation
Increased capacity requirements:
- Stairs: 1 person per 5mm (vs standard 7.6mm)
- Doors: 1 person per 4mm (vs standard 5mm)
Recognizes rapid evacuation need

6.6 SPECIAL ASSEMBLY BUILDINGS
6.6.1 Theaters and Cinemas
- Multiple exits based on seating capacity
- Exit doors at intervals along aisles
- Panic hardware on all exit doors
- Aisle widths: Minimum 1100mm
- Emergency lighting throughout
- Sprinklers required over 300 seats
- Fire curtain for stage (if combustible scenery)
- No locks on exit doors during occupancy

6.6.2 Shopping Centers and Malls
- Sprinklers throughout
- Smoke control in mall common areas
- Fire separation between tenants
- Multiple exits from mall
- Exit travel distance: 60m maximum
- Emergency voice communication
- Fire command center
- Coordination with Fire Service

6.7 HERITAGE AND HISTORIC BUILDINGS
6.7.1 Balancing Preservation and Safety
Alternative compliance approaches allowed:
- Performance-based design
- Enhanced fire detection
- Improved suppression systems
- Operational fire safety measures
- Consultation with heritage authorities

======================================================================
CHAPTER 7: MAINTENANCE AND FIRE SAFETY MANAGEMENT
======================================================================

7.1 OWNER RESPONSIBILITIES
Building owners shall:
- Maintain all fire protection systems
- Conduct regular inspections
- Perform required testing
- Maintain records (minimum 3 years)
- Provide fire safety training
- Conduct fire drills
- Keep exits clear
- Maintain exit signs and emergency lighting
- Appoint fire safety officer (large buildings)

7.2 FIRE SAFETY OFFICER
Required for:
- Buildings over 15 storeys
- Hospitals and care facilities
- Garment factories
- Shopping centers over 5000 m²
- Assembly occupancies over 1000 persons

Responsibilities:
- Daily inspection of fire safety systems
- Coordinate fire drills
- Train occupants
- Maintain fire safety records
- Interface with Fire Service
- Update fire safety plans
- Investigate fire incidents

7.3 FIRE SAFETY PLAN
Required for all non-residential buildings over 3 storeys:
a) Building details and occupancy
b) Fire protection systems inventory
c) Evacuation procedures
d) Floor plans with exit routes
e) Assembly points outside building
f) Fire safety equipment locations
g) Emergency contact numbers
h) Duties of fire safety personnel
i) Procedures for persons with disabilities
j) Fire drill schedule

Submit to Fire Service for approval.

7.4 INSPECTION AND TESTING SCHEDULES
Monthly:
- Emergency lighting function test
- Exit sign illumination
- Fire extinguisher visual inspection
- Fire alarm visual inspection

Quarterly:
- Fire pump full flow test
- Generator load test
- Fire alarm full system test
- Fire door operation

Annual:
- Sprinkler system inspection (certified technician)
- Standpipe system flow test
- Fire alarm certification
- Emergency lighting battery test
- Fire extinguisher service

Five Years:
- Fire extinguisher hydrostatic test
- Sprinkler head replacement (or as recommended)

All testing by BSTI approved agencies. Records maintained on site.
