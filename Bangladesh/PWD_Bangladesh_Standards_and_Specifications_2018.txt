SECTION 1: INTRODUCTION AND GENERAL PROVISIONS
======================================================================

1.1 ABOUT PUBLIC WORKS DEPARTMENT
The Public Works Department (PWD) of Bangladesh is responsible for:
- Planning, design, and construction of government buildings
- Maintenance of public infrastructure
- Development and enforcement of construction standards
- Quality control of public works projects
- Technical guidance to government agencies

PWD maintains standards and specifications that govern all civil works
undertaken by or for the Government of Bangladesh.

1.2 SCOPE OF PWD STANDARDS
1.2.1 Coverage
These standards apply to:
- Government buildings and facilities
- Public infrastructure projects
- Roads, bridges, and drainage systems
- Water supply and sanitation facilities
- Irrigation structures
- Power plants and substations
- Educational and healthcare facilities
- Administrative buildings
- Residential quarters for government employees

1.2.2 Applicability
- Mandatory for all PWD projects
- Reference standard for other government agencies
- Widely adopted by private sector
- Coordinated with BNBC 2020 requirements
- Updated periodically to reflect current practices

1.3 PWD SCHEDULE OF RATES
1.3.1 Purpose
The Schedule of Rates (SOR) provides:
- Standard specifications for materials and workmanship
- Unit rates for construction items
- Measurement procedures
- Quality control requirements
- Testing protocols

1.3.2 Structure
PWD Schedule of Rates organized into sections:
- Section A: Earthwork and Site Preparation

1.3.3 Updates and Revisions

- Technical updates as needed

SECTION 2: EARTHWORK SPECIFICATIONS
======================================================================

2.1 EXCAVATION
2.1.1 Types of Excavation
Classified by material:
- Ordinary soil: Loam, sandy soil, soft clay
- Hard soil: Stiff clay, dense gravel, weathered rock
- Soft rock: Decomposed rock, conglomerate
- Hard rock: Requiring blasting or pneumatic tools

2.1.2 Foundation Excavation
Specifications:
- Excavate to specified depth and dimensions
- Bottom level within ±25mm tolerance
- Remove loose material from bottom
- Keep excavation dry during construction
- Support sides to prevent collapse
- Protect adjacent structures

For pile foundations:
- Excavate for pile caps after pile installation
- Allow minimum 150mm clearance around piles
- Level bottom for lean concrete

2.1.3 Backfilling
Material requirements:
- Free from organic matter, debris, and lumps over 75mm
- Approved soil from excavation or borrow pit
- Sand backfill for drainage layers
- Compaction in layers not exceeding 200mm
- Moisture content: Within 2% of optimum
- Compaction: 95% of maximum dry density (Proctor test)

Around structures:
- No backfilling until structure achieves adequate strength
- Minimum 7 days after concrete casting
- Careful placement to avoid damage
- No heavy compaction equipment within 1m of walls

2.2 SITE GRADING AND PREPARATION
2.2.1 Clearing
Remove all vegetation, roots, topsoil, and debris:
- Depth: 150mm minimum
- Disposal off-site or as directed
- Protected trees to remain unharmed
- Area: As shown on drawings plus 1m beyond building lines

2.2.2 Filling and Compaction
Fill material specifications:
- Approved earth, free from organic matter
- Maximum particle size: 75mm
- Free from roots, debris, and soluble salts

Compaction requirements:
- Layers: 150-200mm loose thickness
- Moisture: Optimum ±2%
- Compaction: 95% Modified Proctor for buildings
- Testing: Every 500 m² or as specified
- Equipment: Rollers, vibro-compactors

2.3 DEWATERING
2.3.1 Methods
- Sump pumping: For small quantities
- Well point system: For continuous dewatering
- Deep wells: For major projects with high water table

Requirements:
- Maintain dry conditions during construction
- Prevent soil piping and erosion
- Protect adjacent structures from settlement
- Proper disposal of pumped water
- Continue until backfilling completed

SECTION 3: CONCRETE WORKS
======================================================================

3.1 CONCRETE SPECIFICATIONS
3.1.1 Materials Compliance
All materials shall conform to:
- Cement: BDS EN 197-1
- Aggregates: ASTM C33 or equivalent
- Water: BDS ISO 12439:2011
- Admixtures: ASTM C494, C260

3.1.2 Concrete Grades
PWD standard grades:

M15 (1:2:4 mix):
- Compressive strength: 15 MPa at 28 days
- Use: Leveling course, plain concrete, mass concrete
- Cement content: 280 kg/m³ minimum

M20 (1:1.5:3 mix):
- Compressive strength: 20 MPa at 28 days
- Use: Reinforced concrete (minimum for structural)
- Cement content: 300 kg/m³ minimum

M25:
- Compressive strength: 25 MPa at 28 days
- Use: Beams, columns, slabs in multi-storey buildings
- Cement content: 320 kg/m³ minimum
- Designed mix required

M30:
- Compressive strength: 30 MPa at 28 days
- Use: High-rise buildings, heavy loads
- Cement content: 350 kg/m³ minimum
- Designed mix required

3.1.3 Mixing Proportions
Nominal mixes (by volume):
- M15: 1 cement : 2 sand : 4 coarse aggregate
- M20: 1 cement : 1.5 sand : 3 coarse aggregate

Designed mixes:
- Required for M25 and above
- Mix design based on trial mixes
- Submit mix design for approval
- Quality control mandatory

3.1.4 Water-Cement Ratio
Maximum w/c ratio:
- Normal exposure: 0.50
- Moderate exposure: 0.45
- Severe exposure (coastal): 0.40
- Very severe exposure: 0.35

3.2 REINFORCEMENT
3.2.1 Steel Specifications
Deformed bars (HYSD - High Yield Strength Deformed):
- Grade Fe 415: fy = 415 MPa (most common)
- Grade Fe 500: fy = 500 MPa (high-strength applications)
- Grade Fe 550: fy = 550 MPa (special applications)

Conform to:
- ASTM A615/A615M
- BDS ISO 6935-2

3.2.2 Bar Sizes and Weights
Common bar sizes (diameter in mm):
- 8mm: 0.395 kg/m
- 10mm: 0.617 kg/m
- 12mm: 0.888 kg/m
- 16mm: 1.578 kg/m
- 20mm: 2.466 kg/m
- 25mm: 3.854 kg/m
- 32mm: 6.313 kg/m

3.2.3 Concrete Cover
Minimum cover to reinforcement:
- Footings (in contact with soil): 75mm
- Beams/columns (interior): 25mm
- Slabs (interior): 20mm
- Exposure to weather: 40mm
- Severe exposure (coastal): 50mm

3.3 FORMWORK
3.3.1 Requirements
Materials:
- Timber: Seasoned, straight, free from defects
- Plywood: Minimum 12mm thickness, waterproof
- Steel formwork: For repetitive use
- Plastic/composite: Approved types

Construction:
- True to dimensions (tolerance ±6mm)
- Smooth internal surface
- Joints tight to prevent grout leakage
- Properly braced and supported
- Oil/release agent applied before use

3.3.2 Removal Time
Minimum periods (from concreting):

Sides of beams and columns: 24-48 hours
Soffit of slabs (props retained): 3 days
Soffit of slabs (props removed): 7 days (14 days for spans over 6m)
Soffit of beams (props removed): 14 days (21 days for spans over 6m)

Conditions:
- Concrete strength adequate
- No damage during removal
- Curing continues after removal
- Engineer's approval obtained

3.4 CONCRETE PLACEMENT
3.4.1 Preparation
Before placing concrete:
- Formwork inspected and approved
- Reinforcement position checked
- Formwork cleaned of debris
- Formwork dampened (not saturated)
- Access for placing arranged
- Vibrators and tools ready

3.4.2 Placement Procedures
General:
- Place within 30 minutes of mixing (90 minutes with retarder)
- Avoid segregation (maximum drop 1.5m)
- Place in horizontal layers
- Continuous operation (avoid cold joints)
- Construction joints at approved locations
- Roughen construction joints before resuming

Special conditions:
- Hot weather: Temperature above 32°C
  * Use cold water for mixing
  * Rapid placing and covering
  * Extra curing precautions

- Cold weather: Temperature below 15°C (rare in Bangladesh)
  * Use warm water
  * Protect from temperature drop
  * Extend curing period

3.4.3 Consolidation
Vibration:
- Internal vibrator (poker): 25-50mm diameter
- Insert vertically at 450mm spacing
- Penetrate 100mm into previous layer
- Withdraw slowly (no voids left)
- Do not touch reinforcement
- Do not over-vibrate (segregation risk)

3.4.4 Curing
Duration:
- OPC concrete: 7 days minimum
- PPC concrete: 10 days minimum
- Hot weather: 14 days
- High-strength concrete: 14 days

Methods:
- Water ponding (slabs, roads)
- Wet burlap/gunny bags
- Polythene sheets
- Curing compounds (spray application)
- Moist sand covering

3.5 QUALITY CONTROL
3.5.1 Testing Requirements
Concrete cubes:
- Size: 150mm × 150mm × 150mm
- Frequency: 1 set (3 cubes) per 50 m³ or per day
- Minimum: 3 sets per grade per project
- Testing age: 7 days and 28 days
- Curing: In water at 27±2°C

Slump test:
- Every concrete batch or every 20 m³
- Check consistency and workability
- Accept if within ±25mm of specified

Acceptance:
- Average strength ≥ specified strength + 3.5 MPa
- No individual cube < 0.85 × specified strength

3.5.2 Approved Testing Laboratories
- BUET (Bangladesh University of Engineering & Technology)
- BSTI (Bangladesh Standards and Testing Institution)
- PWD Materials Testing Laboratory
- Other BSTI accredited labs

SECTION 4: MASONRY WORKS
======================================================================

4.1 BRICK MASONRY
4.1.1 Brick Specifications
Types of bricks:

First Class Bricks:
- Machine-made or hand-molded
- Well burnt, uniform color
- Sharp edges and corners
- Compressive strength: Minimum 10 MPa
- Water absorption: 12-15%
- Dimensions: 240 × 115 × 75 mm (±5mm tolerance)
- Use: Facing work, exposed masonry

Second Class Bricks:
- Slightly under-burnt
- Minor irregularities acceptable
- Compressive strength: Minimum 7 MPa
- Use: Backing, concealed work, foundations

Standards:
- BDS 208: Specification for Burnt Clay Building Bricks
- BDS 1788: Specification for Perforated Clay Bricks

Special bricks:
- Perforated bricks: Reduced weight, better insulation
- Fire bricks: High-temperature applications
- Paving bricks: Roads and walkways

4.1.2 Mortar Specifications
Cement-sand mortar proportions:

1:3 Mortar (Rich):
- 1 cement : 3 sand (by volume)
- Compressive strength: 10-15 MPa
- Use: Exposed work, high stress, water retaining structures

1:4 Mortar (Standard):
- 1 cement : 4 sand (by volume)
- Compressive strength: 7-10 MPa
- Use: Load-bearing walls, general construction

1:5 Mortar:
- 1 cement : 5 sand (by volume)
- Compressive strength: 5-7 MPa
- Use: Internal partition walls (non-load bearing)

1:6 Mortar (Lean):
- 1 cement : 6 sand (by volume)
- Compressive strength: 3-5 MPa
- Use: Backing, temporary work

Sand requirements:
- Clean river sand or crushed stone sand
- Free from silt, clay, salts, and organic matter
- Grading: ASTM C144 or equivalent
- Moisture: Surface dry condition

4.1.3 Construction Procedures
Preparation:
- Wet bricks before laying (soak 2 hours or spray)
- Not saturated (surface moisture only)
- Remove loose particles and dust
- Foundation surface cleaned and leveled

Laying:
- Mortar bed thickness: 10-12mm
- Joints properly filled
- Vertical joints: 10mm
- Horizontal joints: 10-12mm
- Plumb walls (check with plumb bob)
- Level courses (check with spirit level)
- Bond: English bond (stretcher and header courses alternate)
        Or Flemish bond (stretcher and header in same course)

Curing:
- Keep moist for 7 days minimum
- Sprinkle water 3-4 times daily
- Cover with wet gunny bags if needed
- Protect from direct sun and wind

4.1.4 Wall Thickness
Standard thicknesses:
- 100mm (4" wall): One brick width, non-load bearing partitions
- 125mm (5" wall): Common for partitions
- 230mm (9" wall): Half brick thickness, load-bearing walls
- 340mm (13.5" wall): One brick thickness, heavy loads
- 460mm (18" wall): 1.5 brick thickness, very heavy loads

Minimum thickness requirements:
- Ground floor load-bearing: 230mm
- Upper floor load-bearing: 125-230mm
- Non-load bearing partitions: 100mm

4.2 CONCRETE BLOCK MASONRY
4.2.1 Block Specifications
Hollow concrete blocks:
- Dimensions: 400 × 200 × 200mm (nominal)
- Actual: 390 × 190 × 190mm (10mm mortar joint)
- Compressive strength: 4-7 MPa
- Hollow core: 50-60% of volume
- Conform to BDS standards

Solid concrete blocks:
- Compressive strength: 7-10 MPa
- Use: Load-bearing applications

Advantages:
- Faster construction
- Lighter weight
- Better insulation
- Reduced mortar consumption

4.2.2 Construction
Mortar: Same as brick masonry (1:4 or 1:3)
Laying:
- Place with hollow cores vertical
- Mortar on face shells only (not in cores)
- Plumb and level
- Half-lap bond pattern
- Curing for 7 days

4.3 STONE MASONRY
4.3.1 Types
Rubble masonry:
- Random rubble: Irregular stones, roughly dressed
- Coursed rubble: Stones laid in courses
- Use: Retaining walls, foundations, boundary walls

Ashlar masonry:
- Cut stone with fine dressing
- Uniform course heights
- Use: Architectural features, important buildings

4.3.2 Mortar
- Cement mortar 1:4 or 1:5
- Stone pointing after construction

SECTION 5: STEEL WORKS
======================================================================

5.1 STRUCTURAL STEEL
5.1.1 Steel Grades
Available grades:
- Mild Steel (MS): Fe 250, yield strength 250 MPa
- High Tensile Steel: Fe 410, Fe 540

Sections:
- I-beams (ISMB, ISJB, ISLB)
- Channels (ISMC, ISLC)
- Angles (ISA)
- Hollow sections (CHS - circular, SHS - square, RHS - rectangular)
- Plates and flats

5.1.2 Fabrication
Cutting:
- Gas cutting or sawing
- Accurate to ±2mm
- Edges smooth and square

Drilling:
- Holes for bolts and connections
- Diameter 2mm larger than bolt
- Burrs removed

Welding:
- Qualified welders
- Approved welding procedures
- Conform to AWS D1.1 or equivalent
- Non-destructive testing for critical welds

5.1.3 Surface Preparation and Protection
Cleaning:
- Remove mill scale, rust, oil, grease
- Wire brushing or grit blasting
- Clean dry surface before painting

Painting:
- Primer: Red oxide, zinc chromate, or zinc-rich primer
- Intermediate coats: As specified
- Finish coat: Enamel or approved paint
- Total dry film thickness: 150-200 microns

Galvanizing:
- Hot-dip galvanizing for severe exposure
- Zinc coating thickness: Minimum 85 microns
- Conform to ASTM A123 or ISO 1461

5.2 REINFORCEMENT STEEL (covered in Section 3.2)

5.3 METAL DOORS AND WINDOWS
5.3.1 Specifications
Frames:
- Mild steel angles or rectangular hollow sections
- Size as per opening and design
- Rust-proofed and painted

Shutters:
- Steel sheet: 1.2-2.0mm thickness
- Frame: Angle or RHS
- Stiffeners at 600mm spacing

Hardware:
- Hinges: Heavy duty, 100-150mm
- Locks: Approved makes
- Bolts, handles, closers as specified

5.3.2 Installation
- Frame fixed with frame anchors
- Mortar packing around frame
- Shutter hung and adjusted
- Smooth operation verified
- Final painting after installation

SECTION 6: ROOFING AND WATERPROOFING
======================================================================

6.1 ROOFING SYSTEMS
6.1.1 Corrugated Metal Sheet Roofing
Materials:
- Galvanized iron (GI) sheets: 24-28 gauge
- Corrugated aluminum sheets
- Color-coated steel sheets (pre-painted GI)

Specifications:
- Sheet width: 750-900mm (effective cover 600-750mm)
- Sheet length: As required (standard 1.8-3.6m)
- Profile: Corrugated or trapezoidal
- Overlap: Minimum 150mm lengthwise, one corrugation sidewise

Purlins and supports:
- Steel angles, channels, or timber
- Spacing: 1.0-1.5m depending on sheet gauge and load

Fasteners:
- Galvanized hook bolts with rubber washers
- Fix on crests, not valleys
- Spacing: 450mm maximum

6.1.2 Clay/Concrete Tile Roofing
Types:
- Flat tiles: 250 × 150 × 12mm typical
- Mangalore tiles: Interlocking curved tiles
- Concrete tiles: Flat or profiled

Battens:
- Timber or steel
- Spacing based on tile size
- Fixed to rafters/purlins

Installation:
- Start from bottom, work upward
- Overlap as per manufacturer
- Ridge and hip tiles properly fixed
- Nailing or wire tying

6.1.3 RCC Roof with Waterproofing (covered in Section 3)

6.2 WATERPROOFING
6.2.1 Flat Roof Waterproofing
Cement-based waterproofing:
- Waterproof cement plaster 1:3 with admixture
- Thickness: 20mm
- Two coats
- Curing: 7 days

Bituminous waterproofing:
- Hot-applied bitumen felt
- Primer coat on clean dry surface
- Two layers of bitumen felt with bitumen mastic
- Total thickness: 3-4mm

Membrane waterproofing:
- APP/SBS modified bitumen membrane
- Torch-applied or self-adhesive
- Two-layer system
- 100mm overlap at joints
- Turn up 300mm at walls

Cementitious coating:
- Two-component polymer-modified cement coating
- Applied by brush or spray
- Total thickness: 2-3mm
- Two to three coats

6.2.2 Basement Waterproofing
External waterproofing (preferred):
- Applied to outer face of basement walls and slab
- Bituminous coating or membrane
- Protection board over membrane
- Backfill carefully

Internal waterproofing:
- Cementitious waterproofing on inner face
- Less effective than external
- Use when external access not possible

Drainage:
- French drain around perimeter
- Sump pit with pump
- Weep holes in retaining walls

6.2.3 Bathroom and Wet Area Waterproofing
Substrate preparation:
- Clean, sound surface
- Fill cracks and voids
- Prime if required

Waterproofing application:
- Cementitious waterproofing compound
- Brush or trowel applied
- Two coats minimum
- Turn up 300mm on walls
- Cure 7 days before tiling

Critical areas:
- Floor-wall junctions
- Pipe penetrations
- Drain outlets
- Seal all potential leak paths

SECTION 7: FLOORING AND FINISHES
======================================================================

7.1 CEMENT CONCRETE FLOORING
7.1.1 Specifications
Base concrete:
- M15 or M20 concrete
- Thickness: 100-150mm
- Level and compacted base
- DPC below if ground floor

Screed/topping:
- Cement:sand mortar 1:2 or 1:3
- Thickness: 20-40mm
- Neat cement finish or colored cement
- Curing: 7 days

Procedure:
- Clean and dampen base
- Apply slurry bond coat
- Lay screed, compact well
- Finish with trowel (smooth or textured)
- Joints at 3m spacing or as per design
- Cure properly

7.2 TILE FLOORING
7.2.1 Types
Ceramic tiles:
- Size: 300×300mm, 400×400mm, 600×600mm
- Thickness: 8-12mm
- Glazed or vitrified
- Conform to relevant standards

Porcelain tiles:
- Higher strength and durability
- Lower water absorption
- Suitable for heavy traffic

Mosaic tiles:
- Small tiles (25×25mm to 100×100mm)
- Supplied on mesh sheets
- Use: Bathrooms, decorative areas

Natural stone:
- Marble, granite slabs
- Thickness: 18-25mm
- Polished or honed finish

7.2.2 Installation
Substrate:
- Level concrete surface
- ±3mm in 2m
- Clean and dry

Adhesive method (preferred):
- Tile adhesive (thin bed)
- Notched trowel application
- Thickness: 3-6mm
- Press tile firmly

Mortar bed method:
- Cement:sand 1:3 or 1:4
- Thickness: 20-25mm
- Lay tile on fresh mortar
- Tap to level

Jointing:
- Joint width: 2-5mm
- Grout: Cement-based or epoxy
- Clean excess immediately
- Cure 3 days

7.3 TERRAZZO FLOORING
7.3.1 Composition
- Cement matrix with marble chips
- Chip size: 6-12mm
- Laid in-situ or precast tiles

7.3.2 Procedure
- Base: Concrete, clean and rough
- Bedding: 1:3 cement:sand, 12mm
- Topping: 1:2 cement:marble chips, 20mm
- Dividing strips: Brass or aluminum
- Cure 7 days
- Grind and polish after curing
- Multiple grinding passes (coarse to fine)
- Final polishing with oxalic acid

7.4 PLASTERING
7.4.1 Cement Plaster
Mixes:
- Undercoat: 1:4 cement:sand, 12mm
- Finish coat: 1:3 cement:sand, 8-10mm
- Single coat: 1:4, 12-20mm thickness

Procedure:
- Dampen surface
- Apply in layers if thick
- Compact well, no voids
- Finish with trowel
- Cure 7 days

Special plasters:
- External: Waterproof additive
- Textured: Rough or patterned finish
- Gypsum plaster: Interior surfaces

SECTION 8: PAINTING AND GLAZING
======================================================================

8.1 SURFACE PREPARATION
8.1.1 New Plaster
- Allow 28 days drying after plastering
- Fill cracks and irregularities
- Rub down with sandpaper
- Remove dust
- Apply primer/sealer

8.1.2 Wood Surface
- Smooth with sandpaper
- Remove dust and resin
- Knots sealed with shellac
- Prime with wood primer

8.1.3 Metal Surface
- Remove rust, scale, oil
- Wire brushing or grit blasting
- Prime immediately (within 4 hours)
- Red oxide or zinc chromate primer

8.2 PAINT SYSTEMS
8.2.1 Interior Walls (Plaster)
System 1: Emulsion paint
- Primer/sealer: 1 coat
- Emulsion paint: 2-3 coats
- Dry film thickness: 100-120 microns

System 2: Distemper
- Apply to slightly damp surface
- Two coats
- Economical, but less durable

8.2.2 Exterior Walls
Weatherproof paint system:
- Primer: Exterior grade
- Undercoat: 1 coat
- Finish: Exterior emulsion or acrylic, 2 coats
- Total dry film thickness: 150-180 microns

Special coatings:
- Textured coating (Apex, etc.)
- Elastomeric coating
- Waterproofing paint

8.2.3 Wood (Doors, Windows, Furniture)
Enamel paint system:
- Primer: Wood primer, 1 coat
- Putty: Fill holes and gaps
- Undercoat: 1-2 coats
- Enamel finish: 2 coats
- Sand between coats

Varnish/Polish:
- Wood stain (if coloring)
- Sealer coat
- Varnish: 2-3 coats
- Sand between coats
- Final coat: Spirit polish or polyurethane

8.2.4 Steel Work
Protection system:
- Shop primer: At fabrication shop
- Primer: Red oxide, 2 coats (if shop primer not applied)
- Undercoat: 1-2 coats
- Finish: Enamel, 2 coats
- Total DFT: 150-200 microns

For severe exposure:
- Zinc-rich primer
- Epoxy intermediate coats
- Polyurethane finish
- Total DFT: 250-300 microns

8.3 GLAZING
8.3.1 Types of Glass
Clear float glass:
- Thickness: 4-12mm
- Use: Windows, doors

Toughened/tempered glass:
- 4-5 times stronger than regular glass
- Use: Doors, large glazing, safety areas

Laminated glass:
- PVB interlayer
- Safety glass (holds together when broken)
- Use: Security, overhead glazing

Reflective/tinted glass:
- Solar control
- Use: Building facades

8.3.2 Installation
Wood frames:
- Putty glazing
- Putty bed and face putty
- Glass beads (optional)

Metal/aluminum frames:
- Rubber gaskets or structural glazing
- Proper drainage
- Sealed joints

Safety:
- Minimum thickness based on size
- Large panels: Toughened or laminated
- Overhead glazing: Laminated mandatory

SECTION 9: PLUMBING AND SANITARY WORKS
======================================================================

9.1 WATER SUPPLY SYSTEM
9.1.1 Piping Materials
GI (Galvanized Iron) Pipes:
- Size: 15mm to 150mm diameter
- Standard: BS 1387 or ASTM A53
- Use: Water supply, general purpose
- Joints: Threaded with sealant

UPVC Pipes:
- Size: 20mm to 110mm
- Standard: ASTM D1785 or equivalent
- Use: Cold water supply
- Joints: Solvent cement

PPR (Polypropylene Random) Pipes:
- Size: 20mm to 110mm
- Use: Hot and cold water
- Joints: Heat fusion welding
- Durable, corrosion-free

Copper Pipes:
- Size: 15mm to 50mm
- Use: High-quality installations
- Joints: Soldered or compression fittings

9.1.2 Storage Tanks
Materials:
- Reinforced cement concrete (RCC)
- Plastic (polyethylene) tanks
- Fiber-reinforced plastic (FRP)
- Stainless steel (expensive)

Capacity:
- Residential: 150-200 liters per person
- Commercial: Based on occupancy and use
- Overhead tank: Minimum 40% of daily requirement
- Underground tank: Minimum 60% of daily requirement

Location:
- Overhead: Minimum 3m above highest fixture
- Easy access for cleaning and maintenance
- Protected from contamination

9.1.3 Fittings and Fixtures
Taps/faucets:
- Brass or brass-plated
- Chromium-plated finish
- Types: Pillar tap, bib tap, mixer

Sanitary fixtures:
- Water closets (WC): Floor-mounted or wall-hung, ceramic
- Urinals: Ceramic, wall-hung or floor-mounted
- Wash basins: Ceramic, pedestal or wall-hung
- Kitchen sinks: Stainless steel or ceramic

Flush tanks:
- Plastic or ceramic
- Capacity: 6-9 liters
- Dual flush preferred (water conservation)

9.2 DRAINAGE AND SANITARY SYSTEM
9.2.1 Soil and Waste Pipes
Materials:
- Cast iron (CI) pipes: Traditional, durable
- UPVC pipes: Modern, lightweight, economical
- Size: 100mm, 150mm (soil), 40-75mm (waste)

Requirements:
- Soil pipe: Minimum 100mm diameter
- Waste pipe: 40-50mm diameter
- Vent pipe: Minimum 50mm diameter
- Slope: 1:40 to 1:60 for proper flow

9.2.2 Drainage System Design
Two-pipe system (preferred):
- Separate soil pipes (WC, urinals)
- Separate waste pipes (wash basin, sink, bath)
- Join at inspection chamber or septic tank

One-pipe system:
- All fixtures connect to single stack
- Proper venting essential
- Traps prevent odor

9.2.3 Inspection Chambers and Manholes
Specifications:
- Size: Minimum 600×450mm (small), 900×600mm (standard)
- Depth: As per drain depth
- Material: Brick masonry or precast concrete rings
- Benching: Concrete benching with smooth channel
- Cover: CI or concrete cover, grade appropriate for load

Location:
- At junctions
- At changes of direction
- Maximum 30m spacing on straight runs
- Accessible for cleaning

9.2.4 Septic Tank and Soak Pit
Septic tank:
- Capacity: Based on daily sewage flow (3-5 days retention)
- Construction: Brick masonry or concrete
- Compartments: Two or three chambers
- Inlet and outlet baffles
- Access covers for cleaning
- Anaerobic digestion of organic matter

Soak pit/absorption well:
- Receives effluent from septic tank
- Percolates into soil
- Size based on soil percolation rate
- Brick or stone masonry (honeycomb pattern)
- Depth: 1.5-3m
- Distance from water source: Minimum 15m downhill

Alternative systems:
- Sewage treatment plant (STP) for large complexes
- Connection to municipal sewer (where available)

9.3 RAINWATER DRAINAGE
9.3.1 Roof Drainage
Gutters:
- Material: GI, aluminum, or PVC
- Size: Based on roof area and rainfall intensity
- Slope: Minimum 1:100 towards downpipe

Downpipes:
- Size: 75-100mm diameter
- Material: Same as gutter
- Spacing: Maximum 10m
- Fix securely to wall
- Discharge to surface drain or soak pit

9.3.2 Surface Drainage
Required for:
- Paved areas
- Yards and compounds
- Basement ramps

Design:
- Minimum slope 1:100
- Grated drains or open drains
- Connect to storm water system or soak pit
- Keep separate from sanitary drainage

SECTION 10: ELECTRICAL WORKS
======================================================================

10.1 WIRING INSTALLATIONS
10.1.1 Wiring Systems
Concealed conduit wiring (preferred):
- PVC conduits embedded in walls
- FRLS (Flame Retardant Low Smoke) cables
- Safe, aesthetic, durable

Surface conduit wiring:
- Conduits mounted on surface
- Use: Industrial, temporary installations

Conduit specifications:
- Medium duty PVC conduits
- Size: 20mm to 50mm
- IS 9537 or equivalent
- Proper earthing

10.1.2 Cables and Conductors
Types:
- Copper conductors (standard)
- Aluminum conductors (larger sizes, feeders)
- FRLS PVC insulated cables

Sizes:
- Lighting circuits: 1.5 mm² minimum
- Power circuits (plugs): 2.5 mm² minimum
- Air conditioners: 4 mm² or larger
- Feeders: As per load calculation

Color coding:
- Phase: Red, Yellow, Blue (3-phase)
- Neutral: Black
- Earth: Green or Green/Yellow

10.1.3 Switches and Sockets
Specifications:
- Voltage: 240V AC
- Current: 6A (switches), 13A (sockets)
- Material: Thermoplastic or metal
- Type: Modular (modern) or conventional

Requirements:
- Switches: Height 1.3-1.4m from floor
- Sockets: Height 0.3m (standard), 1.3m (counter top)
- Proper earthing connections
- ELCB/RCCB protection for bathrooms

10.2 DISTRIBUTION SYSTEM
10.2.1 Service Entry
Main components:
- Energy meter
- Main switch/MCCB
- Main distribution board
- Earthing system

10.2.2 Distribution Boards
Types:
- Single phase: Residential
- Three phase: Commercial, large residential

Construction:
- Metal enclosure
- MCBs (Miniature Circuit Breakers)
- RCCB/ELCB (Residual Current Circuit Breaker)
- Proper labeling of circuits
- Adequate spare ways

Rating:
- MCB ratings: 6A, 10A, 16A, 32A, etc.
- Selected based on load and cable size
- RCCB: 30mA for shock protection

10.2.3 Earthing System
Methods:
- Plate earthing (copper or GI plate)
- Pipe earthing (GI pipe)
- Rod earthing (copper-bonded steel rod)

Requirements:
- Earth resistance: Maximum 1 ohm (domestic), 0.5 ohm (industrial)
- GI earth wire: Minimum 8 SWG
- Connections: Tightened and corrosion-free
- Testing: Annual verification

10.3 LIGHTING SYSTEMS
10.3.1 Types of Luminaires
Incandescent lamps:
- Traditional, low efficiency (obsolete)

Fluorescent tubes:
- Better efficiency
- 36W, 40W common sizes

Compact Fluorescent Lamps (CFL):
- Screw or bayonet base
- 8W to 25W

LED Lamps (preferred):
- Highest efficiency
- Long life
- Various wattages and types
- Rapid adoption

10.3.2 Lighting Design
Illumination levels (lux):
- Living rooms: 100-150 lux
- Bedrooms: 75-100 lux
- Kitchens: 200-300 lux
- Offices: 300-500 lux
- Reading areas: 500-750 lux
- Corridors: 75-100 lux
- Stairways: 150-200 lux

10.4 POWER SYSTEMS
10.4.1 Circuits
Separate circuits for:
- Lighting
- General power (plugs)
- Air conditioners
- Kitchen equipment
- Water heaters
- Elevators (if applicable)

10.4.2 Load Calculation
Demand factor applied:
- Not all loads operate simultaneously
- Typical residential: 60-80% of connected load
- Commercial: 70-90% of connected load

10.5 EMERGENCY SYSTEMS
10.5.1 Emergency Lighting
Required in:
- Exit routes
- Stairways
- Assembly areas
- Public buildings

Duration: Minimum 90 minutes battery backup

10.5.2 Generator Systems
For essential buildings:
- Hospitals
- Fire stations
- High-rise buildings
- Data centers
- Critical facilities

Capacity: Based on essential loads
Fuel: Diesel or gas
Automatic transfer switch (ATS)

10.6 SPECIAL SYSTEMS
- Cable TV and telephone
- Internet and data networks
- Security and CCTV
- Access control
- Fire alarm (covered in BNBC Part 4)
- Public address system
