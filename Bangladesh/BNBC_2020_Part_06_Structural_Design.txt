CHAPTER 1: DEFINITIONS AND GENERAL REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

1.1 SCOPE
Part 6 establishes minimum requirements for structural design and construction
of buildings and structures in Bangladesh to ensure:
- Structural safety and stability
- Protection against collapse
- Resistance to dead loads, live loads, wind, earthquake, and other forces
- Durability throughout intended service life
- Public safety and property protection

1.2 GENERAL DESIGN PHILOSOPHY
1.2.1 Design Methods
Structures shall be designed using:
a) Load and Resistance Factor Design (LRFD) - Preferred method
b) Allowable Stress Design (ASD) - Alternative method
c) Limit State Design (LSD) - As per specific material codes

1.2.2 Design Objectives
- Ultimate Limit State (ULS): Prevent collapse
- Serviceability Limit State (SLS): Maintain functionality
- Durability: Withstand environmental conditions over design life

1.3 KEY DEFINITIONS

DEAD LOAD: Permanent gravity loads including weight of all structural and
non-structural building components, fixed equipment, and built-in features.

LIVE LOAD: Transient or movable loads from building occupancy and use,
including people, furniture, movable equipment, and stored materials.

WIND LOAD: Lateral and uplift forces on building from wind pressure and suction.

EARTHQUAKE LOAD (SEISMIC LOAD): Lateral forces from ground motion during earthquakes.

LOAD COMBINATIONS: Mathematical combinations of various loads for design,
accounting for probability of simultaneous occurrence.

FACTOR OF SAFETY: Ratio of ultimate strength to allowable working stress,
accounting for uncertainties in loading and material properties.

STRUCTURAL SYSTEM: Complete assembly of structural elements (beams, columns,
slabs, walls, foundation) that work together to resist loads.

DUCTILITY: Ability of structure to deform beyond elastic limit without sudden
failure, critical for earthquake resistance.

SERVICEABILITY: Structure's ability to maintain appearance and functionality
under normal service loads.

1.4 DESIGN RESPONSIBILITY
1.4.1 Qualified Professionals
Structural design shall be performed by registered Professional Engineers:
- Registered with Institution of Engineers, Bangladesh (IEB)
- Minimum Bachelor's degree in Civil/Structural Engineering
- Demonstrated competence in structural design
- Professional liability insurance recommended

1.4.2 Design Documentation
Complete design documents shall include:
a) Design basis and criteria
b) Load calculations
c) Structural analysis
d) Member design calculations
e) Connection design
f) Construction drawings with details
g) Specifications for materials and construction
h) Special inspection requirements
i) Professional engineer's seal and signature

1.5 REFERENCED STANDARDS
BNBC Part 6 incorporates by reference:
- ASCE 7: Minimum Design Loads for Buildings and Other Structures
- ACI 318: Building Code Requirements for Structural Concrete
- AISC: Steel Construction Manual
- NDS: National Design Specification for Wood Construction
- AISI: Cold-Formed Steel Design Manual
- Relevant BDS (Bangladesh Standards) and BSTI standards

CHAPTER 2: LOADS ON BUILDINGS AND STRUCTURES
======================================================================

2.1 GENERAL LOAD REQUIREMENTS
2.1.1 Load Types
All structures shall be designed for:
a) Dead Loads (D)
b) Live Loads (L, Lr - roof live, S - snow)
c) Wind Loads (W)
d) Earthquake Loads (E)
e) Rain Loads (R)
f) Soil Pressure (H)
g) Flood Loads (Fa)
h) Impact Loads (I) where applicable
i) Temperature Effects (T)
j) Settlement Effects (when significant)

2.2 DEAD LOADS
2.2.1 Calculation
Dead load includes weight of:
- Structural elements (beams, columns, slabs, walls)
- Permanent architectural finishes (floor finishes, ceiling, partitions)
- Fixed building services (HVAC, plumbing, electrical)
- Permanent equipment

2.2.2 Unit Weights of Materials (kN/m³)

Concrete:
- Plain concrete: 22-23 kN/m³
- Reinforced concrete: 24-25 kN/m³
- Lightweight concrete: 16-19 kN/m³

Masonry:
- Brick masonry: 18-20 kN/m³
- Hollow concrete block: 13-16 kN/m³
- Stone masonry: 20-26 kN/m³

Steel:
- Structural steel: 77 kN/m³
- Reinforcing steel: 77 kN/m³

Wood:
- Hardwood: 6-11 kN/m³
- Softwood: 4-8 kN/m³

Other Materials:
- Soil (compacted): 17-20 kN/m³
- Water: 9.81 kN/m³
- Glass: 25 kN/m³
- Aluminum: 27 kN/m³

2.2.3 Finish Loads (kPa)
Floor finishes:
- Ceramic tile on mortar bed: 1.2-1.5 kPa
- Terrazzo finish: 1.0 kPa
- Marble slabs: 0.6 kPa
- Vinyl/wood flooring: 0.2-0.3 kPa

Ceiling loads:
- Suspended ceiling with services: 0.5-1.0 kPa
- Plastered ceiling: 0.3 kPa

Partition loads:
- Movable partitions: 1.0 kPa (minimum uniform load on floors)

Roofing:
- Corrugated metal sheet: 0.1-0.15 kPa
- Clay/concrete tiles: 0.5-1.0 kPa
- Waterproofing membrane: 0.05-0.1 kPa

2.3 LIVE LOADS
2.3.1 Uniform Floor Live Loads (kPa)

Residential:
- Dwelling units (apartments, houses): 2.0 kPa
- Hotel guest rooms: 2.0 kPa
- Corridors and public areas: 4.0 kPa
- Balconies (same as room): 2.0 kPa (minimum 1.5 kN concentrated)

Educational:
- Classrooms: 3.0 kPa
- Corridors and stairs: 4.0 kPa
- Assembly areas: 5.0 kPa
- Laboratories: 4.0 kPa

Commercial/Office:
- Office areas: 2.5-3.0 kPa
- Lobbies and corridors: 4.0 kPa
- Computer rooms: 5.0 kPa
- Banking areas: 5.0 kPa

Retail/Mercantile:
- Retail ground floor: 5.0 kPa
- Upper floors: 4.0 kPa
- Storage areas: 6.0-12.0 kPa (depending on use)

Assembly:
- Fixed seating areas: 3.0 kPa
- Movable seating: 5.0 kPa
- Stages and platforms: 5.0 kPa
- Corridors and exit ways: 5.0 kPa

Garages and Parking:
- Light vehicles (cars): 2.5 kPa
- Heavy vehicles (trucks): 12.0 kPa minimum

Industrial:
- Light manufacturing: 6.0 kPa
- Heavy manufacturing: 12.0 kPa (minimum, may be higher)
- Warehouses: 6.0-12.0 kPa

Hospitals:
- Patient rooms: 2.0 kPa
- Operating rooms: 3.0 kPa
- Corridors: 4.0 kPa
- Laboratories: 5.0 kPa

2.3.2 Roof Live Loads
Flat or low-slope roofs:
- Non-accessible roofs: 1.0 kPa (minimum for maintenance)
- Roofs used for assembly: 5.0 kPa
- Roof gardens: 5.0 kPa (plus soil and planting loads)

Sloped roofs:
- Slope less than 20 degrees: 0.6 kPa minimum
- Slope 20-70 degrees: 0.3-0.6 kPa (reducing with steeper slope)
- Slope over 70 degrees: No live load (consider wind)

2.3.3 Concentrated Loads
In addition to uniform loads, consider concentrated loads:
- Office floors: 9.0 kN on 750x750mm area
- Corridors and exits: 4.5 kN on 750x750mm area
- Parking garages: Wheel loads per vehicle specifications
- Stairs: 1.5 kN on 300x300mm area on each tread

2.3.4 Live Load Reduction
For large areas and multiple floors, live load may be reduced:
- Permitted for members supporting large tributary areas
- Reduction factor based on tributary area
- Not applicable to assembly areas, parking garages, roof loads
- Maximum reduction: 40-50% depending on occupancy

Reduction formula (ASCE 7):
L = Lo [0.25 + 15/√(KLL AT)]
Where:
L = Reduced design live load per unit area
Lo = Unreduced design live load per unit area
KLL = Live load element factor
AT = Tributary area in m²

2.4 WIND LOADS
2.4.1 Design Wind Speed
Bangladesh experiences severe tropical cyclones, especially coastal areas.

Basic Wind Speed (3-second gust at 10m height):
Zone I (Coastal): 65 m/s (234 km/h)
- Cox's Bazar, Chittagong coast, Khulna coast, Patuakhali, Barisal coast

Zone II (Near Coast): 55 m/s (198 km/h)
- Areas within 50 km of coast
- Chittagong, Khulna, Barisal cities

Zone III (Interior): 50 m/s (180 km/h)
- Dhaka, Comilla, Sylhet, Rajshahi

Zone IV (Northern): 44 m/s (158 km/h)
- Rangpur, Dinajpur, northern districts

2.4.2 Wind Pressure Calculation
Basic equation:
p = 0.613 × Kz × Kzt × Kd × V² × I
Where:
p = Design wind pressure (N/m²)
Kz = Velocity pressure exposure coefficient (height and terrain)
Kzt = Topographic factor (hills, escarpments)
Kd = Wind directionality factor
V = Basic wind speed (m/s)
I = Importance factor

2.4.3 Exposure Categories
Category A (Urban/City Center):
- Dense buildings over 20m high
- Surface roughness: High
- Kz increases with height above 10m

Category B (Suburban/Town):
- Mixed residential and commercial
- Buildings mostly under 10m
- Moderate surface roughness

Category C (Open Terrain):
- Flat, open country
- Agricultural areas
- Low surface roughness

Category D (Coastal):
- Ocean, bay, or large lake (>5 km fetch)
- Flat coastal areas
- Lowest surface roughness, highest wind speeds

2.4.4 Wind Load Application
Structures designed for:
- Main wind force-resisting system (overall building)
- Components and cladding (windows, wall panels, roofing)

Wind direction:
- Four principal directions
- Critical load combinations determined

Wind effects:
- Lateral pressure on windward side
- Suction on leeward side
- Suction on roof surfaces
- Uplift forces
- Torsional effects on asymmetric buildings

2.4.5 Special Considerations
High-rise buildings:
- Dynamic effects considered above 60m height
- Vortex shedding analysis
- Building motion and occupant comfort

Flexible structures:
- Natural period calculation
- Resonance effects
- Damping considerations

2.5 EARTHQUAKE LOADS
2.5.1 Seismic Zones of Bangladesh
Bangladesh divided into seismic zones based on earthquake risk:

ZONE 1 (Highest Risk):
- Peak Ground Acceleration (PGA): 0.36g
- Seismic Zone Factor: Z = 0.36
- Districts: Sylhet, Sunamganj, Netrokona, Mymensingh, Sherpur, Kishoreganj
- Near plate boundary, severe shaking expected

ZONE 2 (High Risk):
- Peak Ground Acceleration (PGA): 0.28g
- Seismic Zone Factor: Z = 0.28
- Districts: Chittagong, Cox's Bazar, Rangamati, Bandarban, Khagrachari,
  Feni, Noakhali, Comilla
- Active fault zones, strong shaking possible

ZONE 3 (Moderate Risk):
- Peak Ground Acceleration (PGA): 0.20g
- Seismic Zone Factor: Z = 0.20
- Districts: Dhaka, Narayanganj, Gazipur, Tangail, Jamalpur, Brahmanbaria,
  Habiganj, Maulvibazar, Chandpur
- Moderate earthquake hazard

ZONE 4 (Lower Risk):
- Peak Ground Acceleration (PGA): 0.12g
- Seismic Zone Factor: Z = 0.12
- Districts: Rajshahi, Rangpur, Dinajpur, Khulna, Jessore, Kushtia, Bogra,
  Pabna, Faridpur, Barisal
- Lower but not negligible earthquake risk

2.5.2 Seismic Design Category
Based on seismic zone and building importance:

Importance Categories:
I. Low importance: Agricultural, temporary structures (I = 1.0)
II. Normal importance: Residential, commercial, office (I = 1.0)
III. Essential facilities: Hospitals, fire stations, emergency centers (I = 1.5)
IV. Critical facilities: Nuclear power, major dams (I = 1.75)

Seismic Design Categories (A through D):
- Category A: Lowest requirements (Zone 4, Importance I-II)
- Category B: Moderate requirements (Zone 3-4, all; Zone 2, Importance I-II)
- Category C: Enhanced requirements (Zone 2, Importance III-IV; Zone 1, Importance I-II)
- Category D: Highest requirements (Zone 1, Importance III-IV)

2.5.3 Seismic Base Shear Calculation
Equivalent lateral force method:
V = Cs × W

Where:
V = Seismic base shear
Cs = Seismic response coefficient
W = Effective seismic weight (dead load + applicable live load)

Seismic response coefficient:
Cs = (S × I) / (R × g)

Where:
S = Spectral acceleration (function of soil type and zone)
I = Importance factor
R = Response modification factor (ductility factor)
g = Acceleration due to gravity (9.81 m/s²)

2.5.4 Response Modification Factor (R)
Depends on structural system:

High Ductility Systems:
- Special moment-resisting frame (concrete): R = 8
- Special moment-resisting frame (steel): R = 8
- Dual system with special moment frame: R = 8

Intermediate Ductility:
- Intermediate moment frame (concrete): R = 5
- Ordinary moment frame (steel): R = 4.5
- Shear walls (special): R = 6

Low Ductility:
- Ordinary moment frame (concrete): R = 3
- Bearing wall system: R = 4
- Unreinforced masonry: R = 1.5 (limited use)

2.5.5 Soil Classification
Site soil conditions significantly affect seismic response:

SOIL TYPE A (Hard Rock):
- Shear wave velocity > 1500 m/s
- Very stiff material
- Amplification factor: Lowest

SOIL TYPE B (Rock):
- Shear wave velocity 760-1500 m/s
- Competent rock
- Amplification: Low

SOIL TYPE C (Very Dense Soil/Soft Rock):
- Shear wave velocity 360-760 m/s
- Dense sand, gravel, or soft rock
- Amplification: Moderate
- Most common in Bangladesh

SOIL TYPE D (Stiff Soil):
- Shear wave velocity 180-360 m/s
- Medium dense to stiff clay and sand
- Amplification: Moderate to high
- Common in Dhaka, Chittagong

SOIL TYPE E (Soft Clay):
- Shear wave velocity < 180 m/s
- Soft to medium clay
- Amplification: High
- Found in some coastal and riverine areas

SOIL TYPE F (Special Soils):
- Liquefiable soils
- Quick or collapsible soils
- Highly organic soils
- Very soft clays (over 12m thick)
- Site-specific analysis required

2.5.6 Seismic Design Requirements
All buildings in Bangladesh must be designed for earthquake resistance.

Essential Features:
- Continuous load path from roof to foundation
- Proper connections and anchorage
- Diaphragm action of floors and roof
- Ductile detailing of reinforced concrete
- Confinement of columns and beams
- Proper lap lengths and development of reinforcement
- Avoidance of soft storey and short column effects
- Regular structural configuration preferred
- Irregularities identified and addressed

2.6 OTHER LOADS
2.6.1 Flood Loads
For flood-prone areas (as defined by Bangladesh Water Development Board):
- Hydrostatic loads on submerged walls
- Hydrodynamic loads from flowing water
- Wave action loads in coastal areas
- Debris impact loads
- Flotation (buoyancy) forces
- Erosion and scour considerations

Design flood elevation: 100-year return period or as specified by authority.

2.6.2 Rain Loads
Heavy monsoon rainfall in Bangladesh requires proper roof drainage design:
- Minimum roof slope for drainage
- Adequately sized drainage systems
- Blocked drain scenarios (roof ponding)
- Parapet and scupper design
- Prevention of progressive ponding

Rainfall intensity: 200-300 mm/hour for design in Dhaka region.

2.6.3 Temperature Effects
Thermal expansion and contraction:
- Long structures require expansion joints
- Thermal coefficient for concrete: 10 × 10⁻⁶ per °C
- Temperature range: 15°C to 45°C in Bangladesh
- Special consideration for exposed structures

2.6.4 Impact Loads
Where applicable:
- Elevator machinery: 2 × dead load
- Crane loads: Vertical impact 25%, lateral surge 10%
- Parking garages: Vehicle impact on barriers
- Handrails and guards: 0.75 kN/m minimum

2.7 LOAD COMBINATIONS
2.7.1 Ultimate Strength Design (LRFD Method)
Maximum effects from following combinations:

1. 1.4D
2. 1.2D + 1.6L + 0.5Lr
3. 1.2D + 1.6Lr + (0.5L or 0.5S)
4. 1.2D + 1.6S + (0.5L or 0.5Lr)
5. 1.2D + 1.0W + 0.5L + 0.5Lr
6. 1.2D + 1.0E + 0.5L
7. 0.9D + 1.0W
8. 0.9D + 1.0E

Where:
D = Dead load
L = Live load
Lr = Roof live load
S = Snow load (negligible in Bangladesh)
W = Wind load
E = Earthquake load

2.7.2 Service Load (Allowable Stress Design)
1. D + L
2. D + L + W
3. D + L + E
4. D + L + 0.5W
5. D + L + 0.7E

2.7.3 Special Combinations
For foundations and retaining structures:
- Include lateral earth pressure (H)
- Consider uplift and flotation
- Flood loads where applicable

CHAPTER 3: SOILS AND FOUNDATIONS
======================================================================

3.1 GEOTECHNICAL INVESTIGATION
3.1.1 General Requirements
Site investigation mandatory for all buildings except:
- Single-storey residential (area < 90 m²)
- Temporary structures
- Buildings on proven satisfactory soil

3.1.2 Investigation Extent
Minimum requirements:

Small Buildings (< 4 storeys, < 500 m²):
- Minimum 1 borehole at center
- Depth: 2× foundation width or 6m, whichever greater
- SPT testing at 1.5m intervals

Medium Buildings (4-10 storeys or 500-2000 m²):
- Minimum 3 boreholes
- Depth: 1.5× building width or 20m depth
- SPT testing at 1.5m intervals
- Laboratory testing of samples

Large Buildings (> 10 storeys or > 2000 m²):
- Minimum 4 boreholes (1 per 1000 m² approximately)
- Depth: To competent bearing stratum, minimum 25m
- Comprehensive SPT testing
- Undisturbed sampling and laboratory testing
- Groundwater level determination
- Soil classification and properties

3.1.3 Investigation Methods
Field Tests:
- Standard Penetration Test (SPT) - ASTM D1586
- Cone Penetration Test (CPT)
- Plate load test for important structures
- Groundwater observation

Laboratory Tests:
- Moisture content
- Atterberg limits (liquid limit, plastic limit)
- Grain size distribution
- Unconfined compression test
- Consolidation test
- Direct shear or triaxial shear test
- Chemical analysis (for sulfate, chloride content)

3.1.4 Soil Classification
According to Unified Soil Classification System (USCS):

Coarse-Grained Soils:
- GW: Well-graded gravel
- GP: Poorly-graded gravel
- GM: Silty gravel
- GC: Clayey gravel
- SW: Well-graded sand
- SP: Poorly-graded sand
- SM: Silty sand
- SC: Clayey sand

Fine-Grained Soils:
- ML: Low plasticity silt
- CL: Low plasticity clay
- OL: Organic silt/clay (low plasticity)
- MH: High plasticity silt
- CH: High plasticity clay
- OH: Organic clay (high plasticity)

3.2 SHALLOW FOUNDATIONS
3.2.1 Types
- Isolated footings (individual columns)
- Combined footings (two or more columns)
- Strip footings (continuous under walls)
- Mat/raft foundations (entire building area)

3.2.2 Bearing Capacity
Safe bearing capacity from soil testing:

Presumptive Bearing Capacity (kPa) - When testing not done:
- Hard rock: 3000 kPa
- Medium rock: 1500 kPa
- Soft rock: 600 kPa
- Very dense sand/gravel: 450 kPa
- Dense sand/gravel: 300 kPa
- Medium dense sand: 200 kPa
- Loose sand: 100 kPa
- Very stiff clay: 300 kPa
- Stiff clay: 200 kPa
- Medium clay: 100 kPa
- Soft clay: 75 kPa (requires improvement or piling)

Actual bearing capacity from SPT N-values:
For cohesionless soils (sand):
qa = 12 × N × (B + 0.3)² / B² × Df/B kPa

For cohesive soils (clay):
qa = Nc × Cu

Where:
qa = Allowable bearing capacity
N = SPT N-value (corrected)
B = Footing width (m)
Df = Depth of foundation (m)
Nc = Bearing capacity factor (typically 5.7)
Cu = Undrained shear strength

3.2.3 Minimum Depth
- Below frost penetration: Not applicable in Bangladesh
- Below seasonal moisture variation: 1.0m minimum
- Below topsoil and organic matter: 0.5m minimum below finished grade
- Deep enough for adequate bearing capacity
- Protection from erosion and scour

3.2.4 Settlement
Total settlement must be limited:
- Maximum total settlement: 65mm (isolated footing)
- Maximum differential settlement: 40mm between columns
- Angular distortion: 1/300 maximum (1/500 for rigid structures)
- Tilt: 1/250 maximum

Settlement calculation:
- Immediate settlement from elastic compression
- Primary consolidation (fine-grained soils)
- Secondary compression (organic soils)

3.3 DEEP FOUNDATIONS (PILES)
3.3.1 When Required
Deep foundations necessary when:
- Adequate shallow foundation bearing capacity not available
- Soft compressible soils near surface
- High water table problems
- Large loads requiring high capacity
- Control of settlement critical

3.3.2 Pile Types Used in Bangladesh
Driven Piles:
- Precast concrete piles (square, circular)
- Prestressed concrete piles (high capacity)
- Steel H-piles
- Steel pipe piles

Bored Piles (Drilled Shafts):
- Cast-in-place concrete
- Larger diameter (600-3000mm)
- Higher capacity per pile
- Suitable for weak to medium soils

Micro-piles:
- Small diameter (100-300mm)
- Underpinning existing buildings
- Difficult access situations
- Grouted for capacity

3.3.3 Pile Capacity
End-bearing capacity:
Qb = Ab × qb
Where:
Ab = Pile base area
qb = Unit end-bearing resistance

Skin friction capacity:
Qs = As × fs
Where:
As = Pile shaft surface area
fs = Unit skin friction

Total pile capacity:
Qu = Qb + Qs (Ultimate capacity)
Qa = Qu / FOS (Allowable capacity)

Factor of Safety (FOS):
- Based on static analysis: FOS = 2.5-3.0
- Verified by load test: FOS = 2.0

3.3.4 Pile Load Testing
Required for major projects:

Initial Test Piles:
- Minimum 1% of working piles (at least 2)
- Load to 2× design load
- Maintained for 24 hours
- Settlement monitored

Routine Test Piles:
- 2% of working piles
- Load to 1.5× design load
- Verify design assumptions

Methods:
- Static load test (maintained load test)
- Rapid load test
- Dynamic load test (PDA)

3.3.5 Pile Groups
Piles often installed in groups under column footings:

Spacing:
- Minimum 2.5× pile diameter
- Typical 3× diameter
- Closer spacing reduces group efficiency

Group efficiency:
- Efficiency factor < 1.0 for friction piles in clay
- Efficiency ≈ 1.0 for end-bearing piles
- Calculate group settlement separately from individual pile

Pile cap design:
- Reinforced concrete
- Distribute column load to piles
- Adequate embedment of piles
- Minimum 300mm above pile cutoff

3.4 RETAINING STRUCTURES
3.4.1 Lateral Earth Pressure
Active pressure (Ka):
- Used for design of retaining walls
- Soil pushing against wall
- Rankine or Coulomb theory

Passive pressure (Kp):
- Soil resisting lateral movement
- Used for embedded structures
- Higher magnitude than active

At-rest pressure (Ko):
- For non-yielding structures
- Basement walls, rigid structures

3.4.2 Retaining Wall Types
Gravity walls:
- Concrete or masonry
- Resistance from self-weight
- Height limited to 3-4m

Cantilever walls:
- Reinforced concrete
- L-shaped or T-shaped
- Economic for heights up to 8m

Counterfort walls:
- For greater heights (over 8m)
- Vertical triangular supports

Sheet pile walls:
- Steel or concrete driven piles
- Anchored or cantilevered
- Temporary or permanent

3.4.3 Design Requirements
Stability checks:
- Overturning stability: FOS ≥ 2.0
- Sliding stability: FOS ≥ 1.5
- Bearing capacity: FOS ≥ 3.0
- Overall stability of slope

Structural design:
- Reinforced concrete design for stem
- Base slab design
- Heel and toe structural adequacy

Drainage:
- Weep holes at 1-2m spacing
- Drainage layer behind wall
- Prevention of hydrostatic pressure

3.5 SPECIAL FOUNDATION CONDITIONS
3.5.1 Expansive Soils
If highly plastic clays (CH) present:
- Provide stiffened grade beams
- Isolated footings with void beneath
- Deep foundations through expansive layer
- Moisture control measures

3.5.2 Collapsible Soils
Loose silts and sandy silts:
- Prone to collapse when wetted
- Densification by compaction
- Chemical stabilization
- Deep foundations through zone

3.5.3 Liquefiable Soils
Loose saturated sands:
- Susceptible to liquefaction in earthquakes
- Densification by vibro-compaction
- Stone columns for densification
- Deep foundations to non-liquefiable stratum
- Consider lateral spreading effects

3.5.4 High Water Table
Coastal and riverine areas:
- Dewatering during construction
- Waterproofing of basement
- Buoyancy calculations
- Uplift resistance of foundations
- Permanent dewatering system if needed

3.5.5 Foundations in Flood-Prone Areas
Special considerations:
- Elevated foundations above design flood level
- Scour protection around piles
- Erosion-resistant materials
- Breakaway walls below flood level (if used)
- Flood openings in foundation walls

CHAPTER 4: BAMBOO STRUCTURES
======================================================================

4.1 GENERAL PROVISIONS
4.1.1 Scope and Applicability
Bamboo recognized as sustainable construction material for:
- Low-cost housing
- Temporary structures
- Rural construction
- Light roofing and scaffolding
- Traditional architecture

4.1.2 Species and Selection
Common bamboo species in Bangladesh:
- Muli bamboo (Melocanna baccifera)
- Borak bamboo (Bambusa balcooa)
- Tama bamboo (Dendrocalamus strictus)
- Ora bamboo (Bambusa arundinacea)

Selection criteria:
- Age: 3-4 years minimum
- Harvested in dry season
- No cracks, insect damage, or fungal attack
- Straight culms preferred
- Appropriate diameter for application

4.1.3 Treatment and Preservation
Required treatments:
- Borax-boric acid solution (1:1.5 ratio)
- Copper-chrome-boron preservative
- Soaking method: 2 weeks minimum
- Boucherie method for whole culms
- Protection from moisture and soil contact

4.2 DESIGN PROPERTIES
4.2.1 Allowable Stresses (Working Stress Design)
Based on species and treatment:

Bending stress: 8-15 MPa
Tension parallel to fibers: 10-20 MPa
Compression parallel to fibers: 8-15 MPa
Shear parallel to fibers: 1.0-2.0 MPa
Modulus of elasticity: 10,000-20,000 MPa

Reduce by 50% for connections and joints.

4.2.2 Load Factors
Safety factors:
- Dead load: Standard values
- Live load: Reduced (1.5 kPa for bamboo roof structures)
- Wind load: Full design values apply
- Earthquake: Same as conventional structures

4.3 STRUCTURAL SYSTEMS
4.3.1 Post and Beam Construction
- Bamboo posts as columns (embedded or with foundation)
- Bamboo beams and purlins
- Connections with pins, bolts, or lashings
- Bracing for lateral stability

4.3.2 Truss Systems
- Triangulated bamboo trusses for roof
- Span up to 12m possible
- Proper connection design critical
- Wind bracing required

4.3.3 Foundations for Bamboo
- Prevent soil contact (decay)
- Concrete pedestals preferred
- Embed in concrete with bitumen protection
- Minimum 300mm above ground level

4.4 CONNECTIONS
4.4.1 Types
- Lashed connections (traditional)
- Bolted connections
- Pinned connections
- Fish-mouth connections (cut to fit)
- Concrete-filled nodes (increased strength)

4.4.2 Design Principles
- Avoid splitting of culm walls
- Reinforce with wrapping at connection zones
- Load parallel to fibers preferred
- Multiple fasteners for redundancy

4.5 LIMITATIONS
- Building height: Maximum 2 storeys
- Permanent residential: With proper treatment and maintenance
- Not permitted in high seismic zones without engineering
- Regular inspection and maintenance required
- Expected service life: 10-15 years with treatment

CHAPTER 5: CONCRETE MATERIALS
======================================================================

5.1 CONCRETE SPECIFICATIONS
5.1.1 Concrete Strength Classes
Specified compressive strength (f'c) at 28 days:

Low Strength:
- M15 (15 MPa): Plain concrete, leveling courses
- M20 (20 MPa): Minimum for reinforced concrete

Standard Strength:
- M25 (25 MPa): Residential and commercial buildings
- M30 (30 MPa): Multi-storey buildings, beams, columns

High Strength:
- M35 (35 MPa): High-rise buildings
- M40 (40 MPa): Special structures, long spans
- M50+ (≥50 MPa): High-performance applications

5.1.2 Cement
Types conforming to BDS/BSTI standards:

Ordinary Portland Cement (OPC):
- BDS EN 197-1, Type I
- Most common, general use
- Compressive strength ≥ 33 MPa (7 days)

Portland Pozzolana Cement (PPC):
- Contains fly ash or pozzolanic materials
- Better durability, lower heat
- Suitable for mass concrete

Portland Blast Furnace Cement (PSC):
- Contains ground granulated blast furnace slag
- High durability
- Suitable for marine and sulfate environments

Sulfate Resisting Cement:
- For foundations in sulfate-bearing soils
- Coastal and some inland areas
- Lower C3A content

5.1.3 Aggregates
Fine Aggregate (Sand):
- River sand or crushed stone sand
- Clean, free from silt, clay, organic matter
- Grading: BDS ISO 13055
- Fineness modulus: 2.3-3.1
- Deleterious materials < 3%

Coarse Aggregate:
- Crushed stone or natural gravel
- Angular shape preferred for strength
- Maximum size: 20mm (standard), 40mm (mass concrete)
- Minimum size: 4.75mm
- Grading: BDS standards
- Deleterious materials < 2%

Special Aggregates:
- Lightweight aggregates (expanded clay, shale)
- High-density aggregates (special applications)

5.1.4 Water
Mixing water requirements:
- Conform to BDS ISO 12439:2011
- Potable water generally acceptable
- Sea water not permitted for reinforced concrete
- Recycled wash water: Limits on solids and chemicals
- pH 6-8 acceptable

5.1.5 Admixtures
Permitted admixtures:
- Water reducing admixtures (plasticizers)
- High-range water reducers (superplasticizers)
- Air-entraining agents (not common in Bangladesh)
- Set retarders (hot weather concreting)
- Set accelerators (cold weather, rapid construction)
- Fly ash (ASTM C618 Class F or C)
- Silica fume (high-strength concrete)

All admixtures must conform to ASTM or equivalent standards.

5.2 CONCRETE MIX DESIGN
5.2.1 Design Methods
- DOE (UK) method
- ACI 211 method
- Trial mix method

5.2.2 Mix Proportions
Typical mixes (cement:sand:coarse aggregate by volume):

M15: 1:2:4 (or designed mix)
M20: 1:1.5:3
M25: 1:1:2
M30: Designed mix required

Water-cement ratio:
- Maximum 0.50 for normal exposure
- Maximum 0.45 for severe exposure (coastal, sulfate)
- Maximum 0.40 for very severe exposure

Cement content:
- Minimum 300 kg/m³ for M20
- Minimum 320 kg/m³ for M25
- Minimum 350 kg/m³ for M30

5.2.3 Workability
Slump test (ASTM C143):
- Very low: 0-25mm (roads, mass concrete)
- Low: 25-50mm (foundations, slabs)
- Medium: 50-100mm (beams, columns, walls)
- High: 100-175mm (high workability requirements, with superplasticizer)

5.3 CONCRETE PRODUCTION
5.3.1 Batching
- Weigh batching preferred (accurate)
- Volume batching acceptable for small work
- Batch tolerances:
  * Cement: ±1%
  * Aggregates: ±2%
  * Water: ±1%
  * Admixtures: ±3%

5.3.2 Mixing
Types:
- Machine mixing (preferred, required for large projects)
- Hand mixing (only for very small quantity)

Machine mixing time:
- Minimum 1.5 minutes after all materials charged
- Add 15 seconds per additional cubic meter capacity

5.3.3 Transportation
- Transport time: Use within 30 minutes of mixing (90 minutes with retarder)
- Transit mixers (concrete trucks) for ready-mixed concrete
- Prevent segregation and loss of workability
- No water added after mixing (except approved admixtures)

5.3.4 Placement
- Clean formwork and reinforcement
- Dampen formwork and existing concrete
- Place continuously (no cold joints)
- Avoid segregation
- Maximum free drop: 1.5m
- Vibrate adequately but not excessively
- Special considerations for hot/cold weather

5.3.5 Consolidation
- Internal vibration (preferred)
- Vibrator spacing: 450mm maximum
- Insert vertically, withdraw slowly
- Vibrate until surface smooth, no large air bubbles
- Do not over-vibrate (segregation risk)

5.3.6 Curing
Critical for strength development:

Duration:
- Minimum 7 days for OPC concrete
- Minimum 10 days for PPC concrete
- Longer in hot weather
- Mass concrete: 14-21 days

Methods:
- Water ponding on slabs
- Wet burlap or gunny bags
- Plastic sheeting
- Curing compounds (sprayed membranes)
- Steam curing (precast elements)

5.4 QUALITY CONTROL
5.4.1 Concrete Testing
Compression Test:
- Cube specimens: 150mm × 150mm × 150mm
- Cylinder specimens: 150mm diameter × 300mm height (if specified)
- Curing: 28 days in water at 27±2°C
- Testing: ASTM C39 or equivalent

Sampling frequency:
- Minimum 1 set (3 cubes) per 100 m³
- Minimum 1 set per day of concreting
- Minimum 1 set per 500 m² of floor area

Acceptance criteria:
- Average of 3 cubes ≥ f'c + 0.825×S (S = standard deviation)
- No individual cube < 0.85 × f'c

Other Tests:
- Slump test: Every load or every 50 m³
- Water absorption (durability)
- Permeability (water tightness)
- Chloride content (reinforcement corrosion)

5.4.2 Non-Destructive Testing
- Rebound hammer (surface hardness)
- Ultrasonic pulse velocity (quality and cracks)
- Core extraction (doubtful strength)

CHAPTER 6: STRENGTH DESIGN OF REINFORCED CONCRETE
======================================================================

6.1 DESIGN PHILOSOPHY (ULTIMATE LIMIT STATE)
Based on ACI 318 (adapted for Bangladesh conditions).

6.1.1 Design Principles
- Ultimate strength method (Load and Resistance Factor Design)
- Factored loads applied
- Reduced strength (capacity reduction factors)
- Ductile failure mode ensured
- Strain compatibility analysis

6.1.2 Load Factors (as per Chapter 2 load combinations)
Standard factors:
- Dead load: 1.2 or 1.4
- Live load: 1.6
- Wind load: 1.0
- Earthquake load: 1.0

6.1.3 Strength Reduction Factors (φ)
- Flexure (bending): φ = 0.90
- Axial tension: φ = 0.90
- Axial compression (tied columns): φ = 0.65
- Axial compression (spiral columns): φ = 0.75
- Shear and torsion: φ = 0.75
- Bearing on concrete: φ = 0.65

6.2 REINFORCING STEEL
6.2.1 Types and Grades
Deformed bars (ribbed):
- Grade 40 (Fe 280): fy = 280 MPa (not common now)
- Grade 60 (Fe 415): fy = 415 MPa (most common)
- Grade 75 (Fe 500): fy = 500 MPa (high-strength applications)

Conform to:
- ASTM A615/A615M (billet steel)
- ASTM A706/A706M (seismic applications, preferred in Zone 1-2)
- BDS ISO 6935-2 (Bangladeshi standard)

6.2.2 Bar Sizes
Standard bar designations:
#10: 10mm diameter
#12: 12mm diameter
#16: 16mm diameter
#20: 20mm diameter
#25: 25mm diameter
#32: 32mm diameter

6.2.3 Properties
Modulus of elasticity: Es = 200,000 MPa
Maximum strain: 0.005 (typical)
Ductility requirements for seismic design

6.3 FLEXURAL DESIGN (BEAMS AND SLABS)
6.3.1 Design Moment Capacity
Mn = As × fy × (d - a/2)

Where:
As = Area of tension steel
fy = Yield strength of steel
d = Effective depth
a = Depth of equivalent stress block = (As × fy) / (0.85 × f'c × b)
b = Width of beam

Design requirement: φMn ≥ Mu
Where Mu = factored moment

6.3.2 Reinforcement Limits
Minimum reinforcement:
As,min = (0.25√f'c / fy) × b × d ≥ (1.4/fy) × b × d

Maximum reinforcement (ductility):
As,max corresponds to net tensile strain ≥ 0.004
Balanced steel ratio: ρb = 0.85β1(f'c/fy)(600/(600+fy))
Maximum ρ = 0.75ρb

6.3.3 Beam Detailing
Concrete cover:
- Exposure to weather: 40mm minimum
- Not exposed: 25mm (beams), 20mm (slabs)
- Footings: 75mm minimum

Bar spacing:
- Clear spacing ≥ 25mm
- Clear spacing ≥ bar diameter
- Clear spacing ≥ 1.33 × maximum aggregate size

Development length:
- Tension bars: ld = (fy × ψt × ψe × ψs) / (1.1λ√f'c) × db
- Compression bars: ldc = 0.24fy × db / (λ√f'c) ≥ 0.043fy × db

Lap splices:
- Tension: 1.3 × ld (Class B) for seismic regions
- Compression: ldc
- Stagger splices, not all at same location

6.4 SHEAR DESIGN
6.4.1 Shear Strength
Concrete shear capacity:
Vc = 0.17λ√f'c × bw × d

Steel shear capacity (stirrups):
Vs = (Av × fy × d) / s

Total shear capacity:
Vn = Vc + Vs

Design requirement: φVn ≥ Vu

6.4.2 Stirrup Requirements
When Vu > φVc/2, provide stirrups:

Minimum stirrups:
Av,min = 0.062√f'c × (bw × s) / fy ≥ 0.35(bw × s) / fy

Maximum spacing:
- When Vs ≤ 0.33√f'c × bw × d: s ≤ d/2 ≤ 600mm
- When Vs > 0.33√f'c × bw × d: s ≤ d/4 ≤ 300mm

Stirrup sizes:
- #10 bars: Common for beams up to 300mm deep
- #12 bars: Deeper beams

6.5 COLUMN DESIGN
6.5.1 Types
- Tied columns (rectangular or square)
- Spiral columns (circular)

6.5.2 Load Capacity
Axial load capacity:
Po = 0.85f'c(Ag - Ast) + fy × Ast

Design strength:
φPn,max = 0.80φPo (tied) or 0.85φPo (spiral)

Combined axial and flexure:
Use interaction diagrams or calculations

6.5.3 Reinforcement Limits
Minimum steel: As,min = 0.01Ag
Maximum steel: As,max = 0.08Ag (0.04Ag preferred for congestion)

Minimum number of bars:
- Rectangular ties: 4 bars
- Circular spiral: 6 bars minimum

6.5.4 Tie and Spiral Requirements
Tie specifications:
- Minimum #10 bars for #25 or smaller longitudinal bars
- Minimum #12 bars for larger longitudinal bars
- Spacing: ≤ 16× longitudinal bar diameter, ≤ 48× tie diameter, ≤ least column dimension
- Arrangement: Support all longitudinal bars

Spiral specifications:
- Minimum 10mm diameter
- Clear spacing: 25-75mm
- Volumetric ratio: ρs ≥ 0.45(Ag/Ach - 1)(f'c/fy)

6.6 SLAB DESIGN
6.6.1 One-Way Slabs
Behavior: Span primarily in one direction (long/short > 2)

Minimum thickness (to limit deflections):
- Simply supported: L/20
- One end continuous: L/24
- Both ends continuous: L/28
- Cantilever: L/10

Reinforcement:
- Main steel: Based on moment design
- Distribution steel (perpendicular): 0.0018×Ag (Grade 60)

6.6.2 Two-Way Slabs
Methods:
- Direct design method (for regular layouts)
- Equivalent frame method
- Finite element method

Minimum thickness:
Depends on support conditions and span ratio.

6.7 SERVICEABILITY
6.7.1 Deflection Control
Limits on computed deflections:
- Immediate deflection
- Long-term deflection (creep and shrinkage)

Maximum permissible deflections:
- Roof/floor not supporting non-structural elements: L/180
- Floor supporting partitions: L/480 (incremental)
- Cantilever: L/180

6.7.2 Crack Control
Spacing of reinforcement to control cracking:
Maximum bar spacing in tension zone:
s ≤ 15(40/fs) - 2.5cc ≤ 12(40/fs)

Where:
fs = Service level stress in steel ≈ 0.6fy
cc = Clear cover to reinforcement

6.7.3 Durability
Provisions:
- Adequate concrete cover
- Quality concrete (low permeability)
- Proper water-cement ratio
- Good curing
- Consider environmental exposure classification
