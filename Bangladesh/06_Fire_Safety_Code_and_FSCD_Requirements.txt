FIRE SAFETY CODE AND FSCD REQUIREMENTS
COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE TO FIRE SAFETY IN BANGLADESH CONSTRUCTION
======================================================================

REGULATORY FRAMEWORK
---------------------
Primary Legislation: Fire Prevention and Extinguishing Act 2003
Building Code: Bangladesh National Building Code (BNBC) 2020 - Part 4
Enforcement Agency: Fire Service and Civil Defence (FSCD)
Ministry: Ministry of Home Affairs
Standards Reference: NFPA (National Fire Protection Association)

======================================================================
CHAPTER 1: LEGAL AND REGULATORY FRAMEWORK
======================================================================

1.1 FIRE SERVICE ACT

The Fire Service Act and the Bangladesh National Building Code
govern fire safety in Bangladesh.

PRIMARY OBJECTIVES:
- Prevention of fire incidents
- Protection of life and property
- Fire suppression and control
- Rescue operations
- Public safety education

1.2 FIRE PREVENTION AND EXTINGUISHING ACT 2003

The Fire Prevention and Extinguish Act 2003 is a key regulatory
framework establishing:

PROVISIONS:
- Fire safety requirements for buildings
- Fire service access requirements
- Fire fighting equipment specifications
- Inspection and enforcement powers
- Penalties for non-compliance
- Emergency response procedures

AMENDMENTS:
The Act has been subject to several amendments due to past
fire-related experiences, reflecting lessons learned from major
fire incidents in Bangladesh.

1.3 BNBC 2020 PART 4: FIRE SAFETY

Part 4 of the BNBC 2020 covers comprehensive fire prevention
requirements, specifying:

CHAPTER 1: GENERAL CONDITIONS OF FIRE PROTECTION
- Fire safety objectives
- Building classification by fire resistance
- Construction types
- Fire resistance ratings

CHAPTER 2: PRECAUTIONARY REQUIREMENTS
- Fire prevention measures
- Material specifications
- Storage requirements
- Process safety

CHAPTER 3: PROVISIONS FOR EXIT
- Means of egress design
- Exit capacity calculations
- Exit arrangement and marking
- Emergency lighting

CHAPTER 4: REQUIREMENTS TO MINIMIZE DANGER TO LIFE
- Fire detection and alarm systems
- Fire suppression systems
- Smoke control
- Emergency power systems

1.4 FSCD ROLE AND AUTHORITY

Fire Service and Civil Defence (FSCD) is the primary authority
responsible for fire safety enforcement in Bangladesh.

LOCATION:
FSCD Headquarters: Kazi Alauddin Road, Dhaka-1000, Bangladesh

FUNCTIONS:
- Fire safety plan approval
- Issuance of No Objection Certificates (NOC)
- Fire safety inspections
- Fire fighting and rescue operations
- Public awareness and training
- Investigation of fire incidents
- Enforcement of fire safety regulations

CHAPTER 2: FIRE NOC (NO OBJECTION CERTIFICATE) PROCESS
======================================================================

2.1 NOC REQUIREMENT

Before completing construction work, building owners must obtain
Fire Service approval. The Fire Service will check the building and
if it complies with all fire safety design requirements and has
adequate firefighting equipment, then only will the Fire Service
issue a No Objection Certificate (NOC).

2.2 TYPES OF NOC

CONSTRUCTION NOC:
- Required during construction phase
- Based on approved fire safety plan
- Lasts until project completion
- Covers construction safety measures
- Required for building permit issuance

OCCUPANCY NOC:
- Required before building occupancy
- Based on final inspection
- Verifies installed systems operational
- Renewable certificate
- Need renewal every year

2.3 NOC APPLICATION PROCESS

STEP 1: FIRE SAFETY PLAN PREPARATION
Prepare comprehensive fire safety plan including:
- Architectural drawings with fire safety features
- Fire detection and alarm system layout
- Fire suppression system design
- Emergency evacuation plan
- Fire fighting equipment locations
- Access routes for fire service vehicles

STEP 2: DOCUMENT SUBMISSION
Submit to FSCD:
1. NOC application form
2. Fire safety plan (multiple copies)
3. Building plan approval from RAJUK/authority
4. Structural safety certificate
5. Electrical system design
6. HVAC system design
7. Payment receipt of application fee

STEP 3: FSCD REVIEW
Technical review by FSCD officers:
- Compliance with BNBC 2020
- Compliance with NFPA standards
- Adequacy of fire safety systems
- Accessibility for fire service
- Evacuation provisions

STEP 4: SITE INSPECTION
FSCD conducts site inspection:
- Verification of construction
- System installation check
- Testing of equipment
- Access route verification

STEP 5: NOC ISSUANCE
If compliant:
- NOC certificate issued
- Validity period stated
- Conditions of approval listed
- Renewal requirements specified

2.4 COMPLIANCE STANDARDS

The FSCD issues NOCs based on compliance with:
- NFPA (National Fire Protection Association) standards
- BNBC (Bangladesh National Building Code) 2020
- Fire Prevention and Extinguishing Act 2003
- FSCD specific requirements

3.1 OCCUPANCY CLASSIFICATION

RESIDENTIAL:

COMMERCIAL:

- Markets

ASSEMBLY:
- Theaters and cinemas
- Auditoriums

EDUCATIONAL:
- Schools
- Colleges
- Universities
- Training centers

INSTITUTIONAL:

INDUSTRIAL:
- Factories
- Warehouses
- Processing plants
- Workshops

STORAGE:
- Warehouses
- Storage facilities
- Parking garages

HAZARDOUS:
- Chemical storage
- Flammable liquids
- Explosive materials

3.2 CONSTRUCTION TYPES

TYPE I: FIRE-RESISTIVE CONSTRUCTION
Most fire-resistant construction

Structural Frame: 3-4 hours fire resistance
Exterior Walls: 3-4 hours
Interior Walls: 2-3 hours
Floor Construction: 2-3 hours
Roof Construction: 1.5-2 hours

Materials:
- Reinforced concrete
- Protected steel
- Masonry

Applications:
- High-rise buildings
- Large commercial buildings
- Hospitals

TYPE II: NON-COMBUSTIBLE CONSTRUCTION
Fire-resistant but less than Type I

Structural Frame: 1-2 hours
Exterior Walls: 1-2 hours
Interior Walls: 1 hour
Floor Construction: 1-2 hours
Roof Construction: 1 hour

Materials:
- Unprotected steel
- Concrete
- Masonry

TYPE III: ORDINARY CONSTRUCTION
Exterior walls non-combustible, interior may be combustible

Exterior Walls: 2 hours
Interior Elements: 1 hour

TYPE IV: HEAVY TIMBER CONSTRUCTION
Large dimensional timber members

Specific minimum member sizes required
Enhanced fire resistance through mass

TYPE V: WOOD FRAME CONSTRUCTION
Least fire-resistant

Limited applications
Height restrictions
Strict compartmentation required

3.3 FIRE RESISTANCE RATINGS

Fire resistance rating indicates the time (in hours) a building
element can withstand fire exposure while maintaining its structural
integrity and preventing fire spread.

TESTING STANDARDS:
- ASTM E119
- BS 476
- ISO 834

REQUIRED RATINGS BY BUILDING HEIGHT:

Low-rise (up to 4 stories):
- Structural elements: 1-2 hours
- Walls: 1 hour minimum

Medium-rise (5-12 stories):
- Structural elements: 2 hours
- Walls: 2 hours
- Protected stairways: 2 hours

High-rise (above 12 stories):
- Structural elements: 3 hours
- Walls: 3 hours
- Protected stairways: 3 hours
- Elevator shafts: 2 hours

CHAPTER 4: FIRE-RESISTANT CONSTRUCTION REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

4.1 FIRE-RESISTANT MATERIALS

CONCRETE:
- Inherently fire-resistant
- Minimum cover to reinforcement: 25-50mm
- Fire resistance depends on thickness
- Spalling prevention measures

MASONRY:
- Brick walls provide good fire resistance
- Thickness determines rating
- Mortar quality important
- Plastering enhances resistance

STEEL:
- Requires fire protection
- Intumescent coatings
- Concrete encasement
- Spray-applied fire protection
- Board systems

FIRE-RESISTANT DOORS:
- Tested and rated (30, 60, 90, 120 minutes)
- Self-closing mechanisms
- Proper installation crucial
- Latching hardware
- No modifications allowed

FIRE-RESISTANT GLAZING:
- Wire-reinforced glass
- Fire-rated glass systems
- Ceramic glass
- Intumescent interlayers
- Tested assemblies required

4.2 COMPARTMENTATION

FIRE COMPARTMENTS:
Purpose: Contain fire to area of origin

Design principles:
- Enclosed by fire-resistant construction
- All penetrations sealed
- Fire doors at openings
- Maximum compartment size limits

FIRE WALLS:
- Extend from foundation to roof
- 3-4 hour fire resistance
- Parapets above roof
- Independent structural stability

FIRE BARRIERS:
- Separate occupancies
- Separate hazardous areas
- Shaft enclosures
- Corridor walls

4.3 PENETRATIONS AND OPENINGS

PENETRATION PROTECTION:
All penetrations through fire-rated assemblies must be sealed:
- Pipe penetrations: Fire-rated seals
- Cable penetrations: Fire-stopping systems
- Duct penetrations: Fire dampers
- Service penetrations: Tested firestop systems

FIRE DAMPERS:
Required in ducts penetrating:
- Fire walls
- Fire barriers
- Shaft enclosures

Types:
- Curtain type
- Multi-blade
- Ceiling radiation type

Activation:
- Fusible link (automatic)
- Smoke detector (automatic)

4.4 INTERIOR FINISHES

FLAME SPREAD CLASSIFICATION:

Class A (0-25): Minimal spread
- Required in exits
- Vertical shafts
- High-hazard areas

Class B (26-75): Moderate spread
- General corridor use
- Most occupancies acceptable

Class C (76-200): Rapid spread
- Limited applications
- Not in exits or corridors

SMOKE DEVELOPMENT:
Maximum 450 smoke developed index

TESTING:
ASTM E84 (Steiner Tunnel Test)

CHAPTER 5: FIRE DETECTION AND ALARM SYSTEMS
======================================================================

5.1 FIRE ALARM SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS

BUILDINGS REQUIRING FIRE ALARM SYSTEMS:
- All buildings over 10 stories
- All high-rise buildings
- Assembly occupancies (over 300 persons)
- Educational buildings
- Hotels and dormitories
- Hospitals and nursing homes
- Shopping malls
- Industrial buildings (based on hazard)

5.2 SYSTEM COMPONENTS

MANUAL FIRE ALARM STATIONS:
Pull stations at:
- All exit doors
- Each floor
- Within 1.5m of exit
- Maximum 45m travel distance
- Height: 1.2-1.5m above floor

SMOKE DETECTORS:
Required locations:
- Sleeping areas (residential)
- Common corridors
- Stairways and elevator lobbies
- Mechanical rooms
- Storage rooms
- Return air ducts (HVAC)
- Other specified locations

Types:
- Ionization type
- Photoelectric type
- Combination type

Spacing:
- 9m radius coverage (smooth ceiling)
- Adjusted for ceiling height

HEAT DETECTORS:
Used in areas unsuitable for smoke detection:
- Kitchens
- Boiler rooms
- Dusty areas
- High air movement areas

Types:
- Fixed temperature
- Rate-of-rise
- Combination

FLAME DETECTORS:
Special applications:
- Hazardous areas
- High ceiling spaces
- Rapid fire development risk

5.3 NOTIFICATION DEVICES

AUDIBLE ALARMS:
- Bells, horns, or electronic sounders
- Minimum 15 dBA above ambient
- At least 75 dBA at 3m
- Distinctive temporal pattern

VISUAL ALARMS:
- Strobe lights required
- For hearing-impaired occupants
- Specified candela rating
- Synchronized flashing

VOICE ALARM SYSTEMS:
Required in:
- High-rise buildings
- Large assembly occupancies
- Complex buildings

Features:
- Pre-recorded messages
- Live announcements capability
- Zone paging
- Emergency voice/alarm communications

5.4 CONTROL PANEL

FIRE ALARM CONTROL PANEL (FACP):
Functions:
- Monitor detection devices
- Activate notification devices
- Display system status
- Interface with other systems
- Remote monitoring capability

Location:
- Readily accessible
- Near main entrance (preferred)
- Fire command center (high-rise)
- Protected from unauthorized access

5.5 SYSTEM TYPES

CONVENTIONAL SYSTEMS:
- Zone-based addressing
- Suitable for smaller buildings
- Less expensive

ADDRESSABLE SYSTEMS:
- Individual device addressing
- Precise location identification
- Advanced diagnostic capabilities
- Preferred for larger buildings

NETWORKED SYSTEMS:
- Multiple buildings
- Central monitoring
- Integrated emergency management

5.6 POWER SUPPLY

PRIMARY POWER:
- Normal electrical supply
- Dedicated circuit
- Supervised

BACKUP POWER:
- Battery or generator backup
- 24-hour standby capacity
- 15-minute alarm operation capacity
- Automatic switchover

5.7 TESTING AND MAINTENANCE

TESTING FREQUENCY:

Weekly:
- Visual inspection of panel
- Trouble signal check

Monthly:
- Battery voltage check
- Supervisory signals

Quarterly:
- Alarm device testing (rotating basis)

Annually:
- Complete system test
- All devices tested
- Sensitivity testing of detectors
- Battery load test

MAINTENANCE:
- Professional service contract
- Records maintained
- Deficiencies corrected promptly
- Certification updated

CHAPTER 6: FIRE SUPPRESSION SYSTEMS
======================================================================

6.1 AUTOMATIC SPRINKLER SYSTEMS

BUILDINGS REQUIRING SPRINKLERS:
- High-rise buildings (above certain height)
- Large assembly occupancies
- Storage facilities
- Hazardous material storage
- Basements (depending on area)
- Specific occupancy requirements

6.2 SPRINKLER SYSTEM DESIGN

DESIGN STANDARDS:
- Conformance to NFPA 13
- Hydraulic calculations
- Water supply requirements
- System components specifications

SPRINKLER HEAD TYPES:

Pendant:
- Most common type
- Ceiling-mounted
- Downward discharge

Upright:
- Exposed piping
- Upward deflector

Sidewall:
- Wall-mounted
- One-sided distribution
- Residential applications

Concealed:
- Recessed with cover plate
- Aesthetic applications

TEMPERATURE RATINGS:
Ordinary: 57-77°C (most common)
Intermediate: 100-121°C
High: 163-191°C (near heat sources)

COLOR CODING:
Uncolored: 57-77°C
White: 79-107°C
Blue: 121-149°C
Red: 163-191°C

6.3 SPRINKLER SYSTEM COMPONENTS

WATER SUPPLY:
Sources:
- Municipal water supply
- Water tanks (elevated or ground level)
- Fire pumps
- Combined systems

Capacity:
- Minimum 30-90 minutes operation
- Depends on hazard classification
- Hydraulic calculations

CONTROL VALVES:
- Main control valve
- Floor control valves
- Sectional valves
- Indicating type (position visible)
- Supervised (monitored)

ALARM VALVES:
Types:
- Wet pipe valve
- Dry pipe valve
- Deluge valve
- Pre-action valve

Functions:
- Control water flow
- Activate alarms
- Interface with fire alarm system

FIRE PUMPS:
When required:
- Insufficient water pressure
- Tall buildings
- Large areas

Requirements:
- Dedicated pump room
- Backup power
- Weekly testing
- Annual professional test

6.4 SPRINKLER SYSTEM TYPES

WET PIPE SYSTEM:
Most common and reliable
- Piping filled with water
- Immediate discharge when activated
- Suitable for heated spaces
- Fast response

DRY PIPE SYSTEM:
For unheated spaces
- Piping filled with compressed air/nitrogen
- Water released when air pressure drops
- Slower response than wet pipe
- Prevents freezing

PRE-ACTION SYSTEM:
Additional control
- Requires two events for discharge
- Detection system and sprinkler activation
- Protects water-sensitive areas
- Data centers, museums, libraries

DELUGE SYSTEM:
All sprinklers open
- Open sprinkler heads
- Detection system activated valve
- Rapid large-area discharge
- High-hazard areas

6.5 STANDPIPE SYSTEMS

BUILDING HEIGHT REQUIREMENTS:
Required in buildings over:
- 15m height, or
- 4 stories

SYSTEM CLASSES:

Class I:
- 65mm hose connections
- For fire department use
- At each floor landing

Class II:
- 40mm hose stations
- For occupant use
- Equipped with hose
- Maximum 30m hose reach

Class III:
- Combined Class I and II
- Both 65mm and 40mm outlets

SYSTEM TYPES:

Wet:
- Piping filled with water
- Ready for immediate use

Dry:
- Manual or automatic fill
- Fire department connection

6.6 FIRE EXTINGUISHERS

PORTABLE EXTINGUISHERS REQUIRED:
Throughout building based on:
- Fire hazard classification
- Extinguisher type and rating
- Travel distance limitations
- Occupancy type

TYPES AND APPLICATIONS:

Class A (Ordinary Combustibles):
- Wood, paper, textiles
- Water, foam, dry chemical
- Color: Green label

Class B (Flammable Liquids):
- Petroleum, solvents, paints
- Foam, CO2, dry chemical
- Color: Red label

Class C (Electrical):
- Electrical equipment fires
- CO2, dry chemical
- Color: Blue label

Class D (Metals):
- Magnesium, titanium
- Special dry powder
- Color: Yellow label

Class K (Kitchen):
- Cooking oils and fats
- Wet chemical
- Color: Black label

PLACEMENT:

Class A:
- Maximum travel distance: 23m
- Minimum rating: 2A

Class B:
- Maximum travel distance: 15m
- Higher ratings reduce distance

MOUNTING HEIGHT:
- Top of extinguisher: Maximum 1.5m
- Larger units: May be floor-mounted
- Clearly visible and accessible
- Signage indicating location

INSPECTION AND MAINTENANCE:

Monthly:
- Visual inspection by occupant
- Check pressure gauge
- Verify access
- Check for damage

Annually:
- Professional inspection
- Internal examination
- Recharging if needed
- Testing as per schedule

Tag and documentation required

CHAPTER 7: MEANS OF EGRESS
======================================================================

7.1 GENERAL EGRESS REQUIREMENTS

MINIMUM TWO EXITS:
Most occupancies require:
- At least two independent exits
- Remote from each other
- Cannot share common path initially

TRAVEL DISTANCE:
Maximum distance to nearest exit:
- Unsprinklered buildings: 30-45m
- Sprinklered buildings: 45-60m
- Varies by occupancy type

DEAD-END CORRIDORS:
Maximum length:
- 6m (unsprinklered)
- 15m (sprinklered)

7.2 EXIT COMPONENTS

EXIT ACCESS:
Path from any point to exit
- Aisles
- Corridors
- Rooms
- Doorways

EXIT:
Protected path to outside
- Stairways (enclosed)
- Exit doors
- Horizontal exits
- Fire-rated construction

EXIT DISCHARGE:
Path from exit to public way
- Ground level at building exterior
- Must be protected if through building
- Minimum width maintained

7.3 CORRIDOR REQUIREMENTS

CORRIDOR WIDTH:
Minimum widths:
- 1.1m (small occupant loads)
- 1.4m (institutional)
- 1.8m (high occupant loads)
- Obstruction-free

CORRIDOR CONSTRUCTION:
Fire resistance:
- 1 hour minimum (most occupancies)
- 2 hours (hazardous areas)

Doors opening to corridor:
- Self-closing
- 20-minute fire rating minimum
- No locks obstructing egress

7.4 STAIRWAY DESIGN

STAIRWAY DIMENSIONS:

Width:
- Minimum: 900mm
- Preferably: 1100mm
- Wider for high occupant loads
- Uniform width throughout

Riser Height:
- Maximum: 190mm
- Uniform throughout flight

Tread Depth:
- Minimum: 250mm
- Uniform throughout flight

Handrails:
- Both sides required
- Height: 900-1000mm
- Continuous throughout flight
- Graspable shape
- Extensions at top and bottom

Landings:
- At top and bottom of flights
- Minimum width = stair width
- Minimum depth = 1.0m
- Doors must not reduce effective size

STAIRWAY CONSTRUCTION:
Fire resistance:
- 1 hour: Low-rise buildings
- 2 hours: Medium-rise buildings
- 3 hours: High-rise buildings

Enclosure:
- Fire-rated walls
- Fire-rated doors (self-closing)
- No storage permitted
- No other occupancy

LIGHTING:
- Adequate illumination
- Emergency lighting required
- Minimum 50 lux

SIGNAGE:
- Exit signs at all levels
- Floor level identification
- Direction indicators
- Roof access indication

7.5 DOORS

DOOR WIDTH:
Minimum clear width:
- 800mm (most occupancies)
- 900mm (high traffic)
- 1.4m (institutional)

DOOR SWING:
- In direction of egress (if serving >50 persons)
- Full 90° opening
- No obstructions in swing path

DOOR HARDWARE:

Panic Hardware:
Required for:
- Assembly occupancies
- Educational facilities
- High hazard occupancies
- Where serving >100 persons

Features:
- Single action opening
- Maximum 67N force
- Operable from inside
- No key required for egress

DOOR CLOSERS:
- Automatic closing
- Positive latching
- Adjustable speed

LOCKING:
- No locks preventing egress from inside
- Emergency exit devices permitted
- Access control must release on alarm
- Delayed egress permitted (limited cases)

7.6 EMERGENCY LIGHTING

REQUIRED LOCATIONS:
- Exit access corridors
- Stairways
- Exit doors
- Assembly occupancies
- Large spaces

PERFORMANCE:
- Automatic activation on power failure
- Initial illumination: 10 lux minimum
- Average illumination: 5 lux
- Duration: 90 minutes minimum
- Battery-powered units or generator

TESTING:
- Monthly: 30-second test
- Annually: 90-minute test
- Documentation required

7.7 EXIT SIGNS

REQUIRED LOCATIONS:
- Every exit door
- Exit access doors (where needed)
- Change of direction points
- Corridors and paths to exits

DESIGN:
- "EXIT" lettering
- Pictograms (international symbol)
- Minimum letter height: 150mm
- High contrast
- Illuminated or self-luminous

ILLUMINATION:
- Externally lit
- Internally lit (LED)
- Photoluminescent (approved types)
- Connected to emergency power

VISIBILITY:
- Clearly visible
- Not obstructed
- 30m maximum spacing

7.8 OCCUPANT LOAD CALCULATION

OCCUPANT LOAD:
Calculated based on floor area and occupancy type

COMMON OCCUPANT LOAD FACTORS:

Assembly Areas:
- Fixed seating: Per seat
- Standing: 0.5 m²/person
- Unconcentrated: 1.4 m²/person

Educational:
- Classrooms: 1.9 m²/person
- Labs: 4.6 m²/person

Offices:
- 9.3 m²/person

Retail:
- 5.6 m²/person (sales floor)
- 2.8 m²/person (basement/ground)

Residential:
- 18.6 m²/person

EXIT CAPACITY:
Based on occupant load
- Stairs: 0.76cm width per person
- Level exits: 0.51cm width per person

CHAPTER 8: HIGH-RISE BUILDING REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

8.1 DEFINITION

High-rise building:
- Floor used for human occupancy more than 23m above
  fire department access level, OR
- Building height exceeding 33m, OR
- Building with more than 10 stories

8.2 ADDITIONAL REQUIREMENTS

FIRE COMMAND CENTER:
Centralized fire emergency operations
Location:
- Near main entrance
- Direct access from outside
- Protected construction

Contents:
- Fire alarm control panel
- Sprinkler control valve status
- Fire pump status
- HVAC controls
- Elevator controls
- Communication systems
- Building plans
- Emergency procedures

PRESSURIZED STAIRWELLS:
Purpose: Prevent smoke infiltration
Requirements:
- Positive air pressure maintained
- 25-50 Pa pressure differential
- Powered by emergency generator
- Automatic activation

FIRE SERVICE ACCESS ELEVATORS:
One elevator minimum:
- Serves all floors
- Fire service recall
- Protected shaft (2-hour rating)
- Emergency power
- Communication system
- Capacity for fire fighters and equipment

STANDPIPE SYSTEM:
- Class I minimum
- Each floor
- Roof access
- Fire department connections

AUTOMATIC SPRINKLER SYSTEM:
- Throughout building
- All floor levels
- Mechanical spaces
- Storage areas

EMERGENCY POWER:
Required for:
- Fire pumps
- Smoke control
- Emergency lighting
- Fire alarm system
- Elevator (at least one)
- Communication systems
- Stairwell pressurization

Duration:
- 4 hours minimum
- Automatic startup
- Regular testing

VOICE COMMUNICATION SYSTEM:
Two-way communication:
- Fire command center to all floors
- Stairways
- Elevator cars
- Mechanical rooms
- Roof

Public address:
- Zoned paging
- Emergency messages
- Floor-by-floor evacuation

SMOKE CONTROL:
Smoke management system:
- Mechanical smoke removal
- Smoke barriers
- Smoke compartments
- Pressurization
- HVAC controls

8.3 FIRE SAFETY MANAGEMENT

FIRE SAFETY DIRECTOR:
Designated personnel responsible for:
- Emergency planning
- Staff training
- Evacuation procedures
- Fire drill coordination
- Equipment maintenance
- Record keeping

FIRE WARDENS:
Each floor:
- Trained personnel
- Evacuation responsibilities
- Communication duties

EVACUATION PLAN:
- Floor-by-floor strategy
- Phased evacuation
- Refuge areas
- Special needs provisions

FIRE DRILLS:
Frequency:
- Quarterly minimum
- More frequent in assembly/institutional
- Documentation required

CHAPTER 9: SPECIAL OCCUPANCY REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

9.1 ASSEMBLY OCCUPANCIES

THEATERS, AUDITORIUMS, CINEMAS:

Exits:
- Minimum 2 exits (occupancy <300)
- 3 exits (occupancy 300-600)
- 4 exits (occupancy >600)
- Remote locations

Seating:
- Maximum seats per row: 14 (both side aisles)
- Maximum seats per row: 7 (one side aisle)
- Minimum aisle width: 1.1m
- Clear width maintained

Exit Signage:
- Illuminated exit signs
- Aisle lighting
- Emergency lighting

Panic Hardware:
- All exit doors
- Single action release

Occupant Warning:
- Voice alarm system
- Visual notification

9.2 EDUCATIONAL FACILITIES

SCHOOLS, COLLEGES, UNIVERSITIES:

Corridors:
- 2.4m minimum width (main corridors)
- 1.8m (secondary corridors)

Classrooms:
- Two exit access doors (occupancy >50)
- Remote from each other
- Panic hardware if required

Laboratories:
- Special hazard considerations
- Fire suppression
- Emergency shut-offs
- Eye wash stations

Assembly Spaces:
- Follow assembly occupancy rules

Evacuation:
- Evacuation plans posted
- Regular drills
- Staff training

9.3 HOSPITALS AND HEALTHCARE

HORIZONTAL EVACUATION:
- Defend in place initially
- Horizontal evacuation to adjacent compartment
- Vertical evacuation last resort

SMOKE COMPARTMENTS:
- Maximum 2000 m² per compartment
- Fire and smoke barriers
- Horizontal exits

CORRIDORS:
- Minimum 2.4m width
- 1-hour fire rating
- Smoke barriers

ROOMS:
- Sleeping rooms: Maximum 8 patients
- Direct access to corridor
- Vision panels in doors

FIRE ALARM:
- Zoned system
- Notification at staff areas (not patient areas)
- Visual signals only in patient areas

SPRINKLER:
- Throughout facility required
- Quick-response heads in patient rooms

9.4 HOTELS AND DORMITORIES

GUEST ROOMS:
- Smoke detectors in each room
- Sprinklers required
- Two means of egress (certain conditions)
- Emergency information posted

CORRIDORS:
- 1-hour fire rating
- Smoke compartments
- Maximum dead-end: 10m

FIRE ALARM:
- Throughout building
- Audible and visual notification

9.5 SHOPPING MALLS

COVERED MALLS:
Special regulations:
- Tenant separation
- Means of egress through mall
- Smoke control
- Sprinkler systems
- Exit access

ANCHOR STORES:
- Separate means of egress
- Fire separation from mall

9.6 INDUSTRIAL FACILITIES

CLASSIFICATION BY HAZARD:
- Low hazard
- Moderate hazard
- High hazard

REQUIREMENTS VARY BY:
- Processes conducted
- Materials stored
- Building size
- Worker density

SPECIAL SYSTEMS:
- Gas detection
- Emergency shut-offs
- Explosion venting
- Special extinguishing systems

9.7 STORAGE FACILITIES

WAREHOUSE STORAGE:
Requirements based on:
- Commodity classification
- Storage height
- Storage arrangement (rack vs. pile)
- Sprinkler demand

HIGH-PILED STORAGE:
- Storage exceeding 3.7m height
- Permit required
- Enhanced sprinkler design
- Fire department access

CHAPTER 10: CONSTRUCTION SITE FIRE SAFETY

10.1 CONSTRUCTION PHASE REQUIREMENTS

TEMPORARY FACILITIES:
- Portable fire extinguishers
- Emergency evacuation plan
- Access for fire service
- Water supply for fire fighting

- No smoking policy enforcement
- Hot work permits
- Flammable material control
- Electrical safety
- Housekeeping

TEMPORARY EXITS:
- Maintained throughout construction
- Illumination
- Signage

10.2 HOT WORK SAFETY

WELDING, CUTTING, GRINDING:

Permit System:
- Hot work permit required
- Valid for shift or task
- Site-specific

Fire Watch:
- During hot work
- 30 minutes after completion
- Trained personnel
- Fire extinguisher present

Precautions:
- Remove combustibles (10m radius)
- Protect combustibles that cannot be removed
- Close openings
- Fire extinguisher ready
- Monitor for sparks
