CHAPTER 1: WATER SUPPLY AND SEWERAGE AUTHORITIES
======================================================================

1.1 OVERVIEW OF WASA

Water Supply and Sewerage Authority (WASA) is the main body
administering Water supply, Drainage and Sanitation system in
Bangladesh.

ESTABLISHMENT:
- Established in 1963
- Under East Pakistan Ordinance XIX
- Currently operates according to Dhaka WASA Act 1996

1.2 WASA ORGANIZATIONS

DHAKA WATER SUPPLY AND SEWERAGE AUTHORITY (DWASA):
Service Area: Dhaka Metropolitan Area
Establishment: 1963
Population Served: Over 20 million (Greater Dhaka)

Services Provided:
- Potable water supply (domestic, industrial, commercial)
- Sewerage collection and treatment
- Storm water drainage
- Wastewater treatment

CHITTAGONG WATER SUPPLY AND SEWERAGE AUTHORITY (CWASA):
Service Area: Chittagong City
Similar services as DWASA

KHULNA WATER SUPPLY AND SEWERAGE AUTHORITY (KWASA):
Service Area: Khulna City
Website: www.kwasa.org.bd
Similar services as DWASA

1.3 WASA RESPONSIBILITIES

PRIMARY FUNCTIONS:
a) Water supply network development and maintenance
b) Water treatment plants operation
c) Sewerage network construction and maintenance
d) Sewage treatment plants operation
e) Storm water drainage management
f) Quality control of water supply
g) Customer service and billing
h) Infrastructure expansion and rehabilitation

1.4 DISTRICT METERED AREAS (DMA)

WASA has implemented District Metered Area (DMA) system:

DMA STRUCTURE:
- Division of service area into manageable zones
- Each DMA has dedicated staff
- DMA Managers, Deputy Managers, Licensed plumbers deployed
- Responsibilities:
  * Fresh connection installations
  * Repairs and maintenance
  * Illegal connection prevention
  * System sustainability

BENEFITS:
- Better leak detection
- Reduced non-revenue water
- Improved service quality
- Accountability

CHAPTER 2: BNBC PLUMBING AND SANITARY DRAINAGE STANDARDS
======================================================================

2.1 BNBC PART 8 CHAPTER 6: SANITARY DRAINAGE

PURPOSE:
The plumbing includes the practice, materials, and fixtures used in
the installation, maintenance, extension, and alteration of all
piping, fixtures, appliances, and appurtenances in connection with:
- Sanitary drainage or storm drainage facilities
- Venting system
- Public or private water supply systems

2.2 SCOPE OF PLUMBING WORK

COVERED SYSTEMS:
- Water supply piping and fixtures
- Sanitary drainage piping
- Storm drainage piping
- Vent piping
- Hot water systems
- Fixtures and appliances
- Backflow prevention
- Water treatment equipment

2.3 DEFINITIONS

KEY TERMS:

SANITARY DRAINAGE:
Drainage system carrying wastewater from plumbing fixtures to
public sewer or on-site treatment

STORM DRAINAGE:
Drainage system carrying rainwater from roofs and surfaces

SOIL PIPE:
Pipe carrying discharge from water closets, with or without
discharge from other fixtures

WASTE PIPE:
Pipe carrying discharge from fixtures other than water closets

VENT PIPE:
Pipe providing air circulation to drainage system to prevent
trap seal loss

TRAP:
Fitting providing liquid seal to prevent passage of sewer gases

STACK:
Vertical drainage pipe

2.4 GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

DESIGN PRINCIPLES:
- Adequate capacity for expected loads
- Self-cleansing velocities
- Proper slopes and grades
- Accessibility for maintenance
- Prevention of cross-contamination
- Durability and quality materials

ENVIRONMENTAL COMPLIANCE:
Wastes shall be properly treated as specified by environmental
quality standards of Bangladesh before their disposal into public
sewers or into natural bodies of water.

CHAPTER 3: WATER SUPPLY SYSTEMS
======================================================================

3.1 WATER SUPPLY SOURCES

PUBLIC WATER SUPPLY (WASA):
- Municipal water distribution
- Most reliable source
- Quality controlled
- Metered connections

PRIVATE SOURCES:
- Deep tube wells
- Shallow wells (limited use)
- Surface water (with treatment)
- Rainwater harvesting (supplementary)

3.2 WATER DEMAND ESTIMATION

RESIDENTIAL BUILDINGS:

Per Capita Consumption:
- Apartments: 150-200 liters/person/day
- Single-family houses: 200-250 liters/person/day

Number of Occupants:
- Calculated based on unit size and bedrooms
- Typically 4-6 persons per apartment

Peak Demand Factor:
- Peak hourly flow: 1.5-2.0 times average
- Storage tank sizing: 24-hour consumption minimum

COMMERCIAL BUILDINGS:

Offices:
- 50-75 liters/person/day
- Peak factor: 2.0-2.5

Hotels:
- 200-300 liters/guest/day
- Includes laundry and restaurants

Restaurants:
- 50-80 liters/customer/day
- Kitchen demands separate

INSTITUTIONAL:

Schools:
- Day schools: 40-50 liters/student/day
- Boarding schools: 150-200 liters/student/day

Hospitals:
- 300-450 liters/bed/day
- Including laundry, kitchen, treatment areas

3.3 WATER SUPPLY SYSTEMS

DIRECT SUPPLY SYSTEM:
- Municipal supply directly to fixtures
- Requires adequate pressure
- No storage tanks
- Limited application in Bangladesh

INDIRECT SUPPLY SYSTEM (MOST COMMON):
- Municipal supply to underground reservoir
- Pumped to overhead tank
- Gravity feed to fixtures
- Ensures continuous supply

COMPONENTS:

Underground Water Tank (UWT):
- Receive municipal supply
- Storage capacity: 1-2 days consumption
- Reinforced concrete construction
- Access manhole and ladder
- Overflow and drain provisions
- Covered and ventilated

Pumps:
- Submersible or surface pumps
- Capacity: Refill overhead tank in 2-3 hours
- Duty and standby pumps
- Automatic controls

Overhead Water Tank (OWT):
- Reinforced concrete or steel
- Capacity: 1 day consumption minimum
- Height for adequate pressure
- Access, overflow, drain
- Level indicators

Distribution System:
- Piping to all fixtures
- Proper sizing
- Isolation valves
- Pressure regulation if needed

3.4 PIPE MATERIALS

WATER SUPPLY PIPING:

GI (Galvanized Iron) Pipes:
- Traditional choice
- Threaded joints
- Corrosion resistant
- Sizes: 15mm to 150mm

UPVC (Unplasticized Polyvinyl Chloride):
- Increasingly popular
- Light weight
- Corrosion resistant
- Solvent weld or rubber ring joints
- Class: Pressure rating specified

HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene):
- Underground installations
- Flexible
- Fusion welded joints
- Long lengths available

PPR (Polypropylene Random):
- Hot and cold water
- Heat fusion joints
- Durable and hygienic

CPVC (Chlorinated PVC):
- Hot water applications
- Higher temperature resistance

COPPER:
- Expensive but durable
- Excellent for quality installations
- Soldered joints

3.5 PIPE SIZING

METHOD:
- Calculate fixture units
- Determine demand flow rate
- Select pipe size for acceptable velocity and pressure loss

FIXTURE UNITS (WSFU):
Common fixtures:
- Water closet (flush tank): 3 WSFU
- Lavatory: 1 WSFU
- Bath tub: 2 WSFU
- Shower: 2 WSFU
- Kitchen sink: 2 WSFU
- Washing machine: 3 WSFU

VELOCITY LIMITS:
- Minimum: 0.6 m/s (prevent sedimentation)
- Maximum: 2.0-2.5 m/s (prevent erosion and noise)

PRESSURE REQUIREMENTS:
- Minimum at highest fixture: 100 kPa (1 bar)
- Typical supply pressure: 200-400 kPa

3.6 FIXTURES AND FITTINGS

WATER CLOSETS:
Types:
- Flush tank type (9 liters per flush)
- Dual flush (3/6 liters) - water saving
- Flush valve type (commercial)

Installation:
- Proper venting
- Water supply connection
- Drainage connection
- Secure mounting

LAVATORIES (WASH BASINS):
Types:
- Pedestal
- Wall-hung
- Counter-mounted

Requirements:
- Hot and cold water
- Waste connection with trap
- Overflow provision

KITCHEN SINKS:
- Single or double bowl
- Stainless steel common
- Hot and cold water
- Waste connection
- Grease trap (commercial kitchens)

FLOOR DRAINS:
- Toilets, bathrooms
- Balconies, terraces
- Mechanical rooms
- Proper trap seal maintenance

3.7 WATER CONSERVATION

LOW-FLOW FIXTURES:
- Low-flow shower heads (6-9 liters/minute)
- Dual flush toilets
- Sensor faucets
- Aerators on faucets

RAINWATER HARVESTING:
- Collection from roofs
- Storage tanks
- Filtration
- Use for toilets, gardens, car washing

GREYWATER REUSE:
- Reuse of lightly contaminated water
- From sinks, showers, washing machines
- Treatment required
- Use for toilet flushing, irrigation

CHAPTER 4: SANITARY DRAINAGE SYSTEM
======================================================================

4.1 DRAINAGE SYSTEM COMPONENTS

BUILDING DRAIN:
- Lowest horizontal drainage pipe
- Receives discharge from all stacks
- Connects to building sewer

BUILDING SEWER:
- From building drain to public sewer or treatment
- Underground outside building

SOIL STACK:
- Vertical pipe carrying water closet discharge
- May receive other fixture discharge

WASTE STACK:
- Vertical pipe carrying waste (not from WCs)

BRANCH DRAIN:
- Horizontal pipe collecting fixture discharge
- Connects to stacks

4.2 DRAINAGE PIPE MATERIALS

CAST IRON (CI):
- Traditional choice
- Durable and quiet
- Heavy
- Socket and spigot or mechanical joints

UPVC:
- Most common now
- Light weight
- Corrosion resistant
- Solvent weld joints
- Available in various schedules

VITRIFIED CLAY:
- Underground drains
- Resistant to chemicals
- Socket and spigot joints

CONCRETE PIPES:
- Large diameter drains
- Underground sewers
- Reinforced or unreinforced

4.3 PIPE SIZING

METHOD:
- Fixture unit method
- Calculate drainage fixture units (DFU)
- Select pipe size from tables

DRAINAGE FIXTURE UNITS:

Common fixtures:
- Water closet (private): 4 DFU
- Water closet (public): 6 DFU
- Lavatory: 1 DFU
- Bath tub: 2 DFU
- Shower: 2 DFU
- Kitchen sink: 2 DFU
- Floor drain: 2 DFU

PIPE CAPACITY:
Based on:
- Pipe diameter
- Slope
- Fixture units

Example capacities (at 1% slope):
- 75mm: Up to 20 DFU
- 100mm: Up to 160 DFU
- 150mm: Up to 620 DFU

4.4 SLOPES AND GRADES

MINIMUM SLOPES:

Pipe Size    Minimum Slope
50mm         2%
75mm         1.5%
100mm        1%
150mm        0.7%
200mm and up 0.5%

OBJECTIVES:
- Self-cleansing velocity (minimum 0.6 m/s)
- Prevention of solids deposition
- Proper flow characteristics

4.5 TRAPS

PURPOSE:
Provide water seal to prevent sewer gas entry into building

TRAP SEAL:
- Minimum depth: 50mm
- Maximum depth: 100mm
- Maintained by proper venting

TRAP TYPES:

P-Trap:
- Most common
- Outlet horizontal
- Used for lavatories, sinks

S-Trap:
- Outlet vertical
- Legacy installations
- Now discouraged (prone to seal loss)

Bottle Trap:
- Compact design
- Easy to clean
- Aesthetic applications

Floor Drain Trap:
- Cast iron or PVC
- Grating on top
- Trap below

PROHIBITED:
- Double trapping
- Traps with moving parts
- S-traps on fixtures

4.6 VENTING SYSTEM

PURPOSE:
- Maintain trap seals
- Allow air circulation
- Prevent pressure fluctuations
- Enable proper drainage flow

VENT TYPES:

Stack Vent:
- Extension of soil/waste stack above highest fixture

Vent Stack:
- Separate vertical vent
- Relieves pressure in drainage system

Branch Vent:
- Horizontal vent connecting fixture to vent stack

Individual Vent:
- Separate vent for each fixture

Common Vent:
- Vent serving two fixtures

Loop Vent:
- Vent serving multiple fixtures on horizontal branch

VENT TERMINATION:
- Extend through roof
- Minimum 150mm above roof
- Protected from rain and debris
- Away from windows and air intakes

4.7 CLEANOUTS

PURPOSE:
- Access for cleaning and maintenance
- Removal of blockages

LOCATIONS:
- Base of each stack
- Change of direction (horizontal)
- Long horizontal runs (maximum 30m intervals)
- Building drain before sewer connection

DESIGN:
- Same size as pipe (up to 100mm)
- Minimum 100mm for larger pipes
- Accessible
- Proper cover/plug

CHAPTER 5: STORM WATER DRAINAGE
======================================================================

5.1 SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS

SEPARATE SYSTEM:
- Storm water separate from sanitary drainage
- Discharge to storm sewers or natural drainage
- No cross-connections

5.2 RAINFALL DESIGN

DESIGN RAINFALL INTENSITY:
- Based on location
- Return period: 10-25 years
- Duration: 15-30 minutes

Bangladesh typical intensity:
- 150-250 mm/hour (design storm)

5.3 ROOF DRAINAGE

ROOF OUTLETS:
- Sized for roof area
- Spacing based on drainage area
- Protected with strainers/gratings
- Minimum 75mm diameter

SIZING:
100mm outlet can drain approximately:
- 100-150 m² roof area (typical)
- Depends on rainfall intensity

CONDUCTOR PIPES (DOWNPIPES):
- Vertical pipes from roof outlets
- Minimum 75mm diameter
- 100mm diameter common

ROOF GUTTERS:
- Collect rainwater from sloped roofs
- Sized for roof area and rainfall
- Minimum 1% slope to outlets
- Materials: PVC, GI, aluminum

5.4 SURFACE DRAINAGE

BALCONY AND TERRACE DRAINS:
- Minimum one drain per area
- Trapped floor drains
- Connect to storm system

BASEMENT DRAINAGE:
- Floor drains
- Sump pumps if below sewer level
- Backflow prevention

5.5 STORM SEWER CONNECTION

CONNECTION TO PUBLIC STORM SEWER:
- WASA approval required
- Separate from sanitary sewer
- Inspection chamber at property line

DISCHARGE TO NATURAL DRAINAGE:
- If no storm sewer available
- Environmental regulations apply
- Proper outlet design

CHAPTER 6: SEWAGE TREATMENT
======================================================================

6.1 PUBLIC SEWERAGE SYSTEM

WASA SEWERAGE NETWORK:
- Collection sewers
- Trunk sewers
- Pumping stations
- Treatment plants

SERVICE AREAS:
- Limited coverage in Bangladesh
- Expanding gradually
- Priority areas served first

CONNECTION REQUIREMENTS:
- Application to WASA
- Payment of connection fees
- Inspection chamber at property
- Proper design and construction

6.2 ON-SITE SEWAGE TREATMENT

FOR AREAS WITHOUT PUBLIC SEWERS:

SEPTIC TANK SYSTEM:
Most common on-site treatment

Components:
- Septic tank (primary treatment)
- Soak pit or drain field (disposal)

Septic Tank Design:
- Capacity: 3-5 days retention
- Minimum 2 chambers
- Proper baffles
- Access covers
- Ventilation

Sizing:
- Based on number of users
- Typical: 1 m³ per 5-6 persons
- Minimum size: 2.5 m³

Construction:
- Reinforced concrete or brick masonry
- Watertight
- Underground installation

Soak Pit:
- Perforated walls
- Gravel filling
- Sized for soil percolation rate
- Minimum 1.5m from water table

Maintenance:
- Desludging every 2-3 years
- Professional service
- Proper disposal of sludge

PACKAGE TREATMENT PLANTS:
For larger developments:
- Extended aeration
- Moving bed biofilm reactor (MBBR)
- Sequencing batch reactor (SBR)
- Membrane bioreactor (MBR)

Features:
- Compact design
- Higher treatment efficiency
- Suitable for difficult soils
- Regular maintenance required

CONSTRUCTED WETLANDS:
- Natural treatment process
- Large area required
- Low operating cost
- Limited application

6.3 EFFLUENT QUALITY STANDARDS

DISCHARGE TO PUBLIC SEWER:

Typical Requirements:
- pH: 6.0-9.0
- BOD: Maximum 250 mg/L
- COD: Maximum 400 mg/L
- Suspended Solids: Maximum 500 mg/L
- Oil and Grease: Maximum 20 mg/L

DISCHARGE TO NATURAL WATER BODIES:

Stricter Standards:
- pH: 6.5-9.0
- BOD: Maximum 50 mg/L
- COD: Maximum 200 mg/L
- Suspended Solids: Maximum 150 mg/L
- Oil and Grease: Maximum 10 mg/L

(As per Environmental Conservation Rules 1997)

7.1 HOT WATER DEMAND

RESIDENTIAL:

COMMERCIAL:

CENTRAL HOT WATER SYSTEM:

- Solar collectors

CHAPTER 8: BACKFLOW PREVENTION
======================================================================

8.1 BACKFLOW RISKS

CAUSES:
- Back pressure
- Back siphonage
- Cross-connections

CONSEQUENCES:
- Contamination of potable water
- Public health risk

8.2 PREVENTION METHODS

AIR GAP:
- Physical separation
- Most effective
- Vertical distance specified

CHECK VALVES:
- One-way flow
- Spring-loaded
- Installation at critical points

BACKFLOW PREVENTERS:
- Double check valves
- Reduced pressure zone devices
- For high-risk situations

REQUIREMENTS:
- On water supply to buildings
- At fixtures with submerged inlets
- Irrigation systems
- Industrial connections

CHAPTER 9: INSTALLATION REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

9.1 LICENSED PLUMBERS

REQUIREMENTS:
- Licensed plumbers for installations
- Registration with local authority/WASA
- Qualified and experienced
- Compliance with standards

9.2 INSPECTION AND TESTING

PRESSURE TESTING:
- Water supply piping
- Test pressure: 1.5 times working pressure
- Duration: Minimum 2 hours
- No leaks permitted

DRAINAGE TESTING:
- Water test or air test
- Verify proper slopes
- Check for leaks
- Trap seal verification

INSPECTIONS:
- Rough-in inspection (before concealing)
- Final inspection (upon completion)
- WASA inspection for connection
- Authority approval

9.3 DOCUMENTATION

AS-BUILT DRAWINGS:
- Required for large projects
- Show final installation
- Pipe sizes and routes
- Fixture locations

MAINTENANCE MANUALS:
- Equipment specifications
- Operating instructions
- Maintenance schedules

CHAPTER 10: WASA CONNECTION PROCEDURES
======================================================================

10.1 WATER SUPPLY CONNECTION

APPLICATION PROCESS:
1. Submit application to WASA
2. Submit building plan approval
3. Submit plumbing drawings
4. Pay connection fees

FEES:
- Based on pipe size
- Meter size
- Security deposit

INSTALLATION:
- WASA inspects internal plumbing
- WASA installs meter
- Connection activated

METERING:
- All connections metered
- Customer responsible for meter protection
- Reading frequency: Monthly

10.2 SEWERAGE CONNECTION

APPLICATION:
- Similar to water connection
- Additional drainage plan approval

REQUIREMENTS:
- Inspection chamber at property boundary
- Proper invert levels
- WASA inspection and approval

10.3 BILLING AND PAYMENTS

TARIFF STRUCTURE:
- Domestic: Slab-based on consumption
- Commercial: Higher rate
- Industrial: Separate rate

BILLING:
- Monthly bills
- Metered consumption
- Fixed charges
- VAT and other charges

PAYMENT:
- Bank payments
- Mobile banking
- WASA offices
- Online payment

CHAPTER 11: MAINTENANCE AND OPERATIONS
======================================================================

11.1 ROUTINE MAINTENANCE

WATER SUPPLY SYSTEM:
- Check leaks regularly
- Clean overhead tanks (every 6 months)
- Service pumps annually
- Check valves and controls

DRAINAGE SYSTEM:
- Clear traps periodically
- Flush drains with water
- Check vent pipes
- Inspect for leaks

11.2 COMMON PROBLEMS

WATER SUPPLY:
- Low pressure: Check pump, tank levels
- No water: Check supply, pump operation
- Contamination: Clean tanks, check backflow
- Leaks: Repair promptly

DRAINAGE:
- Blockages: Use plunger, snake, professional service
- Slow drainage: Check venting, slopes
- Odors: Check trap seals, venting
- Overflows: Clear blockages, check design

11.3 PROFESSIONAL SERVICES

WHEN TO CALL PROFESSIONAL:
- Major blockages
- System design changes
- Equipment failure
- Contamination issues
- Major leaks
- Compliance issues
