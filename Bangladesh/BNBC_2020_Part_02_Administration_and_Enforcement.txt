CHAPTER 1: PURPOSE AND APPLICABILITY
======================================================================

1.1 GENERAL PURPOSE
Part 2 of the Bangladesh National Building Code establishes the administrative
framework for enforcement of building regulations throughout Bangladesh. It
defines the duties and powers of code officials, establishes permit and
inspection procedures, and sets forth requirements for plan review, construction
oversight, and certificate of occupancy issuance.

1.2 SCOPE OF ADMINISTRATION
1.2.1 Territorial Jurisdiction
These administrative provisions apply to all areas within Bangladesh where
building construction, alteration, or demolition activities occur.

1.2.2 Enforcing Authorities
The following authorities have jurisdiction to enforce BNBC 2020:

a) Rajdhani Unnayan Kartripakkha (RAJUK)
   - Jurisdiction: Dhaka Metropolitan Development Planning Area
   - Covers: Dhaka City Corporation areas and surrounding development zones
   - Primary responsibility: Building plan approval and construction monitoring

b) City Corporations
   - Jurisdiction: Major cities (Chittagong, Khulna, Rajshahi, Sylhet,
     Barisal, Rangpur, Gazipur, Narayanganj, Comilla, Mymensingh)
   - Responsibility: Building permits, inspections, and occupancy certificates

c) Municipalities (Pourashava)
   - Jurisdiction: 330+ municipalities throughout Bangladesh
   - Responsibility: Local building control and code enforcement

d) Local Government Engineering Department (LGED)
   - Jurisdiction: Rural areas and small towns
   - Responsibility: Public infrastructure and development projects

e) Bangladesh Fire Service and Civil Defence
   - Jurisdiction: Nationwide for fire safety compliance
   - Responsibility: Fire safety plan approval for multi-storey buildings

1.3 COMPLEMENTARY LEGISLATION
1.3.1 Related Acts and Rules
BNBC 2020 operates in conjunction with:
- Building Construction Act 1952
- Building Construction Rules 1996
- RAJUK Act 2011 (for Dhaka Metropolitan Area)
- City Corporation Acts (for respective cities)
- Fire Prevention and Extinguishing Act 2003
- Fire Prevention and Extinction Rules 2014
- Environment Conservation Act 1995
- Bangladesh Labour Act 2006 (construction safety)

CHAPTER 2: ESTABLISHMENT OF AUTHORITY
======================================================================

2.1 DEPARTMENT OF BUILDING SAFETY
2.1.1 Organizational Structure
Each enforcing authority shall establish a Department of Building Safety
responsible for code administration and enforcement, headed by a Chief Building
Official with the following divisions:

a) Plan Review Division
   - Architectural review
   - Structural review
   - MEP (Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing) review
   - Fire safety review

b) Inspection Division
   - Foundation and excavation inspections
   - Structural frame inspections
   - MEP rough-in inspections
   - Final inspections

c) Enforcement Division
   - Violation notices and stop-work orders
   - Unsafe building declarations
   - Demolition orders
   - Legal proceedings

d) Records and Documentation Division
   - Permit issuance and tracking
   - Plan storage and retrieval
   - Inspection records management
   - Digital database maintenance

2.2 APPOINTMENT OF BUILDING OFFICIAL
2.2.1 Qualifications
The Building Official shall be appointed by the enforcing authority and shall
possess the following minimum qualifications:

a) Educational Requirements:
   - Bachelor's degree in Architecture, Civil Engineering, or related field
   - For senior positions: Master's degree preferred

b) Professional Experience:
   - Minimum 10 years experience in building design, construction, or code enforcement
   - For RAJUK and major City Corporations: 15 years experience
   - Demonstrated knowledge of building codes and construction practices

c) Professional Registration:
   - Valid registration with Institute of Engineers, Bangladesh (IEB), or
   - Institute of Architects Bangladesh (IAB), or
   - Other recognized professional body

2.2.2 Powers and Duties
The Building Official shall have authority to:

a) Enforcement Powers:
   - Interpret and enforce all provisions of BNBC 2020
   - Issue permits and certificates
   - Conduct or authorize inspections
   - Issue notices and orders
   - Suspend or revoke permits
   - Order cessation of work
   - Initiate legal proceedings

b) Administrative Duties:
   - Receive and review applications
   - Approve or reject construction documents
   - Maintain official records
   - Establish inspection procedures
   - Coordinate with other agencies
   - Provide technical guidance
   - Submit periodic reports

c) Emergency Powers:
   - Order immediate evacuation of unsafe buildings
   - Authorize emergency repairs
   - Demolish dangerous structures (with proper authorization)
   - Take necessary actions to protect public safety

2.3 DEPUTY BUILDING OFFICIALS AND INSPECTORS
2.3.1 Appointment
The Building Official may appoint Deputy Building Officials, Plan Reviewers,
and Building Inspectors to assist in code enforcement duties.

2.3.2 Qualifications for Inspectors
Building Inspectors shall possess:
a) Bachelor's degree in relevant engineering or architecture discipline, or
b) Diploma in Civil Engineering with 5 years field experience
c) Thorough knowledge of construction practices
d) Familiarity with BNBC provisions

2.3.3 Specialized Inspectors
Specialized inspectors may be appointed for:
- Structural systems
- Electrical systems
- Plumbing systems
- Mechanical systems
- Fire protection systems
- Elevator and escalator systems

2.4 BUILDING BOARD OF APPEALS
2.4.1 Establishment
Each enforcing authority shall establish a Building Board of Appeals to hear
appeals from decisions of the Building Official.

2.4.2 Composition
The Board shall consist of five members:
a) Chairman: Retired judge or senior architect/engineer
b) Structural Engineer (registered with IEB)
c) Architect (registered with IAB)
d) Fire Safety Expert
e) Urban Planning Expert

Members shall serve three-year terms and may be reappointed.

2.4.3 Powers
The Board may:
- Hear and decide appeals from Building Official decisions
- Interpret code provisions
- Recommend code modifications
- Grant variances in specific cases
- Uphold, modify, or reverse Building Official decisions

2.4.4 Appeal Procedures
a) Appeals must be filed within 30 days of Building Official decision
b) Written notice of appeal with grounds stated
c) Filing fee as established by authority
d) Board shall conduct hearing within 60 days
e) Decision shall be rendered within 30 days of hearing
f) Board decision may be appealed to civil court

CHAPTER 3: PERMITS AND APPROVALS
======================================================================

3.1 REQUIREMENT FOR BUILDING PERMIT
3.1.1 General Requirement
No person shall construct, alter, repair, move, demolish, or change the
occupancy of any building or structure without first obtaining a building
permit from the Building Official.

3.1.2 Separate Permits Required
Separate permits are required for:
a) Building construction or alteration
b) Electrical installations
c) Plumbing installations
d) Mechanical systems
e) Fire protection systems
f) Elevator installations
g) Demolition work
h) Temporary structures (if required)

3.2 PERMIT APPLICATION PROCEDURES
3.2.1 Application Submission
Applications for building permits shall be submitted to the Building Official
on forms provided by the enforcing authority and shall include:

a) Completed application form with:
   - Owner information (name, address, contact)
   - Property details (location, plot number, khatian number)
   - Project description
   - Estimated cost of construction
   - Proposed use/occupancy
   - Construction timeline

b) Site Documents:
   - Land ownership documents (certified copy of deed/lease)
   - Updated survey plan
   - Soil investigation report
   - Site photographs

c) Construction Drawings:
   - Architectural drawings (plans, elevations, sections)
   - Structural drawings and calculations
   - Electrical drawings and load calculations
   - Plumbing and sanitary drawings
   - Mechanical/HVAC drawings
   - Fire protection drawings
   - Landscaping plan
   - All drawings signed and sealed by licensed professionals

d) Technical Reports:
   - Structural design calculations
   - Geotechnical investigation report
   - Environmental impact assessment (if required)
   - Traffic impact study (for large projects)
   - Fire safety certificate from Fire Service
   - Utility connection approvals

e) Compliance Documents:
   - BNBC compliance statement
   - Setback and FAR calculations
   - Parking provision details
   - Energy efficiency calculations
   - Accessibility compliance statement

3.2.2 Number of Sets
Submit the following number of complete document sets:
- RAJUK/City Corporations: 6 sets
- Municipalities: 4 sets
- One additional set for Fire Service (multi-storey buildings)

3.2.3 Digital Submissions
Authorities may accept or require digital submissions in approved formats
(PDF, DWG, etc.) through online portals.

3.3 PLAN REVIEW PROCESS
3.3.1 Initial Review
Upon receipt of application, the Building Official shall:
a) Assign unique application number
b) Check for completeness
c) Return incomplete applications within 7 days
d) Distribute to review divisions
e) Send to Fire Service if required

3.3.2 Review Timeline
Standard review timelines:
- Small projects (up to 3 storeys): 30 days
- Medium projects (4-10 storeys): 45 days
- Large projects (over 10 storeys): 60 days
- Special structures: 90 days

Timeline begins when complete application is received.

3.3.3 Fire Service Review
For buildings exceeding 3 storeys or special occupancies:
a) Simultaneous submission to Fire Service mandatory
b) Fire Service shall review within 30 days
c) Fire Safety Certificate required before building permit issuance
d) Building Official may not issue permit without Fire Service approval

3.3.4 Review Comments
If corrections are required:
a) Building Official issues written comments within review period
b) Applicant must respond with revised documents
c) Re-review timeline: 50% of original period
d) Additional comment cycles if necessary

3.3.5 Approval or Rejection
The Building Official shall:
a) Approve complete and compliant applications
b) Issue written rejection with specific reasons for non-compliance
c) Notify applicant of decision

3.4 PERMIT ISSUANCE
3.4.1 Permit Requirements
Upon approval, the Building Official shall issue permit including:
a) Permit number and date
b) Project details and location
c) Approved construction scope
d) Validity period
e) Inspection requirements
f) Conditions and restrictions
g) Permit fees paid

3.4.2 Permit Fees
Permit fees shall be established by the enforcing authority based on:
- Project construction value
- Building area
- Number of storeys
- Occupancy type
- Review complexity

Typical fee structure:
- Plan review fee: 0.5-1% of construction cost
- Building permit fee: 0.5-1% of construction cost
- Inspection fees: Included or separate
- Re-inspection fees: As established

3.4.3 Permit Validity
Building permits are valid for:
- Standard buildings: 2 years from issuance
- Large projects: 3 years from issuance
- Extension possible with written request before expiration
- Lapsed permits require new application

3.4.4 Permit Display
The building permit shall be:
a) Displayed prominently at construction site
b) Kept accessible for inspection
c) Protected from weather
d) Visible from street

3.5 WORK EXEMPT FROM PERMITS
The following work does not require a building permit:
a) Minor repairs not affecting structural elements
b) Painting, wallpapering, tiling (interior finishes)
c) Replacement of damaged materials with identical materials
d) Single-storey tool sheds under 10 sq.m
e) Temporary construction site offices (less than 6 months)
f) Agricultural buildings in rural areas (unless specified)
g) Boundary walls under 2 meters height

Note: Exemption from permit does not exempt from code compliance.

3.6 SPECIAL PERMITS AND APPROVALS
3.6.1 Demolition Permits
Required for demolition of any building. Application must include:
- Demolition plan and method statement
- Safety measures for adjacent properties
- Debris disposal plan
- Utility disconnection certificates
- Worker safety plan
- Site security plan

3.6.2 Temporary Structures
Permits required for:
- Construction site offices (over 6 months)
- Temporary event structures
- Festival pandals and stages
- Scaffolding on public ways
- Validity: Specified term only

3.6.3 Moving Buildings
Special permit required to relocate buildings, including:
- Route plan
- Structural assessment
- Transportation method
- New site approvals
- Public safety measures

3.6.4 Change of Occupancy
Permit required when building use changes to different occupancy classification,
including:
- Previous occupancy details
- Proposed new occupancy
- Code compliance analysis
- Required modifications
- Life safety upgrades

CHAPTER 4: CONSTRUCTION INSPECTIONS
======================================================================

4.1 INSPECTION AUTHORITY
4.1.1 General Requirements
The Building Official is authorized to conduct inspections during construction
to verify compliance with approved plans and BNBC provisions.

4.1.2 Right of Entry
Building Officials and inspectors may enter premises at reasonable hours to
perform inspections. Refusal of entry is grounds for permit suspension.

4.2 REQUIRED INSPECTIONS
4.2.1 Mandatory Inspection Points
The following inspections are required for all projects:

a) Pre-Construction Meeting
   - Review approved plans
   - Discuss inspection schedule
   - Verify site conditions
   - Confirm contractor qualifications

b) Foundation Inspection
   - Excavation depth and dimensions
   - Soil bearing verification
   - Reinforcement placement
   - Formwork installation
   - Before concrete placement

c) Plinth Level Inspection
   - Foundation concrete quality
   - Damp proof course installation
   - Plinth filling and compaction
   - Ground floor level verification

d) Structural Frame Inspection (each floor)
   - Column and beam reinforcement
   - Concrete cover verification
   - Formwork adequacy
   - Before concrete pouring
   - Post-pour curing inspection

e) Roof Level Inspection
   - Roof structure completion
   - Water proofing installation
   - Drainage provisions
   - Parapet construction

f) Electrical Rough-In Inspection
   - Conduit and wiring installation
   - Panel locations
   - Grounding system
   - Before concealment

g) Plumbing Rough-In Inspection
   - Pipe installation and sizing
   - Drainage slopes
   - Vent system
   - Pressure testing
   - Before concealment

h) Mechanical Rough-In Inspection
   - Ductwork installation
   - Equipment locations
   - Ventilation provisions

i) Fire Protection System Inspection
   - Sprinkler piping
   - Fire alarm wiring
   - Fire extinguisher placement
   - Emergency lighting

j) Insulation and Weatherproofing
   - Exterior wall insulation
   - Window installation
   - Weather barriers

k) Final Inspection
   - All work completed
   - All systems operational
   - Code compliance verified
   - Safety features functional
   - Required certificates obtained

4.2.2 Special Inspections
For complex or critical elements, special inspections by qualified third-party
agencies may be required:
- Structural steel welding
- High-strength concrete placement
- Post-tensioned concrete
- Deep foundation installation
- Curtain wall systems
- Fire-resistive assemblies
- Special mechanical systems

4.3 INSPECTION PROCEDURES
4.3.1 Requesting Inspections
a) Permit holder must request inspections 48 hours in advance
b) Request methods: Phone, online portal, written notice
c) Work must be ready for inspection at scheduled time
d) Access and lighting must be provided

4.3.2 Inspection Scheduling
The Building Official shall:
a) Schedule inspection within 2 working days of request
b) Conduct inspection on scheduled date
c) Reschedule if site not ready (re-inspection fee may apply)
d) Provide emergency inspection service if needed

4.3.3 Inspection Reports
After each inspection, the inspector shall:
a) Complete inspection report form
b) Indicate "Pass" or "Fail" status
c) Note deficiencies if failed
d) Provide correction requirements
e) Sign and date report
f) Provide copy to permit holder

4.3.4 Failed Inspections
If work fails inspection:
a) Work must stop in that area
b) Corrections must be made
c) Re-inspection must be requested
d) Work may not proceed until passed
e) Re-inspection fee may be charged

4.3.5 Concealment Prohibition
No work shall be concealed before required inspection and approval. If work
is concealed without inspection:
a) Work must be exposed for inspection
b) Re-inspection fee charged
c) Permit may be suspended
d) Civil penalties may apply

4.4 APPROVED INSPECTION AGENCIES
4.4.1 Qualification Requirements
Third-party inspection agencies must be:
a) Registered with BSTI or international accreditation
b) Employ qualified engineers and technicians
c) Have no financial interest in project
d) Maintain liability insurance
e) Submit reports to Building Official

4.4.2 Approved Agencies
Recognized inspection agencies include:
- BUET (Bangladesh University of Engineering & Technology)
- BSTI (Bangladesh Standards and Testing Institution)
- PWD Testing Laboratories
- International firms with local accreditation

4.5 SITE CONDITIONS AND WORK PROGRESS
4.5.1 Contractor Responsibilities
During construction, contractors must:
a) Maintain safe working conditions
b) Provide required safety equipment
c) Control dust and noise
d) Manage construction waste properly
e) Protect adjacent properties
f) Maintain traffic safety
g) Provide site security

4.5.2 Materials on Site
All construction materials must:
a) Comply with specified standards
b) Have valid test certificates
c) Be protected from weather and damage
d) Be available for inspection

CHAPTER 5: CERTIFICATE OF OCCUPANCY
======================================================================

5.1 OCCUPANCY CERTIFICATE REQUIREMENT
5.1.1 General Requirement
No building shall be occupied or used until a Certificate of Occupancy has
been issued by the Building Official.

5.1.2 Temporary Occupancy
Temporary Certificate of Occupancy may be issued for partial completion,
subject to conditions and time limitations.

5.2 APPLICATION FOR CERTIFICATE
5.2.1 Submission Requirements
Application for Certificate of Occupancy shall include:
a) Completed application form
b) Final inspection approval
c) Completion certificates from:
   - Structural engineer
   - Electrical engineer
   - Plumbing engineer
   - Mechanical engineer
   - Fire safety consultant

d) Test certificates for:
   - Electrical installation testing
   - Plumbing pressure testing
   - Fire alarm system testing
   - Elevator load testing (if applicable)
   - Water quality testing

e) As-built drawings showing:
   - Final construction conditions
   - Any approved changes from original plans
   - Sealed by licensed professionals

f) Utility connection certificates:
   - Electricity connection from DESCO/DESA/PDB
   - Water supply connection
   - Gas connection (if applicable)
   - Sewerage connection approval

g) Clearance certificates:
   - Fire Service clearance
   - Environmental clearance (if required)
   - Labour Department clearance (for factories)

5.2.2 Processing Timeline
Certificate of Occupancy shall be issued within:
- Residential buildings: 15 days of complete application
- Commercial buildings: 30 days of complete application
- Industrial buildings: 45 days of complete application

5.3 TYPES OF CERTIFICATES
5.3.1 Permanent Certificate of Occupancy
Issued when:
- All work completed per approved plans
- All inspections passed
- All certificates submitted
- All fees paid
- Full code compliance verified

5.3.2 Temporary Certificate of Occupancy
May be issued for:
- Partial building completion
- Phased occupancy
- Seasonal use
- Maximum validity: 6 months
- Specific areas designated for use
- Conditions specified for remaining work

5.3.3 Certificate of Completion
Issued for buildings not intended for occupancy:
- Parking structures
- Warehouses (unstaffed)
- Telecommunications towers
- Water tanks and utilities

5.4 CHANGE OF OCCUPANCY CERTIFICATE
5.4.1 When Required
Required when building use changes from one occupancy classification to another:
- Residential to commercial
- Office to restaurant
- Retail to assembly
- Any significant use change

5.4.2 Application Requirements
a) Description of previous and new use
b) Code compliance analysis
c) Required modifications
d) Updated fire safety plans
e) Parking adequacy verification
f) Structural adequacy certification

5.5 REVOCATION OF CERTIFICATE
The Building Official may revoke Certificate of Occupancy if:
- Building becomes unsafe
- Code violations discovered
- Occupancy changed without approval
- Required maintenance neglected
- False information in application

CHAPTER 6: ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTIES
======================================================================

6.1 VIOLATIONS AND NOTICES
6.1.1 Notice of Violation
When code violations are observed, the Building Official shall issue written
Notice of Violation specifying:
a) Nature of violation
b) Code section(s) violated
c) Required corrective action
d) Deadline for compliance
e) Consequences of non-compliance

6.1.2 Compliance Timeline
Standard compliance periods:
- Safety hazards: 7 days or immediate
- Structural issues: 30 days
- Non-structural issues: 60 days
- Minor deficiencies: 90 days

Extensions may be granted for cause with written request.

6.2 STOP WORK ORDERS
6.2.1 Authority to Issue
The Building Official may issue Stop Work Order when:
- Work proceeding without permit
- Work deviates from approved plans
- Unsafe conditions exist
- Inspection requirements not met
- Environmental violations occur

6.2.2 Effect of Stop Work Order
Upon issuance:
a) All work must immediately cease
b) Site must be secured
c) Only corrective work permitted
d) Order posted at site
e) Owner notified in writing

6.2.3 Lifting Stop Work Order
Order shall be lifted when:
- Violations corrected
- Required inspections passed
- Applicable fees paid
- Building Official satisfied with compliance

6.3 UNSAFE BUILDINGS
6.3.1 Determination
Building Official may declare building unsafe if:
- Structural failure risk
- Fire hazard exists
- Sanitary hazards present
- Deteriorated condition
- Damage from disaster

6.3.2 Emergency Actions
For unsafe buildings, Building Official may:
a) Order immediate evacuation
b) Post "Unsafe - Do Not Occupy" notice
c) Disconnect utilities
d) Erect protective barriers
e) Order emergency repairs
f) Authorize demolition (with proper approval)

6.3.3 Owner Obligations
Owner of unsafe building must:
- Secure building immediately
- Make required repairs within specified time
- Demolish if repair not feasible
- Bear all costs of emergency actions

6.4 PENALTIES AND PROSECUTION
6.4.1 Civil Penalties
Violations subject to fines:
- Working without permit: BDT 50,000 - 500,000
- Failure to comply with notice: BDT 10,000 - 100,000 per day
- False information: BDT 100,000 - 1,000,000
- Unauthorized occupancy: BDT 50,000 - 500,000
- Repeated violations: Double penalties

6.4.2 Criminal Prosecution
Serious violations may result in:
- Imprisonment up to 2 years
- Fines up to BDT 5,000,000
- Professional license suspension
- Project shutdown
- Demolition orders

6.4.3 Additional Remedies
Enforcing authority may:
- Revoke building permits
- Suspend occupancy certificates
- Record liens on property
- Seek injunctive relief
- File criminal charges
- Report to professional licensing boards

6.5 LIABILITY
6.5.1 Owner Liability
Owners are liable for:
- Compliance with all code requirements
- Contractor actions on their property
- Maintaining building safety
- Damages from code violations

6.5.2 Contractor/Professional Liability
Design professionals and contractors may be:
- Professionally disciplined
- Financially liable for damages
- Criminally prosecuted for willful violations
- Banned from future projects

CHAPTER 7: MEANS OF APPEAL
======================================================================

7.1 RIGHT OF APPEAL
7.1.1 Who May Appeal
The following parties may appeal Building Official decisions:
a) Building permit applicant
b) Property owner
c) Tenant or occupant
d) Person directly affected by decision

7.1.2 Appealable Decisions
Appeals may be filed regarding:
- Permit denial
- Code interpretation
- Violation notice
- Stop work order
- Unsafe building declaration
- Certificate of Occupancy denial
- Penalty assessment

7.2 APPEAL PROCEDURES
7.2.1 Filing Requirements
Appeals must be:
a) Filed within 30 days of decision
b) Submitted in writing
c) State grounds for appeal
d) Include supporting documents
e) Pay required appeal fee (BDT 5,000 - 50,000)

7.2.2 Effect of Filing
Filing appeal does not:
- Stay enforcement action (unless Board orders)
- Permit continued violations
- Excuse code compliance
- Extend permit validity

7.2.3 Board Review Process
a) Board schedules hearing within 60 days
b) Notice sent to all parties
c) Parties may present evidence and testimony
d) Board may inspect property
e) Technical experts may testify
f) Decision rendered within 30 days of hearing

7.3 BOARD DECISION
7.3.1 Powers of Board
Board may:
- Affirm Building Official decision
- Reverse or modify decision
- Grant variance with conditions
- Remand for further review
- Impose alternative remedies

7.3.2 Variance Criteria
Board may grant variance only if:
a) Literal code enforcement causes undue hardship
b) Hardship not self-created
c) Variance maintains code intent
d) Public safety not compromised
e) Minimal impact on neighboring properties

7.3.3 Final Decision
Board decision is final administrative action. Further appeal only to civil
court on legal grounds.

CHAPTER 8: RECORD KEEPING AND REPORTS
======================================================================

8.1 REQUIRED RECORDS
8.1.1 Permanent Records
Building Official shall maintain permanent records of:
a) All permit applications and approvals
b) Building plans and specifications
c) Inspection reports
d) Test certificates
e) Certificates of Occupancy
f) Violation notices and enforcement actions
g) Board of Appeals decisions

8.1.2 Record Retention
Minimum retention periods:
- Active permit files: Duration + 5 years
- Building plans: Permanent (life of building)
- Inspection records: 10 years
- Violation records: 10 years
- Certificates: Permanent

8.1.3 Public Access
Records are public information, except:
- Proprietary technical information
- Security-sensitive details
- Personal privacy information
Copies available for fee covering reproduction costs.

8.2 REPORTING REQUIREMENTS
8.2.1 Annual Reports
Building Official shall submit annual report including:
a) Number of permits issued by type
b) Total construction value
c) Number of inspections conducted
d) Violations and enforcement actions
e) Revenue collected
f) Staffing levels
g) Challenges and recommendations

8.2.2 Statistical Data
Maintain statistics on:
- Building types and occupancies
- Construction trends
- Code compliance rates
- Common violations
- Processing times
