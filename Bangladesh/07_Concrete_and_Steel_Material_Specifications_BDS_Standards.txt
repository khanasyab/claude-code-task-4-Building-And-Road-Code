1.1 CEMENT STANDARDS

PRIMARY BDS STANDARDS FOR CEMENT:

BDS 232: PORTLAND CEMENT
General purpose Portland cement specifications

BDS 612: SULPHATE RESISTING PORTLAND CEMENT - TYPE A
For concrete exposed to sulphate attack

BDS EN 197-1:2003: CEMENT COMPOSITION AND SPECIFICATIONS
Harmonized with European standards

1.2 CEMENT CLASSIFICATION

PORTLAND CEMENT TYPES:

Ordinary Portland Cement (OPC):
- Most common type
- General construction use
- Standard setting time

Portland Pozzolana Cement (PPC):
- Contains pozzolanic materials
- Improved long-term strength
- Better durability
- Reduced heat of hydration

Portland Slag Cement (PSC):
- Contains ground granulated blast furnace slag
- Enhanced durability
- Sulphate resistance

Sulphate Resisting Portland Cement:
- Special applications
- Exposure to sulphate-rich soils/water
- Marine environments

1.3 CEMENT SPECIFICATIONS

MAJOR CEMENT MANUFACTURERS IN BANGLADESH:

Bengal Cement:
Classification: CEM II/B-M, 42.5 N
Composition: Portland cement with composite additives
Strength Class: 42.5 MPa at 28 days
Conformance: BDS EN 197-1:2003

PHYSICAL REQUIREMENTS:

Setting Time:
- Initial setting time: Not less than 45 minutes
- Final setting time: Not more than 8 hours

Soundness:
- Expansion: Not more than 10mm (Le Chatelier test)

Specific Surface:
- Not less than 160 m²/kg (Blaine method)

Compressive Strength:
Ordinary Portland Cement:
- 3 days: Minimum 23 MPa
- 7 days: Minimum 33 MPa
- 28 days: Minimum 43 MPa (Grade 43)
           Minimum 53 MPa (Grade 53)

CHEMICAL REQUIREMENTS:

Limits on constituents:
- Magnesia (MgO): Maximum 6%
- Sulphuric anhydride (SO3): Maximum 3%
- Loss on ignition: Maximum 4%
- Insoluble residue: Maximum 2%
- Chloride content: Maximum 0.1%

1.4 CEMENT STORAGE AND HANDLING

STORAGE REQUIREMENTS:
- Weatherproof storage sheds
- Raised platform (minimum 300mm above ground)
- Protection from moisture
- Stack height: Maximum 10 bags (for stacking duration)
- First-in-first-out inventory system
- Shelf life: 3 months maximum

QUALITY CONTROL:
- Batch testing before use
- Visual inspection for lumps
- Rejection criteria established
- Sampling procedures per BDS

1.5 AGGREGATES

BDS 243:1963: COARSE AND FINE AGGREGATES FROM NATURAL SOURCES
FOR CONCRETE

FINE AGGREGATE (SAND):

Grading Requirements:
Passing through:
- 10mm sieve: 100%
- 4.75mm sieve: 95-100%
- 2.36mm sieve: 80-100%
- 1.18mm sieve: 50-85%
- 600μm sieve: 25-60%
- 300μm sieve: 10-30%
- 150μm sieve: 2-10%

Fineness Modulus:
- Range: 2.3 to 3.1
- Variation: ±0.20 from target

Quality Requirements:
- Free from deleterious materials
- Organic impurities: Light color (colorimetric test)
- Silt and clay content: Maximum 3% (for RCC)
- Salt content: Maximum 3% inland, 1% coastal

COARSE AGGREGATE:

Size Designation:
- Single size: 20mm, 40mm
- Graded: 20mm to 4.75mm (nominal size 20mm)
         40mm to 4.75mm (nominal size 40mm)

Grading Requirements:
Specified in relevant tables per BDS 882:1992 or BS 882:1992

Quality Requirements:

Aggregate Crushing Value:
- Shall be less than 30%
- Test per BS 812

Los Angeles Abrasion Value:
- Maximum 40%
- Test per ASTM C131

Flakiness Index:
- Not exceeding 20% by weight
- Test per BS 812

Elongation Index:
- Not exceeding 35% by weight
- Test per BS 812

Water Absorption:
- Maximum 2% (for good quality aggregate)

Specific Gravity:
- Typically 2.5 to 2.7

Soundness:
- Sodium sulphate test: Maximum 12% loss
- Magnesium sulphate test: Maximum 18% loss

1.6 MIXING WATER

BDS ISO 12439:2011: MIXING WATER FOR CONCRETE

QUALITY REQUIREMENTS:
- Potable water generally suitable
- Free from oils, acids, alkalis, salts, organic matter
- pH value: 6.0 to 8.0
- Chloride content: Maximum 500 ppm
- Sulphate content: Maximum 400 ppm
- Total dissolved solids: Maximum 2000 ppm

TESTING:
Comparative tests with distilled water:
- Setting time: Within ±30 minutes
- Compressive strength: Minimum 90% at 7 and 28 days

1.7 CONCRETE MIX DESIGN

MIX DESIGN METHODOLOGY:

Common Practice in Bangladesh:
- Volumetric ratios: 1:1.5:3 or 1:2:4 (outdated method)
- Weight batching (proper method)
- Mix design per ACI 211.1 or BS method

STANDARD CONVERSION:
Practice in Bangladesh:
- 50 kg cement = 1.25 cft bulk volume of cement
- However, volumetric ratios have no rational basis
- Weight batching strongly recommended

TARGET STRENGTH:
- Target mean strength = Specified strength + (1.65 × Standard deviation)
- Minimum margin: 5 MPa for good quality control

WATER-CEMENT RATIO:
Based on:
- Required strength
- Durability requirements
- Exposure conditions

Typical values:
- 0.40-0.45: High strength, severe exposure
- 0.45-0.50: Medium strength, moderate exposure
- 0.50-0.60: Low strength, mild exposure

COMMON MIX PROPORTIONS:

Grade M15 (15 MPa):
- Cement: 1 part
- Sand: 2 parts
- Aggregate: 4 parts
- W/C ratio: 0.60

Grade M20 (20 MPa):
- Cement: 1 part
- Sand: 1.5 parts
- Aggregate: 3 parts
- W/C ratio: 0.50

Grade M25 (25 MPa):
- Cement: 1 part
- Sand: 1 part
- Aggregate: 2 parts
- W/C ratio: 0.45

Grade M30 (30 MPa):
- Adjusted for higher strength
- W/C ratio: 0.40-0.45

1.8 CONCRETE SPECIFICATIONS

BNBC 2020 - PART 5, CHAPTER 2: BUILDING MATERIALS

MINIMUM CONCRETE STRENGTH:
Structural members: 17.5 MPa (M17.5)

STANDARD STRENGTHS:
- M17.5 (17.5 MPa)
- M20 (20 MPa)
- M25 (25 MPa)
- M30 (30 MPa)
- M35 (35 MPa)
- M40 (40 MPa)

Higher strengths permitted with proper quality control

MAXIMUM AGGREGATE SIZE:
- General construction: 20mm
- Dense reinforcement: 10mm or 12mm
- Mass concrete: Up to 40mm

CONCRETE COVER:

Purpose: Protection of reinforcement

Minimum Cover Requirements:
- Footings (cast against earth): 75mm
- Beams and columns (indoor): 40mm
- Beams and columns (outdoor): 50mm
- Slabs (indoor): 20mm
- Slabs (outdoor): 30mm

Severe Exposure:
- Marine environment: 75mm
- Chemical exposure: As per design

1.9 ADMIXTURES

TYPES OF ADMIXTURES:

Plasticizers/Water Reducers:
- Reduce water content
- Improve workability
- Conformance to ASTM C494 Type A

Superplasticizers:
- High-range water reducers
- High-strength concrete
- Conformance to ASTM C494 Type F or G

Retarders:
- Delay setting time
- Hot weather concreting
- Conformance to ASTM C494 Type B or D

Accelerators:
- Faster strength gain
- Cold weather concreting
- Non-chloride type preferred
- Conformance to ASTM C494 Type C or E

Air-Entraining Agents:
- Improve freeze-thaw resistance
- Limited use in Bangladesh
- Conformance to ASTM C260

Waterproofing Admixtures:
- Reduce permeability
- Basement and water-retaining structures

1.10 CONCRETE PRODUCTION

BATCHING:
- Weight batching required for quality concrete
- Tolerance: ±2% for cement, ±3% for aggregates
- Water measured accurately
- Admixtures per manufacturer's instructions

MIXING:
Mixer Types:
- Drum mixer
- Pan mixer
- Continuous mixer

Mixing Time:
- Minimum 1.5 minutes for mixer up to 1 m³
- Additional 30 seconds per m³ thereafter

TRANSPORTATION:
- Minimize time between mixing and placing
- Maximum 30 minutes in hot weather
- Agitation during transport
- No water addition after batching

PLACING:
- Free fall: Maximum 1.5m
- Chutes or pumps for greater heights
- No segregation
- Layer thickness: Maximum 500mm
- Placement continuous within section

COMPACTION:
Methods:
- Needle vibrator (most common)
- Table vibrator (precast)
- Tamping (small works)

Requirements:
- Remove entrapped air
- Achieve full compaction
- Avoid over-vibration
- Do not vibrate reinforcement

CURING:
Methods:
- Water curing (ponding, spraying, wet covering)
- Membrane curing (curing compounds)
- Steam curing (precast)

Duration:
- Minimum 7 days (ordinary cement)
- Minimum 10 days (pozzolana cement)
- Extended for severe exposure

Requirements:
- Maintain moisture
- Prevent rapid drying
- Protect from temperature extremes

1.11 HIGH STRENGTH CONCRETE IN BANGLADESH

DEVELOPMENT:
Research paper: "Development of High Strength Concrete in Bangladesh"

CHARACTERISTICS:
- Strength exceeding 40 MPa
- Careful material selection
- Low water-cement ratio (0.35-0.40)
- Superplasticizers essential
- Quality control critical

APPLICATIONS:
- High-rise buildings
- Long-span structures
- Precast elements
- Special structures

CHAPTER 2: REINFORCING STEEL SPECIFICATIONS

2.1 BANGLADESH STANDARDS FOR REINFORCING STEEL

PRIMARY STANDARDS:

BDS 1313:1991:
Bangladesh Standard Specification for Steel Bars and Wires for the
Reinforcement of Concrete

BDS ISO 6935-2:2006:
Adopts ISO 6935-2:1991 as Bangladesh Standard
Specifies technical requirements for ribbed bars including:
- Grades of steel
- Chemical composition
- Mechanical properties
- Shape and dimensions

2.2 BNBC 2020 REQUIREMENTS

PERMITTED STANDARDS:
Deformed reinforcing bars shall conform to:
- BDS 1313
- ASTM A615
- ASTM A706
- BS 4461

YIELD STRENGTH LIMITS:
- Bars with specified yield strength exceeding 410 N/mm² are
  permitted if they otherwise conform to the listed ASTM
  specifications
- The yield strength of reinforcement shall not be taken more than
  550 N/mm²

2.3 REINFORCEMENT GRADES

COMMON GRADES IN BANGLADESH:

Grade 40 (280 MPa):
- Mild steel bars
- Plain round bars
- Limited use now

Grade 60 (410 MPa):
- Most common grade
- Deformed bars (ribbed)
- General construction use

Grade B420 DWR:
- Yield strength: 420 MPa minimum
- Cold twisted deformed bars
- Conformance: BDS/ISO 6935-2:2016, ASTM A615/A615M

Grade B500 DWR:
- Yield strength: 500 MPa minimum
- High-strength deformed bars
- Conformance: BDS/ISO 6935-2:2016, ASTM A615/A615M

2.4 MECHANICAL PROPERTIES

GRADE-60 DEFORMED REBARS:
Should conform to ASTM A617M or A615M

Yield Strength:
- Minimum: 420 MPa (N/mm²)

Tensile Strength:
- Minimum: 550 MPa
- Ratio tensile/yield: Minimum 1.25

Elongation:
- Minimum: 14% (bars ≤16mm diameter)
- Minimum: 12% (bars >16mm diameter)

Bend Test:
- 180° bend around specified pin diameter
- No cracks on outside of bend

2.5 BANGLADESH REBAR MANUFACTURERS

2.6 REBAR SIZES AND DIMENSIONS

COMMON SIZES:
Diameter (mm): 6, 8, 10, 12, 16, 20, 22, 25, 28, 32, 36, 40

CROSS-SECTIONAL AREA:
Ø6mm: 28.3 mm²
Ø8mm: 50.3 mm²
Ø10mm: 78.5 mm²
Ø12mm: 113 mm²
Ø16mm: 201 mm²
Ø20mm: 314 mm²
Ø25mm: 491 mm²
Ø32mm: 804 mm²

WEIGHT:
Steel density: 7850 kg/m³
Weight (kg/m) ≈ D² / 162 (where D = diameter in mm)

2.7 REBAR PROPERTIES

RIB PATTERN:
Deformed bars have ribs for better bond with concrete

Requirements:
- Rib height
- Rib spacing
- Rib angle
- Gap between ribs

Specified in BDS ISO 6935-2:2006

SURFACE CONDITION:
- Free from loose rust, scale, oil, paint
- Light rust acceptable (improves bond)
- Clean before placing concrete

STRAIGHTNESS:
- Bars shall be reasonably straight
- Maximum deviation specified

2.8 WELDING OF REINFORCEMENT

WELDABILITY:
ASTM A706: Specifically designed for welding
- Controlled chemical composition
- Carbon equivalent limited
- Preferred for welded connections

ASTM A615: Can be welded with precautions
- Grade 40 and 60
- Preheat and postheat may be needed
- Welding procedures qualified

WELDING PROCEDURES:
- Qualified welders
- Proper electrode selection
- Preheat if required
- Inspection of welds

2.9 MECHANICAL SPLICING

TYPES:
- Coupler systems
- Threaded couplers
- Sleeve couplers
- Swaged couplers

REQUIREMENTS:
- Develop full bar strength
- Testing and qualification
- Proper installation
- Quality control

2.10 LAP SPLICES

LAP LENGTH:
Depends on:
- Bar diameter
- Concrete strength
- Bar position (top or bottom)
- Reinforcement ratio

Typical tension lap lengths:
- 40-60 times bar diameter
- Specified in design calculations

REQUIREMENTS:
- Minimum overlap
- Transverse reinforcement
- Staggered locations preferred

2.11 FABRICATION AND PLACEMENT

CUTTING:
- Shear cutting
- Saw cutting
- Torch cutting (limited use)

BENDING:
- Cold bending preferred
- Bend diameters per code
- Heating not permitted (affects properties)

Standard Hooks:
- 90° bend: 12d extension minimum
- 135° bend: 6d extension minimum
- 180° bend: 4d extension minimum
(where d = bar diameter)

PLACEMENT:
- Proper spacing
- Adequate cover
- Chair supports/spacers
- Tie wire or clips
- Inspection before concreting

2.12 QUALITY CONTROL

MILL CERTIFICATES:
- Chemical composition
- Mechanical properties
- Test results
- Conformance certification

SITE TESTING:
Frequency:
- Every 50 tonnes or lot
- Minimum 3 samples per lot

Tests:
- Tensile strength
- Yield strength
- Elongation
- Bend test

ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA:
- Meet specified minimum properties
- Individual test: ≥95% specified minimum
- Average: ≥100% specified minimum

CHAPTER 4: QUALITY ASSURANCE AND TESTING
======================================================================

4.1 CONCRETE TESTING

SLUMP TEST:
Purpose: Measure workability
Standard: ASTM C143, BS 1881
Frequency: Every batch or 50 m³
Target: As per mix design (typically 75-150mm)

COMPRESSIVE STRENGTH TEST:
Purpose: Verify strength development
Standard: ASTM C39, BS 1881

Specimens:
- Cubes: 150mm × 150mm × 150mm
- Cylinders: 150mm dia × 300mm height

Frequency:
- One set (3 cubes or 2 cylinders) per 50 m³ or per day's pour
- Minimum one set per pour

Testing Ages:
- 7 days
- 28 days
- Additional ages if specified

Acceptance:
- Average ≥ specified strength
- No individual <85% specified strength (or appropriate criteria)

WATER-CEMENT RATIO TEST:
- For quality control
- Moisture content of aggregates
- Free water calculation

4.2 AGGREGATE TESTING

SIEVE ANALYSIS:
- Grading verification
- Conformance to specifications
- Frequency: Every 500 tonnes or source change

SPECIFIC GRAVITY AND ABSORPTION:
- Material properties
- Mix design input
- Frequency: Per source

AGGREGATE CRUSHING VALUE:
- Strength assessment
- Maximum 30% for concrete
- Frequency: Per source or 2000 tonnes

LOS ANGELES ABRASION:
- Wear resistance
- Maximum 40%
- Frequency: Per source

FLAKINESS AND ELONGATION:
- Shape assessment
- Frequency: Per source or 500 tonnes

4.3 CEMENT TESTING

STANDARD TESTS:
- Fineness (Blaine test)
- Setting time (Vicat apparatus)
- Soundness (Le Chatelier)
- Compressive strength (mortar cubes)
- Specific gravity

FREQUENCY:
- Every 50 tonnes or delivery
- More frequent for critical works

4.4 STEEL TESTING

TENSILE TEST:
Equipment: Universal testing machine
Measurements:
- Yield strength
- Ultimate tensile strength
- Elongation

BEND TEST:
- Ductility assessment
- 180° bend around mandrel
- No cracks observed

FREQUENCY:
- Every 50 tonnes or lot
- Minimum 3 samples

ACCREDITED LABORATORIES:
