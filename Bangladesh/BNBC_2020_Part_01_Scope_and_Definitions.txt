CHAPTER 1: TITLE, PURPOSE AND SCOPE
======================================================================

1.1 TITLE
This Code shall be known as the Bangladesh National Building Code (BNBC) 2020.

1.2 PURPOSE
1.2.1 General Purpose
The purpose of this Code is to establish minimum requirements to safeguard
life, health, property, and public welfare by regulating and controlling the
design, construction, quality of materials, use and occupancy, location, and
maintenance of all buildings and structures within Bangladesh.

1.2.2 Specific Objectives
The specific objectives of this Code are to:
a) Provide minimum standards for the design and construction of buildings
b) Ensure structural safety and stability of buildings
c) Protect occupants from fire and other hazards
d) Ensure adequate sanitation, lighting, and ventilation
e) Promote energy efficiency and environmental sustainability
f) Facilitate accessibility for persons with disabilities
g) Establish uniform building regulations throughout Bangladesh
h) Protect adjoining property and public spaces

1.2.3 Application
This Code applies to the construction of new buildings, alteration, addition,
repair, relocation, demolition, removal, use and occupancy of existing buildings,
and building services within Bangladesh.

1.3 SCOPE OF APPLICATION
1.3.1 Buildings Covered
This Code shall apply to:
a) All new buildings and structures
b) Existing buildings undergoing alteration, addition, or change of use
c) Buildings being relocated
d) Building equipment and systems
e) Temporary structures and buildings
f) Signs and outdoor displays

1.3.2 Jurisdiction
This Code is applicable throughout the territory of Bangladesh and supersedes
all previous building codes and regulations except where local authorities
have adopted more stringent requirements.

1.3.3 Mandatory Compliance
Compliance with this Code is mandatory for all building design, construction,
and alteration work in Bangladesh. All buildings constructed after the
gazette notification date must comply with BNBC 2020 provisions.

1.4 EXCLUSIONS
1.4.1 Buildings Not Covered
The following are excluded from the scope of this Code:
a) Agricultural buildings used exclusively for farming purposes
b) Temporary structures for construction site offices (less than 6 months)
c) Small tool sheds and storage structures (less than 10 square meters)
d) Boundary walls less than 2 meters in height
e) Water towers and elevated tanks (subject to separate regulations)
f) Transmission towers and antenna masts

1.5 APPLICABILITY TO EXISTING BUILDINGS
1.5.1 General Requirements
Existing buildings that were legally constructed under previous codes are
not required to comply with this Code unless:
a) Alterations or additions are made
b) Change of occupancy occurs
c) Structural damage requires repair
d) The building official determines the building is unsafe
e) The building is relocated

1.5.2 Extent of Compliance
When alterations are made to existing buildings:
a) The altered portion shall comply with this Code
b) The existing portion shall not be made less conforming
c) The entire building need not be brought into compliance unless specified

CHAPTER 2: DEFINITIONS
======================================================================

2.1 GENERAL
For the purpose of this Code, the following terms shall have the meanings
indicated. Words used in the present tense include the future; words in the
masculine gender include the feminine and neuter; the singular number includes
the plural and the plural the singular.

2.2 ALPHABETICAL DEFINITIONS

ACCESSIBLE: A site, building, facility, or portion thereof that can be
approached, entered, and used by persons with disabilities.

ADDITION: An extension or increase in floor area, number of storeys, or
height of a building or structure.

ALTERATION: Any change, modification, or reconstruction that affects the
structural system, occupancy, use, or fire safety of a building.

APPROVED: Acceptable to the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ).

AUTHORITY HAVING JURISDICTION (AHJ): The organization, office, or individual
responsible for approving equipment, materials, an installation, or a procedure.
In Bangladesh, this typically includes Rajdhani Unnayan Kartripakkha (RAJUK),
City Corporations, Municipalities, and Pourashava authorities.

BASEMENT: That portion of a building between floor and ceiling which is
partly below and partly above grade, but so located that the vertical distance
from grade to the floor below is less than the vertical distance from grade
to ceiling.

BUILDING: Any structure used or intended for supporting or sheltering any
use or occupancy.

BUILDING AREA: The total area of a building measured from the exterior faces
of exterior walls or from the centerline of walls separating two buildings.

BUILDING HEIGHT: The vertical distance measured from the average level of
the highest adjoining sidewalk or ground surface within a distance equal to
the width of the building, to the highest point of the roof for flat roofs,
or to the average height between eaves and ridge for gable, hip, and gambrel roofs.

BUILDING OFFICIAL: The officer or other designated authority charged with
the administration and enforcement of this Code, or the building official's
authorized representative.

CONSTRUCTION DOCUMENTS: Written, graphic, and pictorial documents prepared
or assembled for describing the design, location, and physical characteristics
of the elements of a project necessary for obtaining a building permit.

DEAD LOAD: The weight of all permanent structural and nonstructural components
of a building, including walls, floors, roofs, and fixed service equipment.

DWELLING UNIT: A single unit providing complete, independent living facilities
for one or more persons, including permanent provisions for living, sleeping,
eating, cooking, and sanitation.

EMERGENCY EGRESS OPENING: An operable window, door, or other similar device
that provides for a means of escape in an emergency.

EXIT: That portion of a means of egress system between the exit access and
the exit discharge or public way.

FIRE DOOR: A door assembly including frame and hardware that is designed to
provide protection against the passage of fire and smoke.

FIRE RESISTANCE RATING: The time in minutes or hours that materials or
assemblies have withstood a fire exposure as established in accordance with
test procedures specified in this Code.

FIRE WALL: A wall having a fire-resistance rating designed to prevent the
spread of fire and which is continuous from the foundation to or through the roof.

FLOOR AREA, GROSS: The floor area within the inside perimeter of the exterior
walls of the building under consideration, without deductions for corridors,
shafts, stairways, or other features.

FOUNDATION: The system of structural components that supports a building and
transmits loads directly to the soil or bedrock.

GRADE PLANE: A reference plane representing the average of finished ground
level adjoining the building at exterior walls.

GUARD: A building component or a system of building components located at or
near the open sides of elevated walking surfaces that minimizes the possibility
of a fall from the walking surface to a lower level.

HANDRAIL: A horizontal or sloping rail intended for grasping by the hand for
guidance or support.

LIVE LOAD: The load superimposed by the use and occupancy of the building,
not including wind, earthquake, snow, or dead load.

LOAD-BEARING ELEMENT: Any structural element that supports more than its own weight.

MEANS OF EGRESS: A continuous and unobstructed path of vertical and horizontal
egress travel from any occupied portion of a building to a public way.

OCCUPANCY: The purpose or activity for which a building or portion thereof is
used or intended to be used.

OCCUPANT LOAD: The number of persons for which a building or portion thereof
is designed.

PERMIT: An official document or certificate issued by the Authority Having
Jurisdiction authorizing performance of a specified activity.

RAMP: A walking surface with a running slope steeper than 1 in 20.

REGISTERED DESIGN PROFESSIONAL: An individual who is registered or licensed
to practice their respective design profession as defined by the professional
registration laws of Bangladesh.

REPAIR: The reconstruction or renewal of any part of an existing building
for the purpose of its maintenance.

ROOF ASSEMBLY: The roof covering, roof deck, vapor retarder, substrate or
thermal barrier, insulation, and roof structure.

SHAFT: An interior vertical opening through a building that connects two or
more storeys.

STOREY: That portion of a building included between the upper surface of a
floor and the upper surface of the floor or roof next above.

STOREY, FIRST: The storey having its floor nearest to grade plane.

STRUCTURE: That which is built or constructed, including buildings.

TENANT SPACE: A space or spaces in a building designed or used as the occupancy
unit of a single tenant.

USE: The purpose for which a building or portion thereof is utilized or occupied.

WIND LOAD: The load on a building or structure resulting from wind pressure
or suction.

ZONING: The delineation of areas within which there are regulations governing
the use of buildings and structures, the size of yards and courts, the intensity
of use of lot areas, and the height and bulk of buildings and structures.

SECTION 4: CODE ORGANIZATION AND STRUCTURE
======================================================================

4.1 CODE VOLUMES
The Bangladesh National Building Code 2020 is organized into three volumes:

VOLUME 1 - Administrative and General Requirements
Contains Parts 1 through 5:
- Part 1: Scope and Definitions
- Part 2: Administration and Enforcement
- Part 3: General Building Requirements, Control and Regulation
- Part 4: Fire Protection
- Part 5: Building Materials

VOLUME 2 - Structural Design
Contains Part 6:
- Part 6: Structural Design (13 Chapters covering loads, foundations, and
  structural materials including concrete, masonry, steel, timber, bamboo,
  ferrocement, and composite structures)

VOLUME 3 - Construction and Services
Contains Parts 7 through 10:
- Part 7: Construction Practice and Safety
- Part 8: Building Services (Electrical, HVAC, Plumbing, Elevators, etc.)
- Part 9: Addition, Alteration and Change of Use of Existing Buildings
- Part 10: Signs and Outdoor Display Structures

4.2 TOTAL CODE CONTENT
The complete BNBC 2020 contains:
- 3 Volumes
- 10 Parts
- 49 Chapters
- Over 2000 pages of technical requirements and guidelines

4.3 CODE ADMINISTRATION
4.3.1 Enforcing Authorities
The following authorities are responsible for enforcing BNBC 2020:
- Rajdhani Unnayan Kartripakkha (RAJUK) - Dhaka Metropolitan Area
- City Corporations - Major cities
- Municipalities (Pourashava) - Towns and smaller cities
- Local Government Engineering Department (LGED) - Rural areas

4.3.2 Complementary Regulations
BNBC 2020 works in conjunction with:
- Building Construction Rules 1996
- Fire Prevention and Extinguishing Act 2003
- Fire Prevention and Extinction Rules 2014
- Environment Conservation Act 1995
- Bangladesh Labour Act 2006
- Disaster Management Act 2012

SECTION 5: REFERENCED STANDARDS
======================================================================

5.1 INTERNATIONAL STANDARDS
BNBC 2020 references and incorporates the following international standards:

5.1.1 American Standards
- ASCE 7: Minimum Design Loads for Buildings and Other Structures
- ACI 318: Building Code Requirements for Structural Concrete
- AISC: Steel Construction Manual
- ASTM Standards: Testing materials and construction products
- ASHRAE Standards: HVAC and energy efficiency
- NFPA Codes: Fire protection systems

5.1.2 Other Standards
- ISO Standards: International quality and testing standards
- BS (British Standards): Various construction standards
- Indian Standards (IS): Selected standards adapted for regional conditions

5.2 BANGLADESH STANDARDS (BDS)
5.2.1 BSTI Standards
Bangladesh Standards and Testing Institution (BSTI) standards are mandatory
for the following:
- Cement (BDS EN 197-1)
- Steel reinforcement bars (BDS ISO 6935-2)
- Bricks and blocks (BDS 208, BDS 1788)
- Aggregates (BDS ISO 13055)
- Water for concrete (BDS ISO 12439)
- Bitumen (BDS 1511)
- Paint and protective coatings
- Electrical cables and wiring
- Plumbing fixtures and pipes

5.3 PRECEDENCE OF STANDARDS
Where multiple standards are referenced, the following order of precedence applies:
1. BNBC 2020 specific provisions
2. Bangladesh Standards (BDS/BSTI)
3. Referenced international standards (with local modifications)
4. Professional judgment approved by the Authority Having Jurisdiction

SECTION 6: UPDATES AND REVISIONS
======================================================================

6.1 CODE HISTORY
- BNBC 1993: First edition of Bangladesh National Building Code
- BNBC 2006: First major revision incorporating seismic provisions
- BNBC 2015: Draft version (not officially adopted)
- BNBC 2020: Current edition (Gazetted February 2021)

6.2 KEY CHANGES IN BNBC 2020
Major updates from previous versions include:

6.2.1 Seismic Design
- Updated seismic zone maps
- Revised earthquake acceleration parameters
- Zone 3 areas: 0.28g peak ground acceleration
- Zone 4 areas: 0.36g peak ground acceleration
- Enhanced ductility requirements for reinforced concrete

6.2.2 Wind Load
- Updated wind speed maps
- New provisions for tropical cyclone-prone areas
- Coastal areas: Basic wind speed up to 65 m/s
- Inland areas: Basic wind speed 44-50 m/s

6.2.3 Fire Safety
- Enhanced fire protection requirements
- Mandatory fire service approval for multi-storey buildings
- Improved means of egress provisions
- Updated fire resistance ratings

6.2.4 Accessibility
- Comprehensive provisions for persons with disabilities
- Ramp and elevator requirements
- Accessible toilet facilities
- Tactile paving and signage requirements

6.2.5 Energy Efficiency
- New energy conservation requirements
- Building envelope thermal performance
- HVAC efficiency standards
- Daylighting and natural ventilation provisions

6.2.6 Sustainability
- Green building provisions
- Rainwater harvesting requirements
- Solar energy integration
- Waste management during construction

6.3 PERIODIC REVIEW
The Bangladesh National Building Code shall be reviewed and updated periodically,
with major revisions expected every 5-10 years to incorporate:
- New construction technologies
- Updated safety research
- International best practices
- Lessons learned from disasters
- Changes in climatic conditions
- Advances in building materials

======================================================================
SECTION 7: INTERPRETATION AND APPLICATION
======================================================================

7.1 INTENT OF CODE
7.1.1 Minimum Requirements
This Code establishes minimum requirements for safety, health, and welfare.
Designers and builders are encouraged to exceed these minimum standards.

7.1.2 Performance-Based Approach
Where specific requirements are not provided, alternative solutions may be
approved based on performance criteria that achieve equivalent or superior
safety levels.

7.2 CONFLICTING PROVISIONS
7.2.1 Resolution
When provisions of this Code conflict with each other or with other regulations:
a) The more restrictive provision shall apply
b) The provision more specifically addressing the issue shall apply
c) The Authority Having Jurisdiction shall make final determination

7.2.2 Local Regulations
Local authorities may adopt more stringent requirements than this Code, but
not less stringent requirements.

7.3 INTERPRETATIONS
7.3.1 Official Interpretations
The Housing and Building Research Institute (HBRI) may issue official
interpretations of Code provisions upon written request.

7.3.2 Technical Guidance
HBRI publishes technical guidance documents, commentaries, and design aids
to assist in Code implementation.

======================================================================
SECTION 8: LIABILITY AND RESPONSIBILITY
======================================================================

8.1 DESIGNER RESPONSIBILITY
8.1.1 Professional Liability
Registered design professionals are responsible for ensuring their designs
comply with all provisions of this Code.

8.1.2 Seal and Signature
All construction documents must bear the seal and signature of licensed
professionals:
- Architects for architectural drawings
- Engineers for structural, electrical, and mechanical systems
- Appropriate specialists for specialized systems

8.2 BUILDER RESPONSIBILITY
8.2.1 Construction Quality
Builders and contractors are responsible for:
a) Constructing in accordance with approved plans
b) Using materials meeting Code specifications
c) Following proper construction practices
d) Maintaining site safety

8.3 OWNER RESPONSIBILITY
8.3.1 Maintenance
Building owners are responsible for:
a) Maintaining buildings in accordance with Code requirements
b) Not making unauthorized alterations
c) Ensuring continued compliance during occupancy
d) Obtaining required permits for modifications

8.4 CODE OFFICIAL LIABILITY
Code officials acting in good faith and within their scope of authority shall
not be personally liable for damages arising from Code enforcement activities.
