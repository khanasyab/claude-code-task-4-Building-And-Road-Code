CHAPTER 1: OVERVIEW OF RAJUK
======================================================================

1.1 INTRODUCTION

RAJUK (Rajdhani Unnayan Kartripakkha) is a statutory body under the
Ministry of Housing and Public Works responsible for planning and
developing Dhaka city and its surroundings. In Dhaka city, permission
from RAJUK is required to begin construction work.

RAJUK has the development authority of the capital and approves
building design and plan before the start of any construction project
within its jurisdiction.

1.2 RAJUK JURISDICTION

RAJUK's jurisdiction covers:
- Dhaka North City Corporation
- Dhaka South City Corporation
- Surrounding areas of Dhaka metropolitan region
- Total area: 1,528 square kilometers (590 square miles)

1.3 PRIMARY FUNCTIONS

a) Urban planning and development control
b) Building plan approval and construction permits
c) Land use regulation and zoning enforcement
d) Infrastructure development
e) Planned residential area development
f) Building construction monitoring
g) Enforcement of building codes and regulations

CHAPTER 2: DETAILED AREA PLAN (DAP) 2022-2035
======================================================================

2.1 OVERVIEW OF DAP

The Detailed Area Plan (DAP) 2022-2035 for Dhaka is the primary
planning document that outlines:
- Land use zoning
- Building height restrictions
- Floor Area Ratio (FAR) regulations
- Infrastructure development plans
- Urban development strategies

The DAP covers the entire 1,528 sq km area under RAJUK's
jurisdiction and has been converted into urban residential zones/
residential areas by this comprehensive plan.

2.2 ZONING CATEGORIES

The DAP categorizes various Dhaka neighborhoods according to their
intended use:

RESIDENTIAL ZONES:
- Low-density residential
- Medium-density residential
- High-density residential
- Mixed residential

COMMERCIAL ZONES:
- Local commercial
- Central commercial
- Regional commercial
- Mixed-use commercial

INDUSTRIAL ZONES:
- Light industrial
- Medium industrial
- Heavy industrial
- IT and technology parks

INSTITUTIONAL ZONES:
- Educational institutions
- Healthcare facilities
- Government offices
- Religious institutions

SPECIAL ZONES:
- Agricultural preservation
- Water bodies
- Open spaces and parks
- Heritage areas
- Transport facilities

2.3 FLOOR AREA RATIO (FAR) STANDARDS

The DAP establishes FAR standards across Dhaka:

Only 5 blocks out of 346 will receive the 5-6 FAR standards
10 blocks will receive 4-5 FAR standards
19 blocks will receive 3-4 FAR standards
Remaining blocks will receive 1-3 FAR standards

FAR DEFINITION:
Floor Area Ratio = Total Floor Area of Building / Plot Area

The FAR controls building density and determines how much
construction is permitted on a given plot.

2.4 BUILDING HEIGHT REGULATIONS

Height restrictions are established based on:
- Zone classification
- Plot location
- Airport flight path considerations
- Urban design considerations
- Infrastructure capacity

The plan defines specific height limits for each zone, controlling
building height through:
- Maximum number of floors
- Maximum height in meters
- FAR limitations
- Setback requirements

2.5 LAND USE CLEARANCE EXEMPTIONS

Planned residential areas of RAJUK like Gulshan, Banani, Uttara,
Dhanmondi and other private housing areas that already have RAJUK
approval as residential areas are not subject to land use clearance.

However, for other areas obtaining land use clearance is mandatory
before submitting plans for construction.

CHAPTER 3: PERMIT REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

3.1 TWO MAIN PERMITS REQUIRED

You have to take 2 separate permissions from RAJUK:

PERMIT 1: PLANNING PERMIT
Also called: Land Use Clearance (LUC) or No Objection Certificate (NOC)
Purpose: Confirms proposed use is compatible with zoning

PERMIT 2: CONSTRUCTION PERMIT
Also called: RAJUK Plan Approval
Purpose: Approves detailed building design and construction plans

3.2 WHEN PERMITS ARE REQUIRED

Building permits are required for:
- New construction
- Additions or extensions
- Structural alterations
- Change of occupancy
- Demolition
- Renovations affecting structure
- Installation of major building services

3.3 EXEMPTIONS

Small repairs and maintenance not requiring permits:
- Painting and plastering
- Floor finishing
- Non-structural repairs
- Minor plumbing repairs
- Electrical outlet additions (within existing capacity)

======================================================================
CHAPTER 4: LAND USE CLEARANCE (LUC) PROCESS
======================================================================

4.1 LAND USE CLEARANCE OVERVIEW

The first and essential component is obtaining Land Use Clearance
by submitting an application to the Town Planning Department at
RAJUK.

4.2 LUC APPLICATION REQUIREMENTS

APPLICATION FORM:
Form No. 101 - Land Use Clearance Application Form

REQUIRED DOCUMENTS:
1. Completed application form
2. Land ownership documents:
   - Original deed/title documents
   - CS (Cadastral Survey) records
   - RS (Revisional Survey) records
   - SA (State Acquisition) records
   - BS (Bangladesh Survey) records
   - Mutation/Khatian copies

3. Survey documents:
   - Approved survey plan
   - Plot demarcation plan
   - Location map

4. Utility clearances:
   - WASA connection/NOC (if applicable)
   - Electricity connection feasibility
   - Gas connection feasibility

5. Access verification:
   - Road access confirmation
   - Right of way documentation

6. Payment receipts:
   - LUC application fee
   - Bank draft/online payment receipt

4.3 LUC PROCESSING TIME

Official time for LUC Approval: 30 (Thirty) Days from the date of
submission of the application.

Actual processing time may vary based on:
- Completeness of application
- Complexity of site conditions
- Verification requirements
- Committee meeting schedules

4.4 LUC VALIDITY

Land Use Clearance validity period:
- Typically valid for 1-2 years
- Must be used for construction permit application
- May require renewal if expired

4.5 LUC INSPECTION

RAJUK officials conduct site inspection to verify:
- Plot location and dimensions
- Access availability
- Conformance with DAP zoning
- No encroachment on public land
- No violations of urban planning regulations

CHAPTER 5: CONSTRUCTION PERMIT (PLAN APPROVAL) PROCESS
======================================================================

5.1 CONSTRUCTION PERMIT OVERVIEW

After obtaining Land Use Clearance, the second step is to apply for
Construction Permit through submission of detailed building plans.

5.2 APPLICATION FORM AND FEES

APPLICATION FORM:
Form No. 301 - Application Form of Construction Permit

APPROVAL FEES:
Fees are calculated based on:
- Plot area
- Building area
- Number of floors
- Building type (residential/commercial)
- Location/zone

Fee structure includes:
- Plan scrutiny fee
- Approval fee
- Inspection fee
- Development charges

5.3 REQUIRED DOCUMENTS

GENERAL DOCUMENTS:
1. Application Form (Form-301)
2. Land Use Clearance Copy
3. Bank Draft Copy of Building Approval Fee
4. Soil Test Report (if applicable)
5. FAR Calculation Sheet
6. Plot ownership documents
7. Professional certificates

DRAWING REQUIREMENTS:
Ammonia printed approval drawing sheets in standard sizes (A0 to A4):

1. SITE PLAN (1:500 or 1:200 scale)
   - Plot boundaries and dimensions
   - Adjacent properties and roads
   - North direction
   - Existing structures (if any)
   - Proposed building footprint
   - Setbacks from boundaries
   - Parking layout
   - Drainage connections
   - Utility connections

2. LAYOUT PLAN
   - Plot layout with dimensions
   - Building position
   - Open spaces
   - Landscaping areas
   - Vehicular and pedestrian circulation

3. FLOOR PLANS (All floors)
   - Ground floor plan
   - Typical floor plans
   - Roof plan
   - Basement plan (if applicable)
   - Room dimensions and uses
   - Wall thicknesses
   - Door and window positions
   - Staircase details
   - Toilet layouts
   - Kitchen layouts
   - Balconies and terraces

4. PARKING PLAN
   - Parking space layout
   - Vehicle circulation
   - Number of parking spaces
   - Parking space dimensions (minimum 2.5m x 5.0m)
   - Entry and exit arrangements
   - Ramp details (if multi-level)

5. SECTIONS (Minimum 2)
   - One vertical section
   - One horizontal section
   - Floor heights
   - Ceiling heights
   - Foundation details
   - Staircase sections
   - Natural ground level (NGL)
   - Finished floor level (FFL)

6. ELEVATIONS (All sides)
   - Front elevation
   - Rear elevation
   - Side elevations (both)
   - Architectural features
   - Material indications
   - Window and door details
   - Balcony and projection details

7. STRUCTURAL DRAWINGS
   - Foundation plan
   - Column layout
   - Beam layout
   - Slab details
   - Structural calculations (summary)
   - Load calculations

8. MEP DRAWINGS (If required)
   - Electrical layout
   - Plumbing layout
   - Fire protection system
   - HVAC layout (for commercial/large buildings)

5.4 TECHNICAL CALCULATIONS REQUIRED

FAR CALCULATION:
Total Floor Area / Plot Area = FAR
Must not exceed permissible FAR for the zone

COVERAGE CALCULATION:
Ground floor building area / Plot area Ã— 100 = Coverage %
Must comply with zoning regulations

PARKING REQUIREMENT:
Calculated based on:
- Building use
- Total floor area
- Number of units (residential)
- RAJUK parking standards

Typical requirements:
- Residential: 1 space per apartment
- Commercial: 1 space per 50-100 sqm
- Mixed-use: Combination of above

SETBACK COMPLIANCE:
Front setback: As per DAP (typically 3-6m)
Side setback: As per DAP (typically 3-4.5m)
Rear setback: As per DAP (typically 3-4.5m)

Setbacks vary based on:
- Plot size
- Road width
- Zone classification
- Building height

5.5 COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

Plans must comply with:
1. BNBC 2020 (Bangladesh National Building Code)
2. RAJUK height and coverage rules
3. DAP 2022-2035 provisions
4. FAR regulations
5. Fire safety requirements
6. Structural safety standards
7. Environmental regulations
8. Accessibility requirements

CHAPTER 6: PROFESSIONAL REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

6.1 DESIGN ARCHITECT REQUIREMENTS

Qualifications required:
1. Bachelor of Architecture from IAB Accredited University
2. Member of the Institute of Architects Bangladesh (IAB)
3. Minimum 8 (Eight) years of Experience for Design of any types
   of Building
4. Registered to RAJUK under Legislative of DAP: 2022-2035

Responsibilities:
- Architectural design
- Compliance with building codes
- Coordination with other professionals
- Site supervision certification

6.2 STRUCTURAL ENGINEER REQUIREMENTS

Qualifications required:
1. Member/Fellow of the Institution of Engineers Bangladesh (IEB)
2. Registered to RAJUK as Structural Design Engineer
3. Minimum 8 (Eight) years of Experience in civil Engineering Field
4. Minimum 5 years in Structural Design Field

Responsibilities:
- Structural design and calculations
- Foundation design
- Structural drawings
- Material specifications
- Construction supervision of structural works

6.3 OTHER PROFESSIONALS

ELECTRICAL ENGINEER:
- IEB membership
- Licensed electrical engineer
- Minimum experience as specified

MECHANICAL ENGINEER:
- IEB membership
- HVAC and plumbing design
- Fire protection systems

GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEER:
- Soil investigation and testing
- Foundation recommendations
- Soil test report preparation

6.4 PROFESSIONAL CERTIFICATES

Required certificates to be submitted:
1. Architect's certificate of design
2. Structural engineer's certificate
3. Professional seals and signatures on drawings
4. Registration numbers clearly indicated

CHAPTER 7: APPROVAL PROCESS AND TIMELINE
======================================================================

7.1 SUBMISSION PROCESS

ELECTRONIC CONSTRUCTION PERMITTING SYSTEM (ECPS):
RAJUK has fully digitized the construction permit process through
its Electronic Construction Permitting System (ECPS), available at
rajuk.ecps.gov.bd, making the approval process faster and more
transparent.

ONLINE SUBMISSION:
1. Register on ECPS portal
2. Fill online application form
3. Upload required documents
4. Upload drawings in PDF format
5. Pay fees online
6. Submit application
7. Receive application reference number

HARD COPY SUBMISSION:
Physical submission at RAJUK office:
1. Print application from ECPS
2. Attach all documents and drawings
3. Submit at designated counter
4. Receive acknowledgment receipt

7.2 REVIEW PROCESS

INITIAL SCRUTINY:
- Document verification
- Completeness check
- Preliminary technical review
- Deficiency notice (if any)

TECHNICAL REVIEW:
- Zoning compliance check
- Building code compliance
- Structural adequacy review
- Fire safety review
- MEP systems review

SITE INSPECTION:
RAJUK officials conduct site inspection to verify:
- Plot location and boundaries
- Access roads
- Existing conditions
- Compliance with submitted plans

COMMITTEE REVIEW:
General concern building construction committee approves the design.
Committee members review:
- Technical reports
- Inspection reports
- Professional certifications
- Compliance with regulations

7.3 APPROVAL TIMELINE

REGULAR PROJECTS:
Approval time: Within 45 days of submission

Timeline may vary depending on:
- Frequency of committee meetings
- Completeness of application
- Complexity of project
- Deficiency corrections

SPECIAL PROJECTS:
Projects qualify as special projects if they encompass:
- Residential buildings featuring more than 40 (forty) Apartments
- Gross floor areas exceeding 7,500 sqm
- Shopping malls with floor areas exceeding 5,000 sqm

Special projects require additional review and clearances, which may
extend approval time.

7.4 APPROVAL VALIDITY

The Approved Plan is valid for 3 (Three) years from the date of
approval.

VALIDITY REQUIREMENTS:
You should complete the construction up to the plinth level within
this validity period.

RENEWAL:
If construction does not commence or plinth level is not reached
within validity period:
- Renewal application required
- Updated documents may be needed
- Renewal fee applicable
- Review of current regulations

CHAPTER 8: SPECIAL PROJECT REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

8.1 HIGH-RISE BUILDINGS

If the building to be constructed is more than 10 storied or above
33 meters in height, then Departmental Clearance from following 10
authorities are required:

1. RAJUK (Main approval authority)
2. Fire Service and Civil Defence (FSCD) - Fire NOC
3. Department of Environment (DOE) - Environmental Clearance
4. WASA (Dhaka Water Supply and Sewerage Authority)
5. DESA/DESCO/DPDC (Electricity supply authorities)
6. Titas Gas Transmission & Distribution Company
7. Civil Aviation Authority (if near airport)
8. Archaeological Department (if in heritage area)
9. City Corporation (Building construction committee)
10. Relevant Ward Commissioner

8.2 COMMERCIAL COMPLEXES

Shopping malls and commercial complexes require:
- Enhanced fire safety provisions
- Adequate parking facilities
- Traffic impact assessment
- Utility capacity verification
- Multiple exit arrangements
- Fire service vehicle access

8.3 INDUSTRIAL BUILDINGS

Additional requirements:
- Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA)
- Pollution control measures
- Industrial safety provisions
- Effluent treatment facilities
- DOE clearance

8.4 INSTITUTIONAL BUILDINGS

Educational institutions, hospitals, etc., require:
- Specific occupancy-based requirements
- Enhanced accessibility provisions
- Fire safety standards
- Emergency evacuation plans

CHAPTER 9: CONSTRUCTION PHASE REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

9.1 COMMENCEMENT OF CONSTRUCTION

BEFORE STARTING CONSTRUCTION:
1. Obtain approved building plans
2. Display approval notice at site
3. Appoint licensed professionals
4. Arrange site supervision
5. Notify RAJUK of construction start

SITE REQUIREMENTS:
- Display board showing:
  * Project details
  * Approval information
  * Professional details
  * Expected completion date
  * Safety measures

9.2 CONSTRUCTION INSPECTIONS

RAJUK conducts periodic inspections during:

FOUNDATION STAGE:
- Excavation depth verification
- Foundation dimensions
- Reinforcement inspection
- Concrete quality

PLINTH LEVEL:
- Building position and dimensions
- Setback compliance
- Plinth height
- Drainage arrangements

SUPERSTRUCTURE:
- Floor heights
- Structural elements
- Compliance with approved plans

COMPLETION STAGE:
- Final dimensions
- Building height
- Overall compliance
- Finishing standards

9.3 DEVIATIONS AND MODIFICATIONS

MINOR MODIFICATIONS:
- May be allowed on site
- Must be recorded
- Should not violate major parameters

MAJOR MODIFICATIONS:
- Revised plan submission required
- Additional approval needed
- Fees applicable
- Same review process

PROHIBITED MODIFICATIONS:
- Increasing floors beyond approval
- Exceeding FAR
- Reducing setbacks
- Changing building use
- Structural system changes (without approval)

CHAPTER 10: OCCUPANCY CERTIFICATE
======================================================================

10.1 COMPLETION CERTIFICATE

After construction completion, occupancy certificate required before
building use.

10.2 OCCUPANCY CERTIFICATE REQUIREMENTS

DOCUMENTS REQUIRED:
1. Application for occupancy certificate
2. Approved building plans
3. Completion certificate from architect
4. Structural completion certificate from engineer
5. Utilities connection completion:
   - Electricity connection
   - Water connection
   - Gas connection (if applicable)
   - Sewerage connection
6. Fire safety completion certificate from FSCD
7. Photographs of completed building

10.3 FINAL INSPECTION

RAJUK conducts final inspection verifying:
- Compliance with approved plans
- All systems operational
- Fire safety compliance
- Structural safety verification
- Access and egress arrangements
- Parking facilities completion

10.4 CERTIFICATE ISSUANCE

After satisfactory inspection:
- Occupancy Certificate issued
- Building legal for occupation
- Utilities can be permanently connected
- Trade license can be obtained

CHAPTER 11: VIOLATIONS AND PENALTIES
======================================================================

11.1 COMMON VIOLATIONS

- Construction without approval
- Deviation from approved plans
- Unauthorized additional floors
- FAR violations
- Setback encroachment
- Unauthorized change of use
- Construction during stop-work order

11.2 ENFORCEMENT ACTIONS

NOTICES:
- Show cause notice
- Stop work order
- Compliance notice
- Demolition notice

PENALTIES:
- Fines and monetary penalties
- Permit suspension or revocation
- Forced demolition of violations
- Legal action
- Criminal prosecution (serious violations)

11.3 REGULARIZATION

In some cases, violations may be regularized:
- Payment of penalties
- Submission of revised plans
- Compliance with current regulations
- Structural safety verification

CHAPTER 15: PRACTICAL TIPS FOR APPLICANTS
======================================================================

15.1 PRE-APPLICATION PREPARATION

1. Verify plot documents thoroughly
2. Check zoning and DAP regulations
3. Engage licensed professionals early
4. Conduct soil investigation
5. Assess utility availability
6. Confirm road access

15.2 DOCUMENT PREPARATION

1. Ensure all documents are complete
2. Use standard drawing formats
3. Professional seals on all drawings
4. Clear and legible drawings
5. Accurate calculations
6. Proper signatures and dates

15.3 AVOIDING DELAYS

1. Submit complete applications
2. Respond promptly to deficiency notices
3. Attend site inspections as scheduled
4. Maintain communication with RAJUK
5. Keep copies of all submissions
6. Follow up regularly but professionally

15.4 PROFESSIONAL ADVICE

- Engage experienced architects and engineers
- Consult with professionals familiar with RAJUK procedures
- Consider hiring processing agents for complex projects
- Attend RAJUK help desk for clarifications
