CHAPTER 1: OVERVIEW OF LGED
======================================================================

1.1 LGED MANDATE

The Local Government Engineering Department (LGED) is responsible
for planning, design, construction, and maintenance of rural
infrastructure in Bangladesh.

PRIMARY RESPONSIBILITIES:
- Upazila roads (sub-district roads)
- Union roads
- Village roads
- Rural bridges and culverts
- Rural markets
- Growth centers
- Community buildings
- Union Parishad complexes
- Water supply and sanitation facilities

1.2 ROAD CLASSIFICATION AND LGED JURISDICTION

BANGLADESH ROAD NETWORK:

RHD (Roads and Highways Department):
- National highways
- Regional highways
- Zila roads (district roads)
- Total: Approximately 21,500 km

LGED (Local Government Engineering Department):
- Upazila roads: Approximately 38,000 km
- Union roads: Approximately 75,000 km
- Village roads: Approximately 250,000 km
- Total: Approximately 363,000 km

SUMMARY:
The RHD and the LGED share responsibilities for Bangladesh's road
network, with RHD managing National, Regional and Zila Roads, while
LGED focuses on Upazila Roads, Union Roads and Village Roads.

2.2 ROAD CLASSIFICATION SYSTEM

LGED ROAD CATEGORIES:

UPAZILA ROADS:
Function: Connect upazila headquarters with important centers
Traffic: Moderate to high (for rural areas)
Standard: Higher geometric and structural standards

UNION ROADS:
Function: Connect unions with upazila roads
Traffic: Low to moderate
Standard: Intermediate standards

VILLAGE ROADS:
Function: Provide access to villages
Traffic: Low
Standard: Basic standards, all-weather access

2.3 DESIGN TYPES BASED ON TRAFFIC

Six standard design types outlined:

DESIGN TYPES 5-8:
- Based primarily on forecasts/survey of commercial vehicles
- Higher traffic volumes
- Better structural standards
- Wider pavements

DESIGN TYPES 3 AND 4:
- Based primarily on forecasts of peak hour passenger car units
  (pcu's)
- Lower traffic volumes
- Basic structural standards
- Narrower pavements

2.4 GEOMETRIC DESIGN STANDARDS

DESIGN SPEED:
Based on road classification and terrain:
- Upazila roads (plain terrain): 60-80 km/h
- Union roads: 40-60 km/h
- Village roads: 30-50 km/h

CARRIAGEWAY WIDTHS:

3.7m WIDE CARRIAGEWAY:
- Standard single lane carriageway width
- Suitable for more lightly-trafficked Feeder Roads
- Two-way traffic with passing places

5.5m WIDE CARRIAGEWAY:
- Minimum width two-lane carriageway
- Used for low to moderate traffic
- Standard for many union roads

7.0m WIDE CARRIAGEWAY:
- Full two-lane carriageway
- Higher traffic volumes
- Upazila roads standard

7.3m WIDE CARRIAGEWAY:
- Enhanced two-lane
- Important upazila roads

SHOULDER WIDTHS:

Earthen Shoulders:
- Upazila roads: 1.0-1.5m each side
- Union roads: 0.75-1.0m each side
- Village roads: 0.5m each side

Paved Shoulders:
- Important routes: 0.5-1.0m
- Provides edge support
- Enhances safety

2.5 HORIZONTAL ALIGNMENT

MINIMUM RADIUS:
Based on design speed:
- 80 km/h: 200-250m
- 60 km/h: 120-150m
- 50 km/h: 80-100m
- 40 km/h: 50-60m

WIDENING ON CURVES:
For Design types 6, 7, and 8, a widened paved carriageway up to
5.5m width with required super-elevation should be provided at all
turning points depending on radius of horizontal curves to prevent
hazards of accidents.

All existing curve points on narrower roads with carriageway up to
3.7m should be widened to 5.5m with proper super-elevation.

SUPERELEVATION:
Maximum rate:
- 7% (normal conditions)
- 10% (exceptional cases)

2.6 VERTICAL ALIGNMENT

MAXIMUM GRADIENTS:
Plain terrain:
- Upazila roads: 3-4%
- Union roads: 4-5%
- Village roads: 5-6%

Rolling terrain:
- Up to 6-7% with reduced design speed

MINIMUM GRADIENTS:
- 0.5% for drainage
- Flatter acceptable if proper drainage provided

SIGHT DISTANCE:
Stopping sight distance:
- 80 km/h: 120m
- 60 km/h: 80m
- 50 km/h: 60m
- 40 km/h: 45m

2.7 EMBANKMENT DESIGN

EMBANKMENT CHARACTERISTICS:

Rural roads in Bangladesh are typically on high embankments due to:
- Flooding considerations
- High water table
- Poor soil conditions
- Agricultural land preservation
- Drainage requirements

EMBANKMENT HEIGHT:
- Above highest flood level
- Freeboard: 0.3-0.6m minimum
- Settlement allowance: 10-15% of height

SIDE SLOPES:
The standard side slope for embankments is 2 horizontal and 1
vertical (2:1).

Special conditions:
- Weak soils: 3:1 or flatter
- Canal banks: 1.5:1 to 2:1
- Bridge approaches: Protected with revetment

SLOPE PROTECTION:
- Turfing (grass planting)
- Revetment (brick/concrete) for critical sections
- Geotextiles for weak soils

2.8 DRAINAGE DESIGN

SIDE DRAINS:
Purpose: Intercept surface water, lower water table
Dimensions:
- Depth: 0.6-1.0m below formation level
- Side slopes: 2:1 or flatter
- Grass or concrete lining

CROSS DRAINAGE:
Culverts:
- Box culverts (RCC)
- Pipe culverts (RCC, steel)
- Slab culverts
- Sizes: Hydraulic design based on catchment

Bridges:
- Span > 6m typically classified as bridge
- Various types based on span and load

CHAPTER 3: PAVEMENT DESIGN STANDARDS
======================================================================

3.1 PAVEMENT DESIGN MANUAL

"LGED Road Pavement Design Manual (1999)":
Comprehensive guide for pavement design

KEY PRINCIPLES:
- Traffic-based design
- Subgrade strength consideration
- Climatic factors
- Material availability
- Life-cycle cost analysis

3.2 PAVEMENT TYPES

FLEXIBLE PAVEMENTS:
Most common for LGED roads

Typical Structure:
- Bituminous surface layer
- Granular base course
- Granular sub-base course
- Subgrade

SEMI-RIGID PAVEMENTS:
Water Bound Macadam (WBM):

LGED is favoring WBM/AS (Asphaltic Surface) at present and in order
to protect the investment, the same is being covered with a
bituminous surface layer.

The wearing surface may vary in thickness from 25mm or even less in
the case of Bituminous Surface Treatment to 150mm or more in the
case of asphalt concrete used for heavy-duty traffic.

RIGID PAVEMENTS:
- Concrete pavement
- Limited use in rural areas
- Special applications

3.3 TRAFFIC ANALYSIS

COMMERCIAL VEHICLE COUNT:
- 7-day traffic survey
- Classification of vehicles
- Conversion to equivalent standard axles

GROWTH RATE:
- 5-7% typical for rural roads
- Based on economic development projections

DESIGN PERIOD:
- 15-20 years typical
- Depends on road classification

3.4 SUBGRADE DESIGN

SUBGRADE PREPARATION:
- Remove unsuitable material
- Compaction: 95% Standard Proctor minimum
- Minimum thickness: 300mm compacted

CBR TESTING:
- California Bearing Ratio test
- In-situ or laboratory
- Design CBR: Based on soaked condition

TYPICAL CBR VALUES:
- Poor subgrade: CBR 2-3%
- Fair subgrade: CBR 3-5%
- Good subgrade: CBR 5-10%
- Excellent subgrade: CBR >10%

SUBGRADE IMPROVEMENT:
For weak subgrades (CBR <2%):
- Lime stabilization
- Cement stabilization
- Soil replacement
- Geotextile reinforcement

3.5 PAVEMENT LAYER DESIGN

SUB-BASE COURSE:
Material:
- Crushed stone or well-graded gravel
- Maximum size: 75mm
- CBR: Minimum 25%
- Plasticity Index: Maximum 6%

Thickness:
- Based on design charts
- Typical: 100-200mm

Compaction:
- 95% Standard Proctor
- Layer thickness: Maximum 150mm loose

BASE COURSE:
Material:
- Higher quality than sub-base
- Maximum size: 50mm
- CBR: Minimum 80%
- Aggregate Crushing Value: <30%

Thickness:
- Based on traffic and subgrade
- Typical: 100-200mm

Compaction:
- 98% Standard Proctor (or 100% for high traffic)
- Layer thickness: Maximum 100mm loose

WEARING COURSE:

Bituminous Surface Treatment (BST):
- Single or double surface dressing
- Thickness: 12-25mm
- Low to moderate traffic

Asphaltic Concrete (AC):
- Dense-graded hot mix
- Thickness: 25-75mm
- Higher traffic volumes

3.6 WBM (WATER BOUND MACADAM)

CONSTRUCTION:
- Layer of coarse aggregate (63-90mm)
- Layer of binding aggregate (10-20mm)
- Screening material
- Water application and rolling
- Bituminous surface layer

THICKNESS:
- 75-150mm depending on traffic

ADVANTAGES:
- Lower cost than full-depth bituminous
- Uses local materials
- Good load distribution

MAINTENANCE:
- Regular surface maintenance
- Re-carpeting every 5-7 years

3.7 PAVEMENT MAINTENANCE

ROUTINE MAINTENANCE:
- Pothole patching
- Edge repairs
- Crack sealing
- Vegetation control
- Drainage cleaning

PERIODIC MAINTENANCE:
- Surface resealing
- Thin overlays
- Shoulder repairs

OVERLAYING:
For bituminous carpeting, overlaying of 25-40mm BC (Bituminous
Concrete) will be required after every 7-8 years to maintain
pavement serviceability.

CHAPTER 4: BRIDGE AND CULVERT DESIGN

4.1 BRIDGE DESIGN STANDARDS

LGED BRIDGE DESIGN MANUAL:
Comprehensive standards for rural bridges

DESIGN STANDARDS:
Web Copy document: "Part A_Final.pdf" (2008)
Contains detailed bridge design guidelines

4.2 DESIGN LOADS

VEHICLE LOADS:
- IRC Class A loading (adapted)
- Alternative: AASHTO HS loading
- Local vehicle surveys

PEDESTRIAN LOADS:
- 4 kN/m² on footpaths

SEISMIC LOADS:
- Per BNBC seismic zones
- Importance factor for bridges
- Seismic detailing requirements

4.3 BRIDGE TYPES

COMMON TYPES FOR LGED:

Small Spans (up to 10m):
- RCC slab bridges
- Precast slab bridges
- Pipe culvert bridges

Medium Spans (10-25m):
- RCC beam and slab bridges
- Precast girder bridges
- Composite bridges

Long Spans (25m+):
- Post-tensioned girders
- Steel girder bridges
- Box girder bridges

4.4 FOUNDATION DESIGN

FOUNDATION TYPES:

Shallow Foundations:
- Strip footings
- Isolated footings
- Combined footings
- Mat foundations

Deep Foundations:
- Pile foundations (most common)
  * Cast-in-place piles
  * Precast piles
  * Bored piles
- Well foundations

PILE TYPES:
- RCC piles: 250mm, 300mm, 350mm square
- Length: Based on soil investigation
- Capacity: Based on load tests or formula

4.5 CULVERT DESIGN

TYPES:
- Box culverts (single or multiple cells)
- Pipe culverts (RCC or steel)
- Slab culverts

HYDRAULIC DESIGN:
- Catchment area determination
- Design discharge calculation
- Waterway determination
- Headwater analysis

STRUCTURAL DESIGN:
- Load calculations (earth pressure, live load)
- Reinforcement design
- Detailing per standards

5.3 CONSTRUCTION STANDARDS

MATERIALS:
- Conform to BDS standards
- Cement, aggregates, steel as per specifications
- Brick: First class bricks
- Timber: Specified species and grades

FOUNDATIONS:
- Brick masonry or RCC
- Depth: Below frost line (where applicable)
- Minimum 1.2m depth typical
- Plinth: Minimum 0.5m above ground

WALLS:
Brick Masonry:
- Thickness: 125mm (5"), 250mm (10")
- Mortar: Cement sand (1:4 or 1:6)
- English bond preferred
- Plaster: 12mm cement sand (1:4)

ROOFING:
- RCC slab (most common)
- Corrugated iron sheets (CI sheets)
- Timber trusses with CI sheets
- Minimum slope for drainage

FLOORS:
- Compacted earth (basic)
- Brick flat soling
- RCC slab
- Floor finish: Tiles, cement plaster

5.4 EARTHQUAKE RESISTANCE

SEISMIC DESIGN:
- Per BNBC requirements
- Seismic zone consideration
- Proper detailing
- Connection details critical

SPECIAL MEASURES:
- Band beams at lintel and eaves level
- Proper reinforcement lap lengths
- Confined masonry construction
- Quality control

6.1 QUALITY CONTROL SYSTEM

LGED QUALITY CONTROL:
- Three-tier system
- Field level
- District level
- Central level

RESPONSIBILITIES:
- Material testing
- Construction supervision
- Compliance verification
- Documentation

6.2 MATERIAL TESTING

TESTING REQUIREMENTS:

Soil:
- Moisture content
- Compaction (sand cone, nuclear densometer)
- CBR
- Gradation

Aggregates:
- Gradation
- Crushing value
- Flakiness and elongation
- Specific gravity

Bitumen:
- Penetration
- Softening point
- Ductility

Concrete:
- Slump test
- Compressive strength (cubes)
- Water-cement ratio

Steel:
- Tensile test
- Bend test

6.3 TESTING FREQUENCY

SPECIFIED IN CONTRACTS:
- Soil compaction: Every 100m, every layer
- Aggregate: Every 500 tonnes
- Bitumen: Every tanker
- Concrete: Every 50 m³ or day's work
- Steel: Every 50 tonnes

6.4 FIELD QUALITY CONTROL

CONSTRUCTION SUPERVISION:
- Daily inspection by site engineer
- Periodic inspection by senior engineers
- Quality control team visits

DOCUMENTATION:
- Daily work reports
- Test reports
- Progress photographs
- As-built drawings
