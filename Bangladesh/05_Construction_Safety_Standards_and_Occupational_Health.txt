CHAPTER 1: LEGAL AND REGULATORY FRAMEWORK
======================================================================

1.1 BANGLADESH LABOUR ACT 2006

The Bangladesh Labour Act (2006, amended in 2013 and 2018) is the
primary legal framework outlining employer and employee
responsibilities and mandating specific safety measures in various
industries including construction.

KEY PROVISIONS FOR CONSTRUCTION:
- Chapter V: Health and Hygiene
- Chapter VI: Safety
- Chapter VII: Working Hours and Leave
- Chapter VIII: Employment of Women and Young Persons
- Chapter IX: Wages

APPLICABILITY:
Applies to all establishments employing workers, including:
- Construction sites
- Building projects
- Infrastructure developments
- Renovation and demolition works

1.2 BANGLADESH LABOUR RULES 2015

The Bangladesh Labour Rules 2015 provide detailed rules and
procedures to implement the provisions of the Bangladesh Labour Act.

These rules specify:
- Safety requirements details
- Inspection procedures
- Documentation requirements
- Penalty provisions
- Compliance mechanisms

1.3 NATIONAL OCCUPATIONAL SAFETY AND HEALTH POLICY 2013

The National Occupational Safety and Health Policy 2013 sets a
national framework for:
- Improving health and safety standards
- Promoting awareness
- Enhancing regulatory capacities
- Fostering safety culture
- Protecting workers' rights

POLICY OBJECTIVES:
a) Prevent occupational accidents and diseases
b) Promote safe and healthy working environment
c) Establish national OSH management system
d) Enhance enforcement capacity
e) Promote workers' participation
f) Build awareness and competency

1.4 BNBC 2020 SAFETY PROVISIONS

The Bangladesh National Building Code (BNBC) 2020 includes:
- Fire safety requirements
- Structural safety standards
- Occupational safety regulations for construction sector
- Emergency egress provisions
- Safety equipment specifications

CHAPTER 2: ALARMING STATISTICS AND SAFETY CHALLENGES
======================================================================

2.1 CONSTRUCTION INDUSTRY SAFETY RECORD

Although construction workers comprise 4.4 percent of the total
labour force in Bangladesh, the number of deaths in the construction
industry constitutes 16 percent of total occupational deaths.

This disproportionate death rate highlights the severe safety
challenges in the Bangladesh construction sector.

2.2 COMMON ACCIDENTS IN CONSTRUCTION

ACCIDENT TYPES AND FREQUENCIES:

Falls from Heights: 39%
- Most common cause of fatalities
- Falls from scaffolding
- Falls through openings
- Falls from ladders
- Roof edge falls

Electrocution: 32%
- Contact with overhead lines
- Faulty electrical equipment
- Inadequate grounding
- Temporary electrical installations

Struck by Objects: 15%
- Falling materials
- Swinging loads
- Moving equipment
- Collapsing structures

Caught Between/Crushing: 8%
- Equipment operation
- Structural collapse
- Material handling

Others: 6%
- Drowning
- Suffocation
- Heat stroke
- Chemical exposure

2.3 SAFETY EQUIPMENT DEFICIENCIES

Various studies have shown that in 65 percent of construction sites,
basic safety gears such as gloves, helmets, glasses, safety belts
and first aid kits are not provided.

CRITICAL FINDINGS:
- 87% of workers did not receive health and safety training
- 87% of workers did not wear safety equipment when working
- Majority of sites lack basic safety facilities
- Inadequate first aid provisions
- Insufficient safety supervision

2.4 ROOT CAUSES OF POOR SAFETY

SYSTEMIC ISSUES:
- Lack of safety awareness
- Cost-cutting pressures
- Inadequate enforcement
- Limited training opportunities
- Poor safety culture
- Absence of safety management systems
- Informal employment practices

WORKER VULNERABILITIES:
- Lack of training
- Poverty and desperation
- Fear of job loss
- Limited education
- Migrant workers exploitation
- No bargaining power

CHAPTER 3: EMPLOYER RESPONSIBILITIES
======================================================================

3.1 GENERAL SAFETY OBLIGATIONS

Under the Bangladesh Labour Act, employers must:

a) PROVIDE SAFE WORKPLACE:
   - Identify and assess hazards
   - Implement control measures
   - Maintain safe working conditions
   - Regular safety inspections

b) SAFETY EQUIPMENT AND DEVICES:
   - Provide necessary safety equipment
   - Maintain equipment in working condition
   - Replace damaged equipment
   - Ensure proper use

c) WORKER TRAINING:
   - Safety orientation for new workers
   - Job-specific safety training
   - Equipment operation training
   - Emergency response training

d) SUPERVISION:
   - Adequate supervision of work
   - Competent supervisors
   - Safety monitoring
   - Compliance verification

e) HEALTH FACILITIES:
   - First aid facilities
   - Medical examination (where required)
   - Sanitation facilities
   - Drinking water
   - Washing facilities

f) INFORMATION AND SIGNAGE:
   - Safety signs and warnings
   - Emergency procedures
   - Hazard communication
   - Safety instructions in local language

3.2 SPECIFIC CONSTRUCTION SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

SCAFFOLDING SAFETY:
- Design and erection by competent persons
- Adequate strength and stability
- Proper anchoring
- Guardrails and toe boards
- Regular inspection
- Load limits clearly marked

EXCAVATION SAFETY:
- Shoring and bracing as needed
- Protection against collapse
- Safe access and egress
- Barrier protection around excavations
- Monitoring for hazardous atmospheres
- Dewatering provisions

FALL PROTECTION:
- Guardrails at elevated areas
- Safety nets where appropriate
- Personal fall arrest systems
- Covers for floor openings
- Warning lines and barriers
- Ladder safety

ELECTRICAL SAFETY:
- Qualified electricians for installations
- Proper grounding
- GFCI protection
- Safe distances from overhead lines
- Lockout/tagout procedures
- Regular testing and inspection

MATERIAL HANDLING:
- Proper lifting techniques training
- Mechanical aids where feasible
- Clear pathways
- Secure storage
- Prevention of falling objects

EQUIPMENT SAFETY:
- Operator training and certification
- Regular maintenance
- Safety devices functional
- Operating procedures
- Inspection before use

3.3 WORKING HOURS AND REST

NORMAL WORKING HOURS:
- Maximum 8 hours per day
- Maximum 48 hours per week
- Intervals for rest
- Weekly holiday

OVERTIME:
- Maximum 2 hours overtime per day
- Prior consent of worker required
- Overtime compensation (double rate)
- Register maintenance

NIGHT WORK:
- Additional compensation
- Health considerations
- Safety lighting requirements

3.4 EMPLOYMENT OF WOMEN

SPECIAL PROTECTIONS:
- No work between 8 PM and 6 AM (without permission)
- Maternity leave provisions
- Protection from harassment
- Safe working conditions
- Separate sanitary facilities

3.5 EMPLOYMENT OF YOUNG PERSONS

RESTRICTIONS:
- Minimum age: 14 years
- Prohibited in hazardous work if under 18
- Maximum working hours reduced
- Medical fitness certificate required
- Education provisions

HAZARDOUS WORK PROHIBITION:
Young persons under 18 not employed in:
- Work at dangerous heights
- Heavy lifting
- Exposure to toxic substances
- Operation of dangerous machinery

CHAPTER 4: PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE)
======================================================================

4.1 MANDATORY PPE REQUIREMENTS

HEAD PROTECTION:
Hard Hats/Safety Helmets:
- Required in all construction areas
- Protection from falling objects
- Proper fit and adjustment
- Regular inspection and replacement
- Standards: ANSI Z89.1 or equivalent

4.2 EYE AND FACE PROTECTION

Safety Glasses:
- Protection from flying particles
- Dust and chemical splash protection
- Side shields where needed

Safety Goggles:
- Welding operations
- Chemical handling
- Grinding operations

Face Shields:
- Additional protection for face
- Used with safety glasses
- Welding and grinding

4.3 HAND PROTECTION

Work Gloves:
- General construction work
- Material handling
- Protection from cuts and abrasions

Chemical-Resistant Gloves:
- Handling chemicals and solvents
- Concrete work

Electrical Gloves:
- Live electrical work
- Proper voltage rating

4.4 FOOT PROTECTION

Safety Boots:
- Steel toe caps (200 joules impact resistance)
- Puncture-resistant soles
- Slip-resistant
- Ankle support

Special Purpose Footwear:
- Electrical hazard rated
- Chemical resistant
- Heat resistant

4.5 FALL PROTECTION

Safety Harnesses:
- Full body harness required for work above 1.8m
- Proper fitting and adjustment
- Regular inspection

Lanyards and Lifelines:
- Energy absorbing lanyards
- Adequate length
- Proper anchorage (minimum 2,300 kg strength)

4.6 RESPIRATORY PROTECTION

Dust Masks:
- Protection from construction dust
- Proper filtration rating
- Fit testing

Respirators:
- Chemical exposure
- Confined spaces
- Spray operations
- Medical clearance required

4.7 HEARING PROTECTION

Ear Plugs/Ear Muffs:
- Required when noise exceeds 85 dBA
- Proper insertion and fit
- Regular replacement

4.8 HIGH-VISIBILITY CLOTHING

Reflective Vests:
- Required in areas with vehicle traffic
- Night work
- Adequate retroreflectivity

4.9 PPE MANAGEMENT

EMPLOYER RESPONSIBILITIES:
- Provide PPE at no cost to workers
- Select appropriate PPE for hazards
- Train workers in proper use
- Maintain and replace PPE
- Enforce PPE use

WORKER RESPONSIBILITIES:
- Wear PPE as required
- Proper use and maintenance
- Report damaged PPE
- Attend training

CHAPTER 6: SPECIFIC HAZARD CONTROL
======================================================================

6.1 FALL PREVENTION AND PROTECTION

WORK AT HEIGHT:
Any work where a person could fall and injure themselves requires
protection.

HIERARCHY OF FALL PROTECTION:
1. Eliminate work at height (design)
2. Prevent falls (guardrails, platforms)
3. Mitigate falls (nets, airbags)
4. Personal fall arrest (harnesses)

GUARDRAIL SYSTEMS:
- Top rail: 1.0m height minimum
- Mid-rail at 0.5m
- Toe board: 150mm minimum
- Capable of withstanding 1 kN horizontal force

SCAFFOLDING:
- Design by competent person
- Erected by trained workers
- Inspection before use and regularly
- Tag system (green/red)
- Guardrails on all working platforms
- Access ladder or stairway
- Maximum gaps in platform: 25mm

LADDER SAFETY:
- Proper angle (4:1 ratio)
- Extend 1m above landing
- Secured top and bottom
- Three points of contact
- Appropriate duty rating
- Regular inspection

6.2 EXCAVATION SAFETY

BEFORE EXCAVATION:
- Locate underground utilities
- Obtain permits
- Design shoring system
- Plan emergency response

DURING EXCAVATION:
- Daily competent person inspection
- Protective systems in place
- Safe access/egress (within 7.6m)
- No work under raised loads
- Protection from water accumulation
- Barriers around excavation

SHORING AND SUPPORT:
Required when depth exceeds 1.5m or unstable soil:
- Timber shoring
- Hydraulic shores
- Trench boxes
- Sloping/benching

6.3 ELECTRICAL SAFETY

OVERHEAD POWER LINES:
Minimum clearances:
- 400V: 3 meters
- 11kV: 4 meters
- 33kV: 5 meters
- 132kV and above: 7 meters

If clearances cannot be maintained:
- Power isolation (preferred)
- Physical barriers
- Goal post structures
- Spotters

TEMPORARY ELECTRICAL:
- GFCI protection on all 220V circuits
- Proper grounding
- Cables protected from damage
- Regular inspection and testing
- Qualified electrician installation

ELECTRICAL WORK:
- Only by licensed electricians
- Lockout/tagout procedures
- Voltage testing before work
- Appropriate PPE
- Insulated tools

6.4 LIFTING AND HOISTING

CRANE OPERATIONS:
- Certified operators only
- Load charts displayed and followed
- Pre-operation inspection
- Rigging by qualified persons
- Exclusion zones established
- Signaler when operator view obstructed

LIFTING EQUIPMENT:
- Certification and testing
- Regular inspection
- Safe working load marked
- Proper maintenance records

MANUAL LIFTING:
- Training in proper techniques
- Team lifting for heavy items
- Mechanical aids where feasible
- Weight limits respected

6.5 CONFINED SPACE ENTRY

CONFINED SPACE DEFINED:
- Limited entry/exit
- Not designed for continuous occupancy
- Examples: tanks, sewers, manholes

ENTRY REQUIREMENTS:
- Entry permit system
- Atmospheric testing
- Ventilation
- Emergency equipment
- Attendant outside
- Rescue plan

6.6 HOT WORK

WELDING, CUTTING, GRINDING:
- Hot work permit
- Fire watch
- Fire extinguisher present
- Combustibles removed or protected
- PPE (welding helmet, gloves, apron)
- Ventilation for fumes

6.7 DEMOLITION

PLANNING REQUIREMENTS:
- Structural engineer survey
- Utilities disconnection
- Public protection
- Dust control
- Asbestos assessment
- Method statement

EXECUTION:
- Top-down approach
- No undermining
- Debris removal
- Water for dust suppression

- Conveniently located

CHAPTER 9: ACCIDENT REPORTING AND INVESTIGATION
======================================================================

9.1 ACCIDENT CLASSIFICATION

FATAL ACCIDENT:
Results in death of worker

MAJOR ACCIDENT:
Results in:
- Amputation
- Loss of sight
- Fracture (other than finger/toe)
- Serious burn
- Permanent disability
- Hospitalization exceeding 7 days

MINOR ACCIDENT:
Requires first aid or medical treatment

NEAR MISS:
Incident with potential to cause injury

9.2 REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

INTERNAL REPORTING:
All accidents and near misses reported to:
- Immediate supervisor
- Safety officer
- Management

EXTERNAL REPORTING:
Fatal and major accidents reported to:
- DIFE (Department of Inspection for Factories and Establishments)
- Within 24 hours
- Written report within 7 days

9.3 ACCIDENT INVESTIGATION

OBJECTIVES:
- Determine root causes
- Prevent recurrence
- Comply with legal requirements
- Learn lessons

INVESTIGATION TEAM:
- Safety officer (lead)
- Supervisor
- Worker representative
- Technical specialist (if needed)

INVESTIGATION PROCESS:
1. Secure scene
2. Gather information
3. Interview witnesses
4. Examine physical evidence
5. Analyze causes
6. Determine root causes
7. Develop recommendations
8. Implement corrective actions
9. Follow up

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
Identify:
- Immediate causes
- Underlying causes
- Root causes
- Systemic factors

9.4 ACCIDENT REGISTER

MAINTENANCE:
- Record all accidents
- Details of injury
- Treatment provided
- Time lost
- Investigation findings
- Corrective actions

RETENTION:
- Maintained for minimum 5 years
- Available for inspection

CHAPTER 11: ENFORCEMENT AND COMPLIANCE
======================================================================

11.1 DEPARTMENT OF INSPECTION FOR FACTORIES AND ESTABLISHMENTS (DIFE)

DIFE is the primary agency responsible for enforcing health and
safety laws in Bangladesh.

FUNCTIONS:
- Workplace inspections
- Enforcement of labour laws
- Investigation of accidents
- Approval of plans (for factories)
- Registration of establishments
- Training and awareness

11.2 DIFE INSPECTIONS

INSPECTION COVERAGE:
Key areas examined:
- Building safety
- Occupational health and safety
- Worker welfare
- Fire safety
- Structural safety

INSPECTION FREQUENCY:
- Regular scheduled inspections
- Complaint-based inspections
- Post-accident investigations
- Follow-up inspections

11.3 CHALLENGES IN ENFORCEMENT

DIFE faces challenges with:
- Limited personnel and resources
- Large number of workplaces
- Informal sector coverage
- Political interference
- Corruption

Result: Difficulties ensuring widespread compliance

OHS regulations are less rigorously enforced in Bangladesh's
informal sector, where a large portion of the workforce is employed.

11.4 PENALTIES FOR VIOLATIONS

UNDER BANGLADESH LABOUR ACT:

Safety Violations:
- Fines: Tk 5,000 to Tk 100,000
- Imprisonment: Up to 6 months
- Both fine and imprisonment

Fatal Accidents (Due to Negligence):
- Enhanced penalties
- Compensation to family
- Possible criminal charges

ADMINISTRATIVE ACTIONS:
- Stop work orders
- Closure of site
- Revocation of licenses
- Prosecution
