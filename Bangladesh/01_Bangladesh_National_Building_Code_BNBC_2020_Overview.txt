CHAPTER 1: SCOPE AND PURPOSE
======================================================================

1.1 GENERAL OVERVIEW

The Bangladesh National Building Code (BNBC) 2020 was gazetted in
February 2021 as a mandatory law for building design and construction
by the Government of the People's Republic of Bangladesh. The code
establishes minimum standards for building design, construction,
materials and occupancy to safeguard life, health and property.

BNBC is a pivotal document that provides guidelines for safe and
efficient construction in Bangladesh, serving as the primary reference
for architects, engineers, builders, and regulatory authorities.

1.2 OBJECTIVES

The code was developed to fulfill the following primary objectives:

a) Safeguard public health, safety, and welfare
b) Establish minimum standards for building design and construction
c) Provide guidelines for structural integrity and stability
d) Address seismic safety in earthquake-prone regions
e) Promote sustainable and energy-efficient construction practices
f) Standardize materials, methods, and quality control procedures
g) Facilitate uniform enforcement across Bangladesh

1.3 APPLICABILITY

The code applies to:
- New building construction
- Alterations and additions to existing buildings
- Change of occupancy
- Rehabilitation and retrofitting
- Demolition of structures
- Building services and installations

The code covers all types of buildings including:
- Residential buildings (single-family and multi-family)
- Commercial buildings
- Industrial structures
- Institutional buildings
- Mixed-use developments
- Special occupancy structures

CHAPTER 3: STRUCTURAL DESIGN STANDARDS
======================================================================

3.1 DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

The BNBC 2020 establishes guidelines for structural design,
emphasizing:
- Working stress design methodology
- Permissible stress limits for concrete and steel
- Strength reduction factors
- Load factors for various load combinations
- Serviceability requirements

3.2 REFERENCE TO INTERNATIONAL STANDARDS

The code closely resembles ASCE 7-05 (American Society of Civil
Engineers) standards. Earthquake load and wind load provisions, if
compared to BNBC 2006, have been substantially revised to reflect:
- Updated seismic hazard maps for Bangladesh
- Improved wind load calculation procedures
- Enhanced design requirements for critical structures

3.3 SEISMIC DESIGN REQUIREMENTS

Bangladesh lies within an active seismic zone. The code addresses
earthquake safety through:

SEISMIC ZONING:
Bangladesh is divided into multiple seismic zones with varying levels
of earthquake risk. The code provides seismic zone factors and design
response spectra for each zone.

DESIGN CATEGORIES:
Buildings are classified into seismic design categories based on:
- Seismic zone
- Occupancy importance factor
- Structural system and irregularities

DETAILING REQUIREMENTS:
Specific detailing requirements for:
- Reinforced concrete frames and shear walls
- Steel moment frames and braced frames
- Masonry structures
- Foundation systems

3.4 WIND LOAD PROVISIONS

Updated wind load requirements include:
- Basic wind speed maps for Bangladesh
- Exposure categories (urban, suburban, open terrain)
- Importance factors for various occupancies
- Wind pressure calculation procedures
- Special provisions for:
  * High-rise buildings
  * Coastal structures
  * Structures with unusual geometry

3.5 LIVE LOAD SPECIFICATIONS

Specific live load values are defined for various occupancies:

RESIDENTIAL BUILDINGS:
- Dwelling rooms: 2.0 kN/m²
- Balconies: 3.0 kN/m²
- Corridors and stairs: 3.0 kN/m²

COMMERCIAL BUILDINGS:
- Offices: 2.5 kN/m²
- Retail spaces: 4.0 kN/m²
- Storage areas: Variable based on use

SPECIAL OCCUPANCIES:
- Assembly areas: 4.8 kN/m²
- Parking garages: 2.5 kN/m² (plus vehicle loads)
- Industrial: As per specific requirements

3.6 MATERIAL SPECIFICATIONS

CONCRETE:
- Minimum concrete strength for structural members: 17.5 MPa
- Standard strengths: 17.5, 20, 25, 30, 35, 40 MPa
- Higher strengths permitted with proper quality control
- Maximum aggregate size: 20mm for normal construction

STEEL REINFORCEMENT:
- Yield strength: Not to exceed 550 N/mm²
- Deformed bars conforming to BDS 1313, ASTM A615, ASTM A706,
  or BS 4461
- Bars with specified yield strength exceeding 410 N/mm² permitted
  if conforming to specifications

STRUCTURAL STEEL:
- Minimum yield strength: 250 MPa
- Common grades: 250, 350, 400 MPa
- Conformance to relevant BDS or international standards

MASONRY:
- Minimum compressive strength of units
- Mortar specifications
- Reinforced and unreinforced masonry provisions

CHAPTER 4: FIRE SAFETY REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

4.1 FIRE PROTECTION OBJECTIVES

The code establishes comprehensive fire safety requirements to:
- Prevent fire initiation and spread
- Provide adequate means of escape
- Facilitate fire service access and operations
- Protect property and minimize fire damage
- Ensure life safety of occupants

4.2 FIRE-RESISTANT CONSTRUCTION

FIRE RESISTANCE RATINGS:
Buildings must use fire-resistant materials with ratings appropriate
to occupancy type and building height:

TYPE I CONSTRUCTION (Fire-resistive):
- Structural frame: 3-4 hours
- Exterior walls: 3-4 hours
- Interior walls: 2-3 hours
- Floor construction: 2-3 hours

TYPE II CONSTRUCTION (Non-combustible):
- Structural frame: 1-2 hours
- Exterior walls: 1-2 hours
- Interior walls: 1 hour
- Floor construction: 1-2 hours

TYPE III CONSTRUCTION (Ordinary):
- Exterior walls: 2 hours
- Interior elements: 1 hour

TYPE IV CONSTRUCTION (Heavy timber):
- Specific minimum member sizes required

TYPE V CONSTRUCTION (Wood frame):
- Limited applications with restrictions

4.3 FIRE DETECTION AND ALARM SYSTEMS

Required installations based on occupancy and building size:

MANUAL FIRE ALARM SYSTEMS:
- Pull stations at exits
- Visual and audible notification devices
- Connection to monitoring station

AUTOMATIC FIRE DETECTION:
Smoke detectors required in:
- Sleeping areas
- Common corridors
- Mechanical rooms
- Other specified locations

Heat detectors required in:
- Kitchens
- Boiler rooms
- Areas unsuitable for smoke detection

4.4 MEANS OF EGRESS

GENERAL REQUIREMENTS:
- Minimum two independent means of egress
- Travel distance limitations
- Exit access, exit, and exit discharge components
- Emergency lighting and signage

STAIRWAY DESIGN:
- Minimum width: 900mm (preferably 1100mm)
- Riser height: Maximum 190mm
- Tread depth: Minimum 250mm
- Handrails required on both sides
- Fire-resistant enclosures for exit stairs

EXIT CAPACITY:
Calculated based on:
- Occupant load
- Width of exit components
- Arrangement and configuration

4.5 FIRE SUPPRESSION SYSTEMS

AUTOMATIC SPRINKLER SYSTEMS:
Required in:
- High-rise buildings (above certain height)
- Large assembly occupancies
- Storage facilities
- Specific hazardous areas

Design standards:
- Conformance to NFPA 13
- Water supply requirements
- Sprinkler head spacing and placement
- Testing and maintenance provisions

FIRE EXTINGUISHERS:
- Portable extinguishers throughout building
- Type and size based on hazard classification
- Maximum travel distance to extinguisher
- Inspection and maintenance requirements

4.6 SPECIAL OCCUPANCY REQUIREMENTS

HIGH-RISE BUILDINGS:
Additional requirements including:
- Pressurized stairwells
- Fire command center
- Emergency power systems
- Fire service access elevators
- Voice communication systems

ASSEMBLY OCCUPANCIES:
- Enhanced egress requirements
- Crowd management provisions
- Panic hardware on exit doors
- Special fire alarm features

CHAPTER 6: ADMINISTRATION AND ENFORCEMENT
======================================================================

6.1 REGULATORY AUTHORITIES

Primary authorities responsible for code enforcement:

RAJUK (RAJDHANI UNNAYAN KARTRIPAKKHA):
- Jurisdiction: Dhaka metropolitan area
- Responsibilities: Plan approval, construction permits, inspections
- Electronic Construction Permitting System (ECPS)

PUBLIC WORKS DEPARTMENT (PWD):
- Government building projects
- Technical specifications and standards
- Quality control and testing

LOCAL GOVERNMENT AUTHORITIES:
- Municipal corporations
- Pourashavas (municipalities)
- Union Parishads (rural areas)

FIRE SERVICE AND CIVIL DEFENCE (FSCD):
- Fire safety plan approval
- Fire NOC (No Objection Certificate)
- Fire safety inspections

6.2 PERMIT PROCESS

REQUIRED PERMITS:
1. Land Use Clearance (Planning Permit/NOC)
2. Construction Permit (Building Plan Approval)

SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS:
- Application forms
- Architectural drawings
- Structural drawings
- MEP (Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing) drawings
- Soil investigation report
- Fire safety plan
- Environmental clearance (if required)
- Professional certifications

REVIEW AND APPROVAL:
- Technical review by authority
- Site inspection
- Committee approval
- Issuance of permit

6.3 PROFESSIONAL REQUIREMENTS

DESIGN ARCHITECTS:
- Bachelor of Architecture from IAB accredited university
- Member of Institute of Architects Bangladesh (IAB)
- Minimum 8 years of experience
- Registered with RAJUK under DAP: 2022-2035

STRUCTURAL ENGINEERS:
- Member/Fellow of Institution of Engineers Bangladesh (IEB)
- Registered with RAJUK as Structural Design Engineer
- Minimum 8 years in civil engineering field
- Minimum 5 years in structural design field

6.4 INSPECTION AND COMPLIANCE

CONSTRUCTION PHASE INSPECTIONS:
- Foundation inspection
- Structural frame inspection
- Rough-in inspections (MEP)
- Final inspection

OCCUPANCY CERTIFICATE:
Required before building use, certifying:
- Compliance with approved plans
- All systems operational
- Fire safety compliance
- Structural safety verification
