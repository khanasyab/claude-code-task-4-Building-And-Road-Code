======================================================================
ELECTRICAL INSTALLATIONS AND ENERGY CODE STANDARDS
BNBC ELECTRICAL REQUIREMENTS AND GRID CODE REGULATIONS
======================================================================

REGULATORY FRAMEWORK
---------------------
Building Code: BNBC 2020 - Part 8, Chapter 1 (Electrical Installation)
              BNBC 2012 - Part 8, Chapter 2 (Electrical Installation)
Grid Code: Bangladesh Energy Regulatory Commission - Electricity
           Grid Code 2019
Energy Standards: ISO 50001 Energy Management System
Reference Standards: IEC (International Electrotechnical Commission)
Regulatory Body: Bangladesh Energy Regulatory Commission (BERC)
Utilities: BPDB, DESCO, DPDC, DESA, WZPDCL, NESCO

======================================================================
CHAPTER 1: BNBC ELECTRICAL INSTALLATION REQUIREMENTS
======================================================================

1.1 SCOPE AND APPLICATION

BNBC sets minimum standards for Electrical and Electronic
Engineering Installations in:
- Residential Buildings
- Multistoried Apartment Buildings
- Commercial Buildings
- Office Buildings
- Rail Stations
- Airport Buildings
- Factory Buildings
- Warehouses
- Jetties
- Container Yards
- Other Yards
- Parking lots
- Similar places

1.2 OBJECTIVES

The aim of the codes is to ensure that Electrical and Electronic
installations in buildings are safe for:
- Persons
- The buildings
- The contents of the buildings

Protection from electrical hazards arising from the use of
electricity.

1.3 REFERENCE STANDARDS

INTERNATIONAL STANDARDS:
The code references International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC)
standards for International Standards for all electrical, electronic
and related technologies as applicable to Bangladesh.

ISO 50001: Energy Management System
Standard for energy management referenced for efficiency and
conservation.

APPLICABLE STANDARDS:
- IEC 60364: Low-voltage electrical installations
- IEC 60446: Identification of conductors
- IEC 60445: Basic and safety principles for man-machine interface
- IEC 60529: Degrees of protection (IP codes)
- IEC 60947: Low-voltage switchgear
- IEC 61140: Protection against electric shock
- Others as applicable

CHAPTER 2: ELECTRICAL SUPPLY SYSTEMS
======================================================================

2.1 UTILITY SUPPLY

ELECTRICITY DISTRIBUTION COMPANIES:

Dhaka:
- DESCO (Dhaka Electric Supply Company) - North Dhaka
- DPDC (Dhaka Power Distribution Company) - South Dhaka
- DESA (Dhaka Electric Supply Authority) - Some areas

Other Areas:
- WZPDCL (West Zone Power Distribution Company Limited)
- NESCO (Northern Electricity Supply Company)
- PBS (Palli Bidyut Samities) - Rural areas

SUPPLY CHARACTERISTICS:
- Voltage: 220/230V single phase, 380/400V three phase
- Frequency: 50 Hz
- System: TN-S or TN-C-S earthing system

2.2 SERVICE CONNECTION

CONNECTION TYPES:

Single Phase:
- Residential: Up to 5 kW
- Small commercial: Up to 10 kW
- 220-230V, 50 Hz

Three Phase:
- Commercial buildings
- Large residences
- Industrial facilities
- 380-400V, 50 Hz

CONNECTION PROCESS:
1. Application to utility
2. Load calculation and approval
3. Payment of connection fees
4. Meter installation
5. Service line installation
6. Energization

2.3 METERING

METER TYPES:
- Single phase energy meter
- Three phase energy meter
- Smart meters (being introduced)

METER LOCATION:
- Accessible to utility
- Protected from weather
- Ground floor or external
- Adequate lighting
- Locked meter box

2.4 SUBSTATION REQUIREMENTS

11KV/0.4KV ELECTRICAL INDOOR SUBSTATION:

According to the rule of the distribution companies of Bangladesh,
11KV/0.4KV Electrical substations shall be required for a building
if the load requirement of the building exceeds 50KW.

BNBC REQUIREMENTS FOR SUBSTATIONS:
- Separate room with fireproof construction
- Adequate ventilation
- Fire extinguishers
- Safety signage
- Access control
- Earthing system
- Cable entry/exit provisions

LOCATION:
- Ground floor preferred
- Easily accessible
- Away from water sources
- Adequate clearances
- Emergency access

SAFETY REQUIREMENTS:
- Interlocking system
- Protection relays
- Circuit breakers
- Lightning arresters
- Earthing and bonding

CHAPTER 3: ELECTRICAL DESIGN AND INSTALLATION
======================================================================

3.1 LOAD CALCULATION

RESIDENTIAL BUILDINGS:

Connected Load Method:
- Lighting: 20-30 W/m² floor area
- Power outlets: 5-10 W/m²
- Air conditioning: As per equipment
- Kitchen equipment: As per appliances
- Water heaters: As per capacity

Demand Factor:
- First 5 kW: 100%
- Next 5 kW: 50%
- Above 10 kW: 30%

COMMERCIAL BUILDINGS:

Office Buildings:
- General lighting: 15-25 W/m²
- Task lighting: Additional 10 W/m²
- Power outlets: 20-30 W/m²
- HVAC: 40-80 W/m² (depends on system)
- Elevators, pumps: As per equipment

Demand Factor:
- Based on occupancy and usage pattern
- Typically 60-80% of connected load

SHOPPING MALLS:
- Retail lighting: 25-40 W/m²
- Display lighting: Additional
- HVAC: 60-100 W/m²
- Elevators, escalators
- Common services

3.2 DISTRIBUTION SYSTEM

MAIN DISTRIBUTION BOARD (MDB):
Location:
- Near service entrance
- Easily accessible
- Protected from weather
- Adequate working space

Components:
- Main circuit breaker
- Outgoing circuit breakers
- Bus bars
- Metering (if required)
- Surge protection
- Earth bar

SUB-DISTRIBUTION BOARDS (SDB):
- Each floor or zone
- Circuit protection
- Isolation capability
- Proper labeling

FINAL DISTRIBUTION BOARDS (FDB):
- Local distribution
- For specific areas/apartments
- Circuit breakers for final circuits

3.3 WIRING SYSTEMS

CONDUCTOR TYPES:

Copper Conductors:
- Standard for building wiring
- Single core or multi-core
- PVC insulated

Aluminum Conductors:
- For large feeders
- Cost-effective for large sizes
- Proper terminations required

CABLE TYPES:

PVC Insulated Cables:
- Most common
- Suitable for indoor use
- Various sizes and cores

XLPE Cables:
- Cross-linked polyethylene insulation
- Higher temperature rating
- Underground and industrial use

Flexible Cords:
- Portable equipment
- Temporary connections
- Appropriate current rating

WIRING METHODS:

Conduit System:
- PVC conduits most common
- Surface or concealed
- Proper size for wire fill
- Inspection and access

Trunking:
- PVC or metal trunking
- Surface mounted
- Commercial/industrial
- Easy modifications

Cable Tray:
- Industrial installations
- Large cable runs
- Support and protection

3.4 CONDUCTOR SIZING

FACTORS CONSIDERED:
- Current carrying capacity
- Voltage drop
- Short circuit capacity
- Mechanical strength
- Installation method

CURRENT CARRYING CAPACITY:
Based on:
- Conductor material (copper/aluminum)
- Insulation type
- Installation method
- Ambient temperature
- Grouping of cables

VOLTAGE DROP LIMITS:
BNBC Requirements:
- Lighting circuits: Maximum 3%
- Power circuits: Maximum 5%
- Total from origin: Maximum 5%

3.5 CIRCUIT PROTECTION

OVERCURRENT PROTECTION:

Circuit Breakers:
- Miniature Circuit Breakers (MCB)
- Molded Case Circuit Breakers (MCCB)
- Air Circuit Breakers (ACB)

MCB Types:
- Type B: 3-5 In (residential)
- Type C: 5-10 In (commercial, motors)
- Type D: 10-20 In (heavy industrial)

Ratings:
- Based on conductor capacity
- Discrimination with upstream devices

EARTH FAULT PROTECTION:

Residual Current Devices (RCD):
- RCCB (without overcurrent protection)
- RCBO (with overcurrent protection)
- Sensitivity: 30 mA (personal protection)
             100-300 mA (fire protection)

Applications:
- Bathrooms and wet areas
- Outdoor circuits
- Socket outlets
- As required by code

SURGE PROTECTION:

Surge Protection Devices (SPD):
- Type 1: Main distribution (lightning)
- Type 2: Sub-distribution
- Type 3: Socket outlets (sensitive equipment)

Installation:
- As close to protected equipment as practical
- Proper earthing essential

3.6 EARTHING AND BONDING

EARTHING SYSTEM:

Purpose:
- Personal safety
- Equipment protection
- Proper operation of protective devices
- Lightning protection

Earth Electrodes:
- Rod electrodes (most common)
- Plate electrodes
- Strip electrodes
- Foundation earth electrodes

Earth Resistance:
- Target: Less than 5 ohms
- Maximum: 10 ohms (per utility requirements)
- Lower for lightning protection

MAIN EARTHING TERMINAL:
- All earth connections converge
- Accessible for testing
- Bonded to structural steel, water pipes, etc.

EQUIPOTENTIAL BONDING:

Main Bonding:
- Service pipe entry (water, gas)
- Structural steel
- Air conditioning systems
- Lightning protection system

Supplementary Bonding:
- Bathrooms and wet areas
- Metal fixtures and fittings
- Reduces touch voltage

CHAPTER 4: LIGHTING DESIGN
======================================================================

4.1 LIGHTING REQUIREMENTS

ILLUMINATION LEVELS:

Residential:
- Living rooms: 100-200 lux
- Bedrooms: 100-150 lux
- Kitchen: 200-300 lux
- Bathrooms: 150-200 lux
- Corridors: 100 lux

Commercial Offices:
- General office: 300-500 lux
- Computer workstations: 500 lux
- Conference rooms: 300-500 lux
- Reception: 200-300 lux

Educational:
- Classrooms: 300-500 lux
- Laboratories: 500 lux
- Libraries: 300-500 lux

Industrial:
- General assembly: 200-500 lux
- Precision work: 500-1000 lux
- Warehouses: 100-200 lux

4.2 LIGHTING TYPES

LAMP TECHNOLOGIES:

LED (Light Emitting Diode):
- Most efficient
- Long life (25,000-50,000 hours)
- Low heat generation
- Instant on
- Becoming standard

Fluorescent:
- T8, T5 tubes
- Compact fluorescent lamps (CFL)
- Good efficiency
- Being replaced by LED

Incandescent:
- Traditional bulbs
- Inefficient
- Being phased out
- Limited applications

HID (High Intensity Discharge):
- Metal halide, high pressure sodium
- High output
- Industrial/street lighting
- Being replaced by LED

4.3 LIGHTING CONTROLS

MANUAL CONTROLS:
- Switches at entry/exit
- Convenient locations
- Appropriate switching

AUTOMATIC CONTROLS:

Occupancy Sensors:
- PIR (Passive Infrared)
- Ultrasonic
- Dual technology
- Energy savings 30-60%

Daylight Sensors:
- Photocells
- Dim or switch based on daylight
- Perimeter zones

Time Controls:
- Time switches
- Astronomical clocks
- Common area lighting

DIMMERS:
- LED compatible dimmers
- Energy savings
- Ambiance control

4.4 EMERGENCY LIGHTING

REQUIREMENTS:

Exit Signs:
- All exit routes
- Clearly visible
- Illuminated or self-luminous
- Battery backup

Emergency Lighting:
- Exit routes and stairs
- Assembly areas
- Large spaces
- Minimum illumination: 5 lux

POWER SUPPLY:
- Central battery system, or
- Self-contained luminaires
- Duration: 90 minutes minimum
- Monthly and annual testing

CHAPTER 7: EMERGENCY AND STANDBY POWER
======================================================================

7.1 EMERGENCY POWER REQUIREMENTS

CRITICAL SYSTEMS REQUIRING EMERGENCY POWER:
- Fire alarm system
- Emergency lighting
- Fire pumps
- Smoke extraction
- Emergency elevators (high-rise)
- Communication systems
- Security systems
- Medical equipment (hospitals)

DURATION:
- Minimum 90 minutes (emergency lighting)
- 4 hours (high-rise buildings)
- Continuous (life safety systems in hospitals)

7.2 STANDBY POWER SYSTEMS

GENERATOR SETS:

Types:
- Diesel generators (most common)
- Gas generators
- Dual fuel generators

Sizing:
- Based on essential load
- Starting current consideration
- Diversity factor
- Voltage regulation

Installation:
- Separate generator room
- Ventilation for cooling and exhaust
- Fuel storage (3-6 hours minimum)
- Noise attenuation
- Vibration isolation

Automatic Transfer:
- Automatic Transfer Switch (ATS)
- Transfer time: 10-15 seconds
- Return to normal
- Testing provisions

UNINTERRUPTIBLE POWER SUPPLY (UPS):

Applications:
- Computer systems
- Data centers
- Critical controls
- Medical equipment

Types:
- Off-line (standby)
- Line-interactive
- On-line (double conversion)

Backup Duration:
- 5-15 minutes (ride-through)
- Longer with battery expansion
- Generator backup for extended outages

7.3 SOLAR POWER SYSTEMS

GRID-TIED SYSTEMS:
- Solar panels (PV arrays)
- Inverters
- Net metering
- Grid connection approval
- BERC regulations

OFF-GRID SYSTEMS:
- Solar panels
- Charge controllers
- Battery storage
- Inverters
- Backup generator

HYBRID SYSTEMS:
- Grid connection with solar
- Battery storage
- Load management
- Optimization

DESIGN CONSIDERATIONS:
- Roof orientation and tilt
- Shading analysis
- System sizing
- Regulatory compliance
- Financial analysis

9.1 GENERAL SAFETY

DESIGN FOR SAFETY:
- Adequate clearances
- Proper access
- Warning signs
- Color coding
- Equipment labeling

WORKING SPACE:
Minimum clearances around electrical equipment:
- Front: 1.0m minimum
- Sides: 0.75m (if required access)

9.2 ELECTRICAL HAZARDS

ELECTRIC SHOCK:
Prevention:
- Proper earthing
- RCD protection
- Double insulation
- IP ratings for enclosures
- Safety barriers

ARC FLASH:
High fault current hazard
Prevention:
- Proper protection coordination
- Arc-resistant equipment
- PPE during live work
- Safe work procedures

FIRE HAZARDS:
Prevention:
- Proper sizing of conductors
- Overcurrent protection
- Installation per codes
- Regular maintenance

ISOLATION AND LOCKOUT:
- Isolate before work
- Lock and tag
- Verify dead
- Work permit system

PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE):
- Insulated tools
- Gloves (voltage rated)
- Safety shoes
- Arc flash suit (if required)
- Face shield

QUALIFIED PERSONS:
- Licensed electricians
- Proper training
- Competency assessment
- Continuing education

CHAPTER 10: ELECTRICITY GRID CODE 2019
======================================================================

10.1 OVERVIEW

BANGLADESH ENERGY REGULATORY COMMISSION:
Electricity Grid Code 2019

PURPOSE:
The Grid Code specifies criteria, guidelines, basic rules,
procedures, responsibilities, standards and obligations for the:
- Operation
- Maintenance
- Development
of the Electricity Transmission System of Bangladesh

10.2 SCOPE

APPLICABILITY:
- Transmission system operators
- Distribution system operators
- Generators
- Large consumers
- BPDB as Single Buyer

VOLTAGE LEVELS:
- High voltage transmission: 400 kV, 230 kV, 132 kV
- Medium voltage distribution: 33 kV, 11 kV
- Low voltage distribution: 400 V

10.3 CONNECTION REQUIREMENTS

TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS:
- Voltage limits
- Frequency limits (49.5-50.5 Hz)
- Power factor requirements
- Harmonic limits
- Reactive power capability

PROTECTION:
- Relay protection
- Circuit breakers
- Isolation
- Metering

METERING:
- Accuracy class
- Communication
- Data logging
- Testing and calibration

10.4 POWER QUALITY

VOLTAGE VARIATIONS:
Permissible limits:
- 132 kV and above: ±5%
- 33 kV and 11 kV: ±6%
- 400 V: ±10%

FREQUENCY:
Normal operating range: 49.5-50.5 Hz
Acceptable range: 49.0-51.0 Hz

HARMONICS:
Limits per IEC standards
- Total Harmonic Distortion (THD)
- Individual harmonic limits

10.5 DEMAND SIDE MANAGEMENT

LOAD MANAGEMENT:
- Time-of-use tariffs
- Demand response
- Interruptible loads
- Load shedding protocols

DISTRIBUTED GENERATION:
- Grid connection requirements
- Protection and control
- Power quality
- Safety
