ENVIRONMENTAL CONSERVATION ACT AND CONSTRUCTION EIA REQUIREMENTS
ENVIRONMENTAL REGULATIONS FOR CONSTRUCTION IN BANGLADESH
======================================================================

REGULATORY INFORMATION
-----------------------
Primary Legislation: Environmental Conservation Act 1995 (ECA '95)
Amendment: Environmental Conservation (Amendment) Act 2010
Rules: Environmental Conservation Rules 1997 (ECR '97)
Regulatory Authority: Department of Environment (DOE)
Ministry: Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change
Legal Status: Mandatory compliance required

======================================================================
CHAPTER 1: LEGISLATIVE FRAMEWORK
======================================================================

1.1 ENVIRONMENTAL CONSERVATION ACT 1995

The Bangladesh Environment Conservation Act 1995 was enacted for
fulfilling three major objectives:

a) CONSERVATION OF ENVIRONMENT
   - Protection of natural resources
   - Preservation of ecosystems
   - Sustainable development

b) IMPROVEMENT OF ENVIRONMENTAL STANDARDS
   - Setting quality standards
   - Monitoring and assessment
   - Continuous improvement

c) CONTROL AND MITIGATION OF ENVIRONMENTAL POLLUTION
   - Pollution prevention
   - Emission control
   - Waste management

1.2 LEGAL BASES FOR EIA

Legislative bases for Environmental Impact Assessment in Bangladesh
are:
- Environmental Conservation Act 1995 (ECA '95)
- Environmental Conservation Rules 1997 (ECR '97)

The DOE (Department of Environment), under the Ministry of
Environment and Forest, is the regulatory body responsible for
enforcing the ECA '95 and ECR '97.

1.3 AMENDMENT ACT 2010

Section 12 (4) of the Act (as amended in 2010) provides guidance to
adopt detailed rules on:

- Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) Report preparation
- Environmental Management Plan development
- Judging of Public Opinion
- Public Access to Information
- Structure and Function of the Environment Clearance Committee
- Minimum Necessary Conditions for Clearance
- Appeal procedures
- Other related matters

CHAPTER 2: ENVIRONMENTAL CONSERVATION RULES 1997
======================================================================

2.1 OVERVIEW OF ECR 1997

The Environmental Conservation Rules (ECR) 1997, the main subsidiary
legislation of the BECA, provides specific rules and procedures for
various categories of projects in relation to their approval prior
to construction and operation.

2.2 PROJECT CATEGORIZATION

ECR 1997 categorizes industrial and development projects based on
environmental impact potential:

RED CATEGORY (High Impact):
Projects with significant environmental impact requiring:
- Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) for site clearance
- Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) for environmental clearance
- Environmental Management Plan (EMP)

Examples in construction sector:
- Large industrial facilities
- Power plants
- Major highways and bridges
- Large commercial complexes
- Port and harbor facilities
- Airports

ORANGE-A CATEGORY (Medium-High Impact):
Projects requiring EIA and EMP
Examples:
- Medium-scale industries
- Brick kilns (improved technology)
- Medium commercial developments
- Road construction projects

ORANGE-B CATEGORY (Medium Impact):
Projects requiring IEE and EMP
Examples:
- Small to medium industries
- Housing projects (specific sizes)
- Commercial buildings

GREEN CATEGORY (Low Impact):
Projects with minimal environmental impact
- Require Environmental Clearance Certificate
- No EIA required, but compliance monitoring needed

2.3 CLEARANCE REQUIREMENTS BY CATEGORY

RED CATEGORY INFRASTRUCTURE:
Must submit:
1. IEE (Initial Environmental Examination) for site clearance
2. EIA (Environmental Impact Assessment) for environmental clearance
3. EMP (Environmental Management Plan)

ORANGE CATEGORY PROJECTS:
- Orange-A: EIA and EMP required
- Orange-B: IEE and EMP required

GREEN CATEGORY PROJECTS:
- Environmental Clearance Certificate
- Basic environmental compliance

CHAPTER 3: ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT ASSESSMENT (EIA) PROCESS
======================================================================

3.1 SIX-STEP EIA PROCESS

The Guidelines present six steps of the EIA process:

STEP 1: SCREENING
Purpose: Determine if project requires EIA
Activities:
- Project classification
- Category determination
- Initial assessment of impacts
- Decision on EIA requirement

STEP 2: SCOPING
Purpose: Identify key environmental issues to be studied
Activities:
- Define study boundaries
- Identify significant impacts
- Determine assessment methodology
- Establish Terms of Reference (ToR)
- Stakeholder consultation

STEP 3: BASELINE DATA GENERATION
Purpose: Establish existing environmental conditions
Components:
- Physical environment (air, water, soil, geology)
- Biological environment (flora, fauna, ecosystems)
- Socio-economic environment (population, economy, culture)
- Collection period: Minimum one season, preferably one year

Data Collection Methods:
- Field surveys
- Laboratory testing
- Literature review
- Remote sensing and GIS
- Stakeholder interviews
- Community consultations

STEP 4: IMPACT ASSESSMENT
Purpose: Predict and evaluate environmental impacts
Activities:
- Identify potential impacts (positive and negative)
- Assess magnitude and significance
- Evaluate cumulative impacts
- Consider alternatives
- Quantitative and qualitative analysis

Impact Categories:
- Direct and indirect impacts
- Short-term and long-term impacts
- Reversible and irreversible impacts
- Local and regional impacts

STEP 5: MITIGATION OF IMPACTS
Purpose: Develop measures to reduce adverse impacts
Mitigation Hierarchy:
1. Avoidance: Prevent impacts from occurring
2. Minimization: Reduce impact magnitude
3. Compensation: Offset unavoidable impacts
4. Enhancement: Increase positive impacts

STEP 6: ENVIRONMENTAL MANAGEMENT PLAN (EMP)
Purpose: Ensure implementation of mitigation measures
Components:
- Mitigation measures details
- Implementation schedule
- Responsible parties
- Monitoring program
- Budget allocation
- Reporting requirements

3.2 EIA REPORT STRUCTURE

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Brief overview of project and key findings

CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION
- Project background
- Objectives of EIA
- Regulatory framework
- Study approach

CHAPTER 2: PROJECT DESCRIPTION
- Project location
- Technical specifications
- Construction activities
- Operational activities
- Project schedule
- Resource requirements

CHAPTER 3: POLICY, LEGAL AND ADMINISTRATIVE FRAMEWORK
- Relevant legislation
- Environmental standards
- Institutional arrangements
- International agreements

CHAPTER 4: DESCRIPTION OF ENVIRONMENT
- Study area delineation
- Physical environment
- Biological environment
- Socio-economic environment
- Baseline data presentation

CHAPTER 5: IMPACT ASSESSMENT
- Impact identification
- Impact prediction
- Impact evaluation
- Significance assessment
- Cumulative impacts

CHAPTER 6: MITIGATION MEASURES
- Proposed mitigation measures
- Alternative analysis
- Best management practices
- Technology selection

CHAPTER 7: ENVIRONMENTAL MANAGEMENT PLAN
- Organization and responsibilities
- Mitigation implementation plan
- Monitoring program
- Training requirements
- Budget and resources

CHAPTER 8: PUBLIC CONSULTATION
- Consultation methodology
- Stakeholder identification
- Public concerns
- Incorporation of feedback

CHAPTER 9: CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
- Summary of impacts
- Key mitigation measures
- Recommendations
- Overall assessment

APPENDICES:
- Terms of Reference
- Baseline data
- Calculation details
- Consultation records
- Photographs
- Maps and drawings

CHAPTER 4: EIA FOR CONSTRUCTION PROJECTS
======================================================================

4.1 CONSTRUCTION PROJECTS REQUIRING EIA

BUILDING CONSTRUCTION:
- High-rise buildings (above specified height/area)
- Large commercial complexes
- Industrial buildings
- Special structures

INFRASTRUCTURE PROJECTS:
- Major highways and expressways
- Bridges (especially over water bodies)
- Flyovers and interchanges
- Railway projects
- Airport construction/expansion
- Port and harbor facilities

URBAN DEVELOPMENT:
- Large housing projects
- Township developments
- Industrial parks
- Special economic zones

4.2 KEY ENVIRONMENTAL CONCERNS IN CONSTRUCTION

DURING CONSTRUCTION PHASE:

Air Quality:
- Dust generation from excavation and material handling
- Exhaust emissions from construction equipment
- Material storage and transportation impacts
- Mitigation: Water spraying, covers, emission controls

Noise and Vibration:
- Construction equipment noise
- Blasting (if required)
- Pile driving operations
- Traffic noise
- Mitigation: Noise barriers, timing restrictions, equipment selection

Water Quality:
- Sedimentation and erosion
- Construction site runoff
- Dewatering operations
- Wastewater from construction activities
- Mitigation: Siltation controls, treatment, proper disposal

Soil and Land:
- Topsoil disturbance
- Erosion and sedimentation
- Contamination from spills
- Land use changes
- Mitigation: Erosion control, soil conservation, proper handling

Waste Generation:
- Construction debris
- Excavated materials
- Packaging materials
- Hazardous waste (paints, solvents, oils)
- Mitigation: Waste management plan, recycling, proper disposal

Traffic and Access:
- Construction vehicle movements
- Road congestion
- Safety concerns
- Mitigation: Traffic management plan, designated routes

Social Impacts:
- Community disturbance
- Access disruption
- Employment opportunities
- Health and safety
- Mitigation: Community consultation, safety measures

DURING OPERATIONAL PHASE:

Air Quality:
- Vehicle emissions (if roads)
- HVAC systems
- Backup generators
- Mitigation: Emission standards, energy efficiency

Water and Wastewater:
- Water consumption
- Wastewater generation
- Storm water management
- Mitigation: Water conservation, treatment systems

Solid Waste:
- Domestic and commercial waste
- Special waste handling
- Mitigation: Waste management systems

Energy Consumption:
- Electricity use
- Fuel consumption
- Mitigation: Energy efficiency, renewable energy

4.3 SPECIFIC ASSESSMENTS FOR BRIDGE CONSTRUCTION

A research paper on "Environmental Impact Assessment: Integrated
Evaluation of Bridge Construction Project in Bangladesh" highlights
specific considerations for bridge projects:

AQUATIC ENVIRONMENT:
- Impact on water flow
- Sedimentation during construction
- Impact on aquatic life
- Navigation considerations
- Fisheries impacts

HYDROLOGICAL IMPACTS:
- River morphology changes
- Flood plain modifications
- Drainage pattern alterations
- Bank erosion potential

ECOLOGICAL IMPACTS:
- Habitat disturbance
- Wildlife migration routes
- Vegetation clearing
- Protected species considerations

SOCIO-ECONOMIC IMPACTS:
- Land acquisition and resettlement
- Livelihood impacts (especially fishing communities)
- Access improvements
- Economic development opportunities

CHAPTER 5: ENVIRONMENTAL CLEARANCE PROCESS
======================================================================

5.1 APPLICATION FOR ENVIRONMENTAL CLEARANCE

REQUIRED DOCUMENTS:
1. Prescribed application form
2. Project proposal document
3. EIA report (for Red/Orange-A category)
4. IEE report (for Orange-B category)
5. Environmental Management Plan
6. Maps and drawings
7. Proof of land ownership/permission
8. NOC from local authority
9. Public consultation documentation
10. Fees payment receipt

5.2 DOE REVIEW PROCESS

INITIAL REVIEW:
- Document completeness check
- Administrative compliance
- Fee verification
- Acknowledgment issuance

TECHNICAL REVIEW:
DOE technical team reviews:
- Adequacy of baseline data
- Impact assessment methodology
- Mitigation measures proposed
- EMP comprehensiveness
- Compliance with standards

SITE VISIT:
DOE officials conduct site inspection to verify:
- Project location
- Baseline conditions
- Sensitive receptors
- Feasibility of mitigation measures

EXPERT COMMITTEE REVIEW:
For major projects:
- Multi-disciplinary expert committee
- Technical evaluation
- Recommendations for approval/rejection

PUBLIC HEARING:
For significant projects:
- Public notification
- Stakeholder meeting
- Recording of concerns
- Project proponent response

5.3 CLEARANCE DECISION

APPROVAL:
Environmental Clearance Certificate issued with conditions:
- Specific conditions for implementation
- Monitoring requirements
- Reporting obligations
- Validity period

CONDITIONAL APPROVAL:
Approval with specific requirements:
- Additional studies
- Enhanced mitigation
- Phased implementation
- Regular reporting

REJECTION:
Application rejected if:
- Unacceptable environmental impacts
- Inadequate mitigation measures
- Non-compliance with regulations
- Critical area impacts

5.4 CLEARANCE VALIDITY

- Typically valid for specific period (e.g., 3-5 years)
- Valid for construction phase
- May require renewal
- Conditional on compliance

CHAPTER 6: ENVIRONMENTAL MANAGEMENT PLAN (EMP)
======================================================================

6.1 EMP COMPONENTS

INSTITUTIONAL ARRANGEMENTS:
- Designated environmental officer/manager
- Reporting structure
- Contractor responsibilities
- Supervision mechanisms

MITIGATION MEASURES:
Detailed specifications for each impact:
- Description of measure
- Implementation location
- Timing and duration
- Responsible party
- Cost estimate

MONITORING PROGRAM:
For each environmental parameter:
- Parameters to be monitored
- Monitoring locations
- Frequency of monitoring
- Sampling and analysis methods
- Acceptable standards/limits
- Reporting format

TRAINING AND CAPACITY BUILDING:
- Environmental awareness training
- Safety training
- Equipment operation training
- Emergency response training

REPORTING:
- Monthly progress reports
- Quarterly monitoring reports
- Annual environmental reports
- Incident reporting
- Submission to DOE

BUDGET:
- Cost of mitigation measures
- Monitoring costs
- Training costs
- Contingency allocation

6.2 CONSTRUCTION PHASE EMP

DUST CONTROL:
- Water spraying on roads and work areas (2-3 times daily)
- Covering of material stockpiles
- Vehicle speed limits
- Material transport covering
- Monitoring: TSP, PM10, PM2.5

NOISE CONTROL:
- Equipment maintenance
- Noise barriers/enclosures
- Restricted working hours (especially near residential areas)
- Equipment selection (low-noise options)
- Monitoring: Noise levels at boundaries

WATER QUALITY PROTECTION:
- Silt fences and sediment traps
- Construction site drainage
- Wastewater treatment
- Spill prevention and response
- Monitoring: Turbidity, pH, suspended solids

EROSION AND SEDIMENT CONTROL:
- Progressive site stabilization
- Temporary vegetation
- Drainage controls
- Slope protection
- Monitoring: Visual inspection, erosion extent

WASTE MANAGEMENT:
- Segregation at source
- Designated storage areas
- Regular removal and disposal
- Recycling of materials
- Monitoring: Waste quantities, disposal records

TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT:
- Traffic management plan
- Designated haul routes
- Time restrictions
- Speed limits
- Safety signage

WORKER HEALTH AND SAFETY:
- Personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Safety training
- First aid facilities
- Emergency response plan
- Monitoring: Accident records, near misses

6.3 OPERATIONAL PHASE EMP

ONGOING MONITORING:
- Air quality monitoring
- Water quality monitoring
- Noise monitoring
- Waste management monitoring

COMPLIANCE REPORTING:
- Regular reports to DOE
- Public disclosure
- Complaint handling mechanism

ADAPTIVE MANAGEMENT:
- Review of monitoring results
- Adjustment of measures if needed
- Continuous improvement

CHAPTER 7: ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY STANDARDS
======================================================================

7.1 AIR QUALITY STANDARDS

AMBIENT AIR QUALITY STANDARDS (ECR 1997):

Particulate Matter (PM10):
- Annual average: 50 μg/m³
- 24-hour average: 150 μg/m³

Particulate Matter (PM2.5):
- Annual average: 15 μg/m³
- 24-hour average: 65 μg/m³

Sulfur Dioxide (SO2):
- Annual average: 80 μg/m³
- 24-hour average: 365 μg/m³

Nitrogen Dioxide (NO2):
- Annual average: 100 μg/m³
- 24-hour average: 150 μg/m³

Carbon Monoxide (CO):
- 8-hour average: 10 mg/m³
- 1-hour average: 40 mg/m³

7.2 WATER QUALITY STANDARDS

SURFACE WATER QUALITY STANDARDS:

Classification based on use:
- Drinking water source
- Recreation
- Fisheries
- Industrial use
- Navigation

Key parameters:
- pH: 6.5-8.5
- Dissolved Oxygen: >5 mg/l (fisheries)
- BOD: <2 mg/l (drinking water source)
- Total Suspended Solids: <10 mg/l

WASTEWATER DISCHARGE STANDARDS:

Inland Surface Water:
- pH: 6.5-9.0
- BOD: 50 mg/l
- COD: 200 mg/l
- Total Suspended Solids: 150 mg/l
- Oil and Grease: 10 mg/l

Public Sewer:
- pH: 6.0-9.0
- BOD: 250 mg/l
- Total Suspended Solids: 500 mg/l

7.3 NOISE STANDARDS

NOISE LEVEL STANDARDS (dBA):

Residential Areas:
- Day time (6 AM - 9 PM): 55 dBA
- Night time (9 PM - 6 AM): 45 dBA

Commercial Areas:
- Day time: 65 dBA
- Night time: 55 dBA

Industrial Areas:
- Day time: 75 dBA
- Night time: 70 dBA

7.4 COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

Wastes shall be properly treated as specified by environmental
quality standards of Bangladesh before their disposal into public
sewers or into natural bodies of water.
