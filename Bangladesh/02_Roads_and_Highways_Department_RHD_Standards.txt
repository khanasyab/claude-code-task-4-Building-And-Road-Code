CHAPTER 1: OVERVIEW OF RHD
======================================================================

1.1 ORGANIZATION BACKGROUND

The Roads and Highways Department (RHD) is an agency of the
Government of Bangladesh responsible for the construction and
maintenance of highways and bridges across Bangladesh. RHD is
responsible for the construction and maintenance of the major road
and bridge network of Bangladesh.

The organization operates under the Ministry of Road Transport and
Bridges and plays a crucial role in the development of Bangladesh's
transportation infrastructure.

1.2 JURISDICTION AND RESPONSIBILITIES

RHD is primarily responsible for:

a) National Highways
b) Regional Highways
c) Zila Roads (District Roads)
d) Major bridges on national and regional routes
e) Road maintenance and rehabilitation
f) Quality control and standards development
g) Project planning and implementation
h) Technical specifications and design standards

1.3 ROAD NETWORK CLASSIFICATION

The road network in Bangladesh is classified as follows:

NATIONAL HIGHWAYS (N-Roads):
- Primary arterial routes
- Connect major cities and ports
- International connectivity routes
- Highest design standards

REGIONAL HIGHWAYS (R-Roads):
- Connect districts and important centers
- Secondary arterial network
- Medium to high traffic volumes

ZILA ROADS (Z-Roads):
- District-level connectivity
- Connect upazila headquarters
- Lower traffic volumes

Note: Upazila roads, union roads, and village roads fall under the
jurisdiction of Local Government Engineering Department (LGED).

2.1 GEOMETRIC DESIGN STANDARDS MANUAL

The "Geometric Design Standards Manual (Revised) 2005" is the
primary reference document for geometric design of roads under RHD
jurisdiction.

Key aspects covered:

DESIGN SPEED:
Design speeds are established based on:
- Road classification
- Terrain conditions (plain, rolling, mountainous)
- Traffic characteristics
- Economic considerations

Typical design speeds:
- National highways: 80-100 km/h (plain terrain)
- Regional highways: 60-80 km/h
- Zila roads: 50-60 km/h

HORIZONTAL ALIGNMENT:
- Minimum radius of horizontal curves
- Superelevation rates
- Transition curves
- Sight distance requirements

VERTICAL ALIGNMENT:
- Maximum gradients
- Minimum curve lengths
- Stopping sight distance
- Overtaking sight distance

CROSS-SECTIONAL ELEMENTS:
- Carriageway widths
- Shoulder widths
- Side slopes
- Clear zones

2.2 REFERENCE TO INTERNATIONAL STANDARDS

When RHD Standards have no criteria about a certain item, design is
conducted based on AASHTO (American Association of State Highway
and Transportation Officials) standards.

This ensures that Bangladesh road design remains current with
international best practices while being adapted to local conditions.

CHAPTER 3: CARRIAGEWAY AND SHOULDER STANDARDS
======================================================================

3.1 CARRIAGEWAY WIDTHS

SINGLE LANE CARRIAGEWAYS:
3.7m wide: Standard single lane carriageway width suitable for more
lightly-trafficked feeder roads and local connections.

TWO-LANE CARRIAGEWAYS:
5.5m wide: Minimum width two-lane carriageway for roads with low to
moderate traffic volumes.

7.0m wide: Standard two-lane carriageway for roads with moderate
traffic volumes.

7.3m wide: Enhanced two-lane carriageway for higher traffic volumes
or important routes.

MULTI-LANE CARRIAGEWAYS:
- Each lane: 3.65m (standard)
- Additional width for truck lanes: 3.75m
- Median widths: Variable based on road classification

3.2 SHOULDER DESIGN

Shoulders are essential for safety and capacity and must be provided
on all roads.

SHOULDER FUNCTIONS:
- Emergency stopping area
- Lateral support for pavement structure
- Maintenance access
- Accommodation of stopped vehicles
- Improved sight distance
- Recovery area for errant vehicles

SHOULDER WIDTHS:
National highways:
- Paved shoulder: 1.5-2.5m each side
- Earthen shoulder: Additional 1.0-1.5m

Regional highways:
- Paved shoulder: 1.0-1.5m each side
- Earthen shoulder: Additional 1.0m

Zila roads:
- Paved shoulder: 0.5-1.0m each side
- Earthen shoulder: Additional 0.5-1.0m

SHOULDER CONSTRUCTION:
- Same structural strength as carriageway (or slightly reduced)
- Proper cross-fall for drainage (3-5%)
- Edge drop-off limited to 75mm maximum

3.3 HORIZONTAL CURVES

WIDENING AT CURVES:
For Design types 6, 7, and 8, a widened paved carriageway up to
5.5m width with required super-elevation should be provided at all
turning points depending on radius of horizontal curves to prevent
hazards of accidents.

All existing curve points on narrower roads with carriageway up to
3.7m should be widened to 5.5m with proper super-elevation.

SUPERELEVATION:
Maximum superelevation rates:
- Rural areas: 7-10%
- Urban areas: 4-6%
- Ice and snow areas: 6%

Superelevation runoff length:
Calculated based on:
- Design speed
- Rate of superelevation
- Width of rotation

3.4 EMBANKMENT DESIGN

Rural roads in Bangladesh are typically constructed on high
embankments due to:
- Flooding considerations
- High water table
- Poor soil conditions
- Drainage requirements

EMBANKMENT HEIGHT:
- Minimum height above highest flood level
- Freeboard allowance: 0.3-0.6m
- Settlement allowance

SIDE SLOPES:
The standard side slope for embankments is 2 horizontal to 1
vertical (2:1).

For special conditions:
- Unstable soils: 3:1 or flatter
- Stable rock: 1:1 or steeper
- Economic constraints: Analysis-based optimization

SLOPE PROTECTION:
- Turfing or grass planting
- Revetment for severe erosion areas
- Retaining structures where necessary

CHAPTER 4: PAVEMENT DESIGN STANDARDS
======================================================================

4.1 PAVEMENT DESIGN METHODOLOGY

RHD follows multiple approaches to pavement design:

AASHTO METHOD:
- AASHTO 1993 Guide for Design of Pavement Structures
- Empirical design approach
- Based on AASHO Road Test results
- Adapted for Bangladesh conditions

MECHANISTIC-EMPIRICAL APPROACH:
- Layered elastic theory
- Fatigue and rutting criteria
- Calibrated for local materials and conditions

4.2 TRAFFIC ANALYSIS

TRAFFIC DATA REQUIREMENTS:
- Average Daily Traffic (ADT)
- Commercial vehicle percentage
- Vehicle classification
- Traffic growth rate
- Design period (typically 15-20 years)

EQUIVALENT SINGLE AXLE LOAD (ESAL):
- Standard axle: 80 kN (8.2 tonnes)
- Conversion factors for different vehicle types
- Cumulative ESALs over design period

4.3 PAVEMENT TYPES AND STANDARDS

FLEXIBLE PAVEMENTS:
Most common pavement type in Bangladesh

Typical flexible pavement structure:
- Bituminous wearing course: 25-50mm
- Binder course: 50-75mm
- Base course: 150-250mm
- Sub-base course: 150-300mm
- Subgrade preparation

RIGID PAVEMENTS:
Used for heavy traffic and special applications

Concrete pavement design:
- Concrete slab thickness: 200-300mm
- Concrete strength: Minimum 30 MPa
- Base course (optional): 100-150mm
- Sub-base course: 100-200mm

COMPOSITE PAVEMENTS:
- Bituminous overlay on concrete base
- Used for rehabilitation projects

CHAPTER 5: BITUMINOUS MATERIALS AND SPECIFICATIONS
======================================================================

5.1 BITUMEN SPECIFICATIONS

RHD Technical Specifications - Division 3 (Pavement Works) covers
bituminous materials in detail.

BITUMEN GRADES:
- Penetration grade: 60/70, 80/100
- Viscosity grade: VG-10, VG-30, VG-40
- Modified bitumen (polymer modified)

CUTBACK BITUMEN:
Specifications provided with reference to BDS 867, 1978
- Rapid curing (RC)
- Medium curing (MC)
- Slow curing (SC)

BITUMEN EMULSION:
- Cationic emulsions
- Anionic emulsions
- Application-specific grades

5.2 BITUMINOUS PAVEMENT LAYERS

SURFACE DRESSING / SEAL COAT:
- Single or double coat
- Bitumen application rate: 0.8-1.5 kg/m²
- Aggregate size and grading specified
- Traffic control during curing

BITUMINOUS SURFACE TREATMENT (BST):
Thickness: 25mm or less
Application:
- Prime coat
- Tack coat
- Aggregate spread and rolling

ASPHALTIC CONCRETE (AC):
Dense-graded hot mix asphalt
- Wearing course: 25-50mm
- Binder course: 50-75mm
- Aggregate gradation per specifications
- Bitumen content: 4.5-6.5% by weight
- Marshall stability requirements
- Compaction standards: 95-98% of laboratory density

DENSE BITUMINOUS MACADAM (DBM):
- Coarser aggregate than AC
- Thickness: 50-100mm
- Used as base or binder course

5.3 CONSTRUCTION SPECIFICATIONS

SURFACE PREPARATION:
- Cleaning and sweeping
- Prime coat application (on granular surfaces)
- Tack coat application (on existing bituminous surfaces)

MIXING AND LAYING:
- Hot mix plant requirements
- Temperature control
- Laying equipment and procedures
- Joint construction

COMPACTION:
- Roller types and weights
- Number of passes
- Temperature requirements
- Density specifications

QUALITY CONTROL:
- Mix design approval
- Plant control testing
- Field density testing
- Surface regularity measurements
- Thickness verification

CHAPTER 6: BRIDGE DESIGN STANDARDS
======================================================================

6.1 BRIDGE DESIGN MANUAL

RHD has established comprehensive Bridge Design Standards for Roads
& Highways Department covering all aspects of bridge design.

6.2 DESIGN CODES AND STANDARDS

GEOMETRIC DESIGN:
Based on "Geometric Design Standards for RHD"

LOADING STANDARDS:
The AASHTO LRFD Bridge Design Specifications, SI Units, 2007
(RSM'08), have been followed for:
- Design dead and live loading
- Analysis and design of structures

ENVIRONMENTAL LOADING:
Bangladesh National Building Code (BNBC'93) provisions have been
followed for:
- Wind loading
- Earthquake loading

Note: Current projects may reference BNBC 2020 for seismic design.

6.3 DESIGN LOADS

DEAD LOADS:
- Self-weight of structure
- Wearing surface: 80-100mm thickness
- Railings, parapets, and appurtenances
- Utilities

LIVE LOADS:
- IRC Class A or AASHTO HL-93 loading
- Lane loading
- Truck loading
- Impact factors
- Multiple presence factors

SEISMIC LOADS:
Bangladesh lies well within an active seismic zone.
- Seismic zone classification per BNBC
- Response spectrum analysis
- Seismic detailing requirements
- Foundation design for seismic loads

WIND LOADS:
- Basic wind speed per location
- Exposure category
- Wind pressure on structure and vehicles

HYDRAULIC CONSIDERATIONS:
- Flood discharge calculation
- Scour depth assessment
- Waterway adequacy
- Foundation depth below scour level

6.4 BRIDGE TYPES

COMMON BRIDGE TYPES IN BANGLADESH:
- Reinforced concrete slab bridges (short spans)
- Reinforced concrete girder bridges
- Pre-stressed concrete girder bridges
- Steel girder bridges
- Composite steel-concrete bridges
- Box girder bridges (for longer spans)

FOUNDATION TYPES:
- Shallow foundations (where suitable)
- Pile foundations (most common)
- Well foundations
- Caisson foundations

6.5 SEISMIC DESIGN CONSIDERATIONS

SEISMIC ZONE FACTORS:
Bangladesh divided into multiple seismic zones
- Zone coefficients applied based on location
- Higher requirements for critical/lifeline bridges

DUCTILE DETAILING:
- Reinforcement detailing for ductility
- Confinement requirements
- Plastic hinge zones
- Capacity design principles

SEISMIC ISOLATION:
- Elastomeric bearings
- Seismic isolation devices for critical bridges
- Performance-based design approaches

CHAPTER 7: DRAINAGE DESIGN STANDARDS
======================================================================

7.1 SURFACE DRAINAGE

CROSS-FALL:
Carriageway:
- Straight sections: 2.5-3.0%
- Superelevated sections: As per superelevation design

Shoulders:
- 3.0-5.0% steeper than carriageway

LONGITUDINAL DRAINAGE:
Side drains:
- Minimum depth: 0.6m
- Side slopes: 2:1 or flatter
- Grass or concrete lining as required

CROSS DRAINAGE STRUCTURES:
- Culverts: For natural drainage crossings
- Sizes: Determined by hydraulic analysis
- Types: Box culverts, pipe culverts, slab culverts

7.2 SUBSURFACE DRAINAGE

- Subsoil drains where high water table exists
- Filter materials and geotextiles
- Outlet provisions

7.3 HYDRAULIC DESIGN

DESIGN RETURN PERIOD:
- Major highways: 50-100 years
- Secondary roads: 25-50 years
- Culverts: 25 years minimum

DESIGN METHODOLOGY:
- Rational method for small catchments
- Hydrograph methods for larger catchments
- Manning's equation for open channel flow
- Orifice and weir equations for culverts

SUBGRADE PREPARATION:

- Surface tolerance

8.3 GRANULAR MATERIALS

SUB-BASE COURSE:
Material specifications:
- Grading requirements
- Plasticity limits
- CBR requirements
- Maximum particle size

Construction:
- Layer thickness: Not exceeding 150mm loose
- Compaction: 98% Standard Proctor
- Surface tolerance: ±20mm

BASE COURSE:
Higher quality material than sub-base
Material specifications:
- Aggregate crushing value: <30%
- Los Angeles abrasion value: <40%
- Flakiness index: <20%
- Elongation index: <35%

Construction:
- Layer thickness: Not exceeding 100mm loose
- Compaction: 98-100% Modified Proctor
- Surface tolerance: ±10mm

8.4 WATER BOUND MACADAM (WBM)

WBM construction specifications:
- Aggregate size and grading
- Layer thickness
- Compaction methods
- Binding material application
- Density requirements

LGED favors WBM/AS (Asphaltic Surface) at present, and in order to
protect the investment, it is covered with a bituminous surface
layer. The wearing surface may vary in thickness from 25mm or even
less in the case of Bituminous Surface Treatment to 150mm or more
in the case of asphalt concrete used for heavy-duty traffic.

9.1 MATERIALS TESTING

SOIL TESTING:
- Grain size analysis
- Atterberg limits
- Compaction tests (Standard and Modified Proctor)
- California Bearing Ratio (CBR)
- Specific gravity
- Moisture content

AGGREGATE TESTING:
- Aggregate crushing value
- Los Angeles abrasion test
- Flakiness and elongation indices
- Specific gravity and absorption
- Gradation analysis
- Soundness test

BITUMEN TESTING:
- Penetration test
- Softening point
- Ductility
- Specific gravity
- Flash and fire point
- Viscosity

CONCRETE TESTING:
- Compressive strength (cubes or cylinders)
- Slump test
- Air content
- Cement content
- Aggregate grading

STEEL TESTING:
- Tensile strength
- Yield strength
- Elongation
- Bend test

9.2 FIELD QUALITY CONTROL

IN-PROCESS INSPECTION:
- Material approval before use
- Mix design verification
- Dimensional checks
- Density testing
- Surface regularity

ACCEPTANCE TESTING:
- Core samples from pavement
- Proof loading of structures
- Non-destructive testing

9.3 TESTING FREQUENCY

Specified in contract documents, typically:
- Soil compaction: Every 100m or layer
- Aggregate: Every 500 tonnes or source
- Bitumen: Every tanker load
- Concrete: Every 50m³ or day's work

10.3 OVERLAY DESIGN

For bituminous carpeting, overlaying of 25-40mm BC (Bituminous
Concrete) will be required after every 7-8 years to maintain
pavement serviceability and extend pavement life.

Overlay thickness design based on:
- Existing pavement condition
- Structural capacity deficit
- Traffic projections
- Life-cycle cost analysis
